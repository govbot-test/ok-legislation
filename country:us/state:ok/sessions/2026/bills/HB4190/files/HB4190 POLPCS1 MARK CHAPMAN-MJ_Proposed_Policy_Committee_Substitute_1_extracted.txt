8 PROPOSED POLICY COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 An Act relating to the Oklahoma State Bureau of
Investigation; creating the Cyber Crime and Fraud
10 Unit within the Oklahoma State Bureau of
Investigation (OSBI); providing the purpose of the
11 Cyber Crime and Fraud Unit; clarifying the
jurisdiction of the Unit; directing the Unit to
12 conduct investigations, provide support to OSBI
agents and requesting agencies, coordinate
13 investigations, develop training, and maintain
specialized personnel; permitting the OSBI to assign
14 personnel, reallocate resources, and establish
internal procedures; making a onetime appropriation;
15 providing purpose of appropriated funds; allowing
unexpended funds to be carried forward; creating the
16 OSBI Cyber Crime and Fraud Unit Revolving Fund;
identifying funding source; authorizing OSBI to make
17 necessary rules; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.
18
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
21 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 152.1 of Title 74, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 16296 Page 1
1 A. There is hereby created within the Oklahoma State Bureau of
2 Investigation (OSBI) a Cyber Crime and Fraud Unit. The Unit shall
3 operate as an internal division of the Bureau and shall exercise
4 only those investigative powers and jurisdiction already granted to
5 the OSBI under existing Oklahoma law.
6 B. The purpose of the Cyber Crime and Fraud Unit shall be to
7 enhance the Bureau's capacity to investigate:
8 1. Cyber-enabled criminal activity, including intrusions,
9 network breaches, ransomware digital extortion, and online
10 exploitation;
11 2. Financial fraud and related schemes, including cyber-
12 facilitated fraud, identity theft, and complex financial deception;
13 and
14 3. Criminal activity involving digital evidence, consistent
15 with the Bureau's existing statutory authority.
16 C. Nothing in this act shall be construed to expand or modify
17 the jurisdiction of the OSBI. The Unit may only initiate or conduct
18 investigations:
19 1. Upon request or referral from a district attorney, law
20 enforcement agency, or state officer authorized under existing law;
21 2. Upon direction of the Governor, consistent with current
22 statutory authority; or
23 3. As otherwise permitted under existing OSBI jurisdictional
24 statutes.
Req. No. 16296 Page 2
1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 152.1a of Title 74, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. The Cyber Crime and Fraud Unit shall, within the Oklahoma
5 State Bureau of Investigation's existing jurisdiction:
6 1. Conduct investigations involving cyber-crime, digital
7 evidence, and fraud-related offenses;
8 2. Provide digital forensics, technical assistance, and
9 analytical support to OSBI agents and requesting agencies;
10 3. Coordinate with federal, state, tribal, and local partners
11 on cyber and fraud investigations;
12 4. Develop and deliver training to law enforcement agencies on
13 cyber-crime trends, digital evidence handling, and fraud prevention;
14 and
15 5. Maintain specialized personnel, including agents, analysts,
16 forensic examiners, and technical support staff.
17 B. The OSBI Director may assign personnel, reallocate
18 resources, and establish internal procedures necessary to carry out
19 the responsibilities of the Unit.
20 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 152.1b of Title 74, unless there
22 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 A. There is hereby appropriated, subject to available funding,
24 one time to the Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation, from monies
Req. No. 16296 Page 3
1 not otherwise appropriated from the General Revenue Fund of the
2 State Treasury, the sum of Three Million Dollars ($3,000,000.00) for
3 the purpose of establishing and operating the Cyber Crime and Fraud
4 Unit.
5 B. The funds appropriated in subsection A of this section shall
6 be used for:
7 1. Personnel costs, including agents, analysts, forensic
8 examiners, and support staff;
9 2. Technology, digital forensics equipment, and investigative
10 tools;
11 3. Training, certifications, and professional development; and
12 4. Operational expenses necessary to support cyber-crime and
13 fraud investigations.
14 C. Any unexpended funds shall be carried forward and used for
15 the same purpose.
16 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 152.1c of Title 74, unless there
18 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving fund
20 for the Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation to be designated the
21 "Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation Cyber Crime and Fraud Unit
22 Revolving Fund". The fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject
23 to fiscal year limitations, and shall consist of all monies
24 appropriated by the Legislature, or received by the Oklahoma State
Req. No. 16296 Page 4
1 Bure au of Investigation, designated for the fund. All monies
2 accruing to the credit of said fund are hereby appropriated and may
3 be budgeted and expended by the Oklahoma State Bureau of
4 Investigation for the purpose of theft and fraud enforcement,
5 prevention, public information, education initiatives, and the
6 creation of the Cyber Crime and Fraud Unit. Expenditures from said
7 fund shall be made upon warrants issued by the State Treasurer
8 against claims filed as prescribed by law with the Director of the
9 Office of Management and Enterprise Services for approval and
10 payment.
11 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
12 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 152.1d of Title 74, unless there
13 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
14 The Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation is authorized to
15 promulgate rules necessary to implement the provisions of this act
16 provided that such rules do not expand the Bureau's jurisdiction
17 beyond that established in existing law.
18 SECTION 6. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

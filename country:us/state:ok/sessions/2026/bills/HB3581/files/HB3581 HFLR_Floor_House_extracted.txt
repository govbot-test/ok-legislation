Section 2:
Section 1312, as amended by Section 10, Chapter 486,
12 O.S.L. 2025 (21 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 1312), which

13 modifying scope and penalty provisions for certain
offenses; making certain acts unlawful; providing
14 penalties; amending 51 O.S. 2021, Section 155, as
amended by Section 21, Chapter 228, O.S.L. 2022 (51
15 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 155), which relates to The

19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
20 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 21 O.S. 2021, Section 1312, as
21 amended by Section 10, Chapter 486, O.S.L. 2025 (21 O.S. Supp. 2025,
22 Section 1312), is amended to read as follows:
23 Section 1312. Every person guilty of participating in any riot
24 is punishable as follows:
HB3581 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 1. If any murder, maiming, robbery, rape or arson was committed
2 in the course of such riot, such person is guilty of a Class A1
3 felony offense punishable in the same manner as a principal in such
4 crime;
5 2. If the purpose of the riotous assembly was to resist the
6 execution of any statute of this state or of the United States, or
7 to obstruct any public officer of this state or of the United
8 States, in the performance of any legal duty, or in serving or
9 executing any legal process, such person shall, upon conviction, be
10 guilty of a Class B3 felony offense punishable by imprisonment in
11 the custody of the Department of Corrections for a term not
12 exceeding ten (10) years and not less than two (2) years;
13 3. If such person carried at the time of such riot any species
14 of firearms, or other deadly or dangerous weapon, or was disguised,
15 such person shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a Class B3 felony
16 offense punishable by imprisonment in the custody of the Department
17 of Corrections for a term not exceeding ten (10) years and not less
18 than two (2) years;
19 4. If such person directed, advised, encouraged or solicited
20 other persons, who participated in the riot to acts of force or
21 violence, such person shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a Class
22 B1 felony offense punishable by imprisonment in the custody of the
23 Department of Corrections for a term not exceeding twenty (20) years
24 and not less than two (2) years; or
HB3581 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 5. If such person commits any assault and battery in the course
2 of a riot, such person shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a Class
3 D2 felony offense punishable by imprisonment in the custody of the
4 Department of Corrections for a term not exceeding two (2) years;
5 6. If such person commits any aggravated assault and battery in
6 the course of a riot, such person shall, upon conviction, be guilty
7 of a Class B1 felony offense punishable by imprisonment in the
8 custody of the Department of Corrections for a term not exceeding
9 ten (10) years;
10 7. If such person willfully damaged, destroyed, vandalized, or
11 defaced any structure, building, or office space in the course of a
12 riot, such person shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a Class D3
13 felony offense punishable by imprisonment in the custody of the
14 Department of Corrections for a term not exceeding two (2) years if
15 the damage was less than One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00) or shall
16 be guilty of a Class D1 felony offense punishable by imprisonment in
17 the custody of the Department of Corrections for a term not
18 exceeding four (4) years if the damage was more than One Thousand
19 Dollars ($1,000.00);
20 8. Every person who wears a mask, hood, covering, or disguise
21 without lawful excuse and for the purpose of concealing his or her
22 identity in the course of a riot and commits any felony therein
23 shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a Class D3 felony offense
24 punishable by imprisonment in the custody of the Department of
HB3581 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 Corr ections as provided for in subsections B through F of Section
2 20P of this title, or by both such fine and imprisonment; or
3 9. Every person who shall unlawfully obstruct the normal use of
4 any public street, highway or road within this state by actively
5 impeding, hindering or restraining motor vehicle traffic or passage
6 thereon, by standing or approaching motor vehicles thereon, or by
7 endangering the safe movement of motor vehicles or pedestrians
8 traveling thereon shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor
9 Class D3 felony offense punishable by imprisonment in the county
10 jail custody of the Department of Corrections for a term not
11 exceeding one (1) year as provided for in subsections B through F of
12 Section 20P of this title or by a fine of not less than One Hundred
13 Dollars ($100.00) and not exceeding Five Thousand Dollars
14 ($5,000.00), or by both such fine and imprisonment. In addition,
15 the person shall be liable for all damages to person or property by
16 reason of the same. As used in this paragraph, "obstruct" means to
17 render impassable or to render passage unreasonably inconvenient or
18 hazardous.
19 In all other cases such person is punishable as for a
20 misdemeanor.

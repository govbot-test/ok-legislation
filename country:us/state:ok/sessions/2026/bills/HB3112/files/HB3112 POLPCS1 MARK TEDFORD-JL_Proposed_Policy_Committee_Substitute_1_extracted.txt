8 PROPOSED POLICY COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 An Act relating to the Oklahoma Alcoholic Beverage
Laws Enforcement Commission; amending 37A O.S. 2021,
10 Section 1-110, which relates to restriction on
Oklahoma Alcoholic Beverage Laws Enforcement
11 Commission members and employees; removing the
restriction against members and employees of the ABLE
12 Commission making campaign contributions; removing
fine for violation; and providing an effective date.
13
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 37A O.S. 2021, Section 1-110, is
18 amended to read as follows:
19 Section 1-110. A. No member or employee of the ABLE Commission
20 shall:
21 1. Be appointed or serve who has been convicted of a felony or
22 of any violation of any federal or state law relating to alcoholic
23 beverages;
24
Req. No. 15993 Page 1
1 2. Directly or indirectly, individually or as a member of a
2 partnership, or as a shareholder of a corporation, have any interest
3 whatsoever in the manufacture, sale or distribution of alcoholic
4 beverages;
5 3. Receive any compensation or profit therefrom, nor have any
6 interest, directly or indirectly, in any business authorized by a
7 license issued pursuant to the provisions of the Oklahoma Alcoholic
8 Beverage Control Act. The holding of membership or elective or
9 appointed office in fraternal organizations which obtain licenses
10 authorized by the Oklahoma Alcoholic Beverage Control Act shall not
11 be considered to be engagement in the alcoholic beverage business;
12 4. Solicit or accept any gift, gratuity, emolument or
13 employment from any person subject to the provisions of the Oklahoma
14 Alcoholic Beverage Control Act, or from any officer, agent or
15 employee thereof;
16 5. Solicit, request from or recommend, directly or indirectly,
17 to any such person or to any officer, agent or employee thereof, the
18 appointment of any person to any place or position, and every such
19 person, and every officer, agent or employee thereof, is hereby
20 forbidden to offer to any member or employee of the ABLE Commission
21 any gift, gratuity, emolument or employment;
22 6. Accept employment within the alcoholic beverage industry for
23 any holder of a license issued pursuant to the provisions of the
24 Oklahoma Alcoholic Beverage Control Act; or
Req. No. 15993 Page 2
1 7. Represent, directly or indirectly, any such licensee in any
2 proceedings before the Director, the ABLE Commission or the Tax
3 Commission within two (2) years following separation from the ABLE
4 Commission.
5 B. Violation of any provision of subsection A of this section
6 shall constitute a misdemeanor. In addition to the penal
7 provisions, any person convicted shall be immediately removed from
8 the office or position he or she holds.
9 C. No license of any kind shall be granted to or retained by
10 any person or any partnership containing any partner who is related
11 to any member of the ABLE Commission or to the Director or Assistant
12 Director by affinity or consanguinity within the third degree or who
13 is related to any other employee of the ABLE Commission by affinity
14 or consanguinity in the first degree. If a license is held in
15 violation of the provisions of this subsection, the member or
16 employee of the ABLE Commission shall not be entitled to receive any
17 compensation or other monies from the State of Oklahoma while a
18 license is held in violation of the provisions of this subsection.
19 D. It shall be unlawful for any member or employee of the ABLE
20 Commission to lend, expend or contribute any money, funds, property
21 or other thing of value, or use his or her official position for the
22 purpose of securing the nomination or election or the defeat of any
23 candidate for public office in the State of Oklahoma.
24
Req. No. 15993 Page 3
1 E. Any person who shall violate the provisions of subsection D
2 of this section shall, upon conviction, be fined not less than Two
3 Thousand Five Hundred Dollars ($2,500.00) nor more than Five
4 Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00), or be imprisoned in the county jail
5 for not more than one (1) year, or by both such fine and
6 imprisonment. Any person found guilty of violating the provisions
7 of this subsection shall, upon conviction, in addition to the
8 criminal penalty imposed herein, be discharged from the office or
9 position he or she holds and shall not be rehired to any state
10 position.
11 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

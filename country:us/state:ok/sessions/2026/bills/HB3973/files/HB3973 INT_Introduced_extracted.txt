6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to the Office of Juvenile Affairs;
stating intent with regard to the Office of Juvenile
8 Affairs for development of secure care and specialty
residential services; requiring the implementation of
9 a reestablishment strategy for certain facility;
authorizing certain assistance by the Office of
10 Management and Enterprise Services; authorizing the
acceptance on behalf of the State gifts of facilities
11 and properties; authorizing the utilization of
certain funds for certain purposes; creating the
12 Southwest Oklahoma Juvenile Center Reestablishment
Revolving Fund; establishing revolving fund nature
13 and characteristics; placing the fund under the
authority of the Office of Juvenile Affairs;
14 establishing purpose for fund usage; requiring
certain expenditure procedures repealing 10A O.S.
15 2021, Section 2-7-618, which relates to Office of
Juvenile Affairs planning and execution of
16 construction strategy for youth campuses; providing
for codification; providing an effective date; and
17 declaring an emergency.
18
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
21 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-7-618A of Title 10A, unless
23 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 14188 Page 1

1 A. It is the intent of the State of Oklahoma for the Office of
2 Juvenile Affairs (OJA) to plan, develop, redevelop and occupy campus
3 modifications to serve Oklahoma youth in need of secure care and
4 specialty residential services.
5 B. In furtherance of this intent announced in subsection A of
6 this act, OJA shall plan and execute a reestablishment strategy for
7 the facility previously operated as the Southwest Oklahoma Juvenile
8 Center, located in Manitou. The Office of Management and Enterprise
9 Services (OMES) is authorized to assist OJA in assessing the best
10 option and proceeding with necessary steps. The Office of
11 Management and Enterprise Services is authorized to accept, on
12 behalf of the State, such facilities, associated properties, and
13 connecting properties if offered to the State as a gift or by
14 immaterial charge. OJA is authorized to make improvements to the
15 facility as needed to achieve the goal of reestablishing the
16 facility's ability to provide residential services. OJA may utilize
17 funds available in the revolving fund created pursuant to subsection
18 C of this act, along with any other funds available to OJA to
19 achieve the purpose described in this act.
20 C. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving
21 fund for the Office of Juvenile Affairs to be designated the
22 "Southwest Oklahoma Juvenile Center Reestablishment Revolving Fund".
23 The fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year
24 limitations, and shall consist of all monies legally designated for

1 depo sit to the fund. All monies accruing to the credit of said fund
2 are hereby appropriated and may be budgeted and expended by the
3 Office of Juvenile Affairs to fulfill the provisions of this act.
4 Expenditures from said fund shall be made upon warrants issued by
5 the State Treasurer against claims filed as prescribed by law with
6 the Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise Services for
7 approval and payment.
8 SECTION 2. REPEALER 10A O.S. 2021, Section 2-7-618, is
9 hereby repealed.
10 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
11 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
12 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
13 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
14 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

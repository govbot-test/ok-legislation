10 AS INTRODUCED
11 An Act relating to eminent domain; defining term;
prohibiting taking of private property unless for
12 certain uses; prohibiting expansion of powers absent
express statutory authority; amending 27 O.S.2021,
13 Sections 5 and 17, which relate to local governments
and resale of surplus property; conforming language;
14 updating statutory references; providing for
codification; and providing an effective date.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 41 of Title 27, unless there is
20 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. As used in this section, and subject to subsection B of this
22 section, "public use" means:
23 1. The possession, occupation, ownership, and enjoyment of land
24 by the general public, or by a government entity, for use as a

1 publ ic highway, road, easement or right-of-way, public building,
2 public cemetery, public park, or for any other use expressly
3 authorized in Section 5 of Title 27 of the Oklahoma Statutes; and
4 2. The possession, occupation, and ownership of land for the
5 operations of a public utility or a private entity that is expressly
6 authorized by statute to exercise eminent domain and that serves the
7 general public.
8 B. Limitations on the Exercise of Eminent Domain. Private
9 property shall not be taken or damaged unless the taking is
10 necessary for a public use as defined in subsection A of this
11 section, and just compensation is paid. Economic development,
12 including but not limited to increased tax revenues, tax base,
13 employment, or general economic health, shall not constitute a
14 public use. No taking shall be justified or authorized on the
15 grounds that it may result in increased economic development, or
16 secondary or incidental economic benefits.
17 C. Restriction on Expansion of Eminent Domain Powers. A
18 governmental body subordinate to the state may not exercise, create,
19 extend, or expand any power of eminent domain without express
20 statutory authority. No ordinance, charter, resolution, policy, or
21 local action may broaden the definition of public use beyond the
22 uses set forth in this section.

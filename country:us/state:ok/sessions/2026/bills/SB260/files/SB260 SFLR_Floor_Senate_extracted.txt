12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1150 of Title 74, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 A. There is hereby created the Oklahoma Infrastructure Long-
17 Range Planning Commission to be housed under and staffed by the
18 Oklahoma Department of Commerce.
19 B. The Commission shall be composed of twelve (12) members as
20 follows:
21 1. The Chief Executive Officer of the Oklahoma Department of
22 Commerce, or his or her designee;
23 2. The Director of the Department of Transportation, or his or
24 her designee;

11 7. One county commissioner from Tulsa or Oklahoma County,

13 8. One county commissioner from a county with a population less
14 than one hundred fifty thousand (150,000) persons according to the
15 latest Federal Decennial Census, appointed by the President Pro

17 9. One current or past mayor of a municipality having a
18 population less than thirty-five thousand (35,000) persons according
19 to the latest Federal Decennial Census, appointed by the Speaker of
20 the House of Representatives; and
21 10. One current or past mayor of a municipality having a
22 population less than seventy-five thousand (75,000) persons
23 according to the latest Federal Decennial Census, appointed by the
24 Speaker of the House of Representatives.

1 C. The initial term of office of the appointed members shall be
2 as follows:
3 1. One of each of the members appointed by the Governor, the
4 President Pro Tempore of the Senate, and the Speaker of the House of
5 Representatives shall serve a one-year term;
6 2. One of each of the members appointed by the Governor, the
7 President Pro Tempore of the Senate, and the Speaker of the House of
8 Representatives shall serve a two-year term;
9 3. One of each of the members appointed by the Governor, the
10 President Pro Tempore of the Senate, and the Speaker of the House of
11 Representatives shall serve a three-year term; and
12 4. One of each of the members appointed by the Governor, the
13 President Pro Tempore of the Senate, and the Speaker of the House of
14 Representatives shall serve a four-year term.
15 D. Each member shall hold office until the successor of the
16 member is appointed and has qualified. The term of office for each
17 successor shall be for five (5) years. A member of the Commission
18 may be removed by the appropriate appointing authority for cause. A
19 member appointed to fill a vacancy occurring before the expiration
20 of a term of a member separated from the Commission for any cause
21 shall be appointed for the remainder of the term of the member whose
22 office has been so vacated.
23 E. Members of the Commission shall receive no salary, but shall
24 be entitled to travel reimbursement as provided by the State Travel

4 F. The Commission shall submit a report not later than November
5 1, 2026, and every five (5) years thereafter. The report shall
6 detail economic development trends and the long-term infrastructure
7 needs for this state, separated by Congressional boundaries,
8 including, but not limited to, a prospective look at future demands
9 on roads, bridges, highways, utilities, and water quality and
10 quantity.
11 G. The report shall be published on a publicly available
12 website and transmitted electronically to the Governor, the
13 President Pro Tempore of the Senate, and the Speaker of the House of
14 Representatives.
15 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

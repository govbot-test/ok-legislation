3 The Legislature finds that:

13 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-117.8 of Title 70, unless
15 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 A. This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Local
17 Teacher Licensure Act”. The purpose of the Local Teacher Licensure
18 Act shall be to grant school district boards of education the
19 authority to evaluate and recommend teacher licensure or
20 certification applications. The State Board of Education shall
21 maintain final oversight and ratification authority over the
22 issuance of teacher licenses or certificates in compliance with
23 Section 5 of Article XIII of the Oklahoma Constitution and Section
24 3-104 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes.

1 B. School district boards of education are hereby authorized
2 to evaluate and recommend approval of teacher licensure or
3 certification applications to the State Board of Education based on
4 the standards set forth in Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
5 C. Upon receiving a recommendation for licensure or
6 certification from a school district board of education, the State
7 Board of Education shall:
8 1. Review the recommendation for completeness and compliance
9 with statutory requirements including, but not limited to, Sections
10 5-142, 6-107, 6-122.3, 6-187, and 6-190 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma
11 Statutes. A recommendation shall be considered complete when the
12 Board has received all documentation showing compliance with the
13 statutory provisions in place for teacher licensure or
14 certification when the recommendation was submitted;
15 2. Approve or reject the licensure or certification
16 recommendations within thirty (30) calendar days of receipt; and
17 3. Provide written notification of approval or rejection to
18 the recommending school district board of education and the
19 licensure or certification applicant.
20 D. Failure of the State Board of Education to act on a
21 recommendation within thirty (30) calendar days pursuant to
22 subsection C of this section shall result in the Board’s automatic
23 approval of the license or certificate; provided, however, that the
24 recommendation submitted by the school district board of education

1 meets all statutory and procedural requirements for teacher
2 licensure or certification set forth in Title 70 of the Oklahoma
3 Statutes and the rules promulgated by the Board.
4 E. Teacher licenses or certificates issued pursuant to the
5 provisions of this section shall be valid for a term of five (5)
6 years from the date of issuance and shall be subject to renewal in

9 F. The State Board of Education shall retain oversight of all

13 G. School district boards of education shall establish
14 transparent procedures for licensure or certification
15 recommendations including:
16 1. Timelines for application submission, which shall provide

19 2. A timeline for board of education review and approval or
20 rejection, not to exceed thirty (30) calendar days from the date of
21 application submission; and
22 3. Written notification to the teacher license or certificate
23 applicant and the State Department of Education within five (5)
24 business days of the board of education’s decision.

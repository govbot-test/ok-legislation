7 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
8 An Act relating to career and technology education;
directing the Oklahoma Department of Career and
9 Technology Education to establish certain pilot
program beginning in certain school year; directing
10 the Department to designate two technology center
school districts to coordinate with public school
11 districts; directing certain technology center school
districts to designate employees to serve as
12 apprenticeship coordinators; providing duties of
coordinators; allowing certain students to
13 participate in apprenticeship opportunities and be
paid for work; directing certain participants to
14 indemnify and hold harmless certain participating
employers; providing exceptions; requiring execution
15 of certain agreement on a form prescribed by the
Department; requiring electronic submission of
16 certain report by certain date; providing for
contents of report; providing for codification;
17 providing an effective date; and declaring an
emergency.
18
19
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
22 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 14-127A of Title 70, unless
24 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
Req. No. 3673 Page 1
1 A. Beginning with the 2026-2027 school year, the Oklahoma
2 Department of Career and Technology Education shall establish a two-
3 year apprenticeship pilot program to encourage participation in
4 youth apprenticeship programs.
5 B. The Department shall designate two technology center school
6 districts to coordinate with public school districts in this state
7 to make apprenticeship opportunities available to students.
8 C. A technology center school district designated to
9 participate in the pilot program created pursuant to this section
10 shall designate a district employee to serve as an apprenticeship
11 coordinator. The duties of an apprenticeship coordinator shall
12 include, but not be limited to:
13 1. Building relationships with employers in the area served by
14 the technology center school district to maximize availability of
15 apprenticeship opportunities;
16 2. Facilitating high school student participation in
17 apprenticeship opportunities; and
18 3. Coordinating with public school districts participating in
19 the pilot program.
20 D. Students who are age sixteen (16) and older and enrolled in
21 a public school district participating in the pilot program may
22 participate in an apprenticeship opportunity and may be paid for
23 work conducted as part of an apprenticeship opportunity.
24
Req. No. 3673 Page 2
1 E. 1. Public school districts and students that participate in
2 an apprenticeship opportunity pursuant to this section shall agree
3 to indemnify and hold harmless employers offering the apprenticeship
4 opportunity from claims arising from student participation except in
5 cases of gross negligence, willful misconduct, or a clear violation
6 of law.
7 2. Public school districts and students who are age eighteen
8 (18) and older or the parents or legal guardians of students who are
9 under the age of eighteen (18) shall execute an apprenticeship
10 participation agreement that indemnifies participating employers
11 from claims arising from student participation in an approved
12 apprenticeship opportunity. The Oklahoma Department of Career and
13 Technology Education shall prescribe the apprenticeship
14 participation agreement form required by this subsection.
15 F. The Oklahoma Department of Career and Technology Education
16 shall electronically submit a report to the Governor, the President
17 Pro Tempore of the Senate, and the Speaker of the House of
18 Representatives by December 1, 2028, that includes an evaluation of
19 the pilot program by participating public school districts and
20 technology center school districts, data on whether the pilot
21 program had an impact on increasing student participation in
22 apprenticeship opportunities, and recommendations for continuation
23 of the program.
24 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
Req. No. 3673 Page 3
1 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
2 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
3 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
4 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

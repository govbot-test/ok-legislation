8 An Act relating to property; creating right to sell,
salvage, or remove manufactured home and its contents
9 for certain real property owners; requiring written
notice to be sent via certified mail; establishing
10 requirements of the contents of the written notice;
providing for codification; and providing an
11 effective date.

14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 690 of Title 60, unless there is
17 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 In the absence of any other agreement between the real property
19 owner and the owner of a manufactured home, any person, firm, or
20 corporation that allows an individual to use or occupy real property
21 in order to utilize a manufactured home shall have the right to
22 sell, salvage, or remove the manufactured home and its contents if
23 the owner of the manufactured home has failed to contact the real
24 property owner or to provide payment for the use of the real

3 1. Within thirty (30) days after the failure of the
4 manufactured homeowner to make payment or otherwise contact the real
5 property owner, send by certified mail, return receipt requested,
6 written notice to all recorded lienholders and to the last-known
7 address of the manufactured home owner stating the intent of the
8 real property owner to exercise the right to sell, salvage, or

11 include a description sufficient to identify the manufactured home
12 that is subject of the notice, including, if available, the

14 title number, lot number, or other identifying characteristics, and
15 the physical location of the manufactured home;
16 2. If, thirty (30) days after the mailing of the first notice,
17 payment or contact has not been made, send a second written notice
18 by certified mail, return receipt requested, to all recorded
19 lienholders and to the last-known address of the manufactured home
20 owner restating the intent to exercise such right and including the

22 section; and

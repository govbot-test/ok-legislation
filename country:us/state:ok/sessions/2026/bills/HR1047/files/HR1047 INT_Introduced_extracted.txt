RESOLUTION 1047 By: Sneed

7 AS INTRODUCED
8 A Resolution emphasizing the State's interest in
CompSource Mutual Insurance Company; recognizing the
9 existence of CompSource Mutual Insurance Company as
an entity ensuring compensation under current
10 workers' compensation law; emphasizing that all
monies and assets of CompSource Mutual Insurance
11 Company are held in trust; and emphasizing the
public's interest in CompSource Mutual Insurance
12 Company.
13
14
15 WHEREAS, CompSource Oklahoma was allowed by the Legislature to
16 become a mutual insurance company, CompSource Mutual Insurance
17 Company, effective January 1, 2015, for the purpose of insuring
18 employers against liability for compensation under the
19 Administrative Workers' Compensation Act, the Oklahoma Workers'
20 Compensation Code, and previous workers' compensation laws; and
21 WHEREAS, because of the State Insurance Fund, the predecessor of
22 CompSource Mutual Insurance Company, was created with taxpayer
23 funds, the State of Oklahoma continues to have an important and
24 vital interest in the operation of the company; and
Req. No. 17320 Page 1

1 WHEREAS, CompSource Mutual Insurance Company exists for the
2 purpose of assuring compensation for the persons entitled thereto
3 provided by the workers' compensation law, and for the further
4 purpose of insuring persons, firms, and corporations against loss,
5 expense, or liability by reason of bodily injury, death by accident,
6 occupational disability, or occupational disease suffered by
7 employees, for which the insured may be liable or have assumed
8 liability; and
9 WHEREAS, CompSource Mutual Insurance Company may further provide
10 insurance for employers against liability incurred as the result of
11 injuries sustained by employees engaged in employment subject to the
12 Longshore and Harbor Workers' Compensation Act, 33 U.S.C., Section
13 901 et seq., or employees engaged in employment subject to Title IV
14 of the Federal Coal Mine Health and Safety Act of 1969 as amended by
15 the Black Lung Benefits Act of 1972, as enacted or as may be amended
16 by the Congress of the United States; and
17 WHEREAS, the Supreme Court of Oklahoma has determined that the
18 creation of CompSource Mutual Insurance Company, as a successor to
19 the State Insurance Fund and CompSource Oklahoma, was a proper use
20 of legislative power under the Constitution of Oklahoma; and
21 WHEREAS, the Supreme Court found, and the Legislature affirms,
22 that all monies and other assets of CompSource Mutual Insurance
23 Company are held in trust for the benefit of the insured employers
24 or policyholders.

1 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
2 OF THE 2ND SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE:
3 THAT it is a matter of public interest to establish public
4 policy that Oklahoma maintains a statutory workers' compensation
5 system in which employers may obtain insurance to cover their
6 liability for benefits for workers injured in the course of their
7 employment as prescribed by the workers' compensation laws.
8 THAT the Insurance Commissioner take such actions as are
9 consistent with the provisions of this measure.
10 THAT the Oklahoma Legislature is committed to results which are
11 consistent with the provisions of this measure including, but not
12 limited to, statutory enactment or modification of administrative
13 rules.
14 THAT CompSource Mutual can only transition into a mutual
15 insurance holding company if it pays every policyholder their fair
16 and equitable ownership interest as owners and trustees of
17 CompSource Mutual.
18 THAT CompSource Mutual Insurance Company shall not be considered
19 a state agency, public body, department, or any other term used to
20 describe an entity which is a part of the Executive Branch of the
21 State of Oklahoma under any state statute or regulation, except as
22 otherwise provided for in this act.
23 THAT CompSource Mutual Insurance Company is hereby prohibited
24 from pursuing any means to change its corporate structure from the

10 An Act relating to The Governmental Tort Claims Act;
defining terms; limiting settlement awards for
11 wrongful termination claims; providing for inclusion
of certain damages; providing for exclusion of
12 certain contributions or wages; providing exceptions
to applicability; providing for codification; and
13 providing an effective date.

16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 163.1 of Title 51, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. As used in this section:
21 1. “Faculty member” means any regular faculty member as defined
22 by the applicable policy of the subject institution;
23 2. “Institution” means any institution within The Oklahoma
24 State System of Higher Education, the governing body of any

6 3. “Wrongful termination claim” means any claim, cause of
7 action, complaint, grievance, lawsuit, or demand for monetary or
8 equitable relief, arising under state law alleging wrongful

10 B. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, the
11 total amount paid by an institution to a faculty member to resolve a
12 wrongful termination claim under state law, whether by voluntary
13 settlement between the institution and the faculty member,
14 arbitration award, or court judgment, shall not exceed an amount
15 equal to two (2) years of the faculty member’s base salary,
16 compensation, or contractual benefit, as of the date of termination.
17 Such limitation shall include, but not be limited to, back pay,
18 compensatory damages, liquidated damages, and any other monetary
19 relief, but shall not include retirement contributions already
20 earned by the faculty member or accrued unpaid wages or leave
21 accrued by the faculty member.
22 C. The provisions of this section shall not apply to any

24 date of this act, related to the institution and executed between

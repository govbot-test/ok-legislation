8 An Act relating to alcoholic beverages; requiring the
Alcoholic Beverage Laws Enforcement (ABLE) Commission
9 to issue multiple licenses for manufacturing
beverages on the same premises to certain license
10 holders; providing requirements for acquiring
multiple licenses; requiring a license holder
11 operating under an alternating proprietorship or
premises arrangement to abide by the Oklahoma
12 Alcoholic Beverage Control Act; directing the ABLE
Commission to promulgate rules to authorize
13 alternating proprietorship or premises; providing for
codification; and providing an effective date.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-165 of Title 37A, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. The Alcoholic Beverage Laws Enforcement (ABLE) Commission
21 shall issue multiple licenses for manufacturing beverages on the
22 same premises if the applicant is in good standing with the ABLE
23 Commission and the Oklahoma Tax Commission pursuant to the Oklahoma
24 Alcoholic Beverage Control Act including, but not limited to, small
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3501 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 brew er license, charitable collaboration brewer license, distiller
2 license, winemaker license, rectifier license, and small farm winery
3 license and manufacturer's license. Nothing in this section shall
4 be construed to apply to the issuance of distribution or wholesale
5 licensee.
6 B. Applicants for an additional license must obtain all
7 necessary federal permits and approvals from the Alcohol and Tobacco
8 Tax and Trade Bureau (TTB) for an alternating proprietorship or
9 premises arrangement and the applicant must be in good standing with
10 the TTB. An applicant shall notify the ABLE Commission of its
11 intention to operate an alternating proprietorship or premises when
12 applying for the second and all subsequent licenses for a single
13 premises.
14 C. Any license holder operating under an alternating
15 proprietorship or premises arrangement shall abide by the portions
16 of the Oklahoma Alcoholic Beverage Control Act that are relevant to
17 the license under which they are producing the corresponding
18 beverage governed by the license.
19 D. The ABLE Commission shall promulgate rules, pursuant to the
20 Administrative Procedures Act, to authorize alternating
21 proprietorship or premises arrangements.
22 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

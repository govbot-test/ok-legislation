13 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 68 O.S. 2021, Section 3903, is
14 amended to read as follows:
15 Section 3903. As used in the Small Employer Quality Jobs
16 Incentive Act:
17 1. "Basic industry" means a basic industry as defined under the
18 Oklahoma Quality Jobs Program Act in divisions (1) through (9) of
19 subparagraph a of paragraph 1 of subsection A of Section 3603 of
20 this title, excluding those activities described in division (10) of
21 subparagraph a of paragraph 1 of subsection A of Section 3603 of
22 this title. Provided, for the purposes of the Small Employer
23 Quality Jobs Incentive Act, the determination required by
24 subdivision (b) of division (7) or division (8) of subparagraph a of
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4191 Page 1
1 para graph 1 of subsection A of Section 3603 of this title shall be
2 made by the Oklahoma Department of Commerce and not the Incentive
3 Approval Committee. Basic industry also includes those motion
4 picture and video industries defined or classified in the NAICS
5 Manual under U.S. Industry Group No. 5121, and those sound recording
6 industries defined or classified in the NAICS Manual under U.S.
7 Industry Group No. 5122, and those child daycare industries defined
8 or classified in the NAICS Manual under U.S. Industry Group No.
9 624410;
10 2. "Establishment" means any business, no matter what legal
11 form, including, but not limited to, a sole proprietorship,
12 partnership, corporation, or limited liability corporation;
13 3. "Estimated direct state benefits" means the tax revenues
14 projected by the Oklahoma Department of Commerce to accrue to the
15 state as a result of new direct jobs;
16 4. "Estimated direct state costs" means the costs projected by
17 the Department to accrue to the state as a result of new direct
18 jobs. Such costs shall include, but not be limited to:
19 a. the costs of education of new state resident children,
20 b. the costs of public health, public safety and
21 transportation services to be provided to new state
22 residents,
23 c. the costs of other state services to be provided to
24 new state residents, and
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4191 Page 2
1 d. the costs of other state services;
2 5. "Estimated net direct state benefits" means the estimated
3 direct state benefits less the estimated direct state costs;
4 6. "Full-time employment" means employment of persons residing
5 in this state and working for thirty (30) hours per week or more in
6 this state, which has a minimum six-month duration during any
7 twelve-month period;
8 7. "Gross taxable payroll" means wages, as defined in Section
9 2385.1 of this title, for new direct jobs;
10 8. "Net benefit rate" means the estimated net direct state
11 benefits computed as a percentage of gross payroll; provided:
12 a. the net benefit rate may be variable and shall not
13 exceed five percent (5%), and
14 b. in no event shall incentive payments, cumulatively,
15 exceed the estimated net direct state benefits; and
16 9. "New direct job" means full-time employment which did not
17 exist in this state prior to the date of approval, by the Oklahoma
18 Department of Commerce, of an application made pursuant to the Small
19 Employer Quality Jobs Incentive Act. A job shall be deemed to exist
20 in this state prior to approval of an application if the activities
21 and functions for which the particular job exists have been ongoing
22 at any time within six (6) months prior to such approval; and
23 10. "Qualifying capital investment" means new construction of
24 real property, new renovation of existing real property, or purchase
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4191 Page 3
1 of n ew machinery used in the manufacturing process that occurs after
2 the date of application. It does not include purchase of existing
3 real property.
4 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 68 O.S. 2021, Section 3904, is
5 amended to read as follows:
6 Section 3904. A. An establishment which meets the
7 qualifications specified in the Small Employer Quality Jobs
8 Incentive Act may receive quarterly incentive payments for a seven-
9 year period from the Oklahoma Tax Commission pursuant to the
10 provisions of the Small Employer Quality Jobs Incentive Act in an
11 amount equal to the net benefit rate multiplied by the actual gross
12 taxable payroll of new direct jobs as verified by the Tax
13 Commission.
14 B. In order to receive incentive payments, an establishment
15 shall apply to the Oklahoma Department of Commerce. The application
16 shall be on a form prescribed by the Department and shall contain
17 such information as may be required by the Department to determine
18 if the applicant is qualified. The establishment may apply for an
19 effective date for a project, which shall not be more than twelve
20 (12) months from the date the application is submitted to the
21 Department.
22 C. Before approving an application for incentive payments, the
23 Department must first determine that the applicant meets the
24 following requirements:
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4191 Page 4
1 1. Be engaged in a basic industry;
2 2. Has no more than five hundred full-time employees in this
3 state on the date of application nor an average of more than five
4 hundred full-time employees in this state during the four calendar
5 quarters immediately preceding the date of application;
6 3. Has a projected minimum employment, as determined by the
7 Department, of new direct jobs within twelve (12) months of the date
8 of application, or after July 1, 2011, within twenty-four (24)
9 months of the date of application, as follows:
10 a. if the establishment is located in a municipality with
11 a population less than three thousand five hundred
12 (3,500) fifty thousand (50,000) persons, as determined
13 by the Department of Commerce based on the most recent
14 U.S. Department of Commerce data, or if the
15 establishment is located in an unincorporated area and
16 the largest closest municipality within twenty (20)
17 miles of the establishment is such a municipality, new
18 direct jobs equal to the greater of five (5) jobs or
19 five percent (5%) of the company's full-time
20 employment at the date of application, and
21 b. if the establishment is located in a municipality with
22 a population of three thousand five hundred (3,500)
23 persons or more but less than seven thousand (7,000)
24 persons, as determined by the Department of Commerce
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4191 Page 5
1 based on the most recent U.S. Department of Commerce
2 data, or if the establishment is located in an
3 unincorporated area and the largest municipality
4 within twenty (20) miles of the establishment is such
5 a municipality, new direct jobs equal to the greater
6 of ten (10) jobs or seven and one-half percent (7.5%)
7 of the company's full-time employment at the date of
8 the application, and
9 c. if the establishment is located in a municipality with
10 a population of seven thousand (7,000) fifty thousand
11 (50,000) persons or more, as determined by the
12 Department of Commerce based on the most recent U.S.
13 Department of Commerce data, or if the establishment
14 is located in an unincorporated area and the largest
15 closest municipality within twenty (20) miles of the
16 establishment is such a municipality, new direct jobs
17 equal to the greater of fifteen (15) ten (10) jobs or
18 ten percent (10%) of the company's full-time
19 employment at the date of application.
20 Provided, for an establishment engaged in software publishing as
21 defined or classified in the NAICS Manual under Industry Group No.
22 5112, data processing, hosting and related services as defined or
23 classified in the NAICS Manual under Industry Group No. 5182,
24 computer systems design and related services as defined or
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4191 Page 6
1 clas sified in the NAICS Manual under Industry Group No. 5415,
2 scientific research and development services as defined or
3 classified in the NAICS Manual under Industry Group No. 5417,
4 medical and diagnostic laboratories as defined or classified in the
5 NAICS Manual under Industry Group No. 6215 or testing laboratories
6 as defined or classified in the NAICS Manual under U.S. Industry No.
7 541380, the projected minimum employment requirements of this
8 paragraph must be achieved within thirty-six (36) months of the date
9 of application;
10 4. Has or will have within twelve (12) months of the date of
11 application, or after July 1, 2011, within twenty-four (24) months
12 of the date of application, as determined by the Department,
13 qualifying capital investment greater than Five Hundred Thousand
14 Dollars ($500,000.00) or sales of at least thirty-five percent (35%)
15 for the first two (2) years and subsequently sixty percent (60%) of
16 its total sales to out-of-state customers or buyers, to in-state
17 customers or buyers if the product or service is resold by the
18 purchaser to an out-of-state customer or buyer for ultimate use, or
19 to the federal government, of its total sales to out-of-state
20 customers or buyers, to in-state customers or buyers if the product
21 or service is resold by the purchaser to an out-of-state customer or
22 buyer for ultimate use, or to the federal government except that:

24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4191 Page 7
1 a. those establishments in the NAICS Manual under the
2 U.S. Industry No. 541710 or 541380 are excused from
3 the out-of-state sales requirement, and
4 b. warehouses that serve as distribution centers for
5 retail or wholesale businesses shall be required to
6 distribute forty percent (40%) of inventory to out-of-
7 state locations, and
8 c. adjustment and collection services activities defined
9 or classified in the NAICS Manual under U.S. Industry
10 No. 561440 shall be required to have seventy-five
11 percent (75%) of loans to be serviced made by out-of-
12 state debtors;
13 5. Will pay the individuals it employs in new direct jobs an
14 average annualized wage which equals or exceeds:
15 a. one hundred twenty-five percent (125%) of the average
16 county wage of small employers located in that county
17 as that percentage is determined by the Department of
18 Commerce based on the most recent wage and employment
19 data from the Oklahoma Employment Security Commission
20 for the county in which the new direct jobs are
21 located. For purposes of this subparagraph, health
22 care premiums paid by the applicant for individuals in
23 new direct jobs shall be included in the annualized
24 wage, or
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4191 Page 8
1 b. one hundred ten percent (110%) of the average county
2 wage of small employers located in that county as that
3 percentage is determined by the Department of Commerce
4 based upon the most recent wage and employment data
5 from the Oklahoma Employment Security Commission for
6 the county in which the new direct jobs are located.
7 For purposes of this subparagraph, health care
8 premiums paid by the applicant for individuals in new
9 direct jobs shall not be included in the annualized
10 wage, or
11 c. one hundred percent (100%) of the average county wage,
12 excluding health care premiums paid by the applicant
13 for individuals in new direct jobs if the county in
14 which the new jobs are located has:
15 (1) according to the most recent annual determination
16 by the Oklahoma Employment Security Commission, a
17 county unemployment rate more than ten percent
18 (10%) higher than the state unemployment rate,
19 and
20 (2) according to the most recent United States Census
21 Bureau Data, a county personal poverty rate above
22 fifteen percent (15%);
23 6. Has a basic health benefit plan which, as determined by the
24 Department, meets the elements established under divisions (1)
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4191 Page 9
1 thro ugh (7) of subparagraph b of paragraph 1 of subsection A of
2 Section 3603 of this title and which will be offered to individuals
3 within twelve (12) months of employment in a new direct job; and
4 7. Has not received incentive payments under the Oklahoma
5 Quality Jobs Program Act, the Saving Quality Jobs Act, or the Former
6 Military Facility Development Act; and
7 8. Is not qualified for approval of an application for
8 incentive payments under the Oklahoma Quality Jobs Program Act, the
9 Saving Quality Jobs Act, or the Former Military Facility Development
10 Act.
11 D. The Oklahoma Department of Commerce shall determine if an
12 applicant is qualified to receive the incentive payment. Upon
13 qualifying the applicant, the Department shall notify the Tax
14 Commission and shall provide it with a copy of the application, and
15 approval which shall provide the number of persons employed by the
16 applicant upon the date of approval and the maximum total incentives
17 which may be paid to the applicant during the seven-year period.
18 The Tax Commission may require the qualified establishment to submit
19 additional information as may be necessary to administer the
20 provisions of the Small Employer Quality Jobs Incentive Act. The
21 approved establishment shall report to the Tax Commission quarterly
22 to show its continued eligibility for incentive payments, as
23 provided in Section 3905 of this title. Establishments may be
24 audited by the Tax Commission to verify such eligibility. Once the
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4191 Page 10
1 esta blishment is approved, an agreement shall be deemed to exist
2 between the establishment and the State of Oklahoma, requiring
3 incentive payments to be made for a seven-year period as long as the
4 establishment retains its eligibility and within the limitations of
5 the Small Employer Quality Jobs Incentive Act which existed at the
6 time of such approval. Any establishment which has been approved
7 for incentive payments prior to July 1, 2002, shall continue to
8 receive such payments pursuant to the laws as they existed prior to
9 July 1, 2002, for any period of time of the original five-year
10 period for such payments remaining after July 1, 2002.
11 E. For any contract executed by an establishment on or after
12 August 2, 2018, five percent (5%) of the quarterly incentive payment
13 amount shall be transferred by the Oklahoma Tax Commission to the
14 Oklahoma Quick Action Closing Fund.
15 F. Before any incentive payment is made for any contract
16 executed by an establishment on or after November 1, 2026, the
17 Oklahoma Department of Commerce shall determine if the qualifying
18 capital investment has occurred or the out-of-state sales threshold
19 has been achieved. The Oklahoma Department of Commerce shall notify
20 the Tax Commission upon approval by the Oklahoma Department of
21 Commerce. If this approval has not occurred within thirty-six (36)
22 months of the application date, the contract shall be cancelled by
23 the Oklahoma Department of Commerce and no incentive shall be paid.
24 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

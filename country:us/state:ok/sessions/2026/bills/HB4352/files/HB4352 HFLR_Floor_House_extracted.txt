4 HOUSE BILL 4352 By: Wilk, Moore, and Schreiber
5

7 AS INTRODUCED
8 An Act relating to mortgages; enacting the Oklahoma
Uniform Mortgage Modification Act; providing short
9 title; defining terms; providing scope of act;
stating the effect of mortgage modification;
10 directing courts how to construe act; providing
Relation to Electronic Signatures in Global and
11 National Commerce Act; providing when provisions of
this act are applicable to mortgage modifications;
12 providing for severability of provisions; providing
for codification; and providing an effective date.
13
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 401 of Title 46, unless there is
19 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 This act shall be known and may be cited as the Oklahoma Uniform
21 Mortgage Modification Act.

12 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 403 of Title 46, unless there is
14 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 A. Except as provided in subsection C of this act, this act
16 applies to a mortgage modification.
17 B. This act does not affect:
18 1. Law governing the required content of a mortgage;
19 2. A statute of limitations or other law governing the
20 expiration or termination of a right to enforce an obligation or a
21 mortgage;
22 3. A recording statute;
23 4. A statute governing the priority of a tax lien or other
24 governmental lien;

1 5. A statute of frauds; or
2 6. Except as provided in Section 4, subsection B, paragraph 8
3 of this act, law governing the priority of a future advance.
4 C. This act does not apply to any of the following
5 modifications:
6 1. A release of, or addition to, property encumbered by a
7 mortgage;
8 2. A release of, addition of, or other change in an obligor; or
9 3. An assignment or other transfer of a mortgage or an
10 obligation.
11 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
12 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 404 of Title 46, unless there is
13 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
14 A. For a mortgage modification described in subsection B of
15 this section:
16 1. The mortgage continues to secure the obligation as modified;
17 2. The priority of the mortgage is not affected by the
18 modification;
19 3. The mortgage retains its priority regardless of whether a
20 record of the mortgage modification is recorded in the county
21 records office in the county where the property is located; and
22 4. The modification is not a novation.
23 B. Subsection A of this act applies to one or more of the
24 following mortgage modifications:

6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to schools; authorizing the
Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse
8 Services to create an alternative education pilot
program for certain suspended students; stating
9 applicability; providing program requirements;
allowing students to continue the program after
10 suspension; allowing school district to create a plan
for student reintegration; directing allocation of
11 available funds; and providing an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1210.568a of Title 70, unless
17 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. The Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services
19 is hereby authorized to establish a pilot program to provide
20 alternative education to students who are suspended out-of-school
21 for more than fourteen (14) days. The Department shall design the
22 program in accordance with alternative education program
23 requirements listed in Section 1210.568 of this title.
24

1 B. The program created in subsection A of this section shall
2 incorporate therapy and other restorative practices into the
3 student's daily instruction.
4 C. Students participating in the program shall have the option
5 to continue the program after their out-of-school suspension
6 conditions have been met, or their suspension time has expired. In
7 addition to the education plan for reintegration to core units
8 required under subsection D of Section 24-101.3 of this title,
9 school districts may adopt a plan to help students reintegrate into
10 elective classes, extracurricular activities, and other school
11 programs when they have completed their out-of-school suspension.
12 D. Of the funding available for alternative education programs
13 each fiscal year, the State Department of Education shall designate
14 up to ten percent (10%) for the program established in subsection A
15 of this section based on student participation.
16 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

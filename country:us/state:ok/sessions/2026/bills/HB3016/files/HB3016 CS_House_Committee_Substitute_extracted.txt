8 An Act relating to schools; creating the Binocular
Vision Screening Program to identify certain eye
9 disorders of students in kindergarten, first, or
third grades; directing State Department of Education
10 to administer program in consultation with the State
Department of Health; defining term; requiring
11 schools to provide comprehensive binocular vision
screenings to students; requiring screenings to be
12 conducted by trained personnel; clarifying the
binocular screenings are in addition to others
13 required; creating the Binocular Screening Revolving
Fund; designating a department to administer fund;
14 specifying source of funds and expenditures from
fund; providing procedures for payment of
15 expenditures; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.

19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
20 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1210.286 of Title 70, unless
22 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 A. The State Department of Education, in consultation with the
24 State Department of Health, shall establish a two-year pilot program
Req. No. 17006 Page 1

1 know n as the Binocular Vision Screening Program, for schools to
2 offer vision screenings to students in kindergarten, first, and
3 third grades that are designed to identify convergence insufficiency
4 disorders. "Convergence insufficiency" means a vision disorder
5 characterized by a decreased ability to converge the eyes and
6 maintain binocular fusion while focusing on a near target.
7 B. Beginning with the 2026-2027 school year, the program shall
8 require schools to provide all students in kindergarten, first, and
9 third grades with a comprehensive binocular vision screening within
10 thirty (30) days of the start of the school year.
11 C. The screening required in subsection B of this section shall
12 be performed by school nurses, vision therapists, or optometric
13 technicians who are specifically trained to administer and interpret
14 results of a comprehensive binocular screening.
15 D. The screenings required by this section shall be in addition
16 to the screenings required in Section 5-148.2 of Title 70 of the
17 Oklahoma Statutes.
18 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1210.287 of Title 70, unless
20 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving
22 fund for the State Department of Education to be designated the
23 "Binocular Screening Revolving Fund". The fund shall be a
24 continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year limitations, and shall

1 cons ist of all monies received by the State Department of Education
2 from monies appropriated or apportioned by the Legislature. All

5 Education for the purpose of establishing and maintaining the
6 program created in Section 1 of this act. Monies in the Binocular
7 Screening Revolving Fund shall supplement and not supplant existing
8 school vision screening funding.
9 B. Expenditures from said fund shall be made upon warrants
10 issued by the State Treasurer against claims filed as prescribed by
11 law with the Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise
12 Services for approval and payment.
13 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

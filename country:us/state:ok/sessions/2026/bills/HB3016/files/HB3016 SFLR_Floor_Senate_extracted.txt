15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1210.286 of Title 70, unless
18 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 A. The State Department of Education, in consultation with the
20 State Department of Health, shall establish a two-year pilot program
21 known as the Binocular Vision Screening Program for schools to offer
22 vision screenings to students in kindergarten and first and third
23 grades that are designed to identify convergence insufficiency.
24 “Convergence insufficiency” means a vision disorder characterized by
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3016 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 a de creased ability to converge the eyes and maintain binocular
2 fusion while focusing on a near target.
3 B. Beginning with the 2026-2027 school year, the program shall
4 require schools to provide all students in kindergarten and first
5 and third grades with a comprehensive binocular vision screening
6 within ninety (90) days of the start of the school year.
7 C. The screening required by subsection B of this section shall
8 be performed by personnel listed on the statewide registry
9 maintained by the State Department of Health pursuant to Section
10 1210.284 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
11 D. The screenings required by this section shall be in addition
12 to the screenings required in Section 1210.284 of Title 70 of the
13 Oklahoma Statutes.
14 E. The State Department of Education shall electronically
15 submit a report on the Binocular Vision Screening Program including
16 any legislative recommendations to the Governor, the President Pro
17 Tempore of the Senate, and the Speaker of the House of
18 Representatives by November 1, 2028. The Department shall post the
19 report on its website.
20 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1210.287 of Title 70, unless
22 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 A. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving
24 fund for the State Department of Education to be designated the
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3016 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 “Bin ocular Screening Revolving Fund”. The fund shall be a
2 continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year limitations, and shall
3 consist of all monies received by the State Department of Education
4 from monies appropriated or apportioned by the Legislature for the
5 purpose of implementing the provisions of Section 1 of this act.
6 All monies accruing to the credit of the fund are hereby
7 appropriated and may be budgeted and expended by the State
8 Department of Education for the purpose of establishing and
9 maintaining the Binocular Vision Screening Program created in
10 Section 1 of this act. Monies in the Binocular Screening Revolving
11 Fund shall supplement and not supplant existing school vision
12 screening funding.
13 B. Expenditures from the fund shall be made upon warrants
14 issued by the State Treasurer against claims filed as prescribed by
15 law with the Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise
16 Services for approval and payment.
17 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
18 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
19 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
20 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
21 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
22 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON EDUCATION

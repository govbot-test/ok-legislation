1 BE I T ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
2 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 1, Chapter 106, O.S.L.
3 2024, as amended by Section 1, Chapter 342, O.S.L. 2025 (74 O.S.
4 Supp. 2025, Section 325.20), is amended to read as follows:
5 Section 325.20. A. All mobile food preparation vehicles
6 operating within this state prior to July 1, 2024, that are capable
7 of producing smoke or grease-laden vapors shall be in compliance
8 with the most current adopted fire code. With the exception In lieu
9 of an automatic fire extinguishing system, such requirement an
10 equivalent alternative shall be permitted, whereby such system shall
11 be optional if the mobile food preparation vehicle is equipped with
12 at least two portable fire extinguishers meeting National Fire
13 Protection Association (NFPA) 10: Standard for Portable Fire
14 Extinguishers and all employees receive documented annual fire
15 safety training approved by the State Fire Marshal. Such
16 requirements shall apply to vehicles including, but not limited to,
17 vehicles utilizing electric energy, liquefied petroleum gas,
18 compressed natural gas, or a combination thereof for cooking,
19 provided that portable fire extinguishers are installed and
20 maintained in accordance with the most current version of NFPA 10:
21 Standard for Portable Fire Extinguishers. A six-liter Class K-
22 rated, portable fire extinguisher is required for all cooking
23 equipment involving solid fuels, vegetable or animal oils and fats,

1 or a combination thereof. All portable fire extinguishers shall be
2 in clear view and immediately available for use.
3 B. All mobile food preparation vehicles utilizing, handling, or
4 transporting liquefied petroleum gas or any liquefied petroleum gas
5 system, equipment, container, apparatus, or appliance shall receive
6 and maintain a permit from, and be inspected annually by, the
7 Oklahoma Liquefied Petroleum Gas Administration or its employees, in
8 conjunction with the Oklahoma Liquefied Petroleum Gas Board. The
9 Board shall promulgate rules related to such permits, including
10 inspection requirements. The State Fire Marshal issuing shall issue
11 an annual operation permit decal to mobile food preparation
12 vehicles.
13 C. The provisions in subsection A of this section shall expire
14 on January 1, 2026, and all mobile food preparation vehicles shall
15 comply with the most current adopted fire codes set forth by this
16 state.
17 D. Mobile food preparation vehicles shall be under the
18 jurisdiction of the State Fire Marshal for fire and life safety code
19 compliance and shall be inspected annually by the State Fire Marshal
20 to ensure compliance with the latest version of the state-adopted
21 minimum codes for fire and life safety as adopted by the Oklahoma
22 Uniform Building Code Commission. The State Fire Marshal shall
23 coordinate, at minimum, annual inspections conducted:
24 1. Within each of the four quadrants of this state; and

1 2. Twice within each county with a population exceeding four
2 hundred thousand (400,000) according to the latest Federal Decennial
3 Census.
4 E. D. Upon approval, the State Fire Marshal shall issue an
5 operational permit decal indicating that the vehicle meets the
6 minimum fire and life safety code requirements adopted at the time
7 of inspection.
8 F. E. Nothing in this section shall prevent:
9 1. The State Fire Marshal from enforcing the minimum fire code
10 requirements as allowed by law and revoking the operations of a
11 mobile food preparation vehicle for cause when such vehicle is

14 2. A political subdivision from enforcing the event permits,

18 3. The State Department of Health from licensing and regulating

20 Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes and rules promulgated by the State
21 Commissioner of Health.

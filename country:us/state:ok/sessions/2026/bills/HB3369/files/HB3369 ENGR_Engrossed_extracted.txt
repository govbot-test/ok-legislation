7 An Act relating to food truck fire suppression;
amending Section 1, Chapter 106, O.S.L. 2024, as
8 amended by Section 1, Chapter 342, O.S.L. 2025 (74
O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 325.20), which relates to
9 mobile food establishments, fire and life safety code
compliance, permit, inspection, and rules; modifying
10 mandate for automatic fire extinguishing system in
food trucks; removing expiration date; and providing
11 an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 1, Chapter 106, O.S.L.
16 2024, as amended by Section 1, Chapter 342, O.S.L. 2025 (74 O.S.
17 Supp. 2025, Section 325.20), is amended to read as follows:
18 Section 325.20. A. All mobile food preparation vehicles
19 operating within this state prior to July 1, 2024, that are capable
20 of producing smoke or grease-laden vapors shall be in compliance
21 with the most current adopted fire code. With the exception In lieu
22 of an automatic fire extinguishing system, such requirement an
23 equivalent alternative shall be permitted, whereby such system shall
24 be optional if the mobile food preparation vehicle is equipped with
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3369 Page 1
1 at l east two portable fire extinguishers meeting National Fire
2 Protection Association (NFPA) 10 standards and all employees receive
3 documented annual fire safety training approved by the State Fire
4 Marshal. Such requirements shall apply to vehicles including, but
5 not limited to, vehicles utilizing electric energy, liquefied
6 petroleum gas, compressed natural gas, or a combination thereof for
7 cooking, provided that portable fire extinguishers are installed and
8 maintained in accordance with the most current version of NFPA 10:
9 Standard for Portable Fire Extinguishers. A six-liter Class K-
10 rated, portable fire extinguisher is required for all cooking
11 equipment involving solid fuels, vegetable or animal oils and fats,
12 or a combination thereof. All portable fire extinguishers shall be
13 in clear view and immediately available for use.
14 B. All mobile food preparation vehicles utilizing, handling, or
15 transporting liquefied petroleum gas or any liquefied petroleum gas
16 system, equipment, container, apparatus, or appliance shall receive
17 and maintain a permit from, and be inspected annually by, the
18 Oklahoma Liquefied Petroleum Gas Administration or its employees, in
19 conjunction with the State Fire Marshal issuing an annual operation
20 permit decal to mobile food preparation vehicles.
21 C. The provisions in subsection A of this section shall expire
22 on January 1, 2026, and all mobile food preparation vehicles shall
23 comply with the most current adopted fire codes set forth by this
24 state.
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3369 Page 2
1 D. Mobile food preparation vehicles shall be under the
2 jurisdiction of the State Fire Marshal for fire and life safety code
3 compliance and shall be inspected annually by the State Fire Marshal
4 to ensure compliance with the latest version of the state-adopted
5 minimum codes for fire and life safety as adopted by the Oklahoma
6 Uniform Building Code Commission. The State Fire Marshal shall
7 coordinate, at minimum, annual inspections conducted:
8 1. Within each of the four quadrants of this state; and
9 2. Twice within each county with a population exceeding four
10 hundred thousand (400,000) according to the latest Federal Decennial
11 Census.
12 E. D. Upon approval, the State Fire Marshal shall issue an
13 operational permit decal indicating that the vehicle meets the
14 minimum fire and life code requirements adopted at the time of
15 inspection.
16 F. E. Nothing in this section shall prevent:
17 1. The State Fire Marshal from enforcing the minimum fire code
18 requirements as allowed by law and revoking the operations of a
19 mobile food preparation vehicle for cause when such vehicle is
20 determined to be inherently dangerous to the health, safety, or
21 welfare of persons within this state;
22 2. A political subdivision from enforcing the event permits,
23 operational fees, land use regulations, zoning, or other ordinances
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3369 Page 3
1 in r elation to the operation of a mobile food preparation vehicle
2 within the boundaries of the political subdivision; or
3 3. The State Department of Health from licensing and regulating
4 mobile food preparation vehicles as provided by the provisions of
5 Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes and rules promulgated by the State
6 Commissioner of Health.
7 G. F. The State Fire Marshal may promulgate rules necessary to
8 implement the provisions of this section. Such rules shall have
9 uniform force and effect throughout this state and no political
10 subdivision or other agency shall enact or enforce any fire or life
11 safety code-related ordinances, rules, or other requirements for
12 mobile food preparation vehicles, except in a county with a
13 population exceeding four hundred thousand (400,000) according to
14 the latest Federal Decennial Census.
15 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
16 Passed the House of Representatives the 16th day of March, 2026.

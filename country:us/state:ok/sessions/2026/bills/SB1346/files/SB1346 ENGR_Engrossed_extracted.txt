11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
12 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1085.66 of Title 82, unless
14 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 A. 1. The Oklahoma Water Resources Board shall establish and
16 administer a Water and Wastewater Infrastructure Investment Program.
17 The program shall include development of competitive loans for
18 eligible entities seeking to develop and implement water or
19 wastewater improvement projects. The Board shall promulgate rules
20 to effectuate the provisions of this act and loan criteria for the
21 program.
22 2. Loan applications shall be ranked on criteria including, but
23 not limited to, the critical nature of the project, available
24 financing for the project, conservation and fiscal sustainability
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1346 Page 1
1 effo rts as demonstrated by participation in or completion of the
2 Long Range Sustainability Program administered by the Oklahoma Rural
3 Water Association, and pledge of matching funds, either through the
4 entity or a third-party source.
5 3. For the purposes of this section, “eligible entity” or
6 “eligible entities” means an eligible entity as defined pursuant to
7 Section 1085.32 of Title 82 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
8 B. All loans authorized pursuant to the provisions of this act
9 shall include a clawback provision in the funding agreement with an
10 eligible entity. For purposes of this subsection, a “clawback
11 provision” shall mean a condition precedent to participate in the
12 program whereby a loan recipient agrees in writing, signed by all
13 parties, to reimburse the program all or any part of the loan
14 disbursed to the recipient upon the failure of the recipient to
15 fulfill loan contract terms.
16 C. The Board shall create and publish an interactive map on the
17 Board’s website displaying critical infrastructure needs and
18 proposed projects to be completed pursuant to the provisions of this
19 act. The map shall also display the status of proposed and approved
20 projects, estimated completion dates, and any other information
21 deemed necessary by the Board.
22 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1085.67 of Title 82, unless
24 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1346 Page 2
1 A. 1. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a
2 revolving fund for the Oklahoma Water Resources Board to be
3 designated the “Water and Wastewater Infrastructure Investment
4 Revolving Fund”. The fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject
5 to fiscal year limitations, and shall consist of all monies
6 appropriated to the fund by law. All monies accruing to the credit
7 of the fund are hereby appropriated and may be budgeted and expended
8 by the Board for the purposes provided in this act. The Board may
9 enter into contracts with financial institutions and execute such
10 instruments as may be necessary to hold and disseminate loan funds
11 in accordance with applicable regulations and Board policies.
12 2. Notwithstanding any other provisions of law, income and
13 investment return on fund principal and interest income and
14 repayment of principal on loans made from the balance of the fund
15 shall accrue to the fund.
16 B. 1. The Board shall have full discretion of all monies
17 within the fund for enhancements, leveraging, and reserve capacities
18 for the Board loan and grant programs, system evaluation and risk
19 assessment assistance, and long-range infrastructure planning.
20 2. The Board may reserve up to two percent (2%) per year from
21 the fund for the purpose of administering the Water and Wastewater
22 Infrastructure Investment Program. Any remaining funds may be
23 allocated to satisfy the purposes of paragraph 1 of this subsection.
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1346 Page 3
1 C. The funds remaining following reserves and allocations made
2 pursuant to subsection B of this section shall be loaned to eligible
3 entities as prescribed by the program. Loan monies shall be
4 allocated based on the most current census data available from the
5 Federal Decennial Census or American Community Survey and shall be
6 as follows:
7 1. Twenty-five percent (25%) of the allocated funds shall be
8 for approved projects located within a municipality or county with a
9 population of more than four hundred thousand (400,000), or the
10 equivalent thereof for other eligible entities as prescribed by the
11 Board;
12 2. Twenty-five percent (25%) of the allocated funds shall be
13 for approved projects located within a municipality or county with a
14 population that is greater than thirty thousand (30,000) but less
15 than four hundred thousand (400,000), or the equivalent thereof for
16 other eligible entities as prescribed by the Board; and
17 3. Fifty percent (50%) of the allocated funds shall be for
18 approved projects located within a municipality or county with a
19 population of less than thirty thousand (30,000), or the equivalent
20 thereof for other eligible entities as prescribed by the Board.
21 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
22 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
23 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1346 Page 4
1 decl ared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
2 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
3 Passed the Senate the 10th day of March, 2026.

Section 2:
SECTION 7. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 906 of Title 17, unless there
is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
A. Any large load customer, or any developer, owner, or entity
acting on behalf of a large load customer, that plans to purchase
any land in this state outside of an industrial development
project, as such term is defined in Section 853 of Title 74 of the
Oklahoma Statutes, or a municipality shall be required to notify
the Corporation Commission, the county commissioners of the county
in which the land is to be purchased, and any property owners
within a five (5) mile radius of the proposed land purchase at
least sixty (60) days before the proposed land purchase transaction
is to take place. Such notice shall be given by publication in a
newspaper of general circulation, published in the county where the
land is to be purchased, and include information for a public
meeting. Such notice by publication shall be published in two
consecutive weekly issues of the newspaper, with the first
publication to be at least thirty (30) days prior to the date set
for the public meeting.
B. Any large load customer, or any developer, owner, or entity
acting on behalf of a large load customer, who fails to provide
proper notice as required by this section, shall be subject to an
administrative penalty not to exceed One Thousand Five Hundred
Dollars ($1,500.00) per day, per violation.

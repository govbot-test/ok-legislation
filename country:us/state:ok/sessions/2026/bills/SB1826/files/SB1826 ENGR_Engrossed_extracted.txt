6 An Act relating to development incentives; amending
62 O.S. 2021, Sections 842, as last amended by
7 Section 1, Chapter 203, O.S.L. 2025, and 843 (62 O.S.
Supp. 2025, Section 842), which relate to the
8 Oklahoma Local Development and Enterprise Zone
Incentive Leverage Act; eliminating sunset date
9 provisions; updating statutory reference; updating
statutory language; and providing an effective date.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 62 O.S. 2021, Section 842, as last
14 amended by Section 1, Chapter 203, O.S.L. 2025 (62 O.S. Supp. 2025,
15 Section 842), is amended to read as follows:
16 Section 842. A. An enterprise which locates its facility
17 within an enterprise zone or which expands its existing facility
18 after the designation of an enterprise zone as authorized by law and
19 which is located in an incentive district as authorized pursuant to
20 the provisions of the Local Development Act shall be eligible for
21 the state local enterprise matching payment authorized pursuant to
22 subsection A of Section 844 of this title.
23 B. 1. A local governmental entity which approves a project
24 plan pursuant to the provisions of the Local Development Act within
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1826 Page 1
1 an e nterprise zone or in support of a major tourism destination
2 project which the local governmental entity determines is likely to
3 significantly benefit contiguous or nearby enterprise zone census
4 tracts shall be eligible for the state local government matching
5 payment authorized pursuant to subsection D of Section 844 of this
6 title; provided, no state local government matching payment shall be
7 made for project costs in relation to:
8 a. any gambling establishment, or
9 b. any development within a project plan that provides
10 for more than fifty percent (50%) of the net leasable
11 space of such development to be used for retail
12 purposes except for such portions of a development
13 which includes grocery or specialty food store
14 enterprises defined under NAICS Manual Industry Group
15 No. 4451 or 4452 that provide healthy nutrition
16 options including fresh fruits, vegetables, whole
17 grains, seeds, nuts and healthy protein and that
18 improve access within one-half (1/2) mile of any low
19 income and low access geographies identified by the
20 United States Department of Agriculture.
21 State local government matching payments shall not be used to
22 supplant local revenue currently being expended within the increment
23 district boundaries.
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1826 Page 2
1 2. In order to be eligible for state local government matching
2 payments for approving a project within an enterprise zone, a local
3 governmental entity shall provide to the Oklahoma Department of
4 Commerce as part of the application provided for in subsection J of
5 this section:
6 a. an estimate of incremental revenues likely to be
7 derived from the project, and
8 b. certification that all projects described within the
9 related project plan will generate, in the aggregate,
10 a minimum of either One Million Dollars
11 ($1,000,000.00) in payroll, exclusive of payroll for
12 construction, or Five Million Dollars ($5,000,000.00)
13 in investment.
14 3. In order to be eligible for state local government matching
15 payments in support of a major tourism destination project, a local
16 governmental entity shall provide to the Oklahoma Department of
17 Commerce as part of the application provided for in subsection K J
18 of this section:
19 a. an estimate of incremental revenues new to the state
20 likely to be derived from the project,
21 b. certification that the major tourism destination
22 project meets the applicable criteria described in
23 paragraph 12 of Section 841 of this title, and
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1826 Page 3
1 c. an agreement to provide payment to the Oklahoma
2 Department of Commerce to defray the costs of the
3 study required by paragraph 4 of this subsection.
4 4. To determine if a project qualifies as a major tourism
5 destination project pursuant to subparagraph b of paragraph 12 of
6 Section 841 of this title and to assist in other required
7 determinations, the Oklahoma Department of Commerce shall cause a
8 market and feasibility study to be conducted by an independent
9 consultant with experience in the conduct of such studies. Upon
10 review of the feasibility report, the Oklahoma Department of
11 Commerce shall make its finding as to the reasonable probability
12 that the proposed project is a major tourism destination project as
13 provided in subparagraph b of paragraph 12 of Section 841 of this
14 title.
15 C. For purposes of the Oklahoma Local Development and
16 Enterprise Zone Incentive Leverage Act, an enterprise engaged in a
17 retail activity, where otherwise prohibited by the Oklahoma
18 Enterprise Zone Act for purposes of the benefits and incentives
19 extended pursuant to the Oklahoma Enterprise Zone Act, shall be
20 considered an eligible enterprise for purposes of the state local
21 enterprise matching payment authorized by the Oklahoma Local
22 Development and Enterprise Zone Incentive Leverage Act.
23
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1826 Page 4
1 D. The maximum amount of state local enterprise matching
2 payments for an enterprise per fiscal year shall not exceed Two
3 Hundred Thousand Dollars ($200,000.00).
4 E. Except as provided in subsection H of this section, for
5 purposes of the Oklahoma Local Development and Enterprise Zone
6 Incentive Leverage Act, the maximum amount of aggregate investment
7 in all qualifying facilities located in any single county which can
8 qualify for a state local enterprise matching payment pursuant to
9 subsection A of Section 844 of this title shall be computed for each
10 county of the state by multiplying Two Hundred Dollars ($200.00)
11 times the population of the county according to the most recent
12 estimate provided by the United States Bureau of the Census prior to
13 the date an application is made.
14 F. The computation required by subsection E of this section
15 shall be the maximum amount of aggregated investment qualifying for
16 the purposes of all enterprises for the duration of the Oklahoma
17 Local Development and Enterprise Zone Incentive Leverage Act.
18 G. The aggregate investment limit for all facilities located
19 within a county which may qualify for the state local enterprise
20 matching payments pursuant to subsection A of Section 844 of this
21 title shall:
22 1. Not be less than Twenty Million Dollars ($20,000,000.00) for
23 counties with a population less than one hundred thousand (100,000)
24 persons; and
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1826 Page 5
1 2. Not be greater than Forty Million Dollars ($40,000,000.00)
2 for all other counties of the state.
3 H. The aggregate limit for all state local government matching
4 payments made to any public entity on behalf of any local
5 governmental entity within a single county pursuant to subsection D
6 of Section 844 of this title for the duration of the Oklahoma Local
7 Development and Enterprise Zone Incentive Leverage Act shall be an
8 amount equal to the net benefit rate multiplied by the taxable gross
9 sales derived from the project over the period of apportionment of
10 local sales taxes, as certified by the Secretary of Commerce.
11 I. The payments authorized by Section 844 of this title shall
12 be available for business and governmental entities qualifying
13 pursuant to the Local Development Act for investments made within an
14 incentive district or for improvements made within an increment
15 district prior to December 31, 2007, or for which an incentive
16 district or an increment district has been created prior to December
17 31, 2028, if the investments or improvements are begun not later
18 than December 31, 2029.
19 J. An enterprise or entity receiving payments authorized
20 pursuant to the provisions of Section 844 of this title shall
21 annually report to the Oklahoma Department of Commerce the
22 following:
23 1. All employment resulting from the project or facility
24 location or expansion, including payroll amounts;
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1826 Page 6
1 2. Capital investment amounts resulting from the project or
2 facility location or expansion; and
3 3. Changes in the assessed value of property resulting from the
4 project or facility location or expansion.
5 The enterprise or entity shall also provide consent for the
6 Department to furnish the reported information to the Incentive
7 Evaluation Commission only for evaluation purposes by the Commission
8 or a designee. The information furnished to the Commission shall be
9 disaggregated and on a per project basis. The Department shall make
10 available on the Department website the information reported
11 pursuant to this subsection.
12 K. J. The Oklahoma Department of Commerce shall promulgate
13 rules for administration of the Oklahoma Local Development and
14 Enterprise Zone Incentive Leverage Act. Such rules shall:
15 1. Include a procedure for an enterprise or local governmental
16 entity to make application for state local enterprise and state
17 local government matching payments pursuant to this section;
18 2. Reflect the intent that the Oklahoma Local Development and
19 Enterprise Zone Incentive Leverage Act be fiscally neutral to the
20 state; and
21 3. Establish reporting requirements, including the requirements
22 provided in subsection J I of this section, for successful
23 applicants which allow data collection and analysis by the
24 Department on employment, capital investment, changes in assessed
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1826 Page 7
1 valu e of a project and other impacts resulting from payments and
2 reporting of such data by the Department to the Oklahoma Tax
3 Commission for the purposes of subsection B of Section 847 of this
4 title.
5 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 62 O.S. 2021, Section 843, is
6 amended to read as follows:
7 Section 843. A. In order to receive the state local enterprise
8 matching payment pursuant to the provisions of subsection A of
9 Section 844 of this title, the enterprise shall obtain a
10 certification, provided by the governing body of the local
11 governmental entity creating the incentive district, acknowledged by
12 the chief elected official of the local governing body that the
13 enterprise has qualified pursuant to the Local Development Act for
14 sales tax exemption. The certification document shall include:
15 1. The beginning date of the exemption;
16 2. The ending date of the exemption;
17 3. The total amount of projected investment to construct or
18 expand the facility during the period for which the incentives
19 available pursuant to the Local Development Act will be in force and
20 effect together with a certification by the Oklahoma Department of
21 Commerce that the facility is located in an enterprise zone; and
22 4. The legal name and business entity classification of the
23 entity to which exemption is afforded or to which sales tax payment
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1826 Page 8
1 is m ade by the local governmental entity or entities pursuant to the
2 provisions of the Local Development Act.
3 B. The local governing body shall provide a copy of the
4 certification document to the Oklahoma Tax Commission.
5 C. After the enterprise provides a certification from the local
6 governing body, the Tax Commission shall make payment to the
7 enterprise identified in the certification document equal to the
8 amount of the sales tax from which the enterprise is certified as
9 exempt as identified in the certification in the manner prescribed
10 by subsection A of Section 844 of this title.
11 D. The state local enterprise matching payment shall be made
12 only for sales tax foregone by local governmental entities or
13 rebated to the business enterprise by local entities for purchases
14 made by the business enterprise and not on the basis of any sales
15 tax collected by the business enterprise from consumers or users on
16 taxable sales made by the enterprise.
17 E. In order to receive the state local governmental matching
18 payment pursuant to the provisions of subsection D of Section 844 of
19 this title, the local governmental entity shall provide to the Tax
20 Commission a certification, acknowledged by its mayor or
21 chairperson, that such local governmental entity has created an
22 increment district pursuant to the Local Development Act which
23 qualifies for a state local government matching payment. The
24 certification document shall include:
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1826 Page 9
1 1. The beginning date of the increment district;
2 2. The ending date of the increment district;
3 3. A description of the project costs authorized by the project
4 plan for which the state local government matching payments will be
5 used and the estimated date for substantial completion of the
6 project being assisted as described in the application;
7 4. A certification by the Oklahoma Department of Commerce that
8 the project plan is located in an enterprise zone or supports a
9 qualifying major tourism destination project, and that the
10 qualifying investment and development has been or will be
11 substantially completed no later than December 31, 2034;
12 5. The amount of the local sales taxes which have been
13 apportioned during the previous six-month period by the local
14 governmental entity for the payment of project costs pursuant to the
15 provisions of the Local Development Act; and
16 6. The name of the public entity identified in the project plan
17 pursuant to Section 858 of this title as the entity authorized to
18 carry out activities pursuant to the project plan.
19 After the local governmental entity provides such certification,
20 the Tax Commission shall make payment to the designated public
21 entity in an amount equal to the lesser of the certified amount of
22 the local sales taxes apportioned during the previous six (6) months
23 or the estimated net direct state benefits as prescribed by
24 subsection D of Section 844 of this title.
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1826 Page 10
1 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

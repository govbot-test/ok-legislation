11 An Act relating to domestic abuse; creating the
Domestic Violence Offenders Registration Act;
12 defining terms; directing the Oklahoma State Bureau
of Investigation to create and maintain a domestic
13 violence offenders registry; allowing for public
access to registry; listing information for inclusion
14 on the registry; requiring registrants to pay
registration fee; establishing fee amount; allowing
15 district court clerks to retain portion of fee;
directing district court clerks to remit portion of
16 fee to the Bureau; providing for the removal of
registrants under certain circumstances; prohibiting
17 causes of action for damages; making registration
requirements applicable on certain date; directing
18 the Bureau to promulgate policies and procedures;
amending 74 O.S. 2021, Section 150.121B, which
19 relates to domestic abuse reports; clarifying
procedures and requirements for submitting reports of
20 domestic abuse; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.
21
22
23
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
HB1322 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 150.40 of Title 74, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Domestic
5 Violence Offenders Registration Act".
6 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 150.41 of Title 74, unless there
8 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 As used in this act:
10 1. "Convicted" and "conviction" means a final adjudication of
11 guilt, whether pursuant to a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or
12 otherwise, and any deferred judgment or suspended sentence; and
13 2. "Domestic violence" means the commission of any of the
14 offenses provided for in Section 644 and 644.1 of Title 21 of the
15 Oklahoma Statutes that meet the definition of domestic abuse, as
16 defined in Section 60.1 of Title 22 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
17 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 150.42 of Title 74, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. The Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation shall create a
21 registry of domestic violence offenders.
22 B. Beginning January 1, 2028, the Bureau shall maintain the
23 domestic violence offenders registry based upon information reported
24 to the Bureau in accordance with Section 150.2 of Title 74 of the
HB1322 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 Okla homa Statutes. The Bureau shall make the registry available for
2 public inquiry on the Internet. All information contained in the
3 registry, as provided in subsection C of this section, shall be
4 available to the public.
5 C. Upon notification that an individual has been convicted of a
6 domestic violence offense, as provided in Sections 644 and 644.1 of
7 Title 21 of the Oklahoma Statutes, the Bureau shall list the
8 individual on the domestic violence registry. The domestic violence
9 offenders registry shall contain the following information, as
10 applicable, for each domestic violence offender:
11 1. Name;
12 2. Date of birth;
13 3. Plea and conviction type;
14 4. Conviction offense;
15 5. Disposition or conviction date;
16 6. County or counties of conviction; and
17 7. The most recent booking photograph of the person available
18 in the Automated Fingerprint Certification System (AFIS).
19 D. In addition to any punishment imposed for a conviction of a
20 domestic violence offense, a defendant required to register under
21 the provisions of this act shall be assessed a registration fee in
22 the amount of One Hundred Fifty Dollars ($150.00), which shall be
23 paid to the clerk of the district court imposing the sentence, who
24 shall:
HB1322 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 1. Retain Fifty Dollars ($50.00) of the one-hundred-fifty-
2 dollar fee to cover administrative costs, which shall be deposited
3 in the Court Clerk's Revolving Fund; and
4 2. Remit One Hundred Dollars ($100.00) of the one-hundred-
5 fifty-dollar fee to the Bureau for administering the registry, which
6 shall be deposited in the OSBI Revolving Fund.
7 E. The Bureau shall remove from the domestic violence offenders
8 registry the name and other identifying information of a domestic
9 violence offender five (5) years after the completion of the last
10 sentence. Completion of the sentence shall be calculated by the
11 Bureau based on the sentence length and date of disposition or
12 conviction provided to the Bureau.
13 F. An individual does not have a cause of action for damages as
14 a result of being listed on the registry and an individual does not
15 have a cause of action for damages in the event a domestic violence
16 offender is not listed in the registry in accordance with the
17 provisions of this section.
18 G. The provisions of this act only apply to persons convicted
19 of a domestic violence offense on or after January 1, 2028.
20 H. The Bureau shall promulgate policies and procedures to
21 implement and administer the provisions of this act.
22 SECTION 4. AMENDATORY 74 O.S. 2021, Section 150.12B, is
23 amended to read as follows:
24
HB1322 HFLR Page 4
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 Section 150.12B. A. The Director of the Oklahoma State Bureau
2 of Investigation shall provide forms prescribe the format for the
3 reporting of domestic abuse to each person all state, county, city,
4 and town law enforcement agencies required to submit such reports
5 pursuant to the provisions of Section 3 of this act the Domestic
6 Abuse Reporting Act and shall establish guidelines for the
7 collection and reporting of domestic abuse incident information
8 pursuant to the provisions of the Domestic Abuse Reporting Act and
9 consistent with the provisions of Section 150.10 of this title.
10 B. The Director of the Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation
11 shall compile a monthly and annual statistical report which shall
12 include maintain data collected regarding the number of reported
13 incidents of domestic abuse for each law enforcement agency, county,
14 and for the state as a whole, the types of crime involved in the
15 domestic abuse, the days of the week the incidents occurred, and the
16 hours of the day the incidents occurred. The statistical reports
17 shall not include the names of any of the persons involved in an
18 incident of domestic abuse or any information which would serve to
19 identify such persons as individuals.
20 C. Copies of the monthly and annual statistical reports Data
21 shall be available to the public upon request. Data disseminated
22 shall not include the names of any victim or suspect involved in an
23 incident of domestic abuse or any information which would serve to
24 identify such individuals.
HB1322 HFLR Page 5
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 SECTION 5. This act shall become effective January 1, 2028.

7 An Act relating to domestic abuse; creating the
Domestic Violence Offenders Registration Act;
8 defining terms; directing the Oklahoma State Bureau
of Investigation to create and maintain a domestic
9 violence offenders registry; allowing for public
access to registry; listing information for inclusion
10 on the registry; requiring registrants to pay
registration fee; establishing fee amount; allowing
11 district court clerks to retain portion of fee;
directing district court clerks to remit portion of
12 fee to the Bureau; providing for the removal of
registrants under certain circumstances; prohibiting
13 causes of action for damages; making registration
requirements applicable on certain date; directing
14 the Bureau to promulgate policies and procedures;
amending 74 O.S. 2021, Section 150.12B, which relates
15 to domestic abuse reports; clarifying procedures and
requirements for submitting reports of domestic
16 abuse; providing restrictions on disseminated data;
providing for codification; and providing an
17 effective date.

21 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 150.40 of Title 74, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:

1 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Domestic
2 Violence Offenders Registration Act".
3 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 150.41 of Title 74, unless there
5 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 As used in this act:
7 1. "Convicted" and "conviction" mean a final adjudication of
8 guilt, whether pursuant to a plea of guilty or nolo contendere or
9 otherwise, and any deferred judgment or suspended sentence; and
10 2. "Domestic violence" means the commission of any of the
11 offenses provided for in Sections 644 and 644.1 of Title 21 of the
12 Oklahoma Statutes that meet the definition of domestic abuse, as
13 defined in Section 60.1 of Title 22 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
14 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 150.42 of Title 74, unless there
16 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. The Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation shall create a
18 registry of domestic violence offenders.
19 B. Beginning January 1, 2028, the Bureau shall maintain the
20 domestic violence offenders registry based upon information reported
21 to the Bureau in accordance with Section 150.12 of Title 74 of the
22 Oklahoma Statutes. The Bureau shall make the registry available for
23 public inquiry on the Internet. All information contained in the
24

1 regi stry, as provided in subsection C of this section, shall be
2 available to the public.
3 C. Upon notification that an individual has been convicted of a
4 domestic violence offense, as provided in Sections 644 and 644.1 of
5 Title 21 of the Oklahoma Statutes, the Bureau shall list the
6 individual on the domestic violence offenders registry. The
7 domestic violence offenders registry shall contain the following
8 information, as applicable, for each domestic violence offender:
9 1. Name;
10 2. Date of birth;
11 3. Plea and conviction type;
12 4. Conviction offense;
13 5. Disposition or conviction date;
14 6. County or counties of conviction; and
15 7. The most recent booking photograph of the person available
16 in the Automated Fingerprint Identification System (AFIS).
17 D. In addition to any punishment imposed for a conviction of a
18 domestic violence offense, a defendant required to register under
19 the provisions of this act shall be assessed a registration fee in
20 the amount of One Hundred Fifty Dollars ($150.00), which shall be
21 paid to the clerk of the district court imposing the sentence, who
22 shall:

1 1. Retain Fifty Dollars ($50.00) of the one-hundred-fifty-
2 dollar fee to cover administrative costs, which shall be deposited
3 in the Court Clerk's Revolving Fund; and
4 2. Remit One Hundred Dollars ($100.00) of the one-hundred-
5 fifty-dollar fee to the Bureau for administering the registry, which
6 shall be deposited in the OSBI Revolving Fund.
7 E. The Bureau shall remove from the domestic violence offenders
8 registry the name and other identifying information of a domestic
9 violence offender five (5) years after the completion of the last
10 sentence. Completion of the sentence shall be calculated by the
11 Bureau based on the sentence length and date of disposition or
12 conviction provided to the Bureau.
13 F. An individual does not have a cause of action for damages as
14 a result of being listed on the registry, and an individual does not
15 have a cause of action for damages in the event a domestic violence
16 offender is not listed in the registry in accordance with the
17 provisions of this act.
18 G. The provisions of this act only apply to persons convicted
19 of a domestic violence offense on or after January 1, 2028.
20 H. The Bureau shall promulgate policies and procedures to
21 implement and administer the provisions of this act.

19 termination shall not depend on certain physical

20 Section 6-209, as amended by Section 12, Chapter 11,

21 which relates to surrender and return of license;
requiring the surrender of physical driver license
22 and invalidation of electronic driver license;
modifying means of verifying identification;
23 providing for codification; and providing an

3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
4 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
5 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 110A of Title 47, unless there
6 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
7 "Credential" shall mean a driver license, permit, or
8 identification card issued by Service Oklahoma.
9 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
10 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 110B of Title 47, unless there
11 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
12 "Credential holder" shall mean an individual that has been
13 issued a physical or electronic credential.
14 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 114.1 of Title 47, unless there
16 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 "Electronic credential" shall mean an electronic extension of
18 the physical credential issued by Service Oklahoma that conveys
19 identity and driving privilege information.
20 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 114.2 of Title 47, unless there
22 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 "Electronic credential system" means a digital process that
24 includes a method for provisioning electronic credentials,
Req. No. 16299 Page 2
1 requ esting and transmitting Electronic Credential Data Elements, and
2 performing tasks to maintain the system.
3 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-144A of Title 47, unless there
5 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 "Physical credential" shall mean a document issued by Service
7 Oklahoma that conveys identity and driving privilege information.
8 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
9 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 153A of Title 47, unless there
10 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
11 "Relying party" means the entity to which the credential holder
12 is presenting the electronic credential.
13 SECTION 7. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-187 of Title 47, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 "Verification process" means a method of authenticating the
17 electronic credential through the use of secure encrypted
18 communication.
19 SECTION 8. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-131 of Title 47, unless there
21 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 A. Service Oklahoma may issue an electronic credential only to
23 individuals who are otherwise eligible to hold a physical
24 credential. The data used to build an electronic credential shall
Req. No. 16299 Page 3
1 matc h the current relevant Service Oklahoma records. Service
2 Oklahoma:
3 1. May contract with one or more entities to develop an
4 electronic credential system;
5 2. Shall set the validity period of electronic credentials; and
6 3. Shall promulgate rules to implement the provisions of this
7 section.
8 B. Relying Parties shall authenticate electronic credentials
9 prior to acceptance of the electronic credential. Relying parties
10 shall only request electronic credential data elements that are
11 necessary to complete the transaction for what data is being
12 requested. Electronic credential data is subject to all applicable
13 Oklahoma and federal data security and privacy protection laws and
14 regulations.
15 C. Relying parties shall only retain electronic credential data
16 elements for which the relying party explicitly obtained consent
17 from the electronic credential holder. Relying parties shall inform
18 the electronic credential holder of the use and retention period of
19 the electronic data elements.
20 SECTION 9. AMENDATORY 47 O.S. 2021, Section 1-114, as
21 amended by Section 24, Chapter 282, O.S.L. 2022 (47 O.S. Supp. 2025,
22 Section 1-114), is amended to read as follows:
23 Section 1-114. Driver and driver license.
24
Req. No. 16299 Page 4
1 A. "Driver" means any person who drives, operates or is in
2 actual physical control of a vehicle.
3 B. "Driver license" means a document issued by Service Oklahoma
4 or the driver licensing agency of another state or country which
5 grants to the person named thereon the privilege to drive, operate
6 or be in actual physical control of a motor vehicle. The term shall
7 include an intermediate Class D driver license, a learner permit and
8 commercial learner permit. The term shall also include both the
9 physical document or the digital version of the document approved by
10 Service Oklahoma.
11 SECTION 10. AMENDATORY 47 O.S. 2021, Section 1-155, as
12 amended by Section 26, Chapter 282, O.S.L. 2022 (47 O.S. Supp. 2025,
13 Section 1-155), is amended to read as follows:
14 Section 1-155. Revocation of driving privilege.
15 The termination by formal action of Service Oklahoma of a
16 person's privilege to operate a motor vehicle on the public
17 highways. Such action shall include the requirement of the
18 surrender to Service Oklahoma of the person's physical driver
19 license and the invalidation or electronic disablement of any
20 electronic driver license issued to the person. The effectiveness
21 of the termination shall not depend on physical possession of a
22 credential.
23
24
Req. No. 16299 Page 5
1 SECTION 11. AMENDATORY 47 O.S. 2021, Section 6-209, as
2 amended by Section 12, Chapter 11, O.S.L. 2024 (47 O.S. Supp. 2025,
3 Section 6-209), is amended to read as follows:
4 Section 6-209. A. Service Oklahoma upon canceling or denying
5 the driver license of a person or upon suspending or revoking the
6 driving privilege of a person shall require that the physical driver
7 license of the person be surrendered to Service Oklahoma and that
8 the electronic driver license issued to the person shall be
9 invalidated or disabled. When the statutory requirements for
10 reinstatement are met in accordance with Oklahoma Statutes, the
11 person shall apply for a new driver license from Service Oklahoma;
12 provided Service Oklahoma has determined that the licensee is a
13 person not prohibited from holding a driver license under Section 6-
14 103 of this title, and has successfully completed the customary
15 written, physical and driving tests, if such tests are required.
16 Identification of the person shall be verified through the driver
17 license digital file records of Service Oklahoma.
18 B. Service Oklahoma, upon entering an order canceling or
19 denying a driver license or suspending or revoking the driving
20 privilege of a person, shall forward a copy of the order to the
21 licensee pursuant to the provisions of Section 2-116 of this title
22 and request the immediate return of the driver license to Service
23 Oklahoma. Failure to comply with the order of Service Oklahoma
24 shall constitute a misdemeanor and, upon conviction, the person
Req. No. 16299 Page 6
1 shal l be punished by a fine of not less than Fifty Dollars ($50.00)
2 nor more than One Hundred Dollars ($100.00).
3 C. Any peace officer of this state may seize the license of any
4 person who, according to Service Oklahoma records, is under
5 suspension, cancellation, revocation or denial under the provisions
6 of this title. The officer shall immediately forward the license to
7 Service Oklahoma.
8 D. No person shall have a property interest in the physical
9 driver license issued pursuant to the laws of this state. It shall
10 be the duty of every person whose driving privilege has been
11 suspended, canceled or revoked to immediately surrender his or her
12 driver license upon the request of any peace officer or
13 representative of Service Oklahoma.
14 SECTION 12. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

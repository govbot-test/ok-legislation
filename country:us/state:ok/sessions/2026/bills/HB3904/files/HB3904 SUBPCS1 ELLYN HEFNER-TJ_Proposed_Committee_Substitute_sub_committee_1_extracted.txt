9 An Act relating to Medicaid; clarifying and
standardizing coverage and reimbursement for certain
10 maternal health services; providing for depression
screening for pregnant and postpartum women;
11 authorizing flexible payment structures for prenatal,
delivery, and postpartum care; directing coverage for
12 certain maternal health services delivered through
remote and home-based care models; providing for
13 codification; and providing an effective date.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 4013 of Title 56, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. The Oklahoma Medicaid Program shall reimburse for depression
21 screening during pregnancy and during the postpartum period, as
22 authorized by law.

1 B. The Oklahoma Health Care Authority shall apply for any
2 federal waiver, state plan amendment, or other authority necessary
3 to implement this section.
4 C. The Oklahoma Health Care Authority shall adopt rules to
5 implement this section.
6 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 4014 of Title 56, unless there
8 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 A. The Oklahoma Medicaid Program shall reimburse for prenatal,
10 delivery, and postpartum services separately in lieu of a global
11 payment or an all-inclusive payment methodology for maternity
12 services.
13 B. Prenatal, delivery, and postpartum services include, but are
14 not limited to:
15 1. Office visits;
16 2. Laboratory fees;
17 3. Physician-ordered testing;
18 4. Blood work;
19 5. Remote monitoring;
20 6. Fetal nonstress tests; and
21 7. Continuous glucose monitors or other services for
22 gestational diabetes, when medically necessary.

1 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 4015 of Title 56, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. 1. The Oklahoma Medicaid Program shall reimburse for
5 medically necessary remote ultrasound procedures utilizing
6 established Current Procedural Terminology codes for remote
7 ultrasound procedures when the patient is in a residence or other
8 off-site location from the health care provider and the same
9 standard of care is met.
10 2. Paragraph 1 of this subsection shall apply to the fee-for-
11 service categories of the program and any managed care plan within
12 the program.
13 B. A remote ultrasound procedure shall be reimbursable when the
14 health care provider uses digital technology that:
15 1. Collects medical and other forms of health data from a
16 patient and electronically transmits the information securely to a
17 health care provider in a different location for interpretation and
18 recommendation;
19 2. Is compliant with the Health Insurance Portability and
20 Accountability Act (HIPAA) of 1996, 42 U.S.C., Section 1320d et
21 seq., as it existed on January 1, 2026; and
22 3. Is approved by the United States Food and Drug
23 Administration.

2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 4016 of Title 56, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 The Oklahoma Medicaid Program shall reimburse for home
5 visitation services related to prenatal care and postpartum care
6 when such services are provided by doulas, community health workers,
7 or other qualified personnel, as determined by the Oklahoma Health
8 Care Authority.

7 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 36 O.S. 2021, Section 981, is
8 amended to read as follows:
9 Section 981. Short Title and Purposes of Act.
10 A. Sections 981 through 998 of this title and Sections 22, 23
11 and 24 of this act shall constitute a part of the Oklahoma Insurance
12 Code and shall be known and may be cited as the "Property and
13 Casualty Competitive Loss Cost Rating Act".
14 B. The purposes of the Property and Casualty Competitive Loss
15 Cost Rating Act are:
16 1. To promote price competition among insurers so as to provide
17 rates that are responsive to competitive market conditions;
18 2. To protect policyholders and the public against the adverse
19 effects of excessive, inadequate or unfairly discriminatory rates;
20 3. To prohibit unlawful price-fixing agreements and other
21 anticompetitive behavior by insurers;
22 4. To provide regulatory procedures for the maintenance of
23 appropriate data reporting systems;
24

1 5. To provide regulatory controls in the absence of a
2 competitive marketplace; and
3 6. To authorize essential cooperative action among insurers in
4 the ratemaking process and to regulate such activity to prevent
5 practices that substantially lessen competition or create a
6 monopoly.

23 amended to read as follows:
24 Section 985. Ratemaking Standards.
Req. No. 16470 Page 7
1 A. A rate may not be excessive, inadequate or unfairly
2 discriminatory.
3 1. No rate in a competitive market may be determined to be
4 excessive. A rate in a noncompetitive market may be determined to
5 be excessive if it is likely to produce a profit that is
6 unreasonably high for the insurance provided A rate may be
7 determined to be excessive if it is unreasonably high for the
8 insurance provided or is unreasonably high in relation to the
9 services rendered.
10 2. A rate may not be determined to be inadequate unless:
11 a. the rate is clearly insufficient to sustain projected
12 losses, expenses and special assessments, and
13 b. the rate is unreasonably low and use of the rate by
14 the insurer has tended or, if continued, will tend to
15 create a monopoly in the market.
16 3. Unfair discrimination may be determined to exist if, after
17 allowing for practical limitations, price differentials fail to
18 reflect equitably the differences in expected losses and expenses.
19 A rate may not be determined to be unfairly discriminatory because
20 different premiums result for policyholders with like loss exposures
21 but different expense levels, or like expenses but different loss
22 exposures, or if it averaged broadly among persons insured within a
23 group, franchise or blanket policy or a mass-marketed plan. No rate
24 in a competitive market shall be considered unfairly discriminatory

6 1. Past and prospective loss experience within and outside this
7 state, in accordance with accepted actuarial principles;
8 2. Conflagration and catastrophe hazards;
9 3. A reasonable margin for underwriting profit and
10 contingencies;
11 4. Loadings for leveling premium rates over time for dividends,
12 savings or unabsorbed premium deposits allowed or returned by
13 insurers to their policyholders, members or subscribers;
14 5. Past and prospective expenses both countrywide and those
15 specially applicable to this state; and
16 6. Provisions for special assessments; and to all other
17 relevant factors including judgment within and outside this state.
18 C. Risks may be grouped by classifications for the
19 establishment of rates and minimum premiums. Classification rates
20 may be modified to produce rates for individual risks in accordance
21 with rating plans which establish standards for measuring variations
22 in hazards or expense provisions, or both. Such standards may
23 measure any differences among risks that can be demonstrated to have
24 a probable effect upon losses or expenses. No risk classification
Req. No. 16470 Page 9
1 howe ver, may be based on race, creed, national origin, or the
2 religion of the insured.
3 D. The expense provisions included in the rates for use by an
4 insurer or group of insurers may differ from those of any other
5 insurer or group of insurers to reflect the requirements of the
6 operating methods of the insurer or group of insurers.
7 E. The rates may contain provision for contingencies and an
8 allowance permitting a reasonable profit. In determining the
9 reasonableness of the profit, consideration shall be given to the
10 investment income attributable to the line of insurance.
11 F. Risks may be classified in any way except that no risk may
12 be classified on the basis of race, color, creed, or national
13 origin, or religion.

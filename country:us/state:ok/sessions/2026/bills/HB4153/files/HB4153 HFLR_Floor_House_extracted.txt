school enrollment, completion, or lawful excuse for
22 minors to obtain a driver license; requiring minors
to demonstrate reading proficiency at an eighth-grade
23 reading level unless excused; establishing exception
for summer months; establishing documentation
24 requirements for students educated by other means;

12 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1210.515A of Title 70, unless
14 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 A. Pursuant to the provisions of paragraph 2 of subsection A of
16 Section 2 of this act, any person under the age of eighteen (18)
17 years wishing to apply for a driver license or permit shall
18 successfully demonstrate a satisfactory reading ability at the
19 eighth-grade reading level by meeting the following criteria:
20 1. A student enrolled in a public school shall successfully
21 complete the reading portion of the statewide assessment
22 administered pursuant to Section 1210.508 of this title and that is
23 offered in the eighth grade. Following the administration of this
24 assessment in the eighth grade, any student not successfully

4 reading proficiency test in order to satisfy the criteria for a
5 driver license or permit. Alternative reading proficiency tests
6 shall be approved by the State Department of Education. Subsequent
7 successful completion of an alternative reading proficiency test
8 shall serve to satisfy any retake requirement for the reading
9 portion of the state assessment in the eighth grade in the Oklahoma
10 School Testing Program. School districts shall notify, in writing,
11 each student who takes the reading portion of the state assessment
12 for the eighth grade or who takes an alternative reading proficiency

15 shall inform the student of the reading proficiency driver license
16 requirement and the school's remediation plan for the student. Upon
17 the student's successful completion of the test, the school shall
18 furnish the student with the documentation needed for the driver
19 license application in Oklahoma;
20 2. Unless alternatively documented according to the provisions
21 of subsection C of this section, students under the age of eighteen
22 (18) years shall successfully complete a reading proficiency test
23 approved by the State Department of Education; and

1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11-901f of Title 47, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. This act shall be known and may be cited as "Bridges' Law".
5 B. For purposes of this section "intentional, unsafe driving"
6 shall include committing two or more moving violations that are
7 likely to endanger other people or property, or committing one
8 intentional violation that requires a defensive reaction from
9 another driver.
10 C. Any person operating a motor vehicle who engages in
11 intentional, unsafe driving that poses a risk of harm to property or
12 another shall be guilty of a misdemeanor.
13 D. Any person operating a motor vehicle who engages in
14 intentional, unsafe driving that poses an immediate and significant
15 risk to another person that results in a vehicle collision, that
16 makes contact with another vehicle, sideswipes or rams another
17 vehicle, that forces another car off the road, or that operates his
18 or her motor vehicle in any manner that endangers others while
19 driving shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a Class C1 felony
20 offense, punishable by imprisonment as provided for in subsections B
21 through E of Section 20L of Title 21 of the Oklahoma Statutes, or by
22 a fine not exceeding One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00), or by both
23 such fine and imprisonment.

1 E. Any person who operates a motor vehicle in violation of
2 subsection D of this section who endangers a minor child, whether
3 the minor child is the passenger of the vehicle which that person is
4 operating or in another vehicle shall, upon conviction, be guilty of
5 a Class B6 felony offense, punishable by imprisonment as provided
6 for in subsection B of Section 20K of Title 21 of the Oklahoma
7 Statutes, or by a fine not exceeding Five Thousand Dollars
8 ($5,000.00), or by both such fine and imprisonment.
9 F. Any person who operates a motor vehicle in violation of
10 subsection D of this section who causes great bodily injuries as a

12 offense, punishable by imprisonment as provided for in subsection B
13 of Section 20I of Title 21 of the Oklahoma Statutes, or by a fine
14 not exceeding Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00), or by both such
15 fine and imprisonment.
16 G. Any person who operates a motor vehicle in violation of

20 provided for in subsection B of Section 20K of Title 21 of the
21 Oklahoma Statutes, or by a fine not exceeding Five Thousand Dollars

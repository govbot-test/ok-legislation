13 WHEREAS, The proposed Inola aluminum smelter would constitute
14 one of the largest industrial developments ever proposed in this
15 region and is expected to create significant long-term impacts on
16 Oklahoma and neighboring states; and
17 WHEREAS, This project raises substantial concerns regarding
18 potential emissions of fluorides, particulate matter, sulfur
19 dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and other smelting-related byproducts that
20 may affect public health, property use, agriculture, and quality of
21 life; and
22 WHEREAS, Local government has a duty to exercise caution before
23 supporting any project that may create long-term burdens on roads,

3 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES

5 THAT the Oklahoma House of Representatives formally declares its
6 opposition to advancement of the proposed Inola aluminum smelter
7 project unless and until all environmental, agricultural,
8 infrastructure, public-safety, and fiscal concerns are independently

11 THAT the Oklahoma House of Representatives further recommends

13 entities provide a complete public accounting of all incentives,
14 abatements, grants, TIF districts, state and federal subsidies, and

10 An Act relating to human trafficking; amending 22
O.S. 2021, Section 19c, which relates to criminal
11 arrest record expungements for victims of human
trafficking; deleting good cause standard;
12 authorizing expungements for criminal offenses
committed by human trafficking victims; clarifying
13 notice requirements; providing for the waiver of
filing fees and costs; requiring closed hearings;
14 directing courts to make certain considerations
regarding human trafficking victimization; providing
15 burden of proof standard; directing courts to grant
petitions if burden of proof is met; allowing victims
16 to seek relief at any time; and providing an
effective date.
17
18
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
21 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 22 O.S. 2021, Section 19c, is
22 amended to read as follows:
23 Section 19c. A. 1. The court, upon its own motion or upon
24 petition by the defendant and for good cause shown, may enter an
Req. No. 17068 Page 1
1 orde r for expungement of law enforcement and court records relating
2 to a charge or conviction for a prostitution-related any criminal
3 offense committed as a result of the defendant having been a victim
4 part of the victimization of human trafficking. The order shall
5 contain a statement that the expungement is ordered pursuant to this
6 section. An order entered
7 2. A motion or petition filed by the defendant pursuant to this
8 section shall be subject to the notice requirements and provisions
9 of subsections B through M of Section 19 of Title 22 of the Oklahoma
10 Statutes this title. Any filing fees or costs associated with the
11 motion or petition for expungement shall be waived by the court.
12 3. Upon the court setting a date for hearing the request for
13 expungement, the court shall require that said hearing be closed to
14 the public.
15 4. Records expunged pursuant to this section shall be sealed to
16 the public but not to law enforcement agencies for law enforcement
17 purposes.
18 B. To determine whether a person was a victim of human
19 trafficking, the court shall consider the following:
20 1. The definition of human trafficking, which shall have the
21 same meaning as such term is defined in Section 748 of Title 21 of
22 the Oklahoma Statutes or Sections 1589, 1590, 1591, or subsection A
23 of Section 1594 of Title 18 of the United States Code, depending on
24
Req. No. 17068 Page 2
1 whet her the record sought to be expunged is pursuant to state or
2 federal law; and
3 2. Official documentation or prior determination of
4 victimization. Provided, official documentation or prior
5 determination of victimization is not necessary to obtain an
6 expungement under the provisions of this section. However, the
7 existence of official documentation, prior determination of
8 victimization, or a showing by the defendant that there is a causal
9 link between the period the defendant was trafficked and the crime
10 committed, shall create a presumption that the charge or conviction
11 considered for expungement was a direct result of said
12 victimization. If official documentation or prior determination of
13 victimization does not exist, the defendant shall attach to the
14 petition for expungement an affidavit executed by a service provider
15 expert witness or law enforcement expert witness attesting to the
16 status of the defendant as a victim of human trafficking.
17 C. The burden of proof for determining whether the charge or
18 conviction of the defendant in the petition for expungement was
19 committed as a result of having been a part of the victimization of
20 human trafficking shall be upon the defendant to prove a
21 preponderance of the evidence.
22 D. The court shall grant a petition for expungement filed by
23 the defendant under the provisions of this section if the burden of
24 proof is met.
Req. No. 17068 Page 3
1 E. An expungement may be pursued under the provisions of this
2 section regardless of the amount of time between the relevant charge
3 or conviction and when relief is sought.
4 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

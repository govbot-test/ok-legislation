1 BE I T ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
2 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 1210.163, as
3 last amended by Section 3, Chapter 260, O.S.L. 2025 (70 O.S. Supp.
4 2025, Section 1210.163), is amended to read as follows:
5 Section 1210.163. A. Every school employee having reason to
6 believe that a student under the age of eighteen (18) years is a
7 victim of abuse or neglect shall report the matter immediately to
8 the Department of Human Services and local law enforcement. Reports
9 to the Department shall be made to the hotline provided for in
10 Section 1-2-101 of Title 10A of the Oklahoma Statutes. Any
11 allegation of abuse or neglect reported in any manner to a county
12 office shall immediately be referred to the hotline by the
13 Department.
14 B. Every school employee having reason to believe that a
15 student age eighteen (18) years or older is a victim of abuse or
16 neglect shall report the matter immediately to local law
17 enforcement.
18 C. 1. In addition to the reporting requirements of this
19 section, any superintendent or school administrator of a private
20 school or public school district who has reason to believe, or
21 receives an allegation or disclosure, that a school employee is
22 involved in the abuse or neglect of a student shall report the
23 matter within twenty-four (24) hours to local law enforcement.
24

1 2. All disclosures or allegations made under this subsection
2 shall be reported to law enforcement prior to any formal
3 investigation by the school or school board, or questioning of the
4 subject of the accusation.
5 3. No school investigator, administrator, or official shall
6 conduct formal interviews of the subject of the accusation or engage
7 in disciplinary proceedings until law enforcement has been notified
8 and has had the opportunity to interview the involved parties,
9 unless law enforcement determines that an immediate school response
10 is necessary to protect student safety.
11 4. The resignation, termination, transfer, or other separation
12 from employment of the subject of an allegation or disclosure shall
13 not relieve any person of the duty to report to law enforcement as
14 required by this subsection.
15 D. In reports required by subsection A or, B, or C of this
16 section, local law enforcement shall keep confidential and redact
17 any information identifying the reporting school employee unless
18 otherwise ordered by the court. A school employee with knowledge of
19 a report required by subsection A or, B, or C of this section shall
20 not disclose information identifying the reporting school employee
21 unless otherwise ordered by the court or as part of an investigation
22 by local law enforcement or the Department.
23 D. E. Any superintendent or school administrator of a private
24 school or public school district who knowingly and willfully fails

3 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY Section 1, Chapter 297, O.S.L.
4 2024 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 6-401), is amended to read as
5 follows:
6 Section 6-401. A. As used in this section:
7 1. "Electronic or digital communication" includes, but is not
8 limited to, emails, text messages, instant messages, direct
9 messages, social media messages, messages sent through software
10 applications, and any other electronic digital means of
11 communication; and
12 2. "School personnel" means teachers, coaches, administrators,
13 school bus drivers, or any other persons employed full-time or part-
14 time by a public school or charter school.
15 B. School personnel engaging in electronic or digital
16 communication with an individual student shall include the student's
17 parent or guardian in any electronic or digital communication,
18 unless such communication is on a school-approved platform and
19 related to school and academic communications.
20 C. Exceptions to the requirement in subsection B of this
21 section may be made in case of an emergency, subject to subsequent
22 notification to the parent or guardian. School personnel shall make
23 reasonable efforts to use school-approved platforms, systems, or
24
HB2959 HFLR Page 7
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 appl ications that allow automatic inclusion of parents or guardians
2 in communications with students.
3 D. Schools shall provide training, developed by the State
4 Department of Education, for school personnel on the student
5 communication requirements of this section.
6 E. Any school personnel who is reported to be in violation of
7 subsection B of this section shall be put on administrative leave
8 while the school district investigates the incident and notifies the
9 board of education. If the investigation finds that no misconduct
10 occurred, the school personnel shall be reinstated and the incident
11 shall be noted in the school personnel's employee file. If the
12 investigation finds misconduct occurred, the school personnel shall
13 be disciplined according to the school district board of education's
14 policy, up to and including termination of employment, and the
15 incident shall be reported to law enforcement pursuant to Section
16 1210.163 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
17 F. Law enforcement shall be immediately notified of any
18 violation of subsection B of this section involving sexual activity,
19 sexual contact, or any other inappropriate or unlawful behavior
20 pursuant to Section 1210.163 of this title.
21 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.

23 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby

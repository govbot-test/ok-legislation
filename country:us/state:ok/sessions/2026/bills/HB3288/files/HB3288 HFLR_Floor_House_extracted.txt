11 An Act relating to schools; amending 70 O.S. 2021,
Section 11-103.9, which relates to physical education
12 programs; adding full-day prekindergarten to
elementary physical education instruction
13 requirements; increasing amount of weekly physical
education instruction for elementary school students;
14 prohibiting the withholding of physical education as
a form of punishment; increasing amount of weekly
15 physical activity for elementary school students;
directing schools to provide recess or physical
16 activity before tests or assessments; requiring local
school boards to adopt a policy regarding weekly
17 physical education instruction for students in grades
six through twelve; and providing an effective date.

1 Section 11-103.9. A. Except as otherwise provided for in this
2 section, the State Board of Education shall require, as a condition
3 of accreditation, that school districts provide to all students
4 physical education programs which may include athletics.
5 B. The Board shall require, as a condition of accreditation,
6 that public elementary schools provide instruction, for students in
7 full-day prekindergarten, kindergarten, and grades one through five,
8 in physical education or exercise programs for a minimum of an
9 average of sixty (60) one hundred fifty (150) minutes each week.
10 The time students participate in recess shall not be counted toward
11 the sixty-minutes-per-week one-hundred-fifty-minutes-per-week
12 physical education requirement. Schools may exclude from
13 participation in the physical education or exercise programs
14 required in this subsection those students who have been placed into
15 an in-house suspension or detention class or placement or those
16 students who are under an in-school restriction or are subject to an
17 administrative disciplinary action Physical education activities
18 shall not be withheld as a form of punishment in students in
19 prekindergarten through fifth grade, except in situations where
20 aggression or other threatening behaviors has threatened the safety
21 of the individual student or other students. In this situation, the
22 student involved shall have accommodations made so he or she will be
23 allowed to participate in some type of physical activity.

1 C. The Board shall require, as a condition of accreditation,
2 that public elementary schools provide to students in full-day
3 prekindergarten, kindergarten, and grades one through five, in
4 addition to the requirements set forth in subsection B of this
5 section, an average of sixty (60) minutes each week of physical
6 activity, which may include, but not be limited to, physical
7 education, exercise programs, fitness breaks, recess, and classroom
8 activities, and wellness and nutrition education. During end-of-
9 term or school-year assessment and testing, every attempt shall be
10 made to coordinate recess periods, or periods of moderate physical
11 activity, within the ninety-minute period prior to the time of such
12 assessment or testing session beginning. Each school district board

14 compliance with the provisions of this subsection, giving
15 consideration to the recommendations of each school's Healthy and
16 Fit School Advisory Committee as submitted to the school principal
17 pursuant to the provisions of Section 24-100a of this title.

23 requirement of physical education instruction for students in grades
24 six through twelve. The Board shall also strongly encourage school

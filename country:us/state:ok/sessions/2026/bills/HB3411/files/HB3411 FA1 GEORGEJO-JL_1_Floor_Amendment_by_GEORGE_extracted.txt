8 An Act relating to environment and natural resources;
defining terms; directing the Department of
9 Environmental Quality to purchase certain equipment;
requiring testing of samples of biosolids; requiring
10 certain written warning; prohibiting application of
biosolids to certain crops; prohibiting application
11 of biosolids to land more than once every other year;
directing the Department of Environmental Quality to
12 propose rules; requiring implementation on or before
a certain date; providing for codification; and
13 providing an effective date.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-6-501.7 of Title 27A, unless
19 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. For the purposes of this section:
21 1. "Biosolids" means primarily organically treated wastewater
22 materials from municipal wastewater treatment plants that are
23 suitable for recycling as amendments. This term is within the
24 meaning of "sludge" referenced in Section 2-6-101 of this title.
Req. No. 16937 Page 1
1 Noth ing in this section shall be construed to apply to septage or
2 sewage that is removed from residential septic tanks; and
3 2. "PFAS" means per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances, including
4 but not limited to PFOA, PFOS, and any additional compounds
5 designated by the Department of Environmental Quality.
6 B. The Department of Environmental Quality shall purchase the
7 necessary equipment to test for PFAS in biosolids.
8 C. To protect the health and safety of the people of this
9 state, prior to land application of biosolids, a sample of the
10 biosolids that are to be applied must first be tested by the
11 Department of Environmental Quality or by a laboratory appropriately
12 accredited to perform such an analysis in order to determine the
13 amount of PFAS in the biosolids prior to land application.
14 D. All farmers and ranchers whose lands are being applied with
15 biosolids shall be given test results that include nitrogen,
16 phosphorus, potassium, pH, PFAS, and heavy metal concentrations by
17 the person or entity providing such biosolids. The Department of
18 Environmental Quality shall help identify known potential risks, if
19 any, that must be disclosed. If risks are identified, those risks
20 shall be provided with the test results from the person or entity
21 providing biosolids.
22 E. Biosolids may not be applied to land where crops are grown
23 for human consumption. Furthermore, biosolids may not be applied in
24 the same place more than once every other year.
Req. No. 16937 Page 2
1 F. The Department of Environmental Quality shall propose rules
2 necessary to implement this section, which shall include an approval
3 process for alternative treatment methods such as microbial and
4 supercritical water oxidation methods to reduce or remove PFAS from
5 biosolids.
6 G. The Department of Environmental Quality shall implement the
7 requirements of this section no later than January 1, 2028.
8 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

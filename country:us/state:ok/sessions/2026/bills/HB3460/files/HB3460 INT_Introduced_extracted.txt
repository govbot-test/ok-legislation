6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to the Capitol Patrol; amending 47
O.S. 2021, Section 2-105, as amended by Section 1,
8 Chapter 301, O.S.L. 2022 (47 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section
2-105), which relates to the Oklahoma Highway Patrol
9 Division; removing the Capitol Patrol Section;
amending 73 O.S. 2021, Section 173, as amended by
10 Section 2, Chapter 429, O.S.L. 2024 (73 O.S. Supp.
2025, Section 173), which relates to Oklahoma Capitol
11 Improvement Authority grounds; establishing a Capitol
Patrol Division within the Oklahoma National Guard;
12 granting enforcement powers for the Oklahoma National
Guard Capitol Patrol Division; repealing 47 O.S.
13 2021, Section 11-1009, which relates to parking on
certain state property; repealing 47 O.S. 2021,
14 Section 2-105.7, which relates to the Capitol Patrol
Division; creating the Oklahoma National Guard Patrol
15 Division; requiring Office of Management and
Enterprise Services to provide office space;
16 describing duties; providing for enforcement
mechanisms; providing for codification; and providing
17 an effective date.

3 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 21.2 of Title 44, unless there
5 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 A. There is hereby created within the Oklahoma National Guard
7 an Oklahoma National Guard Capitol Patrol Division which shall
8 consist of such patrolmen and employees as may be necessary to
9 provide law enforcement services to all state buildings and
10 properties, including grounds appurtenant thereto, within Oklahoma
11 County and Tulsa County. All patrolmen of the Oklahoma National
12 Guard Capitol Patrol Division shall have the authority to enforce
13 all parking, traffic, and criminal laws within all state buildings
14 and properties, including grounds appurtenant thereto, within
15 Oklahoma County and Tulsa County.
16 B. All patrolmen of the Oklahoma National Guard Capitol Patrol
17 Division shall have, in addition to their primary duty as prescribed
18 in subsection A of this section, a secondary duty to enforce all
19 state statutes, to make arrests for violations, and to perform other
20 duties as prescribed by the Adjutant General to ensure the safety
21 and security for all state buildings and property.
22 C. The Office of Management and Enterprise Services and the
23 Oklahoma Capitol Improvement Authority shall provide office and
24

2 Division.
3 D. The annual salaries of personnel comprising Oklahoma
4 National Guard Capitol Patrol Division shall be in accordance and
5 conformity with the findings for Department of Public Safety law
6 enforcement personnel of the State of Oklahoma Total Remuneration
7 Study of 2013.
8 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
9 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 21.3 of Title 44, unless there
10 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
11 A. No person shall place, stop, park, or stand any vehicle,
12 including trailers or implements of husbandry, contrary to any
13 official sign reserving, restricting, or regulating the placing,
14 stopping, standing, or parking of a vehicle at any state building or
15 property, including grounds appurtenant thereto, within Oklahoma
16 County and Tulsa County.
17 B. The Oklahoma National Guard Capitol Patrol Division shall be
18 responsible for the enforcement of subsection A of this section.
19 C. A violation of subsection A of this section shall be
20 indicated by the placing of a notice of such violation on the
21 windshield of the vehicle improperly placed, stopped, parked, or
22 standing. The notice shall be on a form prescribed by the Adjutant
23 General.

7 An Act relating to the Oklahoma Strategic Military
Planning Commission; amending 74 O.S. 2021, Section
8 5401, which relates to creation of the Commission;
extending sunset date; updating statutory language;
9 providing an effective date; and declaring an
emergency.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 74 O.S. 2021, Section 5401, is
14 amended to read as follows:
15 Section 5401. A. There is hereby re-created the Oklahoma
16 Strategic Military Planning Commission.
17 B. The Oklahoma Strategic Military Planning Commission shall
18 consist of nine (9) members as follows:
19 1. Five persons to be appointed by the Governor, each of whom
20 shall represent, respectively, the interests of communities that
21 would be affected by realignment or closure of the following
22 military installations:
23 a. Altus Air Force Base,
24 b. Vance Air Force Base,
ENGR. S. B. NO. 392 Page 1
1 c. Fort Sill,
2 d. the Army Ammunition Plant located near McAlester, and
3 e. Tinker Air Force Base;
4 2. One person to be appointed by the Speaker of the House of
5 Representatives from the membership of the House and one person to
6 be appointed by the President Pro Tempore of the Senate from the
7 membership of the Senate. The members appointed pursuant to this
8 paragraph shall be ex officio and nonvoting members of the
9 Commission; and
10 3. The Secretary of Veterans Affairs, or successor member of
11 the Governor’s Executive Cabinet who has responsibility for Veterans
12 Affairs, and the Adjutant General of the Oklahoma Army and Air
13 National Guard. The members appointed pursuant to this paragraph
14 shall be ex officio and nonvoting members of the Commission.
15 C. The members of the Commission shall serve at the pleasure of
16 the appointing authority. The members of the Commission shall
17 select from among their membership a chair and vice-chair vice
18 chair. The chair and vice-chair vice chair shall serve for a period
19 of one (1) year and may succeed themselves in office.
20 D. The Commission shall be subject to the provisions of the
21 Oklahoma Open Meeting Act and the Oklahoma Open Records Act.
22 E. The Commission shall be authorized to meet at such times as
23 may be required in order to fulfill the duties and responsibilities
24 imposed upon it by law.
ENGR. S. B. NO. 392 Page 2
1 F. The provisions of this section shall cease to have the force
2 and effect of law and the Commission shall be dissolved by operation
3 of law on December 31, 2025 December 31, 2030.
4 G. The members of the Commission shall not be deemed to be
5 officers or to hold public office for purposes of Section 6 of Title
6 51 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
7 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
8 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
9 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
10 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
11 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
12 Passed the Senate the 17th day of February, 2026.

8 POLICY COMMITTEE RECOMMENDATION
9 An Act relating to the Children and Juvenile Code;
requiring group homes to maintain general liability
10 insurance coverage; directing the Department of Human
Services to promulgate rules; providing that certain
11 form be completed annually; requiring the group home
to maintain form; requiring that the insurance policy
12 or contract be maintained at all times; providing
that prevailing party shall be entitled to certain
13 fees and costs; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.

17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-8-115 of Title 10A, unless
20 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. A group home, as defined in Section 1-1-105 of this title,
22 shall maintain general liability insurance coverage of at least One
23 Million Dollars ($1,000,000.00) for each occurrence of damage to
24 off-site personal property. An insurance policy or contract

1 requ ired under this section shall cover damage to off-site personal
2 property that occurs while the child is in the care of the group
3 home.
4 B. The Department of Human Services shall promulgate rules
5 providing for a standard form to be signed and dated by an insurance
6 agent licensed in this state confirming that the group home has an
7 unexpired and uncancelled insurance policy or contract of at least
8 One Million Dollars ($1,000,000.00) that meets the requirements of
9 this section. This form shall be completed annually and shall be
10 maintained by the group home.
11 C. The insurance policy or contract shall be maintained at all
12 times in an amount as required by this section.
13 D. The Department shall promulgate rules to implement the
14 provisions of this section.
15 E. The prevailing party in a civil action for damage to off-
16 site personal property that occurs while the child is in the care of
17 the group home shall be entitled to attorney fees and costs.

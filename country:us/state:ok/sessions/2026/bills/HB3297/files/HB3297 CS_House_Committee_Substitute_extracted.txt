9 An Act relating to highway remediation services;
requiring highway remediation and cleanup services
10 maintain certain minimum liability insurance
coverage; requiring coverage for licensure;
11 authorizing certain suspension or revocation of
license; requiring certain price list be published on
12 website; requiring annual update of price list;
listing requirements of price list; providing for
13 codification; and providing an effective date.

18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-7-407 of Title 27A, unless
21 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 Every highway remediation and cleanup service operating within
23 this state shall maintain commercial general liability insurance
24 coverage of not less than Three Million Dollars ($3,000,000.00),
Req. No. 16815 Page 1

1 that includes completed operations and pollution liability coverage.
2 Proof of such coverage shall be provided upon licensure by the
3 Department of Environmental Quality and upon request to law
4 enforcement, the Department of Transportation, insurers, or
5 contracting parties. The Department of Environmental Quality may
6 suspend or revoke the license of a highway remediation and cleanup
7 service for failure to maintain the coverage required pursuant to
8 this section.
9 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
10 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-7-408 of Title 27A, unless
11 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
12 Each highway remediation and cleanup service operating in this
13 state shall publish and maintain on a publicly accessible website a
14 clear, searchable price list of its top fifty (50) most frequently
15 billed nonconsent highway remediation services. The price list
16 shall be updated at least annually and made available prior to or
17 contemporaneously with billing. The price list shall include, at a
18 minimum:
19 1. A plain-language description of each service;
20 2. The standard charge for each service, including, but not
21 limited to, hourly rates, common equipment charges, and emergency
22 surcharges;
23 3. Units of measurement or billing increments; and
24 4. Any commonly applied surcharges.

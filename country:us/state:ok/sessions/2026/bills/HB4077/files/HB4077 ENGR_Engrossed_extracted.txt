7 An Act relating to the Oklahoma Department of
Emergency Management; making an appropriation to the
8 Emergency Relief and Impacts Revolving Fund;
identifying source; establishing amounts; providing
9 purposes; requiring and limiting the utilization of
funds; authorizing certain memorandums of
10 understanding; limiting scope; prohibiting certain
memoranda terms; authorizing and limiting the
11 promulgation of rules and utilization of procedures;
requiring certain reports and submissions to certain
12 entities; requiring appearance before certain joint
committee; limiting duration of certain requirements;
13 providing determination process; providing for
noncodification; and declaring an emergency.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
18 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
19 There is hereby appropriated to the Emergency Relief and Impacts
20 Revolving Fund established in Section 683.24B of Title 63 of the
21 Oklahoma Statutes, from any monies not otherwise appropriated from
22 the Statewide Recovery Fund created pursuant to Section 255 of Title
23 62 of the Oklahoma Statutes, the sum of Ten Million Five Hundred
24 Eighty-two Thousand Five Hundred Ninety-six Dollars ($10,582,596.00)
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4077 Page 1
1 or s o much thereof as may be necessary to administer the Emergency
2 Relief and Impacts Grant Program. Such funds shall be utilized in a
3 manner consistent with the recommendations adopted by the Joint
4 Committee on Pandemic Relief Funding on April 15, 2026.
5 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
6 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
7 The Oklahoma Department of Emergency Management may enter into
8 memorandums of understanding with other agencies of the State of
9 Oklahoma for the auditing, documentation, evaluation,
10 implementation, oversight, reporting, and management of funds and
11 associated efforts related to the appropriations made in this act;
12 provided, that no such memorandum of understanding shall require or
13 include, as an option or condition, the direct or practical transfer
14 or relinquishment of control by the agency appropriated such funds
15 to budget, expend, allocate, and request the distribution of the
16 funds appropriated by this act.
17 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
18 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
19 The Oklahoma Department of Emergency Management may promulgate
20 rules, utilize existing rules, establish procedures, and utilize
21 existing procedures to implement the provisions of this act;
22 provided, such rules and procedures do not conflict with or impede
23 the provisions of this act.
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4077 Page 2
1 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
2 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
3 The Oklahoma Department of Emergency Management shall retain no
4 more than two percent (2%) of the funds appropriated by this act to
5 reimburse costs:
6 1. Incurred by the Oklahoma Department of Emergency Management;
7 or
8 2. Incurred on the agency's behalf,
9 associated with the administration of the appropriated funds and
10 programming required by the Oklahoma Department of Emergency
11 Management under the provisions of this act; provided, that no funds
12 shall be retained that would be disallowable under the provisions of
13 the American Rescue Plan Act of 2021.
14 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
15 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
16 A. The Oklahoma Department of Emergency Management shall:
17 1. Submit to the Chairs of the Joint Committee on Pandemic
18 Relief Funding, or any successor Oklahoma House of Representatives
19 or Oklahoma State Senate legislative committee or joint committee,
20 as designated by the Speaker of the Oklahoma House of
21 Representatives and the President Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State
22 Senate:
23
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4077 Page 3
1 a. a written or electronic quarterly report detailing the
2 budgeting, expenditure, and management of all monies
3 appropriated in this act, and
4 b. a copy of all memorandums of understanding and
5 contracts with third parties entered into by the
6 Oklahoma Department of Emergency Management to
7 facilitate, assist, or administer powers and duties
8 provided to the Department under the provisions of
9 this act; and
10 2. At the Joint Committee on Pandemic Relief Funding's request,
11 appear before the Joint Committee no later than six (6) months after
12 the effective date of this act, and as otherwise requested by the
13 Joint Committee, to provide a status update regarding the
14 implementation of the provisions of this act.
15 B. The provisions of subsection A of this section shall remain
16 applicable for the period of time that monies appropriated under
17 this act are being budgeted, expended, or managed in the state. The
18 ending of such period shall be determined by the State Treasurer,
19 and shall be reported to the Governor, the Speaker of the Oklahoma
20 House of Representatives and the President Pro Tempore of the
21 Oklahoma State Senate.
22 SECTION 6. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
23 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4077 Page 4
1 decl ared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
2 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
3 Passed the House of Representatives the 21st day of April, 2026.

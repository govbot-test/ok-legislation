6 An Act relating to the True Grit Trail; directing the
Department of Transportation to erect signage in
7 certain locations; requiring the Department of
Transportation to collaborate with the Oklahoma
8 Tourism and Recreation Department to maintain certain
online resources for the True Grit Trail; providing
9 source of funding; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.
10
11
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 4101 of Title 69, unless there
16 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. The Oklahoma Department of Transportation shall erect
18 signage designating the following roads as the True Grit Trail:
19 beginning at U.S. Highway 64 at the Oklahoma-Arkansas state line to
20 U.S. Highway 59; from U.S. Highway 59 to U.S. Highway 271; from U.S.
21 Highway 271 to the town of Spiro; from Spiro back to U.S. Highway
22 59; from U.S. Highway 59 to State Highway 31; from State Highway 31
23 to U.S. Highway 270; from U.S. Highway 270 to Main Street in
24 McAlester; from Main Street to East Krebs Avenue; from East Krebs
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1411 Page 1
1 Aven ue and Main Street back to U.S. Highway 270; from U.S. Highway
2 270 to State Highway 2; from State Highway 2 to Robbers Cave State
3 Park; from Robbers Cave State Park on State Highway 2 to U.S.
4 Highway 270; from U.S. Highway 270 to State Highway 82; from State
5 Highway 82 to U.S. Highway 271; from U.S. Highway 271 to State
6 Highway 1; and from State Highway 1 and ending at the intersection
7 of State Highway 1 and U.S. Highway 259.
8 B. The Department of Transportation shall erect informational
9 highway signage designed or approved by the Oklahoma Tourism and
10 Recreation Department to direct visitors to sites of significant
11 interest along the True Grit Trail, including:
12 1. The city of Sallisaw;
13 2. The town of Spiro;
14 3. The town of Bokoshe;
15 4. The town of McCurtain;
16 5. The town of Kinta;
17 6. The town of Quinton;
18 7. The city of Krebs;
19 8. The city of McAlester;
20 9. The city of Hartshorne;
21 10. The city of Wilburton;
22 11. Robbers Cave State Park;
23 12. The town of Red Oak;
24 13. The town of Bengal;
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1411 Page 2
1 14. The town of Talihina; and
2 15. The Talimena National Scenic Byway.
3 C. The Department of Transportation shall collaborate with the
4 Oklahoma Tourism and Recreation Department to maintain online
5 resources for the True Grit Trail, including, but not limited to:
6 1. A map of the True Grit Trail;
7 2. Information about each site of significant interest
8 identified in this act; and
9 3. Historical context regarding the True Grit Trail.
10 D. The cost associated with signage required by this act shall
11 be funded from private sources and may include the cost of the
12 initial erection of signs as well as the cost of potential
13 replacement or reconstruction of signs. Upon receiving adequate
14 funding, the Department of Transportation shall cause suitable
15 permanent markers to be placed as required by this act.
16 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

8 An Act relating to the True Grit Trail; directing the
Department of Transportation to erect signage in
9 certain locations; requiring the Department of
Transportation to collaborate with the Oklahoma
10 Tourism and Recreation Department to maintain certain
online resources for the True Grit Trail; providing
11 source of funding; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 4101 of Title 69, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. The Oklahoma Department of Transportation shall erect
19 signage designating the following roads as the True Grit Trail:
20 beginning at U.S. Highway 64 at the Oklahoma-Arkansas state line to
21 U.S. Highway 59; from U.S. Highway 59 to U.S. Highway 271; from U.S.
22 Highway 271 to the town of Spiro; from Spiro back to U.S. Highway
23 59; from U.S. Highway 59 to State Highway 31; from State Highway 31
24 to U.S. Highway 270; from U.S. Highway 270 to Main Street in
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB1411 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 McAl ester; from Main Street to East Krebs Avenue; from East Krebs
2 Avenue and Main Street back to U.S. Highway 270; from U.S. Highway
3 270 to State Highway 2; from State Highway 2 to Robbers Cave State
4 Park; from Robbers Cave State Park on State Highway 2 to U.S.
5 Highway 270; from U.S. Highway 270 to State Highway 82; from State
6 Highway 82 to U.S. Highway 271; from U.S. Highway 271 to State
7 Highway 1; and from State Highway 1 and ending at the intersection
8 of State Highway 1 and U.S. Highway 259.
9 B. The Department of Transportation shall erect informational
10 highway signage designed or approved by the Oklahoma Tourism and
11 Recreation Department to direct visitors to sites of significant
12 interest along the True Grit Trail, including:
13 1. The city of Sallisaw;
14 2. The town of Spiro;
15 3. The town of Bokoshe;
16 4. The town of McCurtain;
17 5. The town of Kinta;
18 6. The town of Quinton;
19 7. The city of Krebs;
20 8. The city of McAlester;
21 9. The city of Hartshorne;
22 10. The city of Wilburton;
23 11. Robbers Cave State Park;
24 12. The town of Red Oak;
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB1411 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 13. The town of Bengal;
2 14. The town of Talihina; and
3 15. The Talimena National Scenic Byway.
4 C. The Department of Transportation shall collaborate with the
5 Oklahoma Tourism and Recreation Department to maintain online
6 resources for the True Grit Trail, including, but not limited to:
7 1. A map of the True Grit Trail;
8 2. Information about each site of significant interest
9 identified in this act; and
10 3. Historical context regarding the True Grit Trail.
11 D. The cost associated with signage required by this act shall
12 be funded from private sources and may include the cost of the
13 initial erection of signs as well as the cost of potential
14 replacement or reconstruction of signs. Upon receiving adequate
15 funding, the Department of Transportation shall cause suitable
16 permanent markers to be placed as required by this act.
17 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

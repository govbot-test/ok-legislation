10 An Act relating to foreign lobbying; creating the
Foreign Principal Lobbying Oversight Act; providing
11 short title; defining terms; requiring agents of
foreign principals to make certain filing with the
12 Secretary of State; requiring filings to include
certain information; requiring agents to notify the
13 Secretary of State of changes; requiring a
termination notice in certain circumstances;
14 requiring the Secretary of State to publish certain

providing for violations; providing for penalties;
16 providing for noncodification; providing for

20 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
21 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
22 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Foreign
23 Principal Lobbying Oversight Act”.

1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 291.5 of Title 74, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. As used in this act:
5 1. “Agent” means a person representing, acting, or lobbying for
6 compensation on behalf of a foreign principal to influence the laws,
7 policies, or regulations of this state or its political
8 subdivisions; secure funding or incentives for the benefit of a
9 foreign principal; or solicit, collect, or disburse contributions,
10 loans, or other funds on behalf of a foreign principal. Agent shall
11 not include:
12 a. diplomatic or consular officers, staff members of a
13 diplomatic or consular officer, or officials of a
14 foreign government,
15 b. attorneys representing a foreign country, government,
16 political party, or corporation or business entity
17 with a fifty-one percent (51%) or greater interest
18 that is owned by a foreign country, government, or
19 political party in purely commercial endeavors or
20 legal proceedings before state or federal courts,
21 agencies, or other political subdivisions, or
22 c. any news or press service or association or any
23 newspaper, magazine, periodical, or other publication;
24 and

1 2. “Foreign principal” means a foreign country, government,
2 high-ranking government official, political party, or business
3 entity with a fifty-one percent (51%) or greater interest that is
4 owned or controlled by a foreign country, government, high-ranking
5 government official, or political party.
6 B. Any agent for a foreign principal shall register and submit
7 a completed filing with the Secretary of State within fifteen (15)
8 days of becoming an agent for a foreign principal and pay a fee
9 pursuant to Section 1142 of Title 18 of the Oklahoma Statutes. A
10 completed filing shall include the identity of the agent and the
11 foreign principal compensating the agent, the beginning and ending
12 date, if known, of the agency agreement with the foreign principal,
13 the nature of the lobbying the agent is conducting for the foreign
14 principal, and any other information deemed necessary by the
15 Secretary of State.
16 C. An agent for a foreign principal shall notify the Secretary
17 of State within ten (10) business days of any changes to information
18 submitted pursuant to subsection B of this section. An agent shall
19 submit a termination notice with the Secretary of State within ten
20 (10) business days of ceasing representation for a foreign
21 principal.
22 D. The Secretary of State shall cause a list of registrations
23 and filings of agents for foreign principals submitted pursuant to
24 subsection B of this section to be published on its website and

4 E. This act shall not apply to:
5 1. Religious or charitable organizations registered with the
6 Secretary of State or that are exempt from taxation pursuant to the
7 provisions of the Internal Revenue Code, 26 U.S.C., Section
8 501(c)(3);
9 2. Persons engaged in activities benefitting religious,
10 scholastic, academic, scientific, or fine arts interests; or
11 3. Persons involved in private and nonpolitical activities in
12 furtherance of a bona fide trade or commerce of the foreign
13 principal.
14 F. Any person who willfully or knowingly violates any provision
15 of this act, willfully or knowingly makes a false statement of any
16 material fact, or willfully or knowingly omits any material fact
17 required by this act shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a
18 misdemeanor and subject to a fine of Five Hundred Dollars ($500.00).
19 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

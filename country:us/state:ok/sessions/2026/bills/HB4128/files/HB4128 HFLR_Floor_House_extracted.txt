11 An Act relating to game and fish; amending 29 O.S.
2021, Section 5-401, which relates to seasons and
12 rules; amending the Wildlife Conservation
Commission's authority; determining a certain
13 earliest start date; and providing an effective date.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 29 O.S. 2021, Section 5-401, is
18 amended to read as follows:
19 Section 5-401. A. The Wildlife Conservation Commission is
20 authorized to declare an open season on wildlife in any counties or
21 parts of counties of this state where, in the judgment of the
22 Commission, such wildlife exists in sufficient quantity to warrant
23 such open season, and such open season shall be declared by
24
HB4128 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 Comm ission resolution not less than ten (10) days before the season
2 is opened.
3 B. The Commission may designate any two (2) consecutive days
4 after the open season declared for hunting deer to be senior
5 citizen's hunting days. During those two (2) days only legal
6 residents of Oklahoma who are sixty-four (64) years of age or older
7 may hunt deer provided the person has a valid license as otherwise
8 provided by law.
9 C. The Commission is further authorized to declare an open
10 season on mountain lions and black bears; provided, such season for
11 bear shall include a muzzleloader period to run concurrent with
12 muzzleloader deer season, and an archery period starting no later
13 than September 15 of each year running through deer muzzleloading
14 season.
15 D. Except as provided in subsection E of this section, the
16 Commission is further authorized to prescribe rules necessary to the
17 proper conduct and policing of such open season, the amount and kind
18 of wildlife that may be taken, and the dates and time limits of such
19 season.
20 E. Any bowfishing season set for nongame fish by the Commission
21 for the Upper Illinois River shall include the following:
22 1. Upstream from the Horseshoe Bend boat ramp to where State
23 Highway 51 crosses the Illinois River bowfishing is permitted from
24 June 1 to March 31; and
HB4128 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 2. Upstream from where State Highway 51 crosses the Illinois
2 River bowfishing is permitted from December 1 through March 31.
3 F. The Commission is further authorized to require any person
4 participating in such open season or hunting in open areas during
5 open season to procure, under rules prescribed by the Commission, a
6 special permit or license to participate in such season or hunt in
7 such areas during open season and to charge residents not to exceed
8 Twenty Dollars ($20.00), nonresidents not to exceed Seventy-five
9 Dollars ($75.00) and further providing that Five Dollars ($5.00) of
10 this fee shall be set aside for compensation to Oklahoma surface
11 holders participating in the Acres for Wildlife Program at a rate to
12 be determined by the Wildlife Conservation Commission. No
13 exemptions shall be permitted.
14 G. Open seasons, closed seasons, bag limits, catch limits,
15 possession limits and territorial limitations set forth in the
16 statutes of this state, pertaining to wildlife of every sort, are
17 hereby declared to be based on the existence of a normal population
18 of such species of wildlife, compatible with and not damaging to the
19 proper agricultural use of the lands of the state.
20 H. Such seasons, catch limits, bag limits, possession limits
21 and territorial limitations as set by statute shall prevail and be
22 in full force and effect for each and every species of wildlife to
23 which they pertain so long as the population or numbers of such
24
HB4128 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 wild life species remain normal or are not damaging or endangering
2 crops or proper agricultural use of the lands of the state.
3 I. The Commission in session, and after having given ten (10)
4 days' public notice of such meeting being called and the purpose of
5 such meeting, the species of wildlife to be considered and the
6 reasons therefor, shall have the right to determine if there does or
7 does not exist a normal population of the wildlife species under
8 consideration, which does or does not endanger the crops of the
9 state or of any certain areas, or the agricultural use of the lands
10 therein. In the event the consideration is other than statewide,
11 the Commission shall meet at the most convenient place to the people
12 of the area in the area being considered. All meetings shall be
13 open to the public and proper records of those appearing and
14 testifying shall be made. If the Commission, after hearing and
15 after investigation, finds that the populations of the wildlife
16 species under hearing are not normal or that they are not compatible
17 to the agricultural use of the lands or are damaging or endangering
18 the farm crops of the area, they shall, by administrative order,
19 make the necessary changes by either extending, shortening, opening
20 or closing such seasons, and change such bag limits, catch limits
21 and possession limits, and regulate methods or devices for taking,
22 killing or capturing of the wildlife species affected in any area or
23 areas covered by the notice of meeting and hearing, except as
24 otherwise set by the Legislature.
HB4128 HFLR Page 4
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 J. Such administrative order shall take effect after
2 publication in at least one newspaper of general state circulation,
3 or in a newspaper having circulation in the territory affected. A
4 copy of all such orders shall, before publication, be filed with the
5 Secretary of State, and such order shall not be construed as
6 authorizing the Commission to change any penalty for violating any
7 game law or regulation or change the amount of any license
8 established by the Legislature.
9 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

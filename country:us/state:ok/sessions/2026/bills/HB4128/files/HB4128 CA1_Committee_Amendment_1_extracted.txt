I move to amend House Bill No. 4128 as follows:
1. On Page 1, Line 14 ½, by inserting a new SECTION 1 to read as follows, and by
renumbering subsequent sections:
“SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 29 O.S. 2021, Section 4-144, as amended by Section 10,
Chapter 6, O.S.L. 2024 (29 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 4-144), is amended to read as follows:
Section 4-144. A. No person may hunt or take black bear without having first procured a
license from the Director of Wildlife Conservation or an agent of the Director.
B. The Oklahoma Wildlife Conservation Commission shall decide the open season, and the
bag limits, and territorial limitations for hunting or taking black bear.
C. The fees for a license issued pursuant to this section shall be:
1. For legal residents, One Hundred Dollars ($100.00); and
2. For nonresidents, Five Hundred Dollars ($500.00).
D. Any person who fails to obtain a license as required in subsection A of this section and pay
the fee as required in paragraph 1 of subsection C of this section, upon conviction, shall be punished
by a fine of not less than Two Hundred Dollars ($200.00) nor more than Five Hundred Dollars
($500.00) or by imprisonment in the county jail for a period not to exceed six (6) months, or by both
said fine and imprisonment.
E. Any person who fails to obtain a license as required in subsection A of this section and pay
the fee as required in paragraph 2 of subsection C of this section, upon conviction, shall be punished
by a fine of not less than Eight Hundred Dollars ($800.00) nor more than One Thousand Dollars
($1,000.00) or by imprisonment in the county jail for a period not to exceed six (6) months, or by
both such fine and imprisonment.
F. A resident in possession of a lifetime hunting license or a lifetime combination hunting and
fishing license pursuant to the provisions of Section 7 of this act prior to and after the effective date
of this act shall be exempt from the annual black bear license requirement of subsection A of this
section.”
2. On Page 2, Lines 8 through 17, by deleting after the word “bears” and before the period, all
new language and inserting the following:
“, which may be hunted in Adair, Choctaw, Haskell, Latimer, LeFlore, McCurtain,
Pushmataha and Sequoyah Counties, the portion of McIntosh and Muskogee Counties south of
Interstate 40, and the portion of Atoka, Bryan, McIntosh, and Pittsburgh Counties east of U.S.
Highway 69. Archery season for black bears shall be September 21 through October 19 .
Muzzleloader season shall run concurrently with deer muzzleloader season”;
(Floor Amendments Only) Date and Time Filed:
Untimely Amendment Cycle Extended Secondary Amendment

3. On Page 5, Line 11, by deleting SECTION 2 in its entirety and inserting a new SECTION 3
and SECTION 4 to read as follows:
“SECTION 3. This act shall become effective August 1, 2026.
SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation of the public peace, health
or safety, an emergency is hereby declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
be in full force from and after its passage and approval.”; and
4. By amending the title to conform.

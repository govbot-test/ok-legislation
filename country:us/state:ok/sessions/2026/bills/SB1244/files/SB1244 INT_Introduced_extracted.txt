6 AS INTRODUCED
6
7 An Act relating to pardons and paroles; amending 57
7 O.S. 2021, Section 332.21, which relates to parole of
8 certain inmates; modifying criteria for certain
8 parole; modifying criteria for certain risk-
9 assessment instrument; requiring the Pardon and
9 Parole Board to place certain inmates on parole
10 docket; requiring granting of certain parole;
10 updating statutory language and reference; and
11 providing an effective date.
11
12
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13
14 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 57 O.S. 2021, Section 332.21, is
14
15 amended to read as follows:
15
16 Section 332.21. A. The Pardon and Parole Board is empowered to
16
17 parole a prisoner an inmate who:
17
18 1. Is sixty (60) years of age or older;
18
19 2. Has served, in actual custody, the shorter of ten (10) years
19
20 of the term or terms of imprisonment, or one-third (1/3) of the
20
21 total term or terms of imprisonment; and
21
22 3. Poses minimal public Does not pose substantial safety risks
22
23 warranting continued imprisonment;.
23
24
24
Req. No. 2557 Page 1
1 4. Is not imprisoned for a crime enumerated in Section 13.1 of
1
2 Title 21 of the Oklahoma Statutes or Section 571 of Title 57 of the
2
3 Oklahoma Statutes; and
3
4 5. Has not been convicted of a crime that would require the
4
5 person to be subject to the registration requirements of the Sex
5
6 Offenders Registration Act.
6
7 B. The authority to grant parole under Section 332.2 of Title
7
8 57 of the Oklahoma Statutes this title shall rest with the Pardon
8
9 and Parole Board.
9
10 C. The Pardon and Parole Board shall use an evidence-based
10
11 risk-assessment instrument to assess the public safety risk posed by
11
12 aging prisoners inmates upon release. Such instrument shall include
12
13 the health status of the inmates.
13
14 D. Unless eligible for release at an earlier date, an aging
14
15 prisoner inmate who has been committed to the Department of
15
16 Corrections for a term or terms of imprisonment shall have the
16
17 ability to request a parole hearing before the Pardon and Parole
17
18 Board if the prisoner inmate has served, in actual custody, the
18
19 shorter of:
19
20 1. Ten (10) years of the term or terms of actual imprisonment;
20
21 or
21
22 2. One-third (1/3) of the total term or terms of imprisonment.
22
23
23
24
24
Req. No. 2557 Page 2
1 E. Once a prisoner an inmate requests a parole hearing under
1
2 subsection A of this section, the Pardon and Parole Board may shall
2
3 place the prisoner inmate on the next available docket.
3
4 F. The Pardon and Parole Board may shall grant parole to a
4
5 prisoner an inmate if the Board finds by a preponderance of the
5
6 evidence that the prisoner inmate, if released, can live and remain
6
7 at liberty without posing a substantial risk to public safety.
7
8 G. The Pardon and Parole Board may shall use the selected
8
9 evidence-based risk-assessment instrument to make the determination
9
10 provided for in subsection F of this section.
10
11 H. The Pardon and Parole Board may provide the prisoner inmate
11
12 the opportunity to speak on his or her own behalf and the option of
12
13 having counsel present at the parole hearing.
13
14 I. For purposes of this section:
14
15 1. “Aging prisoner” “Aging inmate” means any person imprisoned
15
16 by the Department of Corrections who is sixty (60) years of age or
16
17 older; and
17
18 2. “Evidence-based” means programs or practices that have been
18
19 scientifically tested in controlled studies and proven to be
19
20 effective.
20
21 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

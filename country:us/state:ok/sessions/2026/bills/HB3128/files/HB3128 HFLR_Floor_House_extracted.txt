21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
22 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
23 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:

1 A. The Legislature finds that Oklahoma's long-term economic
2 growth, business competitiveness, and workforce capacity depend upon
3 strategic coordination between employers, education providers,
4 workforce systems, and state agencies.
5 B. It is the intent of the Legislature to:
6 1. Identify barriers to workforce entry and participation;
7 2. Strengthen workforce pipelines in key industries;
8 3. Align education and training systems with employer needs;
9 4. Support apprenticeships, technical training, and career
10 development;
11 5. Enhance Oklahoma's economic competitiveness through a
12 modernized, responsive workforce system; and
13 6. Ensure recommendations are industry-driven, data-informed,
14 and actionable.
15 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 651 of Title 40, unless there is
17 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. There is hereby created the Actionable Response for Industry
19 Skills and Excellence in Oklahoma Task Force (ARISE-OK).
20 B. The purpose of ARISE-OK is to evaluate statewide workforce
21 readiness, identify barriers to employment, strengthen workforce
22 pipelines, and advance strategies that support business growth and
23 economic competitiveness.

1 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 652 of Title 40, unless there is
3 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 For purposes of this act:
5 1. "Industry representative" means an individual employed by or
6 affiliated with a private-sector business operating within the State
7 of Oklahoma;
8 2. "Registered apprenticeship" means a program registered with
9 the United States Department of Labor or the Oklahoma Office of
10 Workforce Development;
11 3. "Task Force" or "ARISE-OK" means the Actionable Response for
12 Industry Skills and Excellence Task Force created by this act; and
13 4. "Workforce barriers" includes transportation, childcare,
14 credentialing, reentry, housing, and other obstacles preventing
15 workforce entry or retention.

1 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 654 of Title 40, unless there is
3 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 The Actionable Response for Industry Skills and Excellence in
5 Oklahoma Task Force (ARISE-OK) shall:
6 1. Evaluate Oklahoma's workforce readiness and talent shortages
7 across all industry sectors;
8 2. Identify regulatory, statutory, and practical barriers to
9 employment;
10 3. Recommend strategies to strengthen workforce training,
11 apprenticeships, career and technical education, education
12 alignment, and employer participation;
13 4. Study workforce challenges within agriculture,
14 manufacturing, energy, construction, health care, technology, and
15 veteran transition sectors;
16 5. Engage rural, urban, and veteran communities to ensure
17 statewide workforce responsiveness;
18 6. Coordinate studies and data requests within the Oklahoma
19 Workforce Commission to avoid duplication and ensure complementary
20 efforts;
21 7. Provide statutory and administrative recommendations to
22 improve workforce effectiveness and business competitiveness;
23
24

1 8. Address workforce challenges not explicitly within the
2 Commission's statutory mission, including cross-sector coordination,
3 long-term strategic workforce policy, and structural barriers; and
4 9. Leverage expertise from ex officio members for emerging
5 industries and workforce insights.
6 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 655 of Title 40, unless there is
8 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 A. The Actionable Response for Industry Skills and Excellence
10 in Oklahoma Task Force (ARISE-OK) shall submit the following written
11 reports of findings and legislative recommendations, as follows:
12 1. Interim report by December 1, 2027;
13 2. Second report by December 1, 2028; and

16 1. The Governor;
17 2. The President Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate;
18 3. The Speaker of the Oklahoma House of Representatives;
19 4. The Oklahoma Workforce Commission; and
20 5. The public via an online posting by the Department of Labor.

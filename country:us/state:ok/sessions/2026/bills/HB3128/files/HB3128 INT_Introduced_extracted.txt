2 2nd Session of the 60th Legislature (2026)
3 HOUSE BILL 3128 By: West (Kevin)

6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to labor, enacting the Actionable
Response for Industry Skills and Excellence in
8 Oklahoma Task Force (ARISE-OK); describing
legislative intent and purpose; defining terms;
9 creating guidelines for Task Force members; creating
guidelines for appointed members; describing duties;
10 requiring reports; describing report deadlines;
providing guidelines for compensation and
11 reimbursement; requiring staffing and support;
providing for codification; and providing an
12 effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
17 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
18 A. The Legislature finds that Oklahoma's long-term economic
19 growth, business competitiveness, and workforce capacity depend upon
20 strategic coordination between employers, education providers,
21 workforce systems, and state agencies.
22 B. It is the intent of the Legislature to:
23 1. Identify barriers to workforce entry and participation;
24 2. Strengthen workforce pipelines in key industries;
Req. No. 14421 Page 1

1 3. Align education and training systems with employer needs;
2 4. Support apprenticeships, technical training, and career
3 development;
4 5. Enhance Oklahoma's economic competitiveness through a
5 modernized, responsive workforce system; and
6 6. Ensure recommendations are industry-driven, data-informed,
7 and actionable.
8 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
9 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 651 of Title 40, unless there is
10 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
11 A. There is hereby created the Actionable Response for Industry
12 Skills and Excellence in Oklahoma Task Force (ARISE-OK).
13 B. The purpose of ARISE-OK is to evaluate statewide workforce
14 readiness, identify barriers to employment, strengthen workforce
15 pipelines, and advance strategies that support business growth and
16 economic competitiveness.
17 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 652 of Title 40, unless there is
19 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 For purposes of this act:
21 1. "Industry representative" means an individual employed by or
22 affiliated wih a private-sector business operating within the State
23 of Oklahoma.

1 2. "Registered apprenticeship" means a program registered with
2 the United States Department of Labor or the Oklahoma Office of
3 Workforce Development.
4 3. "Task Force" or "ARISE-OK" means the Actionable Response for
5 Industry Skills and Excellence Task Force created by this act.
6 4. "Workforce barriers" includes transportation, childcare,
7 credentialing, reentry, housing, and other obstacles preventing
8 workforce entry or retention.

10 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 653 of Title 40, unless there is
11 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:

19 designee;
20 4. The State Director of Career and Technology Education, or
21 designee;
22 5. The Chancellor of the Oklahoma State System of Higher
23 Education, or designee; and
24 6. The Secretary of Agriculture, or designee.

2 b. a healthcare sector representative, and
3 c. a statewide business or industry representative.
4 D. Members shall serve until the Task Force submits its final
5 report, unless replaced by the appointing authority. The appointing
6 authority has discretion to replace a member they appointed.

13 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 654 of Title 40, unless there is
15 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 The Task Force shall:
17 1. Evaluate Oklahoma's workforce readiness and talent shortages
18 across all industry sectors;
19 2. Identify regulatory, statutory, and practical barriers to
20 employment;
21 3. Recommend strategies to strengthen workforce training,
22 apprenticeships, Career and Technical Education, education
23 alignment, and employer participation;
24
Req. No. 14421 Page 5

1 4. Study workforce challenges within agriculture,
2 manufacturing, energy, construction, healthcare, technology, and
3 veteran transition sectors;
4 5. Engage rural, urban, and veteran communities to ensure
5 statewide workforce responsiveness;
6 6. Coordinate studies and data requests within the Oklahoma
7 Workforce Commission to avoid duplication and ensure complementary
8 efforts;
9 7. Provide statutory and administrative recommendations to
10 improve workforce effectiveness and business competitiveness;
11 8. Address workforce challenges not explicitly within OWC's
12 statutory mission, including cross-sector coordination, long-term
13 strategic workforce policy, and structural barriers; and
14 9. Leverage expertise from ex officio members for emerging
15 industries and workforce insights.
16 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 655 of Title 40, unless there is
18 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 A. The Task Force shall submit the following written reports of
20 findings and legislative recommendations annually, as follows:
21 1. Interim Report by December 1, 2027;
22 2. Second Report by December 1, 2028; and
23 3. Final Report by December 1, 2029.
24 B. Each report shall be submitted to:

1 1. The Governor;
2 2. The President Pro Tempore of the Senate;
3 3. The Speaker of the House of Representatives;
4 4. The Oklahoma Workforce Commission; and
5 5. The public via an online posting by the Department of Labor.
6 SECTION 7. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 656 of Title 40, unless there is
8 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 A. Members who are state employees shall receive no additional
10 compensation for duties performed under this act.
11 B. Legislative or appointed members who are not state employees
12 may be reimbursed for necessary travel expenses in accordance with
13 the State Travel Reimbursement Act, subject to available funds.
14 C. No member shall receive a stipend or per diem for service on
15 the Task Force.
16 SECTION 8. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 657 of Title 40, unless there is
18 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 The Department of Labor shall provide staff support, meeting
20 coordination, report posting, and administrative assistance to the
21 Task Force.
22 SECTION 9. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 658 of Title 40, unless there is
24 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:

1 A. The Task Force shall terminate on December 31, 2029, unless

8 minimum safety codes and standards for energy storage resource
9 equipment or facilities:
10 1. The International Fire Code (IFC), as adopted and amended by
11 the Oklahoma Uniform Building Code Commission, in effect at the time
12 a construction permit is applied for; and
13 2. Criteria described in the National Fire Protection
14 Association (NFPA) 855 2026 Standard for the Installation of
15 Stationary Energy Storage Systems.
16 Where a conflict exists between the requirements of the IFC, as
17 adopted and amended by the Oklahoma Uniform Building Code
18 Commission, and the NFPA 855 2026 edition, the provisions of the
19 NFPA 855 2026 edition shall take precedence.
20 B. Before a construction permit is issued for an energy storage
21 resource, the entity responsible for fire inspection and primary
22 emergency response may require the resource owner to pay for an
23 independent, third-party engineer licensed in this state or other
24 consultant with appropriate expertise, to provide a written report

1 to t he entity responsible for fire inspection and primary emergency
2 response and the resource owner determining:
3 1. Whether the energy storage resource, as proposed, complies
4 with subsection A of this section; and
5 2. To the extent the energy storage resource does not comply
6 with subsection A of this section, any appropriate actions to cure
7 such noncompliance in accordance with the NFPA 855 2026 edition.
8 C. Any resource owner that receives a lawful request pursuant
9 to subsection B of this section shall cooperate with the request.
10 Such cooperation shall include providing the engineer or consultant
11 with all necessary documents relating to the energy storage
12 resource's site layout, emergency operations plan, hazard mitigation
13 analysis, manufacturer specifications, product listings, including,
14 but not limited to, UL 9540 and UL 9540A reports, compliance with
15 the NFPA 855 2026 edition and other applicable referenced standards,

17 system, as applicable to the proposed energy storage resource. If

19 noncompliance under paragraph 2 of subsection B of this section, the
20 resource owner shall implement said appropriate actions before
21 commencement of operations.

24 855 2026 edition, in this state shall have and maintain, at an on-

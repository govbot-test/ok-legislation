8 An Act relating to contracts; defining terms;
allowing certain owners to make certain requirement;
9 allowing for certain cancellation of certain
agreement; requiring certain platforms to provide
10 certain option; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.
11
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 520 of Title 12, unless there is
16 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. As used in this act:
18 1. “Guest” means a person other than the renter whose stay at a
19 property is included in an agreement through a short-term rental
20 platform for a short-term rental between the owner and the renter;
21 2. “Owner” means any person whose ownership interest appears on
22 the most recently filed deed or instrument recorded with the county
23 clerk in the county where the property is located, or any property
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1769 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 mana ger or other person designated by the owner to manage or oversee
2 a short-term rental property;
3 3. “Renter” means any person who has entered into an agreement
4 through a short-term rental platform for a short-term rental;
5 4. “Short-term rental” means any lease, license, or agreement
6 allowing occupancy of a residential property, or a portion of a
7 residential property, for less than thirty (30) consecutive days in
8 exchange for compensation; and
9 5. “Short-term rental platform” means any online marketplace,
10 website, application, digital service, or other similar platform
11 that advertises, lists, brokers, or facilitates bookings of short-
12 term rentals.
13 B. 1. Any owner who utilizes his or her property as a short-
14 term rental property in this state may, after the agreement for a
15 short-term rental has been completed between the renter and such
16 owner, require the submission of a photograph or other digital
17 representation of the identification of such renter and any
18 associated guests, provided that the owner informs the renter of
19 such requirement prior to the agreement being completed.
20 2. An owner who finds a discrepancy between the information
21 submitted by a renter in the agreement for a short-term rental and
22 the identification provided by such renter shall have the right to
23 cancel such agreement without penalty.
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1769 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 C. Any short-term rental platform operating in this state shall
2 allow the option for owners to request identification, as provided
3 for in paragraph 1 of subsection B of this section, and to cancel
4 agreements without penalty, as provided for in paragraph 2 of
5 subsection B of this section.
6 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
7 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON JUDICIARY

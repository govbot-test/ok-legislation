Section 1:
1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 2nd Session of the 60th Legislature (2026)
4
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to children; enacting the In the
Child's Best Interest Act; defining terms; providing
8 adoptive, fostering, and legal guardianship
arrangement requirements; limiting arrangement to no
9 more than two adults; permitting other adults to
assist in the care of the child; directing the courts
10 to declare certain arrangements void; directing the
Department of Human Services or other agency to find
11 home for child; providing preference for biologically
related adults; providing for codification; and
12 providing an effective date.
13
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2001 of Title 10, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "In the Child's
21 Best Interest Act".
22 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2002 of Title 10, unless there
24 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
Req. No. 14327 Page 1
1 For the purposes of this act:
2 1. "Adult female" means an individual who is eighteen (18)
3 years of age or older and biologically female;
4 2. "Adult male" means an individual who is eighteen (18) years
5 of age or older and biologically male;
6 3. "Adoption" means the legal process by which a person or
7 persons become the legal parent or parents of a child;
8 4. "Biological relative" means a person related to the child by
9 blood, including, but not limited to, parents, grandparents,
10 siblings, aunts, uncles, or cousins;
11 5. "Foster care" means the temporary placement of a child in a
12 home licensed or approved by the state for the purpose of providing
13 care and supervision; and
14 6. "Legal guardianship" means the legal authority granted by a
15 court to a person or persons to make decisions regarding the care,
16 custody, and welfare of a child.
17 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2003 of Title 10, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. In order to promote the best interest of the child, all
21 adoptive, fostering, or legal guardianship arrangements approved or
22 recognized by any court, agency, or entity in the State of Oklahoma
23 shall consist of either:
24 1. One (1) able and willing adult; or
Req. No. 14327 Page 2
1 2. Two (2) able and willing adults comprising one (1) adult
2 female and one (1) adult male.
3 B. The official adoptive, fostering, or legal guardianship
4 arrangements shall be limited to no more than two (2) positions and
5 shall only be reserved for an adult female and an adult male,
6 conjointly, or for a single adult as provided in subsection A of
7 this section.
8 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
9 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2004 of Title 10, unless there
10 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
11 A. Nothing in this act shall prohibit other adults, including,
12 but not limited, to grandparents, relatives, or friends of the
13 adoptive parents, foster parents, or legal guardians, from assisting
14 in the care of the child in the approved home or in the child's
15 everyday life.
16 B. Biological relatives of the child or of the adoptive
17 parents, foster parents, or legal guardians may assist in caring for
18 the child, provided such assistance does not alter the official
19 adoptive, fostering, or legal guardianship arrangements, which shall
20 remain limited to an adult female and an adult male, conjointly, or
21 a single adult.
22 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2005 of Title 10, unless there
24 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
Req. No. 14327 Page 3
1 A. If any adoption, foster care placement, or legal
2 guardianship is found to violate the provisions of this act, the
3 courts of the State of Oklahoma shall declare such arrangement void
4 and not recognized by the state.
5 B. Upon a finding of violation, the state, through the
6 Department of Human Services or other appropriate agency, shall
7 immediately work to find a safe and suitable home for the child.
8 Placement shall be made with either:
9 1. One (1) able and willing adult; or
10 2. Two (2) able and willing adults comprising one (1) adult
11 male and one (1) adult female.
12 C. In determining placement under subsection B of this section,
13 the state shall give preference to adults who are biologically
14 related to the child in the closest degree of kinship, provided such
15 relatives are proven to be safe and suitable for the child through
16 background checks, home studies, and other evaluations required by
17 state law.
18 SECTION 6. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

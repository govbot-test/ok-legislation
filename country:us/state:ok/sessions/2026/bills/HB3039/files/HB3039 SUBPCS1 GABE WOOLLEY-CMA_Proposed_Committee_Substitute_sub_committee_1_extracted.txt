2 2nd Session of the 60th Legislature (2026)
3 PROPOSED SUBCOMMITTEE
SUBSTITUTE
4 FOR
HOUSE BILL NO. 3039 By: Woolley
5
6
7 PROPOSED SUBCOMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
8 An Act relating to children; enacting the In the
Child's Best Interest Act; defining terms; providing
9 adoptive, fostering, and legal guardianship
arrangement requirements; limiting arrangement to no
10 more than two adults; permitting other adults to
assist in the care of the child; directing the courts
11 to declare certain arrangements void; directing the
Department of Human Services or other agency to find
12 home for child; providing preference for biologically
related adults; providing for codification; and
13 providing an effective date.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2001 of Title 10, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "In the Child's
21 Best Interest Act".
22 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2002 of Title 10, unless there
24 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
Req. No. 16027 Page 1
1 For the purposes of this act:
2 1. "Adult female" means an individual who is eighteen (18)
3 years of age or older and biologically female;
4 2. "Adult male" means an individual who is eighteen (18) years
5 of age or older and biologically male;
6 3. "Adoption" means the legal process by which a person or
7 persons become the legal parent or parents of a child;
8 4. "Biological relative" means a person related to the child by
9 blood, including, but not limited to, parents, grandparents,
10 siblings, aunts, uncles, or cousins;
11 5. "Foster care" means the temporary placement of a child in a
12 home licensed or approved by the state for the purpose of providing
13 care and supervision; and
14 6. "Legal guardianship" means the legal authority granted by a
15 court to a person or persons to make decisions regarding the care,
16 custody, and welfare of a child.
17 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2003 of Title 10, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. In order to promote the best interest of the child, all
21 adoptive, fostering, or legal guardianship arrangements approved or
22 recognized by any court, agency, or entity in the State of Oklahoma
23 shall consist of either:
24 1. One able and willing adult;
Req. No. 16027 Page 2
1 2. Two able and willing adults comprising one adult female and
2 one adult male; or
3 3. Two able and willing adults, both of whom are biological
4 relatives of the child.
5 B. The official adoptive, fostering, or legal guardianship
6 arrangements shall be limited to no more than two positions and
7 shall only be reserved for an adult female and an adult male,
8 conjointly, or for a single adult as provided in subsection A of
9 this section.
10 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
11 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2004 of Title 10, unless there
12 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
13 A. Nothing in this act shall prohibit other adults, including,
14 but not limited, to grandparents, relatives, or friends of the
15 adoptive parents, foster parents, or legal guardians, from assisting
16 in the care of the child in the approved home or in the child's
17 everyday life.
18 B. Biological relatives of the child or of the adoptive
19 parents, foster parents, or legal guardians may assist in caring for
20 the child, provided such assistance does not alter the official
21 adoptive, fostering, or legal guardianship arrangements, which shall
22 remain limited to the provisions set forth in subsection A of
23 Section 3 of this act.
24
Req. No. 16027 Page 3
1 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2005 of Title 10, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. If any adoption, foster care placement, or legal
5 guardianship is found to violate the provisions of this act, the
6 courts of the State of Oklahoma shall declare such arrangement void
7 and not recognized by the state.
8 B. Upon a finding of violation, the state, through the
9 Department of Human Services or other appropriate agency, shall
10 immediately work to find a safe and suitable home for the child.
11 Placement shall be made with either:
12 1. One able and willing adult;
13 2. Two able and willing adults comprising one adult male and
14 one adult female; or
15 3. Two able and willing adults, both of whom are biological
16 relatives of the child.
17 C. In determining placement under subsection B of this section,
18 the state shall give preference to adults who are biologically
19 related to the child in the closest degree of kinship, provided such
20 relatives are proven to be safe and suitable for the child through
21 background checks, home studies, and other evaluations required by
22 state law.
23
24
Req. No. 16027 Page 4
1 SECTION 6. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

6 An Act relating to sex offenders; requiring law
enforcement officers to submit a report or complaint
7 for official investigation to the Oklahoma State
Bureau of Investigation in certain circumstances;
8 requiring confidentiality of reports or complaints
for official investigation; providing exceptions;
9 providing for codification; and providing an
effective date.

12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 40.3B of Title 22, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 A. Upon receipt of a report, complaint, or allegation of an
17 offense which, if convicted, would result in the defendant being
18 required to register as a sex offender pursuant to Section 582 of
19 Title 57 of the Oklahoma Statutes, whether originating from a
20 citizen report, third-party complaint, arrest, or other means, the
21 law enforcement officer who takes the report or initiates the
22 complaint shall, within one (1) month, submit to the Oklahoma State
23 Bureau of Investigation a record of the report or complaint for
24 official investigative and recordkeeping purposes. The record shall

1 incl ude, to the extent known or available at the time of the report,
2 the name or identifying information of the accused, the nature of
3 the alleged offense, the date, time, and location of the alleged
4 offense, and the agency report number.
5 B. The duty to submit information pursuant to this section
6 shall apply regardless of whether an arrest is made or whether
7 criminal charges are subsequently filed.
8 C. Information submitted to the Bureau pursuant to this section
9 shall not be made available for public inspection and shall remain
10 confidential except as authorized by law for official investigative
11 or prosecutorial purposes.
12 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

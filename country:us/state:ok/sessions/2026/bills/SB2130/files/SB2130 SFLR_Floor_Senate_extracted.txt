11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
12 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 11 O.S. 2021, Section 17-105, as
13 last amended by Section 1, Chapter 287, O.S.L. 2024 (11 O.S. Supp.
14 2025, Section 17-105), is amended to read as follows:
15 Section 17-105. A. The governing body of each municipality
16 with Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000.00) or more in total revenue to
17 all funds, including component units of which the municipality is a
18 beneficiary, during a fiscal year shall cause to be prepared, by an
19 independent licensed public accountant or a certified public
20 accountant, an annual financial statement audit to be conducted in
21 accordance with auditing standards generally accepted in the United
22 States of America and Government Auditing Standards as issued by the
23 Comptroller General of the United States. Such audit shall be
24 ordered within thirty (30) days of the close of each fiscal year.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2130 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 Copi es shall be filed with the State Auditor and Inspector within
2 six (6) months after the close of the fiscal year in accordance with
3 the provisions of the Municipal Audit Reform Act of 2022 and with
4 the governing body of the municipality.
5 B. The governing body of each municipality with Fifty Thousand
6 Dollars ($50,000.00) or more in total revenue to all funds,
7 including component units of which the municipality is a
8 beneficiary, and with a population of less than two thousand five
9 hundred (2,500) as of the most recent Federal Decennial Census, and
10 for whom an annual financial statement audit is not required by
11 another law, regulation or contract, shall cause to be prepared, by
12 an independent licensed public accountant or a certified public
13 accountant, a biennial financial statement audit in accordance with
14 auditing standards generally accepted in the United States and
15 Government Auditing Standards as issued by the Comptroller General
16 of the United States. Each biennial audit shall cover the two (2)
17 preceding years.
18 The governing body of each municipality may alternatively
19 request a biennial agreed-upon-procedures engagement. Agreed-upon
20 procedures required under the Municipal Audit Reform Act of 2022
21 shall be performed in accordance with the applicable attestation
22 standards of the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants.
23 The audit or agreed-upon-procedures engagement shall be ordered
24 within thirty (30) days of the close of the fiscal year that the
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2130 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 audi t is due. Copies shall be filed with the State Auditor and
2 Inspector within nine (9) months after the close of the fiscal year
3 in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2 of subsection A of
4 Section 212A of Title 74 of the Oklahoma Statutes and with the
5 governing body of the municipality, with the deadline to order and
6 file the audit or agreed-upon procedures eligible for extension by
7 the State Auditor and Inspector for special circumstances or
8 emergencies.
9 C. 1. The municipal income requirements in subsections A and B
10 of this section shall not include any grant monies provided to a
11 municipality from any federal, state, or other governmental entity.
12 The municipal income requirements shall not include income of any
13 public trust established under Sections 176 through 180.4 of Title
14 60 of the Oklahoma Statutes with a municipality as the beneficiary
15 of the trust; provided, income from trusts established principally
16 for the purpose of operating electric, water, wastewater, and
17 sanitation utilities shall be included for purposes of the municipal
18 income requirements.
19 2. Other than an audit required pursuant to Section 180.1 of
20 Title 60 of the Oklahoma Statutes, a municipality with a total
21 revenue of less than Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000.00) across all
22 funds, including component units of which the municipality is a
23 beneficiary, and a population of less than five hundred (500) as of
24 the most recent Federal Decennial Census shall not be required to
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2130 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 prep are an audit or agreed-upon-procedures engagement solely as a
2 condition to be eligible or a recipient of a grant from any federal,
3 state, or local source.
4 D. The governing body of each municipality that requests the
5 biennial agreed-upon-procedures engagement provided in subsection B
6 of this section shall:
7 1. Determine the establishment of policies related to
8 adjustments, write-downs, or write-offs for various receivables due
9 to the municipality or the utility-related trust and select a sample
10 of adjustments to test for adherence to policies and for appropriate
11 supporting documentation;
12 2. Obtain two (2) months of bank statements of the general fund
13 and utility fund and confirm that cash deposits were made in the
14 appropriate accounts and verify utility billing receipts or posting
15 reports agree with the daily deposits;
16 3. Agree upon a pay rate for the city manager or town
17 administrator, city or town clerk, city or town treasurer, and
18 payroll clerk, to be authorized and documented in the personnel file
19 or in approved meeting minutes. If any employee received
20 compensation over and above his or her authorized salary or hourly
21 rate, the payroll clerk, upon request, shall provide appropriate
22 documentation of authorization for such pay. The requirements of
23 this paragraph shall not include expense reimbursements but shall
24 include any allowances considered taxable;
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2130 SFLR Page 4
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 4. Determine the establishment of policies of use, proper
2 municipal purpose, and adherence to prescribed policies for entities
3 that use debit or credit cards;
4 5. Select a sample of transactions to test for supporting
5 documentation;
6 6. Prepare a cash basis schedule of changes in fund balances
7 for each fund and determine compliance with the statutory
8 prohibition of creating fund balance deficits;
9 7. Agree material fiscal year-end bank account balances to bank
10 statements and trace significant reconciling items to subsequent
11 clearance and determine if any bank accounts exist that are not
12 under city council purview;
13 8. Compare uninsured deposits at fiscal year-end to the fair
14 value of pledged collateral;
15 9. Inquire if any instances of known fraud, illegal acts, or
16 noncompliance with laws and regulations have occurred; and
17 10. Compare the use of material-restricted revenues and
18 resources to the restrictions of the governing body of the
19 municipality.
20 E. A public trust with a municipal government or governments as
21 the beneficiary that meet the same financial requirements
22 established in subsection B of this section may, as an alternative
23 to obtaining an audit as required in Section 180.1 of Title 60 of
24

(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 the Oklahoma Statutes, follow the biennial agreed-upon-procedures
2 engagement outlined in subsection D of this section.
3 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

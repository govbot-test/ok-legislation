6 An Act relating to alcoholic beverage license;
amending 37A O.S. 2021, Sections 3-101, as amended by
7 Section 498, Chapter 486, O.S.L. 2025, and 5-103 (37A
O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 3-101), which relate to
8 possession and making of alcoholic beverages and
exemptions from excise tax; removing certain
9 references to personal use permit; updating statutory
language; updating statutory references; repealing
10 37A O.S. 2021, Section 2-140, which relates to
personal use permit; and providing an effective date.
11
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 37A O.S. 2021, Section 3-101, as
15 amended by Section 498, Chapter 486, O.S.L. 2025 (37A O.S. Supp.
16 2025, Section 3-101), is amended to read as follows:
17 Section 3-101. A. No person shall manufacture, rectify, sell,
18 possess, store, import into or export from this state, transport, or
19 deliver any alcoholic beverage except as specifically provided in
20 the Oklahoma Alcoholic Beverage Control Act. Provided, that nothing
21 herein Nothing in this section shall prevent the possession and
22 transportation of alcoholic beverages for the personal use of the
23 possessor and his or her family and guests, so long as the Oklahoma
24 excise tax has been paid thereon, except for beer. Provided,
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1218 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 furt her, that nothing herein Nothing in this section shall prevent a
2 person from making beer, cider, or wine, by simple fermentation and
3 without distillation for personal use if the maker of such beverages
4 has first applied for and possesses a valid personal use permit
5 issued by the ABLE Commission and the total volume of beer, cider,
6 or wine produced in any given calendar year is less than two hundred
7 (200) gallons. No beverages made pursuant to a for personal use
8 permit shall be sold or offered for sale.
9 B. 1. Any duly licensed physician or dentist may possess and
10 use alcoholic beverages in the strict practice of the profession and
11 any hospital or other institution caring for sick or diseased
12 persons may possess and use alcoholic beverages for the treatment of
13 bona fide patients of such hospital or institution. Any drugstore
14 employing a licensed pharmacist may possess and use alcoholic
15 beverages in the preparation of prescriptions of duly licensed
16 physicians.
17 2. The possession, transportation, and dispensation of wine by
18 any authorized representative of any church for the conducting of a
19 bona fide rite or religious ceremony conducted by such church shall
20 not be prohibited by the Oklahoma Alcoholic Beverage Control Act;
21 nor shall such act prevent the sale, shipping, or delivery of
22 sacramental wine by any person holding a sacramental wine supplier
23 license issued pursuant to the Oklahoma Alcoholic Beverage Control
24 Act to any religious corporation or society of this state holding a
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1218 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 vali d exemption from taxation issued pursuant to Section 501(a) of
2 the Internal Revenue Code, 1954, and listed as an exempt
3 organization in Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code,
4 1954, of the United States, as amended.
5 3. Provided further, that nothing Nothing in the Oklahoma
6 Alcoholic Beverage Control Act shall prevent the possession,
7 transportation, and sale of alcoholic beverages within military
8 reservations and in accordance with the laws and rules governing
9 such military reservations, provided that the Oklahoma excise tax
10 has been paid on such beverages.
11 C. 1. Except as otherwise authorized by law, it is unlawful
12 for any brewer, manufacturer, wine and spirits wholesaler, beer
13 distributor, or retailer of alcoholic beverages, located and doing
14 business from outside this state, to make retail sales of alcoholic
15 beverages to purchasers located in this state or to ship alcoholic
16 beverages sold at retail to persons located in this state. Any
17 person who engages in the sale or shipping of alcoholic beverages in
18 violation of the provisions of this subsection, upon conviction,
19 shall be guilty of a Class D1 felony offense punishable by
20 imprisonment as provided for in subsections B through F of Section
21 20N of Title 21 of the Oklahoma Statutes, if the sale or delivery is
22 made to a person under twenty-one (21) years of age, or a
23 misdemeanor, if the sale or delivery is made to a person twenty-one
24 (21) years of age or older.

1 2. The fine for a violation of this subsection shall be not
2 more than exceed Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00).
3 3. In addition, if the person holds a license issued by the
4 ABLE Alcoholic Beverage Laws Enforcement (ABLE) Commission, the
5 license shall be revoked pursuant to Section 60 2-148 of this act
6 title.
7 D. All brewers, importers, brokers, and others who sell beer or
8 cider to licensed beer distributors in Oklahoma this state or
9 manufacturers, importers, brokers, and others who sell cider to
10 licensed beer distributors in Oklahoma this state, regardless of
11 whether such sales are consummated within or without the state, must
12 shall obtain a license, as the case may be, in order to sell beer or
13 cider intended for consumption within the State of Oklahoma this
14 state.
15 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 37A O.S. 2021, Section 5-103, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 5-103. A. The excise tax levied by Section 104 5-101
18 of this act title shall not apply to:
19 1. Alcohol used exclusively for industrial purposes by the
20 holder of an industrial license;
21 2. Alcohol lawfully withdrawn and used free of tax under a tax-
22 free permit issued by the United States government;
23
24

1 3. Alcoholic beverages used exclusively by licensed physicians
2 and dentists in the bona fide practice of their professions or by
3 licensed pharmacists in compounding prescriptions;
4 4. Beer, cider and wine made for personal use pursuant to a
5 personal use permit issued as provided in Section 70 of this act;
6 5. Wine used exclusively for sacramental purposes in bona fide
7 religious ceremonies;
8 6. 5. Alcoholic beverages, not exceeding one (1) liter,
9 imported into this state by the possessor for his or her own
10 personal use;
11 7. 6. Alcoholic beverages provided to attendees, free of
12 charge, at charitable events licensed and approved by the ABLE
13 Alcoholic Beverage Laws Enforcement (ABLE) Commission; and
14 8. 7. Mixed beverage and public event licensees which utilize
15 the services of a licensed caterer.
16 B. As a condition precedent to the allowance of any exemption
17 authorized by subsection A of this section:
18 1. If a license or permit is required by the Oklahoma Alcoholic
19 Beverage Control Act, for such use, the person claiming any such
20 exemption must shall have obtained from the ABLE Commission the
21 license or permit authorizing such exempt use; and
22 2. There must be furnished such proof Proof of the exclusive
23 use for such exempt purposes shall be provided as required by the
24 Oklahoma Tax Commission may require.

18 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 1210.508B,
19 as amended by Section 4, Chapter 411, O.S.L. 2024 (70 O.S. Supp.
20 2025, Section 1210.508B), is amended to read as follows:
21 Section 1210.508B. A. The Legislature finds that it is
22 essential for children in the public schools to read early and well
23 in elementary school. The Legislature further finds that clear and
24 visible goals, assessments to determine the reading level at each
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4420 Page 4

1 elem entary school, use of a scientifically based and researched
2 methodology in reading instruction in addition to regular and
3 periodic measurements of elementary school reading improvement, and
4 accountability in each level of the educational system will result
5 in a significant increase in the number of children reading at or
6 above grade level.
7 B. The purpose of the Strong Readers Act is to ensure that
8 progression from one grade to another is determined, in part, upon
9 proficiency in reading, that school district board of education
10 policies facilitate reading instruction and intervention services to
11 address student reading needs, and that each student and his or her
12 parent or legal guardian be informed of that student's reading
13 progress. It is the intent of the Legislature that every student
14 reads at or above grade level by the end of third grade.
15 C. To carry out the purpose of this act, it is the intent of
16 the Legislature that the identification, instruction, and
17 intervention required under the Strong Readers Act be implemented
18 within a multi-tiered system of supports (MTSS) framework that
19 emphasizes strong core literacy instruction for all students and
20 provides increasing levels of instructional intensity based on
21 student need and demonstrated response to instruction.
22 D. It is further the intent of the Legislature that strong core
23 literacy instruction consists of explicit, systematic instruction in
24 foundational reading and writing skills, including phonological
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4420 Page 5

1 awar eness, decoding, encoding, spelling, sentence-level writing,
2 language development, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension,
3 recognizing the reciprocal relationship between reading and writing
4 proficiency.
5 E. Each public school district in this state shall ensure that
6 all students receive a well-rounded education that is focused on
7 building deep foundations in reading, writing, and mathematics. The
8 State Board of Education shall encourage school districts to
9 integrate the teaching of the other curricular areas in the subject
10 matter standards adopted by the Board with the instruction of
11 reading, writing, and mathematics. All teachers of reading in the
12 public schools in this state in kindergarten through third grade
13 shall incorporate into instruction the five elements of reading
14 instruction which are phonological awareness, decoding, fluency,
15 vocabulary, and comprehension.
16 D. F. It is the intent of the Legislature that beginning with
17 the 2025-2026 school year, school districts and charter schools in
18 this state shall be prohibited from using the three-cueing system
19 model of teaching students to read. For the purposes of this
20 section, the "three-cueing system" means any model of teaching
21 students to read based on meaning, structure, syntax, and visual
22 cues, which may also be known as meaning, structure, and visual
23 (MSV), balanced literacy, or whole language.

16 practices within a multi-tiered system of supports framework. The
17 primary responsibility of a literacy coach is to support educators
18 and administrators in improving instructional practices and

11 An Act relating to the Secretary of State; defining
terms; requiring submission of certain agency actions
12 to Secretary of State for review; establishing
procedures for review; requiring board to abide by
13 certain recommendations; subjecting board members to
removal for certain misconduct; specifying actions
14 not to be deemed anticompetitive; authorizing board
to make certain request; providing for codification;
15 and providing an effective date.
16
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 664.11 of Title 74, unless there
21 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 A. As used in this section:
23 1. “Proposed qualifying actions” means proposed actions by a
24 qualifying board that include, but are not limited to:
SB835 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 a. actions affecting a class or category of individuals,
2 b. actions contested by the respondent,
3 c. actions based on alleged conduct not admitted by the
4 respondent,
5 d. revocation or suspension of a license,
6 e. actions stemming from a complaint filed by a
7 competitor of the respondent, or
8 f. actions that are purely discretionary by the board;
9 and
10 2. “Qualifying board” means a state licensing board or
11 commission on which a majority of members are market participants in
12 the occupation the board or commission regulates, but shall not
13 include a state licensing board or commission whose actions are
14 actively supervised by a statewide elected official who reviews the
15 substance of the actions, has the power to veto or modify particular
16 decisions to ensure they accord with state policy, and is not an
17 active participant in the occupation the board or commission
18 regulates.
19 B. 1. Any qualifying board proposing to take non-rulemaking
20 action that has anticompetitive effects shall submit such proposed
21 action to the Secretary of State for review and confirmation that
22 the action is enforcing a clearly articulated and affirmatively
23 expressed state policy.
24
SB835 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 2. The Secretary of State, or his or her designee, shall review
2 the action and return a written determination to the qualifying
3 board within sixty (60) days.
4 3. Upon receipt of the written determination of the Secretary
5 of State, the qualifying board shall abide by the recommendation of
6 the Secretary of State, if any.
7 4. Failure of the qualifying board to following the written
8 determination of the Secretary of State shall constitute misconduct
9 and shall subject the board members to removal for cause by the
10 appointing authority.
11 C. Actions taken by qualifying boards that do not carry
12 anticompetitive implications need not be submitted to the Secretary
13 of State for review. Such actions shall include, but not be limited
14 to:
15 1. Actions to which the respondent consents or agrees;
16 2. Actions resulting from a criminal conviction in a court of
17 competent jurisdiction;
18 3. Actions in which the qualifying board has performed the non-
19 discretionary task of implementing a mandatory statute;
20 4. Actions that impose only a fine or a requirement for
21 additional education; and
22 5. Actions that do not affect the status of a license or
23 otherwise prevent a licensee from continuing to participate in the
24 occupation of the licensee.
SB835 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 D. Qualifying boards may request a determination from the
2 Secretary of State as to whether an action of the qualifying board
3 is a proposed qualifying action.
4 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
10 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
11 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 550 of Title 3, unless there is
12 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
13 A. As used in this section:
14 1. “Aircraft” has the same meaning as that provided in Section
15 252 of Title 3 of the Oklahoma Statutes, except that the aircraft
16 shall have a gross weight of nine thousand (9,000) pounds or less
17 and operate under the provisions of 14 C.F.R., Part 91; and
18 2. “Automatic dependent surveillance-broadcast” means an
19 advanced aviation surveillance technology that combines ground
20 infrastructure, aircraft avionics, and an aircraft’s positioning
21 source to create an accurate surveillance interface between an
22 aircraft and air traffic control. The term consists of two
23 different services:
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1950 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 a. ADS-B, which receives data provided by ADS-B Out, as
2 well as weather and traffic information from ground
3 stations, and
4 b. ADS-B Out, which broadcasts an aircraft’s Global
5 Positioning System location, altitude, ground speed,
6 and other data.
7 B. No government entity or private individual or entity shall
8 use information broadcasted or collected by an automatic dependent
9 surveillance-broadcast system as a means for calculating,
10 generating, and collecting fees from owners or operators who operate
11 aircraft within this state.
12 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

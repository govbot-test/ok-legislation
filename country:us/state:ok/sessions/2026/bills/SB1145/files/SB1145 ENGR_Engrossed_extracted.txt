8 An Act relating to the Oklahoma Public Employees
Retirement System; amending 62 O.S. 2021, Section
9 3103, as last amended by Section 2, Chapter 361,
O.S.L. 2024 (62 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 3103), which
10 relates to the Oklahoma Pension Legislation Actuarial
Analysis Act; modifying certain definition;
11 authorizing benefit increase for certain retired
members of the Oklahoma Public Employees Retirement
12 System; updating statutory language; providing for
codification; and providing effective dates.

16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 62 O.S. 2021, Section 3103, as
17 last amended by Section 2, Chapter 361, O.S.L. 2024 (62 O.S. Supp.
18 2025, Section 3103), is amended to read as follows:
19 Section 3103. As used in the Oklahoma Pension Legislation
20 Actuarial Analysis Act:
21 1. “Amendment” means any amendment, including a substitute
22 bill, made to a retirement bill by any committee of the House of
23 Representatives or Senate, any conference committee of the House or
24 Senate or by the House or Senate;

7 into a contract with the Legislative Service Bureau pursuant to

9 actuarial services and other duties provided for in the Oklahoma
10 Pension Legislation Actuarial Analysis Act;
11 4. “Nonfiscal amendment” means an amendment to a retirement

14 Section 3109 of this title;

1 A no nfiscal retirement bill shall include any retirement bill that
2 has as its sole purpose the appropriation or distribution or
3 redistribution of monies in some manner to a retirement system for
4 purposes of reducing the unfunded liability of such system or the
5 earmarking of a portion of the revenue from a tax to a retirement
6 system or increasing the percentage of the revenue earmarked from a
7 tax to a retirement system;
8 6. “Reduction-in-cost amendment” means an amendment to a
9 retirement bill having a fiscal impact which reduces the cost of the
10 bill as such cost is determined by the actuarial investigation for
11 the bill prepared pursuant to Section 3109 of this title;
12 7. “Retirement bill” means any bill or joint resolution
13 introduced or any bill or joint resolution amended by a member of
14 the Legislature which creates or amends any law directly affecting a
15 retirement system. A retirement bill shall not mean a bill or
16 resolution that impacts the revenue of any state tax in which a
17 portion of the revenue generated from such tax is earmarked for the
18 benefit of a retirement system;
19 8. “Retirement bill having a fiscal impact” means any
20 retirement bill creating or establishing a retirement system and any
21 other retirement bill other than a nonfiscal retirement bill; and
22 9. “Retirement system” means the Teachers’ Retirement System of

24 Uniform Retirement System for Justices and Judges, the Oklahoma

1 Fire fighters Pension and Retirement System, the Oklahoma Police
2 Pension and Retirement System, the Oklahoma Law Enforcement
3 Retirement System, or a retirement system established after January
4 1, 2006.
5 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
6 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 930.12 of Title 74, unless there
7 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
8 Any person receiving benefits from the Oklahoma Public Employees
9 Retirement System as of June 30, 2026, who continues to receive
10 benefits on or after the effective date of this section, shall
11 receive an increase in benefits as follows:
12 1. Zero percent (0%) if the person has been retired for less
13 than ten (10) years as of June 30, 2026;
14 2. Three percent (3%) if the person has been retired for at
15 least ten (10) years but less than twenty (20) years as of June 30,
16 2026; and
17 3. Six percent (6%) if the person has been retired for twenty
18 (20) years or more as of June 30, 2026.
19 SECTION 3. Section 1 of this act shall become effective October
20 1, 2026.
21 SECTION 4. Section 2 of this act shall become effective
22 November 1, 2026.

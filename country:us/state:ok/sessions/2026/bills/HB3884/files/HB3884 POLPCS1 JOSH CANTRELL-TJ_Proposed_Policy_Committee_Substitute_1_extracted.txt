10 An Act relating to continuum of care and assisted
living; creating the Providers' Bill of Rights Act of
11 2026; establishing what the Providers' Bill of Rights
contains; providing for codification; and providing
12 an effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-896 of Title 63, unless there
18 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Providers' Bill
20 of Rights Act of 2026".
21 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-896.1 of Title 63, unless
23 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 16387 Page 1

1 A. Each assisted living facility shall post the Providers' Bill
2 of Rights in a prominent place in the facility.
3 B. The Providers' Bill of Rights shall provide, as defined
4 under the Continuum of Care Act, the right to:
5 1. Be shown consideration and respect that recognizes the
6 dignity and individuality of the provider and assisted living
7 facility;
8 2. Terminate a contract immediately, if the provider finds that
9 it is mandated by the resident's immediate health needs or necessary
10 for the physical safety of the resident or of other residents;
11 3. Present grievances, file complaints, or provide information
12 to state agencies or other persons without threat of reprisal or
13 retaliation;
14 4. Refuse to perform services for the resident or the
15 resident's family other than those contracted for by the resident
16 and the provider;
17 5. Contract with the community to achieve the highest level of
18 independence, autonomy, interaction, and services to residents;
19 6. Obtain and request any relevant information or records of a
20 consumer referred to the facility in order to assess for appropriate
21 placement, which shall remain confidential as provided by law;
22 7. Refuse to provide any information to third-party providers;
23 8. Be made aware of a resident's history, including self-abuse,
24 violent behavior, alcoholism, or drug abuse; and

1 9. Be made aware if a resident has contracted, arranged, or is
2 being provided with a sitter or any other provider of personal
3 services in his or her personal apartment pursuant to Section 1-
4 890.8 of Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
5 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

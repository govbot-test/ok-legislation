HOUSE BILL NO. 3884 By: Cantrell

8 PROPOSED POLICY COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 An Act relating to continuum of care and assisted
living; creating the Providers' Bill of Rights Act of
10 2026; establishing what the Providers' Bill of Rights
contains; providing for codification; and providing
11 an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-896 of Title 63, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Providers' Bill
19 of Rights Act of 2026".
20 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-896.1 of Title 63, unless
22 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 A. Each assisted living facility shall post the Providers' Bill
24 of Rights in a prominent location within the facility.
Req. No. 16453 Page 1
1 B. The Providers' Bill of Rights shall provide, consistent with
2 state and federal laws and rules promulgated pursuant to the
3 Continuum of Care Act, the right to:
4 1. Assurance that nothing in the Providers' Bill of Rights
5 shall be construed to diminish, impair, or conflict with resident
6 rights established under state or federal law, including but not
7 limited to the Resident Bill of Rights set forth in Section 1-1918
8 of Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes;
9 2. Be treated in a professional manner consistent with
10 applicable contractual, regulatory, and licensing standards;
11 3. Terminate a resident’s contract immediately when a licensed
12 medical professional determination or documented incident
13 demonstrates that continued placement presents an imminent threat to
14 the health or safety of the resident or other residents, consistent
15 with discharge and transfer requirements established by rule;
16 provided, the facility shall comply with all notice, documentation,
17 and appeal protections required under OAC 310:663-3-5;
18 4. Present grievances, file complaints, or provide information
19 to governmental agencies without retaliation as prohibited by law;
20 5. Decline to provide services that are outside the scope of
21 services agreed to in the resident contract or outside the
22 facility’s licensed level of care, unless otherwise required by law
23 or necessitated by emergency circumstances;
24
Req. No. 16453 Page 2
1 6. Enter into lawful agreements with third-party service
2 providers to support resident independence and services, consistent
3 with resident choice, facility policy, and applicable regulatory
4 requirements;
5 7. Request and obtain records reasonably necessary to determine
6 appropriate placement and care needs of a prospective or current
7 resident, subject to applicable privacy, consent, and
8 confidentiality laws;
9 8. Decline admission or continued placement when a prospective
10 or current resident’s needs exceed the facility’s licensed scope of
11 care, staffing capabilities, or physical environment, as documented
12 through the facility’s assessment process;
13 9. Decline to disclose resident information to third parties
14 except as authorized by the resident or as required by state or
15 federal law;
16 10. Establish and enforce policies governing the possession of
17 weapons and unlawful drugs within the facility in accordance with
18 state law and facility safety policies;
19 11. Receive known and legally shareable information regarding
20 behaviors or conditions that may impact the safety of residents or
21 staff, when such information is available and permitted by law; and
22 12. Be notified when a resident has arranged for third-party
23 personal service providers within the facility when such services
24 may affect facility operations, safety, or regulatory compliance.
Req. No. 16453 Page 3
1 C. Facilities shall continue to prominently post the Resident
2 Bill of Rights as required by Section 1-1918 of Title 63 of the
3 Oklahoma Statutes.
4 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

7 An Act relating to medical marijuana licensees;
7 amending Section 2, Chapter 41, O.S.L. 2023 (63 O.S.
8 Supp. 2025, Section 427.26), which relates to bond
8 required for land used in commercial growing
9 operations; increasing certain bond requirement;
9 updating statutory references; and providing an
10 effective date.
10
11
11
12
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13
14 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 2, Chapter 41, O.S.L. 2023
14
15 (63 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 427.26), is amended to read as follows:
15
16 Section 427.26. A. It shall be unlawful for any holder of a
16
17 medical marijuana business license pursuant to Section 427.14 of
17
18 Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes this title to engage in any
18
19 commercial growing operations in this state without acquiring a
19
20 bond. The bond shall cover that area of land within the permit area
20
21 upon which the business licensee will initiate and conduct
21
22 commercial growing operations.
22
23 B. Every applicant for a commercial grower license or
23
24 commercial grower licensee shall file with the Oklahoma Medical
24
Req. No. 2502 Page 1
1 Mari juana Authority a bond satisfactory to the Authority and in the
1
2 amount no less than Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000.00) One Hundred
2
3 Thousand Dollars ($100,000.00) for each license sought or held, with
3
4 a surety company qualified to do business in this state as a surety.
4
5 The bond shall be furnished to the state for the use of the state
5
6 pursuant to the provisions of this act section and Section 427.14 of
6
7 this title. The bond shall be conditional that the obligor will
7
8 comply with the provisions of this act section and Section 427.14 of
8
9 this title including, but not limited to, building codes,
9
10 administrative rules, and other relevant laws, and all rules and
10
11 regulations made pursuant to this act section and Section 427.14 of
11
12 this title and will pay all amounts of money that may be due to the
12
13 state during the time such bond is in effect.
13
14 C. The Authority or the Department of Environmental Quality may
14
15 require a higher amount depending upon the reclamation requirements
15
16 of the approved application. The amount shall reflect the probable
16
17 difficulty of reclamation with consideration for such factors
17
18 including, but not limited to, topography, hydrology, and
18
19 revegetation potential. The amount of the bond for a commercial
19
20 growing operation shall be sufficient to assure the completion of
20
21 the reclamation plan if the work has to be performed by the
21
22 Authority or the Department of Environmental Quality in the event of
22
23 revocation of license.
23
24
24
Req. No. 2502 Page 2
1 D. An appropriate agency may recall the bond if the property
1
2 has been abandoned, the Authority revokes the commercial growing
2
3 operation’s license, or in response to receiving notice of a
3
4 violation of any law, regulation, policy, or ordinance necessitating
4
5 remedial action. The bond shall be used to defray the cost of
5
6 restoration of the property including, but not limited to, removing
6
7 equipment, destruction of waste, remediation of environmental
7
8 hazards, prohibiting public access, addressing improperly coded
8
9 buildings, or determination of the final disposition of any seized
9
10 property.
10
11 E. A holder of a medical marijuana business license pursuant to
11
12 Section 427.14 of Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes this title
12
13 engaging in a commercial growing operation may operate without
13
14 obtaining a bond upon verification by the Authority that the
14
15 permitted land on which the licensee operates the commercial growing
15
16 operation has been owned by the licensee for at least a five-year
16
17 period prior to submission of application.
17
18 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 840-2.15.1 of Title 74, unless
18 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 A. Except as provided by subsection G of this section, no state
20 employee shall be required to work more than fourteen (14) hours
21 during any rolling period of twenty-four (24) hours.
22 B. The provisions of subsection A of this section shall include
23 regular and overtime hours.
24

1 C. The provisions of subsection A of this section may be
2 suspended during an officially declared emergency or when necessary
3 to protect life, public safety, or critical infrastructure.
4 D. The Office of Management and Enterprise Services shall adopt
5 rules to define the conditions which meet the requirements of
6 subsection C of this section.
7 E. All agencies, boards, commissions, departments, and other
8 entities of state government shall adopt rules for scheduling and
9 staffing policies to ensure compliance with the provisions of this
10 section.
11 F. The Office of Management and Enterprise Services shall adopt
12 rules in order to enforce the requirements of this section.
13 G. The provisions of subsection A of this section shall not be
14 applicable to the employees of the Oklahoma House of
15 Representatives, the Oklahoma State Senate or to employees of any
16 entity that provides direct support services to either chamber of
17 the Legislature including, but not limited to, the Legislative
18 Service Bureau. The provisions of subsection A of this section
19 shall be fully applicable to any entity organized within the
20 executive branch or department of state government.

10 An Act relating to state employees; prescribing limit
on certain working hours; providing for computation
11 of hours; providing exceptions for certain emergency
conditions; requiring administrative rules by
12 executive government entities; requiring adoption of
rules by the Office of Management and Enterprise
13 Services; providing for codification; providing an
effective date; and declaring an emergency.

15

17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 840-2.15.1 of Title 74, unless
20 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. No state employee shall be required to work more than
22 fourteen (14) hours during any rolling period of twenty-four (24)
23 hours.

1 B. The provisions of subsection A of this section shall include
2 regular and overtime hours.
3 C. The provisions of subsection A of this section may be
4 suspended during an officially declared emergency or when necessary
5 to protect life, public safety, or critical infrastructure.
6 D. The Office of Management and Enterprise Services shall adopt
7 rules to define the conditions which meet the requirements of
8 subsection C of this section.
9 E. All agencies, boards, commissions, departments, and other
10 entities of state government shall adopt rules for scheduling and
11 staffing policies to ensure compliance with the provisions of this
12 section.
13 F. The Office of Management and Enterprise Services shall adopt
14 rules in order to enforce the requirements of this section.
15 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
16 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
17 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
18 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
19 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

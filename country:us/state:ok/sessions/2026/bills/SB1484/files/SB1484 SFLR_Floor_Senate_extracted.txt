8 An Act relating to medicolegal investigations;
amending 63 O.S. 2021, Section 1-317d, which relates
9 to sudden unexplained infant death investigations;
defining terms; broadening applicability of certain
10 requirements; describing investigations; requiring
certain documentation and reporting of specified
11 cases; requiring certain notice to parent or legal
guardian; conferring certain right on parent or legal
12 guardian; amending 63 O.S. 2021, Section 938, as
amended by Section 3, Chapter 263, O.S.L. 2024 (63
13 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 938), which relates to types
of deaths to be investigated; requiring investigation
14 of certain deaths; updating statutory reference;
providing for recodification; and providing an
15 effective date.
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 63 O.S. 2021, Section 1-317d, is
19 amended to read as follows:
20 Section 1-317d. A. As used in this section:
21 1. “Sudden arrhythmic death syndrome” (SADS) means the sudden
22 and unexpected death due to cardiac arrhythmia, as determined by
23 performance of an investigation, of a young, apparently healthy
24 individual with no previously diagnosed structural heart disease;
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1484 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. “Sudden death in the young” (SDY) means the sudden and
2 unexpected death of an individual younger than twenty (20) years of
3 age due to natural causes including, but not limited to, sudden
4 cardiac death or sudden unexpected death in epilepsy (SUDEP), which
5 death remains unexplained after investigation;
6 3. “Sudden infant death syndrome” (SIDS) means the sudden death
7 of an infant younger than one year of age that remains unexplained
8 after investigation;
9 4. “Sudden unexpected infant death” (SUID) means the sudden and
10 unexpected death of an infant younger than one (1) year of age,
11 whether explained or unexplained, including, but not limited to,
12 death caused by SIDS, accidental suffocation, or other potential
13 causes; and
14 5. “Sudden Unexpected Infant Death (SUID) and Sudden Death in
15 the Young (SDY) Case Registry” means the national surveillance
16 system coordinated by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention
17 and the National Institutes of Health which collects standardized
18 data on sudden and unexpected deaths in individuals younger than
19 twenty (20) years of age.
20 B. 1. When the death of an infant occurs or child dies
21 suddenly and unexpectedly in this state and the cause of death is
22 considered undetermined, including cases of SIDS, SUID, or SDY, a
23 medical examiner shall conduct a sudden unexplained infant death
24 investigation (SUIDI) an investigation not more than forty-eight
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1484 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 (48) hours after the death has occurred. The medical examiner or a
2 medical examiner investigator currently certified by the American
3 Board of Medicolegal Death Investigators shall investigation shall
4 include:
5 a. an autopsy if deemed necessary by the medical examiner
6 to determine the cause of death,
7 b. microscopic and toxicology studies,
8 c. a review of the infant’s or child’s immunization and
9 medical records, as available through the state’s
10 immunization registry, from the infant’s or child’s
11 pediatrician or primary care practitioner, or from
12 other sources, and
13 d. an interview of the parent, legal guardian or
14 caregiver of, or person who last had contact with, the
15 deceased infant and shall fill out the Centers for
16 Disease Control and Prevention’s SUIDI reporting form
17 accordingly or child.
18 2. The medical examiner shall document in the investigative
19 report any immunizations or emergency countermeasures administered
20 to the infant or child within ninety (90) days before the infant’s
21 or child’s death, report the case to the SUID and SDY Case Registry
22 in accordance with protocols established by the Board of Medicolegal
23 Investigations and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention,
24 and forward such report to the State Department of Health.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1484 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 C. 1. When a sudden death of an individual of any age that is
2 suspected to be caused by SADS occurs, a medical examiner shall
3 conduct an investigation. The investigation shall include:
4 a. an autopsy if deemed necessary by the medical examiner
5 to determine the cause of death,
6 b. microscopic and toxicology studies, and
7 c. a review of the individual’s immunization and medical
8 records, as available through state health databases
9 or other sources.
10 2. The medical examiner shall document in the investigative
11 report any immunizations or emergency countermeasures administered
12 to the individual within ninety (90) days before his or her death,
13 report the case to the SUID and SDY Case Registry if the individual
14 was younger than twenty (20) years of age at the time of death, in
15 accordance with protocols established by the Board of Medicolegal
16 Investigations and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention,
17 and forward such report to the State Department of Health.
18 D. The State Department of Health shall retain a copy of each
19 completed SUIDI reporting form in order to compile report for the
20 purpose of compiling accurate and reliable data on SUIDI SIDS, SUID,
21 SDY, and SADS to aid and inform the policies of the Department, and
22 the Department shall provide a copy of each completed SUIDI
23 reporting form to the Child Death Review Board.
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1484 SFLR Page 4
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 B. As used in this section, “medical examiner” means the Chief
2 Medical Examiner, the Deputy Chief Medical Examiner or a medical
3 examiner appointed by the Chief Medical Examiner pursuant to Section
4 937 of Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
5 E. 1. The medical examiner shall notify the parent or legal
6 guardian of a deceased child or infant prior to conducting an
7 investigation as described in this section and shall inform the
8 parent or legal guardian of the right conferred by paragraph 2 of
9 this subsection.
10 2. The parent or legal guardian of a deceased child or infant
11 may refuse to consent to any investigation or any part of an
12 investigation described in this section of the deceased child or
13 infant, unless the medical examiner suspects that a crime was
14 committed involving the death of the child or infant or the death is
15 otherwise required to be investigated under Section 938 of this
16 title.
17 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 63 O.S. 2021, Section 938, as
18 amended by Section 3, Chapter 263, O.S.L. 2024 (63 O.S. Supp. 2025,
19 Section 938), is amended to read as follows:
20 Section 938. A. All human deaths of the types listed herein
21 shall be investigated as provided by law:
22 1. Violent deaths, whether apparently homicidal, suicidal, or
23 accidental;
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1484 SFLR Page 5
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. Deaths under suspicious, unusual, or unnatural
2 circumstances;
3 3. Deaths related to disease which might constitute a threat to
4 public health;
5 4. Deaths unattended by a licensed physician for a fatal or
6 potentially fatal illness;
7 5. Deaths that are medically unexpected and that occur in the
8 course of a therapeutic procedure;
9 6. Deaths of any persons detained or occurring in custody of
10 the Department of Corrections or a county jail;
11 7. Deaths of persons whose bodies are to be cremated,
12 transported out of the state, donated to educational entities, to
13 include limited portions of the body, or otherwise made ultimately
14 unavailable for pathological study; and
15 8. Maternal deaths that occur during pregnancy or within one
16 (1) year of termination of pregnancy reported by a hospital or
17 birthing center under Section 2 of this act Section 1-702f of this
18 title; and
19 9. Deaths involving sudden infant death syndrome (SIDS), sudden
20 unexpected infant death (SUID), sudden death in the young (SDY), or
21 sudden arrhythmic death syndrome (SADS) as provided by Section 1-
22 317d of this title.
23 B. The Chief Medical Examiner shall state on the certificate of
24 death of all persons whose death was caused by execution pursuant to
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1484 SFLR Page 6
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 a la wful court order that the cause of death was the execution of
2 such order.
3 SECTION 3. RECODIFICATION 63 O.S. 2021, Section 1-317d,
4 as amended by Section 1 of this act, shall be recodified as Section
5 941c of Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes, unless there is created a
6 duplication in numbering.
7 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

2 2nd Session of the 60th Legislature (2026)
3 HOUSE BILL 4070 By: Alonso-Sandoval

6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to schools; creating the Oklahoma
Extracurricular Enrichment Act; creating the Educator
8 Extracurricular Support Program under the State
Department of Education; providing purpose of the
9 program; clarifying program participation is
voluntary; mandating fund usage under the program;
10 providing eligibility; requiring creation of a seed
grant component for the program; authorizing seed
11 funds for certain purposes; outlining school priority
for seed funds; requiring application process for
12 program awards; providing application considerations;
requiring participating districts to submit annual
13 report; requiring the Department to submit summary
report; mandating rule promulgation; creating
14 revolving fund; stating fund purpose; allowing fund-
matching for private donation through available
15 funds; providing contribution match requirements;
providing private contribution usage; amending 3A
16 O.S. 2021, Section 713, as last amended by Section 5,
Chapter 443, O.S.L. 2024 (3A O.S. Supp. 2025, Section
17 713), which relates to the disposition of lottery
proceeds; requiring certain amount of proceeds to be
18 allocated to the Extracurricular Enrichment Revolving
Fund; providing for codification; providing an
19 effective date; and declaring an emergency.
20
21

1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-230 of Title 70, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Oklahoma
5 Extracurricular Enrichment Act".
6 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-231 of Title 70, unless there
8 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 A. There is hereby created within the State Department of
10 Education the Educator Extracurricular Support Program (the
11 Program).
12 B. The Program shall provide funds to participating school
13 districts and charter schools for the purpose of compensating
14 teachers and school staff who assume additional responsibilities in
15 supporting extracurricular programs.
16 C. Participation in the Program shall be voluntary for school
17 districts and charter schools.
18 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-232 of Title 70, unless there
20 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. Funds awarded under the Educator Extracurricular Support
22 Program shall be used to provide stipends to eligible teachers and
23 school staff who serve as:
24
Req. No. 15402 Page 2

1 1. Primary sponsors, co-sponsors, or coordinators of
2 extracurricular clubs or programs; or
3 2. Leaders of student organizations requiring planning,
4 supervision, or instruction outside regular school hours.
5 B. Preference shall be given to extracurricular programs that:
6 1. Take place primarily after the regular school day;
7 2. Serve middle school or high school students;
8 3. Are offered at schools where a significant percentage of
9 students qualify for free or reduced-price lunch, as determined by
10 the State Department of Education; and
11 4. Address gaps in access to enrichment opportunities.
12 C. Eligible extracurricular programs may include, but are not
13 limited to:
14 1. Academic, cultural, leadership, or career-focused clubs;
15 2. Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Mathematics
16 (STEAM)-focused opportunities; and STEAM-focused clubs and programs,
17 including robotics, engineering, computer science, coding,
18 mathematics enrichment, science clubs, makerspaces, and design
19 challenges; and
20 3. Other student programs approved by the local school district
21 and the State Department of Education.
22 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-233 of Title 70, unless there
24 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
Req. No. 15402 Page 3

1 A. The Educator Extracurricular Support Program shall include a
2 STEAM seed grant component to support the creation or expansion of
3 extracurricular clubs and programs with a focus on science,
4 technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics.
5 B. Seed funding may be used for:
6 1. Equipment, supplies, and instructional materials;
7 2. Competition registration fees;
8 3. Curriculum resources and training directly related to the
9 extracurricular program; and
10 4. Other costs approved by the State Department of Education.
11 C. Priority for seed funding shall be given to schools that:
12 1. Do not currently offer a STEAM extracurricular program;
13 2. Serve a high percentage of low-income students; and
14 3. Partner with community organizations, CareerTech centers,
15 higher education institutions, or industries.
16 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-234 of Title 70, unless there
18 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 A. The State Department of Education shall award funds for the
20 Educator Extracurricular Support Program to districts through an
21 application process established by promulgated rules.
22 B. In awarding funds, the Department shall consider:
23 1. The number of eligible educators participating;
24 2. The number of students served;
Req. No. 15402 Page 4

1 3. The extent to which the Program expands access to after-
2 school or Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Mathematics
3 (STEAM)-focused opportunities; and
4 4. Demonstrated need, including student poverty levels.
5 C. Funds awarded under this act shall supplement, and not
6 supplant, local or federal funds used for extracurricular or after-
7 school programs.
8 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
9 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-235 of Title 70, unless there
10 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
11 A. Each participating district shall submit an annual report to
12 the State Department of Education that includes:
13 1. The number of educators receiving stipends;
14 2. The extracurricular programs supported;
15 3. The number of students participating;
16 4. Use of any Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and
17 Mathematics (STEAM) seed funds; and
18 5. Outcomes or indicators of student engagement, as determined
19 by the Department.
20 B. The Department shall submit a statewide summary report
21 annually to the Governor, the Speaker of the Oklahoma House of
22 Representatives, and the President Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State
23 Senate.

5 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-238 of Title 70, unless there
6 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
7 A. The State Department of Education may establish a matching
8 funds component within the Oklahoma Extracurricular Enrichment
9 Program to encourage private-sector and community investment in
10 extracurricular and after-school programming.

15 3. Foundations or philanthropic entities;

18 or another ratio established by rule, with priority given to
19 contributions that support:
20 1. After-school extracurricular programs;
21 2. STEAM-focused clubs and activities;
22 3. Schools serving a high percentage of students from low-
23 income households; and

4 may be used for:

10 governing eligibility, verification of contributions, and

11 An Act relating to animals; enacting the Elk
Population Study and Management Act; providing
12 definitions; providing certain organizations to
establish a plan and outline for a comprehensive
13 study of the elk population within a certain region
of the state; requiring the plan and outline to be
14 completed on a certain date; providing requirements
for the comprehensive study; providing for
15 coordination with other relevant state and federal
agencies; requiring the maintaining of detailed
16 accounting and financial records of appropriations
received and expenditures made; providing that
17 external experts and scientists may be retained to
complete the study; providing for codification;
18 providing an effective date; and declaring an
emergency.
19
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
22 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 4101 of Title 2, unless there is
24 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.

1 A. This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Elk
2 Population Study and Management Act".
3 B. As used in this act:
4 1. "Study zone" refers to the Special Northwest Zone that
5 encompasses all private lands in Woodward, Dewey, and Ellis
6 Counties, and a portion of Roger Mills County north of State
7 Highways 33 and 47 to the north county line and west to the state
8 border and east to the county line;
9 2. "Authority" refers to the Oklahoma State University
10 Veterinary Medicine Authority; and
11 3. "Management plan" refers to a document developed based on
12 the findings of the study that outlines recommended actions for the
13 conservation and management of elk populations.
14 C. No later than November 1, 2026, the Oklahoma State
15 University Veterinary Medicine Authority in cooperation with the OSU
16 College of Veterinary Medicine, shall establish a plan and outline
17 for a comprehensive study of the elk population within the Special
18 Northwest Zone. The comprehensive study shall assess current
19 population levels, trends, habitat usage, and develop science-based
20 management recommendations aimed at ensuring the long-term
21 sustainability of elk populations and the health of the ecosystem.
22 The comprehensive study shall include, but not be limited to, the
23 following program characteristics:
24

1 1. The current elk population size, distribution, and density
2 within the study zone;
3 2. The health and genetic diversity of the elk population,
4 including disease monitoring and signs of inbreeding or other health
5 concerns;
6 3. The effectiveness and impact of current elk population
7 management strategies, including hunting, predation control, and
8 habitat conservation; and
9 4. Recommendations culminating in the form of a management plan
10 for the conservation and management of elk populations within the
11 study zone.
12 D. The Authority may coordinate with other relevant state and
13 federal agencies to effectuate the provisions of this act. Such
14 coordination may include requests for temporary suspension and/or
15 restrictions of elk hunting or harvesting while the comprehensive
16 study is being conducted.
17 E. The Authority shall maintain detailed accounting and
18 financial records of appropriations received and expenditures made
19 pursuant to the purposes of this act. The Authority may engage
20 external experts and scientists to plan and implement the
21 comprehensive study. The engagement of such external experts shall
22 be implemented in accordance with the policies of the Authority.
23 The Authority is authorized to carry forward to a future fiscal year
24 any unexpended funds related to this act.

1 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
2 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
3 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
4 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
5 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

7 An Act relating to animals; enacting the Elk
Population Study and Management Act; defining terms;
8 requiring certain organizations to establish a plan
and outline for a comprehensive study of the elk
9 population within a certain region of the state;
requiring the plan and outline to be completed on a
10 certain date; providing requirements for the
comprehensive study; providing for coordination with
11 other relevant state and federal agencies; requiring
the maintenance of detailed accounting and financial
12 records of appropriations received and expenditures
made; providing that external experts and scientists
13 may be engaged to complete the study; providing for
codification; providing an effective date; and
14 declaring an emergency.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 4101 of Title 2, unless there is
20 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Elk
22 Population Study and Management Act".
23 B. As used in this act:
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1770 Page 1

1 1. "Authority" refers to the Oklahoma State University
2 Veterinary Medicine Authority;
3 2. "Management plan" refers to a document developed based on
4 the findings of the study that outlines recommended actions for the
5 conservation and management of elk populations; and
6 3. "Study zone" refers to the Special Northwest Zone that
7 encompasses all private lands in Woodward, Dewey, and Ellis
8 Counties, and a portion of Roger Mills County north of State
9 Highways 33 and 47 to the north county line and west to the state
10 border and east to the county line.
11 C. No later than November 1, 2026, the Oklahoma State
12 University Veterinary Medicine Authority, in cooperation with the
13 OSU College of Veterinary Medicine, shall establish a plan and
14 outline for a comprehensive study of the elk population within the
15 Special Northwest Zone. The comprehensive study shall assess
16 current population levels, trends, and habitat usage, and develop
17 science-based management recommendations aimed at ensuring the long-
18 term sustainability of elk populations and the health of the
19 ecosystem. The comprehensive study shall include, but not be
20 limited to, the following program characteristics:
21 1. The current elk population size, distribution, and density
22 within the study zone;
23
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1770 Page 2

1 2. The health and genetic diversity of the elk population,
2 including disease monitoring and signs of inbreeding or other health
3 concerns;
4 3. The effectiveness and impact of current elk population
5 management strategies, including hunting, predation control, and
6 habitat conservation; and
7 4. Recommendations culminating in the form of a management plan
8 for the conservation and management of elk populations within the
9 study zone.
10 D. The Authority may coordinate with other relevant state and
11 federal agencies to effectuate the provisions of this act. Such
12 coordination may include requests for temporary suspension and/or
13 restrictions of elk hunting or harvesting while the comprehensive
14 study is being conducted.
15 E. The Authority shall maintain detailed accounting and
16 financial records of appropriations received and expenditures made
17 pursuant to the purposes of this act. The Authority may engage
18 external experts and scientists to plan and implement the
19 comprehensive study. The engagement of such external experts shall
20 be implemented in accordance with the policies of the Authority.
21 The Authority is authorized to carry forward to a future fiscal year
22 any unexpended funds related to this act.
23 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.

Section 16-26, as amended by Section 1, Chapter 217,

13 prohibitions; changing the required conditions for an

16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 16-26, as
18 amended by Section 1, Chapter 217, O.S.L. 2024 (2 O.S. Supp. 2025,
19 Section 16-26), is amended to read as follows:
20 Section 16-26. A. 1. It is unlawful for any person to set
21 fire to any forest, grass, range, crop, or other wildlands, or to
22 build a campfire or bonfire, or to burn trash or other material that
23 may cause a forest, grass, range, crop or other wildlands fire in
24 any county, counties or area within a county where, because of

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 emer gency drought conditions, there is gubernatorially proclaimed
2 extraordinary danger from fire, unless the setting of any backfire
3 during the drought emergency is necessary to afford protection as
4 determined by a representative of the Division of Forestry Services,
5 or unless it can be established that the setting of the backfire was
6 necessary for the purpose of saving life or property. The burden of
7 proving the necessity shall rest on the person claiming a defense.
8 2. The Division of Forestry Services shall advise the Governor
9 when the lands described in paragraph 1 of this subsection in any
10 county, counties, or area within a county of this state, because of
11 emergency drought conditions, are in extraordinary danger from fire.
12 The Governor may, by proclamation, declare a drought emergency to
13 exist and describe the general boundaries of the area affected.
14 3. Any proclamation promulgated by the Governor under authority
15 of this subsection shall be effective immediately upon the
16 Governor's signed approval of the emergency proclamation and shall
17 supersede any resolution passed by a board of county commissioners
18 pursuant to subsection B of this section. Notice of the
19 proclamation shall occur through posting on the Oklahoma Department
20 of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry's website and informing local
21 news media. Evidence of publication or posting as herein provided
22 shall be maintained by the Forestry Division of Forestry Services.

2 the emergency shall be promptly made by proclamation, which shall be
3 published or posted in like manner as when officially declared.
4 5. Any person who violates this subsection is guilty of a
5 misdemeanor punishable by a fine of not more than One Thousand
6 Dollars ($1,000.00), by imprisonment for not more than one (1) year,
7 or both.
8 B. 1. It is unlawful for any person to set fire to any forest,
9 grass, range, crop or other wildlands, or to build a campfire or
10 bonfire, or to burn trash or other material that may cause a forest,
11 grass, range, crop or other wildlands fire in any county of this
12 state in which the board of county commissioners of the county has
13 passed a resolution declaring a period of extreme fire danger. As
14 used in this subsection, "extreme fire danger" means:
15 a. all three either a red flag warning by the National
16 Weather Service (NWS) has been issued, or
17 b. at least three of the following conditions are
18 present:

20 conditions exist as determined by the National

23 criteria,

3 2. A majority of the board of county commissioners may call an
4 emergency meeting at any time to pass or revoke a resolution
5 declaring a period of extreme fire danger in accordance with this
6 section.
7 3. A board of county commissioners shall have the documented
8 concurrence of a majority of the chiefs, or their designees, of the
9 municipal and certified rural fire departments located in the county
10 that a period of extreme fire danger exists prior to passage of a
11 resolution declaring a period of extreme fire danger in the county.
12 The resolution shall be effective for a period not to exceed
13 fourteen (14) eight (8) days from the date of passage by the board
14 of county commissioners, unless the burn ban is removed earlier by
15 the same method by which it was approved. If extreme fire danger
16 conditions persist, subsequent resolutions may be passed by the
17 board of county commissioners in the same manner as provided in this
18 paragraph. The board of county commissioners, in the resolution,
19 may grant exceptions to the fire prohibition based on appropriate
20 precautionary measures.
21 4. A board of county commissioners shall not be liable for
22 damages incurred as a result of a vote taken, or in absence of a
23 vote taken, pursuant to the provisions of this subsection.

6 under authority of this subsection shall be effective immediately

22 and imprisonment.

24 in violation of this subsection.

1 12. 13. All fire departments and appropriate county officials
2 shall be allowed to place "Burn Ban" signs on agreed-upon signposts
3 controlled by the Oklahoma Department of Transportation and Oklahoma
4 Turnpike Authority (OTA). Fire departments and appropriate county
5 officials shall work with the Oklahoma Department of Transportation
6 to agree upon locations for signage. The Oklahoma Department of
7 Transportation shall develop standards that fire departments and
8 appropriate county officials shall adhere to in placing signage. It
9 shall be the responsibility of the entity placing the sign to post
10 and remove signage in a timely manner. Signage shall not obstruct
11 the view of traffic. Signage costs shall be the responsibility of
12 the fire department or county wishing to install.

14 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
15 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
16 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

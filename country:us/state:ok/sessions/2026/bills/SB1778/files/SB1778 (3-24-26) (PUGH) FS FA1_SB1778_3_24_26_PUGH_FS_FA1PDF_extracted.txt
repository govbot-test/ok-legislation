4 Section 1210.508B. A. The Legislature finds that it is
5 essential for children in the public schools to read early and well
6 in elementary school. The Legislature further finds that clear and
7 visible goals, assessments to determine the reading level at each
8 elementary school, use of a scientifically based and researched
9 methodology in reading instruction in addition to regular and
10 periodic measurements of elementary school reading improvement, and
11 accountability in each level of the educational system will result
12 in a significant increase in the number of children reading at or
13 above grade level.
14 B. The purpose of the Strong Readers Act is to ensure that
15 progression from one grade to another is determined, in part, upon
16 proficiency in reading, that school district board of education
17 policies facilitate reading instruction and intervention services to
18 address student reading needs, and that each student and his or her
19 parent or legal guardian be informed of that student’s reading
20 progress. It is the intent of the Legislature that every student
21 reads at or above grade level by the end of third grade.
22 C. To carry out the purpose of this act, it is the intent of
23 the Legislature that the identification, instruction, and
24 intervention required under the Strong Readers Act be implemented
Req. No. 3844 Page 2

1 with in a multi-tiered system of supports (MTSS) framework that
2 emphasizes strong core literacy instruction for all students and
3 provides increasing levels of instructional intensity based on
4 student need and demonstrated response to instruction.
5 D. It is further the intent of the Legislature that strong core
6 literacy instruction consists of explicit, systematic instruction in
7 foundational reading and writing skills including phonological
8 awareness, decoding, encoding, spelling, sentence-level writing,
9 language development, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension,
10 recognizing the reciprocal relationship between reading and writing
11 proficiency.
12 E. Each public school district in this state shall ensure that
13 all students receive a well-rounded education that is focused on
14 building deep foundations in reading, writing, and mathematics. The
15 State Board of Education shall encourage school districts to
16 integrate the teaching of the other curricular areas in the subject
17 matter standards adopted by the Board with the instruction of
18 reading, writing, and mathematics. All teachers of reading in the
19 public schools in this state in kindergarten through third grade
20 shall incorporate into instruction the five elements of reading
21 instruction which are phonological awareness, decoding, fluency,

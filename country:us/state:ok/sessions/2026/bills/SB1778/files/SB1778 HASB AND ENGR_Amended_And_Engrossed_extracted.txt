7 Section 1210.508B. A. The Legislature finds that it is
8 essential for children in the public schools to read early and well
9 in elementary school. The Legislature further finds that clear and
10 visible goals, assessments to determine the reading level at each
11 elementary school, use of a scientifically based and researched
12 methodology in reading instruction in addition to regular and
13 periodic measurements of elementary school reading improvement, and
14 accountability in each level of the educational system will result
15 in a significant increase in the number of children reading at or
16 above grade level.
17 B. The purpose of the Strong Readers Act is to ensure that
18 progression from one grade to another is determined, in part, upon
19 proficiency in reading, that school district board of education
20 policies facilitate reading instruction and intervention services to
21 address student reading needs, and that each student and his or her
22 parent or legal guardian be informed of that student's reading
23 progress. It is the intent of the Legislature that every student
24 reads at or above grade level by the end of third grade.
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1778 Page 6
1 C. To carry out the purpose of this act, it is the intent of
2 the Legislature that the identification, instruction, and
3 intervention required under the Strong Readers Act be implemented
4 within a multi-tiered system of supports (MTSS) framework that
5 emphasizes strong core literacy instruction for all students and
6 provides increasing levels of instructional intensity based on
7 student need and demonstrated response to instruction.
8 D. It is further the intent of the Legislature that strong core
9 literacy instruction consists of explicit, systematic instruction in
10 foundational reading and writing skills including phonological
11 awareness, decoding, encoding, spelling, sentence-level writing,
12 language development, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension,
13 recognizing the reciprocal relationship between reading and writing
14 proficiency.
15 E. Each public school district in this state shall ensure that
16 all students receive a well-rounded education that is focused on
17 building deep foundations in reading, writing, and mathematics. The
18 State Board of Education shall encourage require school districts to
19 integrate the teaching of the other curricular areas in the subject
20 matter standards adopted by the Board with the instruction of
21 reading, writing, and mathematics. All teachers of reading in the
22 public schools in this state in kindergarten through third grade
23 shall incorporate into instruction the five elements of reading
24 instruction which are phonological awareness, decoding, fluency,
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1778 Page 7
1 voca bulary, and comprehension provide systematic and explicit
2 instruction in the five elements of reading, which are phonological
3 awareness, decoding, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension, using
4 high quality instructional materials grounded in the science of
5 reading, selected from a list of approved materials maintained by
6 the State Board of Education.
7 D. F. It is the intent of the Legislature that beginning with
8 the 2025-2026 school year, school districts and charter schools in
9 this state shall be prohibited from using the three-cueing system
10 model of teaching students to read. For the purposes of this
11 section, the "three-cueing system" means any model of teaching
12 students to read based on meaning, structure, syntax, and visual
13 cues, which may also be known as meaning, structure, and visual
14 (MSV), balanced literacy, or whole language.

18 Section 1210.508B. A. The Legislature finds that it is
19 essential for children in the public schools to read early and well
20 in elementary school. The Legislature further finds that clear and
21 visible goals, assessments to determine the reading level at each
22 elementary school, use of a scientifically based and researched
23 methodology in reading instruction in addition to regular and
24 periodic measurements of elementary school reading improvement, and
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1778 Page 4

1 acco untability in each level of the educational system will result
2 in a significant increase in the number of children reading at or
3 above grade level.
4 B. The purpose of the Strong Readers Act is to ensure that
5 progression from one grade to another is determined, in part, upon
6 proficiency in reading, that school district board of education
7 policies facilitate reading instruction and intervention services to
8 address student reading needs, and that each student and his or her
9 parent or legal guardian be informed of that student’s reading
10 progress. It is the intent of the Legislature that every student
11 reads at or above grade level by the end of third grade.
12 C. To carry out the purpose of this act, it is the intent of
13 the Legislature that the identification, instruction, and
14 intervention required under the Strong Readers Act be implemented
15 within a multi-tiered system of supports (MTSS) framework that
16 emphasizes strong core literacy instruction for all students and
17 provides increasing levels of instructional intensity based on
18 student need and demonstrated response to instruction.
19 D. It is further the intent of the Legislature that strong core
20 literacy instruction consists of explicit, systematic instruction in
21 foundational reading and writing skills including phonological
22 awareness, decoding, encoding, spelling, sentence-level writing,
23 language development, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension,
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1778 Page 5

1 reco gnizing the reciprocal relationship between reading and writing
2 proficiency.
3 E. Each public school district in this state shall ensure that
4 all students receive a well-rounded education that is focused on
5 building deep foundations in reading, writing, and mathematics. The
6 State Board of Education shall encourage school districts to
7 integrate the teaching of the other curricular areas in the subject
8 matter standards adopted by the Board with the instruction of
9 reading, writing, and mathematics. All teachers of reading in the
10 public schools in this state in kindergarten through third grade
11 shall incorporate into instruction the five elements of reading
12 instruction which are phonological awareness, decoding, fluency,
13 vocabulary, and comprehension.
14 D. F. It is the intent of the Legislature that beginning with
15 the 2025-2026 school year, school districts and charter schools in
16 this state shall be prohibited from using the three-cueing system
17 model of teaching students to read. For the purposes of this
18 section, the “three-cueing system” means any model of teaching
19 students to read based on meaning, structure, syntax, and visual
20 cues, which may also be known as meaning, structure, and visual
21 (MSV), balanced literacy, or whole language.

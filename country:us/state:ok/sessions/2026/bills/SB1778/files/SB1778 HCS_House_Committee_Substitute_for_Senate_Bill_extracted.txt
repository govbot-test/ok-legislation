11 Section 1210.508B. A. The Legislature finds that it is
12 essential for children in the public schools to read early and well
13 in elementary school. The Legislature further finds that clear and
14 visible goals, assessments to determine the reading level at each
15 elementary school, use of a scientifically based and researched
16 methodology in reading instruction in addition to regular and
17 periodic measurements of elementary school reading improvement, and
18 accountability in each level of the educational system will result
19 in a significant increase in the number of children reading at or
20 above grade level.
21 B. The purpose of the Strong Readers Act is to ensure that
22 progression from one grade to another is determined, in part, upon
23 proficiency in reading, that school district board of education
24 policies facilitate reading instruction and intervention services to
Req. No. 17338 Page 5
1 addr ess student reading needs, and that each student and his or her
2 parent or legal guardian be informed of that student's reading
3 progress. It is the intent of the Legislature that every student
4 reads at or above grade level by the end of third grade.
5 C. To carry out the purpose of this act, it is the intent of
6 the Legislature that the identification, instruction, and
7 intervention required under the Strong Readers Act be implemented
8 within a multi-tiered system of supports (MTSS) framework that
9 emphasizes strong core literacy instruction for all students and
10 provides increasing levels of instructional intensity based on
11 student need and demonstrated response to instruction.
12 D. It is further the intent of the Legislature that strong core
13 literacy instruction consists of explicit, systematic instruction in
14 foundational reading and writing skills including phonological
15 awareness, decoding, encoding, spelling, sentence-level writing,
16 language development, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension,
17 recognizing the reciprocal relationship between reading and writing
18 proficiency.
19 E. Each public school district in this state shall ensure that
20 all students receive a well-rounded education that is focused on
21 building deep foundations in reading, writing, and mathematics. The
22 State Board of Education shall encourage require school districts to
23 integrate the teaching of the other curricular areas in the subject
24 matter standards adopted by the Board with the instruction of

1 read ing, writing, and mathematics. All teachers of reading in the
2 public schools in this state in kindergarten through third grade
3 shall incorporate into instruction the five elements of reading
4 instruction which are phonological awareness, decoding, fluency,
5 vocabulary, and comprehension provide systematic and explicit
6 instruction in the five elements of reading, which are phonological
7 awareness, decoding, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension, using
8 high quality instructional materials grounded in the science of
9 reading, selected from a list of approved materials maintained by
10 the State Board of Education.
11 D. F. It is the intent of the Legislature that beginning with
12 the 2025-2026 school year, school districts and charter schools in
13 this state shall be prohibited from using the three-cueing system
14 model of teaching students to read. For the purposes of this
15 section, the "three-cueing system" means any model of teaching
16 students to read based on meaning, structure, syntax, and visual
17 cues, which may also be known as meaning, structure, and visual
18 (MSV), balanced literacy, or whole language.

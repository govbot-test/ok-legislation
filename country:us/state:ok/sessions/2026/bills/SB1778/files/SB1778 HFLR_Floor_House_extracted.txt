14 Section 1210.508B. A. The Legislature finds that it is
15 essential for children in the public schools to read early and well
16 in elementary school. The Legislature further finds that clear and
17 visible goals, assessments to determine the reading level at each
18 elementary school, use of a scientifically based and researched
19 methodology in reading instruction in addition to regular and
20 periodic measurements of elementary school reading improvement, and
21 accountability in each level of the educational system will result
22 in a significant increase in the number of children reading at or
23 above grade level.
24

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 B. The purpose of the Strong Readers Act is to ensure that
2 progression from one grade to another is determined, in part, upon
3 proficiency in reading, that school district board of education
4 policies facilitate reading instruction and intervention services to
5 address student reading needs, and that each student and his or her
6 parent or legal guardian be informed of that student's reading
7 progress. It is the intent of the Legislature that every student
8 reads at or above grade level by the end of third grade.
9 C. To carry out the purpose of this act, it is the intent of
10 the Legislature that the identification, instruction, and
11 intervention required under the Strong Readers Act be implemented
12 within a multi-tiered system of supports (MTSS) framework that
13 emphasizes strong core literacy instruction for all students and
14 provides increasing levels of instructional intensity based on
15 student need and demonstrated response to instruction.
16 D. It is further the intent of the Legislature that strong core
17 literacy instruction consists of explicit, systematic instruction in
18 foundational reading and writing skills including phonological
19 awareness, decoding, encoding, spelling, sentence-level writing,
20 language development, fluency, vocabulary, and comprehension,
21 recognizing the reciprocal relationship between reading and writing
22 proficiency.
23 E. Each public school district in this state shall ensure that
24 all students receive a well-rounded education that is focused on

1 buil ding deep foundations in reading, writing, and mathematics. The
2 State Board of Education shall encourage require school districts to
3 integrate the teaching of the other curricular areas in the subject

5 reading, writing, and mathematics. All teachers of reading in the
6 public schools in this state in kindergarten through third grade
7 shall incorporate into instruction the five elements of reading
8 instruction which are phonological awareness, decoding, fluency,
9 vocabulary, and comprehension provide systematic and explicit

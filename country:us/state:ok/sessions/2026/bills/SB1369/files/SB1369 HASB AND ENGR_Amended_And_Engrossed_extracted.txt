18 its successor maintained by the Assistant Secretary for Mental
19 Health and Substance Use under Section 520E–3 of the Public Health
20 Service Act, 42 U.S.C., Section 290bb-36c;
21 3. "988 Lifeline Revolving Fund" means the 988 Mental Health
22 Lifeline program fund created pursuant to Section 3 of this act;
23 4. "Behavioral health crisis services" are intensive services
24 that are provided to address or prevent behavioral health symptoms,

1 situ ations, or events that may negatively impact an individual's
2 ability to function within his or her current family or caregiver
3 and living situation, school, workplace, or community. Behavioral
4 health crisis services are for anyone, anywhere, and at any time and
5 can be provided in a variety of settings, including via text or
6 telephone, face-to-face at an individual's home, or in the
7 community;
8 5. "Behavioral health crisis system" is an organized set of
9 structures, processes, and services in place to meet all types of
10 urgent and emerging mental health and substance use needs in a
11 defined population or community, effectively and efficiently.
12 Essential elements of a behavioral health crisis system include 988
13 crisis lines that accept all calls and texts, and provide support
14 and referrals based on the needs of the individual or family member
15 or caregiver; mobile crisis teams that respond to the location of
16 need in the community; and crisis stabilization facilities that
17 serve everyone who enters its doors from all referral sources.
18 Comprehensive behavioral health crisis systems:
19 a. address recovery needs, significant use of peers, and
20 trauma-informed care,
21 b. ensure safety and security for staff and those in
22 crisis, and
23 c. involve collaboration with law enforcement and
24 emergency medical services;

1 6. "Certified community behavioral health clinics (CCBHC)" are

14 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 12-201 of Title 43A, unless
16 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. The State of Oklahoma shall designate the Department of
18 Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services to have primary oversight
19 over the suicide prevention and crisis service activities and
20 essential coordination with designated 988 Lifeline Crisis Centers
21 to provide crisis intervention services and crisis care coordination
22 to individuals accessing the 988 Mental Health Lifeline program from
23 any jurisdiction within Oklahoma twenty-four (24) hours a day, seven
24 (7) days a week.
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1369 Page 5

1 B. The Oklahoma Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse
2 Services (ODMHSAS) shall have primary oversight of suicide
3 prevention and crisis service activities.
4 C. The designated 988 Lifeline Crisis Centers shall meet 988
5 Lifeline Program requirements and best practices guidelines for
6 operational, performance and clinical standards.
7 D. The ODMHSAS shall use its authority to promulgate rules and
8 regulations to allow appropriate information sharing and
9 communication between and across crisis and emergency response
10 systems for the purpose of real-time crisis care coordination
11 including, but not limited to, deployment of crisis and outgoing
12 services and linked, flexible services specific to crisis response.
13 E. The state or related public health authority shall assure
14 active collaborations and coordination of service linkages between
15 the designated centers and crisis receiving and stabilization
16 services for individuals accessing the 988 Lifeline through
17 appropriate information sharing regarding availability of services.
18 F. The state or related public health authority and designated
19 988 Lifeline Crisis Centers shall utilize technology to enhance
20 communication and coordination in the delivery of behavioral health
21 crisis services, and for data collection, analysis and sharing.

1 There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving fund
2 for the Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services to
3 be designated the "988 Lifeline Revolving Fund". The fund shall be
4 a continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year limitations, and shall
5 consist of all monies received by the Department as directed by law,
6 or by grants or gifts to support the Lifeline. All monies accruing
7 to the credit of said fund are hereby appropriated and may be
8 budgeted and expended by the Department of Mental Health and
9 Substance Abuse Services for the purpose of staffing, supporting,
10 and operating the 988 Lifeline system created and maintained in the
11 National Suicide Hotline Designation Act of 2020, the Federal
12 Communication Commission's rules adopted July 16, 2020, and October
13 17, 2024, and the National Guidelines for a Behavioral Health
14 Coordinated System of Crisis Care. Expenditures from said fund
15 shall be made upon warrants issued by the State Treasurer against
16 claims filed as prescribed by law with the Director of the Office of
17 Management and Enterprise Services for approval and payment.

11 An Act relating to general provisions; creating the
Preserving Oklahoma Values Act of 2026; providing
12 short title; providing legislative intent; defining
terms; declaring certain decision of courts void and
13 unenforceable; declaring certain contracts void and
unenforceable; providing exception; construing
14 provision; providing for noncodification; providing
for codification; and declaring an emergency.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
19 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
20 A. This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Preserving
21 Oklahoma Values Act of 2026”.
22 B. It is the intent of the Legislature that it shall be the
23 policy of this state to protect its citizens from the application of
24 laws that are contrary to the provisions of the Oklahoma

1 Cons titution, the United States Constitution, or the shared values
2 of Judeo-Christian Western civilization.
3 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2010 of Title 25, unless there
5 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 A. As used in this section:
7 1. “Court” means any court, board, tribunal, administrative
8 agency, or other adjudicative or enforcement authority of this state
9 or any of its political subdivisions;
10 2. “Foreign law, legal code, or system” means any law, legal
11 code, or system of a jurisdiction outside of any state or territory
12 of the United States, inclusive of the laws, legal codes, and
13 systems of international organizations or tribunals, applied by that
14 jurisdiction’s courts, administrative bodies, or other formal or
15 informal tribunals that are contrary to Western values. This shall
16 not be construed as applying to the laws of any Native American
17 tribes within this state;
18 3. “Religious organization” means a church, seminary,
19 synagogue, temple, mosque, religious order, religious corporation,
20 association, or society with an identity that is distinctive in
21 terms of a common religious creed, beliefs, doctrines, practices, or
22 rituals of any faith or denomination, including any organization
23 qualifying as a church or religious organization under the
24
SB1679 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 prov isions of 26 U.S.C., Section 501(c)(3) or 26 U.S.C., Section
2 501(d); and
3 4. “Judeo-Christian Western rights and values” means a system
4 of values which recognizes:
5 a. the right to due process,
6 b. the right to equal protection under the law,
7 c. freedom of religion,
8 d. freedom of speech,
9 e. freedom of the press,
10 f. the right to keep and bear arms,
11 g. the right to privacy, and
12 h. the right to marry, as “marriage” is defined by the
13 Oklahoma Constitution, to the extent that such
14 definition does not conflict with federal law or a
15 holding by the United States Supreme Court.
16 B. Any court ruling or decision, if such ruling or decision is
17 based in whole or in part on any foreign law, legal code, or system,
18 shall be void and unenforceable if such ruling violates any Judeo-
19 Christian Western rights and values.
20 C. Any contract or contractual provision, if such would allow
21 for a foreign law, legal code, or system to govern all or some of
22 the disputes arising between the parties adjudicated by a court or
23 law or by an arbitration panel arising from the mutually agreed
24 contract, shall be void and unenforceable if such foreign law, legal
SB1679 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 code , or system does not grant the parties any Judeo-Christian
2 Western rights and values.
3 D. Any contract or contractual provision, if such provides for
4 a jurisdiction for purposes of granting the courts or arbitration
5 panels personal jurisdiction over the parties to adjudicate any
6 disputes between parties arising from the mutually agreed contract,
7 shall be void and unenforceable if such jurisdiction would apply any
8 foreign law, legal code, or system to the dispute at issue that is
9 contrary to any Judeo-Christian Western rights and values.
10 E. This section shall not apply to a corporation, partnership,
11 limited liability company, business association, or other legal
12 entity that contracts to subject itself to a foreign law, legal
13 code, or system.
14 F. 1. The provisions of this section shall not be construed to
15 limit the right of any person in this state to the free exercise of
16 religion as guaranteed by the provisions of the Oklahoma
17 Constitution and the First Amendment to the United States
18 Constitution.
19 2. The provisions of this section shall not be construed to
20 require or authorize a court to adjudicate or prohibit any religious
21 organization from adjudicating ecclesiastical matters, including but
22 not limited to the election, appointment, calling, discipline,
23 dismissal, removal, or excommunication of a member, officer,
24 official, priest, nun, monk, pastor, rabbi, imam, or member of the

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 cler gy of such organization, or the determination or interpretation
2 of the doctrine of such organization if such adjudication would
3 violate the provisions of the Oklahoma Constitution and the First
4 Amendment to the United States Constitution.
5 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
6 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
7 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
8 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

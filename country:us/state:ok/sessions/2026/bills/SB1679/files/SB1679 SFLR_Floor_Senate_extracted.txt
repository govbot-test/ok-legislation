3 SENATE BILL NO. 1679 By: Hamilton, Haste, Bullard,
and Thompson
4
5
6 An Act relating to general provisions; creating the
Preserving Oklahoma Values Act of 2026; providing
7 short title; providing legislative intent; defining
terms; declaring certain decision of courts void and
8 unenforceable; declaring certain contracts void and
unenforceable; providing exception; construing
9 provision; providing for noncodification; providing
for codification; and declaring an emergency.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
14 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
15 A. This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Preserving
16 Oklahoma Values Act of 2026”.
17 B. It is the intent of the Legislature that it shall be the
18 policy of this state to protect its citizens from the application of
19 laws that are contrary to the provisions of the Oklahoma
20 Constitution, the United States Constitution, or the shared values
21 of Judeo-Christian Western civilization.
22 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2010 of Title 25, unless there
24 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:

1 A. As used in this section:
2 1. “Court” means any court, board, tribunal, administrative
3 agency, or other adjudicative or enforcement authority of this state
4 or any of its political subdivisions;
5 2. “Foreign law, legal code, or system” means any law, legal
6 code, or system of a jurisdiction outside of any state or territory
7 of the United States, inclusive of the laws, legal codes, and
8 systems of international organizations or tribunals, applied by that
9 jurisdiction’s courts, administrative bodies, or other formal or
10 informal tribunals that are contrary to Western values. This shall
11 not be construed as applying to the laws of any Native American
12 tribes within this state;
13 3. “Religious organization” means a church, seminary,
14 synagogue, temple, mosque, religious order, religious corporation,
15 association, or society with an identity that is distinctive in
16 terms of a common religious creed, beliefs, doctrines, practices, or
17 rituals of any faith or denomination, including any organization
18 qualifying as a church or religious organization under the
19 provisions of 26 U.S.C., Section 501(c)(3) or 26 U.S.C., Section
20 501(d); and
21 4. “Judeo-Christian Western rights and values” means a system
22 of values which recognizes:
23 a. the right to due process,
24 b. the right to equal protection under the law,

1 c. freedom of religion,
2 d. freedom of speech,
3 e. freedom of the press,
4 f. the right to keep and bear arms,
5 g. the right to privacy, and
6 h. the right to marry, as “marriage” is defined by the
7 Oklahoma Constitution, to the extent that such
8 definition does not conflict with federal law or a
9 holding by the United States Supreme Court.
10 B. Any court ruling or decision, if such ruling or decision is
11 based in whole or in part on any foreign law, legal code, or system,
12 shall be void and unenforceable if such ruling violates any Judeo-
13 Christian Western rights and values.
14 C. Any contract or contractual provision, if such would allow
15 for a foreign law, legal code, or system to govern all or some of
16 the disputes arising between the parties adjudicated by a court or
17 law or by an arbitration panel arising from the mutually agreed
18 contract, shall be void and unenforceable if such foreign law, legal
19 code, or system does not grant the parties any Judeo-Christian
20 Western rights and values.
21 D. Any contract or contractual provision, if such provides for
22 a jurisdiction for purposes of granting the courts or arbitration
23 panels personal jurisdiction over the parties to adjudicate any
24 disputes between parties arising from the mutually agreed contract,

1 shal l be void and unenforceable if such jurisdiction would apply any
2 foreign law, legal code, or system to the dispute at issue that is
3 contrary to any Judeo-Christian Western rights and values.
4 E. This section shall not apply to a corporation, partnership,
5 limited liability company, business association, or other legal
6 entity that contracts to subject itself to a foreign law, legal
7 code, or system.
8 F. 1. The provisions of this section shall not be construed to
9 limit the right of any person in this state to the free exercise of
10 religion as guaranteed by the provisions of the Oklahoma
11 Constitution and the First Amendment to the United States
12 Constitution.
13 2. The provisions of this section shall not be construed to
14 require or authorize a court to adjudicate or prohibit any religious
15 organization from adjudicating ecclesiastical matters, including but
16 not limited to the election, appointment, calling, discipline,
17 dismissal, removal, or excommunication of a member, officer,
18 official, priest, nun, monk, pastor, rabbi, imam, or member of the
19 clergy of such organization, or the determination or interpretation
20 of the doctrine of such organization if such adjudication would
21 violate the provisions of the Oklahoma Constitution and the First
22 Amendment to the United States Constitution.

24 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby

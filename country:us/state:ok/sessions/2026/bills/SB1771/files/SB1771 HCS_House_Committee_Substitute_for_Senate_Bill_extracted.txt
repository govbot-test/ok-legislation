11 An Act relating to the Oklahoma Workforce Commission;
amending Section 5, Chapter 361, O.S.L. 2023, as
12 amended by Section 1, Chapter 408, O.S.L. 2025 (40
O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 904), which relates to the
13 powers, duties, and responsibilities of the
Commission; permitting the Commission to collect
14 certain data; authorizing the Commission to employ
outside counsel; and providing an effective date.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 5, Chapter 361, O.S.L.
19 2023, as amended by Section 1, Chapter 408, O.S.L. 2025 (40 O.S.
20 Supp. 2025, Section 904), is amended to read as follows:
21 Section 904. The Oklahoma Workforce Commission shall have the
22 following powers, duties, and responsibilities:
23 1. To administer the provisions of the Oklahoma Workforce
24 Transformation Act or any duty as directed by statute;
Req. No. 17444 Page 1
1 2. To establish an office for the Commission within this state;
2 3. To elect whatever other officers may be necessary to direct
3 operations of the Commission;
4 4. To employ or contract for personnel and administrative
5 support as the Commission deems appropriate to carry out the purpose
6 and provisions of the Oklahoma Workforce Transformation Act,
7 including, but not limited to, a nonprofit corporation to carry out
8 the Commission’s objectives, an executive director or other chief
9 executive officer, and any other employees or contractors the
10 Commission deems appropriate, and to prescribe their duties and fix
11 their compensation;
12 5. To administer the Workforce Coordination Revolving Fund
13 created in Section 255.4 of Title 62 of the Oklahoma Statutes;
14 6. To administer the Oklahoma Workforce Commission Revolving
15 Fund created in Section 3 of this act;
16 7. To approve or disapprove the budget of the Commission;
17 8. To promulgate rules to carry out the provisions of the
18 Oklahoma Workforce Transformation Act;
19 9. To enter into contracts or agreements for studies,
20 professional services, grant administration and procurement,
21 research projects, supplies, or any other services the Commission
22 deems necessary to carry out its purpose, and to incur those
23 expenses necessary to carry out those purposes;
24
Req. No. 17444 Page 2
1 10. To cooperate with any private, local, state, or national
2 commission, organization, agency, or group and to make contracts and
3 agreements for joint programs beneficial to Oklahoma’s workforce
4 development efforts;
5 11. To accept donations, grants, contributions, and gifts from
6 any public or private source and deposit such in the Oklahoma
7 Workforce Commission Revolving Fund, created in Section 3 of this
8 act;
9 12. To oversee and direct to the extent permitted by law the
10 activities and administration of the Governor’s Council for
11 Workforce and Economic Development, including, but not limited to,
12 the creation and submission of state plans to the federal government
13 pursuant to the provisions of the Workforce Innovation and
14 Opportunity Act;
15 13. To collect data from federal, state, and local agencies,
16 departments, educational institutions, boards, commissions, or any
17 other entities including, but not limited to:
18 a. number of participants in workforce development
19 programming,
20 b. cost per participant,
21 c. wages before and after participation in programs,
22 d. degree or certificate conferred on each participant,
23 e. number of jobs open needing degrees or certificates,
24 f. labor force and economic information, and
Req. No. 17444 Page 3
1 g. funds allocated for the purpose of workforce
2 development,
3 h. expenditures made for the purpose of workforce
4 development,
5 i. performance data, and
6 j. any other information the Commission deems appropriate
7 to guide funding decisions, facilitate its work or the
8 work of workforce development entities in the state,
9 or to understand the impact of the state’s workforce
10 efforts and programming;
11 14. To create and maintain a public dashboard and create
12 reports with data concerning workforce development efforts in the
13 state. All state and local agencies, departments, educational
14 institutions, boards, commissions, and other public entities shall
15 cooperate with information and data requests from the Commission or
16 its designees. The Oklahoma Workforce Commission shall serve as the
17 centralized data and information repository for workforce
18 development activities for the state;
19 15. To make recommendations to the Legislature to improve
20 workforce development programs in the state;
21 16. To implement programs designed to expand workforce
22 development; and
23
24
Req. No. 17444 Page 4
1 17. To work with, use and provide data the legislatively
2 created map developed to help make decisions on economic development
3 and workforce needs in the state.
4 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

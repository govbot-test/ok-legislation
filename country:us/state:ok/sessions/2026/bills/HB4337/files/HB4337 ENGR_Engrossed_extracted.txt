7 [ revenue and taxation - Oklahoma Quality Events Act
8 – definition - effective date ]
9
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 68 O.S. 2021, Section 4303, is
14 amended to read as follows:
15 Section 4303. As used in the Oklahoma Quality Events Incentive
16 Act:
17 1. "Certified sponsor" means an entity or organization
18 authorized to promote and conduct a quality event, which is
19 incurring expenses for the promotion of such event to be conducted
20 within the corporate limits of an eligible municipality or an
21 unincorporated area within a county;
22 2. "Economic impact study" means a study which includes:
23 a. a description and, if applicable, history of the
24 quality event,
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4337 Page 1
1 b. information regarding the site selection process for
2 the quality event,
3 c. an estimate of the expenses anticipated to be incurred
4 in connection with hosting the quality event,
5 d. an estimate of the total gross sales made by vendors
6 during any period of time during which no quality
7 event activity occurs,
8 e. a detailed estimate of the anticipated increase in
9 sales tax revenue directly attributable to the quality
10 event, and
11 f. the general economic impact likely to occur as a
12 result of the preparation for, occurrence of and
13 activity occurring in connection with the dissolution
14 of, a quality event;
15 3. "Eligible local support amounts" means:
16 a. any payment made by a local government entity or
17 transfer of monies from the general fund or transfer
18 of tax revenues derived from a locally imposed tax to
19 a certified sponsor for the purpose of attracting,
20 promoting, advertising, organizing, conducting or
21 otherwise supporting a quality event, or
22 b. any direct payment made by a certified sponsor to a
23 for-profit or nonprofit entity, other than the host
24 community, for the purpose of attracting, promoting,
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4337 Page 2
1 advertising, organizing, conducting or otherwise
2 supporting a quality event;
3 4. "Event history" means:
4 a. historical information on the event including past
5 locations of the event,
6 b. a description of previous attempts by the host
7 community to secure the event,
8 c. information regarding attempts by other communities to
9 recruit the event, and
10 d. if applicable, the competitive bidding process for
11 securing the event by the host community;
12 5. "Host community" means any county, incorporated city or
13 town, or any combination of counties, incorporated cities or towns
14 of the state which are authorized by their respective governing
15 bodies to host or assist in the presentation of a quality event;
16 6. "Incremental sales tax revenue" means the amount of
17 additional state sales tax revenue collected as a result of the
18 quality event, as determined by an economic impact study verified by
19 the Oklahoma Tax Commission;
20 7. "New event" means a quality event which did not occur within
21 a period of twenty-four (24) months prior to the month during which
22 a quality event is held;
23 8. "Quality event" means:
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4337 Page 3
1 a. a new event or a meeting of a nationally recognized
2 organization or its members,
3 b. a new or existing event that is a national,
4 international or world championship, or
5 c. a new or existing event that is managed or produced by
6 an Oklahoma-based national or international
7 organization, or
8 d. a nationally televised event by a nationally
9 recognized sanctioned body;
10 9. "Recurring event" means a quality event which occurred at
11 least once within the twenty-four (24) months prior to the month
12 during which a quality event is held;
13 10. "State sales tax revenue" means the proceeds from the state
14 sales tax levy imposed pursuant to Section 1354 of this title upon
15 taxable transactions occurring as a result of the quality event, as
16 determined by an economic impact study verified by the Oklahoma Tax
17 Commission; and
18 11. "Vendors" means those persons or business entities making
19 taxable sales of tangible personal property or services as a result
20 of the quality event, as determined by an economic impact study
21 verified by the Oklahoma Tax Commission and, unless the context
22 otherwise requires, shall have the same meaning as defined by
23 Section 1352 of this title.
24 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

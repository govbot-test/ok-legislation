13 Section 6-122.12. A. Public school districts in this state may
14 employ adjunct teachers who have a minimum of a high school diploma
15 and are persons with distinguished qualifications in their field.
16 The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules providing
17 distinguished qualifications for adjunct teachers.
18 B. 1. Beginning with the 2027-2028 school year, adjunct
19 teachers shall be prohibited from teaching core curriculum subjects
20 including, but not limited to, mathematics, English language arts,
21 science, and social studies in pre-kindergarten through fifth grade.
22 2. If an adjunct teacher was employed by a public school

24 through fifth grade prior to the 2027-2028 school year, the public

2 waiver to continue the adjunct teacher’s employment. To be eligible
3 for a waiver, the adjunct teacher shall be required to complete or
4 shall have completed professional development training in the
5 science of reading provided by the State Department of Education.
6 If the Board grants the waiver, the public school district may
7 continue to employ the adjunct teacher to teach core curriculum
8 subjects in pre-kindergarten through fifth grade, subject to the
9 limitation provided for in subsection C of this section.
10 C. Beginning with the 2027-2028 school year, public school
11 districts shall be prohibited from employing an adjunct teacher for
12 more than five (5) years unless the employing public school district
13 applies to the State Board of Education for a waiver. The State
14 Department of Education shall develop a waiver application, which
15 shall include a requirement to submit the adjunct teacher’s
16 distinguished qualifications. The Board may grant a waiver allowing
17 the adjunct teacher to be employed for an additional five (5) years.
18 D. A person employed as an adjunct teacher pursuant to this
19 section who does not hold a valid certificate to teach shall not be
20 considered a teacher as defined by Section 1-116 of this title.
21 E. The State Department of Education shall publish an annual
22 report on the status of adjunct teachers, authorized by Section 6-
23 122.3 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes this title, in this
24 state. The report shall include:

3 2. The subject areas or courses in which adjunct teachers are
4 teaching; and
5 3. The types of distinguished qualifications adjunct teachers
6 have to make them qualified to teach in certain subject areas or
7 courses.
8 B. F. The annual report shall be published on the website of
9 the State Department of Education and copies of the report shall be
10 provided to elected members of the Oklahoma House of Representatives
11 and the Oklahoma State Senate no later than January 15 of each year.

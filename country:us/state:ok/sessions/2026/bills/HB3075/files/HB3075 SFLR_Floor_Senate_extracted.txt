8 An Act relating to public finance; enacting the
Oklahoma Common Cents Act; providing for system of
9 rounding with respect to certain cash payments;
prescribing method for rounding; providing for
10 permissive computations; prescribing date for
mandatory computations; providing for exclusion of
11 certain transactions; providing for payment of
certain shortages; authorizing certain actions by
12 political subdivisions; requiring entities of this
state and certain entities to round the price of
13 certain transactions paid with cash; prescribing
procedure for rounding; providing for exclusion of
14 certain transactions; providing for noncodification;
providing for codification; and providing an
15 effective date.
16
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
20 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
21 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Oklahoma Common
22 Cents Act".
23
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3075 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 9002 of Title 62, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. Unless actions by the United States Congress direct
5 otherwise, from the effective date of this act until July 1, 2027,
6 any political subdivision may round final amounts owed to the entity
7 as follows if the payment is made in the form of cash so that an
8 amount ending in:
9 1. One cent ($0.01) or two cents ($0.02) is rounded down to
10 zero (0);
11 2. Three cents ($0.03) or four cents ($0.04) is rounded up to
12 five cents ($0.05);
13 3. Six cents ($0.06) or seven cents ($0.07) is rounded down to
14 five cents ($0.05); and
15 4. Eight cents ($0.08) or nine cents ($0.09) is rounded up to
16 ten cents ($0.10).
17 B. The provisions of subsection A of this section shall be
18 mandatory beginning July 1, 2027.
19 C. The provisions of this section shall not apply to any
20 payment method such as a check, debit card, credit card, electronic
21 or digital payment, or other similar form of payment, but shall only
22 apply to payments made using cash or the portion paid in cash.
23
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3075 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 D. For payment of ad valorem taxes, the overage or underage of
2 cash payments shall be accounted for from the county's portion of
3 the unappropriated revenue for the general fund.
4 E. Any political subdivision of the state may account for any
5 over or under cash payments from a fund under its control as
6 determined by the governing body of the political subdivision.
7 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
8 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3120 of Title 74, unless there
9 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
10 A. For any agency, constitutionally or statutorily created
11 state board, bureau, commission, office, authority, public trust in
12 which the state is a beneficiary, or interstate commission accepting
13 cash as payment from an individual, such entity shall adjust the
14 price, fee, penalty, or any other charge, excluding the payment of
15 any tax levy, by rounding the total amount of the transaction as
16 provided by subsection B of this section.
17 B. Final amounts owed to the entity shall be rounded as follows
18 if the payment is made in the form of cash so that an amount ending
19 in:
20 1. One cent ($0.01) or two cents ($0.02) is rounded down to
21 zero (0);
22 2. Three cents ($0.03) or four cents ($0.04) is rounded up to
23 five cents ($0.05);
24

5 C. The provisions of this section shall not apply to any

7 or digital payment, or other similar form of payment, but shall only

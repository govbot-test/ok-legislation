15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6093 of Title 36, unless there
18 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 It shall be unlawful for any insurer to increase a motor vehicle
20 liability insurance policy premium based solely on the removal by a
21 named insured of a deceased spouse from the policy. The removal of
22 a deceased spouse shall not be treated as a material change in risk
23 for purposes of underwriting, rating classification, or premium
24 calculation. Nothing in this section shall be construed as to
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3802 Page 1
1 prev ent insurers from increasing premiums at renewal based on either
2 documented changes in any actuarially supported risk factors not
3 otherwise prohibited by law, including but not limited to driving
4 record, claims history, coverage elections, mileage, garaging
5 location, or other actuarially supported risk factors; or rate
6 changes approved by the Oklahoma Insurance Department that are
7 applied uniformly to a class of insureds. Any premium increase
8 imposed in violation of this section shall be void and
9 unenforceable, and the insurer shall refund any excess premium
10 collected.
11 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
12 Passed the House of Representatives the 24th day of March, 2026.

6 An Act relating to applied behavior analysis;
amending 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1928, which relates to
7 licensing of behavior analysts; modifying
definitions; transferring certain powers and duties
8 from the Department of Human Services to the State
Board of Examiners of Psychologists; granting certain
9 additional power to the Board; requiring a national
criminal history record check for applicants;
10 prohibiting certain dissemination of information;
updating statutory language and references; modifying
11 certain fee limit; and providing an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1928, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 1928. A. As used in this act section and Sections 1-
18 280.1, 1-280.2, and 1-280.3 of Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes:
19 1. a. “Applied behavior analysis” means the design,
20 implementation, and evaluation of instructional and
21 environmental modifications to produce socially
22 significant improvements in human behavior through
23 skill, acquisition, and the reduction of problematic
24 behavior. An applied behavior analysis program shall:
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1557 Page 1
1 (1) be based on empirical research including the
2 direct observation and measurement of behavior as
3 well as a functional behavior assessment, and
4 (2) utilize antecedent stimuli, positive
5 reinforcement, and other consequences to produce
6 behavior change.
7 b. Applied behavior analysis does not include cognitive
8 therapies or psychological testing, neuropsychology,
9 psychotherapy, sex therapy, psychoanalysis,
10 hypnotherapy, and long-term counseling as treatment
11 modalities;
12 2. “Board” means the nationally accredited Behavior Analyst
13 Certification Board State Board of Examiners of Psychologists;
14 3. “Certified assistant behavior analyst” means an individual
15 who is certified by the national Behavior Analyst Certification
16 Board as a Board Certified Assistant Behavior Analyst and certified
17 by the State Board of Examiners of Psychologists;
18 4. “Human services professional” means an individual licensed
19 or certified by the state as one of the following:
20 a. a licensed physical therapist or physical therapist
21 assistant pursuant to Sections 887.1 through 887.18
22 887.19 of Title 59 of the Oklahoma Statutes this
23 title,
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1557 Page 2
1 b. an occupational therapist, occupational therapy
2 assistant, or occupational therapy aide pursuant to
3 Sections 888.1 through 888.15 of Title 59 of the
4 Oklahoma Statutes this title,
5 c. a licensed clinical social worker, licensed masters
6 social worker, or social work associate pursuant to
7 Sections 1250 through 1273 of Title 59 of the Oklahoma
8 Statutes this title,
9 d. a psychologist or health service psychologist pursuant
10 to Sections 1351 through 1376 of Title 59 of the
11 Oklahoma Statutes this title,
12 e. a licensed speech pathologist, licensed audiologist,
13 speech-language pathologist or audiologist pursuant to
14 Sections 1601 through 1622 of Title 59 of the Oklahoma
15 Statutes this title,
16 f. a licensed professional counselor or licensed
17 professional counselor candidate pursuant to Sections
18 1901 through 1920 of Title 59 of the Oklahoma Statutes
19 this title,
20 g. a licensed marital and family therapist or licensed
21 marital and family therapist candidate pursuant to
22 Sections 1925.1 through 1925.18 of Title 59 of the
23 Oklahoma Statutes this title, or
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1557 Page 3
1 h. a licensed behavioral practitioner or licensed
2 behavioral practitioner candidate pursuant to Sections
3 1930 through 1949.1 of Title 59 of the Oklahoma
4 Statutes this title;
5 4. “Certified assistant behavior analyst” means an individual
6 who is certified by the nationally accredited Behavior Analyst
7 Certification Board as a Board-Certified Assistant Behavior Analyst
8 and certified by the Developmental Disabilities Services Division of
9 the Department of Human Services;
10 5. “Licensed behavior analyst” means an individual who is
11 certified by the nationally accredited national Behavior Analyst
12 Certification Board as a Board-Certified Board Certified Behavior
13 Analyst and licensed by the Developmental Disabilities Services
14 Division of the Department State Board of Examiners of
15 Psychologists; and
16 6. “Supervisee” means a person who acts under the extended
17 authority of a licensed behavior analyst to provide applied behavior
18 analysis services or a person who is in training to provide such
19 services.
20 B. Each person wishing to practice as a licensed behavior
21 analyst or a certified assistant behavior analyst shall apply to the
22 Developmental Disabilities Services Division of the Department of
23 Human Services State Board of Examiners of Psychologists using a
24 form and in a manner prescribed by the Division Board and shall
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1557 Page 4
1 furn ish evidence satisfactory to the Division Board that such
2 person:
3 1. Is of good moral character;
4 2. Is at least twenty-one (21) years of age;
5 3. Has passed the Board applicable examination and is certified
6 by the national Behavior Analyst Certification Board as a Board-
7 Certified Board Certified Behavior Analyst or a Board-Certified
8 Board Certified Assistant Behavior Analyst, as applicable;
9 4. Has not had a professional license or certification refused,
10 revoked, suspended, or restricted and does not have a complaint,
11 allegation, or investigation pending in any regulatory jurisdiction
12 in the United States or in another country for reasons that relate
13 to unprofessional conduct unless the Division State Board of
14 Examiners of Psychologists finds, to its satisfaction, that the
15 conduct has been corrected or that mitigating circumstances exist
16 that prevent its resolution; and
17 5. Has at least the minimum graduate or undergraduate degree,
18 appropriate for the level of certification, from an accredited
19 institution of higher learning in a qualifying field of study, as
20 determined by the national Behavior Analyst Certification Board.
21 C. The State Board of Examiners of Psychologists shall have
22 exclusive power and authority to determine the qualifications and
23 fitness of all applicants for admission to practice as a licensed
24 behavior analyst or certified assistant behavior analyst in this
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1557 Page 5
1 stat e. The Board shall require that each applicant submit to a
2 national criminal history record check as defined in Section 150.9
3 of Title 74 of the Oklahoma Statutes. The Board shall not
4 disseminate criminal history record information resulting from the
5 record check.
6 D. A person holding a state license or state certification
7 shall apply for renewal of the state license or state certification
8 on or before April 30 of each odd-numbered year. The application
9 shall be accompanied by a renewal fee to be set by the Division
10 State Board of Examiners of Psychologists in accordance with
11 paragraph 3 of subsection F G of this section.
12 D. E. A person licensed or certified by the Developmental
13 Disabilities Services Division of the Department Board under this
14 section shall:
15 1. Maintain active status and fulfill all requirements for
16 renewal of national certification or recertification with by the
17 national Behavior Analyst Certification Board; and
18 2. Conduct professional activities in accordance with accepted
19 standards such as the Guidelines for Responsible Conduct and
20 Professional Disciplinary Standards Ethics Code for Behavior
21 Analysts of the national Behavior Analyst Certification Board.
22 E. F. 1. No person shall claim the title of licensed behavior
23 analyst or certified assistant behavior analyst unless that person
24 meets the applicable requirements in this section. No person shall
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1557 Page 6
1 prac tice applied behavior analysis without obtaining a license or
2 certification in accordance with this section. Supervisees may only
3 provide applied behavior analysis under the supervision of a
4 licensed behavior analyst. This section shall not restrict the
5 practice of applied behavior analysis by human services
6 professionals, provided such individuals are working within the
7 scope of their professions and the practice of applied behavior
8 analysis is commensurate with their level of training and
9 experience.
10 2. A violation of this subsection shall be punishable by a fine
11 of not more than Five Hundred Dollars ($500.00), the suspension or
12 revocation of a license licensure or certification issued pursuant
13 to this section, or both such fine and loss of licensure or
14 certification.
15 F. G. The Division State Board of Examiners of Psychologists
16 shall:
17 1. Investigate all complaints relating to:
18 a. the practice or supervision of applied behavior
19 analysis by any person licensed by the Developmental
20 Disabilities Services Division of the Department Board
21 as a behavior analyst or certified by the Division
22 Board as an assistant behavior analyst, or
23
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1557 Page 7
1 b. any person alleged to be practicing or providing
2 supervision without a state license or state
3 certification;
4 2. Refer any substantiated complaints to the national Behavior
5 Analyst Certification Board; and
6 3. Charge reasonable fees for issuance or renewal of a license
7 or for certification, not to exceed One Hundred Dollars ($100.00) in
8 an amount sufficient to cover the cost and expense incurred by the
9 State Board of Examiners of Psychologists for such issuance or
10 renewal.
11 G. H. 1. A person having a qualifying degree, as provided for
12 in paragraph 5 of subsection B of this section, and participating in
13 the applied behavior analysis treatment pilot project established in
14 Section 3 of this act Section 1-280.2 of Title 63 of the Oklahoma
15 Statutes shall be exempt from the requirements of this section while
16 such person is actively participating in the project.
17 2. Persons employed by a school district in this state who
18 provide services solely to the school district under the Individuals
19 with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), 20 U.S.C., Section 1400 et
20 seq., shall be exempt from the requirements of this section.
21 H. I. The Department State Board of Examiners of Psychologists
22 shall promulgate rules to implement the provisions of this section.
23 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

9 An Act relating to applied behavior analysis;
amending 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1928, which relates to
10 licensing of behavior analysts; modifying
definitions; transferring certain powers and duties
11 from the Department of Human Services to the State
Board of Examiners of Psychologists; granting certain
12 additional power to the Board; requiring a national
criminal history record check for applicants;
13 prohibiting certain dissemination of information;
updating statutory language and references; modifying
14 certain fee limit; and providing an effective date.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1928, is
19 amended to read as follows:
20 Section 1928. A. As used in this act section and Sections 1-
21 280.1, 1-280.2, and 1-280.3 of Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes:
22 1. a. “Applied behavior analysis” means the design,
23 implementation, and evaluation of instructional and
24 environmental modifications to produce socially

1 significant improvements in human behavior through
2 skill, acquisition, and the reduction of problematic
3 behavior. An applied behavior analysis program shall:
4 (1) be based on empirical research including the
5 direct observation and measurement of behavior as
6 well as a functional behavior assessment, and
7 (2) utilize antecedent stimuli, positive
8 reinforcement, and other consequences to produce
9 behavior change.
10 b. Applied behavior analysis does not include cognitive
11 therapies or psychological testing, neuropsychology,
12 psychotherapy, sex therapy, psychoanalysis,
13 hypnotherapy, and long-term counseling as treatment
14 modalities;
15 2. “Board” means the nationally accredited Behavior Analyst
16 Certification Board State Board of Examiners of Psychologists;
17 3. “Certified assistant behavior analyst” means an individual
18 who is certified by the national Behavior Analyst Certification
19 Board as a Board Certified Assistant Behavior Analyst and certified
20 by the State Board of Examiners of Psychologists;
21 4. “Human services professional” means an individual licensed
22 or certified by the state as one of the following:
23 a. a licensed physical therapist or physical therapist
24 assistant pursuant to Sections 887.1 through 887.18

5 Sections 888.1 through 888.15 of Title 59 of the
6 Oklahoma Statutes this title,
7 c. a licensed clinical social worker, licensed masters
8 social worker, or social work associate pursuant to
9 Sections 1250 through 1273 of Title 59 of the Oklahoma
10 Statutes this title,

10 and certified by the Developmental Disabilities Services Division of

16 Division of the Department State Board of Examiners of
17 Psychologists; and
18 6. “Supervisee” means a person who acts under the extended
19 authority of a licensed behavior analyst to provide applied behavior
20 analysis services or a person who is in training to provide such
21 services.

23 analyst or a certified assistant behavior analyst shall apply to the
24 Developmental Disabilities Services Division of the Department of

1 Huma n Services State Board of Examiners of Psychologists using a
2 form and in a manner prescribed by the Division Board and shall
3 furnish evidence satisfactory to the Division Board that such
4 person:
5 1. Is of good moral character;
6 2. Is at least twenty-one (21) years of age;
7 3. Has passed the Board applicable examination and is certified
8 by the national Behavior Analyst Certification Board as a Board-
9 Certified Board Certified Behavior Analyst or a Board-Certified
10 Board Certified Assistant Behavior Analyst, as applicable;
11 4. Has not had a professional license or certification refused,
12 revoked, suspended, or restricted and does not have a complaint,
13 allegation, or investigation pending in any regulatory jurisdiction
14 in the United States or in another country for reasons that relate
15 to unprofessional conduct unless the Division State Board of
16 Examiners of Psychologists finds, to its satisfaction, that the
17 conduct has been corrected or that mitigating circumstances exist
18 that prevent its resolution; and
19 5. Has at least the minimum graduate or undergraduate degree,
20 appropriate for the level of certification, from an accredited
21 institution of higher learning in a qualifying field of study, as
22 determined by the national Behavior Analyst Certification Board.
23 C. The State Board of Examiners of Psychologists shall have
24 exclusive power and authority to determine the qualifications and

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 fitn ess of all applicants for admission to practice as a licensed
2 behavior analyst or certified assistant behavior analyst in this
3 state. The Board shall require that each applicant submit to a
4 national criminal history record check as defined in Section 150.9
5 of Title 74 of the Oklahoma Statutes. The Board shall not
6 disseminate criminal history record information resulting from the
7 record check.
8 D. A person holding a state license or state certification
9 shall apply for renewal of the state license or state certification
10 on or before April 30 of each odd-numbered year. The application
11 shall be accompanied by a renewal fee to be set by the Division
12 State Board of Examiners of Psychologists in accordance with
13 paragraph 3 of subsection F G of this section.
14 D. E. A person licensed or certified by the Developmental
15 Disabilities Services Division of the Department Board under this
16 section shall:
17 1. Maintain active status and fulfill all requirements for
18 renewal of national certification or recertification with by the
19 national Behavior Analyst Certification Board; and
20 2. Conduct professional activities in accordance with accepted
21 standards such as the Guidelines for Responsible Conduct and
22 Professional Disciplinary Standards Ethics Code for Behavior
23 Analysts of the national Behavior Analyst Certification Board.
24
SB1557 HFLR Page 6
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 E. F. 1. No person shall claim the title of licensed behavior
2 analyst or certified assistant behavior analyst unless that person
3 meets the applicable requirements in this section. No person shall
4 practice applied behavior analysis without obtaining a license or
5 certification in accordance with this section. Supervisees may only
6 provide applied behavior analysis under the supervision of a
7 licensed behavior analyst. This section shall not restrict the
8 practice of applied behavior analysis by human services
9 professionals, provided such individuals are working within the
10 scope of their professions and the practice of applied behavior
11 analysis is commensurate with their level of training and
12 experience.
13 2. A violation of this subsection shall be punishable by a fine
14 of not more than Five Hundred Dollars ($500.00), the suspension or
15 revocation of a license licensure or certification issued pursuant
16 to this section, or both such fine and loss of licensure or
17 certification.
18 F. G. The Division State Board of Examiners of Psychologists
19 shall:
20 1. Investigate all complaints relating to:
21 a. the practice or supervision of applied behavior
22 analysis by any person licensed by the Developmental
23 Disabilities Services Division of the Department Board
24
SB1557 HFLR Page 7
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 as a behavior analyst or certified by the Division
2 Board as an assistant behavior analyst, or
3 b. any person alleged to be practicing or providing
4 supervision without a state license or state
5 certification;
6 2. Refer any substantiated complaints to the national Behavior
7 Analyst Certification Board; and
8 3. Charge reasonable fees for issuance or renewal of a license
9 or for certification, not to exceed One Hundred Dollars ($100.00) in
10 an amount sufficient to cover the cost and expense incurred by the
11 State Board of Examiners of Psychologists for such issuance or
12 renewal.
13 G. H. 1. A person having a qualifying degree, as provided for
14 in paragraph 5 of subsection B of this section, and participating in
15 the applied behavior analysis treatment pilot project established in
16 Section 3 of this act Section 1-280.2 of Title 63 of the Oklahoma
17 Statutes shall be exempt from the requirements of this section while
18 such person is actively participating in the project.
19 2. Persons employed by a school district in this state who
20 provide services solely to the school district under the Individuals
21 with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), 20 U.S.C., Section 1400 et
22 seq., shall be exempt from the requirements of this section.
23 H. I. The Department State Board of Examiners of Psychologists
24 shall promulgate rules to implement the provisions of this section.

1 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

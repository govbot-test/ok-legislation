9 An Act relating to agriculture; amending 2 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Sections 5-4.1, 5-4.2, 5-4.3, as amended by Section
10 1, Chapter 16, O.S.L. 2024, 5-4.4, 5-4.5, and 5-4.6
(2 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 5-4.3), which relate to
11 the Homemade Food Freedom Act; renaming the Homemade
Food Freedom Act to the Local Food Freedom Act;
12 modifying definitions; updating statutory language;
and providing an effective date.

15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 5-4.1, is
17 amended to read as follows:
18 Section 5-4.1. This act shall be known and may be cited as the
19 "Homemade Local Food Freedom Act".
20 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 5-4.2, is
21 amended to read as follows:
22 Section 5-4.2. As used in the Homemade Local Food Freedom Act:
23 1. "Home Local food establishment" shall mean a business at any
24 location, including on the premises of a residence but excluding
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3720 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 rest aurants, in which homemade local food products are created for
2 sale or resale if the business has gross annual sales of prepared
3 food of less than Seventy-five Thousand Dollars ($75,000.00) Two
4 Hundred Fifty Thousand Dollars ($250,000.00). Gross annual sales
5 includes all sales of prepared food produced by the business at any
6 location;
7 2. "Delivered" shall mean transferred to the customer, either
8 immediately upon sale or at a time thereafter;
9 3. "Homemade Local food product" shall mean food, including a
10 beverage, which is produced and, if packaged, packaged at a
11 residence local food establishment; provided, however, homemade
12 local food product shall not mean alcoholic beverages or
13 unpasteurized milk or cannabis or marijuana products;
14 4. "Non-time- or -temperature-controlled for safety" shall mean
15 food that does not require time or temperature control for safety to
16 limit the rapid and progressive growth of infectious or toxigenic
17 microorganisms, including foods that have a pH level of four and
18 six-tenths (4.6) or below or a water activity (aw) value of eighty-
19 five one-hundredths (0.85) or less;
20 5. "Time- or temperature-controlled for safety" shall mean a
21 food that requires time or temperature control for safety to limit
22 infectious or toxigenic microorganisms and is in a form capable of
23 supporting rapid and progressive growth of infectious or toxigenic
24 microorganisms; provided, however, time- or temperature-controlled
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3720 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 for safety shall not include foods that have a pH level of four and
2 six-tenths (4.6) or below or a water activity (aw) value of eighty-
3 five one-hundredths (0.85) or less;
4 6. "Produce" shall mean to prepare a food product by cooking,
5 baking, drying, mixing, cutting, canning, fermenting, preserving,
6 dehydrating, growing, raising or other process; and
7 7. "Producer" shall mean the person or business entity who
8 produces a homemade local food product in a home local food
9 establishment.
10 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 5-4.3, as
11 amended by Section 1, Chapter 16, O.S.L. 2024 (2 O.S. Supp. 2025,
12 Section 5-4.3), is amended to read as follows:
13 Section 5-4.3. A. The production and sale of homemade local
14 food products that meet the following conditions shall be exempt
15 from all licensing and other requirements of the State Department of
16 Health and the Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and
17 Forestry:
18 1. Non-time- or -temperature-controlled-for-safety homemade
19 local food products shall be sold:
20 a. by the producer directly to the consumer, either in
21 person or by remote means, including, but not limited
22 to, the Internet or telephone, or
23 b. by a producer's designated agent or a third-party
24 vendor, such as a retail or grocery store, farm, farm
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3720 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 stand, farmers market, membership-based buying club,
2 craft fair or flea market, to the consumer; provided,
3 the third-party vendor displays a placard where
4 homemade local food products are displayed for sale
5 with the following disclosure: "This product was
6 produced in a private residence facility that is
7 exempt from government licensing and inspection. This
8 product may contain allergens.";
9 2. Non-time- or -temperature-controlled-for-safety homemade
10 local food products shall be delivered:
11 a. by the producer or producer's designated agent
12 directly to the consumer or third-party vendor, or
13 b. by a third-party vendor or a third-party carrier, such
14 as a parcel delivery service, to the consumer or a
15 third-party vendor;
16 3. Time- or temperature-controlled-for-safety homemade local
17 food products shall be sold by the producer directly to the
18 consumer, either in person or by remote means, including, but not
19 limited to, the Internet or telephone;
20 4. Time- or temperature-controlled-for-safety homemade local
21 food products shall be delivered by the producer directly to the
22 consumer;
23 5. Before a producer produces and sells time- or temperature-
24 controlled-for-safety homemade local food products, the producer any
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3720 SFLR Page 4
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 pers on producing the local food shall complete and pass food safety
2 training from a list of providers, including the ServSafe Food
3 Handler Training, approved by the Oklahoma Department of
4 Agriculture, Food, and Forestry. The food safety training shall be
5 available to complete online and shall not exceed eight (8) hours in
6 length. The Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry
7 shall make the list of approved training available on its website by
8 November 1, 2021;
9 6. The following information shall be provided to the consumer
10 in the format required by subsection B of this section:
11 a. the name and phone number of the producer,
12 b. the physical address where the product was produced,
13 c. a description of the homemade local food product,
14 d. the ingredients of the homemade local food product in
15 descending order of proportion,
16 e. a statement indicating the presence of any of the
17 eight most common allergens, including milk, eggs,
18 peanuts, tree nuts, soy and wheat, and
19 f. legible print stating, "This product was produced in a
20 private residence facility that is exempt from
21 government licensing and inspection.";
22 7. If the homemade local food product is packaged and
23 distributed in interstate commerce, it shall also be sold and
24 labeled in accordance with federal law; and
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3720 SFLR Page 5
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 8. Homemade Local food products shall not contain seafood or
2 meat, meat by-products or meat food products as defined by Section
3 301.2 of Title 9 of the Code of Federal Regulations or poultry,
4 poultry products or poultry food products as defined for purposes of
5 the federal Poultry Products Inspection Act; and
6 9. The producer complies with all local zoning and building
7 code laws.
8 B. The information required by paragraph 6 of subsection A of
9 this section shall be provided in a legible format of at least 10-
10 point font, in the following manner:
11 1. On a label affixed to the package if the homemade local food
12 product is packaged;
13 2. On a label affixed to a container, if the homemade local
14 food product is offered for sale from a bulk container directly to
15 the consumer;
16 3. On a placard displayed at the point of sale, and on a card
17 or other item that is made available to the consumer and is readily
18 carriable if the homemade local food product is not packaged; and
19 4. Displayed on the webpage from which the homemade local food
20 product is offered for sale if it is sold on the Internet; provided,
21 that each item sold over the Internet shall be properly labeled or
22 shall have a label included in the shipping container.
23 C. A homemade local food product producer may obtain a
24 registration number upon the payment of an annual fee of Fifteen
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3720 SFLR Page 6
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 Doll ars ($15.00) to the Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food,
2 and Forestry that is good for one (1) year from the date of its
3 issue. The assigned registration number may be used on product
4 labels instead of the producer's name, phone number, and the
5 physical address of the location where the homemade local food
6 product was produced.
7 SECTION 4. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 5-4.4, is
8 amended to read as follows:
9 Section 5-4.4. Nothing in the Homemade Local Food Freedom Act
10 shall be construed to impede the State Department of Health in any
11 investigation of a reported foodborne illness. Upon receipt of a
12 consumer complaint, the Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food,
13 and Forestry shall have the authority to request proof of completion
14 of the food safety training, verify a producer's gross sales, and
15 ensure a producer has complied with the act's labeling and delivery
16 requirements. The Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and
17 Forestry may fine a producer who violates this act in accordance
18 with Section 5-4.5 of this title.
19 SECTION 5. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 5-4.5, is
20 amended to read as follows:
21 Section 5-4.5. Violation of the Homemade Local Food Freedom Act
22 shall be punishable by a fine not exceeding Three Hundred Dollars
23 ($300.00).
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3720 SFLR Page 7
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 SECTION 6. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 5-4.6, is
2 amended to read as follows:
3 Section 5-4.6. Nothing in the Homemade Local Food Freedom Act
4 shall be construed to prevent counties from enacting ordinances
5 regulating the operation of home local food establishments, provided
6 such ordinances do not conflict with the provisions of the Homemade
7 Local Food Freedom Act or impede or restrict the sale of homemade
8 local food products in compliance with the law.
9 SECTION 7. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

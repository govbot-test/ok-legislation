8 PROPOSED POLICY COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 An Act relating to agriculture; creating the Access
to Healthy Local Food Act; defining terms; exempting
10 direct-to-consumer sales of local food from all
licensing, inspection, and other requirements if
11 certain conditions are met; providing conditions;
providing that a homemade food product producer may
12 obtain a registration number upon payment of annual
fee; allowing assigned registration number to be used
13 on product labels instead of certain other
information; allowing farm to sell and serve local
14 food in certain circumstances; providing construing
provision; providing the Department of Agriculture,
15 Food, and Forestry authority to request proof of
certain information from producer; providing fine for
16 violation; providing that this act does not prevent
counties from enacting certain ordinances; providing
17 for codification; and providing an effective date.
18
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
21 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-4A.1 of Title 2, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 16180 Page 1
1 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Access to
2 Healthy Local Food Act".
3 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-4A.2 of Title 2, unless there
5 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 As used in this act:
7 1. "Direct-to-consumer sale" means the sale of food directly to
8 an informed end consumer by the producer at any location, including
9 a farm, but excluding restaurants, not to exceed One Million Five
10 Hundred Thousand Dollars ($1,500,000.00) in gross sales in a single
11 year;
12 2. "Farm" means a farm in this state that grows and harvests
13 agricultural products, including fruit and vegetables, or that
14 raises and produces dairy products;
15 3. "Informed end consumer" means a person who is purchasing
16 local food for immediate consumption or consumption thereafter who
17 has been given written notice that the product is exempt from state
18 licensing and inspection in accordance with Section 3 of this act;
19 4. "Local food" means food that is produced in this state and
20 sold in a direct-to-consumer sale. It excludes meat, poultry,
21 seafood, unpasteurized dairy products, cannabis or marijuana
22 products, and low-acid canned products;
23 5. "Produce" shall have the same meaning as defined in Section
24 5-4.2 of Title 2 of the Oklahoma Statutes; and
Req. No. 16180 Page 2
1 6. "Producer" means the person or business entity that produced
2 the food.
3 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-4A.3 of Title 2, unless there
5 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 A. Direct-to-consumer sales of local food shall be exempt from
7 all licensing, inspection, and other requirements of the State
8 Department of Health and the Oklahoma Department of Agriculture,
9 Food, and Forestry if the following conditions are met:
10 1. Any person producing the local food shall complete and pass
11 food safety training from a list of providers, including ServSafe
12 Food Handler Training, approved by the Department of Agriculture,
13 Food, and Forestry. The food safety training shall be available to
14 complete online and shall not exceed eight (8) hours in length;
15 2. If the food is not a whole, uncut, raw fruit or vegetable,
16 the following information shall be provided in a legible format to
17 an informed end consumer on the label or in writing at the point of
18 sale:
19 a. the name and phone number of the producer,
20 b. the physical address where the local food product was
21 produced,
22 c. a description of the local food,
23 d. the ingredients of the local food in descending order
24 of proportion,
Req. No. 16180 Page 3
1 e. a statement indicating the presence of any of the six
2 most common allergens, including milk, eggs, peanuts,
3 tree nuts, soy, and wheat, and
4 f. written notice stating, "This product is exempt from
5 state licensing and inspection"; and
6 3. The producer complies with all local zoning and building
7 code laws.
8 B. A homemade food product producer may obtain a registration
9 number upon the payment of an annual fee of Fifteen Dollars ($15.00)
10 to the Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry that is good
11 for one (1) year from the date of its issue. The assigned
12 registration number may be used on product labels instead of the
13 producer's name, telephone number, and the physical address of the
14 location where the local food product was produced.
15 C. A farm may sell and serve local food on the farm where the
16 food is grown or produced to an informed end consumer using an agent
17 or employee at the farm, provided the farm satisfies all other
18 conditions provided in subsection A of this section.
19 D. Nothing in the Access to Healthy Local Food Act shall be
20 construed to impede the State Department of Health in any
21 investigation of a reported foodborne illness. Upon receipt of a
22 consumer complaint, the Department of Agriculture, Food, and
23 Forestry shall have the authority to request proof of completion of
24 the food safety training and ensure a producer has complied with the
Req. No. 16180 Page 4
1 act' s labeling and delivery requirements. The Department of
2 Agriculture, Food, and Forestry may fine a producer who violates
3 this act up to Three Hundred Dollars ($300.00) per violation.
4 E. Nothing in the Access to Healthy Local Food Act shall be
5 construed to prevent counties from enacting ordinances regulating
6 the sale of food, provided such ordinances do not conflict with the
7 provisions of this act or impede or restrict the sale of products in
8 compliance with state and federal law.
9 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1632 Page 1
1 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 14-135, is
2 amended to read as follows:
3 Section 14-135. Subject to the availability of funds, beginning
4 with the 2019-2020 school year, the Oklahoma Department of Commerce,
5 in cooperation with the
6 A. The State Department of Education and, in cooperation with
7 the Commission for Educational Quality and Accountability, shall
8 review and approve career-readiness assessments and assessment-based
9 credentials that measure and document foundational workplace skills.
10 Beginning with the 2019-2020 2026-2027 school year, the assessments
11 shall be made available to all public school districts to be
12 administered to students at the discretion of each school district’s
13 administration to students in grades ten through twelve at least
14 once prior to their graduation from high school. The assessment-
15 based credential shall be available to any student who achieves the
16 prescribed level on the required assessments assessment. If the
17 public school district chooses to administer the assessments, the
18 assessments shall be administered at least once to each student who
19 chooses to take the assessment at no cost to the student. The
20 assessments shall:
21 1. Be a standardized, criterion-referenced measure of broadly
22 relevant foundational workplace skills;
23 2. Assess and document student readiness for a wide range of
24 jobs;
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1632 Page 2
1 3. Measure skills in the following areas, including, but not
2 limited to:
3 a. applied mathematics,
4 b. workplace documents,
5 c. graphic literacy, or
6 d. critical thinking and leadership collaboration;
7 4. Align with research-based skill requirement profiles for
8 specific industries and occupations;
9 5. Lead to nationally recognized work-readiness certificates or
10 credentials for students who meet the minimum proficiency
11 requirements on the component assessments; and
12 6. Be available in paper- and computer-based formats.
13 B. The Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education, in
14 consultation with the American Council on Education National Guide,
15 may promulgate rules to allow institutions of higher education
16 within The Oklahoma State System of Higher Education to grant
17 college credit based on assessment-based credentials and scores
18 earned on career-readiness assessments.
19 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
20 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
21 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
22 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
23 be in full force from and after its passage and approval."

8 POLICY COMMITTEE RECOMMENDATION
9 An Act relating to postsecondary education; defining
terms; prohibiting postsecondary institutions from
10 having differentiated standardized test score
requirements based on a prospective student's primary
11 or secondary educational background; authorizing
State Regents for Higher Education and State Board of
12 Career and Technology Education to ensure compliance
and promulgate rules; amending 70 O.S. 2021, Section
13 2603, as last amended by Section 1, Chapter 485,
O.S.L. 2025 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 2603), which
14 relates to the Oklahoma Higher Learning Access
Program eligibility requirements; removing
15 differentiated grade point average and standardized
test score requirements based on educational means;
16 amending 70 O.S. 2021, Section 2605, as last amended
by Section 2, Chapter 485, O.S.L. 2025 (70 O.S. Supp.
17 2025, Section 2605), which relates to Oklahoma Higher
Learning Access Program student agreements; modifying
18 age requirements to be the same for all students
regardless of mode of education; updating statutory
19 language; providing for codification; providing an
effective date; and declaring an emergency.

1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 4316 of Title 70, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. As used in this section:
5 1. "Postsecondary institution" means:
6 a. any public university, college, or other postsecondary
7 institution within The Oklahoma State System of Higher
8 Education, and
9 b. any technology center school or college governed by
10 the State Board of Career and Technology Education;
11 and
12 2. "Standardized test score requirement" means any minimum
13 composite or sub-score on the ACT test, SAT, CLT, or any equivalent
14 assessment used for admission, placement, or scholarship purposes.
15 B. No postsecondary institution shall have differentiated
16 standardized test score requirements based on a prospective
17 student's primary or secondary educational background, including the
18 type of school, whether the school was accredited or unaccredited,
19 the program, or the mode of education, for purposes of:
20 1. General undergraduate admission;
21 2. Assured admission;
22 3. Program-specific admission;
23 4. Concurrent enrollment eligibility; or
24 5. Scholarship or financial aid eligibility administered by the

1 postsecondary institution.

3 State Board of Career and Technology Education shall ensure
4 compliance with this section and may promulgate rules as necessary.

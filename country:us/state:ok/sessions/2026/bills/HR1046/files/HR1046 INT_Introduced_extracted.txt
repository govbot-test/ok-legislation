13 WHEREAS, everyone in Oklahoma and much of the world knows about
14 the horrific events that transpired in Oklahoma City on the morning
15 of April 19, 1995, when a terrorist blast ripped apart the Alfred P.
16 Murrah Federal Building at 9:02 a.m., claiming the lives of 168
17 individuals and leaving many others wounded both physically and
18 mentally; and
19 WHEREAS, we take this opportunity to remember those who died or
20 were wounded. May their lives never be said to have been in vain.
21 They live on in our memories, and their actions while alive
22 influence and inform us to this day of how to live in charity toward
23 others and to do good and not repay evil with evil; and
24
Req. No. 17262 Page 1
1 WHEREAS, we acknowledge the debt of gratitude owed to the many
2 first responders – including local, state, and federal law
3 enforcement personnel, firefighters, search and rescue teams, public
4 and private medical personnel, other emergency services personnel,
5 and thousands of volunteers from the community – who immediately
6 answered the call of helping to rescue the living and recover the
7 dead. They saved lives, assisted the injured and wounded, comforted
8 the bereaved, provided meals and support to those who came to
9 Oklahoma City to offer assistance, and so much more; and
10 WHEREAS, we now renounce these despicable acts of terrorism and
11 ask Almighty God to root out the seeds of such terrorism that grow
12 in the hearts of men and women worldwide, festering into acts of
13 destruction and violence. We ask for an uncovering of such plots
14 before they can claim further lives. We ask for divine justice
15 tempered with divine mercy; and
16 WHEREAS, we repent on behalf of those who harbor such anger,
17 bitterness, and hatred toward others. We ask for love to take root
18 in place of such darkness; we willingly forgive those who have hurt
19 us, and when we can't find it in our own hearts to forgive, we ask
20 for divine help; and
21 WHEREAS, we vow to teach our children and all future generations
22 that love toward their creator, toward themselves, and toward others
23 is the only solution. We remember this day because we choose a
24 different future moving forward. We see what violence and hatred
Req. No. 17262 Page 2
1 brin gs, and we declare we will not pick up the same offense but will
2 instead plant seeds of love and hope in its place. May we rebuild
3 and replant only from this foundation; and
4 WHEREAS, we decree the Oklahoma City National Memorial will not
5 just be a place to mourn the dead and to pity the lives lost. We
6 mourn with those who mourn and extend and receive comfort. But we
7 decree this will be a place to restart hope for a better and more
8 peaceful future. It will not be a place of rekindling anger toward
9 terrorists, which gives the darkness power. It will instead be a
10 place where light dispels darkness; love dispels fear; hope dispels
11 despair; and
12 WHEREAS, we resolve that while we will not be cowed by
13 terrorists, we will not respond in a like spirit; and
14 WHEREAS, we commemorate this day following the 31st anniversary
15 of the bombing of the Alfred P. Murrah Federal Building in Oklahoma
16 City. We do so not in vain, thinking nothing has truly changed in
17 the hearts of men and women in the last 31 years, believing that we
18 are more divided now than perhaps any time in history. We do so
19 instead with the hope that, with divine help, we can begin to truly
20 love our brothers and sisters and move past the hatred that so
21 easily divides us. We bless those who persecute us and despitefully
22 use us, not because we don't desire justice, but because we know
23 divine justice exceeds our own, just as divine mercy is given to
24 those who seek mercy.
Req. No. 17262 Page 3
1 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
2 OF THE 2ND SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE:
3 THAT the Oklahoma House of Representatives sends its heartfelt
4 remembrances to the families, friends, and neighbors of the 168
5 people killed in the terrorist bombing of the Alfred P. Murrah
6 Federal Building in Oklahoma City.
7 THAT the Oklahoma House of Representatives sends its heartfelt
8 remembrances to those injured in the bombing and expresses gratitude
9 to the thousands of first responders, rescue workers, medical
10 personnel, and volunteers from the community and across the nation
11 who answered the call for help on the morning of the attack and in
12 the days and weeks thereafter.
13 THAT a copy of this resolution be sent to Kari Watkins,
14 President and CEO of the Oklahoma City National Memorial and Museum.

1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 2nd Session of the 60th Legislature (2026)
3 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE

10 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
11 An Act relating to emergency management; defining
terms; providing for applicability; directing for
12 assessments and reviews; requiring action plans;
requiring submission of assessments and plans to
13 regulating authority; providing exception; directing
for communication and warning systems; providing
14 standards; requiring training and drills; requiring
disclosure; providing for rulemaking; providing
15 enforcement; amending 63 O.S. 2021, Section 681,
which relates to school buildings and fallout
16 protection; adding recreational campgrounds;
requiring emergency plans be on file with local
17 emergency management director; updating statutory
language; providing for codification; and providing
18 an effective date.
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
21 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 683.24C of Title 63, unless
23 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24 As used in this act:
Req. No. 3931 Page 1
1 1. “Camp facility” means an operator of a facility that hosts
2 or is rented by multiple youth camps;
3 2. “Emergency action plan” or “EAP” means a written, site-
4 specific plan addressing preparedness response, evacuation,
5 sheltering, and communication for severe weather hazards;
6 3. “Hazard exposure zone” means an area identified through
7 federal, state, or local data as subject to one or more severe
8 weather hazards;
9 4. “Outdoor program” means a youth-serving activity conducted
10 primarily outdoors, including wilderness programs, sports camps,
11 adventure camps, or similar operations;
12 5. “Regulating authority” means the emergency management
13 director of the county in which the camp facility, youth camp, or
14 outdoor program is located;
15 6. “Severe weather hazard” means a meteorological or
16 environmental condition that poses a foreseeable risk to life or
17 safety, including but not limited to:
18 a. flooding or flash flooding,
19 b. high winds or straight-line winds,
20 c. tornadic or rotating wind systems,
21 d. hail,
22 e. lightning,
23 f. extreme heat or cold,
24 g. wildfire or wildfire smoke, and
Req. No. 3931 Page 2
1 h. any other weather-related condition designated by the
2 regulating authority; and
3 7. “Youth camp” means any program or operation that:
4 a. provides organized recreational, educational, or
5 residential activities,
6 b. serves individuals under eighteen (18) years of age,
7 and
8 c. operates for compensation or as a nonprofit, including
9 day camps, overnight camps, seasonal camps, and
10 specialty camps.
11 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
12 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 683.24D of Title 63, unless
13 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
14 This act applies to all camp facilities, youth camps, and
15 outdoor programs operating within this state except:
16 1. Programs conducted solely on a single-day basis with no
17 oversight component which may be subject to modified requirements,
18 as determined by rule; and
19 2. Programs operated exclusively by a parent or legal guardian
20 for his or her own children.
21 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 683.24E of Title 63, unless
23 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 3931 Page 3
1 A. Except as provided for in subsection D of this section, camp
2 facilities, youth camps, and outdoor programs shall conduct a site-
3 specific hazard assessment on or before January 1, 2027,
4 identifying:
5 1. Applicable severe weather hazards;
6 2. Proximity to hazard exposure zones;
7 3. Structural vulnerabilities of buildings and shelters; and
8 4. Feasible evacuation routes and shelter-in-place locations.
9 B. Hazard assessments shall be reviewed and updated:
10 1. At least once every three (3) years; and
11 2. Upon material changes to facilities, terrain, or hazard
12 classifications.
13 C. A copy of the site-specific hazard assessment, including
14 updated assessments, shall be submitted to and placed on file with
15 the regulating authority.
16 D. Any youth camp or outdoor program hosted at a camp facility
17 shall utilize the site-specific hazard assessment of the camp
18 facility.
19 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 683.24F of Title 63, unless
21 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 A. Except as provided for in subsection E of this section, on
23 or before January 1, 2027, camp facilities, youth camps, and outdoor
24
Req. No. 3931 Page 4
1 prog rams shall develop and maintain an emergency action plan
2 addressing, at a minimum:
3 1. Monitoring and detection of severe weather hazards;
4 2. Decision-making authority and chain of command;
5 3. Criteria for evacuation versus shelter-in-place;
6 4. Evacuation routes and secondary alternatives;
7 5. Shelter standards appropriate to the identified hazards;
8 6. Accountability procedures for campers and staff; and
9 7. Post-event communication and reunification procedures.
10 B. The plan shall include separate response protocols for each
11 severe weather hazard reasonably applicable to the site.
12 C. Emergency action plans shall be reviewed and updated:
13 1. At least once every three (3) years; and
14 2. Upon material changes to facilities, terrain, or hazard
15 classifications.
16 D. A copy of the emergency action plan, including updated
17 plans, shall be submitted to and placed on file with the regulating
18 authority.
19 E. Any youth camp or outdoor program hosted at a camp facility
20 shall utilize the emergency action plan of the camp facility.
21 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 683.24G of Title 63, unless
23 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 3931 Page 5
1 Each camp facility, youth camp, and outdoor program shall
2 maintain:
3 1. At least two independent methods of receiving severe weather
4 alerts, including one method that does not rely on cellular service;
5 2. An internal communication system capable of alerting staff
6 and campers promptly; and
7 3. Procedures for notifying parents or guardians during
8 emergencies.
9 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
10 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 683.24H of Title 63, unless
11 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
12 A. Where severe weather hazards are identified, camp facilities
13 and youth camps shall provide access to shelters that are:
14 1. Appropriately rated for anticipated wind or impact loads;
15 2. Located outside high-risk zones when feasible; and
16 3. Clearly marked and accessible.
17 B. The regulating authority may prohibit or condition the use
18 of:
19 1. Sleeping facilities located in high-risk hazard exposure
20 zones; or
21 2. Temporary structures unable to withstand foreseeable severe
22 weather.

24
Req. No. 3931 Page 6
1 SECTION 7. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 683.24I of Title 63, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. Camp facility, youth camp, and outdoor program staff shall
5 receive annual training on emergency procedures and hazard
6 recognition.
7 B. Youth camps and camp facilities shall conduct periodic
8 drills appropriate to the identified hazards.
9 C. Training records shall be maintained and made available for
10 inspection by the regulating authority.
11 SECTION 8. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
12 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 683.24J of Title 63, unless
13 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
14 Youth camps, camp facilities, and outdoor programs shall
15 disclose in writing:
16 1. The existence of material severe weather hazards affecting
17 the site; and
18 2. The general nature of emergency response procedures.
19 Acknowledgment of receipt shall be obtained from participants or
20 the parents or legal guardians of participants, if the participant
21 is a minor, prior to participation.
22 SECTION 9. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 683.24K of Title 63, unless
24 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
Req. No. 3931 Page 7
1 A. The regulating authority shall adopt rules necessary to
2 implement this act, including:
3 1. Hazard-specific standards;
4 2. Compliance timelines; and
5 3. Variance procedures for site-specific conditions.
6 B. The regulating authority may impose:
7 1. Corrective action plans;
8 2. Civil penalties; or
9 3. The closure of camp facilities, youth camps, and outdoor
10 programs for material noncompliance.
11 SECTION 10. AMENDATORY 63 O.S. 2021, Section 681, is
12 amended to read as follows:
13 Section 681. A. School authorities of the State of Oklahoma
14 this state, its political subdivisions, and its school districts are
15 authorized to plan, design, and construct new school buildings and
16 make additions to existing school buildings that afford protection
17 for the anticipated school body, faculty, and visitors against
18 tornadoes and severe weather. Each school, administration building
19 and institution of higher learning shall have written plans and
20 procedures in place for protecting students, faculty, administrators
21 and visitors from natural and man-made disasters and emergencies.
22 Plans shall be reviewed and updated annually as appropriate by each
23 school, administration building and institution of higher learning,
24 and placed on file at each school district and each local emergency
Req. No. 3931 Page 8
1 resp onse organization within the district, which may include police,
2 fire, emergency medical services, sheriff and emergency management
3 of the appropriate jurisdiction. The plans shall be submitted in a
4 format acceptable to the emergency agency no later than November 1
5 of each year. Each school district and institution of higher
6 learning shall make annual reports to the local school board or
7 Board of Regents detailing the status of emergency preparedness and
8 identified safety needs for each school or institution.
9 B. Recreational campgrounds within this state are authorized to
10 plan, design, and maintain facilities and grounds in a manner that
11 affords protection for campers, staff, and visitors against flooding
12 and severe weather hazards. Each recreational campground shall have
13 written emergency action plans and procedures in place for
14 protecting campers, staff, and visitors from natural disasters and
15 severe weather events, including those requiring evacuation.
16 1. Such plans shall, at a minimum, identify natural hazards
17 specific to the location that may necessitate evacuation, outline
18 evacuation routes and procedures, and include methods for issuing
19 emergency alerts and warnings to campers and staff. The plans shall
20 be reviewed and updated annually as appropriate by the operator of
21 the recreational campground.
22 2. A copy of the emergency action plan, including evacuation
23 procedures and warning information, shall be submitted to and placed
24 on file with the local emergency management director of the
Req. No. 3931 Page 9
1 appr opriate jurisdiction in a format acceptable to the emergency
2 management agency. The plans shall be submitted or updated no later
3 than January 1 of each year and upon any significant change to
4 facility operations or hazard conditions.
5 SECTION 11. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 271 of Title 56, unless there is
19 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. The Department of Human Services shall, using existing
21 resources and data already collected by the Department, compile and
22 publish an annual report regarding program integrity and payment
23 accuracy within the child care subsidy program administered pursuant

19 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 272 of Title 56, unless there is
21 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 A. The Department of Human Services is hereby directed to amend
23 Section 2.5.3(c) of the State of Oklahoma’s Child Care and
24 Development Fund (CCDF) Plan, relating to circumstances under which
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2984 Page 4

1 the lead agency may discontinue assistance prior to the minimum
2 twelve-month redetermination period.
3 B. Such amendment shall:
4 1. Keep current selection of subsection "iii. a change in
5 residency outside of the State or Territory" as the identified
6 limited circumstance for discontinuation of assistance prior to the
7 twelve-month redetermination; and
8 2. Select and adopt subsection "ii. Excessive unexplained
9 absences despite multiple attempts by the lead agency or designated
10 entity to contact the family and provider, including the prior
11 notification of a possible discontinuation of assistance".
12 C. The Department shall further provide, publish, and maintain
13 within the lead agency’s written policy a clear definition
14 specifying ninety (90) consecutive days as the number of unexplained
15 absences that shall be deemed “excessive” for purposes of
16 discontinuation under this subsection.
17 D. The Department shall implement the amendment required by
18 this act in the next available revision or submission of the Child
19 Care and Development Fund (CCDF) Plan and shall ensure compliance
20 with all applicable federal requirements.
21 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

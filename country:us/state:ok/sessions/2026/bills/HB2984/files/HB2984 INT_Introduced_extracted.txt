6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to the Supplemental Nutrition
Assistance Program; directing the Department of Human
8 Services to submit a request to the United States
Department of Agriculture Food and Nutrition Service;
9 providing information the request shall contain;
directing the Department to carry out certain tasks;
10 directing the Department to submit annual report;
providing for codification; and providing an
11 effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 241.5 of Title 56, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. The Oklahoma Department of Human Services is hereby directed
19 to submit a request to the United States Department of Agriculture
20 Food and Nutrition Service for a waiver to exclude items sold
21 outside of the State of Oklahoma from being eligible for purchase
22 using Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) benefits
23 distributed to recipients residing within the State of Oklahoma.
24 B. The waiver request shall include:
Req. No. 14227 Page 1

1 1. Justification for excluding items sold outside of the State
2 of Oklahoma from SNAP purchases based on economic impacts;
3 2. A proposed implementation plan, including participant
4 education and enforcement mechanisms; and
5 3. Any additional supporting data demonstrating the need for
6 this reform.
7 C. Upon federal approval of the waiver request, the Oklahoma
8 Department of Human Services shall conduct public outreach and
9 education efforts to inform SNAP recipients about the change.
10 D. The Department shall submit an annual report to the
11 Governor, the President Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate,
12 and the Speaker of the Oklahoma House of Representatives detailing:
13 1. The implementation status of the waiver;
14 2. Any challenges encountered in enforcement and compliance;
15 and
16 3. The economic impacts observed as a result of this policy
17 change.
18 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

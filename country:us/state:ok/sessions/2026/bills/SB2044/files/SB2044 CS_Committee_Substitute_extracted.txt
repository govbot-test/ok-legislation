9 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
10 An Act relating to the practice of chiropractic;
amending 59 O.S. 2021, Section 161.12, which relates
11 to grounds for imposing penalties; creating certain
exception; providing certain construction; updating
12 statutory language; and providing an effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 161.12, is
17 amended to read as follows:
18 Section 161.12. A. The Board of Chiropractic Examiners is
19 authorized, after notice and an opportunity for a hearing pursuant
20 to Article II of the Administrative Procedures Act, to issue an
21 order imposing one or more of the following penalties whenever the
22 Board finds, by clear and convincing evidence, that a chiropractic
23 physician has committed any of the acts or occurrences set forth in
24 subsection B of this section:
Req. No. 3719 Page 1
1 1. Disapproval of an application for a renewal license;
2 2. Revocation or suspension of an original license or renewal
3 license, or both;
4 3. Restriction of the practice of a chiropractic physician
5 under such terms and conditions as deemed appropriate by the Board;
6 4. An administrative fine not to exceed One Thousand Dollars
7 ($1,000.00) for each count or separate violation;
8 5. A censure or reprimand;
9 6. Placement of a chiropractic physician on probation for a
10 period of time and under such terms and conditions as the Board may
11 specify, including requiring the chiropractic physician to submit to
12 treatment, to attend continuing education courses, to submit to
13 reexamination, or to work under the supervision of another
14 chiropractic physician; and
15 7. The assessment of costs expended by the Board in
16 investigating and prosecuting a violation. The costs may include,
17 but are not limited to, staff time, salary and travel expenses,
18 witness fees and attorney fees, and shall be considered part of the
19 order of the Board.
20 B. The following acts or occurrences by a chiropractic
21 physician shall constitute grounds for which the penalties specified
22 in subsection A of this section may be imposed by order of the
23 Board:
24
Req. No. 3719 Page 2
1 1. Pleading guilty or nolo contendere to, or being convicted
2 of, a felony, a misdemeanor involving moral turpitude, or a
3 violation of federal or state controlled dangerous substances laws.
4 A copy of the judgment and sentence of the conviction, duly
5 certified by the clerk of the court in which the conviction was
6 obtained, and a certificate of the clerk that the conviction has
7 become final, shall be sufficient evidence for the imposition of a
8 penalty;
9 2. Being habitually drunk or habitually using habit-forming
10 drugs;
11 3. Using advertising in which statements are made that are
12 fraudulent, deceitful or misleading to the public;
13 4. Aiding or abetting any person not licensed to practice
14 chiropractic in this state to practice chiropractic, except students
15 who are regularly enrolled in an accredited chiropractic college;
16 5. Performing or attempting to perform major or minor surgery
17 in this state, or using electricity in any form for surgical
18 purposes, including cauterization;
19 6. Using or having in a chiropractic physician’s possession any
20 instrument for treatment purposes, the use or possession of which
21 has been prohibited or declared unlawful by any agency of the United
22 States or the State of Oklahoma this state;
23 7. Unlawfully possessing, prescribing or administering any
24 drug, medicine, serum or vaccine. For the purposes of this
Req. No. 3719 Page 3
1 para graph, “serum” does not include solutions consisting of
2 vitamins, minerals, nutrients, or any combination thereof. This
3 section shall not prevent a chiropractic physician from possessing,
4 prescribing or administering, by a needle or otherwise, vitamins,
5 minerals or nutritional supplements, or from practicing within the
6 scope of the science and art of chiropractic as defined in Section
7 161.2 of this title; provided, however, nothing in this section
8 shall be construed to authorize the transfusion of donor blood or
9 blood banking services or the administration of vaccines or
10 controlled dangerous substances;
11 8. Advertising or displaying, directly or indirectly, any
12 certificate, diploma or other document which conveys or implies
13 information that the person is skilled in any healing art other than
14 chiropractic unless the chiropractic physician also possesses a
15 valid current license in said such healing art;
16 9. Obtaining an original license or renewal license in a
17 fraudulent manner;
18 10. Violating any provision of the Unfair Claims Settlement
19 Practices Act or any rule promulgated pursuant thereto;
20 11. Willfully aiding or assisting an insurer, as defined in
21 Section 1250.2 of Title 36 of the Oklahoma Statutes, or an
22 administrator, as defined in Section 1442 of Title 36 of the
23 Oklahoma Statutes, to deny claims which under the terms of the
24 insurance contract are covered services and are medically necessary;
Req. No. 3719 Page 4
1 12. Violating any provision of the Oklahoma Chiropractic
2 Practice Act; or
3 13. Violating any of the rules of the Board.
4 C. Any chiropractic physician against whom a penalty is imposed
5 by an order of the Board under the provisions of this section shall
6 have the right to seek a judicial review of the order pursuant to
7 Article II of the Administrative Procedures Act.
8 D. The Board is authorized to issue a confidential letter of
9 concern to a chiropractic physician when, though evidence does not
10 warrant initiation of an individual proceeding, the Board has noted
11 indications of possible errant conduct by the chiropractic physician
12 that could lead to serious consequences and formal action by the
13 Board.
14 E. If no order imposing a penalty against a chiropractic
15 physician is issued by the Board within three (3) years after a
16 complaint against the chiropractic physician is received by the
17 Board, the complaint and all related documents shall be expunged
18 from the records of the Board.
19 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

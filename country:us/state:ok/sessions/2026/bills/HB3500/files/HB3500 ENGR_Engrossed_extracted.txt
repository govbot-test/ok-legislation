15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 58 O.S. 2021, Section 1252, as
16 amended by Section 1, Chapter 101, O.S.L. 2023 (58 O.S. Supp. 2025,
17 Section 1252), is amended to read as follows:
18 Section 1252. A. An interest in real estate may be titled in
19 transfer-on-death form by recording a deed, signed by the record
20 owner of the interest, designating a grantee beneficiary or
21 beneficiaries of the interest. The deed shall transfer ownership of
22 the interest upon the death of the owner. A transfer-on-death deed
23 need not be supported by consideration. For purposes of the
24 Nontestamentary Transfer of Property Act, an “interest in real

3 B. The signature, consent or agreement of or notice to a
4 grantee beneficiary or beneficiaries of a transfer-on-death deed
5 shall not be required for any purpose during the lifetime of the
6 record owner.
7 C. A designated grantee beneficiary may accept real estate
8 pursuant to a transfer-on-death deed only on behalf of himself,
9 herself, or a legal entity over which he or she has proper
10 authority. A beneficiary shall not accept such real estate on
11 behalf of another designated beneficiary.
12 D. Each designated grantee beneficiary wishing to accept real
13 estate pursuant to a transfer-on-death deed shall execute an
14 affidavit affirming:
15 1. Verification of the record owner’s death;
16 2. Whether the record owner and the designated beneficiary were
17 married at the time of the record owner’s death; and
18 3. A legal description of the real estate.
19 E. The grantee shall attach a copy of the record owner’s death
20 certificate to the beneficiary affidavit. For a record owner’s
21 death occurring on or after November 1, 2011, the beneficiary shall
22 record the affidavit and related documents with the office of the
23 county clerk where the real estate is located within nine (9) months
24 of the grantor’s death, otherwise the interest in the property

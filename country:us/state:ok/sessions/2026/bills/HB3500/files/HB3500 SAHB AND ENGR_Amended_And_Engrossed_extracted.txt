1 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 58 O.S. 2021, Section 1252, as
2 amended by Section 1, Chapter 101, O.S.L. 2023 (58 O.S. Supp. 2025,
3 Section 1252), is amended to read as follows:
4 Section 1252. A. An interest in real estate may be titled in
5 transfer-on-death form by recording a deed, signed by the record
6 owner of the interest, designating a grantee beneficiary or
7 beneficiaries of the interest. The deed shall transfer ownership of
8 the interest upon the death of the owner. A transfer-on-death deed
9 need not be supported by consideration. For purposes of the
10 Nontestamentary Transfer of Property Act, an “interest in real
11 estate” means any estate or interest in, over or under land,
12 including surface, minerals, structures and fixtures.
13 B. The signature, consent or agreement of or notice to a
14 grantee beneficiary or beneficiaries of a transfer-on-death deed
15 shall not be required for any purpose during the lifetime of the
16 record owner.
17 C. A designated grantee beneficiary may accept real estate
18 pursuant to a transfer-on-death deed only on behalf of himself,
19 herself, or a legal entity over which he or she has proper
20 authority. A beneficiary shall not accept such real estate on
21 behalf of another designated beneficiary.
22 D. Each designated grantee beneficiary wishing to accept real
23 estate pursuant to a transfer-on-death deed shall execute an
24 affidavit affirming:

1 1. Verification of the record owner’s death;
2 2. Whether the record owner and the designated beneficiary were
3 married at the time of the record owner’s death; and
4 3. A legal description of the real estate.
5 E. The Each designated grantee beneficiary shall attach sign
6 and record an affidavit and a copy of the record owner’s death
7 certificate to the beneficiary affidavit. For a record owner’s
8 death occurring on or after November 1, 2011, the beneficiary shall
9 record the affidavit and related documents with the office of the
10 county clerk where the real estate is located within nine (9) months
11 of the grantor’s death, otherwise the interest in the property
12 reverts to the deceased grantor’s estate; provided, however, for a
13 record owner’s death occurring before November 1, 2011, such
14 recording of the affidavit and related documents by the beneficiary
15 shall not be subject to the nine-month time limitation acknowledging
16 the beneficiary’s acceptance of the interest in the real estate. A
17 beneficiary may only accept real estate on his or her own behalf;
18 provided, however, multiple beneficiaries may sign the same
19 affidavit. The affidavit may be filed at any time after the death
20 of the record owner provided that the presentment date for creditors
21 has not passed for the estate of the record owner as provided in
22 subsection F of this section. Notwithstanding the provisions of
23 Section 26 of Title 16 of the Oklahoma Statutes, an affidavit
24 properly sworn to before a notary shall be received for record and

1 reco rded by the county clerk without having been acknowledged and,
2 when recorded, shall be effective as if it had been acknowledged.
3 F. If no designated grantee beneficiary claims the real estate
4 within one (1) year of the death of the record owner, such property
5 may be included in an estate of the record owner opened pursuant to
6 Title 58 of the Oklahoma Statutes. Notice shall be provided to all
7 named beneficiaries of the deed in the manner required by the
8 probate procedure for a known creditor of the estate. Any unclaimed
9 interest remaining at the expiration of the presentment date for
10 creditor claims in the probate of the estate of the record owner
11 shall be forfeited and barred from being claimed by the named
12 beneficiary and the unclaimed interest shall become property of the
13 estate. If an estate of the record owner is opened for probate
14 prior to the one-year anniversary of the date of death of the record
15 owner, the personal representative shall be required to provide a
16 new notice to each unclaimed beneficiary at his or her last-known
17 address, or if an unclaimed beneficiary's address is unknown or the
18 unclaimed beneficiary has died after the record owner but prior to
19 acceptance, then by publication in one issue of a newspaper in the
20 county in which the property is located. The unclaimed interest
21 shall not be forfeited to the estate until thirty (30) days after
22 the mailing of notice or the publication of notice in the newspaper.
23 Upon the expiration of such period, the unclaimed interest shall
24 become property of the estate.

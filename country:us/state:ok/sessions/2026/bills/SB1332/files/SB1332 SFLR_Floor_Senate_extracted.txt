12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
14 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
15 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Targeted
16 Housing and Responsible Infrastructure for Vital Economies (THRIVE)
17 Act”.
18 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1085.66 of Title 82, unless
20 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. 1. The Oklahoma Water Resources Board shall establish and
22 administer the Targeted Housing and Responsible Infrastructure for
23 Vital Economies (THRIVE) Communities Program. The purpose of the
24 program shall be to develop competitive zero-interest loans for
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1332 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 elig ible entities seeking to implement housing plans that need
2 water, wastewater, or stormwater projects for housing development
3 completion.
4 2. The Board shall develop an application preference rating
5 system for approval based on the following criteria including, but
6 not limited to:
7 a. project alignment with long-term vision and goals for
8 housing in the community,
9 b. expansion of housing stock options and fulfilling
10 identified housing needs in the area of the proposed
11 project,
12 c. defined opportunity for economic development with
13 additional housing units developed,
14 d. opportunity to expand workforce housing availability,
15 e. available financing for the housing development,
16 f. conservation and fiscal sustainability efforts for the
17 infrastructure, and
18 g. pledge of matching funds, either through the entity or
19 a third-party source.
20 3. For the purposes of this section, “eligible entity” shall
21 have the same meaning as provided in Section 1085.32 of Title 82 of
22 the Oklahoma Statutes.
23 B. All loans authorized pursuant to the provisions of this act
24 shall include a clawback provision in the funding agreement with an
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1332 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 elig ible entity. For purposes of this subsection, a “clawback
2 provision” means a condition precedent to participate in the program
3 whereby a loan recipient agrees in writing, signed by all parties,
4 to reimburse the program all or any part of the loan disbursed to
5 the recipient upon the failure of the recipient to fulfill loan
6 contract terms.
7 C. The Board shall publish an annual report on the Board’s
8 website regarding the projects completed pursuant to the provisions
9 of this act. The report shall also display the status of proposed
10 and approved projects, estimated completion dates, resulting housing
11 units completed, unused or misaligned funds recaptured, and any
12 other information deemed necessary by the Board.
13 D. Any eligible entity receiving monies pursuant to the THRIVE
14 Communities Program shall prioritize the utilization of contractors,
15 businesses, and other vendors that are based in this state pursuant
16 to Section 85.17A of Title 74 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
17 E. The Board shall promulgate rules to effectuate the
18 provisions of this act and loan criteria for the program.
19 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1085.67 of Title 82, unless
21 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 A. 1. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a
23 revolving fund for the Oklahoma Water Resources Board to be
24 designated the “THRIVE Communities Program Revolving Fund”. The
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1332 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year
2 limitations, and shall consist of all monies appropriated to the
3 fund by law. All monies accruing to the credit of the fund are
4 hereby appropriated and may be budgeted and expended by the Board
5 for the purposes provided in this act. The Board may enter into
6 contracts with financial institutions and execute such instruments
7 as may be necessary to hold and disseminate loan funds in accordance
8 with applicable regulations and Board policies.
9 2. Notwithstanding any other provisions of law, income and
10 investment return on fund principal and interest income and
11 repayment of principal on loans made from the balance of the fund
12 shall accrue to the fund.
13 B. 1. The Board may have full discretion of all monies within
14 the fund for enhancements, leveraging, and reserve capacities for
15 the Board loan and grant programs, system evaluation and risk
16 assessment assistance, and long-range infrastructure planning.
17 2. The Board may reserve up to two percent (2%) per year from
18 the fund for the purpose of administering the THRIVE Communities
19 Program. Any remaining funds may be allocated to satisfy the
20 purposes of paragraph 1 of this subsection.
21 C. The funds remaining following reserves and allocations made
22 pursuant to subsection B of this section shall be loaned to eligible
23 entities as prescribed by the program. Loan monies shall be
24 allocated based on the most current census data available from the
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1332 SFLR Page 4
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 Fede ral Decennial Census or American Community Survey and shall be
2 as follows:
3 1. Thirty-three percent (33%) of the allocated funds shall be
4 for approved projects located within a municipality or county with a
5 population of more than four hundred thousand (400,000), or the
6 equivalent thereof for other eligible entities as prescribed by the
7 Board;
8 2. Thirty-three percent (33%) of the allocated funds shall be
9 for approved projects located within a municipality or county with a
10 population that is greater than thirty thousand (30,000) but less
11 than four hundred thousand (400,000), or the equivalent thereof for
12 other eligible entities as prescribed by the Board; and
13 3. Thirty-four percent (34%) of the allocated funds shall be
14 for approved projects located within a municipality or county with a
15 population of less than thirty thousand (30,000), or the equivalent
16 thereof for other eligible entities as prescribed by the Board.
17 After fiscal year 2027, remaining reserves may be accessed by
18 any eligible entities. The Oklahoma Water Resources Board shall
19 establish rules to update allocations to ensure geographic
20 representation while maximizing the number and scale of projects
21 completed.
22 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
23 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON APPROPRIATIONS
March 4, 2026 - DO PASS AS AMENDED

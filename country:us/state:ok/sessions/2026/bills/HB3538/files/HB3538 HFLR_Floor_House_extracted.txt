23 A. The Oklahoma Legislature finds that:

1 1. It is beneficial to the State of Oklahoma to support patient
2 access to prescription drugs and pharmacy services at fair prices in
3 a market that supports optimal patient care;
4 2. The Federal Trade Commission and the United States House
5 Committee on Oversight and Government Reform have found evidence of
6 anticompetitive business tactics that have driven locally operated
7 pharmacies out of business, limiting patient choices and inflating
8 drug prices at pharmacies owned by pharmacy benefits managers; and
9 3. The State of Oklahoma wishes to minimize conflicts of
10 interest by stopping the pharmacy benefits managers acting as a "fox
11 guarding the henhouse" by being both a price setter and price taker.
12 B. It is the intent of the Legislature that the State of
13 Oklahoma shall improve health care delivery in the pharmacy market
14 for patients by eliminating certain anticompetitive business tactics
15 as a basic tenet of this act.

20 4. "Pharmacy benefits manager" includes an entity that:
21 a. is managed by a pharmacy benefits manager or is a
22 subsidiary of a pharmacy benefits manager, or

1 B. A pharmacy benefits manager shall not acquire direct or
2 indirect interest in, or otherwise hold, directly or indirectly, a
3 license under Section 358 of this title for the retail sale of drugs

6 State Board of Pharmacy shall either revoke or not renew a license
7 of an entity that violates this section.

6 Section 2306. A. The Board of Investors of the Tobacco

9 1. With the care, skill, prudence, and diligence, under the

11 like capacity and familiar with such matters would use in the
12 conduct of an enterprise of a like character and with like aims;
13 2. By diversifying the investments in the trust fund so as to
14 minimize the risk of large losses, unless, under the circumstances,
15 it is clearly prudent not to do so; and
16 3. In accordance with the laws, documents, and instruments
17 governing the trust fund.

19 managers to provide for the investment of the monies of the trust
20 fund. The investment managers shall be chosen by a solicitation of
21 proposals on a competitive basis pursuant to standards set by the
22 Board of Investors. The investment managers shall manage those
23 monies of the trust fund allocated to the investment managers in
24 compliance with the overall investment guidelines set by the Board

4 amended to read as follows:
5 Section 2307. A. On or before September 1 of each year, the
6 Board of Investors of the Tobacco Settlement Endowment Trust Fund
7 shall determine the earnings of the trust fund for the previous
8 fiscal year ending June 30. In determining earnings, the Board of
9 Investors shall certify an amount which is prudent under the
10 standard set forth in paragraph C of this section. The earnings so
11 determined and certified by the Board of Investors for each fiscal
12 year shall constitute the earnings of the Tobacco Settlement
13 Endowment Trust Fund for the purposes of Section 40 of Article X of
14 the Oklahoma Constitution.
15 B. "Earnings" consist of dividends and interest, less fees to
16 manage the fund.
17 C. In the administration of the powers to determine earnings,
18 to make and retain investments, and to delegate investment
19 management of institutional funds, the Board of Investors shall
20 exercise ordinary business care and prudence under the facts and
21 circumstances prevailing at the time of the action or decision. In
22 so doing, the Board of Investors shall consider:
23 1. Long- and short-term needs of the trust fund in carrying out
24 its purposes;

7 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
8 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-105d.1 of Title 63, unless
9 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
10 A. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving
11 fund for the State Department of Health to be designated the "TSET
12 Legacy Effort Revolving Fund". The fund shall be a continuing fund,
13 not subject to fiscal year limitations, and shall consist of all
14 monies directed to the fund by law. All monies accruing to the
15 credit of said fund are hereby appropriated and may be budgeted and
16 expended by the State Department of Health as provided in subsection
17 B of this section. Expenditures from said fund shall be made upon
18 warrants issued by the State Treasurer against claims filed as
19 prescribed by law with the Director of the Office of Management and
20 Enterprise Services for approval and payment.
21 B. 1. The State Department of Health shall utilize the funds
22 of the TSET Legacy Effort Revolving Fund to fund Legacy Effort
23 Grants;
24
HB4003 HFLR Page 9
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 2. Eligibility for Legacy Effort Grants shall be limited to
2 private, nonprofit, and public entities who were receiving a grant
3 award from the Tobacco Settlement Endowment Trust during the 2025
4 calendar year;
5 3. The selection and awarding of grants, whether in the form of
6 professional service contracts or any other funding mechanism
7 developed by the State Department of Health, awarded pursuant to the
8 grant program developed under this subsection, shall be exempt from
9 the requirements of the Oklahoma Central Purchasing Act;
10 4. The State Department of Health shall develop competitive
11 processes for awarding Legacy Effort Grants under this subsection;
12 5. The terms of any request for proposals, request for
13 applications, invitations for bid, bid notice, grant proposal, or
14 any other solicitation issued by the State Department of Health to
15 solicit or invite applications, proposals, bids, or responses to
16 obtain funding under the Legacy Effort Grants program developed
17 under this subsection shall be confidential until the date and time
18 at which the solicitation is to be made equally and uniformly known
19 to all prospective applicants and the public, at which point all
20 such documents and information shall be uniformly known to all
21 prospective applicants and the public, and shall be subject to the
22 Oklahoma Open Records Act and Oklahoma Open Meetings Act. Any
23 application, proposal, bid, or any other document to obtain funding
24 responsive to any solicitation of the State Department of Health

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 unde r the Legacy Effort Grants program developed under this
2 subsection shall be confidential until the date and time of award of
3 the grant or contract, at which point all such documents and
4 information shall be subject to the Oklahoma Open Records Act and
5 Oklahoma Open Meeting Act. Any unsolicited application, proposal,
6 bid, or any other document to obtain funding shall not be considered
7 confidential and shall be subject to the Oklahoma Open Records Act
8 and Oklahoma Open Meeting Act at all times; and
9 6. The total cost of Legacy Effort Grant awards shall not
10 exceed the monies available in the TSET Legacy Effort Revolving
11 Fund, created in section A of this section.
12 C. The State Department of Health may promulgate rules as
13 required to implement the provisions of this section.

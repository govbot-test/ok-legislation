11 An Act relating to education; defining terms;
authorizing the State Regents for Higher Education
12 and the Department of Career and Technology Education
to designate a credential of value; listing criteria
13 for a credential of value; permitting the designation
of a credential of value if the credential is
14 determined to be essential to meeting documented
state workforce needs; authorizing the State Regents
15 and the Department to adopt a definition of self-
sufficient wage; authorizing the State Regents and
16 the Department to promulgate rules and determine
procedures to implement the section; requiring annual
17 report from institutions, agencies, and schools on
data required to evaluate credentials under this
18 section; requiring the State Regents and the
Department to annually publish a statewide credential
19 of value list; authorizing the State Regents and the
Department to enter into data-sharing agreements; and
20 providing for codification.

23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 9201 of Title 70, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. As used in this section:
5 1. "Coordinating entity" means the Oklahoma State Regents for
6 Higher Education or the Oklahoma Department of Career and Technology
7 Education;
8 2. "Credential of value" means a certificate, degree, license,
9 industry-recognized credential, apprenticeship credential, or other
10 postsecondary or career-technical award issued by an educational
11 provider that meets the criteria established under this section;
12 3. "Educational provider" means any institution, agency, or
13 school coordinated by the Oklahoma State Regents for Higher
14 Education or governed by the Oklahoma Department of Career and
15 Technology Education; and
16 4. "Net cost of attendance" means the cost of attendance minus
17 all grants, scholarships, fee waivers, or other aid received by the
18 individual.
19 B. A coordinating entity may designate a credential as a
20 credential of value if the credential satisfies the following
21 criteria:
22 1. The credential provides a positive return on investment for
23 individuals who complete the credential, demonstrated by:
24
HB2398 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 a. cumulative earnings within a time period specified by
2 the coordinating entity that coordinates or governs
3 the educational provider that are equal to or exceed
4 the statewide median earnings for Oklahoma high school
5 graduates, and
6 b. earnings that allow the credential holder to recoup
7 the net cost of attendance within a time period
8 specified by the coordinating entity that coordinates
9 or governs the educational provider; and
10 2. The credential is aligned with state and regional labor
11 market demand, as determined by the coordinating entity that
12 coordinates or governs the educational provider, using data from the
13 Oklahoma Employment Security Commission, the Oklahoma Workforce
14 Commission, or other labor market sources recognized by the
15 coordinating entity.
16 C. A coordinating entity may designate a credential as a
17 credential of value even if it does not meet one or more criteria
18 under subsection B of this section if the credential is determined
19 by the coordinating entity to be essential to meeting documented
20 state workforce needs.
21 D. A coordinating entity may adopt a definition of a self-
22 sufficient wage for purposes of this section that aligns with:
23 1. Statewide self-sufficiency wage benchmarks; or
24
HB2398 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 2. Regional economic and labor market data that reflect the
2 cost of living and labor market conditions in Oklahoma.
3 E. A coordinating entity may promulgate rules and determine
4 procedures to implement the provisions of this section, including:
5 1. Standard methodologies for calculating return on investment
6 and net cost of attendance;
7 2. Criteria and data sources for assessing alignment with
8 workforce demand;
9 3. A unified statewide process for designating and periodically
10 reviewing credentials of value; and
11 4. A shared reporting structure and timeline for all education
12 providers under the coordination or governance of the coordinating
13 entity.
14 F. 1. Each educational provider shall annually report the data
15 required to evaluate credentials under this section to the
16 coordinating entity that coordinates or governs the educational
17 provider. Such data shall include, but is not limited to, the
18 following:
19 a. enrollment, completion, and credential attainment,
20 b. graduate employment rate and earnings outcomes, and
21 c. program cost information.
22 2. A coordinating entity shall annually publish a statewide
23 credential of value list, accessible to the public, identifying all
24 credentials designated under this section.
HB2398 HFLR Page 4
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 G. A coordinating entity may enter into data-sharing agreements
2 with the Oklahoma Employment Security Commission, the Oklahoma
3 Workforce Commission, and other state agencies to support the
4 evaluation and designation of credentials of value.

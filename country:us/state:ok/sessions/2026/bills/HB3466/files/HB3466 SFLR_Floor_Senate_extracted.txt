8 An Act relating to Corporation Commission; amending
62 O.S. 2021, Section 195, which relates to funds
9 created; removing Corporation Commission petty cash
fund; and providing an effective date.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 62 O.S. 2021, Section 195, is
14 amended to read as follows:
15 Section 195. A. 1. There is hereby created a petty cash fund
16 at each of the following institutions: Oklahoma School for the
17 Blind, Muskogee, Oklahoma; Oklahoma School for the Deaf, Sulphur,
18 Oklahoma; Griffin Memorial Hospital, Norman, Oklahoma; Eastern State
19 Hospital, Vinita, Oklahoma; Northern Oklahoma Resource Center of
20 Enid, Enid, Oklahoma; Southern Oklahoma Resource Center of Pauls
21 Valley, Pauls Valley, Oklahoma; Western State Psychiatric Center,
22 Fort Supply, Oklahoma; Central Oklahoma Juvenile Treatment Center,
23 Tecumseh, Oklahoma; L.E. Rader Children's Diagnostic and Evaluation
24 Center, Sand Springs, Oklahoma; L.E. Rader Intensive Treatment
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3466 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 Cent er, Sand Springs, Oklahoma; the Southwest Oklahoma Juvenile
2 Center, Manitou, Oklahoma; the Office of Juvenile Affairs' Boys'
3 Group Home, Enid, Oklahoma; the Office of Juvenile Affairs' Boys'
4 Group Home, Lawton, Oklahoma; the Office of Juvenile Affairs' Girls'
5 Group Home, Tulsa, Oklahoma; the Oklahoma Medical Center; and the
6 J.D. McCarty Center for Children with Developmental Disabilities.
7 2. The Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise
8 Services and the head of the institution involved are hereby
9 authorized and it shall be their duty to fix the maximum amount of
10 the petty cash funds. The Director of the Office of Management and
11 Enterprise Services shall prescribe all forms, systems, and
12 procedures for administering the petty cash funds of the
13 institution.
14 B. 1. There is hereby created a petty cash fund in the legal
15 division of the Department of Human Services which fund shall be
16 used solely to pay court costs, filing fees, witness fees, and
17 expenses related to any case or proceeding within the responsibility
18 of the legal division.
19 2. There is hereby created a petty cash fund in the Child
20 Support Enforcement Division of the Department of Human Services.
21 The fund shall be used solely to pay litigation expenses, including
22 court costs, filing fees, witness fees, and expenses related to any
23 case or proceeding within the responsibility of the Child Support
24 Enforcement Division.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3466 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 3. The Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise
2 Services and the Director of the Department of Human Services are
3 hereby authorized and it shall be their duty to fix the maximum
4 amount of the petty cash funds. The Director of the Office of
5 Management and Enterprise Services shall prescribe all forms,
6 systems, and procedures for administering the petty cash funds.
7 C. 1. There is hereby created a petty cash fund in the finance
8 department of the Corporation Commission which shall be used solely
9 to pay litigation expenses of the legal division, including court
10 costs, filing fees, witness fees, and other expenses related to any
11 case, proceeding, or matter within the responsibility of the legal
12 division.
13 2. The Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise
14 Services and the Corporation Commission are hereby authorized and it
15 shall be their duty to fix the maximum amount of the petty cash
16 fund, not to exceed Five Hundred Dollars ($500.00). The Director of
17 the Office of Management and Enterprise Services shall prescribe all
18 forms, systems, and procedures for administering the petty cash
19 fund.
20 D. 1. There is hereby created a petty cash fund for the
21 Property Distribution Division of the Office of Management and
22 Enterprise Services.
23 2. The amount of the Property Distribution petty cash fund
24 shall not exceed Five Hundred Dollars ($500.00). The initial amount
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3466 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 shal l be drawn by warrant from the State Surplus Property Revolving
2 Fund. The Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise
3 Services is authorized to prescribe forms, systems and procedures
4 for the administration of the Property Distribution petty cash fund.
5 E. D. 1. There is hereby created a petty cash fund in the
6 legal division of the Oklahoma Health Care Authority which fund
7 shall be used solely to pay for court costs, filing fees, witness
8 fees, and expenses related to any case or proceeding within the
9 responsibility of the legal division.
10 2. The Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise
11 Services and the Chief Executive Officer of the Oklahoma Health Care
12 Authority are hereby authorized and it shall be their duty to fix
13 the maximum amount of the petty cash fund, not to exceed Five
14 Hundred Dollars ($500.00). The Director of the Office of Management
15 and Enterprise Services shall prescribe all forms, systems, and
16 procedures for administering the petty cash fund.
17 F. E. 1. The Director of the Office of Management and
18 Enterprise Services is hereby authorized, upon request by the State
19 Commissioner of Health, to establish a petty cash fund for the State
20 Department of Health in an amount not to exceed One Thousand Dollars
21 ($1,000.00), to be used for the purpose of making change for persons
22 obtaining licenses, paying fees and fines, and transacting other
23 such business with the Department.
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB3466 SFLR Page 4
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. The fund shall be established and replenished from any
2 monies available to the Department for operating expenses.
3 3. The Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise
4 Services shall prescribe all forms, systems, and procedures for
5 administering the fund.
6 G. F. 1. There is hereby created a petty cash fund for the
7 Financial Management section of the Department of Environmental
8 Quality in an amount not to exceed Two Hundred Dollars ($200.00) to
9 be used for the purpose of making change for persons obtaining
10 licenses, paying fees and fines and transacting other such business
11 with the Department.
12 2. The fund shall be established and replenished from any
13 monies available to the Department for operating expenses.
14 3. The Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise
15 Services shall prescribe all forms, systems and procedures for
16 administering the fund.
17 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

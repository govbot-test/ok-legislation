8 An Act relating to criminal procedure; amending 22
O.S. 2021, Section 983, as last amended by Section 2,
9 Chapter 211, O.S.L. 2024 (22 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section
983), which relates to financial obligations in
10 criminal cases; directing the Cost Administration
Implementation Committee to develop and implement a
11 plan to collect certain data; requiring submission of
plan and data to the Office of the Attorney General
12 by certain date; directing certain entities to
cooperate with the Committee; providing an exception;
13 providing an effective date; and declaring an
emergency.

17 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 22 O.S. 2021, Section 983, as last
18 amended by Section 2, Chapter 211, O.S.L. 2024 (22 O.S. Supp. 2025,
19 Section 983), is amended to read as follows:
20 Section 983. A. As used in this section, unless the context
21 otherwise requires:
22 1. "Cost arrest warrant" means a warrant authorizing arrest
23 that is issued by a court under the following circumstances:

6 the county in which the court financial obligation is
7 owed within ten (10) days of being cited by a law
8 enforcement officer to appear;
9 2. "Cost cite and release warrant" means a warrant issued by a
10 court authorizing citation and release under the following
11 circumstances:
12 a. failure to comply with terms of a court financial
13 obligations payment plan, or
14 b. failure to appear at a cost hearing or willfulness
15 hearing;
16 3. "Cost hearing" means a hearing in which the court determines
17 the ability of a defendant to pay court financial obligations. Once
18 a cost hearing date has been set, all court financial obligations
19 shall be suspended until the cost hearing has been held;
20 4. "Court financial obligation" means all financial
21 obligations, including fines, costs, fees, and assessments, imposed
22 by the court or required by law to be paid, excluding restitution or
23 payments to be made other than to the court clerk;

1 5. "Payment-in-full" means a court financial payment term that
2 requires the defendant to pay the full amount of court financial
3 obligations owed within ninety (90) days of a plea or sentence in
4 the district court or within thirty (30) days of a plea or sentence
5 in the municipal court;
6 6. "Payment-in-installments" means payment terms for court
7 financial obligations that require the defendant to make monthly
8 payments in any amount until the amount owed is fully paid; and
9 7. "Willfulness hearing" means a hearing in which the court
10 determines whether a defendant who has previously been found to have
11 the ability to pay court financial obligations has willfully failed
12 to pay the debt.
13 B. 1. Except in cases provided for in Section 983b of this
14 title, when the judgment and sentence of a court, either in whole or
15 in part, imposes court financial obligations upon a defendant, the
16 court at the time of sentencing may immediately, or at any point
17 thereafter until the debt is either paid or waived, determine the
18 ability of a defendant to pay the court financial obligations. The
19 court may make such determinations at a cost hearing or upon written
20 motion or affidavit by the defendant. The ability of a defendant to
21 pay court financial obligations may not impact the sentence imposed.
22 2. Defendants with court financial obligations who are found by
23 the court to be unable to pay, in whole or in part, shall be

1 reli eved of the debt by the court through a hardship waiver of the
2 court financial obligations, either in whole or in part.
3 3. In determining the ability of a defendant to pay, the court
4 shall consider the following factors:
5 a. individual and household income,
6 b. household living expenses,
7 c. number of dependents,
8 d. assets,
9 e. child support obligations,
10 f. physical or mental health conditions that diminish the
11 ability to generate income or manage resources,
12 g. additional case-related expenses to be paid by the
13 defendant, and
14 h. any other factors relevant to the ability of the
15 defendant to pay.
16 4. In determining the ability of a defendant to pay, the
17 following shall not be considered as income or assets:
18 a. child support income,
19 b. any monies received from a federal, state, or tribal
20 government need-based or disability assistance
21 program, or
22 c. assets exempt from bankruptcy.

17 Department of Housing and Urban Development, or other
18 state, local, or federal government housing subsidy
19 program, or
20 d. total income is below one hundred fifty percent (150%)
21 of the federal poverty level.

23 inform the defendant of the total court financial obligations owed,
24 the consequences of failing to pay the court financial obligations,

10 after sentencing at the office of the court clerk to provide current
11 contact information and to either select payment terms or request a
12 cost hearing. Failure to immediately report to the court clerk
13 shall result in the full amount of court financial obligations to be
14 due thirty (30) days from the date of the plea or sentencing in
15 district courts or thirty (30) days from the date of the plea or
16 sentencing in municipal courts.
17 3. Payment of court financial obligations may be made under the
18 following terms:
19 a. payment in full, or
20 b. payment in installments.
21 Upon any change in circumstances affecting the ability of a
22 defendant to pay, a defendant may request a cost hearing before the
23 court by contacting the court clerk.

4 defendant who requests a cost hearing will receive a summons by

8 cost cite and release warrant or a cost arrest warrant. No fees
9 shall be assessed or collected from the defendant as a consequence
10 of either requesting a cost hearing or the issuing of a cost cite
11 and release warrant.
12 D. In determining the ability of the defendant to pay court
13 financial obligations, the court may rely on testimony, relevant
14 documents, and any information provided by the defendant using a
15 cost hearing affidavit promulgated by the Court of Criminal Appeals.
16 In addition, the court may make inquiry of the defendant and
17 consider any other evidence or testimony concerning the ability of
18 the defendant to pay.
19 E. 1. If at the initial cost hearing or any subsequent cost
20 hearing, the court determines that the defendant is able to pay some
21 or all of the court financial obligations, the court may order any
22 of the following conditions for payment:
23 a. payment in full,
24 b. payment in installments,

7 service.
8 2. Any defendant who fails to comply with the terms of the
9 payment plan ordered by the court shall be considered delinquent and
10 the court may issue either a cost cite and release warrant or a cost
11 arrest warrant.
12 F. If the court determines that a waiver of any of the court
13 financial obligations is warranted, the court shall apply the same
14 percentage reduction equally to all fines, costs, fees, and
15 assessments, excluding restitution.
16 G. 1. A defendant is considered delinquent in the payment of
17 court financial obligations under the following circumstances:
18 a. when the total amount due has not been paid by the due
19 date, or
20 b. when no installment payments have been received in the
21 most recent ninety-day period.

24 a delinquent defendant, notify the court which shall, within ten

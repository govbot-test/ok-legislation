24 of legislative power under the Constitution of Oklahoma; and

1 WHEREAS, the Supreme Court found, and the Legislature affirms,
2 that all monies and other assets of CompSource Mutual Insurance
3 Company are held in trust for the benefit of the insured employers
4 or policyholders.
5 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
6 OF THE 2ND SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE:
7 THAT it is a matter of public interest to establish public
8 policy that Oklahoma maintains a statutory workers' compensation
9 system in which employers may obtain insurance to cover their
10 liability for benefits for workers injured in the course of their
11 employment as prescribed by the workers' compensation laws.
12 THAT CompSource Mutual may never transition into a stock
13 insurance company.
14 THAT CompSource Mutual can only transition into a mutual
15 insurance holding company if it pays every policyholder their fair
16 and equitable ownership interest as owners and trustees of
17 CompSource Mutual.
18 THAT CompSource Mutual Insurance Company shall not be considered
19 a state agency, public body, department, or any other term used to
20 describe an entity which is a part of the Executive Branch of the
21 State of Oklahoma under any Oklahoma statute or regulation, except
22 as otherwise provided for in this resolution.
23 THAT CompSource Mutual Insurance Company is hereby prohibited
24 from pursuing any means to change its corporate structure from the

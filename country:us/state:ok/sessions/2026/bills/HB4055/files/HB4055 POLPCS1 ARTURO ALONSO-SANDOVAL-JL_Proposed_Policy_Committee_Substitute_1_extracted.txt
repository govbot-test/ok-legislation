10 An Act relating to animals; creating the Breeding
Animals Responsibly and with Care Act; requiring
11 Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and
Forestry to promulgate certain rules; creating the
12 Commercial Pet Breeders and Animal Shelter Licensing
Fund; providing primary use of fund monies; providing
13 secondary use of fund monies; allowing the Department
to provide grants to certain nonprofit animal rescue
14 organizations and licensed foster programs; providing
grant requirements; listing permissible use of grant
15 funds; granting rulemaking authority; providing that
the grant is discretionary and is not an entitlement;
16 amending 4 O.S. 2021, Sections 30.2, 30.4, and 30.5,
which relate to the Commercial Pet Breeders and
17 Animal Shelter Licensing Act; modifying definitions;
providing fine for violation; permitting forfeiture
18 of animals; providing exception; providing when an
inspection may occur; requiring no-notice annual
19 inspections; providing for noncodification; providing
for codification; and providing an effective date.

Req. No. 16385 Page 1
1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
2 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
3 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Breeding
4 Animals Responsibly and with Care (BARC) Act".
5 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
6 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
7 The Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry shall
8 promulgate rules to increase fees for commercial pet breeder,
9 noncommercial breeder, and animal shelter licenses.
10 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
11 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 30.17 of Title 4, unless there
12 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
13 A. All fees, penalties, and monies collected pursuant to the
14 Commercial Pet Breeders and Animal Shelter Licensing Act shall be
15 deposited into a revolving fund in the State Treasury to be known as
16 the Commercial Pet Breeders and Animal Shelter Licensing Fund. The
17 fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year
18 limitations, and monies in the fund shall be expended by the
19 Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry for the purposes set
20 forth in this section.
21 B. Monies in the fund shall first be used by the Department to
22 adequately administer and enforce the Commercial Pet Breeders and
23 Animal Shelter Licensing Act, including, but not limited to:
24
Req. No. 16385 Page 2
1 1. Employment, training, and retention of inspectors and
2 enforcement personnel;
3 2. Pre-license, renewal, complaint-based, and unannounced
4 inspections;
5 3. Investigations, compliance monitoring, and enforcement
6 actions;
7 4. Administrative functions necessary to carry out licensing,
8 renewals, reporting, recordkeeping, and data collection; and
9 5. Technology, equipment, and systems necessary for inspection
10 reporting, tracking, and program administration.
11 The Department shall determine annually whether sufficient
12 funding exists to meet the administrative needs of the program.
13 C. After the Department determines that the administrative and
14 enforcement needs of the program are adequately funded, any
15 remaining monies may be used, at the discretion of the Department,
16 for the following purposes:
17 1. Public education and outreach related to:
18 a. responsible breeding practices,
19 b. animal welfare standards, and
20 c. licensing and compliance requirements;
21 2. Spay and neuter programs for dogs and cats;
22 3. Educational materials, training resources, and technical
23 assistance for license holders; and
24
Req. No. 16385 Page 3
1 4. Support for animal welfare initiatives consistent with the
2 purposes of the Commercial Pet Breeders and Animal Shelter Licensing
3 Act.
4 The Department shall have discretion to prioritize the use of
5 such funds based on program needs, geographic considerations, and
6 demonstrated impact.
7 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
8 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 30.18 of Title 4, unless there
9 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
10 A. Subject to the availability of funds and after satisfaction
11 of the administrative priorities set forth in the Commercial Pet
12 Breeders and Animal Shelter Licensing Act, the Department of
13 Agriculture, Food, and Forestry may award grants from the Commercial
14 Pet Breeders and Animal Shelter Licensing Fund to eligible nonprofit
15 animal rescue organizations and licensed foster programs.
16 B. To be eligible for a grant under this section, an applicant
17 shall:
18 1. Hold a valid nonprofit rescue license or foster home license
19 issued pursuant to the Commercial Pet Breeders and Animal Shelter
20 Licensing Act;
21 2. Be in good standing with the Department and not be subject
22 to any pending enforcement action;
23
24
Req. No. 16385 Page 4
1 3. Be a nonprofit organization exempt from federal income
2 taxation under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, if
3 applicable; and
4 4. Comply with all applicable state and local animal welfare
5 laws and rules.
6 C. Grant funds awarded pursuant to this section may be used for
7 purposes including, but not limited to:
8 1. Veterinary care, including emergency medical treatment;
9 2. Spay and neuter services;
10 3. Temporary housing, foster support, and animal transport;
11 4. Supplies, equipment, and materials necessary for animal
12 care; and
13 5. Capacity-building efforts to support intake, rehabilitation,
14 and rehoming of animals.
15 D. The Department shall promulgate rules establishing
16 procedures and criteria for the award of grants under this section,
17 including:
18 1. Application requirements;
19 2. Award limits and funding priorities;
20 3. Reporting and accountability standards; and
21 4. Measures to ensure geographic and programmatic balance.
22 E. Nothing in this section shall be construed to create an
23 entitlement to grant funding or to require the Department to award
24 grants in any fiscal year.

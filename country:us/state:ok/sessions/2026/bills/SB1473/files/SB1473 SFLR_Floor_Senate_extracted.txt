11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
12 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 2, Chapter 183, O.S.L.
13 2023 (30 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 31), is amended to read as
14 follows:
15 Section 31. A. As used in this act the Courtney Smith Act:
16 1. “General guardianship” means a relationship where a person
17 has been appointed by a court to serve as the guardian of an
18 incapacitated person to ensure that the essential requirements for
19 the health and safety of the person are met, to manage the estate or
20 financial resources of the person, or both;
21 2. “Gravely disabled” means a condition in which a person,
22 because of a mental illness, is unable to provide for his or her
23 basic personal needs for food, clothing, or shelter. Such condition
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1473 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 shal l be considered an imminent danger to self when the criteria
2 described in subsection B of this section are met;
3 3. “Inpatient mental health treatment” means a treatment
4 service offered or provided for a continuous period of more than
5 twenty-four (24) forty-eight (48) hours in residence after admission
6 to a mental health or substance abuse treatment facility for the
7 purpose of observation, evaluation, or treatment; and
8 4. “Ward” means a person over whom a guardian is appointed and
9 a person over whose property a guardian or conservator is appointed.
10 B. A guardian who has general guardianship and who has obtained
11 an order by a court for inpatient mental health treatment for the
12 ward may apply for an order requiring either municipal or county
13 officials to retrieve, only if in an unsheltered environment, and
14 deliver the gravely disabled ward to an inpatient treatment
15 facility, pursuant to Section 1-110 of Title 43A of the Oklahoma
16 Statutes, when one of the following criteria is met:
17 1. The ward is unable to utilize the means available to provide
18 for his or her basic personal needs regarding food, clothing, or
19 shelter. Considerations that shall be made when making this
20 evaluation shall include, but not be limited to, the following:
21 a. whether lab examinations reveal signs of malnutrition
22 or dehydration,
23
24

1 b. whether there is observed, documented behavior showing
2 an inability to consume adequate amounts of food or
3 water due to a mental illness,
4 c. whether there is a history of public nudity or
5 inadvertent exhibitionism which has been observed and
6 documented and is due to a mental illness,
7 d. the existence of physical evidence of exposure to the
8 environment due to mental illness symptoms which
9 prevent the ward from wearing adequate clothing,
10 e. whether there is observed behavior and symptoms of a
11 mental illness which prevent the ward from utilizing
12 or obtaining adequate shelter,
13 f. the existence of a repeated and recent history of
14 failure to maintain adequate shelter in the community
15 due to behaviors and symptoms of a mental illness, or
16 g. evidence of a failure to maintain a shelter in a
17 manner that is safe to live in, due to symptoms of a
18 mental illness;
19 2. The ward is unable to voluntarily request and receive
20 assistance for his or her basic personal needs; or
21 3. The ward is unable to survive safely without involuntary
22 detention and does not have the help of family members, friends, or
23 others to provide the ward’s basic personal needs regarding food,
24 clothing, or shelter.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1473 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 C. No person shall be presumed to be incompetent because the
2 person has been evaluated or treated for a mental illness,
3 regardless of whether such evaluation or treatment was voluntarily
4 or involuntarily received.
5 D. A guardian who has general guardianship and has been granted
6 an order pursuant to subsection B of this section shall be present
7 for the ward’s intake assessment with the inpatient mental health
8 treatment center.
9 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

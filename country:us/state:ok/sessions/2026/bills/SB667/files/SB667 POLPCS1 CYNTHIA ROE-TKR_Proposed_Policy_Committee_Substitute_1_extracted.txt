11 An Act relating to chiropractors; amending 59 O.S.
2021, Sections 161.2, 161.3, 161.7, 161.8, and 161.9,
12 which relate to the Oklahoma Chiropractic Practice
Act; modifying definitions; requiring use of
13 licensure portals; modifying certain licensing
requirements; modifying requirements for notification
14 of disapproval; updating statutory language;
repealing 59 O.S. 2021, Section 161.12a, which
15 relates to registration as chiropractic claims
consultant; and providing an effective date.

19 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 161.2, is
20 amended to read as follows:
21 Section 161.2. A. Chiropractic is the science and art that
22 teaches health in anatomic relation and disease or abnormality in
23 anatomic disrelation, and includes hygienic, sanitary and
24 therapeutic measures incident thereto in humans. The scope of

4 the Board of Chiropractic Examiners.
5 B. A chiropractic physician may engage in the practice of
6 animal chiropractic diagnosis and treatment if certified to do so by
7 the Board. A licensed chiropractic physician may provide
8 chiropractic treatment to an animal without being certified in
9 animal chiropractic diagnosis and treatment by the Board if an
10 animal has been referred to the chiropractic physician by a licensed
11 veterinarian in writing.

14 Section 161.3. As used in the Oklahoma Chiropractic Practice

17 1. “Accredited chiropractic college program” means a

19 accredited by an accrediting agency recognized by the U.S.
20 Department of Education;

20 7. “Examination” means the process used by the Board, prior to

6 information. It shall also mean the discussion of philosophy as it
7 relates to the performance of chiropractic;
8 10. “Original license” means a license granting initial
9 authorization to practice chiropractic in this state issued by the
10 Board to an applicant found by the Board to meet the licensing
11 requirements of the Oklahoma Chiropractic Practice Act, by
12 examination pursuant to Section 161.7 of this title, or by

14 11. “Preceptor” means a chiropractic physician who is
15 participating in the Chiropractic Undergraduate Preceptorship

17 12. “Relocation of practice” means the recognition and approval

21 13. “Renewal license” means a license issued to a chiropractic
22 physician by the Board, on or before the first day of July of each
23 year, which authorizes such licensee to practice chiropractic in
24 this state during the succeeding calendar year.

6 denial of an application for a permanent license.

8 in this state shall submit to the Board of Chiropractic Examiners
9 documentary evidence of completion of:
10 1. A course of resident study of not less than four (4) years
11 of nine (9) months each in an accredited chiropractic college A
12 valid degree of Doctor of Chiropractic from an accredited program.
13 A senior student at an accredited chiropractic college program may
14 make application for an original license by examination prior to

18 2. Parts I, II, III, IV and physiotherapy as administered by

20 and

24 chiropractic college program. For those graduating from a

1 chir opractic program outside the United States, the applicants must

6 in a foreign language submitted to the Board by such applicants
7 shall be accompanied by notarized English translations.
8 E. International applicants shall provide satisfactory evidence
9 of meeting the requirements for permanent residence or temporary
10 nonimmigrant status as set forth by the United States Citizenship
11 and Immigration Services.

13 shall submit to the Board documentary evidence that the applicant
14 has malpractice insurance. New applicants shall submit to the Board
15 documentary evidence that the applicant has malpractice insurance

6 reasonable threat to public safety; and
7 4. If requested, appear before the Board for a personal
8 interview.
9 H. No later than one (1) year after receiving a license to
10 practice in Oklahoma, chiropractic physicians shall complete an
11 orientation course of training approved by the Board. The
12 orientation course hours shall count as continuing education credits
13 for the year in which they were earned. An association may provide
14 the orientation course of training.
15 I. The Board may issue an original license to those applicants
16 who have passed the required examination with a score acceptable to
17 the Board and who meet all other requirements set forth by the
18 Board. No license fee shall be charged by the Board for the balance
19 of the calendar year in which such a license is issued.
20 J. In addition to an applicant’s failure to meet any other
21 requirements imposed by this section or other applicable law, the

5 2. Been the subject of disciplinary action by the Board; or
6 3. Been the subject of disciplinary action in another

8 K. As used in this section:
9 1. “Substantially relates” means the nature of criminal conduct

13 2. “Poses a reasonable threat” means the nature of criminal
14 conduct for which the person was convicted involved an act or threat

16 to serve the public or work with others in the occupation.

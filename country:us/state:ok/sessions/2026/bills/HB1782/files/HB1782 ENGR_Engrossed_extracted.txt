1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 201 of Title 75A, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving
5 fund to be designated the "Oklahoma AI Education Innovation
6 Revolving Fund". The fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject
7 to fiscal year limitations, and shall consist of:
8 1. All monies appropriated to the fund by the Legislature;
9 2. All monies received from gifts, grants, reimbursements, or
10 other contributions from public or private sources for purposes
11 consistent with this act;
12 3. Any interest or investment earnings attributable to monies
13 in the fund; and
14 4. Any other monies designated for deposit in the fund.
15 B. All monies accruing to the credit of the Oklahoma AI
16 Education Innovation Revolving Fund are hereby appropriated and
17 shall be budgeted and distributed by the Office of Management and
18 Enterprise Services as follows:
19 1. Forty-two and two-tenths percent (42.2%) to the State
20 Department of Education;
21 2. Forty-two and two-tenths percent (42.2%) to the Oklahoma
22 State Regents for Higher Education;
23 3. Eight and nine-tenths percent (8.9%) to the Oklahoma
24 Department of Career and Technology Education; and

1 4. Six and seven-tenths percent (6.7%) to the Oklahoma
2 Department of Libraries.
3 C. Distributions from the fund shall be made quarterly,
4 beginning on the first day of the fiscal quarter following the
5 effective date of this act, and on the first day of each fiscal
6 quarter thereafter. The Office of Management and Enterprise
7 Services shall transfer the amounts specified in subsection B of
8 this section to each designated agency within ten (10) business days
9 of the beginning of each fiscal quarter.
10 D. Monies distributed from the Oklahoma AI Education Innovation
11 Revolving Fund shall be expended by the receiving agencies solely
12 for one or more of the following purposes related to artificial
13 intelligence in education:
14 1. Development, acquisition, and implementation of artificial
15 intelligence tools, platforms, and resources for educational use;
16 2. Professional development and training for educators,
17 administrators, librarians, and staff on the effective and ethical
18 use of artificial intelligence in educational settings;
19 3. Curriculum development integrating artificial intelligence
20 concepts, digital literacy, and computational thinking;
21 4. Research and evaluation of artificial intelligence
22 applications in education;
23 5. Student programs and initiatives promoting artificial
24 intelligence literacy and workforce readiness;
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1782 Page 3

1 6. Infrastructure improvements necessary to support artificial
2 intelligence educational initiatives;
3 7. Public awareness and community education programs regarding
4 artificial intelligence; and
5 8. Administrative costs directly related to the purposes
6 enumerated in this subsection, provided that no more than five
7 percent (5%) of any agency's annual distribution may be expended for
8 administrative costs.
9 E. Monies in the Oklahoma AI Education Innovation Revolving
10 Fund, and monies distributed therefrom to the agencies designated in
11 subsection B of this section, shall be used solely for the purposes
12 specified in subsection D of this section and for no other purpose
13 whatsoever. Any appropriation, transfer, expenditure, or use of any
14 monies in the fund or distributed from the fund for any purpose not
15 authorized by subsection D of this section shall be null and void
16 and of no effect.
17 F. Monies distributed from the fund to the agencies designated
18 in subsection B of this section that are not expended during a
19 fiscal year shall not revert to the fund or to the General Revenue
20 Fund but shall be retained by the receiving agency and shall remain
21 available for expenditure in subsequent fiscal years for the
22 purposes specified in subsection D of this section.
23 G. No later than October 1 of each year, each agency receiving
24 monies from the Oklahoma AI Education Innovation Revolving Fund

8 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
9 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 204 of Title 75A, unless there
10 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
11 A. The Oklahoma AI Education Advisory Council shall meet at
12 least quarterly at the call of the chair. Additional meetings may
13 be called by the chair, or upon written request of a majority of the
14 members.
15 B. At its first meeting, the Council shall elect from among its
16 members a chair and a vice-chair who shall serve for terms of two
17 (2) years. The chair and vice-chair may be reelected.
18 C. A majority of the members of the Council shall constitute a
19 quorum for the transaction of business. All official actions of the
20 Council shall require the affirmative vote of a majority of those
21 members present and voting.
22 D. The Council may establish subcommittees as it deems
23 necessary to carry out its duties. Subcommittees may include
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1782 Page 11

1 indi viduals who are not members of the Council, provided that such
2 individuals shall serve in a nonvoting advisory capacity.
3 E. All meetings of the Council and its subcommittees shall be
4 subject to the Oklahoma Open Meeting Act.
5 F. The State Department of Education, the Oklahoma State
6 Regents for Higher Education, the Oklahoma Department of Career and
7 Technology Education, and the Oklahoma Department of Libraries shall
8 provide staff support, information, and assistance to the Council as
9 requested. The agencies may designate staff to serve jointly as
10 support for the Council and may share the costs of such support
11 proportionally based on the distribution percentages specified in
12 Section 1 of this act.

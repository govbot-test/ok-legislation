8 An Act relating to artificial intelligence education;
creating the Oklahoma AI Education Innovation Act;
9 creating the Oklahoma AI Education Innovation
Revolving Fund; providing funding sources;
10 establishing the Office of Management and Enterprise
Services as the agency to distribute funds;
11 establishing how often funds shall be distributed;
providing allowable uses of funds by agencies;
12 clarifying monies are limited to specified purposes;
establishing that monies not expended shall remain
13 with receiving agencies; requiring annual submission
of report; directing the State Treasurer to maintain
14 separate accounts; creating the Oklahoma AI Education
Advisory Council; providing the membership of
15 Council; providing terms of office for members of the
Council; exempting members from dual office holding
16 prohibition; clarifying members shall receive no
compensation other than necessary travel expenses;
17 providing the powers and duties of the Council;
clarifying limitations of Council's power; directing
18 the Council to submit annual report; directing the
Council to meet at least quarterly; requiring the
19 election of a chair and vice-chair; establishing that
a majority of members constitutes a quorum;
20 permitting the establishment of subcommittees;
clarifying all meetings are subject to the Oklahoma
21 Open Meeting Act; directing agencies to provide
staffing support; making an appropriation; providing
22 for severability; providing for codification;
providing an effective date; and declaring an
23 emergency.

19 in the fund; and
20 4. Any other monies designated for deposit in the fund.

16 b. coordination among agencies to maximize the
17 effectiveness of artificial intelligence education
18 investments,
19 c. best practices for integrating artificial intelligence
20 into curriculum and instruction,
21 d. ethical considerations and responsible use of
22 artificial intelligence in educational settings,
23 e. workforce preparation for careers involving artificial
24 intelligence, and

1 f. digital literacy and equitable access to artificial
2 intelligence educational resources;
3 2. Review the annual reports submitted by agencies pursuant to

5 3. Identify gaps, redundancies, or opportunities for

7 Education Innovation Revolving Fund;
8 4. Monitor national and international trends in artificial
9 intelligence education and make recommendations for Oklahoma to

11 5. Engage with stakeholders including educators, students,

14 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified

17 A. The Oklahoma AI Education Advisory Council shall meet at

19 be called by the chair, or upon written request of a majority of the
20 members.
21 B. At its first meeting, the Council shall elect from among its
22 members a chair and a vice-chair who shall serve for terms of two
23 (2) years. The chair and vice-chair may be reelected.

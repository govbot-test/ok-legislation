7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
8 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 1, Chapter 493, O.S.L.
9 2025 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 698.4), is amended to read as
10 follows:
11 Section 698.4. A. As used in this section, "eligible school
12 district" means a public school district or public school district
13 foundation in this state that has established a program to provide
14 tuition or loan repayment assistance to school district employees
15 who pursue:
16 1. Pursue an undergraduate degree from a teacher preparation
17 program accredited by the Commission for Educational Quality and
18 Accountability leading to a standard teaching certificate; or
19 2. Are enrolled in nondegree-seeking coursework required for
20 completion of an approved alternative certification pathway from a
21 teacher preparation program accredited by the Commission for
22 Educational Quality and Accountability leading to a standard
23 teaching certificate.
24
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2045 Page 2
1 B. There is hereby created the Grow Your Own Educator Program
2 to provide matching fund grants to eligible school districts. The
3 program shall be established and maintained by the State Department
4 of Education.
5 C. An eligible school district seeking to participate in the
6 Grow Your Own Educator Program shall submit an application in the
7 manner and on a form prescribed by the State Department of
8 Education. The application shall include:
9 1. The amount of matching fund grants requested;
10 2. The position of the employee pursuing an undergraduate
11 degree or alternate certification coursework from a teacher
12 preparation program; and
13 3. The subject area in which the employee is pursuing an
14 undergraduate degree or alternate certification from a teacher
15 preparation program.
16 D. Matching fund Fund grants shall be awarded on a first-come,
17 first-served basis. Grants shall be provided without any limitation
18 other than the amount of funds available in the Grow Your Own
19 Educator Revolving Fund created pursuant to Section 2 of this act
20 and the number of eligible school districts that apply.
21 E. By June 30, 2026 2027, and each June 30 thereafter, an
22 eligible school district that receives a matching fund grant
23 pursuant to this section shall electronically submit a report to the
24 State Department of Education that includes:
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2045 Page 3
1 1. A status update on the progress the employee receiving
2 tuition assistance is making in the teacher preparation program in
3 which he or she is enrolled; or
4 2. The employment status of the employee receiving loan
5 repayment assistance.
6 F. By December 31, 2026 2027, and each December 31 thereafter,
7 the State Department of Education shall electronically submit a
8 report to the Governor, the President Pro Tempore of the Senate, and
9 the Speaker of the House of Representatives that includes:
10 1. The school district matching fund grant recipients;
11 2. The amount of matching fund grants allocated to each
12 recipient;
13 3. The subject area in which the employee receiving tuition
14 assistance is pursuing an undergraduate degree, or the subject area
15 in which the employee receiving loan repayment assistance has
16 received an undergraduate degree; and
17 4. The information submitted pursuant to subsection E of this
18 section.
19 G. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules to
20 implement the provisions of this section.
21 H. 1. Employees enrolled in an approved alternative
22 certification pathway as described in subsection A of this section
23 shall be eligible for tuition or course fee reimbursement through
24 the Grow Your Own Educator Program.
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2045 Page 4
1 2. Eligible coursework for alternative certification may
2 include, but is not limited to, classes in classroom management,
3 pedagogy, student assessment, special education, and instructional
4 methodology, as determined by the State Department of Education.
5 3. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules to
6 identify and approve eligible nondegree-seeking courses and
7 alternative certification programs that qualify under this section.
8 SECTION 2. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
9 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
10 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
11 be in full force from and after its passage and approval."

Section 3:
Section 11-103.9, which relates to physical education
7 program requirements; requiring certain schools to
provide students in certain grades certain amount of
8 daily recess as a condition of accreditation;
allowing daily recess to be broken into segments;
9 defining term; requiring daily recess to occur
outdoors when permitted; removing language
10 encouraging certain amount of recess in certain
grades; requiring certain subject matter standards to
11 address certain plan; updating statutory language;
providing an effective date; and declaring an
12 emergency.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 11-103.9, is
17 amended to read as follows:
18 Section 11-103.9. A. Except as otherwise provided for in this
19 section, the State Board of Education shall require, as a condition
20 of accreditation, that school districts provide to all students
21 physical education programs which may include athletics.
22 B. The Board shall require, as a condition of accreditation,
23 that public elementary schools provide instruction, for students in
24 full-day kindergarten and grades one through five, in physical
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2045 Page 1
1 educ ation or exercise programs for a minimum of an average of sixty
2 (60) minutes each week. The time students participate in recess
3 shall not be counted toward the sixty-minutes-per-week physical
4 education requirement. Schools may exclude from participation in
5 the physical education or exercise programs required in this
6 subsection those students who have been placed into an in-house in-
7 school suspension or detention class or placement or those students
8 who are under an in-school restriction or are subject to an
9 administrative disciplinary action.
10 C. The Board shall require, as a condition of accreditation,
11 that public elementary schools provide to students in full-day
12 kindergarten and grades one through five, in addition to the
13 requirements set forth in subsection B of this section, an average
14 of sixty (60) minutes each week of physical activity, which may
15 include, but not be limited to, physical education, exercise
16 programs, fitness breaks, recess, and classroom activities, and
17 wellness and nutrition education. Each school district board of
18 education shall determine the specific activities and means of
19 compliance with the provisions of this subsection, giving
20 consideration to the recommendations of each school’s Healthy and
21 Fit School Advisory Committee as submitted to the school principal
22 pursuant to the provisions of Section 24-100a of this title.
23 D. The Board shall require, as a condition of accreditation,
24 that public elementary schools provide to students in full-day pre-
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2045 Page 2
1 kind ergarten, full-day kindergarten, and grades one through five, in
2 addition to the requirements of subsections B and C of this section,
3 at least thirty (30) minutes of daily recess. The daily recess
4 required by this subsection may be broken up into segments to
5 accommodate each school district’s instructional day. For the
6 purposes of this section, “daily recess” means a scheduled break or
7 breaks during the school day that allow students to engage in
8 unstructured free play or physical activity. Daily recess shall
9 occur outdoors when weather and other relevant conditions permit.
10 E. The Board shall disseminate information to each school
11 district on the benefits of physical education programs and shall
12 strongly encourage districts to provide physical education
13 instruction and daily recess to students in grades six through
14 twelve. The Board shall also strongly encourage school districts to
15 incorporate physical activity into the school day by providing to
16 students in full-day kindergarten and grades one through five at
17 least a twenty-minute daily recess, which shall be in addition to
18 the sixty (60) minutes of physical education as required by
19 subsection B of this section, and by allowing all students brief
20 physical activity breaks throughout the day, physical activity
21 clubs, and special events.
22 E. F. Instruction in physical education required in by this
23 section shall be aligned with the subject matter standards as
24 adopted by the Board.
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2045 Page 3
1 F. G. The physical education curriculum shall be sequential,
2 developmentally appropriate, and designed, implemented, and
3 evaluated to enable students to develop the motor and self-
4 management skills and knowledge necessary to participate in physical
5 activity throughout life. Each school district shall establish
6 specific objectives and goals the district intends to accomplish
7 through the physical education curriculum.
8 G. H. In identifying the essential knowledge and skills, the
9 State Board of Education shall ensure that the subject matter
10 standards for physical education:
11 1. Emphasize the knowledge and skills capable of being used
12 during a lifetime of regular physical activity;
13 2. Are consistent with national physical education standards
14 for:
15 a. the information that students should learn about
16 physical activity, and
17 b. the physical activities that students should be able
18 to perform;
19 3. Require that, on a weekly basis, at least fifty percent
20 (50%) of the physical education class be used for actual student
21 physical activity and that the activity be, to the extent
22 practicable, at a moderate or vigorous level;
23 4. Offer students an opportunity to choose among many types of
24 physical activity in which to participate;
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2045 Page 4
1 5. Offer students both cooperative and competitive games;
2 6. Meet the needs of students of all physical ability levels,
3 including students who have a disability, chronic health problem, or
4 other special need that precludes the student from participating in
5 regular physical education instruction but who might be able to
6 participate in physical education that is suitably adapted and, if
7 applicable, included in the student’s individualized education
8 program developed pursuant to the Individuals with Disabilities
9 Education Act (IDEA) or Section 504 Plan developed pursuant to the
10 Rehabilitation Act of 1973;
11 7. Teach self-management and movement skills;
12 8. Teach cooperation, fair play, and responsible participation
13 in physical activity;
14 9. Promote student participation in physical activity outside
15 of school; and
16 10. Allow physical education classes to be an enjoyable
17 experience for students.
18 H. I. The Board shall adopt promulgate rules to implement the
19 provisions of this section.
20 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
21 SECTION 5. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
22 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
23 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
24 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

23 amended to read as follows:

1 Section 7-403. As used in the Oklahoma Milk and Milk Products
2 Act:
3 1. “Bulk milk hauler/sampler” means any person collecting
4 official samples who may transport raw milk from a farm or raw milk
5 products to or from a milk plant, receiving station, or transfer
6 station and has in his or her possession a permit from any state to
7 sample these products;
8 2. “Dairy farm” means any premises owned or operated by a “milk
9 producer” milk producer where one or more cows or goats hooved
10 mammals are kept, and from which a part or all of the milk or milk
11 products is sold or offered for sale;
12 3. “Goat milk” means the lacteal secretion, practically free
13 from colostrum, obtained by the complete milking of one or more
14 healthy goats;
15 4. “Grade A milk and milk products” means milk and milk
16 products at any stage from production through pasteurization and
17 packaging, that are of the minimum quality and are produced, hauled,
18 processed and distributed under conditions which meet the
19 requirements of the Oklahoma Milk and Milk Products Act;
20 5. 4. “Inhibitor” means any chemical or antibiotic substance
21 which inhibits or retards the growth of bacteria in milk;
22 6. 5. “Milk” means the lacteal secretion, practically free from
23 colostrum, obtained by the complete milking of one or more healthy
24 cows hooved mammals, which contains not less than eight and one-

1 four th percent (8 1/4%) milk solids-not-fat and not less than three
2 and one-fourth percent (3 1/4%) milk fat. The term “milk” milk
3 shall include goat hooved mammal milk, fluid market milk, and milk
4 products for the purpose of distribution to the consumer, and may
5 contain optional ingredients defined by the State Board of
6 Agriculture and shall include ungraded and Grade A milk;
7 7. 6. “Milk contact surface” means any pipe, tank, equipment,
8 or facility which contacts or may contact milk during the producing,
9 transporting, processing, or distributing of milk and milk products;
10 8. 7. “Milk distribution center” means any premises, owned or
11 operated by a “milk distributor” milk distributor, which distributes
12 and sells or offers to sell milk and milk products but does not
13 include an establishment which sells or offers to sell Grade A milk
14 and milk products at retail;
15 9. 8. “Milk facility” means the producing, processing,
16 transporting, or distributing premises and facilities which operate

18 compliance with the Oklahoma Milk and Milk Products Act and the
19 rules of the Board;
20 10. 9. “Milk fat” or “butter fat” means the fat of milk;

8 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 7-415, is
9 amended to read as follows:
10 Section 7-415. A. A fee of one cent ($0.01) shall be assessed
11 on each one hundred (100) pounds of raw milk produced in this state.
12 Each co-op, marketing agent, or processing plant where the raw milk
13 is not collected by a co-op or marketing agent, doing business in
14 Oklahoma this state shall pay the fees each calendar month to the
15 Commissioner of Agriculture to be credited to the Milk and Milk
16 Products Inspection Revolving Fund of the State Treasury.
17 B. There is hereby assessed a fee of one cent ($0.01) on each
18 one hundred (100) pounds of Grade A milk or milk products processed
19 or offered for retail sale in Oklahoma this state. Each milk plant
20 doing business in Oklahoma this state shall pay the fees each
21 calendar month to the Commissioner to be credited to the Milk and
22 Milk Products Inspection Revolving Fund of the State Treasury.
23 C. A fee assessed on each one hundred (100) pounds of milk or
24 milk products produced or processed in another state and imported
Req. No. 17427 Page 5
1 into Oklahoma this state shall be the same amount assessed on milk
2 or milk products shipped to that state from Oklahoma this state.
3 The person shipping or causing the shipment of milk or milk products
4 shall be responsible for paying the fees semi-annually on January 1
5 and July 1 of each year to the Commissioner and be credited to the
6 Milk and Milk Products Inspection Revolving Fund of the State
7 Treasury.
8 SECTION 4. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 7-421, is
9 amended to read as follows:
10 Section 7-421. The Oklahoma State Department of Agriculture,
11 Food, and Forestry is hereby designated as the official state agency
12 of this state for purposes of cooperating with and implementing the
13 dairy inspection and grading program under the jurisdiction of the
14 United States Department of Agriculture, the United States
15 Department of Health and Human Services, the United States Public
16 Health Service, and the United States Food and Drug Administration.
17 SECTION 5. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 17 O.S. 2021, Section 160.14, is
15 amended to read as follows:
16 Section 160.14. A. The owner of a wind energy facility shall
17 be responsible, at its expense, for the proper decommissioning of
18 the facility upon abandonment or the end of the useful life of the
19 commercial wind energy equipment in the wind energy facility.
20 B. Proper decommissioning of a wind energy facility shall
21 include:
22 1. Removal of wind turbines, towers, buildings, cabling,
23 electrical components, foundations, and any other associated
24 facilities, to a depth of thirty (30) inches below grade that
Req. No. 3824 Page 1
1 rest ores the property to, as near as reasonably possible, the
2 condition of the property prior to the establishment of the wind
3 energy facility; and
4 2. Disturbed earth being graded and reseeded or otherwise
5 restored to substantially the same physical condition as existed
6 prior to the construction of the wind energy facility by the owner,
7 excluding roads, unless the landowner specifically requests in
8 writing that the roads or other land surface areas be restored.
9 C. The decommissioning of the wind energy facility, or
10 individual pieces of commercial wind energy equipment, shall be
11 completed as follows:
12 1. By the owner of the wind energy facility within twelve (12)
13 months after abandonment or the end of the useful life of the
14 commercial wind energy equipment in the wind energy facility; and
15 2. If the owner of the wind energy facility fails to complete
16 the decommissioning within the period prescribed in paragraph 1 of
17 this subsection, the Corporation Commission shall take such measures
18 as are necessary to complete the decommissioning.
19 D. A lease or other agreement between a landowner and an owner
20 of a wind energy facility may contain provisions for decommissioning
21 that are either more or less restrictive than provided for in this
22 section.
23 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 17 O.S. 2021, Section 160.15, is
24 amended to read as follows:
Req. No. 3824 Page 2
1 Section 160.15. A. The owner of a wind energy facility shall
2 submit to the Corporation Commission evidence of financial security
3 to cover the anticipated costs of decommissioning the wind energy
4 facility. For a wind energy facility or portion thereof which
5 reaches the commercial generation date prior to December 31, 2016,
6 the evidence of financial security shall be submitted after the
7 fifteenth year of operation of the facility. For a wind energy
8 facility or portion thereof which reaches the commercial generation
9 date on or after December 31, 2016, the evidence of financial
10 security shall be submitted by the fifth year of operation of the
11 facility. Evidence of financial security may shall be in the form
12 of cash or a surety bond, collateral bond, parent guaranty, cash,
13 cashier’s check, certificate of deposit, bank joint custody receipt
14 or other approved negotiable instrument as established in rules
15 promulgated by the Commission underwritten by surety companies
16 listed as certified insurers acceptable as sureties and reinsurers
17 on federal bonds as published in the Federal Register, Treasury
18 Circular 570, latest revision.
19 B. 1. For a wind energy facility which reaches the commercial
20 generation date prior to December 31, 2016, the evidence of
21 financial security shall be accompanied by an estimate of the total
22 cost of decommissioning, minus the salvage value of the equipment,
23 prepared by a professional engineer licensed in this state. The
24 amount of the evidence of financial security shall be either:
Req. No. 3824 Page 3
1 a. the estimate of the total cost of decommissioning
2 minus the salvage value of the equipment which shall
3 be submitted to the Commission in the fifteenth year
4 of the project and every tenth year thereafter for the
5 life of the wind energy facility, or
6 b. one hundred twenty-five percent (125%) of the estimate
7 of the total cost of decommissioning which shall be
8 submitted to the Commission in the fifteenth year of
9 the project.
10 2. For a wind energy facility which reaches the commercial
11 generation date on or after December 31, 2016, the evidence of
12 financial security shall be accompanied by an estimate of the total
13 cost of decommissioning and an estimate of the salvage value of the
14 equipment prepared by a professional engineer licensed in this
15 state. The amount of the evidence of financial security shall be
16 one hundred twenty-five percent (125%) of the estimate of the total
17 cost of decommissioning, minus the salvage value of the equipment,
18 which shall be submitted to the Commission by the fifth year after
19 reaching the commercial generation date and thereafter upon request
20 by the Commission.
21 C. If the owner of a wind energy facility fails to submit the
22 information with the Commission as is required by this section, the
23 owner shall be subject to an administrative penalty not to exceed
24 One Thousand Five Hundred Dollars ($1,500.00) per day.
Req. No. 3824 Page 4
1 D. In the event of a transfer of ownership of a wind energy
2 facility, the evidence of financial security posted by the
3 transferor shall remain in place and shall not be released until
4 such time as evidence of financial security meeting the requirements
5 of this section is posted by the new owner of the wind energy
6 facility and deemed acceptable by the Commission.
7 E. The provisions of this section shall apply to any wind
8 energy facility or portion thereof entering into or renewing a power
9 purchase agreement (PPA) for the energy generated by the wind energy
10 facility on or after January 1, 2011. If a wind energy facility
11 does not sell its energy under a power purchase agreement, the
12 provisions of this section shall apply to the wind energy facility
13 or portion thereof which construction commences on or after January
14 1, 2011.
15 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY Section 6, Chapter 25, O.S.L. 2025
16 (17 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 820.5), is amended to read as follows:
17 Section 820.5. A. A solar power facility agreement shall
18 provide that the grantee obtain and deliver to the landowner
19 evidence of financial assurance that conforms to the requirements of
20 this section to secure the performance of the grantee’s obligation
21 to remove the grantee’s solar power facilities located on the
22 landowner’s property as described by Section 5 of this act 820.4 of
23 this title. Acceptable forms Evidence of financial assurance
24 include a parent company guaranty with a minimum investment grade
Req. No. 3824 Page 5
1 cred it rating for the parent company issued by a major domestic
2 credit rating agency, a letter of credit, a bond, or another form of
3 financial assurance reasonably acceptable to the landowner shall be
4 in the form of cash or a surety bond underwritten by surety
5 companies listed as certified insurers acceptable as sureties and
6 reinsurers on federal bonds as published in the Federal Register,
7 Treasury Circular 570, latest revision.
8 B. The amount of the financial assurance must shall be at
9 either:
10 1. At least equal to the estimated amount by which the cost of
11 removing the solar power facilities from the landowner’s property
12 and restoring the property to, as near as reasonably possible, the
13 condition of the property as of the date the agreement begins
14 exceeds the salvage value of the solar power facilities, less any
15 portion of the value of the solar power facilities pledged to secure
16 outstanding debt; or
17 2. One hundred twenty-five percent (125%) of the estimate of
18 the total cost of decommissioning.
19 C. The agreement shall provide that:
20 1. The estimated cost of removing the solar power facilities
21 from the landowner’s property and restoring the property to, as near
22 as reasonably possible, the condition of the property as of the date
23 the agreement begins and the estimated salvage value of the solar
24
Req. No. 3824 Page 6
1 powe r facilities must shall be determined by an independent, third-
2 party professional engineer licensed in this state;
3 2. The grantee shall deliver to the landowner an updated
4 estimate, prepared by an independent, third-party professional
5 engineer licensed in this state, of the cost of removal and the
6 salvage value:
7 a. on or before the tenth anniversary of the commercial
8 operations date of the solar power facilities, and
9 b. at least once every five (5) years after the
10 commercial operations date of the solar power
11 facilities for the remainder of the term of the
12 agreement; and
13 3. The grantee is responsible for ensuring that the amount of
14 the financial assurance remains sufficient to cover the amount
15 required by subsection B of this section, consistent with the
16 estimates required by this subsection.
17 D. The grantee is responsible for the costs of obtaining
18 financial assurance described by this section and costs of
19 determining the estimated removal costs and salvage value.
20 E. The agreement must shall provide that the grantee shall
21 deliver the financial assurance not later than the earlier of:
22 1. The date the solar power facility agreement is terminated;
23 or
24
Req. No. 3824 Page 7
1 2. The twentieth anniversary of the commercial operations date
2 of the solar power facilities located on the landowner’s leased
3 property.
4 F. For purposes of this section, “commercial operations date”
5 means the date on which the solar power facilities are approved for
6 participation in market operations by a regional transmission
7 organization and does not include the generation of electrical
8 energy or other operations conducted before that date for purposes
9 of maintenance and testing.
10 G. The grantee may not cancel financial assurance before the
11 date the grantee has completed the grantee’s obligation to remove
12 the grantee’s solar power facilities located on the landowner’s
13 property in the manner provided by this act the Commercial Solar
14 Facility Decommissioning Act, unless the grantee provides the
15 landowner with replacement financial assurance at the time of or
16 before the cancellation. In the event of a transfer of ownership of
17 the grantee’s solar power facilities, the financial security
18 provided by the grantee shall remain in place until the date
19 evidence of financial security meeting the requirements of this act
20 the Commercial Solar Facility Decommissioning Act is provided to the
21 landowner.
22 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
6 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-103b of Title 63, unless there
8 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 A. For the purpose of ensuring continuity of services, the
10 State Commissioner of Health shall, in collaboration with the
11 Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services, conduct a
12 feasibility study to gather and analyze information and
13 considerations related to a potential consolidation of the State
14 Department of Health and the Department of Mental Health and
15 Substance Abuse Services. The feasibility study shall examine ways
16 the state can improve efficiency, simplify operations, reduce
17 administrative costs, demonstrate outcomes, and provide quality
18 services for the residents of this state.
19 B. The feasibility study shall compare the State Department of
20 Health and the Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse
21 Services, including a comparison of the areas listed in this
22 subsection, and shall study health agency consolidation initiatives
23 of other states. The feasibility study shall evaluate the following
24 topics:
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1572 Page 2

1 1. Administrative functions of each agency including, but not
2 limited to, inventory, organizational structure and capabilities,
3 management, staffing plans, and operations;
4 2. Finances of each agency, including:
5 a. any estimated projected savings to the state through a
6 consolidation initiative,
7 b. reliance on federal, state, and other funding sources,
8 c. cost drivers, and
9 d. return on investment of programs and services; and
10 3. Programs offered by each agency, services provided,
11 populations served, and costs and outcomes related to programs and
12 services. The study shall evaluate:
13 a. programs, functions, and services that are
14 duplicative, complementary, or unique to each agency
15 and identify opportunities to align programs and
16 services, and
17 b. access to and quality of programs and services and
18 identify any gaps in services.
19 C. The findings of the feasibility study and any
20 recommendations of the Commissioner shall be compiled into a final
21 report, which shall be electronically submitted to the Governor, the
22 President Pro Tempore of the Senate, and the Speaker of the House of
23 Representatives not later than March 1, 2027.

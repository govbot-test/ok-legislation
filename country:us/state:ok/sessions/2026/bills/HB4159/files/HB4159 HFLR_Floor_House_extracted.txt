18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 1210.520, as
20 amended by Section 10, Chapter 411, O.S.L. 2024 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025,
21 Section 1210.520), is amended to read as follows:
22 Section 1210.520. A. Beginning with the 2022-2023 school year
23 and for each school year thereafter, any student enrolled in
24 kindergarten, first, second, or third grade in a public school in
HB4159 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 this state who is assessed through the Strong Readers Act pursuant
2 to Section 1210.508C of this title and who is not meeting grade-
3 level targets in reading after the beginning-of-the-year assessment
4 any of the assessments required in the beginning, middle, and end of
5 the school year shall be screened for dyslexia. Screening also may
6 be requested for a student by his or her parent or legal guardian,
7 teacher, counselor, speech-language pathologist, or school
8 psychologist. The Department of Education shall send notice to each
9 parent or legal guardian that they may request a dyslexia screening
10 for his or her student at any time.
11 B. No later than July 1, 2021, the State Board of Education
12 shall develop policies for dyslexia screening required under this
13 subsection and shall include, but not be limited to:
14 1. The definition and characteristics of dyslexia and related
15 language disorders;
16 2. The process for referring students in kindergarten and
17 grades one through three for screening;
18 3. A process for providing notification to parents or legal
19 guardians of the use of a qualified dyslexia-screening tool and
20 notification of the results of the screening;
21 4. A process for providing the parents or legal guardians of
22 students screened for dyslexia with information and resource
23 material regarding dyslexia;
24

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 5. A process for monitoring the student's progress after the
2 positive identification of characteristics of dyslexia or other
3 disorders; and
4 6. Requirements and qualifications for screeners that
5 demonstrate an understanding of and training to administer the
6 screening instrument.
7 C. The Board shall adopt a list of approved qualified dyslexia
8 screening tools that address the following components, as
9 developmentally appropriate:
10 1. Phonological awareness;
11 2. Advanced phonemic awareness;
12 3. Sound symbol recognition;
13 4. Alphabet knowledge;
14 5. Decoding skills;
15 6. Encoding skills;
16 7. Rapid naming; and
17 8. Developmental language.
18 D. Screening shall be conducted in accordance with the policies
19 developed by the State Board of Education pursuant to subsection B
20 of this section and the Oklahoma Dyslexia Handbook including
21 policies and information developed relating to universal screening
22 of kindergarten students for characteristics of dyslexia.

1 E. Beginning June 30, 2023, and for each year thereafter,
2 school districts shall provide the following data to the State
3 Department of Education:
4 1. The number of students by grade level in kindergarten
5 through grade three who were screened for dyslexia in a school year;
6 2. The number of students by grade level in kindergarten
7 through grade three who were newly identified as having
8 characteristics of dyslexia in a school year;
9 3. The process or tools used to evaluate student progress;
10 4. The number of trained school system personnel or licensed
11 professionals used to administer the qualified dyslexia screening
12 tool;
13 5. The number of students in kindergarten through grade three
14 who were participating in interventions within the school setting
15 and the number of students participating in interventions outside
16 the school setting; and
17 6. The programs used by districts for intervention within the
18 school setting.
19 F. By December 31, 2023, and for each year thereafter, the
20 State Department of Education shall provide a report electronically
21 containing all of the information provided in subsection E of this
22 section to the Governor and Legislature and make the report
23 available on the Department's website.
24

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 G. As funds are available, beginning with the 2021-2022 school
2 year, the Department shall provide training on the best practices
3 for screening for dyslexia.
4 H. The State Board of Education may promulgate rules necessary
5 to implement the provisions of this section.
6 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

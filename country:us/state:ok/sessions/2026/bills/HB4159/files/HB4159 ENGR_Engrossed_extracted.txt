14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 1210.520, as
16 amended by Section 10, Chapter 411, O.S.L. 2024 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025,
17 Section 1210.520), is amended to read as follows:
18 Section 1210.520. A. Beginning with the 2022-2023 school year
19 and for each school year thereafter, any student enrolled in
20 kindergarten, first, second, or third grade in a public school in
21 this state who is assessed through the Strong Readers Act pursuant
22 to Section 1210.508C of this title and who is not meeting grade-
23 level targets in reading after the beginning-of-the-year assessment
24 any of the assessments required in the beginning, middle, and end of

1 the school year shall be screened for dyslexia. Screening also may
2 be requested for a student by his or her parent or legal guardian,
3 teacher, counselor, speech-language pathologist, or school
4 psychologist. The State Department of Education shall send notice
5 to each parent or legal guardian that he or she may request a
6 dyslexia screening for his or her student at any time.
7 B. No later than July 1, 2021, the State Board of Education
8 shall develop policies for dyslexia screening required under this
9 subsection and shall include, but not be limited to:
10 1. The definition and characteristics of dyslexia and related
11 language disorders;
12 2. The process for referring students in kindergarten and
13 grades one through three for screening;
14 3. A process for providing notification to parents or legal
15 guardians of the use of a qualified dyslexia-screening tool and
16 notification of the results of the screening;
17 4. A process for providing the parents or legal guardians of
18 students screened for dyslexia with information and resource
19 material regarding dyslexia;
20 5. A process for monitoring the student's progress after the
21 positive identification of characteristics of dyslexia or other
22 disorders; and

1 6. Requirements and qualifications for screeners that
2 demonstrate an understanding of and training to administer the
3 screening instrument.
4 C. The Board shall adopt a list of approved qualified dyslexia
5 screening tools that address the following components, as
6 developmentally appropriate:
7 1. Phonological awareness;
8 2. Advanced phonemic awareness;
9 3. Sound symbol recognition;
10 4. Alphabet knowledge;
11 5. Decoding skills;
12 6. Encoding skills;
13 7. Rapid naming; and
14 8. Developmental language.
15 D. Screening shall be conducted in accordance with the policies
16 developed by the State Board of Education pursuant to subsection B
17 of this section and the Oklahoma Dyslexia Handbook including
18 policies and information developed relating to universal screening
19 of kindergarten students for characteristics of dyslexia.
20 E. Beginning June 30, 2023, and for each year thereafter,
21 school districts shall provide the following data to the State
22 Department of Education:
23 1. The number of students by grade level in kindergarten
24 through grade three who were screened for dyslexia in a school year;

14 F. By December 31, 2023, and for each year thereafter, the
15 State Department of Education shall provide a report electronically

17 section to the Governor and Legislature and make the report

19 G. As funds are available, beginning with the 2021-2022 school

22 H. The State Board of Education may promulgate rules necessary

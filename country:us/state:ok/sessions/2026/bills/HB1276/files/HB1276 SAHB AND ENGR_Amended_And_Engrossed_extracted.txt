8 An Act relating to schools; requiring district boards
of education to adopt a policy prohibiting student
9 cell phone and personal electronic device use under
certain circumstances; requiring exceptions for
10 emergencies; allowing board approval to opt out of
prohibition; requiring annual approval of alternative
11 policies; defining terms; providing for codification;
providing an effective date; and declaring an
12 emergency.
13
14 AMENDMENT NO. 1. Page 1, strike the title, enacting clause and
entire bill and insert
15
16 “An Act relating to schools; amending Section 1,
Chapter 30, O.S.L. 2025 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section
17 1-126), which relates to prohibited use of cell
phones and personal electronic devices on certain
18 campuses; extending prohibition; removing language
allowing public school district boards of education
19 to adopt certain policy beginning in certain school
year; providing an effective date; and declaring an
20 emergency.
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
23 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 1, Chapter 30, O.S.L. 2025
24 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 1-126), is amended to read as follows:
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 1276 Page 1
1 Section 1-126. A. As used in this section:
2 1. “Bell to bell” means the time between the first bell ringing
3 at the start of the school day to begin instructional time until the
4 dismissal bell at the end of the school day to end instructional
5 time; and
6 2. “Personal electronic device” means a personal device capable
7 of connecting to a smart phone, the Internet, or a cellular or Wi-Fi
8 network, or directly connecting to another similar device. Personal
9 electronic devices include, but are not limited to, smart watches,
10 smart headphones, laptops, tablets, and smart glasses. Personal
11 electronic devices shall not include school-issued or school-
12 approved devices that are specifically limited for use in classroom
13 instruction.
14 B. 1. For the 2025-2026 school year and each school year
15 thereafter, each school district board of education shall adopt a
16 policy prohibiting students from using cell phones and personal
17 electronic devices while on the campus of a public school district
18 from bell to bell. The policy shall include disciplinary procedures
19 for violations.
20 2. For the 2026-2027 school year and each school year
21 thereafter, each school district board of education may adopt a
22 policy prohibiting students from using cell phones and personal
23 electronic devices while on the campus of a public school district
24 from bell to bell.
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 1276 Page 2
1 C. The policy adopted pursuant to subsection B of this section
2 may provide for exceptions including, but not limited to:
3 1. Emergency use of cell phones or personal electronic devices
4 by students during the school day; and
5 2. Use of cell phones or personal electronic devices by
6 students who use them to monitor health issues.
7 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
8 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
9 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
10 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
11 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.”
12 Passed the Senate the 15th day of April, 2026.

7 An Act relating to schools; requiring district boards
of education to adopt a policy prohibiting student
8 cell phone and personal electronic device use under
certain circumstances; requiring exceptions for
9 emergencies; allowing board approval to opt out of
prohibition; requiring annual approval of alternative
10 policies; defining terms; providing for codification;
providing an effective date; and declaring an
11 emergency.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 24-162 of Title 70, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. Before the start of the 2025-2026 school year, each school
19 district board of education shall adopt a policy to prohibit the use
20 of cell phones and personal electronic devices by students enrolled
21 in the district during the entirety of the school day and while
22 present on school grounds.
23 B. District policies described in subsection A of this section
24 shall incorporate provisions for emergency use including, but not
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1276 Page 1
1 limi ted to, use to support medical issues documented by a licensed
2 medical professional.
3 C. Notwithstanding subsection A of this section, a school
4 district board of education may vote to adopt a policy allowing
5 student cell phone or personal electronic device usage during the
6 school day. Any such policy shall be reviewed and approved by the
7 district board on an annual basis.
8 D. For purposes of this section:
9 1. "Cell phone" means a portable wireless telephone device that
10 is designed to send or receive transmissions through a cellular
11 radiotelephone service;
12 2. "Personal electronic device" means any personal device
13 capable of connecting to a smart phone, the Internet, a cellular or
14 Wi-Fi network, or directly connecting to another similar device.
15 Personal electronic devices include, but are not limited to, smart
16 watches, smart headphones, laptops, tablets, and smart glasses.
17 Personal electronic devices shall not include devices that are
18 school-issued or school-approved and specifically limited for use in
19 classroom instruction; and
20 3. "School day" means the entirety of the day, from the first
21 bell indicating the beginning of classroom instruction through the
22 final bell indicating the end of classroom instruction.
23 SECTION 5. This act shall become effective July 1, 2025.
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 1276 Page 2
1 SECTION 6. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
2 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
3 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
4 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
5 Passed the House of Representatives the 13th day of February,
6 2025.

14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 42 of Title 27, unless there is
17 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. In any judicial condemnation proceeding, the owner of an
19 interest in property sought to be condemned shall be entitled,
20 through the timely filing of an exception to the report of the
21 commissioners in such proceeding, to a hearing on the issues of
22 public use or necessity of the taking. At any such hearing, the
23 condemning authority shall bear the burden of proving by a
24 preponderance of the evidence that the proposed taking is for a
Req. No. 16806 Page 1
1 publ ic use authorized by law, and that the property sought to be
2 taken is reasonably necessary to accomplish that public use. The
3 burden of proof shall remain with the condemning authority at all
4 times, and a resolution or declaration of necessity from the
5 condemning authority shall not, in and of itself, be conclusive or
6 sufficient evidence of necessity.
7 B. If, upon a hearing on public use or necessity, the
8 condemning authority shall fail to establish its right to condemn
9 the property, or any part thereof, the property owner shall be
10 entitled to an award of reasonable attorney fees, costs, and expert
11 witness fees, to be taxed and collected as costs in a civil action.
12 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

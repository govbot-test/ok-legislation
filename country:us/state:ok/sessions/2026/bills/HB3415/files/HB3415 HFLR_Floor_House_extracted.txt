11 AS INTRODUCED
12 An Act relating to state government; requiring all
vendors providing services to state agencies to
13 report use of subcontracting; directing vendors to
report to the Central Purchasing Division; providing
14 required details for report; directing vendors to
file within ten days of completion; directing the
15 Central Purchasing Division to maintain public
database of private vendor contracts; providing
16 required information for database; providing an
exemption for information protected in the Oklahoma
17 Open Records Act; requiring state agencies to report
certain information from statewide contracts to the
18 Central Purchasing Division; directing state agencies
to perform post-assessment of fulfilled contract
19 services; providing required information to be
included in assessment; directing agencies to report
20 incomplete contracts in annual budget submissions and
estimated time for completion; providing for
21 codification; and providing an effective date.
22
23
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
HB3415 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 85.41B of Title 74, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 All vendors providing services to a state agency shall report
5 any subcontracting for fulfillment of any terms of the contract
6 between the vendor and the state agency to both the contracting
7 agency and Central Purchasing Division of the Office of Management
8 and Enterprise Services. The report shall include the tasks the
9 subcontractor completed and the percentage of the total contract
10 being fulfilled by the subcontractor. The vendor shall file the
11 report no later than ten (10) days after the conclusion of work
12 completed by the subcontractor.
13 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 85.41C of Title 74, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 A. Every current active contract between a state agency and a
17 private vendor shall be published on a publicly available database
18 maintained by the Central Purchasing Division of the Office of
19 Management and Enterprise Services. The database shall include:
20 1. The vendor's name;
21 2. The total expected cost of the contract;
22 3. The purchase order number; and
23 4. The description of deliverables.
24
HB3415 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 B. Subsection A of this section shall not apply if the contract
2 is related to any information exempted from the Oklahoma Open
3 Records Act, Section 24A.28 of Title 51 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
4 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
5 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 85.41D of Title 74, unless there
6 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
7 State agencies that purchase or secure services through
8 statewide contracts shall provide the following documentation to the
9 Central Purchasing Division of the Office of Management and
10 Enterprise Services upon entering into an agreement for contracted
11 services:
12 1. A statement of work that includes the purpose of the
13 contracted work, the expected deliverables and corresponding due
14 dates, and metrics for evaluating the success of the engagement;
15 2. A description of hourly rates with a "not to exceed" rate
16 that caps the total expense of the contracted work;
17 3. Supporting documentation that includes line-item expenses;
18 and
19 4. Performance contract guarantees to provide recourse to the
20 contracting agency.
21 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 85.41E of Title 74, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
HB3415 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 A. State agencies shall perform post-assessment of the
2 fulfillment of any contracted services within thirty (30) days of
3 either a key milestone identified within the contract or the
4 contract term end. This assessment shall include:
5 1. Whether the contract was completed by the end date
6 specified;
7 2. Whether the services were overdue; and
8 3. Whether the services were over budget.
9 B. State agencies shall report any incomplete contracts,
10 regardless of whether the contract is late or on schedule, in their
11 annual budget submissions and include the estimated time for
12 completion.
13 SECTION 5. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
14
15 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON GOVERNMENT OVERSIGHT, dated
03/05/2026 - DO PASS, As Amended and Coauthored.

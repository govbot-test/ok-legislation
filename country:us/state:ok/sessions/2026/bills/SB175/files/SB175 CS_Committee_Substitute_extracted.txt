9 An Act relating to motor vehicles; creating the
Uninsured Commercial Vehicle Recovery Reimbursement
10 Fund; providing certain limitations; allowing the
Corporation Commission to promulgate certain rules;
11 amending 47 O.S. 2021, Section 1167, as last amended
by Section 57, Chapter 171, O.S.L. 2025 (47 O.S.
12 Supp. 2025, Section 1167), which relates to
apportionment of revenue; directing deposit of
13 certain monies in certain fund; providing for
codification; providing an effective date; and
14 declaring an emergency.
15
16
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1120.4 of Title 47, unless there
21 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 A. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving
23 fund for the Corporation Commission to be designated as the
24 “Uninsured Commercial Vehicle Recovery Reimbursement Fund”. The
Req. No. 3689 Page 1
1 fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year
2 limitations, and shall consist of all monies directed to be
3 deposited in the fund pursuant to Section 1167 of Title 47 of the
4 Oklahoma Statutes for the purpose of reimbursing wrecker or tower
5 operators who provide nonconsensual tow services to uninsured
6 commercial motor vehicles. All monies accruing to the credit of the
7 fund are hereby appropriated and may be budgeted and expended by the
8 Commission for the purpose provided for in this section.
9 Expenditures from the fund shall be made upon warrants issued by the
10 State Treasurer against claims filed and prescribed by law with the
11 Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise Services for
12 approval and payment subject to the following limitations:
13 1. No more than Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000.00) shall be paid
14 from the fund per commercial motor vehicle or detached trailer
15 towed. A commercial motor vehicle and trailer detached by the on-
16 scene wrecker or tower operator shall be considered one unit for the
17 purposes of fund access;
18 2. Only nonconsensual tows of vehicles or trailers where the
19 responsible party is not locatable after a diligent search are
20 eligible for reimbursement from the fund;
21 3. Abandoned commercial motor vehicles with a gross vehicle
22 weight rating of twenty-six thousand (26,000) pounds or more shall
23 be eligible for expenditure from the fund;
24
Req. No. 3689 Page 2
1 4. Abandoned detached trailers with two or more axles shall be
2 eligible for expenditure from the fund; and
3 5. Noncommercial passenger vehicle tows shall not be eligible
4 for reimbursement from the fund.
5 B. The Commission may promulgate rules as needed to implement
6 the provisions of this section.
7 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 47 O.S. 2021, Section 1167, as
8 last amended by Section 57, Chapter 171, O.S.L. 2025 (47 O.S. Supp.
9 2025, Section 1167), is amended to read as follows:
10 Section 1167. A. The Corporation Commission is hereby
11 authorized to promulgate rules pursuant to the Administrative
12 Procedures Act to establish the amounts of fees, fines and penalties
13 as set forth in Section 1166 et seq. of this title. The Corporation
14 Commission shall notify all interested parties of any proposed rules
15 to be promulgated as provided herein and shall provide such parties
16 an opportunity to be heard prior to promulgation.
17 B. The Corporation Commission shall adjudicate enforcement
18 actions initiated by Corporation Commission personnel.
19 C. Revenue derived from all fines and penalties collected or
20 received by the Corporation Commission pursuant to the provisions of
21 the Trucking One-Stop Shop Act shall be apportioned as follows:
22 1. For the period beginning August 23, 2013 July 1, 2026, the
23 first Three Hundred Thousand Dollars ($300,000.00) collected or
24 received each fiscal year shall be remitted to the Department of
Req. No. 3689 Page 3
1 Publ ic Safety for the purpose of staffing the port ports of entry
2 weigh stations to conduct safety inspections. The next Five Hundred
3 Fifty Thousand Dollars ($550,000.00) shall be remitted to Service
4 Oklahoma and apportioned as provided in Section 1104 of this title
5 the Uninsured Commercial Vehicle Recovery Reimbursement Fund as
6 provided for in Section 1 of this act; and
7 2. The remaining amount shall be deposited to the Trucking One-
8 Stop Shop Fund created in subsection D of this section.
9 D. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving
10 fund for the Corporation Commission to be known and designated as
11 the “Trucking One-Stop Shop Fund”. The Trucking One-Stop Shop Fund
12 shall consist of:
13 1. All funds apportioned thereto in subsection C of this
14 section;
15 2. Fees collected by the Commission to be retained as a
16 licensed operator or other Corporation Commission registration or
17 motor fuel fees as allowed by statute or rule; and
18 3. Any other monies to be utilized for the Trucking One-Stop
19 Shop Act.
20 The fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year
21 limitations, and shall not be subject to legislative appropriation.
22 Monies in the Trucking One-Stop Shop Fund shall only be expended for
23 direct expenses relating to the Trucking One-Stop Shop Act.
24 Expenditures from the revolving fund shall be made pursuant to the
Req. No. 3689 Page 4
1 laws of this state. In addition, expenditures from the revolving
2 fund may be made pursuant to the Oklahoma Central Purchasing Act for
3 the purpose of immediately responding to emergency situations,
4 within the Commission’s jurisdiction, having potentially critical
5 environmental or public safety impact. Warrants for expenditures
6 from the fund shall be drawn by the State Treasurer against claims
7 filed as prescribed by law with the Director of the Office of
8 Management and Enterprise Services for approval and payment.
9 E. There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving
10 fund for the Department of Transportation to be designated the
11 “Weigh Station Improvement Revolving Fund”. The fund shall be a
12 continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year limitations, and shall
13 consist of all monies deposited thereto. All monies accruing to the
14 credit of the fund are hereby appropriated and may be budgeted and
15 expended by the Department for the purpose of constructing,
16 equipping and maintaining facilities to determine the weight of
17 vehicles traveling on the roads and highways of this state.
18 Expenditures from the fund shall be made upon warrants issued by the
19 State Treasurer against claims filed as prescribed by law with the
20 Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise Services for
21 approval and payment.
22 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
23 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
24 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
Req. No. 3689 Page 5
1 decl ared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
2 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

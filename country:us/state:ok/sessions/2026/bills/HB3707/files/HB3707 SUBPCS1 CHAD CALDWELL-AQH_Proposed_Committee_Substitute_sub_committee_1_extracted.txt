4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
5 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
6 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
7 A. The Legislature hereby finds:
8 1. Academic growth is a critical indicator of instructional
9 effectiveness;
10 2. Oklahoma's teacher compensation system does not fully align
11 pay with demonstrable growth in student learning;
12 3. Research indicates that performance-based compensation tied
13 to academic growth can improve student achievement and teacher
14 retention when implemented with fidelity; and
15 4. Targeted pilot programs allow the State to rigorously
16 evaluate effectiveness.
17 B. The purpose of this act shall be to establish a pilot
18 program that provides growth-based compensation for teachers in
19 grades fourth through eighth and extends schoolwide incentives to
20 early-grade teachers to encourage vertical alignment of instruction.
21 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-213 of Title 70, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24 A. As used in this section:

1 1. "Eligible teacher" means a full-time certified teacher
2 teaching grades fourth through eighth in English language arts and
3 mathematics at a participating school;
4 2. "Student academic growth" means annual student progress as
5 measured by the Oklahoma State Testing Program;
6 3. "Recognized teacher" means an eligible teacher whose
7 students demonstrate student academic growth at or above an
8 established growth threshold;
9 4. "Participating school" means a public or charter elementary
10 or middle school serving grades fourth through eighth; and
11 5. "Early-grade teachers" means a full-time certified teacher
12 teaching pre-kindergarten through second grade at a participating
13 school.
14 B. There is hereby established a Growth-Based Teacher
15 Compensation Program to be administered by the Office of Educational
16 Quality and Accountability. Beginning with the 2026–2027 school
17 year, the pilot program shall operate for a minimum of four (4)
18 consecutive school years.
19 C. To be eligible to earn individual growth-based compensation
20 under this section, teachers shall be full-time certified teachers
21 teaching grades fourth through eighth in English language arts and
22 mathematics at a participating school. The pilot program shall
23 consist of two thousand eight hundred thirty-one recognized teachers
24 representing approximately one-fifth of all teachers serving grades

1 four th through eighth in Oklahoma. The Commission for Educational
2 Quality and Accountability shall establish criteria for the
3 selection of recognized teachers for the pilot program.
4 D. 1. The Office of Educational Quality and Accountability
5 shall place recognized teachers participating in the pilot program
6 into one of three tiers based on statewide student growth
7 percentiles from the Oklahoma State Testing Program and the
8 recognized teachers shall receive the annual performance bonus
9 associated with the tier:
10 a. Tier 1 (67th–79th percentile) – Above-Average Growth:
11 Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00) performance bonus,
12 b. Tier 2 (80th–94th percentile) – High Growth: Fifteen
13 Thousand Dollars ($15,000.00) performance bonus, and
14 c. Tier 3 (95th percentile and above) – Exceptional
15 Growth: Twenty-five Thousand Dollars ($25,000.00)
16 performance bonus.
17 2. The assignment to a tier shall be based solely on measurable
18 student academic growth, calculated using a rolling two-year period
19 of statewide assessment data. Provided that, during the first year
20 of the pilot program, tier assignment shall be calculated using a
21 single year of available growth data.
22 3. Performance bonuses shall be paid by the Office of
23 Educational Quality and Accountability as supplemental compensation.
24 The performance bonus shall not be included in the calculation of

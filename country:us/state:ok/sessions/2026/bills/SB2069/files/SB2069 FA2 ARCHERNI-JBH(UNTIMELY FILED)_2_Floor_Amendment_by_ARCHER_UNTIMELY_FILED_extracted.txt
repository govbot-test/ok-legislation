1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 10.11 of Title 74, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. There is hereby created the Oklahoma Advanced Nuclear Energy
5 Office. The Office shall be an office administratively housed
6 within the Governor's office and shall be assigned to the Oklahoma
7 Secretary of Energy and Environment.
8 B. The purposes of the Office are to provide statutory and
9 regulatory readiness for advanced nuclear energy and to:
10 1. Provide strategic leadership for the advanced nuclear
11 reactor system in this state;
12 2. Promote the development of advanced nuclear reactors for
13 dispatchable electric generation while creating high-wage advanced
14 manufacturing jobs in this state;
15 3. Advance innovative nuclear energy generation technologies
16 while delivering safe, reliable, and clean energy solutions that
17 address the state's growing demand;
18 4. Enhance the state's energy security, foster economic growth,
19 and ensure the safety of future nuclear energy generation
20 development;
21 5. Identify barriers to the financial viability of nuclear
22 energy generation and regulatory and licensing complexities that
23 increase risk to developers of nuclear energy;
24
Req. No. 17590 Page 4

1 6. Leverage the expertise and capacity of institutions of
2 higher education, the nuclear energy industry, the industrial
3 manufacturing sector, and regulatory stakeholders to develop a
4 comprehensive strategic plan to ensure the development of advanced
5 nuclear energy and associated technologies in this state; and
6 7. Support the development of an advanced nuclear energy supply
7 chain and associated technologies in this state.
8 C. The Office shall:
9 1. Develop and propose a comprehensive statutory and regulatory
10 framework for advanced nuclear energy. The statutory and regulatory
11 framework developed by the Office shall be substantive and ready for
12 implementation;
13 2. Draft and recommend rules addressing areas of state
14 jurisdiction not preempted by the United States Nuclear Regulatory
15 Commission;
16 3. Coordinate with the Oklahoma Corporation Commission;
17 4. Identify any statutory gaps requiring legislative action;
18 and
19 5. Submit completed statutory and regulatory framework and
20 recommendations for advanced nuclear energy to the Governor, Speaker
21 Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate, the Speaker of the
22 Oklahoma House of Representatives, and the Oklahoma Corporation
23 Commission, by December 1, 2027.
24

1 D. The Office may not accept a gift, grant, or loan from or
2 contract with an applicant for or a beneficiary of a grant provided
3 pursuant to this act.
4 E. The Office may not require an advanced nuclear project to be
5 located in a specific location.
6 F. The Office may promulgate rules as necessary to carry out
7 the provisions of this act.
8 G. The Oklahoma Corporation Commission shall promulgate rules
9 within its constitutional and statutory jurisdiction to implement
10 the regulatory framework provided by the Office. The Corporation
11 Commission shall not be compelled to adopt specific draft language

15 the constitutional or statutory authority of the Oklahoma

17 facilities, transmission providers, natural gas utilities, or any
18 other entity subject to its jurisdiction.

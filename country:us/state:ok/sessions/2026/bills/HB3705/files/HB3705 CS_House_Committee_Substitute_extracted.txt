10 eligible students in this state;

12 eligible to enroll in a public school in this state. Eligible

20 authorized by paragraph 1 of subsection C of this section means

6 following expenditures:
7 a. tuition and fees for nonpublic learning programs,
8 online or in person,
9 b. academic tutoring services provided by an individual
10 or a private academic tutoring facility,
11 c. textbooks, curriculum, or other instructional
12 materials including, but not limited to, supplemental
13 materials or associated online instruction required by
14 an education service provider, and
15 d. fees for nationally standardized assessments
16 including, but not limited to, assessments used to
17 determine college admission and advanced placement

20 9. "Taxpayer" means a biological or adoptive parent,
21 grandparent, aunt, uncle, legal guardian, custodian, or other person
22 with legal authority to act on behalf of an eligible student.

6 student is educated pursuant to the other means of education
7 exception provided for in subsection A of Section 10-105 of this
8 title. To claim the credit, the taxpayer shall submit to the
9 Commission receipts for qualified expenses as defined by paragraph 8
10 of subsection A of this section;
11 3. If the eligible student attends a private school in this
12 state, accredited by the State Board of Education or another
13 accrediting association, that exclusively serves students
14 experiencing homelessness, the credit amount shall be Seven Thousand
15 Five Hundred Dollars ($7,500.00) or the amount of the cost to

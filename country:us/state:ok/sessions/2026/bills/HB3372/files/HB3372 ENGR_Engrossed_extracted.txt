17 A. There is hereby established the Revolving Loan Fund Program
18 for Charter School Capital Expenditures, subject to appropriations
19 by the Legislature.
20 B. The Revolving Loan Fund Program for Charter School Capital
21 Expenditures shall be managed by the Statewide Charter School Board.
22 The Board shall conduct a competitive procurement process to select
23 a qualified third-party financial administrator with demonstrated
24 expertise in public finance, charter school facilities financing, or
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3372 Page 8

1 muni cipal bond underwriting to administer the program. The selected
2 administrator shall manage underwriting, loan structuring, and risk
3 assessment functions in accordance with rules promulgated by the
4 Board.
5 C. The Revolving Loan Fund Program for Charter School Capital
6 Expenditures shall provide loans and loan guarantees that allow
7 charter schools to access funding at lower interest rates.
8 D. The third-party financial administrator selected pursuant to
9 subsection B of this section shall determine the interest rates,
10 underwriting standards, and loan terms applicable to loans and loan
11 guarantees issued under this section, subject to oversight and
12 approval by the Statewide Charter School Board. In establishing
13 such rates and terms, the administrator shall seek to provide
14 competitive, market-aligned interest rates and prudent risk
15 management.
16 E. Eligible recipients of loans or loan guarantees through this
17 program include:
18 1. Nonprofit charter school organizations;
19 2. Nonprofit charter school management organizations;
20 3. Affiliated entities designed to own property for charter
21 schools;
22 4. A nonprofit corporation that develops and finances a
23 facility that will be occupied by a public charter school throughout
24 the term of the loan; and
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3372 Page 9

1 5. One or more third-party, nonprofit charter school loan fund
2 designed to leverage the total loan amounts to eligible recipients.
3 F. Funds distributed pursuant to this section shall be for
4 purchase, construction, renovation, and maintenance of charter
5 school capital projects including:
6 1. Purchase of land and related costs;
7 2. Construction of a new facility and related costs;
8 3. Purchase of an existing property and related costs;
9 4. Renovation of a property and related costs; and
10 5. Capital improvements of an existing property and related
11 costs.
12 G. 1. To be eligible to benefit from a loan or loan guarantee
13 under this section, a charter school shall:
14 a. operate pursuant to Section 3-137 of Title 70 of the
15 Oklahoma Statutes,
16 b. provide a full-time, in-person academic program,
17 c. certify that any facility financed in whole or in part
18 under this section shall be used primarily for full-
19 time, in-person student instruction, and
20 d. be in good standing with its sponsor.
21 2. A charter school shall not be eligible to receive a loan or
22 loan guarantee under this section if, within the three (3) years
23 immediately preceding the date of application:

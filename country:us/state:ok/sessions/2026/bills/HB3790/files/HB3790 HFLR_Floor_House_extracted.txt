11 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
12 An Act relating to contracts; creating the Oklahoma
Homeowner Repair Contract Protection Act; defining
13 home solicitation contract; allowing a five-day
voidable contract period for homeowners; providing
14 for codification; and providing an effective date.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1100 of Title 15, unless there
20 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 This act shall be known as the "Oklahoma Homeowner Repair
22 Contract Protection Act".

1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1101 of Title 15, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 "Home solicitation contract" means entering into a home repair
5 contract or services in which the home repair contractor or a person
6 acting for him or her engages in a personal solicitation of the sale
7 at a residence of the buyer, and the buyer's agreement is given to
8 the contractor or a person acting for him or her. It does not
9 include a sale made pursuant to a preexisting revolving contract, or
10 a contract made pursuant to prior negotiations between the parties
11 at a business establishment at a fixed location where goods or
12 services are offered or exhibited for sale. Nothing in this act
13 shall prohibit advertising, responding to homeowner-initiated
14 contact, or performing services when contacted directly by the
15 property owner.
16 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1102 of Title 15, unless there
18 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 1. In addition to any right otherwise to revoke an offer, the
20 homeowner has the right to cancel a home solicitation contract until
21 midnight of the fifth calendar day after the day on which the
22 homeowner signs an agreement for home repairs;
23

1 2. Cancellation occurs when the homeowner gives written notice
2 of cancellation via certified mail to the contractor at the address
3 stated in the agreement or offer to purchase;
4 3. Notice of cancellation is given when it is deposited in a
5 mailbox properly addressed and postage prepaid; and
6 4. Notice of cancellation given by the homeowner need not take
7 a particular form and is sufficient if it indicates by any form of
8 written expression the intention of the homeowner not to be bound by
9 the home solicitation sale.
10 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

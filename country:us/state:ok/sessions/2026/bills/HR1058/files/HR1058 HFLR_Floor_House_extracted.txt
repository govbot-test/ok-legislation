15 WHEREAS, across the United States, the week of May 10 through
16 May 15, 2026, is being recognized as "National Police Week"; and
17 WHEREAS, on October 1, 1962, the United States Congress approved
18 a joint resolution authorizing the President of the United States to
19 designate May 15 of each year as "Peace Officers Memorial Day" in
20 honor of law enforcement officers who have been killed, disabled, or
21 otherwise injured in the line of duty and the calendar week during
22 which May 15 occurs as "National Police Week"; and
23 WHEREAS, by proclamation dated May 4, 1963, President John F.
24 Kennedy designated May 15, 1963, and each succeeding May 15 as

1 WHEREAS, within the last year, numerous law enforcement officers
2 from across the United States have made the ultimate sacrifice; and
3 WHEREAS, six Oklahoma law enforcement officers died in the line
4 of duty within the last year, Deputy Sheriff Thomas "Walker" Lemay
5 of the LeFlore County Sheriff's Office, Trooper Vernon Brake of the
6 Oklahoma Highway Patrol, Sergeant Thomas Duran of the El Reno Police
7 Department, Deputy Gary Brooks of the Seminole County Sheriff's
8 Office, Recruit Franklin Leinen of the Broken Arrow Police
9 Department, and Reserve Officer Clint Lawrence of the Haileyville
10 Police Department; and
11 WHEREAS, the Oklahoma House of Representatives honors the memory
12 of the hundreds of law enforcement officers who have given their
13 lives in the line of duty in this state and throughout the United
14 States.
15 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
16 OF THE 2ND SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE:
17 THAT May 10 through May 15, 2026, is hereby recognized by the
18 Oklahoma House of Representatives as "National Police Week" and
19 recognizes May 15, 2026, as "Peace Officers Memorial Day" in
20 Oklahoma.
21 THAT the Oklahoma House of Representatives expresses condolences
22 to the loved ones of each law enforcement officer who has made the
23 ultimate sacrifice in the line of duty.

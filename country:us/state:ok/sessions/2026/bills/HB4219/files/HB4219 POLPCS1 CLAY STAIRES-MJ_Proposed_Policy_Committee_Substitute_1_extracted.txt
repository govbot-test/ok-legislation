7 PROPOSED POLICY COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
8 An Act relating to data centers; providing
description of covered facilities; requiring covered
9 facilities to be set back from property line;
requiring covered facilities to possess capital for
10 infrastructure and operations; requiring covered
facilities to create an Environmental Improvement and
11 Mitigation Fund; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1000 of Title 75A, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. As used in this act, "covered facility" means any proposed
19 facility or installation falling within one or more of the following
20 North American Industry Classification System (NAICS) codes or
21 functionally equivalent operations:
22 1. NAICS 221115, relating to wind electric power generation;
23 2. NAICS 221121, relating to electric bulk power transmission
24 and control, when used primarily for wind energy transmission;
Req. No. 16115 Page 1
1 3. NAICS 423930 or 562920, relating to recyclable material
2 merchants and materials recovery facilities, when used for wind-
3 turbine component recycling or decommissioning;
4 4. NAICS 518210, relating to data processing, hosting, and
5 computing infrastructure providers, when used for operation of a
6 large-scale data center;
7 5. NAICS 221122 relating to grid-scale battery storage
8 installations; and
9 6. Activities relating to carbon capture, carbon recapture,
10 carbon-sequestration, or related scientific or industrial
11 operations.
12 B. A covered facility shall:
13 1. Be set back no less than five hundred (500) feet from the
14 property line;
15 2. Possess the capital to pay for all the infrastructure needed
16 for construction, upgrades, and ongoing operation of the facility;
17 and
18 3. Create an Environmental Improvement and Mitigation Fund to
19 pay for the removal of material, supplies, and facilities when they
20 become obsolete.
21 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

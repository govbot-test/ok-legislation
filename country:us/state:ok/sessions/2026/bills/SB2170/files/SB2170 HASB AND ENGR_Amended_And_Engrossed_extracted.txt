1 BE I T ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
2 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 43 O.S. 2021, Section 110.1a, as
3 amended by Section 1, Chapter 34, O.S.L. 2024 (43 O.S. Supp. 2025,
4 Section 110.1a), is amended to read as follows:
5 Section 110.1a. A. This section shall be known and may be
6 cited as the “Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation Program”.
7 B. It is the policy of this state to ensure that the health,
8 safety, and welfare of the child is paramount when supervised
9 visitation is ordered by the court.
10 C. For purposes of the Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation
11 Program As used in this section:
12 1. “Supervised visitation” means the court-ordered contact
13 between a noncustodial parent and one or more children of such
14 parent in the presence of a third-party person who is responsible
15 for observing and overseeing the visitation in order to provide for
16 the safety of the child and any other parties during the visitation.
17 The court may require supervised visitation when deemed necessary by
18 the court to protect the child or other parties and, if the court
19 orders visitation, the court shall require supervised visitation for
20 any person that is the subject of a referral with substantiated
21 findings from the Department of Human Services relating to sexual
22 abuse of a child;
23
24
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2170 Page 2
1 2. An “alcohol-dependent person” has the same meaning as such
2 term is defined in Section 3-403 of Title 43A of the Oklahoma
3 Statutes;
4 3. A “drug-dependent person” has the same meaning as such term
5 is defined in Section 3-403 of Title 43A of the Oklahoma Statutes;
6 and
7 4. “Domestic abuse” has the same meaning as such term is
8 defined in Section 60.1 of Title 22 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
9 D. 1. The associate district judge in each county within this
10 state may select trained volunteers to provide supervised visitation
11 pursuant to the Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation Program.
12 2. The associate district judge of each county may appoint a
13 judicial district supervised visitation team to:
14 a. identify public and private entities which will be
15 willing to provide location sites for purposes of the
16 Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation Program,
17 b. identify individuals who will be willing to serve as
18 third-party persons to observe and oversee court-
19 ordered supervised visitations,
20 c. establish training requirements for volunteers,
21 d. identify programs which may be available for the
22 training of the volunteers including, but not limited
23 to, the Department of Human Services, Office of the
24 Attorney General, child advocacy centers, domestic
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2170 Page 3
1 violence groups, and the Department of Mental Health
2 and Substance Abuse Services,
3 e. develop written protocol for handling supervised
4 visitations so as to provide safety of the child and
5 other parties during the supervised visitation,
6 f. develop application forms for volunteers applying for
7 the Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation Program.
8 Information listed on the form shall include, but not
9 be limited to:
10 (1) name, address and phone number of the volunteer,
11 (2) place of employment and phone number of the
12 volunteer,
13 (3) areas of expertise,
14 (4) listing of professional training in areas
15 including, but not limited to, child abuse,
16 domestic abuse, alcohol or drug abuse, mental
17 illness or conflict management,
18 (5) consent form specifying release of information,
19 and
20 (6) professional references, and
21 g. identify which information of the parties and the
22 child will be confidential and which may be available
23 to others.
24
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2170 Page 4
1 3. From recommendations of the team established pursuant to
2 this subsection, the associate district judge in each county within
3 this state may authorize one or more public or private agencies to
4 provide location sites for the Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation
5 Program. A district judge may require either party requesting
6 supervised visitation of a child to identify a trained third-party
7 volunteer to observe and oversee the visitation. A district court
8 shall not:
9 a. require any state agency location or state employee to
10 observe and oversee any supervised visitation, or
11 b. appoint a third party to observe and oversee a
12 supervised visitation who has not received the
13 training as specified by the judicial district
14 supervised visitation team unless agreed to by the
15 parties.
16 4. A participating public or private agency location site may
17 charge a fee for each visit.
18 E. Prior to approval of any third-party volunteer to observe
19 and oversee visitation under this section, the court shall make a
20 written determination of the fitness of the volunteer by reviewing
21 information including, but not limited to:
22 1. A national criminal history record check conducted by the
23 Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation within one (1) year prior to
24 the appointment of the volunteer;
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2170 Page 5
1 2. A sworn affidavit from the volunteer accepting the rules of
2 supervision and whether the volunteer:
3 a. is an alcohol- or drug-dependent person,
4 b. is residing with a person who is required by law to
5 register pursuant to the Sex Offenders Registration
6 Act of this state or any other state,
7 c. is residing with a person who has been convicted of
8 domestic abuse within the past five (5) years, or
9 d. has had custody, guardianship, or visitation rights
10 terminated to any child; and
11 3. Any testimony by the potential volunteer or other witness
12 testimony deemed necessary by the court to determine the fitness of
13 the volunteer.
14 F. The protocol for supervised visitation established by each
15 judicial district supervised visitation team shall require that:
16 1. The volunteer supervising the visitation shall be able to
17 see and hear all interactions between the supervised parent and the
18 child; and
19 2. If the court determines that the supervised parent has
20 exhibited suicidal or violent behavior, all supervised visitation
21 shall occur within a professional setting unless and until the
22 supervised parent has submitted a non-self-reporting psychological
23 evaluation to the court.
24
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2170 Page 6
1 G. The protocol for supervised visitation established by each
2 judicial district supervised visitation team may require that:
3 1. The location site require each participant who has court-
4 ordered supervised visitation for a child and who is participating
5 in the supervised visitation program to sign a time log upon arrival
6 and departure. The agency location site must have an employee
7 assigned to verify identification of each participant, initial each
8 signature, and record the time of arrival and departure of each
9 person; and
10 2. The agency location site also contain information on each
11 client case including, but not limited to:
12 a. a copy of the court order requiring supervised
13 visitation, and
14 b. the name of individuals authorized to pick up or
15 deliver a child to the agency location site for
16 supervised visitation.
17 H. Each judicial district supervised visitation team may
18 include, but not be limited to:
19 1. Mental health professionals;
20 2. Police officers or other law enforcement agents;
21 3. Medical personnel;
22 4. Child protective services workers;
23 5. Child advocacy individuals; and
24 6. The district attorney or designee.
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2170 Page 7
1 I. An associate district judge of a county, the judicial
2 district supervised visitation team created pursuant to this section
3 and the Administrative Office of the Courts may develop an
4 informational brochure outlining the provisions of the Oklahoma
5 Child Supervised Visitation Program and procedures to be used by
6 volunteers in that judicial district. The brochure may be
7 distributed through the municipal and district court, social service
8 agency centers, county health departments, hospitals, crisis or
9 counseling centers, and community action agencies.
10 J. Except for acts of dishonesty, willful criminal acts, or
11 gross negligence, no member of the judicial district supervised
12 visitation team or volunteer shall be charged personally with any
13 liability whatsoever by reason of any act or omission committed or
14 suffered in the performance of the duties pursuant to the provisions
15 of this section.
16 K. The provisions of this section shall not apply to cases
17 subject to the Oklahoma Children’s Code and the Oklahoma Juvenile
18 Code.
19 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026."

12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 43 O.S. 2021, Section 110.1a, as
14 amended by Section 1, Chapter 34, O.S.L. 2024 (43 O.S. Supp. 2025,
15 Section 110.1a), is amended to read as follows:
16 Section 110.1a. A. This section shall be known and may be
17 cited as the “Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation Program”.
18 B. It is the policy of this state to ensure that the health,
19 safety, and welfare of the child is paramount when supervised
20 visitation is ordered by the court.
21 C. For purposes of the Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation
22 Program As used in this section:
23 1. “Supervised visitation” means the court-ordered contact
24 between a noncustodial parent and one or more children of such
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2170 Page 1

1 pare nt in the presence of a third-party person who is responsible
2 for observing and overseeing the visitation in order to provide for
3 the safety of the child and any other parties during the visitation.
4 The court may require supervised visitation when deemed necessary by
5 the court to protect the child or other parties and shall require
6 supervised visitation for any person that is the subject of a
7 referral with substantiated findings from the Department of Human
8 Services relating to sexual abuse of a child;
9 2. An “alcohol-dependent person” has the same meaning as such
10 term is defined in Section 3-403 of Title 43A of the Oklahoma
11 Statutes;
12 3. A “drug-dependent person” has the same meaning as such term
13 is defined in Section 3-403 of Title 43A of the Oklahoma Statutes;
14 and
15 4. “Domestic abuse” has the same meaning as such term is
16 defined in Section 60.1 of Title 22 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
17 D. 1. The associate district judge in each county within this
18 state may select trained volunteers to provide supervised visitation
19 pursuant to the Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation Program.
20 2. The associate district judge of each county may appoint a
21 judicial district supervised visitation team to:
22 a. identify public and private entities which will be
23 willing to provide location sites for purposes of the
24 Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation Program,
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2170 Page 2

1 b. identify individuals who will be willing to serve as
2 third-party persons to observe and oversee court-
3 ordered supervised visitations,
4 c. establish training requirements for volunteers,
5 d. identify programs which may be available for the
6 training of the volunteers including, but not limited
7 to, the Department of Human Services, Office of the
8 Attorney General, child advocacy centers, domestic
9 violence groups, and the Department of Mental Health
10 and Substance Abuse Services,
11 e. develop written protocol for handling supervised
12 visitations so as to provide safety of the child and
13 other parties during the supervised visitation,
14 f. develop application forms for volunteers applying for
15 the Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation Program.
16 Information listed on the form shall include, but not
17 be limited to:
18 (1) name, address and phone number of the volunteer,
19 (2) place of employment and phone number of the
20 volunteer,
21 (3) areas of expertise,
22 (4) listing of professional training in areas
23 including, but not limited to, child abuse,

1 domestic abuse, alcohol or drug abuse, mental
2 illness or conflict management,
3 (5) consent form specifying release of information,
4 and
5 (6) professional references, and
6 g. identify which information of the parties and the
7 child will be confidential and which may be available
8 to others.
9 3. From recommendations of the team established pursuant to
10 this subsection, the associate district judge in each county within
11 this state may authorize one or more public or private agencies to
12 provide location sites for the Oklahoma Child Supervised Visitation
13 Program. A district judge may require either party requesting
14 supervised visitation of a child to identify a trained third-party
15 volunteer to observe and oversee the visitation. A district court
16 shall not:
17 a. require any state agency location or state employee to
18 observe and oversee any supervised visitation, or
19 b. appoint a third party to observe and oversee a
20 supervised visitation who has not received the
21 training as specified by the judicial district
22 supervised visitation team unless agreed to by the
23 parties.

1 4. A participating public or private agency location site may
2 charge a fee for each visit.
3 E. Prior to approval of any third-party volunteer to observe
4 and oversee visitation under this section, the court shall make a
5 written determination of the fitness of the volunteer by reviewing
6 information including, but not limited to:
7 1. A national criminal history record check conducted by the
8 Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation within one (1) year prior to
9 the appointment of the volunteer;
10 2. A sworn affidavit from the volunteer accepting the rules of
11 supervision and whether the volunteer:
12 a. is an alcohol- or drug-dependent person,
13 b. is residing with a person who is required by law to
14 register pursuant to the Sex Offenders Registration
15 Act of this state or any other state,
16 c. is residing with a person who has been convicted of
17 domestic abuse within the past five (5) years, or
18 d. has had custody, guardianship, or visitation rights
19 terminated to any child; and
20 3. Any testimony by the potential volunteer or other witness
21 testimony deemed necessary by the court to determine the fitness of
22 the volunteer.
23 F. The protocol for supervised visitation established by each
24 judicial district supervised visitation team shall require that:
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2170 Page 5

1 1. The volunteer supervising the visitation shall be able to
2 see and hear all interactions between the supervised parent and the
3 child; and
4 2. If the court determines that the supervised parent has
5 exhibited suicidal or violent behavior, all supervised visitation
6 shall occur within a professional setting unless and until the
7 supervised parent has submitted a non-self-reporting psychological
8 evaluation to the court.
9 G. The protocol for supervised visitation established by each
10 judicial district supervised visitation team may require that:
11 1. The location site require each participant who has court-
12 ordered supervised visitation for a child and who is participating
13 in the supervised visitation program to sign a time log upon arrival
14 and departure. The agency location site must have an employee
15 assigned to verify identification of each participant, initial each
16 signature, and record the time of arrival and departure of each
17 person; and
18 2. The agency location site also contain information on each
19 client case including, but not limited to:
20 a. a copy of the court order requiring supervised
21 visitation, and
22 b. the name of individuals authorized to pick up or
23 deliver a child to the agency location site for
24 supervised visitation.

1 H. Each judicial district supervised visitation team may
2 include, but not be limited to:
3 1. Mental health professionals;
4 2. Police officers or other law enforcement agents;

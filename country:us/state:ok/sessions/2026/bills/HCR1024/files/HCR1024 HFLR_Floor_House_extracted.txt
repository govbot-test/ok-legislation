1 WHEREAS, Cyrus Avery and B.P. Piepmeier sent a telegram to the
2 Bureau of Public Roads in Washington, D.C., on April 30, 1926,
3 accepting the designation of the route from Chicago to Los Angeles
4 as Highway 66; and
5 WHEREAS, Cherokee citizen, Andy Payne, from Foyil, Oklahoma, won
6 the "Bunion Derby" in 1928, a 3,423-mile transcontinental race from
7 Los Angeles to New York in 573 hours, 4 minutes, and 34 seconds,
8 conceived by sports promoter Charles Pyle to promote Route 66; and
9 WHEREAS, in 1935, the U.S. Highway 66 Association designated
10 Route 66 as the "Will Rogers Highway" in honor of Will Rogers,
11 Oklahoma's favorite son; and
12 WHEREAS, Oklahoma has over 400 drivable miles of Route 66 today,
13 more than any other state; and
14 WHEREAS, Oklahoma counties with a section of Route 66 realize
15 more than 12 million annual visitors with more than $1 billion in
16 direct earnings due to travel and support more than 46,000 jobs
17 directly related to travel and tourism; and
18 WHEREAS, in 2023, the Oklahoma Legislature created the Oklahoma
19 Route 66 Commission to provide grants for historic preservation and
20 economic development in communities along the road and over $21
21 million has been awarded to date; and
22 WHEREAS, the Oklahoma Route 66 Centennial Commission has planned
23 special events throughout 2026 including the largest centennial
24 celebration in the nation on May 30.

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
2 OF THE 2ND SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE, THE SENATE
3 CONCURRING THEREIN:
4 THAT the Oklahoma House and Senate recognize April 21, 2026, as
5 Route 66 Day at the Capitol.
6 THAT the Oklahoma House and Senate celebrate the one hundredth
7 anniversary of Route 66 and the many Oklahomans throughout the years
8 who have contributed to its success.
9 THAT the Oklahoma House and Senate offer sincere thanks to the
10 Oklahoma Route 66 Centennial Commission and the Oklahoma Route 66
11 Commission and their partner agencies.
12 THAT copies of this resolution be distributed to Lt. Governor
13 Matt Pinnell, chairman of the Oklahoma Route 66 Centennial
14 Commission, and Thomas Tillison, chairman of the Oklahoma Route 66
15 Commission.
16
17 DIRECT TO CALENDAR.

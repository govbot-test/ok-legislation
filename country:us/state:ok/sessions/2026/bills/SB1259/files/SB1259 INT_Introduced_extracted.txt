7 An Act relating to aerospace, aircraft, and aviation

9 date; and declaring an emergency.

12
13 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 65.23 of Title 3, unless there
14
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15
16 A. As used in this section, “public airport” means an airport
16
17 open to use by all persons without prior permission of the airport
17
18 lessee or operator, and without restrictions within the physical
18
19 capacities of its available facilities.
19
20 B. A public airport that owns, operates, or leases parking
20
21 facilities shall grant free parking to a vehicle owned and occupied
21
22 as either a driver or passenger by a veteran who has been honorably
22
23 discharged from active service in any branch of the Armed Forces of
23
24 the United States or the Oklahoma National Guard and who has been

1 cert ified by the United States Department of Veterans Affairs or the
1
2 Armed Forces of the United States as having a disability of one
2
3 hundred percent (100%). Documentation certifying such disability
3
4 shall be presented to the public airport to waive any fee associated
4
5 with using such facility.
5
6 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
6
7 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
7
8 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
8
9 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
9
10 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

6 An Act relating to Medicaid home- and community-based
services; amending 10 O.S. 2021, Section 1408, which
7 relates to definitions; modifying certain definition;
updating statutory language and references; and
8 providing an effective date.
9
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
12 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 10 O.S. 2021, Section 1408, is
13 amended to read as follows:
14 Section 1408. A. “Individual with intellectual disability” as
15 used in Sections Section 1406 through 1424 et seq. of this title
16 means a person who has significantly subaverage functioning,
17 intelligence quotient of less than seventy (70), manifested before
18 the age of eighteen (18) and existing concurrently with related
19 limitations in two or more of the following applicable adaptive
20 skill areas:
21 1. Communication;
22 2. Self-care;
23 3. Home living;
24 4. Social skills;
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1555 Page 1
1 5. Use of community resources;
2 6. Self-direction;
3 7. Health and safety;
4 8. Functional academics;
5 9. Leisure; and
6 10. Work meets the criteria for intellectual disorder as listed
7 in the Social Security Administration Listing of Impairments for
8 disability evaluation, 20 C.F.R., Part 404, Subpart P.
9 B. “Resident” as used in Sections Section 1406 through 1424 et
10 seq. of this title shall mean a person admitted to and in residence
11 in any of the institutions named in Section 1406 of this title, or
12 on a vacation or extended vacation status from such institution.
13 C. “Developmental disability” as used in Sections Section 1406
14 through 1424 et seq. of this title means a severe, chronic
15 disability of a person which:
16 1. Is attributable to a mental or physical impairment or
17 combination of mental and physical impairments, such as intellectual
18 developmental disorder, cerebral palsy, or autism;
19 2. Is manifested before the person attains twenty-two (22)
20 years of age;
21 3. Is likely to continue indefinitely;
22 4. Results in substantial functional limitations in three or
23 more of the following areas of major life activity:
24 a. self-care,
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1555 Page 2
1 b. receptive and expressive language,
2 c. learning,
3 d. mobility,
4 e. self-direction,
5 f. capacity for independent living, and
6 g. economic self-sufficiency; and
7 5. Reflects the person’s need for a combination and sequence of
8 special, interdisciplinary, or generic care, treatment, or other
9 services which are of lifelong or extended duration and are
10 individually planned and coordinated. The term developmental
11 disability shall not include mentally ill incompetent persons, as
12 those persons are defined by Section 1-103 of Title 43A of the
13 Oklahoma Statutes, whose sole disability is mental illness as
14 defined by Section 1-103 of Title 43A of the Oklahoma Statutes.
15 D. Nothing in subsection C of this section shall be construed
16 to render persons who are receiving services upon September 1, 1991,
17 through programs and services for individuals with intellectual
18 disabilities offered by the Department of Human Services as
19 ineligible for such services. The Department of Human Services may
20 provide, within the limitations of funds and other resources
21 available for such purpose, programs and services for persons with
22 developmental disabilities who are not presently served by the
23 Department of Human Services.
24 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

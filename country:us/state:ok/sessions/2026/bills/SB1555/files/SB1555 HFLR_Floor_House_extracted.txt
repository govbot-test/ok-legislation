9 An Act relating to Medicaid home- and community-based
services; amending 10 O.S. 2021, Section 1408, which
10 relates to definitions; modifying certain definition;
updating statutory language and references; and
11 providing an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 10 O.S. 2021, Section 1408, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 1408. A. “Individual with intellectual disability” as
18 used in Sections Section 1406 through 1424 et seq. of this title
19 means a person who has significantly subaverage functioning,
20 intelligence quotient of less than seventy (70), manifested before
21 the age of eighteen (18) and existing concurrently with related
22 limitations in two or more of the following applicable adaptive
23 skill areas:
24 1. Communication;
SB1555 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 2. Self-care;
2 3. Home living;
3 4. Social skills;
4 5. Use of community resources;
5 6. Self-direction;
6 7. Health and safety;
7 8. Functional academics;
8 9. Leisure; and
9 10. Work meets the criteria for intellectual disorder as listed
10 in the Social Security Administration Listing of Impairments for
11 disability evaluation, 20 C.F.R., Part 404, Subpart P.
12 B. “Resident” as used in Sections Section 1406 through 1424 et
13 seq. of this title shall mean a person admitted to and in residence
14 in any of the institutions named in Section 1406 of this title, or
15 on a vacation or extended vacation status from such institution.
16 C. “Developmental disability” as used in Sections Section 1406
17 through 1424 et seq. of this title means a severe, chronic
18 disability of a person which:
19 1. Is attributable to a mental or physical impairment or
20 combination of mental and physical impairments, such as intellectual
21 developmental disorder, cerebral palsy, or autism;
22 2. Is manifested before the person attains twenty-two (22)
23 years of age;
24 3. Is likely to continue indefinitely;
SB1555 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 4. Results in substantial functional limitations in three or
2 more of the following areas of major life activity:
3 a. self-care,
4 b. receptive and expressive language,
5 c. learning,
6 d. mobility,
7 e. self-direction,
8 f. capacity for independent living, and
9 g. economic self-sufficiency; and
10 5. Reflects the person’s need for a combination and sequence of
11 special, interdisciplinary, or generic care, treatment, or other
12 services which are of lifelong or extended duration and are
13 individually planned and coordinated. The term developmental
14 disability shall not include mentally ill incompetent persons, as
15 those persons are defined by Section 1-103 of Title 43A of the
16 Oklahoma Statutes, whose sole disability is mental illness as
17 defined by Section 1-103 of Title 43A of the Oklahoma Statutes.
18 D. Nothing in subsection C of this section shall be construed
19 to render persons who are receiving services upon September 1, 1991,
20 through programs and services for individuals with intellectual
21 disabilities offered by the Department of Human Services as
22 ineligible for such services. The Department of Human Services may
23 provide, within the limitations of funds and other resources
24 available for such purpose, programs and services for persons with

1 deve lopmental disabilities who are not presently served by the
2 Department of Human Services.
3 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

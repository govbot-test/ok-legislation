13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 21 O.S. 2021, Section 1289.4, is
15 amended to read as follows:
16 Section 1289.4.
17 DEFINITIONS FOR FIREARMS ACT
18 "Rifles" as used in the Oklahoma Firearms Act of 1971, Sections
19 1289.1 through 1289.17 of this title and the Oklahoma Self-Defense
20 Act, shall mean any firearm capable of discharging a projectile
21 composed of any material which may reasonably be expected to be able
22 to cause lethal injury, with a barrel or barrels more than sixteen
23 (16) inches in length, and using either gunpowder, gas or any means
24 of rocket propulsion, but not to include archery equipment, flare

1 guns or underwater fishing guns. In addition, any rifle capable of
2 firing "shot" but primarily designed to fire single projectiles will
3 be regarded as a "rifle".
4 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 21 O.S. 2021, Section 1289.5, is
5 amended to read as follows:
6 Section 1289.5.
7 DEFINITIONS FOR FIREARMS ACT
8 "Shotguns" as used in the Oklahoma Firearms Act of 1971 and the
9 Oklahoma Self-Defense Act, shall mean any firearm capable of
10 discharging a series of projectiles of any material which may
11 reasonably be expected to be able to cause lethal injury, with a
12 barrel or barrels more than eighteen (18) inches or more in length,
13 and using a combustible propellant charge, but not to include any
14 weapon so designed with a barrel less than eighteen (18) inches in
15 length unless the overall length of the firearm is twenty-six (26)
16 inches or more. In addition, any "shotgun" capable of firing single
17 projectiles but primarily designed to fire multiple projectiles such
18 as "shot" will be regarded as a "shotgun".

21 2025, Section 1289.18), is amended to read as follows:
22 Section 1289.18.
23 DEFINITIONS

1 A. "Sawed-off shotgun" shall mean any firearm capable of
2 discharging a series of projectiles of any material which may
3 reasonably be expected to be able to cause lethal injury, with a
4 barrel or barrels less than eighteen (18) inches in length, and
5 using a combustible propellant charge, but does not include any
6 weapon so designed with a barrel less than eighteen (18) inches in
7 length, provided it has an overall length of twenty-six (26) inches

9 B. "Sawed-off rifle" shall mean any rifle having a barrel or
10 barrels of less than sixteen (16) inches in length or any weapon
11 made from a rifle (whether by alteration, modification, or
12 otherwise) if such a weapon as modified has an overall length of
13 less than twenty-six (26) inches in length, including the stock
14 portion.
15 C. Every person who knowingly has in his or her possession or
16 under his or her immediate control a sawed-off shotgun or a sawed-
17 off rifle that is not in compliance with the provisions of Sections
18 1289.4 and 1289.5 of this title, whether concealed or not, shall
19 upon conviction be guilty of a Class D2 felony offense misdemeanor
20 for the possession of such device, and shall be punishable punished
21 by a fine not to exceed One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00), or by
22 imprisonment as provided for in subsections B through F of Section
23 20O of this title in the county jail not to exceed one (1) year, or
24 both such fine and imprisonment.

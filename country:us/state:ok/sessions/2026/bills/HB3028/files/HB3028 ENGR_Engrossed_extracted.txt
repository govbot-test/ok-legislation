12 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 14A O.S. 2021, Section 2-211, as
13 amended by Section 1, Chapter 410, O.S.L. 2025 (14A O.S. Supp. 2025,
14 Section 2-211), is amended to read as follows:
15 Section 2-211. A. With respect to all sales, service, and
16 lease transactions including, but not limited to, any consumer
17 credit sales transaction, a discount which a seller offers, allows,
18 or otherwise makes available for the purpose of inducing payment by
19 cash, check, debit card, or similar means rather than by use of a
20 credit card shall not constitute a credit service charge as
21 determined under Section 2-109 of this title if the discount is
22 offered to all prospective buyers clearly and conspicuously in
23 accordance with regulations of the Administrator of Consumer Credit.
24 There shall be no limit on the discount that may be offered by the

4 B. No seller may impose a surcharge on a cardholder who elects
5 to pay using a credit card instead of paying by cash, check, debit
6 card, or similar means unless the seller complies with the following
7 requirements:
8 1. Notice displaying the amount of the surcharge applicable
9 shall be clearly and conspicuously posted at the point of entry and
10 the point of sale for in-person transactions and the home page and
11 the point-of-sale webpage for online transactions. Notice,
12 including all required information, shall be verbally disclosed to
13 the customer for transactions processed over the phone; and
14 2. No surcharge shall exceed two percent (2%) of the total
15 transaction or the actual amount to be charged to the person or
16 retailer to process the credit card transaction, whichever is less.
17 A customer shall not be considered to have chosen to use a credit
18 card as a method of payment under this section if, at the time of
19 the transaction, the person or retailer accepts only credit cards as
20 payment.
21 C. A seller who is registered with the United States Department
22 of the Treasury as a money transmitter pursuant to 31 C.F.R.,
23 Section 103.41, and who provides an electronic funds transmission
24 service, including service by telephone and the Internet, may charge

8 POLICY COMMITTEE RECOMMENDATION
9 An Act relating to medical micropigmentation;
amending 63 O.S. 2021, Section 1-1452, which relates
10 to who can perform micropigmentation; authorizing
independent authority once certain criteria are met;
11 and providing an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 63 O.S. 2021, Section 1-1452, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 1-1452. On and after May 1, 2002, medical
18 micropigmentation may only be performed in a physician's office by:
19 1. A physician as defined by the Oklahoma Medical
20 Micropigmentation Regulation Act;
21 2. A person licensed to practice registered nursing by the
22 Oklahoma Board of Nursing who holds a current certificate issued by
23 the State Commissioner of Health pursuant to the provisions of the
24 Oklahoma Medical Micropigmentation Regulation Act while working
Req. No. 16101 Page 1
1 unde r supervision of a physician. Supervision may cease once a
2 person has been supervised for six thousand two hundred forty
3 (6,240) or more hours, who has reported those hours to the State
4 Board of Health, and who does not use prescription medication on the
5 client while performing the micropigmentation service. The level of
6 supervision shall be determined by the physician in whose office
7 medical micropigmentation is being performed; and
8 3. A person who holds a current certificate issued by the State
9 Commissioner of Health pursuant to the provisions of the Oklahoma
10 Medical Micropigmentation Regulation Act while working under
11 supervision of a physician. Supervision may cease once a person has
12 been supervised for six thousand two hundred forty (6,240) or more
13 hours, who has reported those hours to the State Board of Health,
14 and who does not use prescription medication on the client while
15 performing the micropigmentation service. The level of supervision
16 shall be determined by the physician in whose office medical
17 micropigmentation is being performed.
18 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

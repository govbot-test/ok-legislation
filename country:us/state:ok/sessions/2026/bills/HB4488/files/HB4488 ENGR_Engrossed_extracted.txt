9 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
10 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1250.8a of Title 36, unless
11 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
12 A. As used in this section:
13 1. "Appraiser" means a person selected by the insurer or the
14 insured to place a value on or estimate the amount of loss under an
15 appraisal clause in an insurance contract;
16 2. "Competent" means the person has subject matter expertise,
17 relevant training, and experience to make decisions and valuations
18 relating to the amount of loss; and
19 3. "Disinterested" means the person does not have a direct
20 financial interest in the outcome of the appraisal process.
21 B. Every motor vehicle insurance policy that includes first-
22 party coverage for physical damage issued or renewed effective on or
23 after January 1, 2027, shall include a provision for the right to an
24 appraisal to resolve disputes between the insurer and the insured
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4488 Page 6

1 rega rding the actual cash value and amount of loss on the damaged
2 automobile. The appraisal clause shall include the following
3 language, or corresponding language that the insurer certifies is at
4 least as favorable to the insured:
5 "1. If you and we fail to agree on the amount of loss, either
6 party may make written demand for an appraisal;
7 2. Each party will select an appraiser and notify the other
8 party of the identity of each appraiser within twenty (20) days
9 after the demand is received;
10 3. The appraisers will select a competent and impartial umpire;
11 4. If the appraisers are unable to agree upon an umpire within
12 fifteen (15) days after receiving notice of the identity of the
13 appraisers pursuant to item 2. above, you or we can ask a judge of a
14 court of record in the county where your residence is located to
15 select an umpire;
16 5. The appraisers shall then appraise the loss and submit to us
17 written reports stating separately the loss to each item;
18 6. If the reports of the appraisers are in agreement, we will
19 pay the agreed upon amount;
20 7. If the appraisers cannot agree, they will submit reports to
21 the umpire, and we will pay the amount agreed upon by any two; and
22 8. Each party will pay the appraiser it has chosen and will pay
23 an equal share of the expenses for the umpire and all other expenses
24 of the appraisal."

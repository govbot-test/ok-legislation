Section 1250.8, which relates to motor vehicle total
12 loss or damage claim; providing reference to
appraisal process; providing definitions; requiring

15 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1250.8a of Title 36, unless
17 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. As used in this section:
19 1. "Appraiser" means a person selected by the insurer or the
20 insured to place a value on or estimate the amount of loss under an
21 appraisal clause in an insurance contract;
22 2. "Competent" means the person has subject matter expertise,
23 relevant training, and experience to make decisions and valuations
24 relating to the amount of loss; and
HB4488 HFLR Page 6
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.

1 3. "Disinterested" means the person does not have a direct
2 financial interest in the outcome of the appraisal process.
3 B. Every motor vehicle insurance policy that includes first-
4 party coverage for physical damage issued or renewed effective on or
5 after January 1, 2027, must include a provision for the right to an
6 appraisal to resolve disputes between the insurer and the insured
7 regarding the actual cash value and amount of loss on the damaged
8 automobile. The appraisal clause must include the following
9 language, or corresponding language that the insurer certifies is at
10 least as favorable to the insured:
11 "If . . . (the insurance company) . . . and . . . (the
12 policyholder) . . . are unable to agree as to the amount of loss,
13 either party may make a written demand for an appraisal, and within
14 ten (10) days, the parties shall select a competent and
15 disinterested appraiser. The designation of an appraiser shall be
16 selected from a pool of three recommendations from the insured, with
17 the insurer choosing one. Except as otherwise provided in this
18 section, the cost of the appraiser shall be paid equally by the
19 parties.
20 The appraiser shall then appraise the actual cash value and the
21 amount of loss and make findings regarding the amount of loss for
22 each element of loss. Appraisal findings are binding upon the
23 parties.

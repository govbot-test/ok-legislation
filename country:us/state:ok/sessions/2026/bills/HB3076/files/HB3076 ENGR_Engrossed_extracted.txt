7 An Act relating to teachers; amending 70 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 6-133, as amended by Section 1, Chapter 349,
8 O.S.L. 2023 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 6-133),
which relates to new and innovative pathways toward
9 teacher certification; expanding definition of
provider; clarifying definition of program; replacing
10 State Board of Education with Commission for
Educational Quality and Accountability; striking
11 pilot program language; requiring the Commission to
initiate a request for information from potential
12 providers; providing a timeline for the approval or
denial of applications; requiring the Commission to
13 provide notice for a denial and an opportunity to
remedy deficiencies; directing the Commission to
14 develop, promote, and coordinate a public awareness
program; requiring the Commission to promulgate
15 rules; amending 70 O.S. 2021, Section 6-122.3, as
last amended by Section 162, Chapter 452, O.S.L. 2024
16 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 6-122.3), which relates
to alternative placement teaching certificates;
17 adding alternative teacher preparation programs to
eligibility requirements; providing an effective
18 date; and declaring an emergency.
19
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
22 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 6-133, as
23 amended by Section 1, Chapter 349, O.S.L. 2023 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025,
24 Section 6-133), is amended to read as follows:
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3076 Page 1
1 Section 6-133. A. As used in this section:
2 1. "Provider" means an eligible school district, regional
3 education service agency, institution of higher education, or other
4 private or not-for-profit entity which is, or will be within three
5 (3) years of being approved by the Commission for Educational
6 Quality and Accountability, accredited by the Council for the
7 Accreditation of Educator Preparation or the Association for
8 Advancing Quality in Educator Preparation that seeks or has obtained
9 approval of an alternative teacher preparation program consistent
10 with this section; and
11 2. "Program" means an alternative teacher preparation program
12 with content provided by a provider that leads toward teacher
13 certification or licensure in a specific content area.
14 B. 1. The State Board of Education Commission for Educational
15 Quality and Accountability shall identify providers to implement new
16 and innovative pathways toward teacher certification on a pilot
17 program basis through alternative teacher preparation programs. The
18 Commission shall initiate a request for information from potential
19 providers and shall provide eligible providers with information
20 concerning the application process. Providers seeking to
21 participate in the pilot program shall submit applications to the
22 State Board of Education Commission. The Commission shall approve
23 or deny an application within sixty (60) days of submission. If the
24 Commission denies an application, the Commission shall provide
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3076 Page 2
1 noti ce with the reasons for the denial and permit the provider an
2 opportunity to remedy the deficiencies.
3 2. The Commission for Educational Quality and Accountability
4 shall develop, promote, and coordinate a public awareness program to
5 be utilized in making potential teachers aware of approved providers
6 and their programs. The State Board of Education shall post on its
7 Internet website a list of approved providers, program requirements,
8 and the cost of each program.
9 C. A provider chosen approved by the State Board of Education
10 to participate in the pilot program Commission for Educational
11 Quality and Accountability shall offer a program that:
12 1. Provides evidence and history of fiscal solvency, capacity,
13 operation, and program effectiveness;
14 2. Provides evidence of necessary infrastructure to provide
15 accurate, timely, and secure data for the purpose of admission,
16 teacher candidate monitoring, testing, and certification
17 recommendations;
18 3. Has policies and procedures in place ensuring the security
19 of teacher candidate records under applicable laws and regulations;
20 4. Has the instructional capacity within an existing novice
21 teacher support program, the ability to obtain the instructional
22 capacity, or has established a partnership with an accredited
23 institution of higher education institution to provide adequate
24 instruction in accordance with this subsection;
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3076 Page 3
1 5. Offers an instructional phase that provides intensive
2 preparation before the teacher candidates assume classroom
3 responsibilities;
4 6. Offers a research-based and results-oriented approach
5 focused on best teaching practices to increase student achievement
6 and growth measured against state academic standards;
7 7. Provides assessment, supervision, and evaluation of teacher
8 candidates to determine their specific needs throughout the program
9 and to support efforts to successfully complete the program;
10 8. Provides intensive and ongoing professional development
11 opportunities that accelerate a teacher candidate's professional
12 growth, support student learning, and provide a workplace
13 orientation, professional staff development, and mentoring;
14 9. Offers peer review focused on standards of professional
15 practice and continuous professional growth; and
16 10. Provides a process to review a teacher candidate's final
17 competency of required certification content standards that leads to
18 a potential candidate being recommended for teacher certification in
19 accordance with subsection F of this section.
20 D. The State Board of Education Commission for Educational
21 Quality and Accountability shall ensure that providers approved to
22 participate in the pilot program allow teacher candidates to
23 demonstrate pedagogy and content standard proficiency in school-
24 based programs and through other nontraditional means.
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3076 Page 4
1 Nont raditional means may include, but not be limited to, previous
2 work experience, teacher experience, educator evaluations, industry-
3 recognized certifications, and other essentially equivalent
4 demonstrations.
5 E. If the State Board of Education Commission determines that a
6 provider fails to meet or is deficient in any of the requirements of
7 this section including failing to become accredited by the Council
8 for the Accreditation of Educator Preparation or the Association for
9 Advancing Quality in Educator Preparation within three (3) years of
10 the date of approval by the Commission, it the Commission may
11 suspend or revoke the approval of the provider after providing
12 notification of the deficiencies and an opportunity to remedy the
13 deficiencies within a specified period of time as determined by the
14 Commission.
15 F. A teacher candidate that completes a program offered by an
16 approved provider shall be eligible for certification, provided he
17 or she meets the certification requirements set forth in Section 6-
18 187 of this title in the area of specialization for which
19 certification is sought and is recommended for certification by the
20 approved provider.
21 G. The State Board of Education shall issue a certificate to
22 teach to any person who meets the requirements set forth in this
23 section and who has on file with the Board a current Oklahoma
24 criminal history record check from the Oklahoma State Bureau of
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3076 Page 5
1 Inve stigation as well as a national criminal history record check as
2 defined in Section 150.9 of Title 74 of the Oklahoma Statutes. The
3 person applying for the certificate shall be responsible for the
4 cost of the criminal history record checks.
5 H. A provider shall submit a report to the State Department of
6 Education Office of Educational Quality and Accountability after one
7 (1) year of operation showing the percentage of teacher candidates
8 who have completed the program and who have successfully completed
9 or who have failed the competency examinations for certification and
10 the provider's efforts to help candidates successfully pass the
11 examinations. A provider shall annually submit a report to the
12 State Department of Education Office of Educational Quality and
13 Accountability outlining the success of the program.
14 I. The State Board of Education may Commission for Educational
15 Quality and Accountability shall promulgate rules to implement the
16 provisions of this section.
17 J. The pilot program created in this section shall end no later
18 than July 1, 2026.
19 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 6-122.3, as
20 last amended by Section 162, Chapter 452, O.S.L. 2024 (70 O.S. Supp.
21 2025, Section 6-122.3), is amended to read as follows:
22 Section 6-122.3. A. The State Board of Education shall grant
23 an alternative placement teaching certificate to a person who makes
24 application to the Board and meets the following criteria:
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3076 Page 6
1 1. a. holds at least a baccalaureate degree from an
2 institution whose accreditation is recognized by the
3 Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education and has
4 attained a retention grade point average of not less
5 than 2.50 on a 4.0 scale, or
6 b. has successfully completed a terminal degree, such as
7 a doctorate of philosophy, a doctorate in education,
8 professional doctorates, a master of fine arts degree
9 or a master of library science degree, from an
10 institution accredited by a national or regional
11 accrediting agency which is recognized by the
12 Secretary of the United States Department of
13 Education. The Oklahoma State Regents for Higher
14 Education shall be consulted to verify other terminal
15 degrees, or
16 c. holds at least a baccalaureate degree from an
17 institution whose accreditation is recognized by the
18 Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education and has
19 qualified work experience in a field that corresponds
20 to an area of certification as determined by the State
21 Board of Education, and
22 d. in addition to the requirements of subparagraphs a, b
23 and c of this paragraph, has demonstrated competency
24 or completed a major in a field that corresponds to an
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3076 Page 7
1 area of specialization for an Elementary-Secondary
2 Certificate or a Secondary Certificate as determined
3 by the State Board of Education or a vocational-
4 technical certificate as recommended by the Oklahoma
5 Department of Career and Technology Education;
6 2. Declares the intention to earn standard certification by
7 means of an alternative placement program or an alternative teacher
8 preparation program provided for in Section 6-133 of this title in
9 not more than three (3) years. The State Board of Education shall
10 determine the subject matter and the number of clock or semester
11 hours required for the professional education component for each
12 person making application for an alternative placement teaching
13 certificate based on the criteria of paragraph 1 of this subsection.
14 The State Board of Education shall establish a core minimum of
15 six (6) semester hours or ninety (90) clock hours and a maximum of
16 eighteen (18) semester hours or two hundred seventy (270) clock
17 hours for the professional education component.
18 The requirements set forth in this subsection shall exclude all
19 student teaching requirements pursuant to the provisions of
20 subsection E of this section;
21 3. Has passed the subject area portion of the competency
22 examination required in Section 6-187 of this title in the area of
23 specialization for which certification is sought; and
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3076 Page 8
1 4. Either presents a document from an accredited public school
2 district in this state offering employment in the area of
3 specialization for which certification is sought on condition that
4 the person enroll in an alternative placement program approved by
5 the State Board of Education or declares the intention to seek
6 employment as a teacher at an accredited public school district in
7 this state. The certificate granted pursuant to this subsection
8 shall be considered a "valid certificate of qualification" for the
9 purposes of Sections 6-107 and 6-108 of this title, and the holder
10 of the certificate shall be considered an inductee for the purposes
11 of Section 6-195 of this title.
12 B. An alternative placement teaching certificate shall be
13 renewed for not more than a maximum of three (3) years upon
14 presentation of a document from an accredited public school district
15 in this state offering renewed employment in the same area of
16 specialization and a document from a teacher education institution
17 verifying satisfactory progress in an appropriate alternative
18 placement program.
19 C. Persons enrolled in an alternative placement program shall:
20 1. Have never been denied admittance to a teacher education
21 program approved by the Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education,
22 the North Central Association of Colleges and Schools and by the
23 Oklahoma Commission for Teacher Preparation to offer teacher
24 education programs, nor have enrolled in and subsequently failed
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3076 Page 9
1 cour ses necessary to successfully meet the minimum requirements of
2 the program, except those persons who hold a certificate;
3 2. Have on file with the director of teacher education at an
4 Oklahoma institution of higher education a plan for meeting standard
5 certification requirements within three (3) years; and
6 3. Participate in an induction program as required in Section
7 6-195 of this title and have the same duties and responsibilities as
8 other inductees.
9 D. The State Board of Education may grant an exception to the
10 requirements for certification and, upon demonstration by an
11 individual of specific competency in the subject area of
12 specialization, may grant a certificate to the individual. The
13 State Board may establish other requirements necessary to grant
14 exceptions.
15 E. The State Board of Education, in consultation with the
16 Commission for Educational Quality and Accountability, may grant an
17 exception to the requirement to complete a subject area examination
18 for initial certification in a field which does not require an
19 advanced degree pursuant to this section if the candidate has an
20 advanced degree in a subject that is substantially comparable to the
21 content assessed on a subject area examination. The degree shall be
22 from an institution accredited by a national or regional accrediting
23 agency which is recognized by the Secretary of the U.S. Department
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3076 Page 10
1 of E ducation. The Commission shall provide the Board with the
2 necessary information to determine comparability.
3 F. Student teaching and a prestudent teaching field experience
4 shall not be required of alternative placement program participants
5 for standard certification.
6 G. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules
7 authorizing adjunct teachers who shall be persons with distinguished
8 qualifications in their field. Adjunct teachers shall not be
9 required to meet standard certification. A person employed as an
10 adjunct teacher pursuant to this subsection who does not hold a
11 valid certificate to teach shall not be considered a teacher as
12 defined by Section 1-116 of this title.
13 H. Each teacher education institution shall provide the
14 Oklahoma Commission for Teacher Preparation an annual report of
15 information as specified by the Commission regarding participation
16 in the alternative placement programs offered by the institution.
17 I. The Oklahoma Commission for Teacher Preparation shall not
18 accredit, renew the accreditation of, or otherwise approve any
19 teacher education program of any institution of higher education in
20 this state that has not implemented alternative placement programs
21 in at least four areas of specialization including mathematics,
22 science and a foreign language. Each institution shall allow
23 individuals who meet the criteria of subsections A and C of this
24 section to be:
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3076 Page 11
1 1. Admitted to an alternative placement program without further
2 qualification; and
3 2. Offered the opportunity to complete the requirements for
4 standard certification set forth in subsection A of this section
5 during the summer preceding and the summer following the first year
6 of teaching with an alternative placement teaching certificate. Any
7 person seeking standard certification through an alternative
8 placement program shall be permitted to take necessary courses
9 during regular semesters if offered.
10 J. The criteria specified in subsection I of this section can
11 be met through a cooperative arrangement entered into by two or more
12 institutions of higher education.
13 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
14 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
15 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
16 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
17 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

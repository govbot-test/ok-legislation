11 An Act relating to the state Medicaid program;
directing the Secretary of State to refer to the
12 people for their approval or rejection a proposed
amendment to Section 2 of Article XXV-A of the
13 Oklahoma Constitution; removing prohibition for
imposing certain eligibility restrictions; directing
14 the Secretary of State to refer to the people for
their approval or rejection a proposed amendment to
15 repeal Sections 1, 2, 3, and 4 of Article XXV-A of
the Oklahoma Constitution, which relate to Medicaid
16 expansion; making repealers contingent upon certain
federal action; ordering a legislative referendum;
17 defining terms; requiring state to provide certain
medical assistance; requiring Oklahoma Health Care
18 Authority to maximize certain federal funding; making
provisions contingent upon certain federal action;
19 providing ballot title; ordering special election on
certain date; providing for codification; and
20 directing filing.

22
23 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
24
Req. No. 3933 Page 1
1 SECTION 1. The Secretary of State shall refer to the people for
2 their approval or rejection, as and in the manner provided by law, a
3 proposed amendment to Section 2 of Article XXV-A of the Oklahoma
4 Constitution to read as follows:
5 Section 2. A. In addition to those otherwise eligible for
6 medical assistance under Oklahoma’s Medicaid program, the State
7 shall provide medical assistance under Oklahoma’s Medicaid program
8 to Low Income Adults.
9 B. No greater or additional burdens or restrictions on
10 eligibility or enrollment shall be imposed on persons eligible for
11 medical assistance pursuant to this Article than on any other
12 population eligible for medical assistance under Oklahoma’s Medicaid
13 program.
14 SECTION 2. The Secretary of State shall refer to the people for
15 their approval or rejection, as and in the manner provided by law, a
16 proposed amendment to repeal Sections 1, 2, 3, and 4 of Article XXV-
17 A of the Oklahoma Constitution. Such repeal will only occur if the
18 federal medical assistance percentage (FMAP) established under 42
19 U.S.C., Section 1396d(y) is reduced below ninety percent (90%).
20 SECTION 3. Pursuant to Section 3 of Article V of the Oklahoma
21 Constitution, there is hereby ordered the following legislative
22 referendum which shall be filed with the Secretary of State and
23 addressed to the Governor of the state, who shall submit the same to
24 the people for their approval or rejection at a special election to
Req. No. 3933 Page 2
1 be h eld throughout the State of Oklahoma on the date of August 25,
2 2026.
3 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1011.26 of Title 56, unless
5 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 A. As used in this section:
7 1. “Low-income adults” refers to those individuals over
8 eighteen (18) years of age and under sixty-five (65) years of age
9 whose income does not exceed one hundred thirty-three percent (133%)
10 of the federal poverty level, as described by and using the income
11 methodology provided in the federal Medicaid statute at 42 U.S.C.,
12 Section 1396a(a)(10)(A)(i)(VIII), and who meet applicable
13 nonfinancial eligibility conditions for Medicaid under 42 C.F.R.,
14 Part 435, Subpart E;
15 2. “Medical assistance” means payment of part or all of the
16 cost of the care and services, or the care and services themselves,
17 or both, as provided in the federal Medicaid statute, 42 U.S.C.,
18 Section 1396 et seq.; and
19 3. “Oklahoma Health Care Authority” refers to the single state
20 agency responsible for administering the state Medicaid program
21 pursuant to 42 U.S.C., Section 1396a(a)(5).
22 B. In addition to those otherwise eligible for medical
23 assistance under the state Medicaid program, the state shall provide
24
Req. No. 3933 Page 3
1 medi cal assistance under the state Medicaid program to low-income
2 adults.
3 C. The Oklahoma Health Care Authority shall take all actions
4 necessary to maximize federal financial participation in funding
5 medical assistance pursuant to this section.
6 D. The provisions of this section shall only become effective
7 if the federal medical assistance percentage (FMAP) established
8 under 42 U.S.C., Section 1396d(y) is reduced below ninety percent
9 (90%).
10 SECTION 5. The proposed Constitutional amendment as set forth
11 in SECTION 2 of this act and the statutory provisions as set forth
12 in SECTION 4 of this act shall only become effective if the federal
13 medical assistance percentage (FMAP) established under 42 U.S.C.,
14 Section 1396d(y) is reduced below ninety percent (90%).
15 SECTION 6. The Ballot Title for the proposed Constitutional
16 amendments as set forth in SECTIONS 1 and 2 of this act and the
17 legislative referendum as ordered in SECTION 3 of this act and as
18 set forth in SECTION 4 of this act shall be in the following form:
19 BALLOT TITLE
20 Legislative Referendum No. ____ State Question No. ____
21 THE GIST OF THE PROPOSITION IS AS FOLLOWS:
22 This measure amends Section 2 of Article 25-A of the Oklahoma
23 Constitution. It would remove the prohibition against enacting
24 additional restrictions on eligibility for Medicaid.
Req. No. 3933 Page 4
1 Additionally, if federal funding for the Medicaid expansion
2 population drops below 90%, this measure will repeal Sections 1,
3 2, 3, and 4 of Article 25-A of the Oklahoma Constitution and
4 will enact substantially the same language related to Medicaid
5 expansion from the repealed constitutional provisions into the
6 Oklahoma Statutes. The statutes could be amended, repealed, or
7 otherwise modified by the Legislature pursuant to the same
8 procedures that allow enactment of laws during any regular or
9 extraordinary session of the Legislature.
10 SHALL THE PROPOSAL BE APPROVED?
11 FOR THE PROPOSAL — YES _____________
12 AGAINST THE PROPOSAL — NO _____________
13 SECTION 7. A special election is hereby ordered to be held
14 throughout the State of Oklahoma on the date of August 25, 2026, at
15 which time the proposed amendments to the Oklahoma Constitution as
16 set forth in SECTION 1 and SECTION 2 of this act, and the
17 legislative referendum as ordered in SECTION 3 of this act and as
18 set forth in SECTION 4 of this act, shall be submitted to the people
19 of Oklahoma for their approval or rejection as and in the manner
20 provided by law.
21 SECTION 8. The Chief Clerk of the House of Representatives
22 shall, immediately after the passage of this act, prepare and file
23 one copy thereof, including the Ballot Title set forth in SECTION 6

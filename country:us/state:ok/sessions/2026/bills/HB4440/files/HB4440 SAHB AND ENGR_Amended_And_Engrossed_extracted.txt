7 An Act relating to public health and safety;
ordering a legislative referendum pursuant to the
8 Oklahoma Constitution to amend the Oklahoma
Constitution by repealing Sections 1, 2, 3 and 4 of
9 Article XXV-A; ordering a legislative referendum;
providing for enactment of statutes related to
10 Medicaid and Medicaid expansion; defining terms;
requiring certain medical services be made available
11 to certain persons; imposing duty on Oklahoma Health
Care Authority; providing ballot title; ordering a
12 special election; and directing filing.

directing the Secretary of State to refer to the
17 people for their approval or rejection a proposed
amendment to Section 2 of Article XXV-A of the
18 Oklahoma Constitution; removing prohibition for

the Oklahoma Constitution, which relate to Medicaid

3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
4 SECTION 1. The Secretary of State shall refer to the people for
5 their approval or rejection, as and in the manner provided by law, a
6 proposed amendment to Section 2 of Article XXV-A of the Oklahoma
7 Constitution to read as follows:
8 Section 2. A. In addition to those otherwise eligible for
9 medical assistance under Oklahoma’s Medicaid program, the State
10 shall provide medical assistance under Oklahoma’s Medicaid program
11 to Low Income Adults.
12 B. No greater or additional burdens or restrictions on
13 eligibility or enrollment shall be imposed on persons eligible for
14 medical assistance pursuant to this Article than on any other
15 population eligible for medical assistance under Oklahoma’s Medicaid
16 program.
17 SECTION 2. The Secretary of State shall refer to the people for
18 their approval or rejection, as and in the manner provided by law, a
19 proposed amendment to repeal Sections 1, 2, 3, and 4 of Article XXV-
20 A of the Oklahoma Constitution. Such repeal will only occur if the
21 federal medical assistance percentage (FMAP) established under 42
22 U.S.C., Section 1396d(y) is reduced below ninety percent (90%).
23 SECTION 3. Pursuant to Section 3 of Article V of the Oklahoma
24 Constitution, there is hereby ordered the following legislative
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 4440 Page 2
1 refe rendum which shall be filed with the Secretary of State and
2 addressed to the Governor of the state, who shall submit the same to
3 the people for their approval or rejection at the next general
4 election.
5 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
6 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1011.26 of Title 56, unless
7 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
8 A. As used in this section:
9 1. “Low-income adults” refers to those individuals over
10 eighteen (18) years of age and under sixty-five (65) years of age
11 whose income does not exceed one hundred thirty-three percent (133%)
12 of the federal poverty level, as described by and using the income
13 methodology provided in the federal Medicaid statute at 42 U.S.C.,
14 Section 1396a(a)(10)(A)(i)(VIII), and who meet applicable
15 nonfinancial eligibility conditions for Medicaid under 42 C.F.R.,
16 Part 435, Subpart E;
17 2. “Medical assistance” means payment of part or all of the
18 cost of the care and services, or the care and services themselves,
19 or both, as provided in the federal Medicaid statute, 42 U.S.C.,
20 Section 1396 et seq.; and
21 3. “Oklahoma Health Care Authority” refers to the single state
22 agency responsible for administering the state Medicaid program
23 pursuant to 42 U.S.C., Section 1396a(a)(5).
24
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 4440 Page 3
1 B. In addition to those otherwise eligible for medical
2 assistance under the state Medicaid program, the state shall provide
3 medical assistance under the state Medicaid program to low-income
4 adults.
5 C. The Oklahoma Health Care Authority shall take all actions
6 necessary to maximize federal financial participation in funding
7 medical assistance pursuant to this section.
8 D. The provisions of this section shall only become effective
9 if the federal medical assistance percentage (FMAP) established
10 under 42 U.S.C., Section 1396d(y) is reduced below ninety percent
11 (90%).
12 SECTION 5. The proposed Constitutional amendment as set forth
13 in SECTION 2 of this act and the statutory provisions as set forth
14 in SECTION 4 of this act shall only become effective if the federal
15 medical assistance percentage (FMAP) established under 42 U.S.C.,
16 Section 1396d(y) is reduced below ninety percent (90%).
17 SECTION 6. The Ballot Title for the proposed Constitutional
18 amendments as set forth in SECTIONS 1 and 2 of this act and the
19 legislative referendum as ordered in SECTION 3 of this act and as
20 set forth in SECTION 4 of this act shall be in the following form:
21 BALLOT TITLE

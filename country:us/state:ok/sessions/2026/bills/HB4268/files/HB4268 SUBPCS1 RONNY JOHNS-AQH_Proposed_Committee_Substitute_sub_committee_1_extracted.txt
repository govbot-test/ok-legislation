1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
2 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
3 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Teacher
4 Effectiveness and Excellence Act".
5 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
6 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-213 of Title 70, unless there
7 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
8 A. As used in this section:
9 1. “Eligible teacher” means a full-time certified teacher in
10 grades fourth through eighth teaching English language arts and
11 mathematics;
12 2. “Student academic growth” means annual student progress as
13 measured by the Oklahoma State Testing Program;
14 3. “Recognized teacher” means an eligible teacher whose
15 students demonstrate student growth at or above an established
16 growth threshold; and
17 4. “Participating school” means a public or charter elementary
18 or middle school serving students in grades fourth through eighth.
19 B. There is hereby established the Growth-Based Teacher
20 Compensation Program to be administrated by the State Department of
21 Education. Beginning with the 2026-2027 school year, the pilot
22 program shall operate for a minimum of three (3) consecutive school
23 years.
24
Req. No. 16232 Page 2

1 C. To be eligible to participate in the program, teachers shall
2 be a full-time certified teacher in grades fourth through eighth
3 teaching English language arts and mathematics at a participating
4 school. The pilot program shall consist of one thousand five
5 hundred (1,500) teachers representing approximately fifteen percent
6 (15%) of the highest performing teachers serving grades fourth
7 through eighth in eligible schools in Oklahoma. The State Board of
8 Education shall establish criteria for the selection of teachers to
9 participate in the pilot program.
10 D. Recognized teachers shall receive annual performance-based
11 compensation under the program as follows:
12 1. Compensation shall be based solely on measurable student
13 academic growth. Such growth shall be calculated using a value-
14 added model based on a rolling two-year period of statewide
15 assessment data; provided, however, during the first year of program
16 implementation, compensation shall be calculated using the previous
17 and current year of academic growth data;
18 2. The State Department of Education shall place participating
19 recognized teachers into three (3) performance tiers based on their
20 statewide student growth percentiles as determined by student growth
21 on the Oklahoma State Testing Program assessment data;
22 3. The percentile distribution shall be adjusted as necessary
23 to ensure that total annual expenditures do not exceed
24 appropriations;

1 4. Participating recognized teachers shall be identified using
2 equivalent performance tiers which may include Tier 1 (95th
3 percentile and above), Tier 2 (90th through 94th percentiles), and
4 Tier 3 (85th through 89th percentiles); and
5 5. Awards shall be paid by the State Department of Education as
6 supplemental compensation. The performance-based compensation shall
7 not be included in the calculation of the teacher's salary for
8 purposes of meeting the district or statutory minimum salary
9 schedule or for purposes of compensating Oklahoma Teachers'
10 Retirement System contributions or benefits. No participating
11 school shall be liable for payment of the performance-based
12 compensation pursuant to this section.
13 F. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules to
14 implement the Growth-Based Teach Compensation Program in accordance
15 with this section.

18 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 A. As used in this section:
20 1. “Eligible teacher” means any certified teacher in any
21 subject area or grade level who has obtained National Board
22 Certification during the academic year; and
23 2. “National Board Certification” means certification granted
24 by the National Board for Professional Teaching Standards to a

1 teac her who has successfully completed all requirements for initial
2 certification or renewal, as applicable.
3 B. There is hereby established a National Board Certification
4 Bonus Program for eligible teachers employed by a public school
5 district in this state.
6 C. An eligible teacher who earns National Board Certification
7 shall receive a one-time bonus of Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00)
8 from the State Department of Education.
9 D. To be eligible for the bonus, a teacher shall have
10 successfully obtained National Board Certification during the
11 applicable school year with certification awarded on or before May 1
12 to receive a one-time bonus payment.
13 E. Bonuses shall be paid by the State Department of Education
14 as supplemental compensation. The bonus shall not be included in
15 the calculation of the teacher's salary for purposes of meeting the
16 district or statutory minimum salary schedule or for purposes of
17 compensating Oklahoma Teachers' Retirement System contributions or
18 benefits. No participating school shall be liable for payment of
19 bonuses pursuant to this section.
20 F. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules to
21 implement the National Board Certification Bonus Program in
22 accordance with this section.

1 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-215 of Title 70, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. 1. The Office of Educational Quality and Accountability
5 shall contract with an independent evaluator to conduct a rigorous
6 evaluation and report of both:
7 a. Growth-based Teacher Compensation Program implementation
8 fidelity, and
9 b. Growth-based Teacher Compensation Program and the
10 National Board Certification bonus effects on student
11 achievement and teacher workforce outcomes.
12 2. The evaluation created by an independent evaluator shall
13 include:
14 a. Comparison groups,
15 b. Longitudinal student growth analysis,
16 c. Workforce outcomes including retention and recruitment,
17 and
18 d. The number of teachers who have received a bonus for
19 obtaining National Board Certification as provided for in
20 Section 2 of this act.
21 B. 1. Beginning January 1, 2028, the State Department of
22 Education shall submit the results of the evaluation and report to:
23 a. The Governor,
24 b. The President Pro Tempore of the Senate, and

8 SECTION 5. There is hereby appropriated to the State Department

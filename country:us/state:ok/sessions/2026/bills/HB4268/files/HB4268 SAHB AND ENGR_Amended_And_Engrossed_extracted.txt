1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-213 of Title 70, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. As used in this section:
5 1. “Eligible teacher” means a full-time certified teacher in
6 grades four through eight teaching English language arts and
7 mathematics;
8 2. “Participating school” means a public or charter elementary
9 or middle school serving students in grades four through eight;
10 3. “Recognized teacher” means an eligible teacher whose
11 students demonstrate student growth at or above an established
12 growth threshold; and
13 4. “Student academic growth” means annual student progress as
14 measured by the Oklahoma School Testing Program.
15 B. There is hereby established the Growth-Based Teacher
16 Compensation Program to be administered by the State Board of
17 Education. Beginning with the 2026-2027 school year, the pilot
18 program shall operate for a minimum of three (3) consecutive school
19 years.
20 C. To be eligible to participate in the program, a teacher
21 shall be a full-time certified teacher in grades four through eight
22 teaching English language arts and mathematics at a participating
23 school. The pilot program shall consist of one thousand five
24 hundred (1,500) teachers representing approximately fifteen percent
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 4268 Page 3

1 (15% ) of the highest-performing teachers serving grades four through
2 eight in eligible schools in this state. The State Board of
3 Education shall establish criteria for the selection of recognized
4 teachers to participate in the pilot program.
5 D. Recognized teachers participating in the pilot program shall
6 receive annual performance-based compensation under the program as
7 follows:
8 1. Compensation shall be based solely on measurable student
9 academic growth. Such growth shall be calculated using a value-
10 added model based on a rolling two-year period of statewide
11 assessment data; provided, however, during the first year of program
12 implementation, compensation shall be calculated using the previous
13 and current years of academic growth data;
14 2. The State Department of Education shall place participating
15 recognized teachers into three performance tiers based on their
16 statewide student growth percentiles as determined by student growth
17 on Oklahoma School Testing Program assessment data;
18 3. The percentile distribution shall be adjusted as necessary
19 to ensure that total annual expenditures do not exceed available
20 funding for the program;
21 4. Participating recognized teachers shall be identified using
22 equivalent performance tiers which may include Tier 1 for the
23 ninety-fifth percentile and above, Tier 2 for the ninetieth through
24

1 nine ty-fourth percentiles, and Tier 3 for the eighty-fifth through
2 eighty-ninth percentiles; and
3 5. Awards shall be paid from the Teacher Empowerment Revolving
4 Fund created in Section 6-190.2 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes
5 by the State Department of Education as supplemental compensation.
6 The performance-based compensation shall not be included in the
7 calculation of the teacher’s salary for purposes of meeting the
8 district or statutory minimum salary schedule or for purposes of
9 compensating Teachers’ Retirement System of Oklahoma contributions
10 or benefits. No participating school shall be liable for payment of
11 the performance-based compensation pursuant to this section.
12 E. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules to
13 implement the Growth-Based Teacher Compensation Program in
14 accordance with this section.

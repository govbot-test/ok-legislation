18 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
19 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Teacher
20 Effectiveness and Excellence Act”.
21 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-213 of Title 70, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24 A. As used in this section:
Req. No. 3853 Page 1
1 1. “Eligible teacher” means a full-time certified teacher in
2 grades four through eight teaching English language arts and
3 mathematics;
4 2. “Participating school” means a public or charter elementary
5 or middle school serving students in grades four through eight;
6 3. “Recognized teacher” means an eligible teacher whose
7 students demonstrate student growth at or above an established
8 growth threshold; and
9 4. “Student academic growth” means annual student progress as
10 measured by the Oklahoma School Testing Program.
11 B. There is hereby established the Growth-Based Teacher
12 Compensation Program to be administered by the State Board of
13 Education. Beginning with the 2026-2027 school year, the pilot
14 program shall operate for a minimum of three (3) consecutive school
15 years.
16 C. To be eligible to participate in the program, a teacher
17 shall be a full-time certified teacher in grades four through eight
18 teaching English language arts and mathematics at a participating
19 school. The pilot program shall consist of one thousand five
20 hundred (1,500) teachers representing approximately fifteen percent
21 (15%) of the highest-performing teachers serving grades four through
22 eight in eligible schools in this state. The State Board of
23 Education shall establish criteria for the selection of recognized
24 teachers to participate in the pilot program.
Req. No. 3853 Page 2
1 D. Recognized teachers participating in the pilot program shall
2 receive annual performance-based compensation under the program as
3 follows:
4 1. Compensation shall be based solely on measurable student
5 academic growth. Such growth shall be calculated using a value-
6 added model based on a rolling two-year period of statewide
7 assessment data; provided, however, during the first year of program
8 implementation, compensation shall be calculated using the previous
9 and current years of academic growth data;
10 2. The State Department of Education shall place participating
11 recognized teachers into three performance tiers based on their
12 statewide student growth percentiles as determined by student growth
13 on Oklahoma School Testing Program assessment data;
14 3. The percentile distribution shall be adjusted as necessary
15 to ensure that total annual expenditures do not exceed available
16 funding for the program;
17 4. Participating recognized teachers shall be identified using
18 equivalent performance tiers which may include Tier 1 for the
19 ninety-fifth percentile and above, Tier 2 for the ninetieth through
20 ninety-fourth percentiles, and Tier 3 for the eighty-fifth through
21 eighty-ninth percentiles; and
22 5. Awards shall be paid from the Teacher Empowerment Revolving
23 Fund created in Section 6-190.2 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes
24 by the State Department of Education as supplemental compensation.
Req. No. 3853 Page 3
1 The performance-based compensation shall not be included in the
2 calculation of the teacher’s salary for purposes of meeting the
3 district or statutory minimum salary schedule or for purposes of
4 compensating Teachers’ Retirement System of Oklahoma contributions
5 or benefits. No participating school shall be liable for payment of
6 the performance-based compensation pursuant to this section.
7 E. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules to
8 implement the Growth-Based Teacher Compensation Program in
9 accordance with this section.
10 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
11 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 6-214 of Title 70, unless there
12 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
13 A. As used in this section:
14 1. “Eligible teacher” means any certified teacher in any
15 subject area or grade level who is employed by a public school
16 district in this state; and
17 2. “National Board certification” means certification granted
18 by the National Board for Professional Teaching Standards to a
19 teacher who has successfully completed all requirements for initial
20 certification or renewal, as applicable.
21 B. There is hereby established a National Board Certification
22 Bonus Program for eligible teachers who hold or attain National
23 Board certification.
24
Req. No. 3853 Page 4
1 C. An eligible teacher who attains, or has attained and
2 continues to hold, National Board certification on or after the
3 effective date of this act shall receive an annual bonus of Five
4 Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00) from the State Board of Education for a
5 five-year period subject to his or her continued employment with a
6 public school district in this state and maintenance of National
7 Board certification.
8 D. To begin receiving bonus payments, an eligible teacher shall
9 submit proof of his or her National Board certification to the State
10 Department of Education to receive his or her first annual bonus
11 payment which shall be paid no later than January 31 each year.
12 Bonuses shall be paid from the Teacher Empowerment Revolving Fund
13 created in Section 6-190.2 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes by
14 the State Department of Education as supplemental compensation. The
15 bonus shall not be included in the calculation of the teacher’s
16 salary for purposes of meeting the district or statutory minimum
17 salary schedule or for purposes of compensating Teachers’ Retirement
18 System of Oklahoma contributions or benefits. No school district
19 shall be liable for payment of bonuses pursuant to this section.
20 E. The State Board of Education shall promulgate rules to
21 implement the National Board Certification Bonus Program in
22 accordance with this section.

FOR ENGROSSED
4 SENATE BILL NO. 1090 By: Adams of the House
5 and
6 Thompson of the Senate
7
8
9 FLOOR SUBSTITUTE
10 An Act relating to contracts; creating the Oklahoma
Homeowner Repair Contract Protection Act; defining
11 home solicitation contract; allowing a five-day
voidable contract period for homeowners; providing
12 for codification; and providing an effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1100 of Title 15, unless there
18 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Oklahoma
20 Homeowner Repair Contract Protection Act".
21 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1101 of Title 15, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 17607 Page 1
1 "Home solicitation contract" means entering into a home repair
2 contract or services in which the home repair contractor or a person
3 acting for him or her engages in a personal solicitation of the sale
4 at a residence of the buyer, and the buyer's agreement is given to
5 the contractor or a person acting for him or her. It does not
6 include a sale made pursuant to a preexisting revolving contract, or
7 a contract made pursuant to prior negotiations between the parties
8 at a business establishment at a fixed location where goods or
9 services are offered or exhibited for sale. Nothing in this act
10 shall prohibit advertising, responding to homeowner-initiated
11 contact, or performing services when contacted directly by the
12 property owner.
13 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1102 of Title 15, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 1. In addition to any right otherwise to revoke an offer, the
17 homeowner has the right to cancel a home solicitation contract until
18 midnight of the fifth calendar day after the day on which the
19 homeowner signs an agreement for home repairs;
20 2. Cancellation occurs when the homeowner gives written notice
21 of cancellation via certified mail to the contractor at the address
22 stated in the agreement or offer to purchase;
23 3. Notice of cancellation is given when it is deposited in a
24 mailbox properly addressed and postage prepaid; and
Req. No. 17607 Page 2
1 4. Notice of cancellation given by the homeowner need not take
2 a particular form and is sufficient if it indicates by any form of
3 written expression the intention of the homeowner not to be bound by
4 the home solicitation sale.
5 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

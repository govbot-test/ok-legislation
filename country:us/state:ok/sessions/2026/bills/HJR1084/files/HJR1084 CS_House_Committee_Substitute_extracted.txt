8 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 A Joint Resolution directing the Secretary of State
to refer to the people for their approval or
10 rejection a proposed amendment to Section 1 of
Article VII of the Oklahoma Constitution; prohibiting
11 courts and the state or political subdivisions from
recognizing and enforcing certain codes or laws;
12 updating language; providing ballot title; and
directing filing.
13
14
15 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES AND THE SENATE OF THE
16 2ND SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE:
17 SECTION 1. The Secretary of State shall refer to the people for
18 their approval or rejection, as and in the manner provided by law,
19 the following proposed amendment to Section 1 of Article VII of the
20 Oklahoma Constitution to read as follows:
21 Section 1. A. The judicial power of this State shall be vested
22 in the Senate, sitting as a Court of Impeachment, a Supreme Court,
23 the Court of Criminal Appeals, the Court on the Judiciary, the State
24 Industrial Court, the Court of Bank Review, the Court of Tax Review,
Req. No. 16930 Page 1

1 and such intermediate appellate courts as may be provided by
2 statute, District Courts, and such Boards, Agencies and Commissions
3 created by the Constitution or established by statute as exercise
4 adjudicative authority or render decisions in individual
5 proceedings. Provided that the Court of Criminal Appeals, the State
6 Industrial Court, the Court of Bank Review and the Court of Tax
7 Review and such Boards, Agencies and Commissions as have been
8 established by statute shall continue in effect, subject to the
9 power of the Legislature to change or abolish said such Courts,
10 Boards, Agencies, or Commissions. Municipal Courts in cities or
11 incorporated towns shall continue in effect and shall be subject to
12 creation, abolition or alteration by the Legislature by general
13 laws, but shall be limited in jurisdiction to criminal and traffic
14 proceedings arising out of infractions of the provisions of
15 ordinances of cities and towns or of duly adopted regulations
16 authorized by such ordinances.
17 B. No court, the state, or any political subdivision, board,
18 agency, or commission created by the Constitution or established by
19 statute shall recognize or enforce any religious code, body of law,
20 or practice that is repugnant to, incompatible with, or that does
21 not recognize and protect the same or equivalent individual rights
22 and liberties guaranteed under the Constitution of the State of
23 Oklahoma or the Constitution of the United States of America.

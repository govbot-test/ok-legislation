12 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 36 O.S. 2021, Section 981, is
13 amended to read as follows:
14 Section 981. Short Title and Purposes of Act.
15 A. Sections 981 through 998 of this title and Sections 22, 23
16 and 24 of this act shall constitute a part of the Oklahoma Insurance
17 Code and shall be known and may be cited as the “Property and
18 Casualty Competitive Loss Cost Rating Act”.
19 B. The purposes of the Property and Casualty Competitive Loss
20 Cost Rating Act are:
21 1. To promote price competition among insurers so as to provide
22 rates that are responsive to competitive market conditions;
23 2. To protect policyholders and the public against the adverse
24 effects of excessive, inadequate or unfairly discriminatory rates;

1 3. To prohibit unlawful price-fixing agreements and other
2 anticompetitive behavior by insurers;
3 4. To provide regulatory procedures for the maintenance of
4 appropriate data reporting systems;
5 5. To provide regulatory controls in the absence of a
6 competitive marketplace; and
7 6. To authorize essential cooperative action among insurers in
8 the ratemaking process and to regulate such activity to prevent
9 practices that substantially lessen competition or create a
10 monopoly.

4 amended to read as follows:
5 Section 985. Ratemaking Standards.
6 A. A rate may shall not be excessive, inadequate, or unfairly
7 discriminatory.
8 1. No rate in a competitive market may be determined to be
9 excessive. A rate in a noncompetitive market may be determined to
10 be excessive if it is likely to produce a profit that is
11 unreasonably high for the insurance provided A rate may be
12 determined to be excessive if it is unreasonably high for the
13 insurance provided or is unreasonably high in relation to the
14 services rendered.
15 2. A rate may shall not be determined to be inadequate unless:
16 a. the rate is clearly insufficient to sustain projected
17 losses, expenses and special assessments, and
18 b. the rate is unreasonably low and use of the rate by
19 the insurer has tended or, if continued, will tend to
20 create a monopoly in the market.
21 3. Unfair discrimination may be determined to exist if, after
22 allowing for practical limitations, price differentials fail to
23 reflect equitably the differences in expected losses and expenses.
24 A rate may shall not be determined to be unfairly discriminatory

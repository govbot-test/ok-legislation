14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 36 O.S. 2021, Section 985, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 985. Ratemaking Standards. A. A rate may not be
18 excessive, inadequate or unfairly discriminatory.
19 1. No rate in a competitive market may be determined to be
20 excessive. A rate in a noncompetitive market may be determined to
21 be excessive if it, based on actuarial review, the rate is likely to
22 produce a profit that is unreasonably high for the insurance
23 provided. The Insurance Commissioner shall send an objection to the
24 insurer and shall give the insurer the opportunity to respond and
Req. No. 3839 Page 1
1 prov ide additional supporting information prior to making a final
2 determination that the rate is excessive.
3 2. A rate may not be determined to be inadequate unless:
4 a. the rate is clearly insufficient to sustain projected
5 losses, expenses and special assessments, and
6 b. the rate is unreasonably low and use of the rate by
7 the insurer has tended or, if continued, will tend to
8 create a monopoly in the market.
9 3. Unfair discrimination may be determined to exist if, after
10 allowing for practical limitations, price differentials fail to
11 reflect equitably the differences in expected losses and expenses.
12 A rate may not be determined to be unfairly discriminatory because
13 different premiums result for policyholders with like loss exposures
14 but different expense levels, or like expenses but different loss
15 exposures, or if it averaged broadly among persons insured within a
16 group, franchise or blanket policy or a mass-marketed plan. No rate
17 in a competitive market shall be considered unfairly discriminatory
18 unless it classifies risk on the basis of race, color, creed, or
19 national origin, or religion.
20 B. In determining whether rates in a noncompetitive market are
21 excessive, inadequate, or unfairly discriminatory, due consideration
22 may be given to:
23 1. Past and prospective loss experience within and outside this
24 state, in accordance with accepted actuarial principles;
Req. No. 3839 Page 2
1 2. Conflagration and catastrophe hazards;
2 3. A reasonable margin for underwriting profit and
3 contingencies;
4 4. Loadings for leveling premium rates over time for dividends,
5 savings or unabsorbed premium deposits allowed or returned by
6 insurers to their policyholders, members or subscribers;
7 5. Past and prospective expenses both countrywide and those
8 specially applicable to this state; and
9 6. Provisions for special assessments; and to all other
10 relevant factors including judgment within and outside this state.
11 C. Risks may be grouped by classifications for the
12 establishment of rates and minimum premiums. Classification rates
13 may be modified to produce rates for individual risks in accordance
14 with rating plans which establish standards for measuring variations
15 in hazards or expense provisions, or both. Such standards may
16 measure any differences among risks that can be demonstrated to have
17 a probable effect upon losses or expenses. No risk classification,
18 however, may be based on race, color, creed, national origin, or the
19 religion of the insured.
20 D. The expense provisions included in the rates for use by an
21 insurer or group of insurers may differ from those of any other
22 insurer or group of insurers to reflect the requirements of the
23 operating methods of the insurer or group of insurers.
24
Req. No. 3839 Page 3
1 E. The rates may contain provision for contingencies and an
2 allowance permitting a reasonable profit. In determining the
3 reasonableness of the profit, consideration shall be given to the
4 investment income attributable to the line of insurance.
5 F. Risks may be classified in any way except that no risk may
6 be classified on the basis of race, color, creed, or national
7 origin, or religion of the insured.
8 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 36 O.S. 2021, Section 987, is
9 amended to read as follows:
10 Section 987. Rate Filings. A. In a competitive market, every
11 insurer shall file with the Insurance Commissioner all rates and,
12 supplementary rate information to be used in this state no later
13 than thirty (30) days after the effective date; provided, that the
14 rates and supplementary rate information need not be filed for
15 commercial risks, which by general custom are not written according
16 to manual rules or rating plans.
17 B. In a noncompetitive market, every insurer shall file with
18 the Commissioner all rates, supplementary rate information and
19 supporting information at least thirty (30) days before the proposed
20 effective date. The Commissioner may give written notice, within
21 thirty (30) days of receipt of the filing, that the Commissioner
22 needs additional time, not to exceed thirty (30) days from the date
23 of the notice to consider the filing. Upon written application of
24 the insurer, the Commissioner may authorize rates to be effective
Req. No. 3839 Page 4
1 befo re the expiration of the waiting period or an extension thereof.
2 A filing shall be deemed to meet the requirements of the Property
3 and Casualty Competitive Loss Cost Rating Act and to become
4 effective unless disapproved pursuant to this title by the
5 Commissioner before the expiration of the waiting period or an
6 extension thereof.
7 In a noncompetitive market, the filing shall be deemed in
8 compliance with the filing provision of this section unless the
9 Commissioner informs the insurer within ten (10) days after receipt
10 of the filings as to what supplementary rate information or
11 supporting information is required to complete the filing.
12 C. Every authorized insurer shall file with the Commissioner,
13 except as to rates for those lines of insurance exempted from the
14 provisions of the Property and Casualty Competitive Loss Cost Rating
15 Act by the Commissioner under subsections E and F of this section
16 and except for those risks designated as special risks under Section
17 997 of this title, all rates, supplementary rate information and any
18 changes and amendments which it proposes to use. An insurer may
19 file its rates by either filing its final rates or by filing a
20 multiplier and, if applicable, an expense constant adjustment to be
21 applied to prospective loss costs that have been filed by an
22 advisory organization as permitted by this title. Such loss cost
23 multiplier filing and expense constant filings made by insurers
24
Req. No. 3839 Page 5
1 shal l remain in effect until amended or withdrawn by the insurer.
2 Every filing shall state the effective date.
3 D. Under rules as may be adopted, the Commissioner may, by
4 written order, suspend or modify the requirement of filing as to any
5 kind of insurance, subdivision or combination thereof, or as to
6 classes of risks.
7 E. Notwithstanding any other provision of the Property and
8 Casualty Competitive Loss Cost Rating Act, upon the written consent
9 of the insured in a separate written document, a rate in excess of
10 that determined in accordance with the other provisions of the
11 Property and Casualty Competitive Loss Cost Rating Act may be used
12 on a specific risk.
13 F. A filing and any supporting information required to be filed
14 shall be open to public inspection once the filing becomes effective
15 except information marked confidential, trade secret, or proprietary
16 by the insurer or filer and except the filings of an advisory
17 organization which shall be open to public inspection upon the
18 received date of the rate, loss cost, or manual rule change. The
19 insurer or filer shall have the burden of asserting to the
20 Commissioner that a filing and supporting information are
21 confidential, upon the request of the Commissioner. The
22 Commissioner may disapprove of the insurer’s request for
23 confidential filing status, and supporting information for the rates
24 and for the risks that are to be written in this state, and any
Req. No. 3839 Page 6
1 addi tional information or documentation requested by the
2 Commissioner specific to this state. All rates, supplementary
3 information, and supporting information shall be filed thirty (30)
4 days prior to the effective date of such rate. A filing shall be
5 deemed to meet the requirements of this act and may become
6 effective:
7 1. Upon the expiration of the waiting period unless objected to
8 or disapproved by the Commissioner; or
9 2. Prior to expiration of the waiting period, upon application
10 by the insurer that has been approved by the Commissioner.
11 B. In a competitive market, if the Commissioner determines,
12 after a notice or by agreement, that an insurer’s rates require
13 closer supervision, the insurer shall file with the Commissioner all
14 rates, supplementary information, and supporting information
15 prescribed by the Commissioner at least sixty (60) days prior to the
16 effective date of such rates. The insurer may request a hearing
17 before the Commissioner or an independent hearing examiner to
18 determine the reasonableness of the Commissioner’s action by written
19 request upon the Commissioner within thirty (30) days of the notice
20 of the Commissioner requiring closer supervision of such insurer.
21 C. In a noncompetitive market, every insurer shall file with
22 the Commissioner all rates, supplementary information, and
23 supporting information for the rates and for the risks that are to
24 be written in this state, and any additional information or
Req. No. 3839 Page 7
1 docu mentation requested by the Commissioner specific to this state
2 for such market. All rates, supplementary information, and
3 supporting information shall be filed at least sixty (60) days prior
4 to the effective date of such rates. A filing shall be deemed to
5 meet the requirements of this act and may become effective:
6 1. Upon the expiration of the waiting period unless disapproved
7 by the Commissioner; or
8 2. Prior to expiration of the waiting period, upon application
9 by the insurer that has been approved by the Commissioner.
10 D. If a private passenger automobile, homeowner’s multi-peril,
11 or dwelling fire policy’s overall rate is increased pursuant to the
12 provisions of this section, the Commissioner shall publish notice of
13 such rate increase and overall percentage of such rate increase on
14 the Insurance Department’s website.
15 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 36 O.S. 2021, Section 989, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 989. Improper Rates; Disapproval; Hearing. A. Basis
18 for disapproval.
19 1. The Insurance Commissioner shall disapprove a rate in a
20 competitive market only if the Commissioner finds, pursuant to
21 subsection B of this section, that the rate is excessive,
22 inadequate, or unfairly discriminatory pursuant to Section 985 of
23 this title.
24
Req. No. 3839 Page 8
1 2. The Commissioner may disapprove a rate for use in a
2 noncompetitive market only if the Commissioner finds, pursuant to
3 subsection B of this section, that the rate is excessive, inadequate
4 or unfairly discriminatory under this subsection.
5 B. Procedures for disapproval.
6 1. Prior to the expiration of a waiting period or an extension
7 thereof, made pursuant to subsection B of Section 987 of this title,
8 the Commissioner may disapprove, by written order, rates filed
9 pursuant to subsection B of Section 987 of this title with notice
10 and opportunity for a hearing. The order shall specify in what
11 respects the filing fails to meet the requirements of this act the
12 Property and Casualty Competitive Loss Cost Rating Act. Any insurer
13 whose rates are disapproved pursuant to this section shall be given
14 a hearing upon written request made within thirty (30) days of
15 disapproval.
16 2. If, at any time after the rate goes into effect, the
17 Commissioner finds that a rate applicable to insurance sold in a
18 noncompetitive market does not comply with the standards set forth
19 in Section 985 of this title, the Commissioner may, after a hearing
20 held upon not less than twenty (20) days’ written notice, issue an
21 order pursuant to subsection C of this section, disapproving such
22 rate. The hearing notice shall be sent to every insurer and
23 advisory organization that adopted the rate and shall specify the
24 matters to be considered at the hearing. The disapproval order
Req. No. 3839 Page 9
1 shal l not affect any contract or policy made or issued prior to the
2 effective date set forth in the order.
3 3. If, at any time, the Commissioner finds that a rate
4 applicable to insurance sold in a competitive market is inadequate
5 or unfairly discriminatory under paragraph 2 or 3 of subsection A of
6 Section 985 of this title, the Commissioner may issue an order
7 pursuant to subsection C of this section disapproving the rate. The
8 order shall not affect any contract or policy made or issued prior
9 to the effective date set forth in the order.
10 C. Order of disapproval.
11 If the Commissioner disapproves a rate pursuant to subsection B
12 of this section, the Commissioner shall issue an order within thirty
13 (30) days of the close of the hearing specifying in what respects
14 the rate fails to meet the requirements of this act the Property and
15 Casualty Competitive Loss Cost Rating Act. The order shall state an
16 effective date no sooner than thirty (30) business days after the
17 date of the order when the use of the rate shall be discontinued.
18 This order shall not affect any policy made before the effective
19 date of the order.
20 D. Appeal of orders and establishment of reserves.
21 If an order of disapproval is appealed pursuant to Section 990
22 of this title, the insurer may implement the disapproved rate upon
23 notification to the court, in which case any excess of the
24 disapproved rate over a rate previously in effect shall be placed in
Req. No. 3839 Page 10
1 a re serve established by the insurer. The court shall have control
2 over the disbursement of funds from such reserve. The funds shall
3 be distributed as determined by the court in its final order except
4 that de minimus minimis refunds to policyholders shall not be
5 required.
6 E. All determinations made by the Commissioner under this
7 section shall be on the basis of findings of fact and conclusions of
8 law.
9 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective July 1, 2027.

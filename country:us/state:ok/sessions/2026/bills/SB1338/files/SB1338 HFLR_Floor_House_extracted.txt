12 An Act relating to the education initiatives;
defining terms; providing the Education Impact and
13 Innovation Revolving Fund shall be used for a
competitive grants pool to fund education initiatives
14 meant to further and improve the education of
Oklahoma students; directing the State Department of
15 Education to administer grant program; authoring the
State Board of Education to award one or more
16 competitive grants to qualifying entities to fund
education initiatives; requiring grants be awarded
17 based on approved rubric; listing required
information to be submitted in grant applications;
18 requiring identifying information be removed before
evaluation by Board; requiring use of rubric for
19 scoring and ranking of grant applications; directing
grant application scores be made public before Board
20 vote; requiring Board to vote to approve grant
application and amount to be awarded; creating the
21 Education Impact and Innovation Revolving Fund;
providing for codification; and providing an
22 effective date.

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 BE I T ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
2 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
3 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3-123.2 of Title 70, unless
4 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
5 A. As used in this section:
6 1. "Board" means the Oklahoma State Board of Education;
7 2. "Department" means the Oklahoma State Department of
8 Education; and
9 3. "Education initiative" means a structured program aimed at
10 improving learning outcomes, solving specific educational
11 challenges, or designed to promote student achievement; and
12 4. "Qualifying entity" means any for-profit or nonprofit
13 corporation, partnership, limited liability company, association, or
14 any other legal or commercial entity.
15 B. The Education Impact and Innovation Revolving Fund created
16 pursuant to Section 2 of this act shall be used for a competitive
17 grants pool to fund education initiatives meant to further and
18 improve the education of Oklahoma students. The Department shall
19 administer the competitive grant program. Contingent upon the
20 availability of funds in the Education Impact and Innovation
21 Revolving Fund created pursuant to Section 2 of this act, the Board
22 shall award one or more competitive grants to qualifying entities to
23 fund education initiatives to be implemented within this state.
24
SB1338 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 C. 1. Grants shall be awarded to qualifying entities based on
2 a rubric designed by the Department and approved by the Board.
3 2. Grant applications shall include, but are not limited to:
4 a. a description of the education initiative,
5 b. what educational need the education initiative will
6 serve,
7 c. the dollar amount requested,
8 d. the population to be served,
9 e. what metrics will be used to evaluate the efficacy of
10 the education initiative, and
11 f. if the education initiative has already been
12 implemented in any school districts within this state,
13 the data from that education initiative and the
14 efficacy of the education initiative.
15 3. Before any grant application is evaluated by the Board, all
16 identifying information shall be removed from the grant application.
17 The Board shall score grant applications using the approved rubric
18 and rank the applications according to the evaluation score. Final
19 scores of the grant application shall be made public prior to any
20 vote by the Board. The Board shall vote to approve a grant
21 application and the amount to be awarded.
22 D. The Board shall promulgate rules as necessary to administer
23 the competitive grant program created in this section.
24
SB1338 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3-123.3 of Title 70, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving fund
5 for the State Department of Education to be designated the
6 "Education Impact and Innovation Revolving Fund". The fund shall be
7 a continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year limitations, and shall
8 consist of all monies received by the State Department of Education
9 from appropriations, gifts, grants, donations, and bequests. All
10 monies accruing to the credit of said fund are hereby appropriated
11 and may be budgeted and expended by the State Department of
12 Education for the purpose of implementing the provisions of Section
13 1 of this act. Expenditures from said fund shall be made upon
14 warrants issued by the State Treasurer against claims filed as
15 prescribed by law with the Director of the Office of Management and
16 Enterprise Services for approval and payment.
17 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

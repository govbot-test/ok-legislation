8 An Act relating to probate procedure; amending 58
O.S. 2021, Section 246, as amended by Section 1,
9 Chapter 114, O.S.L. 2025 (58 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section
246), which relates to notice for petition for
10 summary administration; removing the publication
requirement for certain notices and allowing
11 publication of a combined notice; providing
publication timeline for combined notice; and
12 providing an effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 58 O.S. 2021, Section 246, as
17 amended by Section 1, Chapter 114, O.S.L. 2025 (58 O.S. Supp. 2025,
18 Section 246), is amended to read as follows:
19 Section 246. A. Upon the filing of the petition and combined
20 notice, the court shall dispense with the regular estate proceedings
21 prescribed by law and the court shall order notice to creditors and
22 issue an order granting final hearing upon the petition for
23 admission of the will, if any, to probate, the petition for summary
24 administration, the final accounting, and the petition for
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB4339 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 dete rmination of heirship, distribution and discharge. However,
2 nothing in this section shall affect the lien upon any property for
3 any estate or transfer tax which may be due upon the estate of the
4 decedent.
5 B. Notice to creditors and notice of hearing upon the petition
6 for summary administration and the final accounting, determination
7 of heirship, and distribution and discharge shall be combined into
8 one notice, referred to as a "combined notice". Combined notice
9 shall be filed within five (5) days from the granting of the order
10 admitting the petition and order for combined notice. The combined
11 notice shall set forth the following:
12 1. The name, address, and date of death of the decedent;
13 2. The name and address of the petitioner;
14 3. Whether a will exists;
15 4. The name and address of the personal representative, if
16 specified;
17 5. The name and address of the heirs or devisees;
18 6. The probable value of the estate of the decedent as set
19 forth in the petition;
20 7. The date, time and place of the final hearing;
21 8. That the person receiving the notice or any interested party
22 may file objections to the petition at any time before the final
23 hearing and send a copy to the petitioner or that person will be
24 deemed to have waived any objections to the petition;
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB4339 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 9. That if an objection is filed before the hearing, the court
2 will determine at the hearing whether the will attached to the
3 petition shall be admitted to probate, whether summary proceedings
4 are appropriate and, if so, whether the estate will be distributed
5 and to whom the estate will be distributed; and
6 10. The claim of any creditor will be barred unless the claim
7 is presented to the personal representative no more than thirty (30)
8 days following the granting of the order admitting the petition and
9 combined notice.
10 C. Within ten (10) days of the granting of the order admitting
11 the petition and combined notice, notice of the petition, notice to
12 creditors, and notice of final accounting, determination of
13 heirship, distribution and discharge The combined notice shall be
14 published once each week for two (2) consecutive weeks in a
15 newspaper that is authorized by law to publish legal notices and
16 that is published in the county where the petition is filed, and the
17 first publication of the combined notice shall appear on, or before,
18 the tenth day after the filing of the combined notice. If no
19 newspaper authorized by law to publish legal notices is published in
20 the county, the notice shall be posted in three public places in the
21 county, one of which shall be the county courthouse. Within ten
22 (10) days of the granting of the order admitting the petition and
23 combined notice, the combined notice shall be mailed to creditors of
24 the decedent as provided in Sections 331 and 331.1 of this title.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB4339 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 With in ten (10) days of the granting of the order admitting the
2 petition and combined notice, the combined notice shall be mailed to
3 all persons interested in the estate of the decedent at their
4 respective last-known addresses.
5 D. The matter shall be set for final hearing not less than
6 forty-five (45) days following the granting of the order admitting
7 the petition and combined notice.
8 E. If there is a defect in notice or in the form of the
9 petition or if objections are filed, or for other good cause shown,
10 the hearing may be postponed to a date certain.
11 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

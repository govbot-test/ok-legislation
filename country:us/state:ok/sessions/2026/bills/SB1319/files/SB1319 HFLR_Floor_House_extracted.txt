1 A. The Corporation Commission shall establish a process to
2 alleviate new and ongoing serious environmental emergencies through
3 which a homeowner whose residence has become contaminated by a
4 substance within the Corporation Commission’s jurisdiction,
5 including, but not limited to, brine or oil, may apply for
6 remediation assistance.
7 B. 1. To request the use of funds from the Remediation
8 Assistance Revolving Fund created in Section 2 of this act, the
9 homeowner shall file an application to be considered by the
10 Corporation Commission. The Corporation Commission may hear the
11 application with or without notice.
12 2. After considering the evidence presented, the Corporation
13 Commission shall rule on the application and determine the amount of
14 remediation assistance necessary to remediate and abate the
15 environmental emergency.
16 3. The amount of remediation assistance may include the cost to
17 stop the substance from continuing the contamination, the cost of
18 removing and cleaning up the substance, and the cost of purchasing
19 the residence and property at fair market value. The Remediation
20 Assistance Revolving Fund shall not be used to pay for any damages.
21 4. Prior to any expenditure from the Remediation Assistance
22 Revolving Fund, the Corporation Commission shall comply with the
23 requirements set forth in Section 85.41A of Title 74 of the Oklahoma
24 Statutes.

7 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
8 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 180.15 of Title 17, unless there
9 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
10 There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving fund
11 for the Corporation Commission to be designated the “Remediation
12 Assistance Revolving Fund”. The fund shall be a continuing fund,
13 not subject to fiscal year limitations, and shall consist of all
14 monies received by the Corporation Commission from any public or
15 private donations, contributions, and gifts received for the benefit
16 of the fund and any amounts appropriated by the Legislature
17 designated for deposit in the fund. All monies accruing to the
18 credit of the fund are hereby appropriated and may be budgeted and
19 expended by the Corporation Commission or the Department of
20 Environmental Quality for the purpose of providing remediation
21 assistance as provided for in Section 1 of this act. Expenditures
22 from the fund shall be made upon warrants issued by the State
23 Treasurer against claims filed as prescribed by law with the

8 An Act relating to technology; providing definitions;
clarifying applicability of act; establishing
9 prohibited uses of artificial intelligence system
(AI) by state agencies; establishing allowed uses of
10 AI and applicable restrictions; directing state
agencies to take action and report compliance to the
11 Office of Management and Enterprise Services (OMES);
requiring future AI systems comply with act;
12 directing OMES to provide report on AI systems used
by state agencies; providing for codification; and
13 providing an effective date.

16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 200 of Title 75A, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 A. As used in this act:
21 1. "Artificial intelligence system" or "AI" means a machine-
22 based system that, for explicit or implicit objectives, infers from
23 the input it receives how to generate outputs, such as predictions,
24 content, recommendations, or decisions, that can influence a

1 phys ical or virtual environment, with varying levels of autonomy and
2 adaptiveness after deployment. The term "artificial intelligence
3 system" or "AI" does not include robotic process automation or other
4 systems whose behavior is defined solely by human-defined rules to
5 automatically execute operations;
6 2. "Deepfake" means a video of a person in which his or her
7 face or body has been digitally altered so that the person appears
8 to be someone else, the person appears to be saying something that
9 he or she has never said, or the person appears to be doing
10 something that he or she has never done. A deepfake is typically
11 used maliciously or to spread false information;
12 3. "Generative artificial intelligence system" or "GenAI
13 system" means an artificial intelligence system that is designed to
14 generate synthetic content, including text, images, video, audio, or
15 other media, in response to prompts or other inputs. A generative
16 artificial intelligence system does not include software that
17 performs basic formatting, grammar correction, translation, or other
18 assistive editing functions that do not materially generate new
19 substantive content;
20 4. "High-risk AI decision" means an automated determination,
21 classification, scoring, or recommendation generated in whole or in
22 part by an artificial intelligence system that:
23 a. is used as the primary basis for a final agency action
24 affecting an individual's legal rights, eligibility

1 for government benefits or services, imposition of
2 civil penalties, or legal obligations,
3 b. results in a substantial and non de minimis impact,
4 and
5 c. is not subject to meaningful human review prior to
6 implementation, and
7 5. "State agency" means any department, commission, board,
8 institution, bureau, office, law enforcement agency, or other
9 entity, by whatever name called, including the legislative and
10 judicial branches of state government, established in the state
11 constitution, statutes, session laws, or executive orders.
12 B. This act shall apply to all computer systems operated by any
13 state agency, except for systems used in research by state-funded
14 institutions of higher learning. Also excepted are installed
15 consumer systems in common personal use, including, but not limited
16 to, facial recognition used to unlock a smartphone.
17 C. The following uses of AI by state agencies shall be
18 prohibited:
19 1. Cognitive behavioral manipulation of individual persons or
20 specific groups of persons;
21 2. Classifying persons based on behavior, socioeconomic status,
22 or personal characteristics in a manner that results in unlawful
23 discrimination prohibited by state or federal law;
24

1 3. Real-time and remote biometric identification systems used
2 for surveillance in public spaces, such as facial recognition,
3 except when such use is conducted pursuant to a valid warrant or
4 court order, or in exigent circumstances involving a specific and
5 articulable imminent threat to life or serious bodily injury,
6 including, but not limited to, active shooter incidents or credible
7 terrorist threats; provided, that any use under exigent
8 circumstances shall be strictly limited in scope and duration to
9 address the emergency and shall be subject to post-incident
10 documentation and supervisory review by the agency; and
11 4. Deepfakes when used for any deceptive or malicious purpose.
12 D. Use of AI by state agencies shall be allowed under the
13 following circumstances and with the following restrictions:
14 1. No high-risk AI decision shall take effect unless reviewed
15 and approved by a human employee or officer of the state agency who
16 has decision-making authority and is trained in and aware of the
17 limitations of the AI system;
18 2. Any material produced by a generative artificial
19 intelligence system that has not been reviewed, and possibly edited
20 by a human in an appropriate responsible position, must be
21 accompanied by a disclosure that the content was generated by AI;
22 and
23 3. In all other circumstances in which a human user is
24 interacting with an AI system, either directly or indirectly, the
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3545 Page 4

1 user must be informed that he or she is interacting with an AI
2 system.
3 E. All state agencies shall take the following actions no later
4 than nine (9) months after the effective date of this act and report
5 their compliance with the Office of Management of Enterprise
6 Services (OMES):
7 1. Review the use of AI in their computer systems to verify
8 that they comply with the provisions of this act. Any AI system
9 that is prohibited shall be removed; and
10 2. Modify all procedures relative to any use of AI that are
11 inconsistent with this act so that these procedures are consistent
12 with the requirements of this act.
13 F. Any AI systems newly deployed by a state agency after the
14 effective date of this act shall comply with the provisions of this
15 act. All newly implemented procedures relative to any use of AI
16 that are implemented by a state agency after the effective date of
17 this act shall be consistent with the requirements of this act.
18 G. On December 1, 2026, and every year thereafter, OMES shall
19 provide to the Governor, the Speaker of the Oklahoma House of
20 Representatives, and the President Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State
21 Senate a report summarizing AI systems identified by state agencies,
22 recording which of those systems were prohibited and removed in
23 compliance with this act, which systems are allowed according to
24 this act, and what procedures have been implemented to ensure that

1 the procurement and use of these systems will be in compliance with
2 this act. This report shall be updated annually to include new
3 systems that state agencies have purchased. All such reports shall
4 also be posted on the OMES website.
5 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 2nd Session of the 60th Legislature (2026)
4
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to crimes and punishments; amending
21 O.S. 2021, Sections 1767.1, as amended by Section
8 459, Chapter 486, O.S.L. 2025 and 1767.3 (21 O.S.
Supp. 2024, Section 1767.1), which relate to the
9 unlawful use of bombs or explosive devices and
related definitions; modifying elements of certain
10 crimes; updating certain defined term; and providing
an effective date.
11
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 21 O.S. 2021, Section 1767.1, as
16 amended by Section 459, Chapter 486, O.S.L. 2025 (21 O.S. Supp.
17 2024, Section 1767.1), is amended to read as follows:
18 Section 1767.1. A. Any person who shall willfully or
19 maliciously commit any of the following acts shall be deemed guilty
20 of a Class D1 felony offense:
21 1. Place or conspire to place in, upon, under, against or near
22 to any building, car, truck, aircraft, motor or other vehicle,
23 vessel, railroad, railway car, or locomotive or structure, any
24 explosive or incendiary device with unlawful intent to destroy,
Req. No. 14700 Page 1
1 thro w down, or injure, in whole or in part, such property, or
2 conspire, aid, counsel or procure the destruction of any building,
3 public or private, or any car, truck, aircraft, motor or other
4 vehicle, vessel, railroad, railway car, or locomotive or structure;
5 or
6 2. Place or conspire to place in, upon, under, against or near
7 to any building, car, truck, aircraft, motor or other vehicle,
8 vessel, railroad, railway car, or locomotive or structure, any
9 explosive or incendiary device with intent to destroy, throw down,
10 or injure in whole or in part, under circumstances that, if such
11 intent were accomplished, human life or safety would be endangered
12 thereby; or
13 3. By the explosion of any explosive or the igniting of any
14 incendiary device destroy, throw down, or injure any property of
15 another person, or cause injury to another person; or
16 4. Manufacture, sell, transport, or possess any explosive, the
17 component parts of an explosive, an incendiary device, or simulated
18 bomb with knowledge or intent that it or they will be used to
19 unlawfully kill, injure or intimidate any person, or unlawfully
20 damage any real or personal property; or
21 5. Place or conspire to place in, upon, under, against or near
22 to any building, car, truck, aircraft, motor or other vehicle,
23 vessel, railroad, railway car, or locomotive or structure, any foul,
24 poisonous, offensive or injurious substance or compound, explosive,
Req. No. 14700 Page 2
1 ince ndiary device, or simulated bomb with intent to wrongfully
2 injure, molest or coerce another person or to injure or damage the
3 property of another person; or
4 6. Injure, damage or attempt to damage by an explosive or
5 incendiary device any person, persons, or property, whether real or
6 personal; or
7 7. Make any threat or convey information known to be false,
8 concerning an attempt or alleged attempt to kill, injure or
9 intimidate any person or unlawfully damage any real or personal
10 property by means of an explosive, incendiary device, or simulated
11 bomb; or
12 8. Manufacture, sell, deliver, mail or send an explosive,
13 incendiary device, or simulated bomb to another person; or
14 9. While committing or attempting to commit any felony,
15 possess, display, or threaten to use any explosive, incendiary
16 device, or simulated bomb.
17 B. Nothing contained herein shall be construed to apply to, or
18 repeal any laws pertaining to, the acts of mischief of juveniles
19 involving no injurious firecrackers or devices commonly called
20 "stink bombs".
21 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 21 O.S. 2021, Section 1767.3, is
22 amended to read as follows:
23 Section 1767.3. As used in Section 1767.1 of this title:
24
Req. No. 14700 Page 3
1 1. "Explosive" or "explosives" means any chemical compound,
2 mixture or device, the primary or common purpose of which is to
3 function by explosion or which, although not its primary or common
4 purpose, has been modified, manipulated, altered, enhanced, or
5 otherwise caused to function by explosion (that is, with substantial
6 instantaneous release of gas, heat, debris, or concussive pressure
7 or force, or any combination of such actions), unless such compound,
8 mixture or device is otherwise specifically classified by the United
9 States Department of Transportation. The term "explosive" or
10 "explosives" shall include, but not be limited to, gunpowder,
11 dynamite, any bomb, all materials as defined in paragraphs 1 and 2
12 of Section 121.1 of Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes, and all
13 material which is classified as explosives by the United States
14 Department of Transportation;
15 2. "Person" means any individual or individuals, firm,
16 copartnership, corporation, company, association, joint stock
17 association, and includes any trustee, receiver, assignee or
18 personal representative thereof;
19 3. "Incendiary device" means any chemical compound, mixture or
20 device, the primary purpose of which is to ignite on impact or as a
21 result of chemical reaction such as a "Molotov cocktail" or
22 "firebomb" which is ignited on impact, causing a mechanical reaction
23 of the container's breaking of the container and permitting the
24
Req. No. 14700 Page 4
1 infl ammable matter to spread or splatter, and is ignited from the
2 burning wick or hypergolic reaction of chemicals;
3 4. "Component parts" means separate parts which if assembled
4 would form an explosive device. Component parts of an "incendiary
5 device" shall consist of an inflammable material, a breakable
6 container and a source of ignition; and
7 5. "Simulated bomb" means any device or object that by its
8 design, construction, content, or characteristics appears to be, or
9 to contain, an incendiary device, explosive, or explosives, as
10 defined in this section, but is, in fact, an inoperative facsimile
11 or imitation of such a device or explosive.
12 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

7 An Act relating to civil remedies; creating the
Uniform Civil Remedies for Unauthorized Disclosure of
8 Intimate Images Act; providing short title; defining
terms; authorizing cause of action for unauthorized
9 disclosure of certain material; providing exceptions
to liability for certain disclosures; providing for
10 inapplicability of certain exceptions under certain
circumstances; authorizing exclusion or redaction of
11 identifying information in certain pleadings and
documents; authorizing recovery of damages and other
12 relief; establishing statute of limitations for
certain actions; construing provisions; providing for
13 codification; and providing an effective date.

and insert:
17
18
19 "An Act relating to civil remedies; creating the
Uniform Civil Remedies for Unauthorized Disclosure of
20 Intimate Images Act; defining terms; authorizing
cause of action for the intentional disclosure or
21 threatened disclosure of an intimate image without
the depicted individual's consent; limiting use of
22 prior consent; providing for a reasonable expectation
of privacy under certain circumstances; defining
23 terms; establishing the burden of proof for the
disclosing party to not be found liable under this
24 act; excluding from liability defendants who are
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2182 Page 1
1 parents, legal guardians, or individuals with legal
custody of a child unless plaintiff meets certain
2 burden of proof; prohibiting a finding of public
concern or public interest solely based on the
3 depicted individual being a public figure; excluding
certain date providers from being liable unless the
4 provider had actual knowledge the specific content
constitutes an intimate image disclosed without
5 consent and failed to remove or disable access to the
content; authorizing the exclusion or redaction of
6 identifying characteristics of the plaintiff in court
proceedings; providing for recovery of damages by
7 prevailing plaintiff; authorizing the court to award
a prevailing plaintiff reasonable attorney fees and
8 costs and other additional relief including
injunctive relief; providing a statute of limitations
9 of four years for actions brought under this act;
applying tolling statutes; tolling actions for
10 depicted individuals who are minors on the date of
disclosure or threatened disclosure; construing
11 provisions; providing for codification; and providing
an effective date.

14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1121 of Title 12, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Uniform Civil
19 Remedies for Unauthorized Disclosure of Intimate Images Act”.
20 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1122 of Title 12, unless there
22 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 As used in this act:
24
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2182 Page 2
1 1. “Consent” means affirmative, conscious, and voluntary
2 authorization by an individual with legal capacity to give
3 authorization;
4 2. “Depicted individual” means an individual whose body is
5 shown in whole or in part in an intimate image;
6 3. “Disclose” or “disclosure” means transfer, publication, or
7 distribution to another person;
8 4. “Identifiable” means recognizable by a person other than the
9 depicted individual:
10 a. from an intimate image itself, or
11 b. from an intimate image and identifying characteristic
12 displayed in connection with the intimate image;
13 5. “Identifying characteristic” means information that may be
14 used to identify a depicted individual;
15 6. “Individual” means a human being;
16 7. “Intimate image” means a photograph, film, video recording,
17 or other similar medium that shows:
18 a. the uncovered genitals, pubic area, anus, or female
19 post-pubescent nipple of a depicted individual, or
20 b. a depicted individual engaging in or being subjected
21 to sexual conduct;
22 8. “Person” means an individual, estate, business or nonprofit
23 entity, public corporation, government or governmental subdivision,
24 agency, or instrumentality, or other legal entity; and
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2182 Page 3
1 9. “Sexual conduct” includes:
2 a. masturbation,
3 b. genital, anal, or oral sexual intercourse,
4 c. sexual penetration of, or with, an object,
5 d. bestiality, or
6 e. the transfer of semen onto a depicted individual.
7 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
8 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1123 of Title 12, unless there
9 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
10 A. As used in this section:
11 1. “Harm” includes physical harm, economic harm, and emotional
12 distress whether or not accompanied by physical or economic harm;
13 and
14 2. “Private” means:
15 a. created or obtained under circumstances in which a
16 depicted individual had a reasonable expectation of
17 privacy, or
18 b. made accessible through theft, bribery, extortion,
19 fraud, false pretenses, voyeurism, or exceeding
20 authorized access to an account, message, file,
21 device, resource, or property.
22 B. Except as otherwise provided in Section 4 of this act, a
23 depicted individual who is identifiable and who suffers harm from a
24 person’s intentional disclosure or threatened disclosure of an
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2182 Page 4
1 inti mate image that was private without the depicted individual’s
2 consent shall have a cause of action against the person if the
3 person knew that the content was of the type prohibited.
4 C. Consent to creation of the image or previous consensual
5 disclosure of the image by a depicted individual shall not establish
6 by itself that the individual consented to the disclosure of the
7 intimate image that is the subject of an action under this act or
8 that the individual lacked a reasonable expectation of privacy.
9 D. A depicted individual who does not consent to the sexual
10 conduct or uncovering of the part of the body depicted in an
11 intimate image of the individual shall retain a reasonable
12 expectation of privacy even if the image was created when the
13 individual was in a public place.
14 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1124 of Title 12, unless there
16 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. As used in this section:
18 1. “Child” means an unemancipated individual who is less than
19 eighteen (18) years of age; and
20 2. “Parent” means an individual recognized as a parent under
21 the laws of this state.
22 B. A person shall not be liable under this act if the person
23 proves that disclosure of, or a threat to disclose, an intimate
24 image was:
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2182 Page 5
1 1. Made in good faith:
2 a. to law enforcement,
3 b. in a legal proceeding, or
4 c. during medical education or treatment;
5 2. Made in good faith in the reporting or investigation of:
6 a. unlawful conduct, or
7 b. unsolicited and unwelcome conduct;
8 3. Related to a matter of public concern or public interest; or
9 4. Reasonably intended to assist the depicted individual.
10 C. Subject to subsection D of this section, a defendant who is
11 a parent, legal guardian, or individual with legal custody of a
12 child shall not be liable under this act for a disclosure or
13 threatened disclosure of an intimate image of the child.
14 D. If a defendant asserts an exception to liability under
15 subsection C of this section, the exception shall not apply if the
16 plaintiff proves the disclosure was:
17 1. Prohibited by law; or
18 2. Made for the purpose of sexual arousal, sexual
19 gratification, humiliation, degradation, or monetary or commercial
20 gain.
21 E. Disclosure of, or a threat to disclose, an intimate image
22 shall not be a matter of public concern or public interest solely
23 because the depicted individual is a public figure.
24
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2182 Page 6
1 F. A provider of cloud computing, data storage, web hosting, or
2 other infrastructure services shall not be liable under this act
3 solely by reason of providing such services to a person who
4 discloses or threatens to disclose an intimate image, provided that
5 the provider:
6 1. Does not have actual knowledge that the specific content
7 constitutes an intimate image disclosed without consent under this
8 act; or
9 2. Upon obtaining actual knowledge of such content, acts
10 expeditiously to remove or disable access to the content.
11 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
12 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1125 of Title 12, unless there
13 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
14 In a cause of action filed pursuant to this act:
15 1. The court may exclude or redact from all pleadings and
16 documents filed in the action other identifying characteristics of
17 the plaintiff;
18 2. A plaintiff to whom paragraph 1 of this section applies
19 shall file with the court and serve on the defendant a confidential
20 information form that includes the excluded or redacted plaintiff’s
21 name and other identifying characteristics; and
22 3. The court may make further orders as necessary to protect
23 the identity and privacy of a plaintiff.

1 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1126 of Title 12, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. In a cause of action filed pursuant to this act, a
5 prevailing plaintiff may recover:
6 1. The greater of:
7 a. economic and noneconomic damages proximately caused by
8 the defendant’s disclosure or threatened disclosure,
9 including damages for emotional distress whether or
10 not accompanied by other damages, or
11 b. statutory damages not to exceed Ten Thousand Dollars
12 ($10,000.00) against each defendant found liable under
13 this act for all disclosures and threatened
14 disclosures by the defendant of which the plaintiff
15 knew or reasonably should have known when filing the
16 action or which became known during the pendency of
17 the action. In determining the amount of statutory
18 damages under this subparagraph, consideration shall
19 be given to the age of the parties at the time of the
20 disclosure or threatened disclosure, the number of
21 disclosures or threatened disclosures made by the
22 defendant, the breadth of distribution of the image by
23 the defendant, and other exacerbating or mitigating
24 factors;
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2182 Page 8
1 2. An amount equal to any monetary gain made by the defendant
2 from disclosure of the intimate image; and
3 3. Punitive damages as allowed under law of this state other
4 than this act.
5 B. In a cause of action filed pursuant to this act, the court
6 may award a prevailing plaintiff:
7 1. Reasonable attorney fees and costs; and
8 2. Additional relief, including injunctive relief.
9 C. This act shall not affect any other right or remedy
10 available under the laws of this state.
11 SECTION 7. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
12 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1127 of Title 12, unless there
13 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
14 A. An action authorized by subsection B of Section 3 of this
15 act for:
16 1. An unauthorized disclosure may not be brought later than
17 four (4) years from the date the disclosure was discovered or should
18 have been discovered with the exercise of reasonable diligence; and
19 2. A threat to disclose may not be brought later than four (4)
20 years from the date of the threat to disclose was made.
21 B. Except as otherwise provided in subsection C of this
22 section, this section shall be subject to the tolling statutes of
23 this state.
24
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 2182 Page 9
1 C. In an action under subsection B of Section 3 of this act by
2 a depicted individual who was a minor on the date of the disclosure
3 or threat to disclose, the time specified in subsection A of this
4 section shall not begin to run until the depicted individual attains
5 the age of majority.
6 SECTION 8. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1128 of Title 12, unless there
8 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 A. This act shall be construed to be consistent with the
10 Communications Decency Act of 1996, 47 U.S.C., Section 230.
11 B. This act may not be construed to alter the laws of this
12 state on sovereign immunity.
13 SECTION 9. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1129 of Title 12, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 In applying and construing this uniform act, consideration shall
17 be given to the need to promote uniformity of the law with respect
18 to its subject matter among states that enact it.
19 SECTION 10. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026."

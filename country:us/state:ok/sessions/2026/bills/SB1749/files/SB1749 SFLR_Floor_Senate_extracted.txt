12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY Section 1, Chapter 106, O.S.L.
14 2024, as amended by Section 1, Chapter 342, O.S.L. 2025 (74 O.S.
15 Supp. 2025, Section 325.20), is amended to read as follows:
16 Section 325.20. A. All mobile food preparation vehicles
17 operating within this state prior to July 1, 2024, that are capable
18 of producing smoke or grease-laden vapors shall be in compliance
19 with the most current adopted fire code. With the exception of an
20 automatic fire extinguishing system, such requirement shall apply to
21 vehicles including, but not limited to, vehicles utilizing electric
22 energy, liquefied petroleum gas, compressed natural gas, or a
23 combination thereof for cooking, provided that portable fire
24 extinguishers are installed and maintained in accordance with the

1 most current version of NFPA 10: Standard for Portable Fire
2 Extinguishers. A six-liter Class K-rated, portable fire
3 extinguisher is required for all cooking equipment involving solid
4 fuels, vegetable or animal oils and fats, or a combination thereof.
5 All portable fire extinguishers shall be in clear view and
6 immediately available for use.
7 B. All mobile food preparation vehicles utilizing, handling, or
8 transporting liquefied petroleum gas or any liquefied petroleum gas
9 system, equipment, container, apparatus, or appliance shall receive
10 and maintain a permit from, and be inspected annually by, the
11 Oklahoma Liquefied Petroleum Gas Administration or its employees, in
12 conjunction with the Oklahoma Liquefied Petroleum Gas Board. The
13 Board shall promulgate rules related to such permits, including
14 inspection requirements. The State Fire Marshal issuing shall issue
15 an annual operation permit decal to mobile food preparation
16 vehicles.
17 C. The provisions in subsection A of this section shall expire
18 on January 1, 2026, and all mobile food preparation vehicles shall
19 comply with the most current adopted fire codes set forth by this
20 state.
21 D. Mobile food preparation vehicles shall be under the
22 jurisdiction of the State Fire Marshal for fire and life safety code
23 compliance and shall be inspected annually by the State Fire Marshal
24 to ensure compliance with the latest version of the state-adopted

1 mini mum codes for fire and life safety as adopted by the Oklahoma
2 Uniform Building Code Commission. The State Fire Marshal shall
3 coordinate, at minimum, annual inspections conducted:
4 1. Within each of the four quadrants of this state; and
5 2. Twice within each county with a population exceeding four
6 hundred thousand (400,000) according to the latest Federal Decennial
7 Census.
8 E. Upon approval, the State Fire Marshal shall issue an
9 operational permit decal indicating that the vehicle meets the
10 minimum fire and life safety code requirements adopted at the time
11 of inspection.
12 F. Nothing in this section shall prevent:
13 1. The State Fire Marshal from enforcing the minimum fire code
14 requirements as allowed by law and revoking the operations of a
15 mobile food preparation vehicle for cause when such vehicle is
16 determined to be inherently dangerous to the health, safety, or
17 welfare of persons within this state;
18 2. A political subdivision from enforcing the event permits,
19 operational fees, land use regulations, zoning, or other ordinances
20 in relation to the operation of a mobile food preparation vehicle
21 within the boundaries of the political subdivision; or
22 3. The State Department of Health from licensing and regulating
23 mobile food preparation vehicles as provided by the provisions of

4 Section 9202. A. 1. There is hereby created until June 30,
5 2028 December 31, 2030, the Broadband Governing Board. The Board
6 shall oversee the Oklahoma Broadband Office. Effective June 30,
7 2028 December 31, 2030, the Board shall terminate. The Board shall
8 utilize the year prior to the termination date for the purpose of
9 ceasing the Board's affairs.
10 2. The Board shall receive regular reports from the Office's
11 Executive Director and the Executive Director's staff on the status
12 of the Statewide Broadband Plan, the State Broadband Grant Program,
13 other developed grant programs, and other projects the Office is
14 undertaking, during regularly scheduled meetings.
15 B. The Board shall consist of nine (9) members. Two members
16 shall be appointed by the Speaker of the Oklahoma House of
17 Representatives, two members shall be appointed by the President Pro
18 Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate, three members shall be
19 appointed by the Governor, one member shall be the Lieutenant
20 Governor, and one member shall be the State Treasurer. Except as
21 provided in subsection C of this section, appointees shall serve
22 until June 30, 2028 December 31, 2030. No individual with an
23 official affiliation, paid or unpaid, with any potential recipient
24

1 of a grant administered under the provisions of this act, shall be
2 appointed to or serve on the Board.
3 C. Each appointee shall serve at the pleasure of his or her
4 appointing authority and may be removed or replaced without cause.
5 D. 1. The Board shall hold an organizational meeting not later
6 than thirty (30) days from the effective date of this act. At such
7 meeting, the Board shall adopt qualifications and a salary range for
8 an Executive Director for the Oklahoma Broadband Office based on the
9 recommendations of the Human Capital Management Division of the
10 Office of Management and Enterprise Services, and provide for the
11 process of filling the position in a reasonable time in accordance
12 with the laws of this state.
13 2. The Board shall hold meetings as necessary at a place and
14 time to be fixed by the Board. The Board shall elect, at its first
15 meeting, one of its members to serve as chair and another of its
16 members to serve as vice-chair. At the first meeting in each
17 calendar year thereafter, the chair and vice-chair for the ensuing
18 year shall be elected. Special meetings may be called by the chair
19 or by five members of the Board by delivery of written notice to
20 each member of the Board. A majority of members serving on the
21 Board shall constitute a quorum of the Board.
22 E. If a Board member is unable to fulfill his or her term for
23 any reason, the appointing authority of such member may appoint a
24 replacement to complete the remainder of the term.

6 Section 9204. A. There is hereby created until June 30, 2028
7 December 31, 2030, the Oklahoma Broadband Office, to serve as the
8 state's sole administrator of the functions, powers, and duties
9 assigned to the Office in the Oklahoma Broadband Expansion Act or
10 under law. Effective June 30, 2028 December 31, 2030, the Office
11 shall terminate and all personnel positions shall be abolished. The
12 Office shall utilize the year prior to the termination date for the
13 purpose of ceasing the Office's affairs.
14 B. The Office shall receive administrative assistance from the
15 Office of Management and Enterprise Services (OMES), which shall
16 provide, through existing OMES resources, administrative assistance
17 upon request in writing or electronic correspondence from the
18 Office's Executive Director. OMES shall provide assistance in the
19 manner requested within thirty (30) days of determination by the
20 Agency that the request is able to be fulfilled. If for any reason
21 the request cannot be fulfilled within thirty (30) days, the Office
22 and OMES shall enter into a written agreement expressing an agreed
23 upon timeline for fulfilling the needs of the Office. In the event
24 that the request cannot be fulfilled, OMES shall respond in writing
HB2293 HFLR Page 8
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 with in ten (10) days of the request providing the reasoning for
2 denial. If the request cannot be fulfilled utilizing existing
3 resources, OMES may charge the Office for the actual cost to fulfill
4 the request.
5 C. The Office shall be governed by the Broadband Governing
6 Board, created pursuant to Section 9202 of this title.
7 D. The Office shall be advised by the Broadband Expansion
8 Council, created pursuant to Section 9203 of this title.
9 E. The Office shall have an Executive Director who shall
10 oversee the operations of the Office including, but not limited to:
11 1. Overseeing the creation, maintenance, and completion of the
12 Statewide Broadband Plan;
13 2. Communicating the state's progress in achieving the goals
14 and implementation of the Statewide Broadband Plan;
15 3. The creation, housing, and updating of a statewide broadband
16 services map;
17 4. Overseeing of grant applications and awards for connectivity
18 and affordability projects;
19 5. Making recommendations to the Board regarding procedural and
20 administrative rules for the Office; and
21 6. Employment and management of Office staff subject to the
22 limitations and requirements of the Oklahoma Broadband Expansion Act
23 and subject to the limitations and requirements of the Broadband
24 Governing Board.

22 enhance access and adoption; and
23 2. Maintain, update, and execute the Statewide Broadband Plan

15 3. Upon successful protest action, result in a timely
16 correction of the map.
17 E. The Office shall perform and maintain a study of existing
18 grants, incentives, and programs that may improve physical access to
19 broadband along with adoption of broadband technologies. The
20 grants, incentives, and programs may include federal funds, state
21 funds or resources, tribal funds or resources, donated funds, or
22 funding available from foundations, endowments or similar resources,

24 or options, or federal, tribal, state or local regulatory policies

6 a Statewide Broadband Plan. The Plan shall detail what areas are
7 served, underserved, or unserved according to the prevailing
8 definitions of the FCC, and how best to improve the infrastructure
9 and connectivity in underserved and unserved areas. The Plan shall
10 include, but shall not be limited to, detailing a pathway for
11 ninety-five percent (95%) of the state's population to be adequately
12 served by June 30, 2028 December 31, 2030.
13 G. The Office shall create the Capital Projects Fund Grant
14 Report or provide the necessary information to the state entity
15 drafting the Report. The Report shall be submitted to the
16 Department of the Treasury no later than September 24, 2022. The
17 Office shall maintain the Report and submit any and all additional
18 information as required. The Office shall work with the Joint
19 Committee to ensure all approved broadband projects utilizing funds
20 from the Capital Projects Fund follow the correct reporting
21 requirements based on Department of Treasury guidance.

3 I. On or before October 31, 2022, the Office, with the advice
4 of the Council, shall develop a set of broadband grants or incentive
5 awards guidelines to be approved by the Board. The grants and
6 incentive awards shall be for unserved and underserved areas. The
7 Office shall submit a copy of the guidelines to the Office of the
8 Speaker of the Oklahoma House of Representatives, the Office of the
9 President Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate, and to the
10 offices of the chairs of the appropriate legislative committees.
11 The guidelines shall:
12 1. Consider a weighted approach for awards based upon the
13 following:
14 a. the area's need for services, including, but not
15 limited to, whether the area is underserved or
16 unserved,
17 b. whether there are existing broadband assets in the
18 area, based on the statewide map,
19 c. whether existing federal, state, local, tribal, or
20 private resources have been allocated to broadband
21 services in the area,
22 d. a preference for federal, state, local, tribal, or
23 private partnerships, and
24
HB2293 HFLR Page 13
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 e. the capacity of the provider to maintain assets for an
2 extended period of time;
3 2. Recommend any necessary controls including, but not limited
4 to, capping the dollar amount of awards, allowing for an auditing
5 process, and a process that allows for award clawbacks. These
6 controls shall be in place to ensure the maximum efficiency of the
7 incentive award and to protect against waste, fraud, or abuse; and
8 3. Include annual reporting requirements of award recipients to
9 assess effectiveness of such incentives which shall include, but not
10 be limited to, changes in coverage resulting from implementing

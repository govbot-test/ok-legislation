7 An Act relating to the Corporation Commission;
creating the Plug-In Solar Power Amendments Act;
8 defining terms; making certain exemptions;
prohibiting electric utilities from making certain
9 requirements of users; providing for safety
standards; limiting certain liability; creating
10 certain responsibility; authorizing the adoption of
certain rules; prohibiting certain aspects of rules;
11 limiting applicability of act; providing for
codification; and providing an effective date.

15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 875 of Title 17, unless there is
18 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Plug-In Solar
20 Power Amendments Act".
21 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 876 of Title 17, unless there is
23 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24 As used in this act:
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4060 Page 1

1 1. "Electric utility" means any public utility providing retail
2 electric service subject to the jurisdiction of the Oklahoma
3 Corporation Commission;
4 2. "Portable solar generation device" means a solar
5 photovoltaic device that:
6 a. has a rated alternating current output capacity of not
7 more than one thousand two hundred (1,200) watts in
8 the aggregate,
9 b. is designed to connect to a customer's premises wiring
10 through a standard grounded one hundred twenty-volt
11 electrical outlet,
12 c. includes integrated anti-islanding or equivalent
13 safety functionality that prevents energizing a
14 building's electrical system during a power outage,
15 and
16 d. is intended primarily to offset electricity consumed
17 on the premises of the retail electric customer; and
18 3. "Retail electric customer" means a residential customer
19 receiving electric service from an electric utility.
20 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 877 of Title 17, unless there is
22 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 A portable solar generation device that meets the requirements
24 of this act:
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4060 Page 2

1 1. Shall not be considered an interconnection under state law
2 or Oklahoma Corporation Commission rules;
3 2. Shall not be subject to net metering statutes, tariffs, or
4 programs; and
5 3. Shall not require an interconnection agreement, application,
6 inspection, or approval from an electric utility or the Oklahoma
7 Corporation Commission.
8 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
9 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 878 of Title 17, unless there is
10 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
11 An electric utility shall not require a retail electric customer
12 using a qualifying portable solar generation device to:
13 1. Obtain prior authorization or permission from the utility;
14 2. Pay any fee, charge, or tariff related to the installation
15 or use of the device;
16 3. Install additional metering, controls, or equipment beyond
17 what is integrated into the device;
18 4. Execute an interconnection, net metering, or service
19 modification agreement; or
20 5. Purchase insurance or provide indemnification related to the
21 device.
22 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 879 of Title 17, unless there is
24 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4060 Page 3

1 A portable solar generation device shall comply with nationally
2 recognized electrical safety standards, including anti-islanding
3 protection. Nothing in this act limits the authority of state or
4 local electrical inspectors to enforce generally applicable
5 electrical safety codes unrelated to utility interconnection.
6 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 880 of Title 17, unless there is
8 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 An electric utility shall not be liable for any damage, injury,
10 or loss caused by a portable solar generation device or the
11 installation, operation, or maintenance of such a device by a retail
12 electric customer. The retail electric customer bears
13 responsibility for the proper installation and maintenance of the
14 device and compliance with applicable safety standards.
15 SECTION 7. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 881 of Title 17, unless there is
17 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. The Oklahoma Corporation Commission may adopt administrative
19 rules solely as necessary to:
20 1. Enforce the provisions of this act; and
21 2. Ensure compliance with applicable safety standards.
22 B. Rules adopted under this section shall not:
23 1. Require registration, approval, or notification for portable
24 solar generation devices;
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4060 Page 4

1 2. Impose fees or charges;
2 3. Reduce the maximum wattage authorized by statute; or
3 4. Subject such devices to net metering or interconnection
4 requirements.
5 SECTION 8. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
6 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 882 of Title 17, unless there is
7 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
8 Nothing in this act shall be construed to:
9 1. Authorize compensation for exported electricity;
10 2. Modify existing distributed generation or net metering
11 programs; or
12 3. Limit the authority of the Oklahoma Corporation Commission
13 over traditional interconnection of electric generation facilities.
14 SECTION 9. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

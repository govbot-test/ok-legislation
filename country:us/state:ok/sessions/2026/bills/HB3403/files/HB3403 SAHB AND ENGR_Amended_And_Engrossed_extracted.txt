creating the Oklahoma Biosolids Land Application

of Environmental Quality to administer Pilot Program
10 in cooperation with *** funding; requiring deposit of
funds in certain revolving fund; creating the
11 Oklahoma Biosolids Land Application Research Pilot
Program Act Revolving Fund; describing fund;
12 providing for content of fund; providing for deposits
and expenditures; providing for codification; and
13 providing an effective date.

resources; creating the Oklahoma Biosolids Land
19 Application Research Pilot Program Act; providing
short title; requiring the Department of
20 Environmental Quality to administer the Oklahoma
Biosolids Land Application Research Pilot Program in
21 cooperation with Oklahoma State University; providing
for expiration of the pilot program; listing
22 requirements for the pilot program; requiring
electronic submission of certain annual report;
23 listing requirements for annual report; authorizing
partnerships with private landowners; limiting
24 liability; making certain exemption; requiring

1 certain documentation be kept; authorizing continued
application upon certain finding; requiring
2 utilization of certain technology; prohibiting land
application of certain materials upon certain date;
3 prohibiting issuance of certain permits; requiring
cessation of certain actions by certain dates;
4 requiring plan submission by certain permittees by
certain date; directing rule promulgation; defining

expenditures; providing for codification; and
8 providing an effective date.

10 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
11 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
12 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-10-1120 of Title 27A, unless
13 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
14 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Oklahoma
15 Biosolids Land Application Research Pilot Program Act”.
16 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-10-1121 of Title 27A, unless
18 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 A. There is hereby created at Oklahoma State University (OSU)
20 the Oklahoma Biosolids Land Application Research Pilot Program. The
21 pilot program shall be administered by the Department of
22 Environmental Quality (DEQ) in cooperation with the OSU Division of
23 Agricultural Sciences and Natural Resources and in coordination with
24 the OSU College of Engineering, Architecture and Technology, the OSU
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 3403 Page 2
1 Depa rtment of Environmental Science, and the OSU Cooperative
2 Extension Service. The pilot program shall operate for an initial
3 three-year period.
4 B. The pilot program shall:
5 1. Identify and approve study plots on agricultural lands,
6 including, but not limited to, OSU-owned research farms and, as
7 appropriate, cooperating private farms and farms that have a history
8 of using biosolids;
9 2. Develop biosolids application protocols, including:
10 a. nutrient content evaluation,
11 b. pathogen and contaminant screening, including
12 perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS),
13 heavy metals, and pharmaceuticals,
14 c. application rates, and
15 d. soil amendment practices;
16 3. Conduct agronomic research, including crop yield impacts,
17 soil health changes, and nutrient cycling;
18 4. Conduct environmental impact studies, including:
19 a. surface and groundwater monitoring,
20 b. soil residual testing, and
21 c. air quality, odor, and vector considerations;
22 5. Assess regulatory models, including best management
23 practices, permitting options, and compliance structures suitable
24 for this state;
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 3403 Page 3
1 6. Coordinate with the DEQ and may utilize biosolids supplied
2 by municipal wastewater treatment facilities of this state that meet
3 DEQ and United States Environmental Protection Agency (EPA)
4 standards; and
5 7. Provide outreach and education to farmers, ranchers,
6 conservation districts, and municipalities in this state through OSU
7 Extension programs.
8 C. An electronic report shall be submitted annually to the
9 Governor, the President Pro Tempore of the Senate, the Speaker of
10 the House of Representatives, and the DEQ regarding findings, data,
11 and recommendations. Annual reports shall be submitted no later
12 than December 1 and include:
13 1. Agronomic outcomes;
14 2. Environmental monitoring results;
15 3. Risk assessments;
16 4. Recommendations for statewide standards or rules; and
17 5. Cost-benefit analysis for farmers and municipalities.
18 D. OSU may partner with private agricultural landowners who
19 volunteer to participate in research trials. OSU shall secure
20 written consent, ensure DEQ compliance, and provide guidelines for
21 application, monitoring, and sampling. No landowner shall be held
22 liable for environmental impacts resulting solely from adherence to
23 OSU protocols developed under the pilot program.
24
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 3403 Page 4
1 E. Biosolids applied under the pilot program shall be exempt
2 from state permitting otherwise required under environmental or land
3 application rules, only to the extent necessary to conduct
4 controlled research, and only when research protocols meet or exceed
5 DEQ and EPA requirements. OSU shall coordinate with the DEQ to
6 ensure proper recordkeeping and transport documentation.
7 F. The pilot program may be funded by legislative
8 appropriations, federal research grants, municipal contributions,
9 private or industry research partnerships, or in-kind contributions
10 of biosolids or laboratory services. Funds for administering the
11 pilot program shall be deposited into the Oklahoma Biosolids Land
12 Application Research Pilot Program Act Revolving Fund created in
13 Section 3 of this act.
14 G. 1. Upon completion of the three-year pilot program, if the
15 findings, data, and recommendations in the reports required pursuant
16 to subsection C of this section indicate that land application of
17 biosolids is considered to be safe and cost-effective, the DEQ shall
18 continue to issue new permits and renewals for such application of
19 biosolids in accordance with the report recommendations.
20 2. Municipalities shall require the use of microbial
21 biotechnology approved by the DEQ to reduce the volume of biosolids
22 at a wastewater treatment plant. Additionally, such municipalities
23 shall adopt technology approved by the DEQ to reduce biosolid odor
24 and dry solid matter at a wastewater treatment plant.
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 3403 Page 5
1 H. 1. Upon completion of the three-year pilot program, if the
2 findings, data, and recommendations in the reports required pursuant
3 to subsection C of this section indicate that land application of
4 biosolids would be a detriment to the health and safety of this
5 state, such application shall be prohibited on and after December 1,
6 2029, and the DEQ shall not issue any new permit for the land
7 application of biosolids. Renewal of permits for land application
8 shall be left to the discretion of the DEQ for the purpose of aiding
9 municipalities with the reduction of land application of biosolids.
10 2. Not later than December 1, 2027, all persons or entities
11 permitted to land apply biosolids shall reduce the amount of such
12 material land applied by at least twenty-five percent (25%).
13 3. Not later than December 1, 2028, all persons or entities
14 permitted to land apply biosolids shall reduce the amount of such
15 material land applied by at least fifty percent (50%).
16 4. Not later than December 1, 2029, any person or entity
17 permitted to land apply biosolids shall submit a plan for full
18 cessation of such actions to the DEQ. The plan shall be compiled
19 and submitted in the form and manner prescribed by the DEQ.
20 I. The DEQ shall promulgate rules to implement the provisions
21 subsections G and H of this section.
22 J. For the purposes of this section, “biosolids” means treated
23 sewage sludge derived from municipal or domestic wastewater
24 treatment plants.
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 3403 Page 6
1 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-10-1122 of Title 27A, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving fund
5 for the Department of Environmental Quality to be designated the
6 “Oklahoma Biosolids Land Application Research Pilot Program Act
7 Revolving Fund”. The fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject
8 to fiscal year limitations, and shall consist of all monies received
9 by the Department from appropriations, apportionments, donations,
10 and federal grants received for the purpose of conducting the
11 provisions of the Oklahoma Biosolids Land Application Research Pilot
12 Program created pursuant to Section 2 of this act. All monies
13 accruing to the credit of the fund are hereby appropriated and may
14 be budgeted and expended by the Department for the purpose provided
15 for in this section. Expenditures from the fund shall be made upon
16 warrants issued by the State Treasurer against claims filed as
17 prescribed by law with the Director of the Office of Management and
18 Enterprise Services for approval and payment.
19 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.”

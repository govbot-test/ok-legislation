1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-10-1120 of Title 27A, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Oklahoma
5 Biosolids Land Application Research Pilot Program Act".
6 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-10-1121 of Title 27A, unless
8 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 A. There is hereby created at Oklahoma State University (OSU)
10 the Oklahoma Biosolids Land Application Research "Pilot Program".
11 The Pilot Program shall be administered by the Department of
12 Environmental Quality (DEQ) in cooperation with the Division of
13 Agricultural Sciences and Natural Resources in coordination with the
14 OSU College of Engineering, Architecture and Technology, the OSU
15 Department of Environmental Science, and the Cooperative Extension
16 Service. The Pilot Program shall operate for an initial five-year
17 period.
18 B. The Pilot Program shall:
19 1. Identify and approve study plots on agricultural lands,
20 including, but not limited to, OSU-owned research farms and, as
21 appropriate, cooperating private farms;
22 2. Develop biosolids application protocols, including:
23 a. nutrient content evaluation,
24

1 b. pathogen and contaminant screening (including
2 perfluoroalkyl and polyfluoroalkyl substances (PFAS),
3 heavy metals, and pharmaceuticals),
4 c. application rates, and
5 d. soil amendment practices;
6 3. Conduct agronomic research, including crop yield impacts,
7 soil health changes, and nutrient cycling;
8 4. Conduct environmental impact studies, including:
9 a. surface and groundwater monitoring,
10 b. soil residual testing, and
11 c. air quality, odor, and vector considerations;
12 5. Assess regulatory models, including best management
13 practices, permitting options, and compliance structures suitable
14 for Oklahoma;
15 6. Coordinate with the DEQ and may utilize biosolids supplied
16 by Oklahoma municipal wastewater treatment facilities that meet DEQ
17 and Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) standards; and
18 7. Provide outreach and education to Oklahoma farmers,
19 ranchers, conservation districts, and municipalities through OSU
20 Extension programs.
21 C. A report shall be submitted annually to the Governor, the
22 President Pro Tempore of the Oklahoma State Senate, the Speaker of
23 the Oklahoma House of Representatives, and the DEQ regarding

3 1. Agronomic outcomes;
4 2. Environmental monitoring results;
5 3. Risk assessments;
6 4. Recommendations for statewide standards or rules; and
7 5. Cost-benefit analysis for farmers and municipalities.
8 D. OSU may partner with private agricultural landowners who
9 volunteer to participate in research trials. OSU shall secure
10 written consent, ensure DEQ compliance, and provide guidelines for
11 application, monitoring, and sampling. No landowner shall be held
12 liable for environmental impacts resulting solely from adherence to
13 OSU protocols developed under the Pilot Program.
14 E. Biosolids applied under the Pilot Program shall be exempt

16 application rules, only to the extent necessary to conduct
17 controlled research, and only when research protocols meet or exceed
18 DEQ and EPA requirements. OSU shall coordinate with DEQ to ensure
19 proper recordkeeping and transport documentation.

3 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-10-1122 of Title 27A, unless
5 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
6 There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving fund
7 for the Department of Environmental Quality to be designated the
8 "Oklahoma Biosolids Land Application Research Pilot Program Act
9 Revolving Fund". The fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject
10 to fiscal year limitations, and shall consist of all monies received
11 by the Department from appropriations, apportionments, donations,
12 and federal grants received for the purpose of conducting the
13 provisions of the Oklahoma Biosolids Land Application Research Pilot
14 Program Act created pursuant to Section 2 of this act. All monies
15 accruing to the credit of the fund are hereby appropriated and may
16 be budgeted and expended by the Department for the purpose provided
17 for in this section. Expenditures from the fund shall be made upon
18 warrants issued by the State Treasurer against claims filed as
19 prescribed by law with the Director of the Office of Management and
20 Enterprise Services for approval and payment.
21 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

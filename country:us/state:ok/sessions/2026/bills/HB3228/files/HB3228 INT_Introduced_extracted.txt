Section 1:
1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 2nd Session of the 60th Legislature (2026)
4
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to medical malpractice; requiring the
State Board of Medical Licensure and Supervision to
8 establish and maintain database of medical
malpractice lawsuits; listing requirements for
9 database; listing required information for database;
requiring physicians report lawsuits within certain
10 time; requiring Board to conduct an educational
campaign on database; providing for codification; and
11 providing an effective date.
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 481.2 of Title 59, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. The State Board of Medical Licensure and Supervision shall
19 establish and maintain a comprehensive public database of medical
20 malpractice lawsuits filed against licensed physicians in this
21 state. The database shall:
22 1. Be user-friendly;
23 2. Be accessible to the public through the Board's official
24 website;
Req. No. 15569 Page 1
1 3. Be regularly updated; and
2 4. Comply with all applicable privacy laws concerning sensitive
3 patient information.
4 B. The database shall include:
5 1. The name of the physician;
6 2. The nature of the medical malpractice claim;
7 3. The date the lawsuit was filed;
8 4. The disposition of the case;
9 5. Any disciplinary action taken by the Board as a result of
10 the lawsuit; and
11 6. Internet links to relevant court documents when available.
12 C. All licensed physicians shall report to the Board any
13 medical malpractice lawsuit filed against him or her within thirty
14 (30) days of becoming aware of the lawsuit.
15 D. The Board shall implement an education campaign to inform
16 healthcare facilities, physicians, and the public about the database
17 and the purpose of the database including, but not limited to, how
18 it can be used to make informed healthcare decisions.
19 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2027.

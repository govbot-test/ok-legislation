5 1. To manufacture, bottle, package and store beer and cider
6 produced by the licensee on licensed premises;
7 2. To sell beer and cider in this state to holders of beer
8 distributor licenses and retail licenses or to sell beer and cider
9 out of this state to qualified persons;
10 3. To serve free samples of beer and cider produced by the
11 licensee to visitors twenty-one (21) years of age or older;
12 4. To sell beer and cider produced by the licensee for either
13 on-premises or off-premises consumption to consumers on the brewery
14 premises, or on premises located contiguous thereto;
15 5. To sell beer and cider at events attended by the public
16 including, but not limited to, trade shows, festivals, farmers
17 markets, boat shows, RV shows, home and garden shows, fairs, car
18 shows, swap meets, city events, county events, or state events for
19 either on-premises or off-premises consumption, regardless of
20 whether such events are held at premises covered by a license to
21 sell, serve, or store alcoholic beverages. A small brewer license
22 holder shall not be required to secure or control the premises of an
23 event attended by the public where the small brewer license holder
24 sells beer or cider;

1 6. To purchase wine in retail containers from the holder of a

3 7. To sell, offer for sale and possess wine for on-premises

5 8. To host off-site events pursuant to Section 1 2-162 of this
6 act title; and
7 9. To purchase from licensed brewers, small brewers, and
8 brewpubs in this state, and to import beer into this state for use

12 licensed premises. However, a licensee shall not exceed one

14 this paragraph, a small brewer may sell spirits in this state to
15 licensed wholesalers and manufacturers only and may sell spirits out
16 of this state to qualified persons. A small brewer may purchase
17 from licensed distillers and rectifiers in this state, and import
18 spirits from without this state for manufacturing purposes in
19 accordance with federal laws and regulations. A small brewer
20 seeking to manufacture, bottle, package, and store spirits pursuant
21 to this paragraph must first request and obtain a Distilled Spirits
22 Plant (DSP) permit from the Alcohol and Tobacco Tax and Trade Bureau
23 (TTB) and file such permit with the ABLE Commission.

1 C. The holder of multiple small brewer licenses may sell beer
2 and cider produced at up to three breweries for which the licensee
3 has a license, at any other of such three licensed breweries or on
4 premises located contiguous thereto.
5 D. Nothing in the Oklahoma Alcoholic Beverage Control Act shall
6 prohibit the holder of a small brewer license from also holding or
7 owning an interest in the holder of a brewpub license.
8 E. For purposes of this section, no visitor may sample more
9 than a total of twelve (12) fluid ounces of beer and cider per day.

11 to an area within the licensed premises as defined in this

15 designated serving area. Accompanied visitors under twenty-one (21)
16 years of age shall be allowed anywhere on the premises except for a
17 serving area. Samples of beer and cider served by a brewery under
18 this section shall not be considered a sale of beer and cider within
19 the meaning of Article XXVIII-A of the Oklahoma Constitution or
20 Section 1-103 of this title; however, such samples of beer and cider
21 shall be considered beer and cider removed or withdrawn from the
22 brewery for use or consumption within the meaning of Section 5-110
23 of this title for excise tax determination and reporting
24 requirements. Sales and sampling may only occur between the hours

2 "serving area" means the area of the bar where drinks are sold,
3 prepared, and served to paying customers and shall not include other
4 areas of the brewery where customers consume purchased products.
5 F. A small brewer self-distribution license shall authorize
6 holders of a small brewer license to distribute beer and cider
7 produced only by such licensee to a holder of a retail beer license,
8 retail spirits license, mixed beverage license, beer and wine
9 license, caterer's license, special event license, public event
10 license, charitable auction license or brewpub license. A small
11 brewer shall elect whether it will distribute through a distributor

13 may not elect to do both simultaneously in a subject territory. The

15 changes to the election shall require immediate notification to the
16 ABLE Commission before the change in election will take effect. A
17 small brewer that elects to self-distribute in multiple territories
18 shall only be required to have one small brewer self-distribution
19 license.
20 G. All manufacturer's licenses held by brewers during the first

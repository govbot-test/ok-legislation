10 An Act relating to professions and occupations;
amending 59 O.S. 2021, Sections 1003, 1005, 1006, and
11 1009, which relate to The Plumbing License Law of
1955; modifying definitions; decreasing certain
12 prohibited examination period; establishing certain
passing score; allowing Construction Industries Board
13 to issue licenses to certain persons; requiring
certain plumbing contractors to meet certain
14 requirements; limiting certain residential plumbers
to certain buildings; updating statutory language;
15 and providing an effective date.
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1003, is
19 amended to read as follows:
20 Section 1003. As used in The Plumbing License Law of 1955:
21 1. “Board” means the Construction Industries Board;
22 2. “Committee” means the Oklahoma State Committee of Plumbing
23 Examiners appointed by the Construction Industries Board;
24
Req. No. 3960 Page 1
1 3. “Plumbing Hearing Board” means the State Plumbing Hearing
2 Board which shall consist of a designee of the Construction
3 Industries Board, as chair, and the members of the Oklahoma State
4 Committee of Plumbing Examiners;
5 4. “Apprentice” or “plumber’s apprentice” means any person
6 sixteen (16) years of age or older who, as the principal occupation
7 of the person, is engaged in learning and assisting in the
8 installation of plumbing under the direct supervision of a licensed
9 journeyman plumber or plumbing contractor;
10 5. “Journeyman plumber” means any person other than a master
11 plumber or plumbing contractor who engages in or works at the actual
12 installation, alteration, repair, and/or or renovation of plumbing
13 site;
14 6. “Temporary journeyman plumber” means any person other than a
15 person permanently licensed as a journeyman plumber, master plumber,
16 or plumbing contractor in this state who meets the temporary
17 licensure requirements of Section 1006.1 of this title;
18 7. “Master plumber” is a term used and defined under laws which
19 have been repealed. A person formerly known as a master plumber is
20 henceforth to be known as a “plumbing contractor” as defined in this
21 section;
22 8. “Plumbing contractor” means any person who is skilled in the
23 planning, superintending, and practical installation of plumbing and
24 who is familiar with the laws and rules governing the same. This
Req. No. 3960 Page 2
1 defi nition may be construed to mean any person who has qualified and
2 is licensed under The Plumbing License Law of 1955 as a plumbing
3 contractor, who may operate as an individual, a firm, partnership,
4 limited liability company, or corporation, or other legal entity to
5 engage in the business of plumbing, or the business of contracting
6 to do plumbing, or furnish labor or materials or both for the
7 installation, repair, maintenance, or renovation of plumbing
8 according to the requirements of The Plumbing License Law of 1955;
9 9. 8. “Plumbing” means, and includes:
10 a. all piping, fixtures, appurtenances, and appliances
11 for, and in connection with, a supply of water within
12 or adjacent to any building, structure, or conveyance,
13 on the premises and to the connection with a water
14 main or other source of supply,
15 b. all piping, fixtures, appurtenances, and appliances
16 for sanitary drainage or storm drainage facilities,
17 including venting systems for such facilities, within
18 or adjacent to any building, structure, or conveyance,
19 on the premises and to the connection with a public
20 disposal system or other acceptable terminal,
21 c. the installation, repair, maintenance, and renovation
22 of all piping, fixtures, appurtenances, and appliances
23 for a supply of water, or for the disposal of waste
24 water, liquid waste, or sewage within or adjacent to
Req. No. 3960 Page 3
1 any building, structure, or conveyance, on the
2 premises and to the source of supply of water or point
3 of disposal of wastes, and
4 d. the installation, repair, and maintenance of radiant-
5 floor heating system piping in residential homes with
6 capacities no greater than one hundred thousand
7 (100,000) BTU’s BTUs using only piping approved by the
8 most current adopted edition of the International
9 Mechanical Code; and
10 9. “Residential journeyman plumber” means any person who is
11 qualified and licensed, other than a plumbing contractor, to engage
12 in or work at the actual installation, alteration, repair, or
13 renovation site of residential plumbing. Residential plumbing shall
14 be limited to the type and category of journeyman plumbing license
15 for supervising and performing plumbing work for detached one- or
16 two-family dwellings and does not include commercial plumbers; and
17 10. “Variance and Appeals Board” means the Oklahoma State
18 Plumbing Installation Code Variance and Appeals Board.
19 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1005, is
20 amended to read as follows:
21 Section 1005. (a) A. Examinations shall be uniform and shall
22 be practical in nature but sufficiently strict to test the
23 qualification and fitness of the applicant as a journeyman plumber
24
Req. No. 3960 Page 4
1 or r esidential journeyman plumber or as a plumbing contractor, as
2 the case may be. It shall be in whole or in part in writing.
3 (b) B. Regular examinations shall be held at least twice each
4 year and special examinations may be fixed by the Oklahoma State
5 Committee of Plumbing Examiners. Any applicant initially failing to
6 pass the examination shall not be permitted to take another
7 examination for a period of thirty (30) days, and thereafter any
8 such applicant subsequently failing to pass the examination shall
9 not be permitted to take a subsequent examination for a period of
10 ninety (90) days thirty (30) days.
11 C. A passing score on all examinations shall be a grade not
12 less than seventy percent (70%).
13 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1006, is
14 amended to read as follows:
15 Section 1006. A. The Construction Industries Board shall issue
16 licenses to persons who have been certified by the Board as having
17 successfully passed the examination for journeyman plumber or,
18 plumbing contractor, or residential journeyman plumber, as the case
19 may be, and who have paid the fees and have otherwise complied with
20 the applicable requirements of The Plumbing License Law of 1955.
21 B. Plumbing contractors shall meet the same requirements as
22 applicants for the journeyman plumber examination with an additional
23 one (1) year of experience. Plumbing contractors shall notify their
24 surety of any municipalities wherein plumbing work will be performed
Req. No. 3960 Page 5
1 and may operate as an individual, firm, partnership, limited
2 liability company, or corporation, or other legal entity to engage
3 in the business of plumbing, or the business of contracting to do
4 plumbing.
5 C. Residential plumbers shall be limited to engage in work on
6 one- and two-family dwelling units in a singularly constructed
7 structure that have a maximum of two dwelling units for each
8 structure, and have a minimum of five (5) feet of separation of
9 construction to any other dwelling unit or structure regardless and
10 independent of firewalls, such as a single independent residential
11 home or duplex. Residential plumbers shall not engage in work on
12 dwelling units or structures that include occupancies of more than
13 two dwelling units such as apartments, motels, and multifamily
14 townhomes.
15 D. A residential journeyman plumber license shall entitle the
16 licensee to install plumbing as defined in OAC 158:30-1-2. To
17 engage in the act of residential plumbing, a residential journeyman
18 plumber shall be employed and supervised by a licensed plumbing
19 contractor. A residential journeyman plumber shall not contract or
20 furnish labor and materials. If a residential journeyman plumber
21 performs plumbing work in commercial industrial plumbing, he or she
22 shall be employed by a licensed plumbing contractor and maintain an
23 apprentice plumber registration, as defined in OAC 158:30-5-1.
24
Req. No. 3960 Page 6
1 E. Applicants for a journeyman plumber examination shall be
2 eighteen (18) years of age or older and have:
3 1. Three (3) years of experience in the plumbing trade while
4 employed by a licensed plumbing contractor; or
5 2. Three (3) years of experience in the plumbing trade while
6 serving in the United States Armed Forces.
7 F. Applicants for a residential journeyman plumber examination
8 shall be eighteen (18) years of age or older and have:
9 1. Two (2) years and four thousand (4,000) verified hours of
10 work experience in the plumbing trade while employed by a licensed
11 plumbing contractor;
12 2. An associate’s degree or vocational diploma certifying
13 completion of a formal plumbing education program approved by the
14 Oklahoma State Committee of Plumbing Examiners consisting of a
15 minimum of one thousand (1,000) classroom hours from an educational
16 institution approved by the Committee and three thousand (3,000)
17 verified hours of work experience in the plumbing trade while
18 employed by a licensed plumbing contractor; or
19 3. Two (2) years of experience in the plumbing trade while
20 serving in the United States Armed Forces and three thousand (3,000)
21 verified hours of work experience in the plumbing trade while
22 employed by a licensed plumbing contractor.
23 G. A verifiable out-of-state plumbing license may be used to
24 expedite processing of the requirements or be considered for
Req. No. 3960 Page 7
1 reci procity pursuant to OAC 158:30-5-4 if there is a written
2 reciprocity agreement with that state, the license is currently in
3 good standing, and the licensee meets the requirements set forth in
4 subsections E and F of this section.
5 H. All licenses shall be nontransferable. It shall be unlawful
6 for any holder of a license under The Plumbing License Law of 1955
7 to loan or allow the use of such license by any other person, firm
8 or corporation, except as specifically provided in The Plumbing
9 License Law of 1955.
10 C. I. The Board shall adopt procedures and rules to review and
11 accept proof and documentation of an applicant’s qualified training
12 and experience as a journeyman plumber or, plumbing contractor, or
13 residential journeyman plumber while on active duty with the armed
14 forces Armed Forces of the United States when such service member
15 has been honorably discharged from active duty within one (1) year
16 of the application for examination or licensure in this state as a
17 journeyman plumber or, plumbing contractor, or residential
18 journeyman plumber.
19 SECTION 4. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1009, is
20 amended to read as follows:
21 Section 1009. A. No license shall be issued for longer than
22 one (1) year and all licenses shall expire on the last day in the
23 birth month of the licensee and shall be in alignment with
24 continuing education requirements. Such licenses may be renewed
Req. No. 3960 Page 8
1 upon application and payment of fees within thirty (30) days
2 preceding or following the date the license renewal is due. No
3 journeyman or contractor license shall be renewed unless the
4 licensee has completed the required hours of continuing education as
5 determined and approved by the Oklahoma State Committee of Plumbing
6 Examiners.
7 B. The Committee may renew licenses upon application made more
8 than thirty (30) days following the date of expiration only upon
9 payment of the renewal and additional fee prescribed and upon
10 compliance with any applicable continuing education requirements as
11 established by the Construction Industries Board and this act The
12 Plumbing License Law of 1955. Provided, that no penalty for renewal
13 shall be charged to any holder of a license which expires while such
14 holder is in military service if application is made within one (1)
15 year following discharge from the military service.
16 C. Apprentice registration certificates expire one (1) year
17 after date of registration, at which time the apprentice may
18 reregister.
19 SECTION 5. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

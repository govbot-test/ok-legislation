15 Section 1005. (a) A. Examinations shall be uniform and shall
16 be practical in nature but sufficiently strict to test the
17 qualification and fitness of the applicant as a journeyman plumber
18 or as a plumbing contractor, as the case may be. It An examination
19 shall be in whole or in part in writing.

21 year and special examinations may be fixed by the Oklahoma State
22 Committee of Plumbing Examiners. The maximum value of each part of
23 the plumbing examination shall be 100 points. An examinee shall
24 make seventy percent (70%) or more on each part to pass the

5 to take a subsequent examination for a period of ninety (90) days.

7 examination shall be eighteen (18) years of age or older and have:
8 1. Two (2) years of experience in the plumbing trade while

20 4. Two (2) years of experience in the plumbing trade while
21 serving in the United States Armed Forces; or

4 amended to read as follows:
5 Section 1009. No license shall be issued for longer than one
6 (1) year and all licenses shall expire on the last day in the birth
7 month of the licensee and shall be in alignment with continuing
8 education requirements. Such licenses may be renewed upon
9 application and payment of fees within thirty (30) days preceding or
10 following the date the license renewal is due. No journeyman or
11 contractor license shall be renewed unless the licensee has
12 completed the required hours of continuing education as determined
13 and approved by the Oklahoma State Committee of Plumbing Examiners.
14 B. The Committee may renew licenses upon application made more
15 than thirty (30) days following the date of expiration only upon
16 payment of the renewal and additional fee prescribed and upon
17 compliance with any applicable continuing education requirements as
18 established by the Construction Industries Board and this act The
19 Plumbing License Law of 1955. Provided, that no penalty for renewal
20 shall be charged to any holder of a license which expires while such
21 holder is in military service if application is made within one (1)
22 year following discharge from the military service.

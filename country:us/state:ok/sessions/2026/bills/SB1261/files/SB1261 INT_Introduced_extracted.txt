6 AS INTRODUCED
6
7 An Act relating to higher education; defining term;
7 prohibiting certain institutions of higher education
8 from being authorized to accept certain donations,
8 gifts, grants, or devises; providing for promulgation
9 of rules; amending 51 O.S. 2021, Section 24A.16a,
9 which relates to confidentiality of certain donors;
10 prohibiting institutions or agencies of The Oklahoma
10 State System of Higher Education from keeping certain
11 donor information confidential; amending 70 O.S.
11 2021, Section 4306, as amended by Section 576,
12 Chapter 486, O.S.L. 2025 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section
12 4306), which relates to authorization of state higher
13 educational institutions, agencies, or entities to
13 accept gifts; exempting names of donors to certain
14 foundations from disclosure for certain audit;
14 updating statutory language; providing for
15 codification; providing an effective date; and
15 declaring an emergency.
16
16
17
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18
19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3260 of Title 70, unless there
20
21 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21
22 A. For the purposes of this section, “foreign source” means:
22
23 1. A foreign government including any agency of a foreign
23
24 government; and
24
Req. No. 2863 Page 1
1 2. An agent including a subsidiary or affiliate of a foreign
1
2 legal entity acting on behalf of a foreign source.
2
3 B. Upon the effective date of this act, no institution of
3
4 higher education within The Oklahoma State System of Higher
4
5 Education shall be authorized to accept donations, gifts, grants, or
5
6 devises from a foreign source.
6
7 C. The Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education may
7
8 promulgate rules to implement the provisions of this section.
8
9 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 51 O.S. 2021, Section 24A.16a, is
9
10 amended to read as follows:
10
11 Section 24A.16a. Institutions or agencies of The Oklahoma State
11
12 System of Higher Education may keep shall be prohibited from keeping
12
13 confidential all any information pertaining to donors and
13
14 prospective donors to or for the benefit of the institutions or
14
15 agencies.
15
16 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 4306, as
16
17 amended by Section 576, Chapter 486, O.S.L. 2025 (70 O.S. Supp.
17
18 2025, Section 4306), is amended to read as follows:
18
19 Section 4306. A. All state higher educational institutions,
19
20 constituent agencies, or other entities are hereby authorized to
20
21 accept and receive any and all grants or contracts of all kinds,
21
22 gifts, devises, and bequests of money or property, either real or
22
23 personal, which may be, or which may heretofore have been tendered
23
24 to them by grant or contract, will, or gift, conditionally or
24
Req. No. 2863 Page 2
1 unco nditionally; and the Board of Regents of said such institutions,
1
2 constituent agencies, or other entities are hereby directed,
2
3 authorized, and empowered to hold such funds or property in trust,
3
4 or invest or sell them and use either principal or interest or the
4
5 proceeds of sale for the benefit of such institutions or entities or
5
6 the students or others for whose benefit such institutions or
6
7 entities are conducted; all in any manner which is consistent with
7
8 the terms of the gift as stipulated by the donor and with the
8
9 provisions of any applicable laws. Money donated to a college- or
9
10 university-related foundation for student scholarships or grants to
10
11 students of an institution of The Oklahoma State System of Higher
11
12 Education shall not be loaned or given to any regent, officer,
12
13 director, or employee of such foundation or institution or to any
13
14 relative of such person within the third degree of affinity or
14
15 consanguinity. The following, however, shall not be prohibited:
15
16 1. Students in the employ of employed by such foundation or
16
17 institution may be given scholarships; and
17
18 2. Scholarships may be awarded to an otherwise disqualified
18
19 relative of any faculty member, staff employee, foundation or
19
20 institution officer, or maintenance worker of such foundation or
20
21 institution if such relative is meritoriously qualified.
21
22 B. Any person willfully violating the prohibitions of
22
23 subsection A of this section shall be guilty of a Class D1 felony
23
24 offense punishable by imprisonment as provided for in subsections B
24
Req. No. 2863 Page 3
1 thro ugh F of Section 20N of Title 21 of the Oklahoma Statutes, or by
1
2 a fine of not more than Twenty Thousand Dollars ($20,000.00), or by
2
3 both such fine and imprisonment. Any person found guilty of said
3
4 such violations shall also be subject to immediate removal from
4
5 office or employment where applicable.
5
6 C. The Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education, any
6
7 institution or agency of The Oklahoma State System of Higher
7
8 Education, or the regents or governing board of such institution or
8
9 agency shall not directly or indirectly transfer any funds to any
9
10 college- or university-related foundation or render services or
10
11 provide any thing of value to any such foundation without receiving
11
12 documented adequate payment or reimbursement therefor according to
12
13 written contract; provided, nothing herein shall be construed as
13
14 prohibiting payment by the institution or agency of claims for
14
15 expenses of fund raising fundraising for the benefit of the
15
16 institution or agency by state employees if such fund raising
16
17 fundraising activities are approved in advance by the governing
17
18 board of regents responsible for such institution or agency and made
18
19 a part of the minutes of the meeting of the board.
19
20 D. Neither the Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education nor
20
21 any institution or agency of The Oklahoma State System of Higher
21
22 Education shall receive any funds, services, or thing of value from
22
23 any college- or university-related foundation which has any officers
23
24 or employees who are officers or employees of any institution or
24
Req. No. 2863 Page 4
1 agen cy of the State System or State Regents unless such foundation
1
2 makes all its financial records and documents, including work
2
3 papers, except for and names of donors, available to auditors who
3
4 are performing audits of the institution or agency.
4
5 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
5
6 SECTION 5. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
6
7 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
7
8 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
8
9 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

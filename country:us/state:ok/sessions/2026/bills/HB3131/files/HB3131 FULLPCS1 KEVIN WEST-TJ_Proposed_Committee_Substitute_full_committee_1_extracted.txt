8 PROPOSED OVERSIGHT COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 An Act relating to homeless shelter standards;
creating the Oklahoma Homeless Shelter Safety and
10 Accountability Act; establishing minimum statewide
standards for certain providers receiving state-
11 administered funds and state-administered federal
funds; limiting rulemaking authority; providing for
12 financial transparency and aggregate reporting;
providing complaint, enforcement, and appeal
13 procedures; preserving local zoning authority;
creating an advisory board with limited duration;
14 providing for sunset; providing for noncodification;
providing for codification; and providing an
15 effective date.

19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
20 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
21 This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Oklahoma
22 Homeless Shelter Safety and Accountability Act".

1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1200 of Title 63, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 As used in this act:
5 1. "Homeless shelter provider" means a public or private entity
6 operating a facility providing temporary overnight shelter to
7 individuals experiencing homelessness and receiving state-
8 administered funds or state-administered federal funds;
9 2. "Material noncompliance" means failure to meet a minimum
10 standard expressly authorized in Section 5 of this act after written
11 notice and expiration of the cure period provided in Section 9 of
12 this act;
13 3. "Material safety event" means:
14 a. death not resulting from natural causes,
15 b. serious bodily injury requiring emergency transport or
16 hospitalization,
17 c. fire requiring response by a fire department,
18 d. violent felony offense reported to law enforcement, or
19 e. a condition presenting an imminent threat to life or
20 physical safety;
21 4. "On the premises" means within property under control of the
22 provider;

1 5. "State-administered federal funds" means federal funds
2 received by a state agency and distributed through grant, contract,
3 or subrecipient agreement; and
4 6. "State-administered funds" means funds appropriated by the
5 Legislature or otherwise disbursed by a state agency.
6 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1201 of Title 63, unless there
8 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 A. Nothing in this act shall create, replace, alter, redefine,
10 or supersede any continuum of care established pursuant to federal
11 law or administered by the United States Department of Housing and
12 Urban Development.
13 B. Nothing in this act shall:
14 1. Establish a statewide continuum of care;
15 2. Replace or modify federal homeless management information
16 system (HMIS) governance structures; or
17 3. Alter coordinated entry systems established under federal
18 law.
19 C. In the event of a conflict between this act and applicable
20 federal grant requirements, federal requirements shall control.
21 D. Compliance with this act shall be a condition of eligibility
22 for receipt of state-administered funds and state-administered
23 federal funds.
24
Req. No. 16868 Page 3

1 E. To the extent practicable, the State Department of Health
2 shall accept existing federal or state reports in satisfaction of
3 reporting requirements under this act.
4 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
5 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1202 of Title 63, unless there
6 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
7 This act applies only to homeless shelter providers receiving
8 state-administered funds and state-administered federal funds.
9 Providers receiving federal funds directly from a federal agency
10 without state administration are not subject to this act unless
11 otherwise required by state law.
12 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1203 of Title 63, unless there
14 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 A. The State Department of Health may promulgate rules strictly
16 limited to implementing the minimum standards expressly authorized
17 in this section.
18 B. Rulemaking authority is confined solely to:
19 1. Basic life safety standards consistent with existing
20 applicable building and fire codes;
21 2. Minimum sanitation and hygiene standards;
22 3. Reporting procedures for material safety events;
23 4. Documentation verifying occupancy limits consistent with
24 local approvals; and
Req. No. 16868 Page 4

1 5. Staff training standards limited to safety, emergency
2 response, and facility operations.
3 C. The State Department of Health shall not:
4 1. Create or establish a statewide continuum of care structure;
5 2. Establish mandatory performance benchmarks or outcome
6 thresholds;
7 3. Create system-wide reduction targets;
8 4. Require submission of client-level personally identifiable
9 information;
10 5. Duplicate federal homeless management information system
11 (HMIS) reporting requirements;
12 6. Impose zoning or land use requirements;
13 7. Establish licensing requirements beyond those required by
14 law;
15 8. Regulate clinical or medical services unless required by
16 separate statute; or
17 9. Expand the scope of this act beyond providers receiving
18 state-administered funds.
19 D. Any expansion beyond subsection B of this section requires
20 express legislative enactment.

4 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1207 of Title 63, unless there

18 Commerce may:
19 1. Suspend, withhold, or declare ineligible the provider's
20 receipt of state-administered funds and state-administered federal
21 funds; and
22 2. Issue formal written recommendations to both the Board of
23 County Commissioners and the governing body of the municipality in

Section 1:
1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 2nd Session of the 60th Legislature (2026)
4
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to school employees; prohibiting
specified existing organizations from continuing to
8 represent employees after certain date; requiring a
school district board of education to hold secret
9 ballot election; directing the board of education to
conduct in person elections; authorizing the board of
10 education to establish a fee schedule to fund the
election; eliminating recognition of organization if
11 it fails to receive a majority of votes; providing
for continuation of contract terms if an organization
12 is eliminated; providing exception; allowing board of
education to recognize a new employee organization
13 under certain circumstances; providing requirements
for elections to certify majority support; amending
14 70 O.S. 2021, Section 5-139, which relates to payroll
deductions; prohibiting certain payroll deductions;
15 amending 62 O.S. 2021, Section 34.70.1, which relates
to state agency payroll deductions; modifying
16 prohibition against payroll deductions on behalf of
certain employees for certain membership dues;
17 modifying date of application; providing for
codification; providing an effective date; and
18 declaring an emergency.
19
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
22 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 509.13 of Title 70, unless there
24 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
Req. No. 14781 Page 1
1 A. No existing employee organization established pursuant to
2 the provisions of Section 509.2 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes
3 shall continue to represent employees as defined in Section 509.2 or
4 1-116 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes after December 31, 2020,
5 except as provided for in this section.
6 B. 1. A school district board of education shall hold a secret
7 ballot election for all employees in any bargaining unit represented
8 by an existing employee organization to determine whether the
9 organization retains support of a majority of all employees in the
10 bargaining unit.
11 2. The school district board of education shall conduct
12 elections in person with valid government-issued identification.
13 The board of education shall allow employees in the bargaining unit
14 to cast ballots for three (3) business days, excluding weekends,
15 holidays, or any day school is not in session.
16 3. Elections shall use only paper ballots and shall have a five
17 (5) day period for challenging election results. The board of
18 education shall prescribe rules for submitting and reviewing
19 election result challenges.
20 4. The school district board of education may establish a fee
21 schedule, consistent with the provisions of Section 509.2 of Title
22 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes, for employee organizations subject to
23 elections conducted pursuant to the provisions of this section for
24 the purpose of funding the election.
Req. No. 14781 Page 2
1 C. If a majority of all employees in the bargaining unit votes
2 in favor of retaining the existing employee organization, the
3 organization shall remain the representative of employees in the
4 bargaining unit pursuant to the provisions of Section 509.2 of Title
5 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes. If a majority of all employees in the
6 bargaining unit do not vote in favor of retaining the existing
7 employee organization, the school district board of education shall
8 no longer recognize the organization as the representative of
9 employees in the unit and the employees shall be unrepresented.
10 D. When an existing employee organization is no longer
11 recognized, the terms of any preexisting contract between the
12 employee organization and the school district employees represented
13 by the organization shall continue and remain in effect for the
14 remaining contract term, except for any provisions involving in any
15 manner the employee organization including, but not limited to,
16 organization security, dues, fees, grievances and arbitration.
17 E. A school district board of education may recognize a new
18 employee organization in accordance with the provisions of Section
19 509.2 of Title 70 of the Oklahoma Statutes, but the new employee
20 organization may not be substantially similar to or affiliated with
21 an employee organization that has lost recognition pursuant to the
22 provisions of subsection C of this section for twelve (12) months
23 from the date of the loss of recognition.
24
Req. No. 14781 Page 3
1 F. The school district board of education shall hold an
2 election to certify majority support of existing employee
3 organizations no earlier than August 1, 2026, and no later than
4 December 1, 2026, and every three (3) years thereafter. Elections
5 shall occur no earlier than August 1 and no later than December 1.
6 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 5-139, is
7 amended to read as follows:
8 Section 5-139. School A. Except as provided by subsection B of
9 this section, school districts shall make payroll deductions for
10 either or both professional organization dues and political
11 contributions at the written request of any school employee and
12 shall transmit deducted funds to the organization designated by the
13 school district employee. Such deductions shall be on a ten-month
14 basis unless otherwise designated by the employee organization.
15 However, a school employee may request at any time in writing,
16 including by email or fax, for the school district to immediately
17 terminate or initiate payroll deductions to a professional
18 organization. Upon receipt of any written request, the school
19 district shall within five (5) business days or by the next pay
20 period, whichever is earlier, terminate or initiate any future
21 payroll deductions of the requesting school employee to a
22 professional organization. Upon receipt of a written request, the
23 school district shall notify the professional organization of the
24 initiation or termination of payroll deductions within fifteen (15)
Req. No. 14781 Page 4
1 busi ness days. If the request is to terminate a deduction, the
2 school district shall not make any advance payments to any
3 professional organization of any future dues on behalf of the school
4 employee. No school district policy or negotiated agreement shall
5 place any other conditions or requirements, including but not
6 limited to personal hand delivery, upon any employee to initiate or
7 terminate any payroll deduction to a professional organization
8 except for a written request as outlined in this section. Any
9 school district policy or provision in a negotiated agreement that
10 places additional conditions or requirements on initiation or
11 termination beyond those outlined in this section shall be null and
12 void. A school district which fails to terminate payroll deductions
13 for an employee in accordance with this section shall immediately
14 reimburse the employee the amount of all payroll deductions made by
15 the school district after the employee's request, and the amount due
16 the employee shall double every thirty (30) days until paid in full.
17 B. School districts shall not make payroll deductions for any
18 public employee association or organization or professional
19 organization that collectively bargains on behalf of its membership.
20 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 62 O.S. 2021, Section 34.70.1, is
21 amended to read as follows:
22 Section 34.70.1. A. It shall be unlawful for any state agency
23 or school district to make payroll deductions on behalf of a state
24 an employee for membership dues in any public employee association
Req. No. 14781 Page 5
1 or o rganization or professional organization that on or after
2 November 1, 2015 December 31, 2026, collectively bargains on behalf
3 of its membership pursuant to any provision of federal state law.
4 B. For purposes of this section:
5 1. "State agency" means any office, officer, bureau, board,
6 counsel, court, commission, institution, unit, division, body or
7 house of the executive or judicial branches of the state government,
8 whether elected or appointed, excluding political subdivisions of
9 the state. State agency shall include public school districts, the
10 Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education, the institutions,
11 centers, or other constituent agencies of The Oklahoma State System
12 of Higher Education, the State Board of Career and Technology
13 Education, technology center school districts, the State
14 Legislature, and the Office of the Governor; and
15 2. "Public employee" means an elected or appointed officer or
16 employee or contract employee of a state agency as defined in this
17 section, unless otherwise indicated.
18 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
19 SECTION 5. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
20 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
21 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
22 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
23

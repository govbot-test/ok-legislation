6 An Act relating to home service contracts; enacting
the Oklahoma Home Service Act; requiring home
7 warranty companies to post fees; requiring certain
certifications; setting time frame to assess certain
8 claims; providing for codification; and providing an
effective date.
9
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
12 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 7600 of Title 36, unless there
14 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 A. This act shall be known and may be cited as the "Oklahoma
16 Home Service Act".
17 B. Home warranty companies shall make visible in their
18 principal place of business and official company website, if
19 applicable, by posting in an inconspicuous location the following:
20 1. All applicable fees; and
21 2. Detailed pricing for covered modifications, including labor
22 and parts.
23 C. Any employed or contracted repair personnel of a home
24 warranty company shall:
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4139 Page 1

1 1. Be bonded and insured;
2 2. Hold a valid state license; or
3 3. Present documented proof of required training or
4 certifications.
5 D. Any employed or contracted repair personnl of a home
6 warranty company performing work regulated by Oklahoma professional
7 and occupational licensing and registration laws shall comply with
8 all Oklahoma state licensing and registration laws, including
9 holding a current license or registration in good standing.
10 E. Home warranty companies shall provide all specific licenses,
11 training standards, or certifications required of hired contractors
12 and employees to the policyholder within twenty-four (24) hours upon
13 request.
14 F. Home warranty companies shall arrange the appropriate
15 representative to assess any covered warranty item no later than
16 forty-eight (48) hours after a claim is submitted.
17 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

12 Section 1175.8. A. If the medical supervisor reports that the

16 been achieved, the criminal proceedings shall be resumed.

18 Services or its designee wishes to administer medication, including
19 psychotropic medication, to a person in custody under the provisions
20 of subsection A of Section 1175.6a of this title and has reason to

3 an order authorizing medication for purposes of competency
4 restoration with the court. Any such application shall also seek
5 authorization to continue medication for purposes of maintaining the
6 level of restoration while incarcerated following competency
7 restoration.
8 C. An application seeking authorization of medication shall
9 indicate:
10 1. If the treating physician of the person believes the person
11 lacks the capacity to make a decision regarding administration of
12 the medication and the reasons for that belief;
13 2. A summary of the individualized treatment plan of the
14 person, including the class of medications to be potentially
15 administered and the corresponding dosage ranges;
16 3. The diagnosis of the person made by the treating physician;
17 and
18 4. The proposed method for administering the medication and, if
19 the method is not customary, an explanation justifying the departure
20 from the customary method.
21 D. The hearing on the application shall be held no later than
22 thirty (30) days after the filing of the application, unless good
23 cause is shown.

1 E. A person for whom an application for an order to authorize

14 6. Request from the court an independent expert; and

17 F. The administration of medication shall not be ordered unless
18 the petitioning party proves by clear and convincing evidence that:
19 1. There exists an important state interest that justifies

15 medication.

8 An Act relating to construction zones; defining term;
requiring the Department of Public Safety and
9 Department of Transportation to place certain devices
and signage in construction zones; stating allowable
10 use; providing permissible image retention;
prohibiting certain uses; requiring certain
11 calibration; requiring certain audit; providing
penalty; providing for codification; and providing an
12 effective date.
13
14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11-806.3 of Title 47, unless
18 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 A. For the purposes of this section, “automated enforcement
20 device” means a system operated by a law enforcement agency in this
21 state that:
22 1. Is capable of detecting a speeding violation; and
23

1 2. Photographs or records an image of the vehicle used in
2 committing a speeding violation, the operator of the vehicle, or the
3 license plate of the vehicle.
4 B. 1. The Department of Public Safety and the Department of
5 Transportation may place automated enforcement devices at the start
6 of a construction zone. A sign shall be placed one hundred (100)
7 feet before the automated enforcement device to warn drivers that
8 their speed will be monitored and an image may be captured while
9 entering the construction zone.
10 2. The automated enforcement device shall only capture an image
11 and alert a peace officer if a driver is exceeding the speed limit
12 in a work zone in excess of ten (10) miles per hour of a speed limit
13 violation.
14 3. Any image captured but not used as evidence must be deleted
15 from the automated enforcement device within fifteen (15) minutes.
16 4. Any image captured that is used as evidence of a speed limit
17 violation shall be deleted within twenty-four (24) hours of
18 resolution of the citation.
19 5. The Department of Public Safety shall not retain, except as
20 required in this subsection, sell, or transfer any image captured by
21 an automated enforcement device.
22 6. The automated enforcement device shall only be used to
23 capture an image and shall not be used for purposes of
24 identification or analysis.

1 7. The automated enforcement device shall be calibrated every
2 time its position is changed. The results of the calibration shall
3 be made available to the public.
4 8. An independent audit of the calibration records and image
5 retention shall be conducted at least twice a year for each
6 automated enforcement device. If an audit reveals that images were
7 retained in violation of this subsection, the vendor of the device
8 shall be fined One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00) per image.
9 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

7 An Act relating to ticket sales; creating the
7 Oklahoma Fraud and Ticketing Accountability Act;
8 providing short title; amending Sections 1 and 2,
8 Chapter 218, O.S.L. 2023 (15 O.S. Supp. 2025,
9 Sections 774 and 774.1), which relate to definitions,
9 and prohibited use or sale of software to be used on
10 ticket sellers’ websites; updating statutory
10 reference; updating statutory language; defining
11 terms; prohibiting use or creation of certain bot;
11 requiring certain seller to disclose certain
12 requirements at certain point; prohibiting certain
12 seller from offering or selling certain ticket;
13 prohibiting certain resale of certain admission
13 ticket; allowing certain persons to restrict
14 transferability; allowing venue operators to enforce
14 certain policies; prohibiting certain seller from
15 facilitating sale of ticket above certain price;
15 requiring certain seller to issue certain refund;
16 allowing nonprofit organization to offer certain
16 choices for refund of certain ticket; requiring
17 certain venue to provide certain contact information
17 for the Attorney General; prohibiting certain use or
18 display without authorization; prohibiting
18 implication of certain affiliations; establishing
19 certain violations as unfair or deceptive trade
19 practice subject to the Oklahoma Consumer Protection
20 Act; allowing certain persons to bring certain civil
20 action; construing provisions; providing for
21 noncodification; providing for codification; and
21 providing an effective date.

2 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
2
3 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
3
4 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Oklahoma Fraud
4
5 and Ticketing Accountability Act”.

7 2023 (15 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 774), is amended to read as
7
8 follows:
8
9 Section 774. As used in this act Section 774 et seq. of this
9
10 title:
10
11 1. “Admission ticket” means any physical, electronic, or other
11
12 form of evidence of a right of entry to a venue or an entertainment
12
13 event for one or more events at a specified date and time;
13
14 2. “Bot” means any machine, device, computer program,
14
15 artificial intelligence, or software that bypasses security measures
15
16 or ticket purchase limits, alone or with human assistance, to
16
17 acquire admission tickets on a retail platform;
17
18 3. “Event” means a concert, theatrical performance, sporting
18
19 event, exhibition, show, or other similar activity held in this
19
20 state;
20
21 4. “Face value” means a price printed on the admission ticket
21
22 or listed by the primary ticket seller at the point of initial sale,
22
23 excluding taxes, service fees, or delivery charges;
23
24
24

1 3. 5. “Initial sale” means the first sale of an admission
1
2 ticket by the ticket seller, which may include the distribution of
2
3 admission tickets under an agreement between the ticker ticket
3
4 seller and the recipient;
4
5 4. 6. “Person” means any individual, partnership, corporation,
5
6 limited liability company, other organization, or any combination
6
7 thereof;
7
8 5. 7. “Place of entertainment” means any privately or publicly
8
9 owned or operated entertainment facility within this state, such as
9
10 a theater, stadium, museum, arena, park, racetrack, or other place
10
11 where concerts, theatrical performances, sporting events,
11
12 exhibitions, shows, or other similar activities are held and for
12
13 which an entry fee is charged;
13
14 6. 8. “Presale” means a sale of admission tickets at or below
14
15 the price printed on the ticket by, or with the permission of, a
15
16 ticket seller, prior to their release to the general public;
16
17 9. “Primary ticket seller” means a venue, promoter, or provider
17
18 authorized to sell tickets to the public at the point of initial
18
19 sale including, but not limited to, public school districts and
19
20 nonprofit organizations;
20
21 7. 10. “Promoter” means a person who organizes financing and
21
22 publicity for an entertainment event; and
22
23 11. “Resale” means any second or subsequent sale of an
23
24 admission ticket, by any method including, but not limited to, in-
24

1 pers on, telephone, electronic mail, facsimile, or electronic
1
2 platforms. Resale shall not include tickets returned to or
2
3 exchanged by the primary ticket seller;
3
4 12. “Reseller” means a person or entity that offers or sells an
4
5 admission ticket after its initial sale;
5
6 13. “Secondary ticket exchange” means a marketplace, online
6
7 platform, or other entity facilitating resale of admission tickets;
7
8 14. “Speculative ticket” means an admission ticket offered for
8
9 sale that the reseller does not own, possess, or have the
9
10 contractual right to deliver;
10
11 15. “Sports” means any athletic competition, contest, or event
11
12 in which individuals or teams participate in games, matches, or
12
13 exhibitions of skill, agility, strength, or endurance whether
13
14 amateur, collegiate, or professional. Sports shall not mean
14
15 theatrical, musical, or other live entertainment performances, even
15
16 if occurring in a sports venue;
16
17 8. 16. “Ticket seller” means a person that makes admission
17
18 tickets available, directly or indirectly, at an initial presale or
18
19 sale to the general public, and may include an owner or operator of
19
20 a place of entertainment, a sponsor or promoter of an event, a
20
21 sports team participating in an event, a theater company, a musical
21
22 group, or similar participant in an event, or an employee or agent
22
23 of any such person; and
23
24
24

1 17. “Total price” means the price paid for an admission ticket,
1
2 including all mandatory fees, service charges, and taxes, and
2
3 excluding shipping charges and government-imposed fees.

4
5 2023 (15 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 774.1), is amended to read as
5
6 follows:
6
7 Section 774.1. A. A person shall not:
7
8 1. Use software or a bot to circumvent, thwart, interfere with,
8
9 or evade a security measure, access control system, or other control
9
10 measure on a ticket seller’s Internet website; or
10
11 2. Sell software or a bot that is advertised for profit with
11
12 the express purpose to circumvent, thwart, interfere with, or evade
12
13 a security measure, access control system, or other control or
13
14 measure on a ticket seller’s Internet website; or
14
15 3. Use or create a bot to:
15
16 a. purchase tickets for any single Internet admission
16
17 ticket sale,
17
18 b. use multiple Internet protocol (IP) addresses,
18
19 purchaser accounts, or electronic mail addresses to
19
20 exceed posted ticket limits, or
20
21 c. circumvent electronic queues, presale codes, sales
21
22 volume limitations, security or access control
22
23 measures.
23
24
24
Req. No. 2522 Page 5
1 B. The user or seller shall be in violation of subsection A of
1
2 this section if the user or seller knows or should know that:
2
3 1. The purpose of the software or bot is to circumvent, thwart,
3
4 interfere with, or evade a security measure, access control system,
4
5 or other control or measure on a ticket seller’s Internet website in
5
6 order to purchase admission tickets during the initial sale or
6
7 presale for an event held at a place of entertainment; or
7
8 2. The use of the software or bot led to the admission tickets
8
9 being resold on the initial seller’s Internet website or an Internet
9
10 site used for the resell resale of admission tickets for a price
10
11 above the admission ticket’s initial cost.
11
12 C. Any violation of this section shall constitute an unlawful
12
13 business unfair or deceptive trade practice and shall be subject to
13
14 the provisions of the Oklahoma Consumer Protection Act.
14
15 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 774.2 of Title 15, unless there
16
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17
18 A. A primary ticket seller, secondary ticket exchange, or
18
19 reseller shall clearly and conspicuously disclose at the point of
19
20 selection:
20
21 1. The total price, including all mandatory fees and taxes;
21
22 2. Itemization of base price, face value, service fees, and
22
23 taxes; and
23
24
24
Req. No. 2522 Page 6
1 3. Seat location or section, row, and seat number, if
1
2 applicable.
2
3 B. 1. No reseller shall offer or sell a speculative ticket or
3
4 more than one copy of the same ticket for an event.
4
5 2. Admission tickets shall not be resold prior to the initial
5
6 public sale. Complimentary admission tickets shall not be resold by
6
7 ticket exchanges.
7
8 3. Artists, venues, and private ticketing providers may
8
9 restrict transferability if terms and conditions are clearly
9
10 disclosed and acknowledged by the purchaser prior to sale.
10
11 4. Tickets shall be considered licenses. Venue operators may
11
12 enforce policies regarding conduct, behavior, public health, safety,
12
13 or age, and may establish limits on the quantity of tickets
13
14 purchased.
14
15 5. No primary ticket seller, secondary ticket exchange, or
15
16 reseller shall sell, offer, or facilitate resale of any admission
16
17 ticket at a total price greater than one hundred ten percent (110%)
17
18 of the total price of the original ticket, including applicable fees
18
19 and taxes. This paragraph shall not apply to charitable fundraising
19
20 events, school auctions, or other fundraising activities with
20
21 explicit written permission from the place of entertainment,
21
22 promoter, venue, or artist to exceed ticket resale above one hundred
22
23 ten percent (110%).
23
24
24
Req. No. 2522 Page 7
1 6. For admission tickets purchased for a series of events,
1
2 resale of a single event admission ticket shall not exceed the total
2
3 price of a comparable admission ticket, including fees and taxes.
3
4 C. 1. A primary ticket seller, secondary ticket exchange, or
4
5 reseller shall issue a full refund within ten (10) days if the:
5
6 a. event is canceled,
6
7 b. ticket is counterfeit or invalid,
7
8 c. ticket location does not conform to the description
8
9 provided at the time of purchase, or
9
10 d. ticket is not made available prior to the event.
10
11 2. Pursuant to paragraph 1 of this subsection, a nonprofit
11
12 organization may offer the choice to donate the cost of the
12
13 admission ticket to the nonprofit organization, exchange to another
13
14 performance or event, or receive a refund subject to the terms of
14
15 the organization’s refund policy.
15
16 3. The venue shall provide contact information for the Attorney
16
17 General if the purchaser presents an invalid ticket.
17
18 4. Nothing in this subsection shall be construed to alter any
18
19 rights or obligations under federal payment card laws including, but
19
20 not limited to, Regulation E of 12 C.F.R., Part 1005.
20
21 D. No person, resale platform, reseller, or operator of any
21
22 website purporting to sell or offer for sale event tickets shall:
22
23
23
24
24
Req. No. 2522 Page 8
1 1. Use or display, without authorization, the name, mark, logo,
1
2 likeness, or intellectual property of an artist, venue, or event
2
3 organizer in promotional materials, social media, or advertisement;
3
4 2. Imply affiliation or endorsement with a venue, team, or
4
5 artist, including the use of the word “official”, unless authorized
5
6 in writing; or
6
7 3. Engage in commercial representations that are reasonably
7
8 likely to mislead consumers.
8
9 E. Any violation of this act shall constitute an unfair or
9
10 deceptive trade practice and shall be subject to the provisions of
10
11 the Oklahoma Consumer Protection Act.
11
12 F. Any aggrieved person may bring a civil action for actual
12
13 damages and restitution. The court may award court costs and
13
14 attorney fees to a prevailing party.
14
15 G. Nothing in this act shall be construed to include sports in
15
16 the provisions of this act.
16
17 SECTION 5. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

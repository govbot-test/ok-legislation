6 An Act relating to higher education; amending 70 O.S.
2021, Section 4663, as amended by Section 1, Chapter
7 230, O.S.L. 2024 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 4663),
which relates to the Board of Trustees for Oklahoma
8 State University/Tulsa; removing requirement for the
Board to promulgate certain rules and enact certain
9 policies; removing outdated language; removing
language regarding presidential search process;
10 providing an effective date; and declaring an
emergency.
11
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 4663, as
15 amended by Section 1, Chapter 230, O.S.L. 2024 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025,
16 Section 4663), is amended to read as follows:
17 Section 4663. A. On January 1, 1999, there shall be created
18 the Board of Trustees for Oklahoma State University/Tulsa which
19 shall consist of seven members who reside in Tulsa County or
20 contiguous counties, one member of the Board of Regents for the
21 Oklahoma Agricultural and Mechanical Colleges (A&M Board) who shall
22 reside in the First Congressional District, and one member who shall
23 be appointed by the chair of the A&M Board. The member of the A&M
24 Board and the member appointed by the chair of the A&M Board shall
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1593 Page 1
1 serv e as voting, ex officio members. For purposes of serving on the
2 Board of Trustees, the member of the A&M Board and the member
3 appointed by the chair of the A&M Board who are appointed to the
4 Board of Trustees shall not be subject to the dual office holding
5 restrictions in Section 6 of Title 51 of the Oklahoma Statutes. The
6 Governor shall appoint the other members of the Board of Trustees
7 with the advice and consent of the Senate. The members shall be
8 appointed to numbered positions with staggered terms to expire as
9 provided in this section. Successors to the initial board members
10 shall serve seven-year terms that shall expire on June 30 of the
11 seventh year following appointment. Vacancies on the Board shall be
12 filled for the unexpired term by the Governor with the advice and
13 consent of the Senate. The member of the A&M Board who resides in
14 the First Congressional District shall hold position number eight.
15 The member appointed by the chair of the A&M Board shall hold
16 position number nine. The numbered positions and terms shall be as
17 follows:
18 1. Position No. 1: The term of office of one member shall
19 expire on the 30th day of June, 2000, and each seven (7) years
20 thereafter;
21 2. Position No. 2: The term of office of one member shall
22 expire on the 30th day of June, 2001, and each seven (7) years
23 thereafter;
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1593 Page 2
1 3. Position No. 3: The term of office of one member shall
2 expire on the 30th day of June, 2002, and each seven (7) years
3 thereafter;
4 4. Position No. 4: The term of office of one member shall
5 expire on the 30th day of June, 2003, and each seven (7) years
6 thereafter;
7 5. Position No. 5: The term of office of one member shall
8 expire on the 30th day of June, 2004, and each seven (7) years
9 thereafter;
10 6. Position No. 6: The term of office of one member shall
11 expire on the 30th day of June, 2005, and each seven (7) years
12 thereafter;
13 7. Position No. 7: The term of office of one member shall
14 expire on the 30th day of June, 2006, and each seven (7) years
15 thereafter;
16 8. Position No. 8: The term of office shall coincide with the
17 member’s term on the A&M Board; and
18 9. Position No. 9: The term of office shall be at the
19 direction of the chair of the A&M Board.
20 B. The Board of Trustees shall be a body corporate and shall
21 adopt and use an official seal. The Board shall elect a chair, vice
22 chair, and secretary annually, each of whom shall serve for a term
23 of one fiscal year and until a successor is elected and qualified,
24 and who shall perform such duties as the Board directs. The Board
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1593 Page 3
1 shal l adopt rules which it deems necessary for the operation of the
2 Board and the discharge of its duties and shall cause the minutes of
3 all meetings to be kept. The Board shall comply with the Oklahoma
4 Open Meeting Act and the Oklahoma Open Records Act.
5 C. The Board of Trustees for Oklahoma State University/Tulsa
6 may be authorized by the A&M Board to supervise and manage the
7 campus of Oklahoma State University/Tulsa and perform other
8 functions as necessary. The Board of Trustees shall promulgate
9 rules and enact policies to govern the processes and procedures of
10 the Board. It is the intent of the Legislature that in the
11 interests of immediate responsiveness to the needs of students,
12 employees, and the business community that the decision or approval
13 process be expedited.
14 D. On or after January 1, 1999, the A&M Board may assign and
15 delineate roles and responsibilities for the Board of Trustees for
16 Oklahoma State University/Tulsa. Such responsibilities may include,
17 but are not limited to, student services, facility operations,
18 student financial services, budget preparations, endowed chairs at
19 Oklahoma State University/Tulsa, and administrative operations as
20 specified by the A&M Board.
21 E. The Board of Trustees shall establish the usual and
22 customary accounts established by higher education institutions
23 within the Office of Management and Enterprise Services necessary to
24 carry out its duties.
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1593 Page 4
1 F. Until July 1, 2001, during the transition and development of
2 Oklahoma State University/Tulsa, acting on behalf of the Board of
3 Trustees for Oklahoma State University/Tulsa, Oklahoma State
4 University is authorized and directed to negotiate contracts with
5 institutions for courses and degree programs of study approved by
6 the Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education, as necessary to
7 meet the higher education needs for the Tulsa metropolitan area.
8 The number of degree programs offered by Northeastern State
9 University and Langston University shall not be diminished or
10 duplicated.
11 G. The president of Oklahoma State University/Tulsa shall be
12 selected by the Board of Regents for the Oklahoma Agricultural and
13 Mechanical Colleges after completion of a search process as set
14 forth in this subsection. The A&M Board shall appoint at least two
15 of its members, one of whom must reside in the First Congressional
16 District, and the Board of Trustees shall appoint at least two of
17 its members to a search committee. The president of Oklahoma State
18 University may appoint other representatives of faculty, staff, and
19 students to the search committee. The president of Oklahoma State
20 University shall recommend a candidate in consultation with the
21 search committee to the Board of Trustees and the A&M Board.
22 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
23 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
24 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1593 Page 5
1 decl ared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
2 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
3 Passed the Senate the 11th day of March, 2026.

9 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
10 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 3 O.S. 2021, Section 421, as last
11 amended by Section 3, Chapter 237, O.S.L. 2025 (3 O.S. Supp. 2025,
12 Section 421), is amended to read as follows:
13 Section 421. A. The Oklahoma Department of Aerospace and
14 Aeronautics is hereby established as the clearinghouse for unmanned
15 aircraft systems (UAS) and advanced air mobility (AAM) in this state
16 and shall be designated as the agency of this state for the
17 promotion, enhancement, and development of UAS and AAM as well as
18 any associated infrastructure necessary to ensure the safe
19 integration and use of this new technology within the state. The
20 purpose of this clearinghouse is to create a partnership between
21 those entities that currently operate UAS, those that desire to use
22 this technology in the future, and other entities that can support
23 the research and development of UAS to ensure that this state can
24 more effectively respond to the needs of this critical sector of the
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1531 Page 1
1 avia tion and aerospace industry. In the operation of this
2 clearinghouse, the Department shall cooperate, assist, and
3 coordinate with the federal government, agencies of this state,
4 tribal entities, municipalities, and other persons in the
5 development of unmanned aircraft systems throughout the state to
6 ensure the acceptance of this technology and the successful
7 integration of UAS into the National Airspace System. Contingent
8 upon the availability of funds, the Oklahoma Department of Aerospace
9 and Aeronautics may use established program processes or may
10 contract with other qualified entities to carry out the duties and
11 responsibilities of the Unmanned Aircraft Systems Development Act of
12 2021.
13 B. The primary goal of the clearinghouse within the Department
14 is to establish a central point within state government to develop
15 and implement the strategy for how this state can become a leader in
16 the UAS and AAM industry. It will focus the collective resources,
17 knowledge, information, and assets within state government to ensure
18 coordinated efforts amongst all parties. The clearinghouse will
19 have the authority to:
20 1. Conduct research on what other states and localities are
21 doing insofar as their UAS rules and regulations so that it can
22 provide recommendations to ensure this state is in the best position
23 within the industry;
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1531 Page 2
1 2. Organize and coordinate the application for any UAS and AAM
2 test site, integration opportunity, pilot program, or grant funding
3 on behalf of this state;
4 3. Maintain a registry of UAS being operated by state agencies,
5 except those UAS that are part of a university-affiliated research
6 program;
7 4. Maintain a registry of educational institutions that offer
8 training programs for users of UAS;
9 5. Investigate the development of, and if necessary, create a
10 statewide system plan that will provide the framework for the
11 construction, development, siting, and potential partnerships
12 required for vertiports and other infrastructure needed to integrate
13 AAM and UAS into the existing air transportation system of the
14 state;
15 6. Develop a statewide network of UAS and AAM detection systems
16 as necessary to support the safe integration of these technologies
17 into the state’s existing air transportation system; and
18 7. Create statewide or regional command, control, and radar
19 systems or centers to carry out the provisions of paragraph 6 of
20 this subsection and the Department’s efforts to bring UAS and AAM
21 testing and development to the state. These systems or centers can
22 be stand-alone or in partnership with other federal, state, local,
23 tribal, or non-profit entities;
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1531 Page 3
1 8. Invest in publicly owned infrastructure to support the
2 mission of the clearinghouse and the provisions of this section; and
3 9. Partner and collaborate with other states and industry
4 stakeholders as necessary to create regional or technical centers of
5 excellence with an interest in integration for UAS and AAM
6 technologies.
7 C. The Department is authorized to enter into partnerships with
8 any city or town of this state and any county or political
9 subdivision or district in this state, or any public trust thereof,
10 or any federal government entity for the purpose of investing in and
11 operating infrastructure and any other items necessary to safely and
12 effectively integrate AAM and UAS into the existing air
13 transportation system of this state as well as the testing and
14 development of these aeronautical technologies.
15 D. The Department is authorized to promulgate any rules
16 necessary to implement the provisions of the Unmanned Aircraft
17 Systems Development Act of 2021.
18 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

15 "An Act relating to aerospace, aircraft, and aviation
infrastructure; amending 3 O.S. 2021, Section 421, as
16 last amended by Section 3, Chapter 237, O.S.L. 2025
(3 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 421), which relates to
17 the clearinghouse for unmanned aircraft systems;
authorizing the Oklahoma Department of Aerospace and
18 Aeronautics to take certain actions; providing for
the promulgation of rules; and providing an effective
19 date.

ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1531 Page 1
1 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 3 O.S. 2021, Section 421, as last
2 amended by Section 3, Chapter 237, O.S.L. 2025 (3 O.S. Supp. 2025,
3 Section 421), is amended to read as follows:
4 Section 421. A. The Oklahoma Department of Aerospace and
5 Aeronautics is hereby established as the clearinghouse for unmanned
6 aircraft systems (UAS) and advanced air mobility (AAM) in this state
7 and shall be designated as the agency of this state for the
8 promotion, enhancement, and development of UAS and AAM as well as
9 any associated infrastructure necessary to ensure the safe
10 integration and use of this new technology within the state. The
11 purpose of this clearinghouse is to create a partnership between
12 those entities that currently operate UAS, those that desire to use
13 this technology in the future, and other entities that can support
14 the research and development of UAS to ensure that this state can
15 more effectively respond to the needs of this critical sector of the
16 aviation and aerospace industry. In the operation of this
17 clearinghouse, the Department shall cooperate, assist, and
18 coordinate with the federal government, agencies of this state,
19 tribal entities, municipalities, and other persons in the
20 development of unmanned aircraft systems throughout the state to
21 ensure the acceptance of this technology and the successful
22 integration of UAS into the National Airspace System. Contingent
23 upon the availability of funds, the Oklahoma Department of Aerospace
24 and Aeronautics may use established program processes or may
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1531 Page 2
1 cont ract with other qualified entities to carry out the duties and
2 responsibilities of the Unmanned Aircraft Systems Development Act of
3 2021.
4 B. The primary goal of the clearinghouse within the Department
5 is to establish a central point within state government to develop
6 and implement the strategy for how this state can become a leader in
7 the UAS and AAM industry. It will focus the collective resources,
8 knowledge, information, and assets within state government to ensure
9 coordinated efforts amongst all parties. The clearinghouse will
10 have the authority to:
11 1. Conduct research on what other states and localities are
12 doing insofar as their UAS rules and regulations so that it can
13 provide recommendations to ensure this state is in the best position
14 within the industry;
15 2. Organize and coordinate the application for any UAS and AAM
16 test site, integration opportunity, pilot program, or grant funding
17 on behalf of this state;
18 3. Maintain a registry of UAS being operated by state agencies,
19 except those UAS that are part of a university-affiliated research
20 program;
21 4. Maintain a registry of educational institutions that offer
22 training programs for users of UAS;
23 5. Investigate the development of, and if necessary, create a
24 statewide system plan that will provide the framework for the
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1531 Page 3
1 cons truction, development, siting, and potential partnerships
2 required for vertiports and other infrastructure needed to integrate
3 AAM and UAS into the existing air transportation system of the
4 state;
5 6. Develop a statewide network of UAS and AAM detection systems
6 as necessary to support the safe integration of these technologies
7 into the state’s existing air transportation system; and
8 7. Create statewide or regional command, control, and radar
9 systems or centers to carry out the provisions of paragraph 6 of
10 this subsection and the Department’s efforts to bring UAS and AAM
11 testing and development to the state. These systems or centers can
12 be stand-alone or in partnership with other federal, state, local,
13 tribal, or non-profit entities;
14 8. Invest in publicly owned infrastructure to support the
15 mission of the clearinghouse and the provisions of this section; and
16 9. Partner and collaborate with other states and industry
17 stakeholders as necessary to create regional or technical centers of
18 excellence with an interest in integration for UAS and AAM
19 technologies.
20 C. The Department is authorized to enter into partnerships with
21 any city or town of this state and any county or political
22 subdivision or district in this state, or any public trust thereof,
23 or any federal government entity for the purpose of investing in and
24 operating infrastructure and any other items necessary to safely and
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 1531 Page 4
1 effe ctively integrate AAM and UAS into the existing air
2 transportation system of this state as well as the testing and
3 development of these aeronautical technologies.
4 D. The Department is authorized to promulgate any rules
5 necessary to implement the provisions of the Unmanned Aircraft
6 Systems Development Act of 2021.
7 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026."
8 Passed the House of Representatives the 29th day of April, 2026.

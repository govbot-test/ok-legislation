3 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 19 O.S. 2021, Section 868.3, is
4 amended to read as follows:
5 Section 868.3. A. The county board of adjustment shall be
6 composed of three (3), five (5), or seven (7) resident property
7 owners of the county to be appointed by the board of county
8 commissioners. At least one member shall be a resident of the
9 county seat of such county and at least one member shall be duly
10 licensed as an attorney in the State of Oklahoma. Members shall be
11 nominated by the county commissioners in as nearly equal number as
12 practicable, and when the total number of authorized positions
13 cannot be divided equally among the three county commissioners, the
14 remaining nomination or nominations shall be allocated by the board
15 of county commissioners.
16 B. The term of each member shall be three (3) two (2) years
17 except that the terms shall be overlapping and the membership of the
18 board first appointed shall be for terms of one (1), two (2) and
19 three (3) years, respectively respective terms of those first
20 appointed may be less than two (2) years. The board of county
21 commissioners is hereby authorized to fill vacancies which may occur
22 in the board and to appoint a substitute or substitutes to serve in
23 a particular case in which a member or members shall certify his
24 their disqualification. Members may be removed by the board of
ENGR. H. A. to ENGR. S. B. NO. 563 Page 3
1 coun ty commissioners for cause after a public hearing held for that
2 purpose, and vacancies shall be filled by additional appointments.
3 All members of the county board of adjustment shall serve as such
4 without compensation.
5 C. The board of county commissioners may retain private legal
6 counsel to represent and advise the county board of adjustment in
7 the performance of its official duties.
8 D. The county board of adjustment shall elect its own chairman
9 and shall adopt rules of procedure consistent with the provisions of
10 the zoning regulations and the provisions of this act. The
11 chairman, or in his absence the acting chairman, may administer
12 oaths and compel the attendance of witnesses. All hearings of the
13 county board of adjustment shall be open to the public, and minutes
14 shall be kept of all proceedings and official actions, which minutes
15 shall be filed in the office of the board and shall be a public
16 record. The board of adjustment shall fix a reasonable time for the
17 hearing of any appeal and every appeal shall be heard within sixty
18 (60) days from the date of filing.
19 E. Notice of hearings before the county board of adjustment
20 shall be given by at least one (1) publication in a newspaper of
21 general circulation in the county and by mail to the record owners
22 of lands contiguous to the affected area at least fifteen (15) days
23 prior to the date of such hearing.
24

1 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 868.23 of Title 19, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 On and after the date a county becomes subject to Sections 868.1
5 et seq. of Title 19 of the Oklahoma Statutes, all comprehensive
6 plans, master plans, zoning regulations, floodplain regulations,
7 subdivision regulations, resolutions, ordinances, codes, and all
8 amendments, supplements, and modifications thereto, previously
9 adopted or enacted pursuant to any other provision of Section 863.1
10 through Section 869.7 of Title 19 of the Oklahoma Statutes, and all
11 actions taken thereunder, are hereby validated, ratified, and
12 continued in full force and effect as if adopted under the authority
13 of Sections 868.1 et seq. of Title 19 of the Oklahoma Statutes,
14 until amended, revised, or replaced by appropriate official action
15 taken in accordance with such sections.
16 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
17 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
18 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
19 be in full force from and after its passage and approval."

13 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 180.91 of Title 19, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 The board of county commissioners may develop a policy in
17 accordance with the secretary of the county election board to
18 authorize county employees to receive up to three (3) working days’
19 paid administrative leave per calendar year for the purpose of
20 serving as a precinct official pursuant to Section 2-124 of Title 26
21 of the Oklahoma Statutes, absentee voting board member pursuant to
22 Section 14-115.5 of Title 26 of the Oklahoma Statutes, or other
23 election worker authorized by law, if the employee is qualified to
24 serve in such position.

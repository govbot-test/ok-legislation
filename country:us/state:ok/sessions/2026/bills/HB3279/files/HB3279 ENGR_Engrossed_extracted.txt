6 An Act relating to state government; amending 74 O.S.
2021, Section 85.42, as amended by Section 9, Chapter
7 336, O.S.L. 2025 (74 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 85.42),
which relates to the Oklahoma Central Purchasing Act;
8 broadening scope of certain prohibited act; deleting
certain contract penalties and employment
9 prohibitions; providing employment restrictions for
state officers or employees under certain
10 circumstances; requiring inclusion of certified
statements for purchase contracts exceeding certain
11 dollar amount; authorizing the appointment of
contract signatory designees; requiring written
12 designations; voiding contracts or agreements for
certain violations; prohibiting persons from entering
13 into state contracts or being rehired for specified
period of time; amending 74 O.S. 2021, Section 590,
14 which relates to the Oklahoma Privatization of State
Functions Act; broadening scope of certain prohibited
15 act; authorizing the Attorney General and state
agencies to terminate certain contracts; increasing
16 time limitation that bars business organizations from
contracting with the state, its agencies, or
17 political subdivisions of the state; allowing the
Attorney General or state agency to pursue monetary
18 damages and certain relief; and providing an
effective date.
19
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
22 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 74 O.S. 2021, Section 85.42, as
23 amended by Section 9, Chapter 336, O.S.L. 2025 (74 O.S. Supp. 2025,
24 Section 85.42), is amended to read as follows:
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3279 Page 1
1 Section 85.42. A. 1. Except as otherwise provided for in this
2 section or other applicable law, any agency, whether or not such
3 agency is subject to the Oklahoma Central Purchasing Act, shall be
4 prohibited from entering into a sole-source contract or agreement or
5 a contract or agreement for professional services with or for the
6 services of any person who has terminated employment with or who has
7 been terminated by that agency for one (1) year after the
8 termination date of the employee from the agency. Any contract or
9 agreement entered into in violation of this subsection shall be
10 void. Any person found to have violated this subsection shall be
11 prohibited from entering into any state contract for a period of
12 five (5) years from the date of the execution of the contract or
13 agreement. The provisions of this subsection shall not prohibit an
14 agency from hiring or rehiring such person as a state employee.
15 2. Any chief administrative officer of an agency, whether or
16 not such agency is subject to the Oklahoma Central Purchasing Act,
17 shall not enter into any contract for nonprofessional or
18 professional services for the purpose of or which would result in
19 the circumvention of the full-time-equivalent employee limitation
20 established by law for such agency.
21 3. Any state officer or employee, who exercises discretionary
22 or decision-making authority in entering into a contract of Twenty-
23 five Thousand Dollars ($25,000.00) or more, shall be prohibited for
24 a period of one (1) year from the date of execution or renewal of
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3279 Page 2
1 the contract from becoming an officer or employee of a business
2 organization which is a party to any contract with the state agency
3 in which the state officer or employee exercised such discretionary
4 or decision-making authority.
5 B. Each contract for purchases of Twenty-five Thousand Dollars
6 ($25,000.00) or more, including change orders, extensions, renewals,
7 or amendments, entered into by any person or firm with the State of
8 Oklahoma shall include a statement certifying that no person who has
9 been materially involved in any manner in the development, approval,
10 or negotiation of such contract while employed by the state shall be
11 employed or given anything of value in consideration of receiving
12 the contract to fulfill any of the services provided for under the
13 contract. Each contract for purchases of Twenty-five Thousand
14 Dollars ($25,000.00) or more, including change orders, extensions,
15 renewals, or amendments, shall also include a statement from the
16 chief executive officer or head of the contracting state agency
17 certifying that no known officer or employee of the contracting
18 state agency who has any direct or indirect financial, pecuniary, or
19 other personal interest in the contract has been involved in any
20 manner in the development, approval, or negotiation of the contract
21 through influence, decision, recommendation, or otherwise. The
22 chief executive officer or head of the contracting state agency may
23 appoint an employee of the agency as a contract signatory designee.
24 Such designation shall be made in writing. However, such
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3279 Page 3
1 desi gnation shall not absolve the chief executive officer or head of
2 the contracting agency from responsibility for implementing the
3 provisions of this subsection. This subsection shall not preclude
4 faculty and staff of the institutions within The Oklahoma State
5 System of Higher Education from negotiating and participating in
6 research grants and educational contracts. This subsection shall
7 not apply to Oklahoma Department of Commerce personnel who contract
8 to provide services to the Oklahoma Capital Investment Board.
9 C. As used in this section, "person" means any state official
10 or employee of a department, board, bureau, commission, agency,
11 trusteeship, authority, council, committee, trust, school district,
12 fair board, court, executive office, advisory group, task force,
13 study group, supported in whole or in part by public funds or
14 entrusted with the expenditure of public funds or administering or
15 operating public property, and all committees, or subcommittees
16 thereof, judges, justices and state legislators.
17 D. Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in this section,
18 the following sole-source or professional services contracts are
19 allowed at any time:
20 1. A contract for professional services at any time with a
21 person who is a qualified interpreter for the deaf; and
22 2. A contract between a business entity that is a part-time
23 certified court reporter and the Administrative Office of the
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3279 Page 4
1 Cour ts, on behalf of the district courts, or the Office of the
2 Attorney General.
3 E. Provided the provisions specified in subsection B of this
4 section are satisfied, the following professional services contracts
5 are allowed:
6 1. The Department of Transportation, Oklahoma Water Resources
7 Board, Department of Environmental Quality, Oklahoma Tourism and
8 Recreation Department, the Oklahoma Turnpike Authority and the
9 Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry may contract
10 with a person who has retired from state service;
11 2. To maintain public health infrastructure and preparedness,
12 the State Department of Health and city-county health departments
13 may contract with a physician assistant, Registered Nurse, advanced
14 practice nurse, Nurse-Midwife, registered dietician, occupational
15 therapist, physical therapist or speech-language pathologist who has
16 retired from state service; and
17 3. The Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services
18 may contract with a physician, Registered Nurse, registered
19 pharmacist or person meeting the definition of a licensed mental
20 health professional, as defined in Title 43A of the Oklahoma
21 Statutes, who has separated and/or retired from state service.
22 F. Any contract or agreement entered into in violation of this
23 section shall be void. Any person found to have violated this
24 section shall be prohibited from entering into any state contract or
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3279 Page 5
1 from being rehired as a state employee for a period of three (3)
2 years from the date of voiding, termination, or cancellation of the
3 contract or agreement.
4 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 74 O.S. 2021, Section 590, is
5 amended to read as follows:
6 Section 590. A. Any state officer or employee who exercises
7 discretionary or decision-making authority in awarding entering into
8 a privatization contract pursuant to the Oklahoma Privatization of
9 State Functions Act, shall be prohibited for a period of one (1)
10 year from the effective date that of the privatization contract is
11 awarded from becoming an officer or employee of a business
12 organization which is a party to any privatization contract with the
13 state agency in which the state officer or employee exercised such
14 discretionary or decision-making authority.
15 B. In the event of a violation of the provisions of subsection
16 A of this section, the:
17 1. The Attorney General or the state agency may terminate the
18 contract with the business organization;
19 2. The Attorney General may terminate any other state contract
20 with the business organization; and
21 3. The business organization shall be prohibited, for a period
22 of one (1) year three (3) years from the date of the violation,
23 employment or association of the former state officer or employee
24 with the business organization, from contracting with the state
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3279 Page 6
1 Stat e of Oklahoma or any agency in which the state officer or state
2 employee exercised discretionary or decision-making authority or
3 political subdivision of the State of Oklahoma.
4 The Attorney General or state agency may pursue monetary damages
5 and declaratory and equitable relief from the business organization
6 for repayment of any funds expended or encumbered pursuant to the
7 contract and any losses attributable to the contract or its
8 termination.
9 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
10 Passed the House of Representatives the 10th day of March, 2026.

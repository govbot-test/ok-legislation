9 An Act relating to financial institutions; amending 6
O.S. 2021, Section 2205, which relates to the
10 Financial Privacy Act; permitting disclosure or
release of information for investigations of
11 financial exploitation of protected adults; defining
terms; requiring employees of certain institutions to
12 notify the institution of suspected financial
exploitation of a protected adult; directing certain
13 institution to notify proper agency; permitting
certain institution to notify trusted contact of
14 suspected financial exploitation of a protected
adult; authorizing temporary holds; requiring
15 provision of certain notice of temporary hold;
requiring provision of certain timeline for temporary
16 hold; exempting notice to trusted contact if contact
is suspected of financial exploitation of the
17 protected adult; providing immunity for certain
institution and employees for compliance with
18 section; requiring retention of certain records;
permitting access to certain records upon
19 certification by an agency that an investigation into
financial exploitation of a protected adult is being
20 undertaken by the agency; authorizing certain
statement containing financial information during
21 certain time frame; requiring requests to comply with
the Financial Privacy Act; providing for
22 codification; and providing an effective date.
23
24 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
SB2067 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 6 O.S. 2021, Section 2205, is
2 amended to read as follows:
3 Section 2205. A. Nothing in the Financial Privacy Act shall
4 prohibit the disclosure or release of any financial record or
5 information to any supervisory agency in the exercise of its
6 supervisory or regulatory functions with respect to a financial
7 institution.
8 B. Nothing in the Financial Privacy Act prohibits a financial
9 institution from disclosing or releasing any financial record or
10 information to another financial institution for the usual and
11 regular business purposes of the latter or from providing copies of
12 any financial record to any court or government authority as an
13 incident to perfecting a security interest, proving a claim in
14 bankruptcy, or otherwise collecting on a debt either owed the
15 financial institution itself or owed the financial institution in
16 its role as a fiduciary.
17 C. Nothing in the Financial Privacy Act prohibits a financial
18 institution from notifying a government authority that such
19 institution or an officer, employee, or agent of such institution
20 has information that may be relevant to a possible violation of any
21 statute or regulation.
22 D. Sections 2201 through 2204 of this title shall not apply to
23 any court order or subpoena issued in connection with proceedings
24 before a multicounty grand jury, except that a court shall have
SB2067 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 auth ority to order a financial institution, on which a multicounty
2 grand jury subpoena for customer records has been served, not to
3 notify the customer of the existence of the subpoena or information
4 that has been furnished to the multicounty grand jury. The court
5 may order that the customer not be notified only if it finds:
6 1. That the requested records are relevant to an ongoing
7 criminal investigation being conducted by the multicounty grand
8 jury; and
9 2. That disclosure of the existence or issuance of, or
10 compliance with the subpoena may frustrate or impede the
11 investigation.
12 E. Nothing in the Financial Privacy Act shall prohibit the
13 disclosure or release of any financial record or information as
14 required by Section 2 of this act.
15 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 3401 of Title 6, unless there is
17 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. As used in this section:
19 1. “Account” means any account of a financial institution or
20 trust business for which a protected adult has the authority to
21 transact business;
22 2. “Agency” means one or more of the following:
23 a. the Department of Human Services,
24
SB2067 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 b. the office of the district attorney in the county in
2 which the suspected exploitation occurred, or
3 c. any state or federal law enforcement agency with
4 jurisdiction over the area in which the suspected
5 exploitation occurred;
6 3. “Financial exploitation” means:
7 a. the wrongful or unauthorized taking, withholding,
8 appropriation, or use of money, assets, or property of
9 a protected adult, or
10 b. any act or omission taken by a person, including
11 through the use of a power of attorney, guardianship,
12 conservatorship, or any other authority, regarding a
13 protected adult, to:
14 (1) obtain control, through the use of intimidation,
15 undue influence, coercion, harassment, duress,
16 deception, false representation, or false
17 pretense, over the protected adult’s money,
18 assets, or property, or
19 (2) convert or divert money, assets, or property of
20 the protected adult to deprive the protected
21 adult of the ownership, use, benefit, or
22 possession of the money, assets, or property;
23 4. “Financial institution” has the same meaning as defined in
24 Section 2202 of Title 6 of the Oklahoma Statutes;
SB2067 HFLR Page 4
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 5. “Institution” means a financial institution or trust
2 business;
3 6. “Protected adult” means:
4 a. an individual sixty-two (62) years of age or older, or
5 b. an incapacitated person or a vulnerable adult as such
6 terms are defined in the Protective Services for
7 Vulnerable Adults Act;
8 7. “Trust business” means the same as defined in Section 1702
9 of Title 6 of the Oklahoma Statutes; and
10 8. “Trusted contact” means any adult person designated by a
11 customer of an institution that the institution may contact in the
12 event of an emergency or loss of contact with the customer, or in
13 the event of suspected third-party fraud or financial exploitation
14 targeting the customer.
15 B. If an employee of an institution reasonably believes that
16 financial exploitation of a protected adult in this state has
17 occurred, is occurring, has been attempted, is being attempted, or
18 will be attempted, the employee shall notify the institution of the
19 suspected financial exploitation. If the institution reasonably
20 believes that financial exploitation has occurred or is occurring,
21 the institution shall promptly notify one or more of the proper
22 agencies pursuant to the provisions of the Protective Services for
23 Vulnerable Adults Act.
24
SB2067 HFLR Page 5
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 C. If an institution believes that financial exploitation of a
2 protected adult has occurred, is occurring, has been attempted, is
3 being attempted, or will be attempted, in or from this state, the
4 institution may notify the trusted contact, or in the absence of
5 such designation, any other third party that the institution
6 reasonably believes is associated with the protected adult.
7 D. 1. An institution transacting business in or from this
8 state with a protected adult may place a temporary hold on a
9 transaction with or a disbursement of funds from an account of such
10 protected adult or an account on which such protected adult is a
11 beneficiary if:
12 a. the institution believes that financial exploitation
13 of a protected adult has occurred, is occurring, has
14 been attempted, is being attempted, or will be
15 attempted, and
16 b. the institution:
17 (1) immediately, but in no event more than three (3)
18 business days after the date the temporary hold
19 is first placed, provides oral or written
20 notification, which may be electronic, of the
21 temporary hold and the reason to all parties
22 authorized to transact business with the account
23 and to any trusted contact, and
24
SB2067 HFLR Page 6
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 (2) immediately initiates an internal review of the
2 suspected or attempted financial exploitation of
3 the protected adult, as necessary.
4 2. An agency or a court of competent jurisdiction may terminate
5 or extend a temporary hold authorized by this subsection. Any
6 temporary hold of a transaction or disbursement of funds as
7 authorized by this subsection shall expire upon the earlier of:
8 a. a determination by the institution that the
9 transaction or disbursement of funds shall not result
10 in financial exploitation of the protected adult, or
11 b. not later than ten (10) business days after the date
12 on which the institution first placed the temporary
13 hold on the transaction or disbursement of funds,
14 unless the institution’s internal review of the facts
15 and circumstances supports the institution’s
16 reasonable belief that financial exploitation of the
17 protected adult has occurred, is occurring, has been
18 attempted, or will be attempted, or the institution
19 receives a request for an extension by one or more of
20 the agencies, in which case the institution may extend
21 the temporary hold to not later than twenty (20)
22 business days after the date the institution first
23 placed the temporary hold on the transaction or
24 disbursement of the funds.
SB2067 HFLR Page 7
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 3. Nothing in this subsection shall require an institution to
2 place a temporary hold on any transaction with or disbursement of
3 funds from an account of a protected adult or an account on which
4 such protected adult is a beneficiary.
5 E. Notwithstanding the provisions of subsections C and D of
6 this section, a notification permitted or required by this section
7 shall not be made to any person the institution reasonably believes
8 has engaged, is engaging, or will engage in suspected or attempted
9 financial exploitation of the protected adult.
10 F. 1. An institution, or any employee of an institution, who
11 makes a notification or report under subsection B of this section,
12 makes a notification or report to a third party under subsection C
13 of this section, provides records to any agency pursuant to
14 subsection G of this section, or testifies or otherwise participates
15 in a judicial proceeding arising from such notification, report, or
16 provision of records, shall be immune from any civil or criminal
17 liability arising from the notification, report, provision of
18 records, testimony, or participation in the judicial proceeding,
19 unless the employee or institution acted in bad faith or with a
20 malicious purpose.
21 2. An institution that places, releases, or does not place a
22 hold on any transaction pursuant to subsection D of this section
23 shall be immune from any civil or criminal liability, as well as any
24 disciplinary action from the Banking Department, from that action or
SB2067 HFLR Page 8
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 fail ure to act, unless the institution acted in bad faith or with a
2 malicious purpose.
3 G. An institution shall retain and, to the extent permitted by
4 state and federal law, may provide access to or copies of records
5 that are relevant to the suspected or attempted financial
6 exploitation of a protected adult to any agency upon the written
7 certification of the agency that an investigation into the financial
8 exploitation of a protected adult is being undertaken by the agency.
9 Upon such certification, the agency may request an institution to
10 furnish, and an institution may, but shall not be required to,
11 furnish, a statement setting forth the following information with
12 respect to a customer account specified by the requesting party for
13 a period of ninety (90) days before and up to sixty (60) days
14 following the date of occurrence of the alleged illegal act
15 involving the account:
16 1. The number of items dishonored;
17 2. The number of items paid that created overdrafts;
18 3. The dollar volume of the dishonored items and items paid
19 which created overdrafts and a statement explaining any credit
20 arrangement between the bank, credit union, or savings association
21 and customer to pay overdrafts;
22 4. The dates and amounts of deposits and debits and the account
23 balance;
24
SB2067 HFLR Page 9
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 5. A copy of the signature card, including the signature and
2 any addresses appearing on a customer’s signature card;
3 6. New bank cards issued;
4 7. Change of address requests received;
5 8. Power of attorney or trust documents submitted or executed;
6 9. The date the account opened and, if applicable, the date the
7 account closed; and
8 10. Surveillance photographs and video recordings of persons
9 accessing the victim’s financial account via an automated teller
10 machine (ATM) or from within the institution for dates on which
11 illegal acts involving the account were alleged to have occurred.
12 This paragraph shall not be construed to:
13 a. require an institution to produce a photograph or
14 video recording if the institution does not possess
15 the photograph or video recording, or
16 b. affect any existing civil immunities under state law.
17 A bank, credit union, or savings association that provides the
18 requesting party with copies of one or more complete account
19 statements prepared in the regular course of business shall be
20 deemed to be in compliance with paragraphs 1 through 4 of this
21 subsection.
22 H. Any request for information from an agency, not including
23 the provisions of this section, shall comply with the Financial
24 Privacy Act. Nothing in subsection G of this section shall be
SB2067 HFLR Page 10
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 cons trued to prohibit an institution from requiring the agencies to
2 comply with the Financial Privacy Act.
3 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

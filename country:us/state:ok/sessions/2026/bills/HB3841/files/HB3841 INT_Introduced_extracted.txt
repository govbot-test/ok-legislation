6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to public finance; amending 62 O.S.
2021, Sections 851, 853, and 855, which relate to the
8 Local Development Act; modifying definitions;
modifying references to blight; modifying procedures
9 for approval of certain district, plan or project;
requiring submission of question to voters of
10 applicable jurisdiction; requiring approval of
district, plan or project by majority vote; modifying
11 provisions related to supermajority approval by
governing board; requiring separate approval by local
12 taxing jurisdictions; modifying provisions related to
confidential information; prohibiting members of
13 review committees from receiving things of value;
requiring members of review committees to complete
14 certain instruction; requiring annual meetings of
review committees; requiring for certain
15 presentations to review committees; requiring review
committees to obtain certain professional opinions;
16 imposing limitation based upon certain advice
provided to governing body or other entities;
17 requiring review committee to obtain certain
information; requiring economic impact statement;
18 providing for codification; and providing an
effective date.

11 Section 855. A. Prior to the adoption and approval of a
12 project plan and the ordinance or resolution required under Section
13 856 of this title and prior to the public hearing required under

17 committee shall consist of the following: a representative of the
18 governing body who shall serve as chairperson; a representative of
19 the planning commission having jurisdiction over the proposed
20 district; a representative designated by each taxing jurisdiction
21 within the proposed district whose ad valorem taxes might be
22 impacted according to the plan; and three members representing the
23 public at large and selected by the other committee members from a
24 list of seven names submitted by the chairperson of the review
Req. No. 15926 Page 10

1 comm ittee; provided, at least one of the members representing the
2 public at large shall be a representative of the business community
3 in the city, town, or county considering the proposed plan and
4 project, and if a proposed plan objective is development of
5 principally commercial retail, such representative shall be either a
6 retailer or a representative of a retail organization.
7 B. The review committee shall consider and make its findings
8 and recommendations to the governing body with respect to the
9 conditions establishing the eligibility of the proposed district.
10 The review committee recommendations shall include the analysis used

12 the taxing entities and the appropriateness of the approval of the

17 committee shall consider and determine whether the proposed plan and

21 concurrent with or subsequent to the review and consideration of the
22 committee provided for in subsection B of this section. The
23 approval of any district plan or project by the governing body shall
24 address any findings of such impact by the review committee.

12 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 855.1-A of Title 62, unless
14 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 A. No member of a review committee shall accept anything of
16 value from a person, firm, partnership, limited partnership, limited
17 liability partnership, limited liability company, corporation, or
18 other legal entity that would benefit, directly or indirectly, from
19 the formation of an incentive district or an increment district.
20 B. No member of a review committee shall accept anything of
21 value from any person or legal entity acting on behalf of an entity
22 described in subsection A of this section.
23 C. Before a review committee votes to recommend the creation of
24 an incentive district or increment district pursuant to the

1 prov isions of the Local Development Act, each member of the
2 committee shall be required to complete at least twelve (12) hours
3 of instruction which includes the provisions of the Local
4 Development Act, applicable concepts related to the utilization of
5 sales tax revenue or other locally authorized revenues, including ad
6 valorem tax revenue, in either an incentive district or an increment
7 district. The provider for the instruction shall issue a
8 certificate of completion to a person who successfully completes the
9 course of instruction required by this subsection.
10 D. A review committee shall be required to meet at least once
11 each calendar year.
12 E. Before a review committee makes a recommendation to the
13 applicable governing body for the creation of an incentive district

1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
1
2 2nd Session of the 60th Legislature (2026)
2
3
4
4
5
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
6
7 An Act relating to schools; requiring school district
7 boards of education to adopt certain policy;
8 requiring boards of education to provide certain
8 program on sex trafficking and exploitation
9 prevention and awareness to certain students;
9 directing the State Department of Education to
10 develop certain curriculum on human trafficking;
10 requiring curriculum for certain grades to address
11 specific topics; directing the Department to develop
11 certain curriculum on social media; allowing
12 collaboration to develop curriculum; requiring school
12 district to post curriculum on district’s website for
13 public inspection; requiring curriculum to be
13 provided to students in certain grades in certain
14 manner; requiring written notification of curriculum;
14 allowing boards of education to partner with certain
15 organizations and law enforcement agencies; directing
15 the Department to develop and make available to the
16 public certain education video that addresses certain
16 topics; directing the Department to identify certain
17 resources and provide certain units or lesson plans;
17 providing for codification; and providing an
18 effective date.
18
19
19
20
20
21 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
21
22 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 24-100.11 of Title 70, unless
23
24 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 2528 Page 1
1 A. Each school district board of education in this state shall
1
2 adopt a policy regarding sex trafficking and exploitation prevention
2
3 and education programs for students.
3
4 B. Each school district board of education in this state shall
4
5 provide schoolwide education programs to all students in grades six
5
6 through twelve and staff addressing sex trafficking and exploitation
6
7 prevention and awareness.
7
8 C. The State Department of Education shall develop and make
8
9 available to every public school that serves sixth through twelfth
9
10 grade in this state curriculum that shall be taught as a stand-alone
10
11 unit of instruction regarding human trafficking with an emphasis on
11
12 sex trafficking. The curriculum shall be age appropriate and shall
12
13 include an emphasis on exploitation prevention. For students in
13
14 grades nine through twelve, curriculum shall specifically include
14
15 the following:
15
16 1. Definitions and nature of sex trafficking and exploitation;
16
17 2. Current laws on sex trafficking and exploitation in this
17
18 state;
18
19 3. Facts and statistics of sex trafficking and exploitation in
19
20 this state and throughout the United States;
20
21 4. How sex traffickers profile victims;
21
22 5. The role of online computer activity;
22
23 6. How demand is created and the industries that influence sex
23
24 trafficking and exploitation;
24
Req. No. 2528 Page 2
1 7. How to identify a potential victim and what to do when a
1
2 person identifies a victim;
2
3 8. Understanding force, fraud, and coercion;
3
4 9. Understanding how traffickers groom a victim;
4
5 10. The differences between prostitution and sex trafficking
5
6 and exploitation;
6
7 11. The role that pornography plays in sex trafficking and
7
8 exploitation;
8
9 12. Understanding the complexities of abuse, indoctrination,
9
10 and manipulation;
10
11 13. Understanding the physiological makeup of a sex trafficking
11
12 victim;
12
13 14. Rescuing victims of sex trafficking and exploitation; and
13
14 15. How to engage in the fight against sex trafficking and
14
15 exploitation.
15
16 D. The State Department of Education shall develop and make
16
17 available to every public school that serves sixth through twelfth
17
18 grade in this state curriculum that shall be taught as a stand-alone
18
19 unit of instruction regarding social media and how students may best
19
20 protect themselves from exploitation when using social media and
20
21 internet services including, but not limited to, online gaming, chat
21
22 rooms, and email. The curriculum shall be age appropriate and shall
22
23 be updated annually to respond to changes in social media.
23
24
24
Req. No. 2528 Page 3
1 E. The State Department of Education may collaborate with
1
2 outside organizations including law enforcement agencies to develop
2
3 the curriculum required by this section at no cost to the State
3
4 Department of Education. The curriculum shall be provided at no
4
5 cost to school districts.
5
6 F. Curriculum used by a school district pursuant to this
6
7 section shall be made available for public inspection on the school
7
8 district’s website.
8
9 G. The curriculum required in subsections C and D of this
9
10 section shall be combined into a single unit.
10
11 1. For grades six through eight, the curriculum shall be taught
11
12 as a ninety-minute unit divided into forty-five-minute segments over
12
13 two (2) days, which may not be consecutive, but each segment shall
13
14 be provided no more than one week apart.
14
15 2. For grades nine through twelve, the curriculum shall be
15
16 taught as a one-hundred-thirty-five-minute unit divided into forty-
16
17 five-minute segments over three (3) days, which may not be
17
18 consecutive, but each segment shall be provided no more than one
18
19 week apart.
19
20 H. School districts shall provide parents and legal guardians
20
21 of students written notification, which includes an explanation of
21
22 the presentation, who the presenter(s) will be, and any organization
22
23 with which the presenter is affiliated, at least seven (7) days
23
24 prior to presentation of the curriculum. The notification shall
24
Req. No. 2528 Page 4
1 incl ude information to parents and legal guardians that they may opt
1
2 their student out of the curriculum by providing written notice to
2
3 the school district.
3
4 I. Each school district board of education may partner with
4
5 nonprofit organizations and law enforcement agencies that specialize
5
6 in outreach and education programs on sex trafficking and
6
7 exploitation to make literature that addresses sex trafficking and
7
8 exploitation available and to assist in presenting the curriculum to
8
9 students. Literature and assistance provided pursuant to this
9
10 subsection shall be at no cost to school districts and shall be
10
11 limited in scope to the topics and guidance provided in subsections
11
12 B, C, and D of this section. Any literature provided pursuant to
12
13 this subsection shall be made available for public inspection on the
13
14 school district’s website.
14
15 J. The State Department of Education shall develop and make
15
16 available to the public on its website a ninety-minute education
16
17 video that addresses sex trafficking, exploitation prevention, and
17
18 social media use. The program shall be divided into segments not to
18
19 exceed twenty (20) minutes each. The Department shall also make
19
20 available on its website links to materials that complement the
20
21 education video and links to nonprofit organizations that specialize
21
22 in outreach and education programs on sex trafficking and
22
23 exploitation. The education video required by this subsection shall
23
24 address the topics outlined in paragraphs 1 through 15 of subsection
24
Req. No. 2528 Page 5
1 C of this section as well as concrete steps that parents and legal
1
2 guardians can take to protect children from exploitation.
2
3 K. The State Department of Education shall identify resources
3
4 and provide exemplar units or sample lesson plans designed to help
4
5 teachers provide instruction on the subject matters outlined in this
5
6 section.
6
7 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2027.

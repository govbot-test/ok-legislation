11 An Act relating to agriculture; amending 2 O.S. 2021,

Section 2:
Section 16-28.3, which relates to the Oklahoma
12 Controlled Burn Indemnity Fund; changing name to the
Oklahoma Prescribed Burn Indemnity Fund; updating
13 statutory references; providing that the fund shall
not reimburse the participating landowner for certain
14 damages; removing certain requirement to participate
in fund; providing additional requirements to
15 participate in fund; and providing an effective date.
16
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 16-28.3, is
20 amended to read as follows:
21 Section 16-28.3. A. There is hereby created within the
22 Oklahoma Conservation Commission, the "Oklahoma Controlled
23 Prescribed Burn Indemnity Fund".
24
HB3404 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 B. The Oklahoma Conservation Commission shall administer the
2 Oklahoma Controlled Prescribed Burn Indemnity Fund. The Indemnity
3 Fund shall be established for the benefit of landowners who perform
4 controlled prescribed burns. The Indemnity Fund shall compensate
5 landowners for losses incurred from a prescribed fire that spreads
6 beyond the control of the burner, except for losses covered by
7 insurance. The fund shall not reimburse the participating landowner
8 for damages incurred on their own property.
9 C. In order to participate in the Indemnity Fund, the landowner
10 shall:
11 1. Work with the local conservation district office and the
12 Natural Resources Conservation Service of the United States
13 Department of Agriculture to develop a controlled burn plan based on
14 the United States Department of Agriculture Natural Resources
15 Conservation Service guidelines; and Have a written prescribed burn
16 plan for the parcel of land to be covered by the Oklahoma Prescribed
17 Burn Fund prepared by either the local conservation district office,
18 the Natural Resources Conservation Service of the United States
19 Department of Agriculture, the Oklahoma Department of Wildlife
20 Conservation, Oklahoma State University Extension or other
21 recognized entity that works with landowners to develop prescribed
22 burn plans or a prescribed burn plan developed and written by the
23 landowner of the property to be burned.
24
HB3404 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 2. All burn plans submitted to the Conservation Commission for
2 coverage by the controlled burn indemnity fund shall include, at a
3 minimum, the following information:
4 a. the name and telephone number of the landowner, group
5 or organization conducting the burn,
6 b. the address and legal description of the area to be
7 burned,
8 c. the objective and purpose of the burn,
9 d. a list of fire departments and/or sheriff's offices
10 that are required to be notified pursuant to part 5 of
11 this section,
12 e. a list of adjoining landowners required to be notified
13 pursuant to paragraph 3 of this subsection,
14 f. a description of any firebreaks used to define the
15 boundary of the prescribed burn,
16 g. a statement of prescribed weather conditions,
17 h. a description of any smoke-management considerations,
18 i. a plan for adequate manpower and firefighting
19 equipment for the control of the fire, and a plan
20 providing for adequate personnel to watch over the
21 fire until it is extinguished and
22 j. an ignition plan for the burn.
23 3. Notify in writing or orally all landowners whose lands
24 adjoin the owner's land to be burned within sixty (60) days prior of
HB3404 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 cond ucting the burn and include in such notice the proposed date and
2 location of the burn and a telephone number where the owner can be
3 reached for information regarding the prescribed burn. If any
4 landowner is burning on a large, consolidated tract of land in which
5 there are multiple adjacent owners, only those owners with adjoining
6 land within one (1) mile of the proposed burn area must be notified.
7 4. File with the Oklahoma Conservation Commission at least
8 thirty (30) days prior to conducting the burn a copy of the
9 prescribed burn plan provided for in paragraph 1 of this subsection;
10 5. Notify, in writing or orally, within forty-eight (48) hours
11 of conducting the burn, the local fire departments whose response
12 area covers the land to be burned, the local county sheriff’s office
13 and, if the burn is to be conducted in a forestry service protection
14 area, a representative of the forestry service. Such notification
15 shall include the proposed date and location of the burn, a
16 telephone number where the owner can be reached for information
17 regarding the prescribed burn.
18 6. At the time of filing the completed plan, provide payment of
19 One Hundred Dollars ($100.00) Two Hundred Fifty Dollars ($250.00) to
20 the Oklahoma Conservation Commission.
21 D. The Conservation Commission shall have authority to invest
22 the assessments. All proceeds of the investment shall be placed in
23 the Indemnity Fund. Fifty Thousand Dollars ($50,000.00) from the
24 interest income for each year on the total proceeds in the Indemnity
HB3404 HFLR Page 4
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 Fund shall be paid to the Conservation Commission annually for the
2 development of controlled prescribed burn plans and for
3 administration of the Indemnity Fund. The balance of the accrued
4 interest each year shall remain a part of the Indemnity Fund.
5 E. The maximum amount the fund shall pay for losses arising
6 from any one prescribed fire event is One Million Dollars
7 ($1,000,000.00). For purposes of this subsection, "losses arising
8 from any one prescribed fire event" means all activities conducted
9 pursuant to any one burn plan and, if required, burn permit.
10 F. When a loss is incurred, the landowner shall present a claim
11 to the Conservation Commission. To verify the claim, the landowner
12 shall present any evidence required by the Conservation Commission
13 including, but not limited to, evidence that the landowner strictly
14 adhered to the plan provided for in subsection C of this section.
15 All landowner claim payments shall be made by the Conservation
16 Commission from the Indemnity Fund as soon as practicable and not
17 later than one (1) year following the date of claim. The price per
18 acre of land shall be established on the day of the loss and shall
19 be for the full market value on that day.
20 F. G. If there is an insufficient amount of funds in the
21 Indemnity Fund to cover all claims for a certain year, payments
22 shall be made on a pro rata basis up to one hundred percent (100%)
23 of the total loss of each landowner. If payment is not received in
24 the amount of one hundred percent (100%) of total loss for a certain
HB3404 HFLR Page 5
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 year , then additional amounts shall be paid as funds become
2 available in succeeding years until repayment of one hundred percent
3 (100%) of total loss is attained. If, at any time, a landowner
4 receives payment totaling more than one hundred percent (100%) of
5 total loss, the excess payment shall be returned to the Indemnity
6 Fund within thirty (30) days. Upon final payment of a claim to a
7 landowner from the Indemnity Fund, the landowner shall subrogate the
8 interest to the Conservation Commission in a cause of action against
9 any and all parties, to the amount of loss that the producer was
10 reimbursed by the Indemnity Fund.
11 G. H. The landowner shall, within sixty (60) days of the date
12 of loss, present the claim to the Conservation Commission.
13 Landowners may submit a written request to the Conservation
14 Commission for a sixty-day extension of the filing period, if the
15 landowner can show that the landowner was not provided notification
16 and reasonable time to file a claim. If the claim of loss is not
17 presented within the time and in the manner required, the claim
18 shall be forever barred and the landowner shall forfeit all rights
19 to remuneration or payment.
20 H. I. If state funds are appropriated to the Indemnity Fund,
21 the Conservation Commission shall establish the rules and procedures
22 necessary to ensure that the General Revenue Fund shall be
23 reimbursed from the assessments in an amount equal to the total
24 appropriation made to the Indemnity Fund. The reimbursement shall
HB3404 HFLR Page 6
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 be m ade in a timely manner, provided the intents and purposes of
2 this section to compensate landowners for losses incurred shall not
3 be adversely affected.
4 I. J. The monies deposited in the Indemnity Fund shall at no
5 time become part of the general budget of any state board,
6 commission, or agency, except the Conservation Commission.
7 J. K. The Executive Director of the Conservation Commission
8 shall investigate all potential civil action claims against persons
9 for recovery of any losses paid by the Indemnity Fund.
10 K. L. No provision of this section shall preclude any landowner
11 from conducting a lawful burn without participating in the Oklahoma
12 Controlled Prescribed Burn Indemnity Fund.
13 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
14

03/04/2026 - DO PASS, As Coauthored.

6 An Act relating to the Oklahoma Central Purchasing
Act; amending 74 O.S. 2021, Section 85.3A, as amended
7 by Section 53, Chapter 228, O.S.L. 2022 (74 O.S.
Supp. 2025, Section 85.3A), which relates to exempted
8 entities; updating statutory language; adding certain
exemption; exempting certain purchases made by the
9 Oklahoma Tourism and Recreation Department from the
Oklahoma Central Purchasing Act; exceeding certain
10 amount; construing provisions; providing for
codification; and declaring an emergency.

6 B. The State Purchasing Director may form an advisory committee
7 consisting of representatives from entities exempted from the
8 provisions of the Oklahoma Central Purchasing Act. The purpose of
9 the committee shall be to allow committee members to provide input
10 into the development of shared state purchasing contracts,
11 collaboratively participate in the integration of their purchasing
12 platforms or electronic purchasing catalogs, analyze solutions that
13 may be used by state government to meet the purchasing needs of the
14 entities, explore joint purchases of general use items that result
15 in mutual procurement of quality goods and services at the lowest
16 reasonable cost and explore flexibility, administrative relief and
17 transformation changes through utilization of procurement
18 technology.

1 D. The State Purchasing Director may invite representatives of
2 political subdivisions, and local common education entities to
3 participate as members of the advisory committee.
4 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
5 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2211.1 of Title 74, unless there
6 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
7 A. The Oklahoma Tourism and Recreation Department shall be
8 exempt from the Oklahoma Central Purchasing Act for purchases in an
9 amount not to exceed Seventy-five Thousand Dollars ($75,000.00)
10 related to merchandise for resale purchased for and sold over the
11 Internet, in publications, or through Department retail outlets
12 including, but not limited to, lodges, gift shops, golf course pro
13 shops, restaurants, and other purchases made for the production of
14 such merchandise.
15 B. An exemption authorized in this section shall not be
16 construed for the use of leasing or contracting for state-owned
17 restaurants in state parks.
18 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation

21 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

15 c. state and local revenues generated from the
16 investment,
17 d. the economic benefits to the state,
18 e. the type and amount of the investment,
19 f. the current capitalization level and strategy, and
20 g. the industry classification of the entity;
21 3. "Eligible Oklahoma venture capital company" means a lawfully
22 recognized business entity the primary business purpose of which is
23 to accumulate funds for making investments in lawful for profit

7 e. corporation, or

9 4. "Lawful business entity" means the following:
10 a. a person,
11 b. a general partnership,

13 d. a limited liability partnership,
14 e. a limited liability company, or
15 f. a corporation; and

18 venture capital company and for purposes of the deduction authorized
19 by this section in an amount not in excess of Twenty-five Million
20 Dollars ($25,000,000.00) by an accredited investor during a taxable
21 year.

1 the amount of qualified equity investment in an eligible Oklahoma
2 venture capital entity made by an accredited investor.
3 C. The maximum amount of qualified equity investment made by an
4 accredited investor for purposes of the deduction authorized by this
5 section shall not exceed Twenty-five Million Dollars
6 ($25,000,000.00) for any taxable year of the investor.
7 D. Any qualified equity investment made for purposes of the
8 deduction authorized by this section shall be documented by the
9 issuance of shares of stock, membership interest or other evidence
10 of the equity interest acquired by the accredited investor. Such
11 evidence may take the form of physical shares or the electronic
12 equivalent of physical shares.
13 E. Records of the equity interest acquired by an accredited
14 investor shall be maintained by the accredited investor and the
15 eligible Oklahoma venture capital company for a period of at least
16 five (5) years from the date the equity investment is made by an
17 accredited investor.
18 F. A qualified equity investment made by an accredited investor
19 for purposes of the deduction authorized by this section shall not
20 be returned by the eligible Oklahoma venture capital company to the
21 accredited investor, if the accredited investor is a natural person,
22 or to any person related to such natural person within the third
23 degree of consanguinity or affinity, for a period of three (3) years
24 from the date of the qualified equity investment unless the return

6 eligible Oklahoma venture capital company or the financial
7 performance of one or more for profit business entities in which the
8 accumulated equity funds of the eligible Oklahoma venture capital

23 Oklahoma venture capital company and only if the return of some part
24 of the qualified equity investment is based on the financial

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 perf ormance of either the eligible Oklahoma venture capital company
2 or the financial performance of one or more for profit business
3 entities in which the accumulated equity funds of the eligible
4 Oklahoma venture capital company are further invested or both such
5 measures of financial performance.
6 H. The deduction authorized by the provisions of this section
7 shall not be used to reduce the Oklahoma taxable income amount or
8 the Oklahoma adjusted gross income amount to less than zero (0).
9 There shall not be any carryover with respect to a deduction
10 authorized by the provisions of this section.
11 I. If the Oklahoma Tax Commission determines, either from
12 information accompanying any applicable income tax return or
13 schedule, form or supporting documentation filed in order to claim
14 the deduction authorized by this section, that the requirements of
15 this section were not fulfilled, the Oklahoma Tax Commission shall
16 notify the taxpayer claiming the deduction that the deduction has
17 been disallowed and the income tax liability for the taxpayer shall
18 be recalculated. The taxpayer shall retain all rights authorized
19 pursuant to the provisions of the Uniform Tax Procedure Code and the
20 Oklahoma Income Tax Code in order to contest the disallowance of
21 part or all of such deductions.
22 J. OCAST may promulgate rules to enforce the provisions of this
23 act. OCAST shall annually publish a report on the program created
24 in this section.

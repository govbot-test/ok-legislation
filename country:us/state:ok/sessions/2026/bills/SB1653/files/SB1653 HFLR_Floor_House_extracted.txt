1 OCCUPATIONAL THERAPY LICENSURE COMPACT
2 SECTION 1: PURPOSE
3 The purpose of this Compact is to facilitate interstate practice
4 of Occupational Therapy with the goal of improving public access to
5 Occupational Therapy services. The Practice of Occupational Therapy
6 occurs in the State where the patient or client is located at the
7 time of the patient or client encounter. The Compact preserves the
8 regulatory authority of States to protect public health and safety
9 through the current system of State licensure.
10 This Compact is designed to achieve the following objectives:
11 A. Increase public access to Occupational Therapy services by
12 providing for the mutual recognition of other Member State licenses.
13 B. Enhance the States’ ability to protect the public’s health
14 and safety.
15 C. Encourage the cooperation of Member States in regulating
16 multi-State Occupational Therapy Practice.
17 D. Support spouses of relocating military members.
18 E. Enhance the exchange of licensure, investigative, and
19 disciplinary information between Member States.
20 F. Allow a Remote State to hold a provider of services with a
21 Compact Privilege in that State accountable to that State’s practice
22 standards.
23 G. Facilitate the use of telehealth technology in order to
24 increase access to Occupational Therapy services.

8 Action against an Occupational Therapist’s or Occupational Therapy

2 SECTION 1: PURPOSE
3 The purpose of this Compact is to facilitate interstate practice
4 of Occupational Therapy with the goal of improving public access to
5 Occupational Therapy services. The Practice of Occupational Therapy
6 occurs in the State where the patient or client is located at the
7 time of the patient or client encounter. The Compact preserves the
8 regulatory authority of States to protect public health and safety
9 through the current system of State licensure.
10 This Compact is designed to achieve the following objectives:
11 A. Increase public access to Occupational Therapy services by
12 providing for the mutual recognition of other Member State licenses.
13 B. Enhance the States’ ability to protect the public’s health
14 and safety.
15 C. Encourage the cooperation of Member States in regulating
16 multi-State Occupational Therapy Practice.
17 D. Support spouses of relocating military members.
18 E. Enhance the exchange of licensure, investigative, and
19 disciplinary information between Member States.
20 F. Allow a Remote State to hold a provider of services with a
21 Compact Privilege in that State accountable to that State’s practice
22 standards.
23 G. Facilitate the use of telehealth technology in order to
24 increase access to Occupational Therapy services.

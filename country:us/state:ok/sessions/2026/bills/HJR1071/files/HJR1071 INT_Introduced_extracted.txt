15 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES AND THE SENATE OF THE
16 2ND SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE:
17 SECTION 1. The Secretary of State shall refer to the people for
18 their approval or rejection, as and in the manner provided by law,
19 the following proposed amendment to Section 8E of Article X of the
20 Constitution of the State of Oklahoma to read as follows:
21 Section 8E. A. Despite any provision to the contrary,
22 beginning January 1, 2006, each head of household who has been
23 honorably discharged from active service in any branch of the Armed
24 Forces of the United States or Oklahoma National Guard and who has
Req. No. 15133 Page 1
1 been certified by the United States Department of Veterans Affairs
2 or its successor to have a one hundred percent (100%) permanent
3 disability sustained through military action or accident or
4 resulting from disease contracted while in such active service or
5 the surviving spouse of such head of household shall be entitled to
6 claim an exemption for the full amount of the fair cash value of the
7 homestead.
8 B. Beginning January 1, 2027, in addition to the exemption
9 provided for in subsection A of this section, each veteran head of
10 household who has been honorably discharged from active service in
11 any branch of the Armed Forces of the United States or Oklahoma
12 National Guard and who has been certified by the United States
13 Department of Veterans Affairs or its successor as having a service-
14 connected disability rating of not less than ten percent (10%) shall
15 be allowed a partial exemption from ad valorem taxation upon the
16 homestead of such veteran in the amount corresponding to the
17 veteran's certified disability rating as follows:
18 1. For a veteran having a service-connected disability rating
19 of ten percent (10%) but less than thirty percent (30%), an
20 exemption of Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00) of the assessed value
21 of the homestead;
22 2. For a veteran having a service-connected disability rating
23 of thirty percent (30%) but less than fifty percent (50%), an
24

1 exem ption of Seven Thousand Five Hundred Dollars ($7,500.00) of the
2 assessed value of the homestead;
3 3. For a veteran having a service-connected disability rating
4 of fifty percent (50%) but less than seventy percent (70%), an
5 exemption of Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000.00) of the assessed value
6 of the homestead; and
7 4. For a veteran having a service-connected disability rating
8 of seventy percent (70%) but less than one hundred percent (100%),
9 an exemption of Twelve Thousand Dollars ($12,000.00) of the assessed
10 value of the homestead.
11 C. In order to be eligible for the exemption exemptions
12 authorized by this section, the individual shall be required to
13 prove residency within the State of Oklahoma and must have
14 previously qualified for the homestead exemption authorized by law
15 or be eligible for the homestead exemption pursuant to law.
16 C. D. If a homestead otherwise eligible for the exemption
17 exemptions authorized by this section is transferred on or after
18 January 1 of a calendar year, another homestead property acquired by
19 the qualifying head of household or by the surviving spouse of such
20 qualifying head of household shall be exempt to the same extent as
21 the homestead property previously owned by such person or persons
22 for the year during which the new homestead is acquired and, subject
23 to the requirements of this section, for each year thereafter.

1 SECTION 2. The Ballot Title for the proposed Constitutional
2 amendment as set forth in SECTION 1 of this resolution shall be in
3 the following form:
4 BALLOT TITLE
5 Legislative Referendum No. ____ State Question No. ____
6 THE GIST OF THE PROPOSITION IS AS FOLLOWS:
7 This measure amends Section 8E of Article 10 of the Oklahoma
8 Constitution. It expands the existing ad valorem tax exemption
9 for veterans with service-connected disabilities. Currently,
10 veterans certified as one hundred percent (100%) disabled by the
11 United States Department of Veterans Affairs are fully exempt
12 from ad valorem taxes on their homestead. This measure would
13 create partial exemptions for veterans with disability ratings
14 of less than one hundred percent (100%), based on the percentage
15 of disability. Veterans with a service-connected disability
16 rating between ten percent (10%) and twenty-nine percent (29%)
17 would be exempt from Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00) of the
18 assessed value of their homestead. Veterans with ratings
19 between thirty percent (30%) and forty-nine percent (49%) would
20 be exempt from Seven Thousand Five Hundred Dollars ($7,500.00).
21 Veterans with ratings between fifty percent (50%) and sixty-nine
22 percent (69%) would be exempt from Ten Thousand Dollars
23 ($10,000.00). Veterans with ratings between seventy percent
24
Req. No. 15133 Page 4
1 (70%) and ninety-nine percent (99%) would be exempt from Twelve
2 Thousand Dollars ($12,000.00).

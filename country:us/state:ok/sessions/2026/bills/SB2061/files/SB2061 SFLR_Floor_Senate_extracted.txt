6 An Act relating to food policy; creating the Oklahoma
Food Policy Council Act; providing short title;
7 defining terms; establishing membership of the
Oklahoma Food Policy Council; providing for
8 membership terms; providing for filling of vacancies;
requiring compliance with the Oklahoma Open Meeting
9 Act and the Oklahoma Open Records Act; allowing for
reimbursement of certain expenses; providing for an
10 advisory and coordinating role to connect farmers,
community gardens, and local organizations; promoting
11 sustainable locally grown food and farm-to-school
initiatives; supporting producer-to-consumer access,
12 farmers markets, and agritourism; providing for
collaboration with the Oklahoma Department of
13 Agriculture, Food, and Forestry; requiring annual
reporting to the Governor and the Legislature;
14 providing for noncodification; providing for
codification; and providing an effective date.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
19 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
20 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Oklahoma Food
21 Policy Council Act”.
22 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
23 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-3.6 of Title 2, unless there
24 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:

1 As used in this act:
2 1. “Community garden” means any land cultivated by individuals
3 or groups for production of food for personal or local use or

5 2. “Farmers hub” means a facility, organization, or coordinated
6 network that supports the handling, storage, processing, or
7 distribution of agricultural products produced by multiple farmers

9 3. “Local food” means food produced, grown, or processed within
10 this state; and
11 4. “Sustainable food” means an integrated food system that
12 supports economic viability for farmers or producers, protects
13 natural resources, enhances efficient use of land and water, and
14 promotes accessibility to safe and nutritious food.

3 Agriculture, Food, and Forestry who shall serve an
4 initial term of three (3) years;
5 4. Members appointed to the Council shall serve staggered terms

7 shall be for three (3) years; and
8 5. Any vacancy on the Council resulting from the resignation of

10 original appointment was made and the term shall be for the amount

12 C. The Council shall elect from among its members a chair, vice
13 chair, and any other officers that the Council deems necessary.
14 D. The Council shall meet at least four (4) times per year or
15 as the chair deems necessary and shall comply with the Oklahoma Open
16 Meeting Act and the Oklahoma Open Records Act.
17 E. The Council members shall receive no compensation for
18 serving on the Council but shall be eligible for reimbursement of
19 travel expenses incurred pursuant to the State Travel Reimbursement
20 Act.

1 A. The Oklahoma Food Policy Council shall serve in a solely
2 advisory and coordinating capacity and shall:
3 1. Create networks of farmers to local organizations to provide
4 sustainable locally grown food to consumers throughout this state;
5 2. Strengthen coordination between existing programs that
6 support sustainable food production, farm-to-schools initiatives,
7 and producer-to-consumer access;
8 3. Assess the impact of the production of sustainable food
9 distributed locally on the economic development of the state,
10 enhancing the use of locally produced food for use in restaurants,
11 retail, and agritourism;
12 4. Assess the utilization of urban gardens and community
13 gardens for the purpose of improving the health of citizens, and
14 allowing for the use of urban property; and
15 5. Connect producers of sustainable food to farmers markets in
16 the state.
17 B. The Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry
18 may coordinate with the Council to identify any existing farmers
19 hubs, and promote collaboration between producers, community
20 gardens, local food banks or organizations, and local governments to
21 share best practices and resources to support the distribution of
22 sustainable food.
23 C. The Council shall electronically submit an annual report to
24 the Governor, the President Pro Tempore of the Senate, and the

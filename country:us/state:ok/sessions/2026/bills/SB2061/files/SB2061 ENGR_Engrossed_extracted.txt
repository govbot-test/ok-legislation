7 An Act relating to food policy; creating the Oklahoma
Food Policy Council Act; providing short title;
8 defining terms; establishing membership of the
Oklahoma Food Policy Council; providing for
9 membership terms; providing for filling of vacancies;
requiring compliance with the Oklahoma Open Meeting
10 Act and the Oklahoma Open Records Act; allowing for
reimbursement of certain expenses; providing for an
11 advisory and coordinating role to connect farmers,
community gardens, and local organizations; promoting
12 sustainable locally grown food and farm-to-school
initiatives; supporting producer-to-consumer access,
13 farmers markets, and agritourism; providing for
collaboration with the Oklahoma Department of
14 Agriculture, Food, and Forestry; requiring annual
reporting to the Governor and the Legislature;
15 providing for noncodification; providing for
codification; and providing an effective date.
16
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
20 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
21 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Oklahoma Food
22 Policy Council Act”.
23
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2061 Page 1
1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-3.6 of Title 2, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 As used in this act:
5 1. “Community garden” means any land cultivated by individuals
6 or groups for production of food for personal or local use or
7 community benefit;
8 2. “Farmers hub” means a facility, organization, or coordinated
9 network that supports the handling, storage, processing, or
10 distribution of agricultural products produced by multiple farmers
11 or producers;
12 3. “Local food” means food produced, grown, or processed within
13 this state; and
14 4. “Sustainable food” means an integrated food system that
15 supports economic viability for farmers or producers, protects
16 natural resources, enhances efficient use of land and water, and
17 promotes accessibility to safe and nutritious food.
18 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-3.7 of Title 2, unless there
20 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. There is hereby created the Oklahoma Food Policy Council
22 within the Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry.
23 B. The Council shall consist of eleven (11) members:
24 1. The Governor shall appoint three members as follows:
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2061 Page 2
1 a. one member representing the Farm to School program who
2 shall serve an initial term of one (1) year,
3 b. one member representing local and county food security
4 initiatives who shall serve an initial term of two (2)
5 years, and
6 c. one member representing the State Department of Health
7 who shall serve an initial term of three (3) years;
8 2. The President Pro Tempore of the Senate shall appoint four
9 members as follows:
10 a. one member from the Food Safety Division of the
11 Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry
12 who shall serve an initial term of one (1) year,
13 b one member from Langston University Cooperative
14 Extension Service who shall serve an initial term of
15 one (1) year,
16 c. one member who is an agricultural producer within this
17 state who shall serve an initial term of two (2)
18 years, and
19 d. one member representing conservation and sustainable
20 agricultural practices who shall serve an initial term
21 of three (3) years;
22 3. The Speaker of the House of Representatives shall appoint
23 four members as follows:
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2061 Page 3
1 a. one member from a food bank within this state who
2 shall serve an initial term of one (1) year,
3 b one member from Oklahoma State University Cooperative
4 Extension Service who shall serve an initial term of
5 one (1) year,
6 c. one member representing local food distribution and
7 local food supply chains who shall serve an initial
8 term of two (2) years, and
9 d. one member representing the Agricultural Environmental
10 Management Division of the Oklahoma Department of
11 Agriculture, Food, and Forestry who shall serve an
12 initial term of three (3) years;
13 4. Members appointed to the Council shall serve staggered terms
14 of three (3) years, and after the initial appointment, each term
15 shall be for three (3) years; and
16 5. Any vacancy on the Council resulting from the resignation of
17 a member or otherwise shall be filled in the same manner as the
18 original appointment was made and the term shall be for the amount
19 of time of the unexpired term of the member who created the vacancy.
20 C. The Council shall elect from among its members a chair, vice
21 chair, and any other officers that the Council deems necessary.
22 D. The Council shall meet at least four (4) times per year or
23 as the chair deems necessary and shall comply with the Oklahoma Open
24 Meeting Act and the Oklahoma Open Records Act.
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2061 Page 4
1 E. The Council members shall receive no compensation for
2 serving on the Council but shall be eligible for reimbursement of
3 travel expenses incurred pursuant to the State Travel Reimbursement
4 Act.
5 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
6 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 5-3.8 of Title 2, unless there
7 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
8 A. The Oklahoma Food Policy Council shall serve in a solely
9 advisory and coordinating capacity and shall:
10 1. Create networks of farmers to local organizations to provide
11 sustainable locally grown food to consumers throughout this state;
12 2. Strengthen coordination between existing programs that
13 support sustainable food production, farm-to-schools initiatives,
14 and producer-to-consumer access;
15 3. Assess the impact of the production of sustainable food
16 distributed locally on the economic development of the state,
17 enhancing the use of locally produced food for use in restaurants,
18 retail, and agritourism;
19 4. Assess the utilization of urban gardens and community
20 gardens for the purpose of improving the health of citizens, and
21 allowing for the use of urban property; and
22 5. Connect producers of sustainable food to farmers markets in
23 the state.
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2061 Page 5
1 B. The Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry
2 may coordinate with the Council to identify any existing farmers
3 hubs, and promote collaboration between producers, community
4 gardens, local food banks or organizations, and local governments to
5 share best practices and resources to support the distribution of
6 sustainable food.
7 C. The Council shall electronically submit an annual report to
8 the Governor, the President Pro Tempore of the Senate, and the
9 Speaker of the House of Representatives with any recommendations and
10 a summary of its findings. The annual report shall be published on
11 the website of the Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and
12 Forestry beginning one (1) year after the effective date of this
13 act.
14 SECTION 5. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
15 Passed the Senate the 25th day of March, 2026.

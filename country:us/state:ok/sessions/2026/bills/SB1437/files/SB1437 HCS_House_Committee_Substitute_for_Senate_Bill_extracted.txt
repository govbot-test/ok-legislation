11 An Act relating to schools; creating the President
Donald J. Trump Physical Fitness Act; providing short
12 title; directing the State Department of Education to
establish certain guidelines to administer certain
13 physical fitness test; directing guidelines to be
based on certain recommendations; providing for
14 contents of guidelines; requiring school districts to
administer certain test after certain guidelines have
15 been established; directing school districts to use
student performance results in certain manner;
16 providing for promulgation of rules; providing for
noncodification; providing for codification; and
17 providing an effective date.
18
19
20 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
21 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
22 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
23 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “President
24 Donald J. Trump Physical Fitness Act”.
Req. No. 17465 Page 1
1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11-103.9c of Title 70, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. The State Department of Education shall establish guidelines
5 for school districts in this state to assess the overall fitness of
6 students and encourage physical activity and healthy habits by
7 administering the Presidential Fitness Test. The physical fitness
8 test guidelines shall be based on the recommendations of the
9 President’s Council on Sports, Fitness, and Nutrition, which was
10 reconstituted by Executive Order 14327. The guidelines shall
11 include:
12 1. Training for personnel to administer the test;
13 2. Exemption guidelines for students with disabilities, medical
14 conditions, or other factors that would impact their ability to
15 complete the test; and
16 3. An option for a parent or legal guardian of a student to opt
17 the student out of the test.
18 B. Upon development of the physical fitness test
19 recommendations by the President’s Council on Sports, Fitness, and
20 Nutrition and the subsequent establishment of guidelines by the
21 State Department of Education, school districts in this state shall
22 administer the Presidential Fitness Test to students using the
23 guidelines established pursuant to subsection A of this section or
24 from nationally accepted fitness testing programs designed for
Req. No. 17465 Page 2
1 scho ol-aged children that test cardiovascular fitness, muscular
2 strength and endurance, flexibility, and body composition.
3 C. School districts shall utilize student performance results
4 on the Presidential Fitness Test to:
5 1. Promote the physical and mental benefits of daily movement,
6 exercise, and good nutrition;
7 2. Modify physical activity opportunities offered to students;
8 and
9 3. Encourage participation in physical activities available to
10 students inside and outside the school setting.
11 D. The State Board of Education may promulgate rules to
12 implement the provisions of this section.
13 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

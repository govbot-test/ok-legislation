7 An Act relating to schools; creating the President
Donald J. Trump Physical Fitness Act; providing short
8 title; directing the State Department of Education to
establish certain guidelines to administer certain
9 physical fitness test; directing guidelines to be
based on certain recommendations; providing for
10 contents of guidelines; requiring school districts to
administer certain test after certain guidelines have
11 been established; directing school districts to use
student performance results in certain manner;
12 providing for promulgation of rules; providing for
noncodification; providing for codification; and
13 providing an effective date.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
18 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
19 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “President
20 Donald J. Trump Physical Fitness Act”.
21 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 11-103.9c of Title 70, unless
23 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1437 Page 1
1 A. The State Department of Education shall establish guidelines
2 for school districts in this state to assess the overall fitness of
3 students and encourage physical activity and healthy habits by
4 administering the Presidential Fitness Test. The physical fitness
5 test guidelines shall be based on the recommendations of the
6 President’s Council on Sports, Fitness, and Nutrition, which was
7 reconstituted by Executive Order 14327. The guidelines shall
8 include:
9 1. Training for personnel to administer the test;
10 2. Exemption guidelines for students with disabilities, medical
11 conditions, or other factors that would impact their ability to
12 complete the test; and
13 3. An option for a parent or legal guardian of a student to opt
14 the student out of the test.
15 B. Upon development of the physical fitness test
16 recommendations by the President’s Council on Sports, Fitness, and
17 Nutrition and the subsequent establishment of guidelines by the
18 State Department of Education, school districts in this state shall
19 administer the Presidential Fitness Test to students using the
20 guidelines established pursuant to subsection A of this section.
21 C. School districts shall utilize student performance results
22 on the Presidential Fitness Test to:
23 1. Promote the physical and mental benefits of daily movement,
24 exercise, and good nutrition;
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1437 Page 2
1 2. Modify physical activity opportunities offered to students;
2 and
3 3. Encourage participation in physical activities available to
4 students inside and outside the school setting.
5 D. The State Board of Education may promulgate rules to
6 implement the provisions of this section.
7 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
8 Passed the Senate the 24th day of March, 2026.

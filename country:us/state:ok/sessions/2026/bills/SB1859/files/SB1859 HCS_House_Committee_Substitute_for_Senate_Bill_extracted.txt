10 An Act relating to Oklahoma State Bureau of
Investigation; creating the Cyber Crime and Fraud
11 Unit; prescribing purpose and duties of the unit;
prohibiting provisions from being construed to expand
12 the jurisdiction of the Bureau; authorizing the
Director of the Bureau to reassign personnel and
13 resources to carry out certain duties; authorizing
the Bureau to promulgate rules; creating the Cyber
14 Crime and Fraud Unit Fund; providing sources of
funds; providing for expenditures from fund;
15 providing for codification; providing an effective
date; and declaring an emergency.
16
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 150.19c of Title 74, unless
21 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 A. There is hereby created within the Oklahoma State Bureau of
23 Investigation a Cyber Crime and Fraud Unit. The unit shall operate
24 as an internal division of the Bureau and shall exercise only those
Req. No. 17539 Page 1
1 inve stigative powers and the jurisdiction granted to the Bureau by
2 Oklahoma Statutes.
3 B. The purpose of the Cyber Crime and Fraud Unit shall be to
4 enhance the capacity of the Bureau to investigate:
5 1. Cyber-enabled criminal activity including intrusions,
6 network breaches, ransomware, digital extortion, and online
7 exploitation;
8 2. Financial fraud and related schemes including cyber-
9 facilitated fraud, identity theft, and complex financial deception;
10 and
11 3. Criminal activity involving digital evidence, consistent
12 with the existing statutory authority of the Bureau.
13 C. Nothing in this act shall be construed to expand or modify
14 the jurisdiction of the Bureau. The unit may only initiate or
15 conduct investigations as follows:
16 1. Upon request or referral from a district attorney, law
17 enforcement agency, or state officer authorized under existing law;
18 2. Upon direction of the Governor, consistent with current
19 statutory authority; or
20 3. As the Bureau is otherwise permitted under existing
21 statutes.
22 D. The Cyber Crime and Fraud Unit shall:
23 1. Conduct investigations involving cyber-crime, digital
24 evidence, and fraud related offenses;
Req. No. 17539 Page 2
1 2. Provide digital forensics, technical assistance, and
2 analytical support to Bureau agents and requesting agencies;
3 3. Coordinate with federal, state, tribal, and local partners
4 on cyber and fraud investigations;
5 4. Develop and provide training to law enforcement agencies on
6 cyber-crime trends, digital evidence handling, and fraud prevention;
7 and
8 5. Maintain specialized personnel, including agents, analysts,
9 forensic examiners, and technical support staff.
10 E. The Director of the Bureau may assign personnel, reallocate
11 resources, and establish internal procedures necessary to carry out
12 the responsibilities of the unit.
13 F. The Bureau may promulgate rules to effectuate the provisions
14 of this act.
15 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 150.19d of Title 74, unless
17 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving fund
19 for the Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation to be designated the
20 “Cyber Crime and Fraud Unit Fund”. The fund shall be a continuing
21 fund, not subject to fiscal year limitations, and shall consist of
22 any monies received by the Bureau for deposit in the fund. All
23 monies accruing to the credit of such fund are hereby appropriated
24 and may be budgeted and expended by the Oklahoma State Bureau of
Req. No. 17539 Page 3
1 Inve stigation for the purpose of carrying out the duties and
2 functions prescribed in Section 1 of this act. Expenditures from
3 such fund shall be made upon warrants issued by the State Treasurer
4 against claims filed as prescribed by law with the Director of the
5 Office of Management and Enterprise Services for approval and
6 payment.
7 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
8 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
9 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
10 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
11 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

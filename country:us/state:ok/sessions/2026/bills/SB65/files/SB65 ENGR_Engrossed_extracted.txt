6 An Act relating to substance abuse services; defining
terms; authorizing the possession, acquisition,
7 distribution, and administration of emergency opioid
antagonists by any person; providing criminal and
8 civil immunities; providing for codification;
providing an effective date; and declaring an
9 emergency.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2-403 of Title 43A, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 A. As used in this section:
17 1. “Emergency opioid antagonist” means a drug including, but
18 not limited to, naloxone that blocks the effects of opioids and that
19 is approved by the United States Food and Drug Administration for
20 the treatment of an opioid overdose; and
21 2. “Person” means, unless the context otherwise requires, any
22 individual, partnership, association, corporation, limited liability
23 company, governmental entity, tribal entity, public or private
24 institution of postsecondary education, or other legal entity.
ENGR. S. B. NO. 65 Page 1

1 B. Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, any
2 person may:
3 1. Possess and store emergency opioid antagonists;
4 2. Acquire emergency opioid antagonists by purchase or
5 donation;
6 3. Distribute or make emergency opioid antagonists available to
7 any person to the extent permitted under applicable federal law; and
8 4. Administer an emergency opioid antagonist to any person who
9 the person believes, in good faith, is experiencing an opioid
10 overdose.
11 C. A person shall not be subject to criminal prosecution for
12 possessing, storing, acquiring, distributing, making available, or
13 administering emergency opioid antagonists pursuant to this section.
14 D. Any person who, in good faith, distributes, makes available,
15 or administers emergency opioid antagonists pursuant to this section
16 shall not be liable for any civil damages as a result of any acts or
17 omissions by such person, except for acts or omissions constituting
18 gross negligence or willful or wanton wrongs in distributing, making
19 available, or administering emergency opioid antagonists.
20 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
21 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
22 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
23 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
24 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

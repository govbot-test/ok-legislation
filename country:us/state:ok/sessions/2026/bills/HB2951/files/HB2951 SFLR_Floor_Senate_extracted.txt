8 An Act relating to prisons and reformatories;
amending 57 O.S. 2021, Section 95, which relates to
9 the delivery of sentenced persons; authorizing the
transport of inmates to certain facility; amending 57
10 O.S. 2021, Sections 502, as amended by Section 2,
Chapter 22, O.S.L. 2023, 508, and 509, as last
11 amended by Section 2, Chapter 467, O.S.L. 2025 (57
O.S. Supp. 2025, Sections 502 and 509), which relate
12 to the Oklahoma Corrections Act of 1967; removing and
adding correctional centers to certain defined term;
13 updating reference to certain named act; deleting
certain salary restriction; adding correctional
14 centers to list of penal institutions maintained by
the Department of Corrections; repealing 57 O.S.
15 2021, Section 504.7, which relates to the Kate
Barnard Community Corrections Center; and providing
16 an effective date.
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 57 O.S. 2021, Section 95, is
20 amended to read as follows:
21 Section 95. A. Any person convicted of an offense against the
22 laws of this state and sentenced to imprisonment that is not to be
23 served in a county jail shall be transported by the sheriff of the
24 county where the person is sentenced, or transported by a designated
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2951 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 repr esentative of the sheriff, to the Department of Corrections at
2 the Lexington Assessment and Reception Center, the Mabel Bassett
3 Correctional Center, or other location designated by the Director of
4 the Department of Corrections.
5 B. Any person convicted of an offense against the laws of this
6 state and sentenced to imprisonment that is not to be served in a
7 county jail and who is not housed in a county jail shall be
8 transported by the detention center, or transported by a designated
9 representative of the detention center, to the Department of
10 Corrections at the Lexington Assessment and Reception Center, the
11 Mabel Bassett Correctional Center, or other location designated by
12 the Director of the Department of Corrections.
13 C. The sheriff shall deliver the person to the Department at
14 such center together with:
15 1. A certified copy of the judgment and sentence from the court
16 ordering such imprisonment, unless the judgment and sentence
17 previously has been sent electronically by an authorized clerk of
18 the court;
19 2. A certificate setting forth the number of days served in the
20 county jail after the pronouncement of judgment and rendering of
21 sentence for the offenses committed;
22 3. A copy of any medical, dental, or mental health records of
23 the defendant for conditions reviewed or treated while in the
24 custody of the sheriff;
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - HB2951 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 4. Any medication or medical or dental device prescribed for
2 the defendant while in the custody of the sheriff or for a
3 preexisting condition; and
4 5. A copy of the presentence investigation report, if a report
5 was prepared.
6 D. The Department of Corrections shall give the sheriff a
7 receipt for each person received into the custody of the Department.
8 The receipt shall be filed by the sheriff in the office of the clerk
9 of the court where the sentence was made.
10 E. The Department of Corrections shall reimburse the
11 transporting agency as follows:
12 1. Mileage from the county sheriff's office to the appropriate
13 reception center and back to the county sheriff's office; and
14 2. Hourly wage reimbursement for the transporting officer for
15 the hours of transport travel and time spent at the reception center
16 based on the transporting officer's normal hourly wage.
17 Reimbursement shall not exceed Thirty Dollars ($30.00) an hour per
18 officer. Documentation of the officer's hourly wage shall accompany
19 all reimbursement requests to the Department.
20 The Department of Corrections shall have the authority to
21 promulgate rules and forms for the reimbursement procedures provided
22 in this section.

5 officer. "Intensive programmatic services" offered by the
6 Department of Corrections includes, but shall not be limited to,
7 alcohol and substance abuse counseling and treatment, mental health
8 counseling and treatment and domestic violence courses and treatment
9 programs;

17 amended to read as follows:
18 Section 508. The Director, subject to the approval of the
19 Board, is hereby authorized to create divisions within the
20 Department of Corrections as he they may deem appropriate to
21 effectively manage the Department. The divisions shall be under the
22 immediate supervision and control of the Director. The Director is
23 hereby authorized to appoint Deputy Directors for the divisions of
24 the Department, who shall be exempt from the Merit System of

1 Pers onnel Administration Act Civil Service and Human Capital
2 Modernization Act, and to fix the salaries and duties thereof;
3 provided, the salary ranges of said Deputy Directors shall be set by
4 the Legislature in the Department's annual appropriation. The
5 Deputy Directors shall have at least a master's degree from an
6 accredited college or university and at least four (4) years of
7 professional level work experience in corrections; or a bachelor's
8 degree and at least five (5) years of work experience in
9 corrections. Provided, however, that for the position of Deputy
10 Director of administrative services, professional level
11 administrative experience may be substituted for work experience in
12 corrections. The provisions of this act shall not apply to those
13 presently serving as Deputy Director as herein defined.

7 An Act relating to prisons and reformatories;
amending 57 O.S. 2021, Section 95, which relates to
8 the delivery of sentenced persons; authorizing the
transport of inmates to certain facility; amending 57
9 O.S. 2021, Sections 502, as amended by Section 2,
Chapter 22, O.S.L. 2023, 508, and 509, as last
10 amended by Section 2, Chapter 467, O.S.L. 2025 (57
O.S. Supp. 2025, Sections 502 and 509), which relate
11 to the Oklahoma Corrections Act of 1967; removing and
adding correctional centers to certain defined term;
12 updating reference to certain named act; deleting
certain salary restriction; adding correctional
13 centers to list of penal institutions maintained by
the Department of Corrections; repealing 57 O.S.
14 2021, Section 504.7, which relates to the Kate
Barnard Community Corrections Center; and providing
15 an effective date.
16
17
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
20 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 57 O.S. 2021, Section 95, is
21 amended to read as follows:
22 Section 95. A. Any person convicted of an offense against the
23 laws of this state and sentenced to imprisonment that is not to be
24 served in a county jail shall be transported by the sheriff of the
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2951 Page 1
1 coun ty where the person is sentenced, or transported by a designated
2 representative of the sheriff, to the Department of Corrections at
3 the Lexington Assessment and Reception Center, the Mabel Bassett
4 Correctional Center, or other location designated by the Director of
5 the Department of Corrections.
6 B. Any person convicted of an offense against the laws of this
7 state and sentenced to imprisonment that is not to be served in a
8 county jail and who is not housed in a county jail shall be
9 transported by the detention center, or transported by a designated
10 representative of the detention center, to the Department of
11 Corrections at the Lexington Assessment and Reception Center, the
12 Mabel Bassett Correctional Center, or other location designated by
13 the Director of the Department of Corrections.
14 C. The sheriff shall deliver the person to the Department at
15 such center together with:
16 1. A certified copy of the judgment and sentence from the court
17 ordering such imprisonment, unless the judgment and sentence
18 previously has been sent electronically by an authorized clerk of
19 the court;
20 2. A certificate setting forth the number of days served in the
21 county jail after the pronouncement of judgment and rendering of
22 sentence for the offenses committed;
23
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2951 Page 2
1 3. A copy of any medical, dental, or mental health records of
2 the defendant for conditions reviewed or treated while in the
3 custody of the sheriff;
4 4. Any medication or medical or dental device prescribed for
5 the defendant while in the custody of the sheriff or for a
6 preexisting condition; and
7 5. A copy of the presentence investigation report, if a report
8 was prepared.
9 D. The Department of Corrections shall give the sheriff a
10 receipt for each person received into the custody of the Department.
11 The receipt shall be filed by the sheriff in the office of the clerk
12 of the court where the sentence was made.
13 E. The Department of Corrections shall reimburse the
14 transporting agency as follows:
15 1. Mileage from the county sheriff's office to the appropriate
16 reception center and back to the county sheriff's office; and
17 2. Hourly wage reimbursement for the transporting officer for
18 the hours of transport travel and time spent at the reception center
19 based on the transporting officer's normal hourly wage.
20 Reimbursement shall not exceed Thirty Dollars ($30.00) an hour per
21 officer. Documentation of the officer's hourly wage shall accompany
22 all reimbursement requests to the Department.
23
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2951 Page 3
1 The Department of Corrections shall have the authority to
2 promulgate rules and forms for the reimbursement procedures provided
3 in this section.
4 The Department of Corrections shall submit a quarterly report to
5 the Chair of the Senate Appropriations Committee and Chair of the
6 House of Representatives Appropriations and Budget Committee.
7 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 57 O.S. 2021, Section 502, as
8 amended by Section 2, Chapter 22, O.S.L. 2023 (57 O.S. Supp. 2025,
9 Section 502), is amended to read as follows:
10 Section 502. As used in this title, unless the context
11 otherwise requires:
12 1. "Board" means the State Board of Corrections;
13 2. "Department" means the Department of Corrections of this
14 state;
15 3. "Director" means the Director of the Department of
16 Corrections;
17 4. "Halfway house" means a private facility for the placement
18 of inmates in a community setting for the purpose of reintegrating
19 into the community inmates who are nearing their release dates. The
20 term shall not include private prisons;
21 5. "Institutions" means the Oklahoma State Penitentiary located
22 at McAlester, Oklahoma; the Oklahoma State Reformatory located at
23 Granite, Oklahoma; the Lexington Assessment and Reception Center
24 located at Lexington, Oklahoma; the Joseph Harp Correctional Center
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2951 Page 4
1 loca ted at Lexington, Oklahoma; the Jackie Brannon Correctional
2 Center located at McAlester, Oklahoma; the Howard McLeod
3 Correctional Center located at Atoka, Oklahoma; the Mack H. Alford
4 Correctional Center located at Stringtown, Oklahoma; the Jim E.
5 Hamilton Correctional Center located at Hodgen, Oklahoma; the Mabel
6 Bassett Correctional Center located at McLoud, Oklahoma; the R.B.
7 "Dick" Conner Correctional Center located at Hominy, Oklahoma; the
8 James Crabtree Correctional Center located at Helena, Oklahoma; the
9 Jess Dunn Correctional Center located at Taft, Oklahoma; the John
10 Lilley Correctional Center located at Boley, Oklahoma; the William
11 S. Key Correctional Center located at Fort Supply, Oklahoma; the Dr.
12 Eddie Warrior Correctional Center located at Taft, Oklahoma; the
13 Northeast Oklahoma Community Corrections Center located at Vinita,
14 Oklahoma; the Clara Waters and Kate Barnard Community Corrections
15 Centers Center located at Oklahoma City, Oklahoma; the Community
16 Corrections Centers located at Lawton, Enid, Oklahoma City and Union
17 City; the Charles E. "Bill" Johnson Correctional Center, located
18 east of Alva, Oklahoma; the Southern Oklahoma Resource Center
19 located at Pauls Valley, Oklahoma the Great Plains Correctional
20 Center located at Hinton, Oklahoma; the Allen Gamble Correctional
21 Center located at Holdenville, Oklahoma; the Red Rock Correctional
22 Center located at Lawton, Oklahoma; and other facilities under the
23 jurisdiction and control of the Department of Corrections or
24 hereafter established by the Department of Corrections;
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2951 Page 5
1 6. "Intermediate revocation facility" means a corrections
2 center operated by the Department of Corrections or a private
3 facility or public trust operating pursuant to contract with the
4 Department of Corrections which provides housing and intensive
5 programmatic services for offenders who have violated the terms or
6 conditions of probation as determined by a supervising probation
7 officer. "Intensive programmatic services" offered by the
8 Department of Corrections includes, but shall not be limited to,
9 alcohol and substance abuse counseling and treatment, mental health
10 counseling and treatment and domestic violence courses and treatment
11 programs;
12 7. "Intermediate sanctions facility" means a community
13 corrections center operated by the Department of Corrections or a
14 private facility or public trust operating pursuant to contract with
15 the Department of Corrections which provides for the housing and
16 programmatic services of offenders such as probation or parole
17 violators or community sentenced offenders placed in the facility
18 for disciplinary sanctions, work release offenders, offenders who
19 need intensive programmatic services, or offenders who have
20 demonstrated positive adjustment while in an institutional setting
21 who need additional programmatic services to enhance their reentry
22 into society upon release from a prison term; and
23 8. "Private prison contractor" means:
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2951 Page 6
1 a. a nongovernmental entity or public trust which,
2 pursuant to a contract with the Department of
3 Corrections, operates an institution within the
4 Department other than a halfway house or intermediate
5 sanctions facility, or provides for the housing, care,
6 and control of inmates and performs other functions
7 related to these responsibilities within a minimum,
8 medium, or maximum security level facility not owned
9 by the Department but operated by the contractor, or
10 b. a nongovernmental entity or public trust which,
11 pursuant to a contract with the United States or
12 another state, provides for the housing, care, and
13 control of minimum or medium security inmates in the
14 custody of the United States or another state, and
15 performs other functions related to these
16 responsibilities other than a halfway house or
17 intermediate sanctions facility within a facility
18 owned or operated by the contractor.
19 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 57 O.S. 2021, Section 508, is
20 amended to read as follows:
21 Section 508. The Director, subject to the approval of the
22 Board, is hereby authorized to create divisions within the
23 Department of Corrections as he they may deem appropriate to
24 effectively manage the Department. The divisions shall be under the
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2951 Page 7
1 imme diate supervision and control of the Director. The Director is
2 hereby authorized to appoint Deputy Directors for the divisions of
3 the Department, who shall be exempt from the Merit System of
4 Personnel Administration Act Civil Service and Human Capital
5 Modernization Act, and to fix the salaries and duties thereof;
6 provided, the salary ranges of said Deputy Directors shall be set by
7 the Legislature in the Department's annual appropriation. The
8 Deputy Directors shall have at least a master's degree from an
9 accredited college or university and at least four (4) years of
10 professional level work experience in corrections; or a bachelor's
11 degree and at least five (5) years of work experience in
12 corrections. Provided, however, that for the position of Deputy
13 Director of administrative services, professional level
14 administrative experience may be substituted for work experience in
15 corrections. The provisions of this act shall not apply to those
16 presently serving as Deputy Director as herein defined.
17 SECTION 4. AMENDATORY 57 O.S. 2021, Section 509, as last
18 amended by Section 2, Chapter 467, O.S.L. 2025 (57 O.S. Supp. 2025,
19 Section 509), is amended to read as follows:
20 Section 509. A. The Department of Corrections is authorized to
21 maintain the following facilities in this state:
22 1. Oklahoma State Penitentiary in McAlester;
23 2. Oklahoma State Reformatory in Granite;
24 3. Lexington Assessment and Reception Center in Lexington;
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2951 Page 8
1 4. Jackie Brannon Correctional Center in McAlester;
2 5. Joseph Harp Correctional Center in Lexington;
3 6. Howard McLeod Correctional Center in Atoka;
4 7. Mack H. Alford Correctional Center in Stringtown;
5 8. Jim E. Hamilton Correctional Center in Hodgen;
6 9. Mabel Bassett Correctional Center in McLoud;
7 10. R.B. "Dick" Conner Correctional Center in Hominy;
8 11. James Crabtree Correctional Center in Helena;
9 12. Jess Dunn Correctional Center in Taft;
10 13. Northeast Oklahoma Community Corrections Center in Vinita;
11 14. John Lilley Correctional Center in Boley;
12 15. Dr. Eddie Warrior Correctional Center in Taft;
13 16. Clara Waters Community Corrections Center in Oklahoma City;
14 17. Lawton Community Corrections Center in Lawton;
15 18. Enid Community Corrections Center in Enid;
16 19. Union City Community Corrections Center in Union City;
17 20. Charles E. "Bill" Johnson Correctional Center in Alva; and
18 21. A facility purchased pursuant to the provisions of Enrolled
19 House Bill No. 2780 of the 1st Session of the 60th Oklahoma
20 Legislature Great Plains Correctional Center in Hinton;
21 22. Allen Gamble Correctional Center in Holdenville; and
22 23. Red Rock Correctional Center in Lawton.
23 B. The Department shall be the legal successor of and, except
24 as otherwise provided in the Oklahoma Corrections Act of 1967, shall
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2951 Page 9
1 have the powers and duties vested by law in the Office of Management
2 and Enterprise Services in all matters relating to penal
3 institutions, heretofore or hereafter established by the Department,
4 which institutions and community corrections centers shall be under
5 the administrative direction and control of the Department.
6 SECTION 5. REPEALER 57 O.S. 2021, Section 504.7, is
7 hereby repealed.
8 SECTION 6. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
9 Passed the House of Representatives the 4th day of March, 2026.

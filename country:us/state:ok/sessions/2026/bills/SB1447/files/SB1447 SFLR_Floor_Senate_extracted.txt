6 An Act relating to the Oklahoma Employees Insurance
Plan; defining terms; prohibiting certain contracts
7 from being awarded in certain situations; requiring
certain scoring to include certain treatment;
8 requiring certain scoring to disfavor certain
pharmacy benefits managers; requiring certain
9 certifications; providing for codification; and
providing an effective date.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1329.2 of Title 74, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 A. As used in this act:
17 1. “Pharmacy benefits manager” means the same as defined in
18 Section 6960 of Title 36 of the Oklahoma Statutes; and
19 2. “Protected health information” means the same as defined in
20 Section 6475.3 of Title 36 of the Oklahoma Statutes.
21 B. No contract between the Oklahoma Employees Insurance Plan
22 and a pharmacy benefits manager (PBM) shall be awarded to a PBM that
23 has settled a lawsuit, been fined, or had judgment against such PBM
24 over Four Million Dollars ($4,000,000.00) in the previous five (5)
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1447 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 year s. The scoring of a request for proposal (RFP) shall include
2 favorable treatment for any bidder who has been domiciled in this
3 state for at least one (1) year on the date of the release of the
4 RFP. The scoring of such RFP shall disfavor a PBM that has a
5 corporate structure that includes a:
6 1. Health insurance company;
7 2. Retail pharmacy chain;
8 3. Specialty pharmacy business;
9 4. Mail-order pharmacy business; or
10 5. Company that manufactures pharmaceuticals.
11 C. The contracted PBM shall certify that such PBM and all
12 contractors who will have access to protected health information are
13 compliant with the Oklahoma Health Information Exchange Act and are
14 SOC 2 Type 2 certified.
15 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
16 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON BUSINESS AND INSURANCE

11 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
12 An Act relating to voter registration; amending 26
O.S. 2021, Section 3-129, which relates to
13 publication of election information; modifying
information that may be published; making language
14 gender-neutral; requiring the Secretary of the State
Election Board to annually issue report with certain
15 statistics; amending 26 O.S. 2021, Sections 4-112, as
amended by Section 18, Chapter 282, O.S.L. 2022, and
16 4-120.6 (26 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 4-112), which
relate to applications and cancellation; requiring
17 certain information to be included on voter
registration applications; requiring certain
18 applications to be deemed requests for cancellation;
requiring the Secretary to send certain notices;
19 updating statutory language; providing for
noncodification; and declaring an emergency.
20
21
22 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
23 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 26 O.S. 2021, Section 3-129, is
24 amended to read as follows:
SB1451 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 Section 3-129. A. The Secretary of the State Election Board is
2 authorized to publish the Roster of State and County Officials
3 Officers, along with election results and statistics, lists of
4 candidates filing for office, voter registration statistics, and
5 such other publications as he the Secretary deems necessary for the
6 discharge of his or her duties.
7 B. The Secretary of the State Election Board shall cause a
8 report to be issued electronically at least once per calendar year
9 with the total number of:
10 1. Notices sent to the chief election official of another state
11 providing information about a voter in that state who is registered
12 to vote in this state;
13 2. Notices sent to each state;
14 3. Notices received from the chief election official of another
15 state indicating that a voter from this state registered to vote in
16 that state;
17 4. Notices received from each state; and
18 5. Voter registrations canceled in this state due to a
19 definitive match of the information provided in such notices with
20 registered voters in another state.
21 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 26 O.S. 2021, Section 4-112, as
22 amended by Section 18, Chapter 282, O.S.L. 2022 (26 O.S. Supp. 2025,
23 Section 4-112), is amended to read as follows:
24
SB1451 HFLR Page 2
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 Section 4-112. A. The Secretary of the State Election Board
2 shall devise and distribute a registration application form to be
3 used for registering voters. Such registration application shall
4 contain the following information:
5 1. The applicant's full name and date of birth, county and
6 place of residence, and mailing address pursuant to the provisions
7 of subsection G of this section;
8 2. A space or section to designate a political party recognized
9 by the laws of the State of Oklahoma this state with which the
10 applicant chooses to be affiliated;
11 3. The Oklahoma driver license or identification card number if
12 the applicant has been issued a current and valid driver license or
13 identification card by Service Oklahoma, or if the applicant does
14 not have a valid Oklahoma driver license or identification card, the
15 last four digits of the voter's Social Security number;
16 4. An oath of the eligibility of the applicant to become a
17 registered voter; and
18 5. A space or section for the applicant to indicate whether he
19 or she has previously been registered to vote in another county or
20 state and, if so, a space or section to indicate his or her prior
21 registration information including his or her name, residence
22 address and county, and political affiliation; and
23
24
SB1451 HFLR Page 3
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 5. 6. Such other information as may be deemed necessary by the
2 Secretary to identify such applicant and to ascertain his or her
3 eligibility.
4 B. A voter registration application shall be signed by the
5 applicant in writing. The applicant shall personally subscribe his
6 or her name to or make his or her mark on the application, and no
7 agent, representative, or employee of the applicant may sign or mark
8 on the applicant's behalf. The signature or mark must shall be the
9 original, handwritten signature, autograph, or mark of the
10 applicant. No facsimile, reproduction, typewritten, or other
11 substitute signature, autograph, or mark will be valid.
12 Notwithstanding any law to the contrary, the Secretary of the State
13 Election Board shall prescribe procedures to authorize any person
14 incapable of personally making a mark to complete a voter
15 registration application with assistance of an official of any voter
16 registration agency or licensed operator specified in Sections 4-
17 109.2 and 4-109.3 of this title. Provided, for applications
18 submitted electronically, in lieu of the signature requirements set
19 forth in this subsection, the applicant shall consent to the use of
20 his or her driver license or identification card signature as
21 provided in Section 4-109.4 of this title.
22 C. Persons who do not indicate a recognized political party or
23 political organization on their registration application shall be
24 designated as Independents.
SB1451 HFLR Page 4
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 D. Any person may apply in writing to the Secretary of the
2 State Election Board for permission to print, copy, or otherwise
3 prepare and distribute the registration applications designed by the
4 Secretary of the State Election Board. The Secretary may revoke any
5 such permission at any time.
6 E. All registration applications shall be distributed to the
7 public at no charge.
8 F. The Secretary also shall prescribe procedures to accept and
9 use the National Mail Voter Registration Form, or its successor, as
10 required by the National Voter Registration Act of 1993. Provided,
11 to be accepted as a valid voter registration application, the form
12 shall include the applicant's original, handwritten signature,
13 autograph, or mark as described in subsection B of this section.
14 G. Applicants for voter registration or for change of voter
15 registration in any way shall provide a residence address and, if
16 different from the residence address, a mailing address. A
17 residence address shall include the street address of the residence,
18 including a full house number, street name or number, apartment or
19 suite number, if applicable, and zip code. If a street address is
20 not available for the residence, applicants shall provide such
21 information as the Secretary of the State Election Board deems
22 necessary for voter registration purposes. A post office box may
23 not be given as a residence address. A mailing address, which shall
24 include the city and zip code, may be the actual emergency
SB1451 HFLR Page 5
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 noti fication or 911 9-1-1 address on file in the local community, a
2 rural route and box number, a post office box number, or a street
3 address.
4 H. A full or partial Social Security number or driver license
5 number in a voter registration record or a voter registration
6 application shall not be considered a public record and shall be
7 kept confidential by the State Election Board and each county
8 election board.
9 I. By completing and submitting a signed voter registration
10 application, the application shall be deemed an applicant's request
11 for the cancellation of any prior voter registration in another
12 county or state pursuant to 52 U.S.C., Section 20507(a)(3)(A).
13 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 26 O.S. 2021, Section 4-120.6, is
14 amended to read as follows:
15 Section 4-120.6. The secretary of each county election board
16 shall cancel the registration of allregistered all registered voters
17 who have registered in another county in Oklahoma this state, or in
18 another state, upon receipt of notice of same. For any registered
19 voter who indicated on his or her voter registration application
20 that he or she was previously registered to vote in another state,
21 the Secretary of the State Election Board shall cause a notice to be
22 sent to the chief election official of the state where the voter was
23 previously registered.
24
SB1451 HFLR Page 6
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
2 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
3 The changes to voter registration applications required by this
4 act shall be effective for any such applications created or printed
5 on or after January 1, 2027, or earlier where practicable.
6 SECTION 5. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
7 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
8 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
9 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

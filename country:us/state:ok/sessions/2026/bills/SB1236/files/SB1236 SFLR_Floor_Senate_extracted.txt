11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
12 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
13 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-127 of Title 70, unless there
14 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
15 A. This act shall be known and may be cited as the
16 “Administrative Report Consolidation Act”.
17 B. There is hereby created the Administrative Report
18 Consolidation Board to evaluate administrative reporting
19 requirements for public school districts and technology center
20 school districts in this state to eliminate duplicative or outdated
21 reporting requirements, consolidate reports where necessary, make
22 legislative recommendations regarding statutory reporting
23 requirements, and create an action plan for continual review of
24 reporting requirements. The Board shall consist of:
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1236 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 1. Four members appointed by the President Pro Tempore of the
2 Senate from a list submitted by members of the Senate consisting of:
3 a. two school district superintendents who have been
4 employed as superintendents for at least five (5)
5 years and who are employed by school districts located
6 in counties with a population of four hundred thousand
7 (400,000) persons or more according to the last
8 preceding Federal Decennial Census,
9 b. a school district superintendent who has been employed
10 as a superintendent for at least five (5) years and
11 who is employed by a school district located in a
12 county with a population of two hundred fifty thousand
13 (250,000) persons or more but fewer than four hundred
14 thousand (400,000) persons according to the last
15 preceding Federal Decennial Census, and
16 c. a technology center school district superintendent who
17 has been employed as a superintendent for at least
18 five (5) years and who is employed by a technology
19 center school district located in a county with a
20 population of four hundred thousand (400,000) persons
21 or more according to the last preceding Federal
22 Decennial Census;
23
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1236 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. Four members appointed by the Speaker of the House of
2 Representatives from a list submitted by members of the House of
3 Representatives consisting of:
4 a. two school district superintendents who have been
5 employed as superintendents for at least five (5)
6 years and who are employed by school districts located
7 in counties with a population of four hundred thousand
8 (400,000) persons or more according to the last
9 preceding Federal Decennial Census,
10 b. a school district superintendent who has been employed
11 as a superintendent for at least five (5) years and
12 who is employed by a school district located in a
13 county with a population of two hundred fifty thousand
14 (250,000) persons or more but fewer than four hundred
15 thousand (400,000) persons according to the last
16 preceding Federal Decennial Census, and
17 c. a technology center school district superintendent who
18 has been employed as a superintendent for at least
19 five (5) years and who is employed by a technology
20 center school district located in a county with a
21 population of fewer than four hundred thousand
22 (400,000) persons according to the last preceding
23 Federal Decennial Census; and
24 3. Three members appointed by the Governor consisting of:
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1236 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 a. a school district superintendent who has been employed
2 as a superintendent for at least five (5) years and
3 who is employed by a school district located in a
4 county with a population of two hundred fifty thousand
5 (250,000) persons or more but fewer than four hundred
6 thousand (400,000) persons according to the last
7 preceding Federal Decennial Census, and
8 b. two school district superintendents who have been
9 employed as superintendents for at least five (5)
10 years and who are employed by school districts located
11 in counties with a population of fewer than two
12 hundred fifty thousand (250,000) persons according to
13 the last preceding Federal Decennial Census.
14 C. Initial appointments to the Administrative Report
15 Consolidation Board shall be made within thirty (30) days after the
16 effective date of this act. The President Pro Tempore of the Senate
17 and the Speaker of the House of Representatives shall each appoint
18 one member for one (1) year, one member for two (2) years, and two
19 members for three (3) years. The Governor shall appoint one member
20 for one (1) year, one member for two (2) years, and one member for
21 three (3) years. Members shall serve until their successors are
22 duly appointed for a term of three (3) years. Vacancies shall be
23 filled by the appointing authority. The Board shall annually by
24 August 30 elect from its membership a chair and vice chair.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1236 SFLR Page 4
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 D. The Board shall conduct an organizational meeting not later
2 than August 1, 2026. The Board shall meet at least annually, or as
3 often as may be required to perform the duties imposed upon it.
4 Board members may participate via videoconference if they are
5 visible and audible to each other through a video monitor.
6 E. A quorum of the Board shall be required to approve any final
7 action of the Board. For the purposes of this section, six members
8 shall constitute a quorum.
9 F. Members of the Board shall receive no compensation or travel
10 reimbursement.
11 G. The Board may contract with an independent entity to assist
12 in the evaluation and analysis of reporting requirements required by
13 subsection B of this section.
14 H. The State Department of Education and the Oklahoma
15 Department of Career and Technology Education shall provide staff
16 and administrative support for the Board.
17 I. The Board shall electronically submit a report of its
18 findings and recommendations by August 1, 2027, to the Governor, the
19 President Pro Tempore of the Senate, the Speaker of the House of
20 Representatives, the State Department of Education, and the Oklahoma
21 Department of Career and Technology Education. The report shall
22 include a recommendation to consolidate all required annual reports
23 to not more than ten (10) each for public school districts and
24 technology center school districts. The Board may electronically
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1236 SFLR Page 5
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 subm it additional reports of its findings and recommendations by
2 August 1, 2028, and each August 1 thereafter.
3 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
4 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
5 of the public peace, health, or safety, an emergency is hereby
6 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
7 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
8 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON EDUCATION

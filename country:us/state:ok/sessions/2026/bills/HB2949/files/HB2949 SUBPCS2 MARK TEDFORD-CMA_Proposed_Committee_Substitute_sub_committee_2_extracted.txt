7 PROPOSED SUBCOMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
8 An Act relating to child care facilities; defining
terms; providing method for calculating child's age;
9 directing that licensed child care facilities
maintain certain staff-to-child ratios; permitting
10 providers to utilize mixed-age groupings; providing
minimum square footage required per child; directing
11 the Department of Human Services to promulgate rules;
providing that rules be subject to review and
12 approval by certain subcommittee; amending 10 O.S.
2021, Section 405.4, which relates to the Oklahoma
13 Child Care Facilities Licensing Act; removing floor
area requirement for certain rooms; providing for
14 codification; providing an effective date; and
declaring an emergency.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 405.6 of Title 10, unless there
20 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. For purposes of this section:
22 1. "Mixed-age group" means a group of children containing two
23 or more age categories;
24
Req. No. 16162 Page 1
1 2. "Standard ratio" means a staff-to-child ratio applicable to
2 a group composed entirely of children within the same age category;
3 3. "Usable indoor space" means space available for children's
4 activities and movement, excluding bathrooms, kitchens, storage
5 areas, mechanical rooms, offices, and closets; and
6 4. "Youngest age category present" means the youngest age
7 category represented within a group at any time.
8 B. For purposes of staff-to-child ratios and group placement, a
9 child's age may be calculated within thirty (30) calendar days
10 before or after the child's birthday.
11 C. When a group consists solely of children within a single-age
12 category, licensed child care providers shall maintain the following
13 minimum staff-to-child ratios:
14 1. For infants from birth through eleven (11) months of age,
15 there shall be one staff member for every five children;
16 2. For children twelve (12) months of age through twenty-three
17 (23) months of age, there shall be one staff member for every eight
18 children;
19 3. For children twenty-four (24) months of age through thirty-
20 five (35) months of age, there shall be one staff member for every
21 eleven children;
22 4. For children thirty-six (36) months of age through forty-
23 seven (47) months of age, there shall be one staff member for every
24 fifteen children;
Req. No. 16162 Page 2
1 5. For children forty-eight (48) months of age through fifty-
2 nine (59) months of age, there shall be one staff member for every
3 eighteen children;
4 6. For children sixty (60) months of age through seventy-one
5 (71) months of age, there shall be one staff member for every
6 twenty-five children; and
7 7. For children six (6) years of age and older, there shall be
8 one staff member for every twenty-eight children.
9 D. Licensed child care providers may utilize mixed-age
10 groupings as an alternative to standard ratios. When a mixed-age
11 group is used, licensed child care providers shall maintain the
12 following minimum staff-to-child ratios:
13 1. For infants, there shall be one staff member for every eight
14 children;
15 2. For children one (1) year of age, there shall be one staff
16 member for every twelve children;
17 3. For children two (2) years of age, there shall be one staff
18 member for every sixteen children;
19 4. For children three (3) years of age, there shall be one
20 staff member for every twenty children;
21 5. For children four (4) years of age, there shall be one staff
22 member for every twenty-four children;
23 6. For children five (5) years of age, there shall be one staff
24 member for every thirty children; and
Req. No. 16162 Page 3
1 7. For children six (6) years of age and older, there shall be
2 one staff member for every thirty-five children.
3 In any mixed-age group, no more than fifty percent (50%) of the
4 total group size may consist of children in the youngest age
5 category present. The applicable ratio shall be determined by the
6 youngest age category present.
7 E. Licensed child care providers shall provide not less than
8 thirty (30) square feet of usable indoor space per child, based on
9 the maximum number of children present at any one time.
10 F. The Department of Human Services shall promulgate rules as
11 necessary to implement the provisions of this section. Any rule
12 promulgated pursuant to this section shall be subject to review and
13 approval by the Oklahoma Child Care Advisory Committee Programs
14 Subcommittee.
15 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 10 O.S. 2021, Section 405.4, is
16 amended to read as follows:
17 Section 405.4. A. Any person with a bachelor's degree or
18 postgraduate degree shall be considered as having met the
19 educational requirements for a director of a full-time child care
20 center required by the Department of Human Services. Nothing in
21 this section shall be construed as to affect annual continuing
22 education requirements. The director of a child care center may
23 also qualify as a master teacher for children of all ages.
24
Req. No. 16162 Page 4
1 B. Any person replacing a master teacher at a child care center
2 shall be granted a one-year probationary period to fulfill the
3 educational qualifications required by the Department for a master
4 teacher. The Department may extend the probationary period an
5 additional year as long as the person is actively pursuing that
6 goal.
7 C. Licensed child care facilities opened or expanded before
8 November 1, 2016, shall have thirty-five (35) square feet of floor
9 area per infant in rooms occupied only by infants. New construction
10 and existing space not previously licensed for child care after
11 November 1, 2016, shall have forty (40) square feet of floor area
12 per infant in rooms occupied only by infants.
13 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
14 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
15 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
16 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
17 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

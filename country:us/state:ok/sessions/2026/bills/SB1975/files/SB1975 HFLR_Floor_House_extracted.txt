1 Section 1210.704. A. Beginning with the 2024–2025 school year,
2 all public high schools in this state shall make a minimum of four
3 advanced placement courses available to students.
4 B. School district boards of education shall be responsible for
5 ensuring annually that all high school students have access to
6 advanced placement courses beginning in the 2024-2025 school year.
7 Such access may be provided through enrollment in courses offered
8 through:
9 1. A school site or sites within the district;
10 2. A technology center school within the district;
11 3. An online learning program offered by the Statewide Charter
12 School Board or one of its vendors; or
13 4. A school site or sites in another school district.
14 C. Each public school district in this state shall make
15 advanced placement examinations available each school year to any
16 student who resides in the district. No later than August 31 of
17 each year, each school district shall post on its website notice of
18 the date each advanced placement examination is to be administered.
19 The notice shall state that the examination is available to students
20 who reside in the district and shall include registration dates and
21 procedures to register for the examination.
22 D. The Statewide Charter School Board shall maintain an online
23 learning platform to provide high quality online learning
24 opportunities for Oklahoma students that are aligned with the

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 subj ect matter standards adopted by the State Board of Education
2 pursuant to Section 11-103.6 of this title. The Board shall
3 implement online courses, with an emphasis on science, technology,
4 engineering, and math (STEM) courses, foreign language courses, and
5 advanced placement courses. The online platform shall be available
6 to all Oklahoma school districts students in this state.
7 D. E. The State Department of Education shall provide
8 information to all boards of education, to be distributed to their
9 students and parents, on available opportunities and the enrollment
10 process for students to take advanced placement courses. The
11 information shall explain the value of advanced placement courses in
12 preparing students for postsecondary-level coursework, enabling
13 students to gain access to postsecondary opportunities, and
14 qualifying for scholarships and other financial aid opportunities.
15 E. F. The State Department of Education shall retain records of
16 which options outlined in subsection B of this section boards of
17 education selected for their students and make the information
18 available on the Department’s website.
19 F. G. As used in this section, “advanced placement course”
20 shall have the same meaning as provided in paragraph 1 of Section
21 1210.702 of this title.

8 POLICY COMMITTEE RECOMMENDATION
9 An Act relating to milk; amending 2 O.S. 2021,
Section 7-414, which relates to the Oklahoma Milk and
10 Milk Products Acts; providing additional acts that
are not prohibited; removing gallon limit for
11 incidental sale of raw milk; providing certain
protection from liability; and providing an effective
12 date.

14
15 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
16 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 2 O.S. 2021, Section 7-414, is
17 amended to read as follows:
18 Section 7-414. A. The provisions of the Oklahoma Milk and Milk
19 Products Act shall not be construed to:
20 1. Include incidental sales of raw, unpasteurized cow milk,
21 goat milk, sheep milk, donkey milk, and horse milk directly to
22 consumers at:
23 a. the farm where the milk is produced,
24 b. feed stores,
Req. No. 16424 Page 1

1 c. restaurants, for off-premise consumption only,
2 d. at a farmer's market, or
3 e. via delivery from the farm where the milk is produced;
4 2. Preclude Prohibit the advertising of the incidental sale of
5 raw, unpasteurized cow milk, goat milk, sheep milk, donkey milk, and
6 horse milk; and or
7 3. Prohibit any farmer or producer from making cheese using
8 milk or cream produced on the farm of such farmer or producer.
9 B. For purposes of this section, incidental sales of goat milk
10 are those sales where the average monthly number of gallons sold
11 does not exceed one hundred (100) Any producer or seller of raw,
12 unpasteurized milk, sold pursuant to this section, shall not be
13 liable for any incidental illness due to the consumption of raw,
14 unpasteurized milk by a consumer.
15 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

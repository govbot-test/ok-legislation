FOR
4 HOUSE JOINT
RESOLUTION NO. 1060 By: Pae
5
6

8 PROPOSED COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 A Joint Resolution directing the Secretary of State
to refer to the people for their approval or
10 rejection a proposed amendment to the Constitution of
the State of Oklahoma by adding a new Section 8G to
11 Article X; establishing ad valorem tax liability
amount equal to tax year 2026 ad valorem liability
12 amount for disabled firefighters, disabled police
officers, disabled volunteer firefighters, and their
13 surviving spouses; requiring individual to own and
occupy homestead by certain date; requiring proof of
14 ownership and occupancy; prohibiting provisions from
applying after certain date; providing ballot title;
15 and directing filing.
16
17
18 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES AND THE SENATE OF THE
19 2ND SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE:
20 SECTION 1. The Secretary of State shall refer to the people for
21 their approval or rejection, as and in the manner provided by law,
22 the following proposed amendment to the Constitution of the State of
23 Oklahoma by adding a new Section 8G to Article X thereof, to read as
24 follows:
Req. No. 16225 Page 1
1 A. Despite any provision to the contrary, beginning January 1,
2 2027, the following individuals shall have an ad valorem tax
3 liability for their homestead in the amount equal to the ad valorem
4 tax liability for such homestead in the 2026 tax year:
5 1. Each head of household who has retired from active duty as a
6 firefighter and has been certified as one hundred percent (100%)
7 permanently disabled by the Oklahoma Firefighters Pension and
8 Retirement System or its successor or by the pension authority of
9 another state that administers retirement and disability benefits
10 for firefighters and has certified the individual as one hundred
11 percent (100%) disabled due to a service-connected or line of duty
12 injury;
13 2. Each head of household who has retired from active duty as a
14 police officer and has been certified as one hundred percent (100%)
15 permanently disabled by the Oklahoma Police Pension and Retirement
16 System or its successor or by the pension authority of another state
17 that administers retirement and disability benefits for police
18 officers and has certified the individual as one hundred percent
19 (100%) disabled due to a service-connected or line of duty injury;
20 and
21 3. Each head of household who has served as a volunteer
22 firefighter and has been certified as one hundred percent (100%)
23 permanently disabled under the federal Public Safety Officers'
24 Benefits Program or its successor.
Req. No. 16225 Page 2
1 B. The provisions of this section shall only apply if the head
2 of household described in subsection A of this section owns and
3 occupies the property as his or her primary residence as of January
4 1, 2027. The provisions of this section shall not apply to any
5 homestead acquired after January 1, 2027.
6 C. The provisions of this section shall also apply to the
7 surviving spouse of a head of household described in subsection A of
8 this section.
9 D. The head of household described in subsection A of this
10 section or his or her surviving spouse shall be required to provide
11 proof of ownership and occupancy as necessary to the county assessor
12 in the county in which the homestead resides.
13 SECTION 2. The Ballot Title for the proposed Constitutional
14 amendment as set forth in SECTION 1 of this resolution shall be in
15 the following form:
16 BALLOT TITLE
17 Legislative Referendum No. ____ State Question No. ____
18 THE GIST OF THE PROPOSITION IS AS FOLLOWS:
19 This measure amends Article 10 of the Oklahoma Constitution. It
20 creates a new section that provides the amount of ad valorem tax
21 liability on the homestead owned by a disabled police officer,
22 disabled firefighter, volunteer firefighter, and their surviving
23 spouse shall be in the amount equal to the ad valorem tax
24 liability on such homestead in the 2026 tax year. This
Req. No. 16225 Page 3
1 treatment shall only apply if the head of household owns and
2 occupies the homestead as of January 1, 2027, and does not apply
3 to homesteads acquired after such date. Proof of ownership and
4 occupancy is required to be provided to the county assessor as
5 necessary.
6 SHALL THE PROPOSAL BE APPROVED?
7 FOR THE PROPOSAL — YES _____________
8 AGAINST THE PROPOSAL — NO _____________
9 SECTION 3. The Chief Clerk of the House of Representatives,
10 immediately after the passage of this resolution, shall prepare and
11 file one copy thereof, including the Ballot Title set forth in
12 SECTION 2 hereof, with the Secretary of State and one copy with the
13 Attorney General.

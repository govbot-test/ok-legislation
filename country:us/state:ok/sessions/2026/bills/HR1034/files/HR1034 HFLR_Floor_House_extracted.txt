1 WHEREAS, Black History Month is celebrated in February to
2 commemorate the birthdays of Frederick Douglass and Abraham Lincoln,
3 both significant leaders in the abolition of slavery; and
4 WHEREAS, numerous Oklahomans identify as African American; and
5 WHEREAS, Black History Month serves as an opportunity to
6 acknowledge and recognize the role Black Americans have played in
7 shaping of our culture, society, and history, in Oklahoma and the
8 United States; and
9 WHEREAS, the contributions of African Americans include but are
10 not limited to advancements in civil rights, business, education,
11 innovation, and leadership that have shaped this state and country
12 to what we know today; and
13 WHEREAS, the State of Oklahoma acknowledges the impact and role
14 Black Americans have played in shaping the culture, progress, and
15 history of our country; and
16 WHEREAS, it is important to recognize and celebrate the
17 significant achievements and progress made by groups of Americans
18 that strengthen our society as a whole; and
19 WHEREAS, the observance of Black History Month serves as an
20 opportunity for all citizens to celebrate the achievements of
21 African Americans, furthering an environment of togetherness.
22 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
23 OF THE 2ND SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE:
24

BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 THAT the month of February is formally recognized by the
2 Oklahoma House of Representatives as Black History Month.
3 THAT the Oklahoma House of Representatives recognizes the
4 contributions of Black Americans to the history of Oklahoma and the
5 United States.

14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-271 of Title 63, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. There is hereby created the Insulin Access and Affordability
19 Program, which shall be administered by the State Department of
20 Health for the purpose of increasing patient access to affordable
21 insulin in this state, reducing prescription drug costs for
22 consumers and payors, bolstering American manufacturing of
23 pharmaceuticals, increasing marketplace competition, and addressing
24 shortages in the market for generic insulin.
Req. No. 3807 Page 1
1 B. The Department shall, with funds appropriated by the
2 Legislature, provide financial support to one or more pharmaceutical
3 manufacturers that manufacture, or are currently developing, a fast-
4 acting biosimilar insulin. Each such pharmaceutical manufacturer
5 shall manufacture the fast-acting biosimilar insulin in the United
6 States.
7 C. Prior to disbursement of any funds, the Department shall
8 enter into a memorandum of understanding (MOU) with the
9 pharmaceutical manufacturer or manufacturers, and each manufacturer
10 shall demonstrate a match of non-state funds equal to the amount
11 received by the manufacturer under the Insulin Access and
12 Affordability Program. The MOU shall include, but not be limited
13 to:
14 1. A commitment by the manufacturer to produce a fast-acting
15 biosimilar insulin at a low net cost without rebates, except as
16 required by law, within a time frame agreed upon between the
17 Department and the manufacturer;
18 2. A commitment by the manufacturer to manufacture fast-acting
19 biosimilar insulin in the United States;
20 3. Estimated savings from the purchase of low-cost, fast-acting
21 biosimilar insulin including, but not limited to, the estimated
22 savings to residents of this state and public and private payors
23 from the availability of low-cost, fast-acting biosimilar insulin;
24 and
Req. No. 3807 Page 2
1 4. Provisions for the repayment of the funds provided under the
2 Insulin Access and Affordability Program if the manufacturer fails
3 to produce and distribute a low-cost, fast-acting biosimilar insulin
4 or otherwise fails to substantially comply with the requirements of
5 this section or the MOU.
6 D. The pharmaceutical manufacturer or manufacturers shall
7 submit reports annually to the Department on the development,
8 production, and distribution, as applicable, of the fast-acting
9 biosimilar insulin in a manner prescribed by the Department. The
10 Department shall compile the reports into a single report and shall
11 annually submit such report electronically to the Governor, the
12 President Pro Tempore of the Senate, and the Speaker of the House of
13 Representatives.
14 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
15 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
16 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
17 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
18 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

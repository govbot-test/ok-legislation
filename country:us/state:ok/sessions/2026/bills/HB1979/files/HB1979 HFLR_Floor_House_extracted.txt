11 An Act relating to children; creating the Early
Childhood Task Force; providing for task force
12 membership; providing for election of chair and vice-
chair; permitting the selection of honorary chair;
13 directing task force to provide reports; disallowing
certain members from receiving compensation or
14 reimbursement; permitting certain members to claim
certain expenses; requiring task force to be subject
15 to the Oklahoma Open Records Act and Oklahoma Open
Meeting Act; providing that task force documents and
16 recommendations shall only be considered advice;
providing a vision statement; providing a mission;
17 providing principles the task force is to be guided
by; providing tasks for the task force; providing for
18 codification; and providing an effective date.

3 C. The Early Childhood Task Force shall elect a chair and vice-
4 chair, or co-chairs, from the Task Force's membership, and the chair
5 or co-chairs shall establish rules for the Task Force's meetings and
6 the conducting of business. Upon the absence or vacancy of the
7 chair, the vice-chair shall serve as chair in an acting capacity.
8 D. An honorary chair may be selected to serve in a ceremonial
9 capacity. The honorary chair shall not serve as a voting member of
10 the Task Force but may engage with the work of the Task Force in an
11 advisory role or in a manner deemed appropriate by the chair or co-
12 chairs.
13 E. The Task Force shall develop and submit an initial report to
14 the President Pro Tempore of the Senate and the Speaker of the House
15 of Representatives by November 2027 and a final report by November
16 2028.
17 F. Members shall receive no compensation or reimbursement for
18 expenses and shall serve voluntarily. Officers or employees of
19 state agencies who are appointed to the Task Force as part of their
20 duties shall be authorized to participate on the Task Force and may
21 claim subsistence, allowance, mileage, or associated expenses from
22 their respective agency budgets as permitted by law.
23 G. The Task Force shall be subject to the Oklahoma Open Records
24 Act and the Oklahoma Open Meeting Act.

1 H. Plans, reports, or recommendations of any nature adopted by
2 the Task Force shall be considered advice to the Legislature and
3 shall not be construed as official policies, positions, or
4 interpretations of laws, rules, or regulations by any department or
5 agency of state government, nor shall any such department or agency
6 be bound in any manner to consider such advice when conducting its
7 advisory and regulatory affairs.
8 I. The Task Force shall be guided by the following vision
9 statement: Oklahoma children, especially those between zero (0) and
10 five (5) years of age, deserve healthy childhoods that are supported
11 by a robust early childhood system which improves outcomes and
12 effectively provides high-quality early care and education, improves
13 and enhances school readiness, adapts to the needs of families and
14 communities, and is equitably accessible to all communities across
15 the state.
16 J. The Task Force shall work towards the mission of creating a
17 framework for a more efficient and effective governance structure
18 for early childhood programming, such as a coordinated,
19 consolidated, or new independent agency.
20 K. The Task Force's work shall be guided by a commitment to the
21 following principles:
22 1. Children, family, and community well-being shall be centered
23 throughout the Early Childhood Task Force's work;
24

15 6. Access to affordable and quality health care, nutrition,
16 early education and care, mental health, home visiting, early
17 intervention, support for families, early learning, and other

19 decisively impact academic success, economic development
20 opportunities, and the prosperity of Oklahoma;
21 7. Efforts shall be made to use data-informed approaches and
22 explore better ways to develop shared data systems;
23 8. Work shall focus on strengthening and expanding the early
24 education and care delivery system already in use in Oklahoma;

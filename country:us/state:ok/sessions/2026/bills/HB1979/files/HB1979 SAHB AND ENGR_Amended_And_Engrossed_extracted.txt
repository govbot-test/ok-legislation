7 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
8 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
9 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 390 of Title 10, unless there is
10 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
11 A. There is hereby created the Early Childhood Task Force to
12 study and make recommendations to improve service access to and
13 delivery to families and children and increase the quantity of high-
14 quality early childhood services.
15 B. The task force shall be comprised of eighteen (18) members
16 to be appointed as follows:
17 1. The Director of Human Services, or a designee;
18 2. The State Commissioner of Health, or a designee;
19 3. The Chief Executive Officer of the Oklahoma Department of
20 Commerce, or a designee;
21 4. The Superintendent of Public Instruction, or a designee;
22 5. The Administrator of the Oklahoma Health Care Authority, or
23 a designee;
24

1 6. A representative of a statewide association representing the
2 business community that is exempt from taxation under Section
3 501(c)(6) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended,
4 appointed by the Governor;
5 7. Two child care providers, each of whom is a member of a
6 statewide association representing child care providers, appointed
7 by the Director of Human Services;
8 8. A representative of an Oklahoma parent-child support
9 organization, selected by the Director of the Early Childhood
10 Education Institute of the University of Oklahoma;
11 9. The Executive Director of Oklahoma Partnership for School
12 Readiness, or a designee;
13 10. A representative of a statewide association that advocates
14 for early childhood education, appointed by the Superintendent of
15 Public Instruction;
16 11. A Senator, appointed by the President Pro Tempore of the
17 Senate;
18 12. A Representative, appointed by the Speaker of the House of
19 Representatives;
20 13. A director of a public school early childhood program,
21 appointed by the Superintendent of Public Instruction;
22 14. A representative responsible for programs under Part C of
23 the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), appointed by
24 the State Commissioner of Health;

1 15. A representative from Oklahoma State University’s
2 Department of Human Development and Family Science, appointed by the
3 Dean of the College of Education and Human Sciences of Oklahoma
4 State University;
5 16. A pediatric specialist appointed by the Chair of the
6 Department of Pediatrics of the University of Oklahoma College of
7 Medicine; and
8 17. A representative of a statewide professional association
9 representing the workforce for infant and early childhood mental
10 health services, appointed by the Commissioner of Mental Health and
11 Substance Abuse Services.
12 C. The Early Childhood Task Force shall elect a chair and vice
13 chair, or co-chairs, from the task force’s membership, and the chair
14 or co-chairs shall establish rules for the task force’s meetings and
15 the conducting of business. Upon the absence or vacancy of the
16 chair, the vice chair shall serve as chair in an acting capacity.
17 D. An honorary chair may be selected to serve in a ceremonial
18 capacity. The honorary chair shall not serve as a voting member of
19 the task force but may engage with the work of the task force in an
20 advisory role or in a manner deemed appropriate by the chair or co-
21 chairs.
22 E. The task force shall develop and electronically submit an
23 initial report to the President Pro Tempore of the Senate and the

1 Spea ker of the House of Representatives by November 2027 and a final
2 report by November 2028.
3 F. Members shall receive no compensation or reimbursement for
4 expenses and shall serve voluntarily. Officers or employees of
5 state agencies who are appointed to the task force as part of their
6 duties shall be authorized to participate on the task force and may
7 claim subsistence, allowance, mileage, or associated expenses from
8 their respective agency budgets as permitted by law.
9 G. The task force shall be subject to the Oklahoma Open Records
10 Act and the Oklahoma Open Meeting Act.
11 H. Plans, reports, or recommendations of any nature adopted by
12 the task force shall be considered advice to the Legislature and
13 shall not be construed as official policies, positions, or
14 interpretations of laws, rules, or regulations by any department or
15 agency of state government, nor shall any such department or agency
16 be bound in any manner to consider such advice when conducting its
17 advisory and regulatory affairs.
18 I. The task force shall be guided by the following vision
19 statement: Oklahoma children, especially those between zero (0) and
20 five (5) years of age, deserve healthy childhoods that are supported
21 by a robust early childhood system which improves outcomes and
22 effectively provides high-quality early care and education, improves
23 and enhances school readiness, adapts to the needs of families and
24

1 comm unities, and is equitably accessible to all communities across
2 the state.
3 J. The task force shall work toward the mission of creating a
4 framework for a more efficient and effective governance structure
5 for early childhood programming, such as a coordinated,
6 consolidated, or new independent agency.
7 K. The task force’s work shall be guided by a commitment to the
8 following principles:
9 1. Children, family, and community well-being shall be centered
10 throughout the Early Childhood Task Force’s work;
11 2. Efforts shall be focused on maximizing efficiencies to
12 reduce the administrative burdens on families and improve access to
13 early childhood services through the creation of a new governance
14 structure;
15 3. Any possible consolidation of early childhood services shall
16 be informed by what families and communities say are wanted and
17 needed;
18 4. Access to programs and services shall be equitable and
19 available to all children and families regardless of socioeconomic
20 status or geographic location;
21 5. Intentional focus shall be placed on developing strategies
22 to reach families and young children in health care, early
23 education, and care deserts or furthest from opportunity and
24 existing resources;
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 1979 Page 6

1 6. Access to affordable and quality health care, nutrition,
2 early education and care, mental health, home visiting, early
3 intervention, support for families, early learning, and other
4 services in the critical prenatal to five-year age period will
5 decisively impact academic success, economic development
6 opportunities, and the prosperity of Oklahoma;
7 7. Efforts shall be made to use data-informed approaches and
8 explore better ways to develop shared data systems;
9 8. Work shall focus on strengthening and expanding the early
10 education and care delivery system already in use in Oklahoma;
11 9. Alignment across sectors and state agencies shall increase
12 capacity, maximize funding, and create a more streamlined delivery
13 of services;
14 10. The importance of growing a well-educated and well-
15 supported early childhood workforce shall remain at the forefront
16 and be informed by the task force’s work;
17 11. While focusing on advancing the delivery system, work shall
18 highlight the importance of defining quality care and expanding
19 capacity and access to create a greater quantity of quality
20 services;
21 12. To incentivize innovation and respond to family needs,
22 communities must be empowered to develop local, community-based
23 solutions that take a whole-child and whole-family approach in
24 responding to needs;
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 1979 Page 7

1 13. To ensure holistic childhood well-being and improve health
2 outcomes, there must be alignment between state efforts to improve
3 early learning and care and existing prenatal and maternal health
4 initiatives; and
5 14. Strengthening early childhood developmental health is vital
6 to ensuring that children are prepared to achieve academic success
7 and are prepared for lifelong learning.
8 L. The task force’s duties shall include:
9 1. Conducting an analysis of the current early childhood
10 delivery system in Oklahoma and how it is financed with specific
11 focus on gaps, inefficiencies, and redundancies. This work should
12 focus on how creating a new governance structure for early childhood
13 services could overcome these challenges and improve how care is
14 delivered, accessed, and funded. This analysis must specifically
15 include:
16 a. a review of current early childhood programs and
17 policies dispersed between the Department of Human
18 Services, State Department of Health, Department of
19 Mental Health and Substance Abuse Services, Oklahoma
20 Department of Commerce, Oklahoma Health Care
21 Authority, and State Department of Education. The
22 review should pay special attention to where current
23 statutory authority over certain operations exists and
24 where possible areas of redundancy occur,
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 1979 Page 8

1 b. a review of how current funding systems blend and
2 various funding opportunities, with special attention
3 as to how funding streams are currently utilized for
4 early childhood programs that are housed across
5 departments. This fiscal review should map the routes
6 all private, public, federal, and state funding
7 streams take from funding authority to program
8 application,
9 c. a review of how Oklahoma might integrate existing
10 programming into a consolidated delivery system and
11 what needs might exist for new programming that the
12 state currently lacks,
13 d. recommendations on how cross-agency collaboration and
14 partnerships can be fostered to develop a whole-of-
15 government approach to strengthening the delivery of
16 early childhood services, specifically ensuring that
17 data infrastructure is integrated across agencies,
18 accounts for both qualitative and quantitative
19 methods, and can be widely utilized by multiple state
20 agencies,
21 e. recommendations on how a new governance structure
22 might maximize efficiencies for early childhood
23 providers and their workforce. This work should
24 determine how licensing, workforce development and

1 support, and facility review can be improved to
2 eliminate barriers to entry for providers and
3 professionals. This should also include a review of
4 current regulatory structures that complicate the
5 process of entering the early childhood sector,
6 f. recommendations on how a new governance structure can
7 strengthen the existing health care workforce to
8 better support the development of young children and
9 well-being of mothers through improved access to
10 comprehensive and preventive care. This should also
11 include a review of how to secure improved health
12 outcomes for children, financial sustainability of
13 delivery models, and robust workforce pipelines, and
14 g. an analysis of key lessons learned from states already
15 advancing consolidated systems of early childhood
16 governance to inform Oklahoma’s own process;
17 2. Conducting a series of stakeholder engagement opportunities
18 to elicit feedback on the current early childhood governance
19 structure and better understand the needs of parents, families,
20 providers, and businesses;
21 3. Following the engagement of stakeholders, conducting a
22 review that synthesizes feedback received from families, providers,
23 community leaders, and the business sector. This review shall
24 include recommendations on how the state delivery system and

2 needs. It shall also include recommendations for how the state can

6 4. Drafting a proposed transition plan to ensure the continuous

9 a. include detailed recommendations for what programs and

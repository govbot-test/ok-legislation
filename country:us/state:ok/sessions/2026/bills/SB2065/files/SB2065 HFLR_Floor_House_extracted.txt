9 An Act relating to state symbols; creating the Lucile
Morehouse Pollinator Act; providing short title;
10 designating the European honeybee as the state
agricultural pollinator; designating the American
11 bumblebee as the state native insect; designating the
Carolina mantis as the state predator insect;
12 designating the rainbow scarab as the state soil
conservation insect; providing for noncodification;
13 providing for codification; and providing an
effective date.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law not to be
18 codified in the Oklahoma Statutes reads as follows:
19 This act shall be known and may be cited as the “Lucile
20 Morehouse Pollinator Act”.
21 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 98.26 of Title 25, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:

1 The European honeybee, Apis mellifera, is hereby designated and
2 adopted as the state agricultural pollinator of the State of
3 Oklahoma.
4 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
5 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 98.27 of Title 25, unless there
6 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
7 The American bumblebee, Bombus pensylvanicus, is hereby
8 designated and adopted as the state native insect of the State of
9 Oklahoma.
10 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
11 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 98.28 of Title 25, unless there
12 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
13 The Carolina mantis, Stagmomantis carolina, is hereby designated
14 and adopted as the state predator insect of the State of Oklahoma.
15 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 98.29 of Title 25, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 The rainbow scarab, Phanaeus vindex, is hereby designated and
19 adopted as the state soil conservation insect of the State of
20 Oklahoma.
21 SECTION 6. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 15-101 of Title 43A, unless
16 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 As used in this section:
18 1. "Behavioral health clinical intern" means a graduate student
19 enrolled in a nationally accredited clinical training program that
20 meets minimum credentials for service provided or code billed;
21 2. "Clinical training program" means programs of study that are
22 nationally accredited master's or doctoral programs preparing
23 students for the licensed marital and family therapist (LMFT),
24 licensed professional counselor (LPC), licensed clinical social
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2947 Page 1
1 work er (LCSW), licensed alcohol and drug counselor (LADC), or
2 psychologist state licensures to practice;
3 3. "Licensed behavioral health provider" means a behavioral
4 health professional who is a licensed professional counselor (LPC),
5 licensed marital and family therapist (LMFT), licensed behavioral
6 practitioner (LBP), licensed clinical social worker (LCSW), licensed
7 alcohol and drug counselor (LADC), or licensed psychologist; and
8 4. "Nationally accredited program" means that the training
9 program has met all training standards to hold an active
10 accreditation aligned with the discipline of study, including, but
11 not limited to, the Commission on Accreditation for Marriage and
12 Family Therapy Education (COAMFTE), the Council for Accreditation of
13 Counseling and Related Educational Programs (CACREP), the Council on
14 Social Work Education (CSWE), or the American Psychological
15 Association (APA).
16 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
17 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 15-102 of Title 43A, unless
18 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
19 The Oklahoma Medicaid plan administrator shall expand services
20 by adding a provider code for master's and doctoral level behavioral
21 health clinical interns.
22 Behavioral health clinical intern providers shall only provide
23 services billable through clinical sites that are accredited in good
24 standing with behavioral health services site accreditations such
ENGR. H. B. NO. 2947 Page 2
1 as: the Commission on Accreditation of Rehabilitation Facilities
2 (CARF), The Joint Commission, the American Society of Addiction
3 Medicine (ASAM) Level of Care Certification, program certification
4 through the Oklahoma Department of Mental Health and Substance Abuse
5 Services, the Council on Accreditation (COA), or other national
6 behavioral health service site accrediting body.
7 Behavioral health clinical interns may provide services within
8 their clinical scope of practice under the on-site supervision of a
9 licensed behavioral health provider. On-site supervisors may only
10 supervise six or fewer behavioral health clinical interns at any
11 time.
12 Behavioral health clinical interns shall comply with all
13 registration, training, documentation, and protocols required of
14 other behavioral health providers in the Oklahoma Medicaid plan.
15 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
16 Passed the House of Representatives the 4th day of March, 2026.

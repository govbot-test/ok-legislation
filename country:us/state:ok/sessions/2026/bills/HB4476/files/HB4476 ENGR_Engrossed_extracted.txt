19 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2236-1 of Title 74, unless there
21 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 The purpose of this act shall be to establish a comprehensive
23 program that supports both permanent music infrastructure and
24 community-based music festivals.

1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2236-2 of Title 74, unless there
3 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 The Legislature finds:
5 1. Music venues, rehearsal facilities, cultural districts, and
6 event spaces are essential components of Oklahoma's economy and
7 support the development of small businesses and skilled technical
8 workers;
9 2. Locally produced music festivals activate communities,
10 stimulate tourism, enhance economic vitality, and promote Oklahoma
11 as a destination for music;
12 3. Many Oklahoma communities lack year-round music
13 infrastructure necessary to support sustainable programming; and
14 4. Strengthening music infrastructure and festival capacity
15 will improve workforce readiness in sound engineering, lighting,
16 stage production, safety, hospitality, and event management.
17 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
18 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2236-3 of Title 74, unless there
19 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
20 As used in this act:
21 1. "Community music infrastructure" means permanent or semi-
22 permanent facilities, structures, equipment, or improvements that
23 support music performance, rehearsal, or community music activity;
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4476 Page 2

1 2. "Eligible applicant" includes municipalities, counties,
2 tribal nations, nonprofit organizations, Main Street programs, arts
3 councils, or privately operated venues or festival organizations
4 partnered with a nonprofit or public entity;
5 3. "Festival" or "music festival" means a community-organized
6 musical event presented over one (1) or more consecutive days that
7 includes live music components and is open to the public;
8 4. "Main Street community" means any community participating in
9 the Oklahoma Main Street Program administered by Oklahoma Main
10 Street within the Department of Commerce;
11 5. "Music venue" means an independently operated facility that
12 hosts live music performances on a regular basis;
13 6. "Office" means the Oklahoma Film + Music Office (OF+MO)
14 within the Department of Commerce;
15 7. "Rehearsal space" means any facility that may be
16 acoustically treated, used for music creation, practice, or
17 education available to the public; and
18 8. "Rural community" means a city or town having a population
19 not in excess of twenty-five thousand (25,000) persons or county not
20 in excess of two hundred fifty thousand (250,000) persons.
21 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2236-4 of Title 74, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:

1 A. The Community Music Infrastructure and Events Development
2 Program is hereby established within the Oklahoma Film + Music
3 Office.
4 B. The purpose of the program is to:
5 1. Develop, expand, and preserve year-round community music
6 infrastructure;
7 2. Support community-produced music festivals;
8 3. Improve workforce development opportunities through
9 partnerships with educational institutions and Oklahoma CareerTech;
10 4. Activate public spaces, Main Street districts, and tourism
11 destinations through music; and
12 5. Expand performance opportunities for Oklahoma musicians.
13 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2236-5 of Title 74, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 A. The Community Music Infrastructure Program shall consist of
17 two tracks as follows:
18 1. Track A: Community Music Infrastructure Awards
19 For Track A, the Office shall ensure projects are capital-
20 worthy, not cosmetic and shall exclude from consideration projects
21 that should be handled privately.
22 For Track A, the Office shall also confirm construction or
23 renovation costs, permanent equipment, (sound, lighting, staging),
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4476 Page 4

1 Amer icans with Disabilities Act compliance and safety upgrades and
2 the use of professional labor and materials.
3 2. Track B: Community Music Festivals
4 For Track B, the Office shall confirm that the event has market
5 viability to prevent funding of small, informal gatherings, and to
6 ensure that the minimum festival budget is in alignment with the
7 concept of a twenty-percent award.
8 The Office shall verify artist fees, production and staging
9 costs, security costs, logistics and permitting, market and
10 promotion, workforce or Higher Education/CareerTech engagement.
11 B. Awards pursuant to the provisions of this section may be
12 used for, but not limited to:
13 1. Capital improvements to music venues or community
14 facilities;
15 2. Electrical, plumbing, HVAC, or structural upgrades;
16 3. ADA accessibility improvements;
17 4. Safety and compliance measures, including fire suppression
18 and crowd-management systems;
19 5. Soundproofing and acoustic treatment;
20 6. Permanent staging, lighting, or audiovisual systems;
21 7. Conversion of public or underutilized buildings into music-
22 use facilities;
23 8. Rehearsal space creation or enhancement;
24

5 SECTION 6. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
6 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 2236-6 of Title 74, unless there
7 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
8 Track B — Community Music Festivals Awards
9 Awards pursuant to the provisions of this section may be used
10 for, but not limited to:
11 1. Development or expansion of community music festivals;
12 2. Temporary festival infrastructure that complements permanent
13 assets;
14 3. Workforce training in partnership with CareerTech or Higher
15 Education (audio, lighting, staging, event management);
16 4. Festival safety planning, ADA accommodations, and
17 accessibility improvements;
18 5. Event equipment rentals or site preparation; and
19 6. Artists, directors, managers, crew, and staff.

8 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
9 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 602 of Title 3, unless there is
10 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
11 A. The Oklahoma Department of Aerospace and Aeronautics shall
12 designate not less than five sites as Advanced Air Mobility (AAM)
13 pilot sites within the state. Such sites shall be eligible for AAM-
14 specific support and regulatory assistance; provided, the site meets
15 program guidance as issued by the Department. Of the designated
16 sites, not less than two sites shall be designated as supporting
17 airports and demonstrate a commitment to publicly operated AAM
18 infrastructure, the public benefit of such technology, and a pathway
19 to financial stability.
20 B. The Department may approve and provide funding opportunities
21 at approved sites for infrastructure, equipment, software, or other
22 needs the site may have to work with AAM companies, operators, and
23 supportive industries. The Department shall not approve funding for
24 research and development opportunities nor the purchase of

1 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 603 of Title 3, unless there is
3 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving fund
5 for the Oklahoma Department of Aerospace and Aeronautics to be
6 designated the "Sustainable Emerging Aviation Services Investment
7 Program (SEA SIP) Revolving Fund". The fund shall be a continuing
8 fund, not subject to fiscal year limitations, and shall consist of
9 all monies received by the Department as directed by law, from
10 federal and state grants or appropriations, from private donations,
11 and from revenues or fees recouped from assets in which the
12 Department invests. All monies accruing to the credit of said fund
13 are hereby appropriated and may be utilized in accordance with the
14 provisions of the Sustainable Emerging Aviation Services Investment
15 Program. Expenditures from said fund shall be made upon warrants
16 issued by the State Treasurer against claims filed as prescribed by
17 law with the Director of the Office of Management and Enterprise
18 Services for approval and payment.
19 SECTION 5. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

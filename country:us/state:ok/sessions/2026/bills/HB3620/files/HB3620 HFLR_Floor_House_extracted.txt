10 An Act relating to the 2030 census; creating the 2030
Census Complete Count Committee; providing the
11 membership of the Committee; directing the Committee
to select a chair and vice-chair; clarifying that a
12 majority of the members shall constitute a quorum;
providing process for members to be reimbursed for
13 travel expenses; stipulating that members shall serve
at the pleasure of their respective appointing
14 authority; directing for staff to be provided by the
State Data Center; directing the Committee to develop
15 and implement a census outreach, promotion,
recruiting, and enumeration assistance plan;
16 permitting the creation of subcommittees; providing
additional duties and responsibilities of the
17 Committee; requiring the first meeting to be called
by certain date; requiring appointments by certain
18 date; directing the Committee to submit a final
report; providing for codification; and providing an
19 effective date.

1 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 146 of Title 14, unless there is
3 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. There is hereby created the "2030 Census Complete Count
5 Committee" hereinafter referred to as the Committee.
6 B. The Committee shall consist of sixteen (16) members as
7 follows:
8 1. One legislative member from the Oklahoma State Senate
9 appointed by the President Pro Tempore of the Senate;
10 2. One legislative member from the Oklahoma House of
11 Representatives appointed by the Speaker of the House of
12 Representatives;
13 3. One legislative member from the Oklahoma State Senate
14 appointed by the Minority Leader of the Senate;
15 4. One legislative member from the Oklahoma House of
16 Representatives appointed by the Minority Leader of the House of
17 Representatives;
18 5. Six members representing state and local governments
19 reflecting the geographic diversity of the state. Two members will
20 be appointed by the Governor, two members will be appointed by the
21 Speaker of the House of Representatives and two members will be
22 appointed by the President Pro Tempore of the Senate;
23 6. Three members representing nonprofit and private
24 organizations, one each appointed by the Governor, the Speaker of

6 C. The Committee shall select a chair and vice-chair and such
7 other officers as the members of the Committee deem necessary. A
8 majority of the members of the Committee shall constitute a quorum
9 to transact business, but no vacancy shall impair the right of the
10 remaining members to perform the duties and responsibilities of the
11 Committee.
12 D. All nonlegislative members shall be reimbursed for travel
13 expenses incurred in carrying out their duties as members of the
14 Committee, by the appointing authority, pursuant to the provisions
15 of the State Travel Reimbursement Act. Legislative members shall be
16 reimbursed by the body from which they serve.
17 E. The members shall serve at the pleasure of their respective
18 appointing authority.
19 F. The staff for the Committee shall be provided by the State
20 Data Center of the Legislative Services Bureau.
21 G. The Committee is charged with developing and implementing a
22 census outreach, promotion, recruiting, and enumeration assistance
23 plan of action designed to assure a full count of the population of
24 Oklahoma for the 2030 Decennial Census. The census outreach

1 stra tegy shall include, but not be limited to, state agency
2 initiatives to encourage participation in the 2030 Census, the

10 H. Additional duties and responsibilities of the Committee
11 shall be to:
12 1. Establish a set of goals and the steps to be taken each year
13 so that Oklahoma will achieve a full count for the 2030 Census,
14 including implementing strategies to reach hard-to-count populations
15 and hard-to-enumerate areas;
16 2. Set a framework for state participation in the programs of
17 the U.S. Census Bureau for improving both the state's housing unit
18 database and counting methodology;
19 3. Explain the importance of a full count to the people of
20 Oklahoma through public announcements and publications;
21 4. Increase the self-response rate for households responding
22 online, by phone, or mailing back their questionnaire through a
23 focused, structured, neighbor-to-neighbor program;

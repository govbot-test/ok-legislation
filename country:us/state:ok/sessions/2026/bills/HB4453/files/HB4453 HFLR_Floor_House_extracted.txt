11 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
12 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 8000 of Title 36, unless there
13 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
14 A. There is hereby created within the Oklahoma Insurance
15 Department, the Oklahoma Health Care Cost Transparency Board,
16 hereinafter referred to as the Board.
17 B. The purpose of the Board is to:
18 1. Measure statewide health care cost growth and primary care
19 investment trends across commercial insurance, Medicaid, and
20 Medicare;
21 2. Ensure consistent statewide evaluation of total health
22 expenditures and primary care spending; and
23 3. Foster transparency and accountability in Oklahoma's health
24 care system.

6 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
7 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 8002 of Title 36, unless there
8 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
9 A. The State Coordinator for the Health Information Exchange,
10 created in Section 1-132.1 of Title 63 of the Oklahoma Statutes,
11 shall establish and maintain an All Payer Claims Database (APCD) to
12 collect and analyze health care costs, utilization, and spending
13 data from all payer types operating within this state.
14 B. The APCD shall, to the extent permitted by law, include data
15 from:
16 1. Commercial health insurers and third-party administrators;
17 2. The Oklahoma Health Care Authority, covering all Medicaid
18 programs; and
19 3. The Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services, providing
20 Medicare data under applicable data use agreements.
21 C. Data collected shall be used to:
22 1. Measure statewide health care cost trends and cost growth;
23 2. Determine rates of investment in primary care across all
24 payer segments; and

2 D. The Department shall adopt rules setting standards for data
3 submission, validation, and confidentiality consistent with federal
4 and state privacy laws.
5 E. The Department may publish de-identified, aggregated data
6 through regular public reports and dashboards.
7 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
8 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 8003 of Title 36, unless there
9 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
10 A. The Oklahoma Health Care Authority (OHCA) shall maintain and
11 periodically update the state primary care spending methodology and
12 definitions originally established for Medicaid, which shall serve
13 as the uniform statewide standard for measuring primary care
14 expenditures under this act.
15 B. All commercial health insurers regulated under Title 36 of
16 the Oklahoma Statutes shall:
17 1. Measure their annual primary care spending using the OHCA-
18 approved methodology; and
19 2. Submit annual reports of primary care and total medical
20 expenditures to the Insurance Department.
21 C. The Oklahoma Health Care Cost Transparency Board, in
22 consultation with OHCA, shall establish interim benchmarks for the
23 years 2027 through 2029 and monitor progress toward the 2030 goal.

4 SECTION 5. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
5 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 8004 of Title 36, unless there
6 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
7 A. The Insurance Commissioner shall have exclusive authority to
8 determine and implement enforcement mechanisms and incentive
9 programs under this act.
10 B. The Commissioner may:
11 1. Require commercial insurers not meeting progress benchmarks
12 to submit corrective action plans;
13 2. Impose administrative penalties not to exceed Five Thousand
14 Dollars ($5,000.00) per day for willful noncompliance with data
15 reporting or benchmark requirements;
16 3. Develop incentive programs or recognition designations for
17 insurers demonstrating sustained investment in primary care,
18 achievement of benchmarks, or innovations in value-based care; and
19 4. Consider primary care investment performance when conducting
20 rate, form, or network adequacy reviews.
21 C. The Board may make advisory recommendations to the
22 Commissioner, but all enforcement and incentive actions shall be
23 determined and administered solely by the Insurance Commissioner's
24 Office.

7 An Act relating to children; authorizing childcare
providers to charge differential; prohibiting the
8 Department of Human Services from penalizing or
taking adverse action against providers; requiring
9 providers to apply differential uniformly; directing
the Department to promulgate rules; providing for
10 codification; and declaring an emergency.
11
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 431 of Title 10, unless there is
16 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. Upon approval by the Administration for Children and
18 Families, a licensed or permitted childcare provider serving a child
19 eligible for the Department of Human Services' Child Care Subsidy
20 Program may charge the child's parent or legal guardian the
21 difference between:
22 1. The Department's subsidy reimbursement rate applicable to
23 the child; and
24
ENGR. H. B. NO. 3552 Page 1
1 2. The provider's standard private tuition rate for the same
2 age group and schedule.
3 B. The Department shall not:
4 1. Prohibit, restrict, or penalize a provider for charging a
5 differential authorized pursuant to this act; or
6 2. Take adverse action against a provider, including, but not
7 limited to, disqualification, enhanced monitoring, subsidy
8 enrollment restrictions, or corrective action, solely due to the
9 provider charging a differential pursuant to this act.
10 C. Providers who charge a differential shall apply the
11 differential uniformly to all similarly situated families.
12 D. The Department shall promulgate rules, update subsidy
13 agreements, and provide guidance documents to comply with the
14 provisions of this act.
15 SECTION 2. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
16 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
17 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
18 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

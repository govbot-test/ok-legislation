10 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
11 An Act relating to children; authorizing childcare
providers to charge differential; prohibiting the
12 Department of Human Services from penalizing or
taking adverse action against providers; requiring
13 providers to apply differential uniformly; directing
the Department to promulgate rules; providing for
14 codification; and providing an effective date.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 431 of Title 10, unless there is
20 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. Upon approval by the Administration for Children and
22 Families, a licensed or permitted childcare provider serving a child
23 eligible for the Department of Human Services' Child Care Subsidy
24
HB3552 HFLR Page 1
BOLD FACE denotes Committee Amendments.
1 Prog ram may charge the child's parent or legal guardian the
2 difference between:
3 1. The Department's subsidy reimbursement rate applicable to
4 the child; and
5 2. The provider's standard private tuition rate for the same
6 age group and schedule.
7 B. The Department shall not:
8 1. Prohibit, restrict, or penalize a provider for charging a
9 differential authorized pursuant to this act; or
10 2. Take adverse action against a provider, including, but not
11 limited to, disqualification, enhanced monitoring, subsidy
12 enrollment restrictions, or corrective action, solely due to the
13 provider charging a differential pursuant to this act.
14 C. Providers who charge a differential shall apply the
15 differential uniformly to all similarly situated families.
16 D. The Department shall promulgate rules, update subsidy
17 agreements, and provide guidance documents to comply with the
18 provisions of this act.
19 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
20

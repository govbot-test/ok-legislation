Section 1:
1 STATE OF OKLAHOMA
2 2nd Session of the 60th Legislature (2026)
4
5
6 AS INTRODUCED
7 An Act relating to children; authorizing childcare
providers to charge differential; prohibiting the
8 Department of Human Services from penalizing or
taking adverse action against providers; requiring
9 providers to apply differential uniformly; directing
the Department to promulgate rules; providing for
10 codification; and providing an effective date.
11
12
13 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
14 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
15 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 431 of Title 10, unless there is
16 created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
17 A. A licensed or permitted childcare provider serving a child
18 eligible for the Department of Human Services' Child Care Subsidy
19 Program may charge the child's parent or legal guardian the
20 difference between:
21 1. The Department's subsidy reimbursement rate applicable to
22 the child; and
23 2. The provider's standard private tuition rate for the same
24 age group and schedule.
Req. No. 15167 Page 1
1 B. The Department shall not:
2 1. Prohibit, restrict, or penalize a provider for charging a
3 differential authorized pursuant to this act; or
4 2. Take adverse action against a provider, including, but not
5 limited to, disqualification, enhanced monitoring, subsidy
6 enrollment restrictions, or corrective action, solely due to the
7 provider charging a differential pursuant to this act.
8 C. Providers who charge a differential shall apply the
9 differential uniformly to all similarly situated families.
10 D. The Department shall promulgate rules, update subsidy
11 agreements, and provide guidance documents to comply with the
12 provisions of this act.
13 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

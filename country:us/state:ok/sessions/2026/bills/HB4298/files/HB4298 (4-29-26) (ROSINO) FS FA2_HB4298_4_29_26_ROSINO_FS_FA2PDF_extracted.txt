11 An Act relating to child care; amending 10 O.S. 2021,
Section 405.4, which relates to master teachers;
12 directing establishment of certain master teacher
ratios; authorizing child care facilities to charge
13 certain fees subject to specified conditions;
requiring the Department of Human Services to seek
14 certain federal approval; providing for codification;
providing an effective date; and declaring an
15 emergency.

18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 10 O.S. 2021, Section 405.4, is
20 amended to read as follows:
21 Section 405.4. A. Any person with a bachelor’s degree or
22 postgraduate degree shall be considered as having met the
23 educational requirements for a director of a full-time child care
24 center required by the Department of Human Services. Nothing in
Req. No. 4008 Page 1

1 this section shall be construed as to affect annual continuing
2 education requirements. The director of a child care center may
3 also qualify as a master teacher for children of all ages.
4 B. Any person replacing a master teacher at a child care center
5 shall be granted a one-year probationary period to fulfill the
6 educational qualifications required by the Department for a master
7 teacher. The Department may extend the probationary period an
8 additional year as long as the person is actively pursuing that
9 goal.
10 C. Not later than the effective date of this act, the Director
11 of Human Services shall establish master teacher ratios for two-,
12 three-, four-, and five-star child care facilities that are the most
13 permissive ratios possible without harming the safety of children or
14 the quality of care provided, as determined by the Director.
15 D. Licensed child care facilities opened or expanded before
16 November 1, 2016, shall have thirty-five (35) square feet of floor
17 area per infant in rooms occupied only by infants. New construction
18 and existing space not previously licensed for child care after
19 November 1, 2016, shall have forty (40) square feet of floor area
20 per infant in rooms occupied only by infants.
21 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
22 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 410.2 of Title 10, unless there
23 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
24
Req. No. 4008 Page 2

1 A. Subject to federal approval as described in subsection B of
2 this section, a licensed child care facility that participates in
3 the child care subsidy program may charge a family receiving subsidy
4 assistance an additional differential fee above the subsidy
5 reimbursement rate established by the Department of Human Services,
6 provided the total amount paid by the family for the required
7 copayment and the additional differential fee does not exceed ten
8 percent (10%) of household income. The Department shall not be
9 responsible for payment of any amount charged above the applicable
10 subsidy reimbursement rate.
11 B. The Department shall seek any approval necessary from the
12 federal Administration for Children and Families to implement the
13 provisions of this section.
14 SECTION 3. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
15 SECTION 4. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
16 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
17 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
18 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

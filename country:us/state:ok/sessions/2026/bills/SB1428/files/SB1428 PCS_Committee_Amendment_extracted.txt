9 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
10 An Act relating to Alzheimer’s disease and related
dementias; creating the Office of Alzheimer’s Disease
11 and Related Dementia Service Coordination within the
State Department of Health; providing powers and
12 duties; establishing dementia services coordinator
position; creating the Alzheimer’s Disease and
13 Related Dementia Service Coordination Revolving Fund;
specifying type of fund and source of monies; stating
14 purpose of fund; providing for expenditure of monies;
repealing 63 O.S. 2021, Sections 1-879.2 and 1-879.3,
15 which relate to Alzheimer’s disease and related
dementias; providing for codification; and providing
16 an effective date.
17
18
19 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
20 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
21 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-879.5 of Title 63, unless
22 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
23 A. Subject to availability of funding, there is hereby created
24 within the State Department of Health the Office of Alzheimer’s
Req. No. 3695 Page 1
1 Dise ase and Related Dementia Service Coordination for the purpose of
2 providing statewide coordination, service system development, and
3 information on education, support, and other available services for
4 individuals with Alzheimer’s disease or related dementias and their
5 caregivers.
6 B. The Office of Alzheimer’s Disease and Related Dementia
7 Service Coordination shall have the power and duty to:
8 1. Evaluate the coordination of dementia services within this
9 state and identify any duplicated services;
10 2. Coordinate informational resources affecting individuals
11 with Alzheimer’s disease or related dementias and their caregivers.
12 Such coordination shall include, but not be limited to:
13 a. using dementia-related data to coordinate strategies
14 designed to improve public health outcomes and service
15 delivery for individuals with Alzheimer’s disease or
16 related dementias and their caregivers,
17 b. establishing and maintaining relationships with other
18 state agencies, providers, organizations, and
19 community stakeholders to meet the needs of affected
20 populations and prevent the duplication of services,
21 c. supporting the provision of dementia-specific staff
22 training across all relevant state agencies and
23 organizations, and
24
Req. No. 3695 Page 2
1 d. recommending strategies to improve coordination of
2 dementia-related services and resources provided by
3 public and private entities;
4 3. Streamline all applicable state government services to
5 increase efficiency and improve the quality of dementia-related care
6 in residential, home-based, and community-based settings;
7 4. Identify grant opportunities to expand the scope of services
8 for individuals with Alzheimer’s disease or related dementias and
9 their caregivers and apply for the grant opportunities;
10 5. Perform other duties relevant to supporting policy
11 development and implementation to enhance the quality of life for
12 individuals with Alzheimer’s disease or related dementias and their
13 caregivers;
14 6. Promote public and professional awareness and education
15 related to Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias and access to
16 needed services and programs; and
17 7. Collect and monitor data concerning the impact of
18 Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias in this state.
19 C. Subject to availability of funding, the Office of
20 Alzheimer’s Disease and Related Dementia Service Coordination shall
21 maintain on staff at least one dementia services coordinator. The
22 dementia services coordinator shall be a full-time, permanent
23 employee and shall be responsible for the staffing and operational
24 details of the Office.
Req. No. 3695 Page 3
1 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-879.6 of Title 63, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving fund
5 for the State Department of Health to be designated the “Alzheimer’s
6 Disease and Related Dementia Service Coordination Revolving Fund”.
7 The fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year
8 limitations, and shall consist of all monies received by the
9 Department from grants, gifts, and donations provided for the
10 purpose of funding the Office of Alzheimer’s Disease and Related
11 Dementia Service Coordination created under Section 1 of this act.
12 All monies accruing to the credit of the fund are hereby
13 appropriated and may be budgeted and expended by the Department for
14 the purpose provided for in this section. Expenditures from the
15 fund shall be made upon warrants issued by the State Treasurer
16 against claims filed as prescribed by law with the Director of the
17 Office of Management and Enterprise Services for approval and
18 payment.
19 SECTION 3. REPEALER 63 O.S. 2021, Sections 1-879.2 and
20 1-879.3, are hereby repealed.
21 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

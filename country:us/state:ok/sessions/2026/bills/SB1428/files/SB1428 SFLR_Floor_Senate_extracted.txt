14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-879.5 of Title 63, unless
17 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. Subject to availability of funding, there is hereby created
19 within the State Department of Health the Office of Alzheimer’s
20 Disease and Related Dementia Service Coordination for the purpose of
21 providing statewide coordination, service system development, and
22 information on education, support, and other available services for
23 individuals with Alzheimer’s disease or related dementias and their
24 caregivers.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1428 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)

1 B. The Office of Alzheimer’s Disease and Related Dementia
2 Service Coordination shall have the power and duty to:
3 1. Evaluate the coordination of dementia services within this
4 state and identify any duplicated services;
5 2. Coordinate informational resources affecting individuals
6 with Alzheimer’s disease or related dementias and their caregivers.
7 Such coordination shall include, but not be limited to:
8 a. using dementia-related data to coordinate strategies
9 designed to improve public health outcomes and service
10 delivery for individuals with Alzheimer’s disease or
11 related dementias and their caregivers,
12 b. establishing and maintaining relationships with other
13 state agencies, providers, organizations, and
14 community stakeholders to meet the needs of affected
15 populations and prevent the duplication of services,
16 c. supporting the provision of dementia-specific staff
17 training across all relevant state agencies and
18 organizations, and
19 d. recommending strategies to improve coordination of
20 dementia-related services and resources provided by
21 public and private entities;
22 3. Streamline all applicable state government services to
23 increase efficiency and improve the quality of dementia-related care
24 in residential, home-based, and community-based settings;
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1428 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)

1 4. Identify grant opportunities to expand the scope of services
2 for individuals with Alzheimer’s disease or related dementias and
3 their caregivers and apply for the grant opportunities;
4 5. Perform other duties relevant to supporting policy
5 development and implementation to enhance the quality of life for
6 individuals with Alzheimer’s disease or related dementias and their
7 caregivers;
8 6. Promote public and professional awareness and education
9 related to Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias and access to
10 needed services and programs; and
11 7. Collect and monitor data concerning the impact of
12 Alzheimer’s disease and related dementias in this state.
13 C. Subject to availability of funding, the Office of
14 Alzheimer’s Disease and Related Dementia Service Coordination shall
15 maintain on staff at least one dementia services coordinator. The
16 dementia services coordinator shall be a full-time, permanent
17 employee and shall be responsible for the staffing and operational
18 details of the Office.
19 SECTION 2. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
20 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 1-879.6 of Title 63, unless
21 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
22 There is hereby created in the State Treasury a revolving fund
23 for the State Department of Health to be designated the “Alzheimer’s
24 Disease and Related Dementia Service Coordination Revolving Fund”.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB1428 SFLR Page 3
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)

1 The fund shall be a continuing fund, not subject to fiscal year
2 limitations, and shall consist of all monies received by the
3 Department from grants, gifts, and donations provided for the
4 purpose of funding the Office of Alzheimer’s Disease and Related
5 Dementia Service Coordination created under Section 1 of this act.
6 All monies accruing to the credit of the fund are hereby
7 appropriated and may be budgeted and expended by the Department for
8 the purpose provided for in this section. Expenditures from the
9 fund shall be made upon warrants issued by the State Treasurer
10 against claims filed as prescribed by law with the Director of the
11 Office of Management and Enterprise Services for approval and
12 payment.
13 SECTION 3. REPEALER 63 O.S. 2021, Sections 1-879.2 and
14 1-879.3, are hereby repealed.
15 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

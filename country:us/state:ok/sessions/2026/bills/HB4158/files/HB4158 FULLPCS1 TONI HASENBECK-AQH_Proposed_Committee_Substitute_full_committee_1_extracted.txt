8 PROPOSED COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 An Act relating to higher education; amending 70 O.S.
2021, Section 2632, as amended by Section 1, Chapter
10 129, O.S.L. 2025 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025, Section 2632),
which relates to eligibility requirements for the
11 Oklahoma Tuition Equalization Grant; increasing
income cap from Fifty Thousand Dollars to Eighty
12 Thousand Dollars; clarifying permissible use of grant
to be only for courses or programs for completion of
13 a baccalaureate degree; providing an effective date;
and declaring an emergency.
14
15
16 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
17 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 2632, as
18 amended by Section 1, Chapter 129, O.S.L. 2025 (70 O.S. Supp. 2025,
19 Section 2632), is amended to read as follows:
20 Section 2632. A. To be eligible for an Oklahoma Tuition
21 Equalization Grant, a student shall:
22 1. Be an Oklahoma resident as defined by the Oklahoma State
23 Regents for Higher Education;
24
Req. No. 16840 Page 1
1 2. Have enrolled as a full-time undergraduate student in an
2 eligible institution of higher education as prescribed in subsection
3 D of this section;
4 3. Meet the income eligibility level, which is the student's
5 parents' income from taxable sources which shall not be more than
6 Fifty Eighty Thousand Dollars ($50,000.00) ($80,000.00) per year or
7 the student's income if the student is independent and self-
8 supporting as determined by the State Regents consistent with
9 federal financial aid regulations;
10 4. Pay more tuition than is required at a comparable public
11 institution of higher education and pay no less than the regular
12 tuition required of all students enrolled at the institution where
13 the student is enrolled; and
14 5. Maintain at least minimum standards of academic performance
15 as required by the enrolling institution.
16 B. The enrolling institution shall forward a completed student
17 application, documentation of full-time enrollment status, and
18 certification of resident status to the State Regents no later than
19 October 15 for the fall semester and March 15 for the spring
20 semester of each academic year.
21 C. Subject to the availability of funds in the Oklahoma Tuition
22 Equalization Grant Trust Fund, an Oklahoma Tuition Equalization
23 Grant in the amount of Two Thousand Dollars ($2,000.00) per academic
24 year, or One Thousand Dollars ($1,000.00) per academic semester
Req. No. 16840 Page 2
1 shal l be awarded by the State Regents by allocation from the
2 Oklahoma Tuition Equalization Grant Trust Fund, beginning with
3 eligible students enrolled in the 2003-2004 school year. No grants
4 pursuant to this act shall be provided to students for attending
5 summer terms or intersessions.
6 D. The State Regents shall implement policies and procedures
7 for the administration of this act. The State Regents shall approve
8 as eligible institutions of higher education only those private or
9 independent, not-for-profit colleges or universities that are
10 domiciled within this state, meet approved program and accreditation
11 standards as determined by the State Regents, and are fully
12 accredited by the Higher Learning Commission of the North Central
13 Association on Colleges and Schools or a national accrediting body
14 recognized by the United States Department of Education. To be
15 eligible, institutions must have been granted not-for-profit status
16 by the Internal Revenue Service and the U. S. U.S. Department of
17 Education prior to July 1, 2012. Additionally, eligible
18 institutions shall adhere to the complaint process policies and
19 procedures administered by the State Regents. The State Regents
20 shall review and take action, as authorized, on complaints
21 concerning eligible institutions. The eligible institutions shall
22 include Family of Faith College, Mid-America Christian University,
23 Oklahoma Baptist University, Oklahoma Christian University, Oklahoma
24 City University, Oklahoma Wesleyan University, Oral Roberts
Req. No. 16840 Page 3
1 Univ ersity, Southern Nazarene University, Southwestern Christian
2 University, the University of Tulsa and Randall University.
3 E. If funds are not sufficient in the Oklahoma Tuition
4 Equalization Grant Trust Fund to provide grants for all eligible
5 applicants, the State Regents shall award grants on the basis of
6 need and take into consideration other grants and scholarships
7 received by an eligible applicant when awarding grants. Students
8 who have previously received a tuition equalization grant and who
9 continue to meet the requirements for eligibility shall have
10 absolute priority for continued financial support superior to any
11 student who is applying for a grant for the first time.
12 F. Students enrolling as first-time freshmen for the 2003-2004
13 school year shall be the first class of students eligible to apply
14 for Oklahoma Tuition Equalization Grants. In subsequent years, all
15 previously eligible students and the first-time enrolling freshmen
16 students shall be entitled to apply for a grant pursuant to this
17 section. Beginning in the 2007-2008 school year, all eligible
18 students shall be entitled to apply for a grant pursuant to this
19 section.
20 G. A grant provided by this section shall not only be allowed
21 for courses or other postsecondary units taken in excess of the
22 requirements programs offered at eligible institutions of higher
23 education as prescribed in subsection D of the section for
24 completion of a baccalaureate program or taken more than five (5)
Req. No. 16840 Page 4
1 year s after the student's first grant is received. The State
2 Regents may award an Oklahoma Tuition Equalization Grant for courses
3 of postsecondary units taken more than five (5) years after the
4 student's first grant is received only in hardship circumstances.
5 No Oklahoma Tuition Equalization Grant recipient may receive
6 benefits beyond a cumulative time period of five (5) years.
7 H. On or before February 1 after the first semester in which
8 grants are awarded pursuant to this act and every year thereafter,
9 the State Regents shall provide a report to the Governor, President
10 Pro Tempore of the Senate, and the Speaker of the House of
11 Representatives to include, but not be limited to the number of
12 students awarded grants, grade point average, persistence rates,
13 degree completion rates, demographic data, and any other indicators
14 the State Regents deem appropriate.
15 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
16 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
17 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
18 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
19 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

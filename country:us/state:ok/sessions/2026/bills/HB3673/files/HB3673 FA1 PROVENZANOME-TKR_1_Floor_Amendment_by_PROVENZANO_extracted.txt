10 An Act relating to professions and occupations;
amending 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1688, as amended by
11 Section 3, Chapter 355, O.S.L. 2024 (59 O.S. Supp.
2025, Section 1688), which relates to electrical
12 licenses; providing renewal and reinstatement
procedures for expired unlimited journeyman
13 electrician and residential journeyman licenses;
authorizing renewal without reexamination after more
14 than one year of expiration under certain conditions;
directing the Construction Industries Board to
15 establish fees and implement rules; and providing an
effective date.
16
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1688, as
20 amended by Section 3, Chapter 355, O.S.L. 2024 (59 O.S. Supp. 2025,
21 Section 1688), is amended to read as follows:
22 Section 1688. A. No license shall be issued for longer than
23 one (1) year, and all licenses shall expire on the last day in the
24 birth month of the licensee. A license may be renewed upon
Req. No. 17138 Page 1
1 appl ication and payment of fees thirty (30) days preceding or
2 following the date the license is due. Licenses renewed more than
3 thirty (30) days following the date of expiration may be renewed
4 only upon application and payment of all required fees and payment
5 of any penalty for late renewal established by the Construction
6 Industries Board and upon compliance with any applicable continuing
7 education requirements established by the Board and the Electrical
8 License Act. No penalty for late renewal shall be charged to any
9 holder of a license which expires while the holder is in military
10 service, if an application for renewal is made within one (1) year
11 following the service discharge of the holder.
12 B. No journeyman or contractor license shall be renewed unless
13 the licensee has completed the required hours of continuing
14 education, pursuant to the Electrical License Act and as determined
15 and approved by the Committee of Electrical Examiners and approved
16 by the Construction Industries Board.
17 C. Any license which remains expired for longer than one (1)
18 year shall not be renewed but shall be reinstated pursuant to this
19 subsection when compliant with all license requirements. To obtain
20 the license formerly held, the former license holder shall be
21 required to submit a reinstatement application provided by the
22 Construction Industries Board, along with payment of the application
23 and license fees set at the same fee amounts as an initial
24
Req. No. 17138 Page 2
1 appl ication and license fee, and either retest or satisfy all of the
2 following:
3 1. Submit a complete reinstatement application to the
4 Construction Industries Board;
5 2. Pay the standard renewal fee for each year the license has
6 been expired, plus all accrued late fees, plus a reinstatement
7 penalty fee of Two Hundred Dollars ($200.00) per full year of
8 expiration;
9 3. Submit certification of completion within the preceding
10 twelve-month period of all continuing education approved by the
11 Committee of Electrical Examiners as follows:
12 a. if the applicant has satisfied the continuing
13 education requirements required for license renewal
14 within the preceding thirty-six (36) months, no
15 additional continuing education shall be required, or
16 b. if the license has been expired for more than thirty-
17 six (36) months, the applicant shall complete
18 continuing education as follows:
19 (1) after being expired for more than one (1) year,
20 the applicant shall take twelve (12) hours of
21 continuing education,
22 (2) after being expired for more than two (2)
23 consecutive years, the applicant shall take a
24
Req. No. 17138 Page 3
1 total of twenty-four (24) hours of continuing
2 education,
3 (3) after being expired for more than three (3)
4 consecutive years, the applicant shall take a
5 total of thirty-six (36) hours of continuing
6 education,
7 (4) after being expired for more than four (4)
8 consecutive years, the applicant shall take a
9 total of forty-eight (48) hours of continuing
10 education; and
11 4. Submits an original sworn affidavit or other verification
12 satisfactory to the Board that the applicant has not been subject to
13 final disciplinary action that would preclude licensure and has not
14 been convicted of a crime that has a direct bearing on the
15 applicant's fitness to practice as an electrician.
16 D. A licensee is exempt from the continuing education
17 requirements for license renewal for three (3) years from the date
18 he or she passed their current licensing exam.
19 E. A licensee reinstated through the option of completing the
20 required hours of continuing education in lieu of retesting after
21 being expired for more than one (1) year shall, for the first timely
22 submitted license renewal of the reinstated license, complete the
23 required continuing education pursuant to this act for renewing his
24 or her license within thirty-six (36) months beginning from the date
Req. No. 17138 Page 4
1 of t he last verified continuing education course completed for
2 reinstatement of an expired license.
3 C. F. An apprentice registration certificate shall be issued
4 for one (1) year, at which time the apprentice may reregister upon
5 meeting the requirements of the Construction Industries Board and
6 paying the renewal fee.
7 D. G. A student apprentice registration certificate shall be
8 issued for the registrant's school year. A student apprentice
9 registration expires on the last day of the registrant's school
10 year, at which time the apprentice may reregister upon meeting the
11 requirements of the Construction Industries Board and paying the
12 renewal fee.
13 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

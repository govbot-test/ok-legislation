9 COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
10 An Act relating to professions and occupations;
amending 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1688, as amended by
11 Section 3, Chapter 355, O.S.L. 2024 (59 O.S. Supp.
2025, Section 1688), which relates to electrical
12 licenses; providing renewal and reinstatement
procedures for expired unlimited journeyman
13 electrician and residential journeyman licenses;
authorizing renewal without reexamination after more
14 than one year of expiration under certain conditions;
directing the Construction Industries Board to
15 establish fees and implement rules; and providing an
effective date.
16
17
18 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
19 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1688, as
20 amended by Section 3, Chapter 355, O.S.L. 2024 (59 O.S. Supp. 2025,
21 Section 1688), is amended to read as follows:
22 Section 1688. A. No license shall be issued for longer than
23 one (1) year, and all licenses shall expire on the last day in the
24 birth month of the licensee. A license may be renewed upon
Req. No. 16832 Page 1
1 appl ication and payment of fees thirty (30) days preceding or
2 following the date the license is due. Licenses renewed more than
3 thirty (30) days following the date of expiration may be renewed
4 only upon application and payment of all required fees and payment
5 of any penalty for late renewal established by the Construction
6 Industries Board and upon compliance with any applicable continuing
7 education requirements established by the Board and the Electrical
8 License Act. No penalty for late renewal shall be charged to any
9 holder of a license which expires while the holder is in military
10 service, if an application for renewal is made within one (1) year
11 following the service discharge of the holder.
12 B. No journeyman or contractor license shall be renewed unless
13 the licensee has completed the required hours of continuing
14 education, as determined and approved by the Committee of Electrical
15 Examiners and approved by the Construction Industries Board.
16 C. Notwithstanding any other provision of law or rule, an
17 Unlimited Journeyman Electrician license, Residential Journeyman
18 Electrician license, Unlimited Electrical Contractor license,
19 Residential Electrical Contractor license, or Limited Electrical
20 Contractor license that has been expired for longer than one (1)
21 year may be renewed or reinstated without reexamination if the
22 applicant satisfies all of the following:
23 1. Submits a complete renewal or reinstatement application to
24 the Construction Industries Board;
Req. No. 16832 Page 2
1 2. Pays the standard renewal fee for each year the license has
2 been expired, plus all accrued late fees, plus a reinstatement
3 penalty fee established by the Board not to exceed Two Hundred
4 Dollars ($200.00) per full year of expiration;
5 3. Completes all continuing education hours that would have
6 been required had the license remained active during the period of
7 expiration, plus an additional six (6) hours of Board-approved
8 continuing education for each full year (or portion thereof) the
9 license has been expired; and
10 4. Provides verification satisfactory to the Board that the
11 applicant has not been subject to final disciplinary action that
12 would preclude licensure and has not been convicted of a crime that
13 has a direct bearing on the applicant's fitness to practice as an
14 electrician.
15 D. The provisions of subsection B of this section shall apply
16 exclusively to the Unlimited Journeyman Electrician licenses,
17 Residential Journeyman Electrician licenses, Unlimited Electrical
18 Contractor licenses, Residential Electrical Contractor licenses, and
19 Limited Electrical Contractor licenses issued pursuant to the
20 Electrical License Act. These provisions shall not apply to any
21 other license classification.
22 E. The Construction Industries Board, upon recommendation of
23 the Committee of Electrical Examiners, shall promulgate rules
24 necessary to implement subsection B of this section, including but
Req. No. 16832 Page 3
1 not limited to, procedures for documenting continuing education
2 completed during periods of expiration and the schedule of
3 reinstatement penalty fees. Such rules shall be promulgated no
4 later than one hundred eighty (180) days following the effective
5 date of this act.
6 C. F. An apprentice registration certificate shall be issued
7 for one (1) year, at which time the apprentice may reregister upon
8 meeting the requirements of the Construction Industries Board and
9 paying the renewal fee.
10 D. G. A student apprentice registration certificate shall be
11 issued for the registrant's school year. A student apprentice
12 registration expires on the last day of the registrant's school
13 year, at which time the apprentice may reregister upon meeting the
14 requirements of the Construction Industries Board and paying the
15 renewal fee.
16 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

7 An Act relating to professions and occupations;
amending 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1688, as amended by
8 Section 3, Chapter 355, O.S.L. 2024 (59 O.S. Supp.
2025, Section 1688), which relates to electrical
9 licenses; providing renewal and reinstatement
procedures for expired unlimited journeyman
10 electrician and residential journeyman licenses;
authorizing renewal without reexamination after more
11 than one year of expiration under certain conditions;
directing the Construction Industries Board to
12 establish fees and implement rules; and providing an
effective date.

20 “An Act relating to professions and occupations;
amending 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1688, as amended by
21 Section 3, Chapter 355, O.S.L. 2024 (59 O.S. Supp.
2025, Section 1688), which relates to term of license
22 and registration certificates; prohibiting certain
expired license from being renewed; allowing certain
23 expired license to be reinstated; requiring certain
former licensee to submit certain application;
24 requiring certain application to include certain
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 3673 Page 1
1 information; exempting certain licensee from certain
continuing education requirements for certain time
2 period; requiring certain licensee to complete
certain continuing education requirement within
3 certain time period; updating statutory language; and
providing an effective date.
4
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
6 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 59 O.S. 2021, Section 1688, as
7 amended by Section 3, Chapter 355, O.S.L. 2024 (59 O.S. Supp. 2025,
8 Section 1688), is amended to read as follows:
9 Section 1688. A. No license shall be issued for longer than
10 one (1) year, and all licenses shall expire on the last day in the
11 birth month of the licensee. A license may be renewed upon
12 application and payment of fees thirty (30) days preceding or
13 following the date the license is due. Licenses renewed more than
14 thirty (30) days following the date of expiration may be renewed
15 only upon application and payment of all required fees and payment
16 of any penalty for late renewal established by the Construction
17 Industries Board and upon compliance with any applicable continuing
18 education requirements established by the Board and the Electrical
19 License Act. No penalty for late renewal shall be charged to any
20 holder of a license which expires while the holder is in military
21 service, if an application for renewal is made within one (1) year
22 following the service discharge of the holder.
23 B. No journeyman or contractor license shall be renewed unless
24 the licensee has completed the required hours of continuing
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 3673 Page 2
1 educ ation, pursuant to the Electrical License Act and as determined
2 and approved by the Committee of Electrical Examiners and approved
3 by the Construction Industries Board.
4 C. Any license that remains expired for longer than one (1)
5 year shall not be renewed but shall be reinstated pursuant to this
6 subsection when compliant with all license requirements. To obtain
7 the license formerly held, the former license holder shall be
8 required to submit a reinstatement application provided by the Board
9 and either retest or satisfy all of the following:
10 1. Submit a complete reinstatement application to the Board;
11 2. Pay the standard license renewal fee and a reinstatement
12 penalty fee of Two Hundred Dollars ($200.00);
13 3. Submit certification of completion within the preceding
14 twelve-month period of all continuing education approved by the
15 Committee of Electrical Examiners as follows:
16 a. if the applicant has satisfied the continuing
17 education requirements required for license renewal
18 within the preceding thirty-six (36) months, no
19 additional continuing education shall be required, or
20 b. if the license has been expired for more than thirty-
21 six (36) months, the applicant shall complete twelve
22 (12) hours of continuing education for each full
23 thirty-six-month period the license has remained
24 expired, not to exceed twenty-four (24) hours; and
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 3673 Page 3
1 4. Submit an original sworn affidavit or other verification
2 satisfactory to the Board that the applicant has not been subject to
3 final disciplinary action that would preclude licensure and has not
4 been convicted of a crime that has a direct bearing on the
5 applicant’s fitness to practice as an electrician.
6 D. A licensee shall be exempt from the continuing education
7 requirements for license renewal for three (3) years from the date
8 the licensee passed his or her current licensing exam.
9 E. A licensee whose license was reinstated through the option
10 of completing the required hours of continuing education in lieu of
11 retesting after being expired for more than one (1) year shall, for
12 the first timely submitted license renewal of the reinstated
13 license, complete the continuing education required pursuant to this
14 section for renewing his or her license within thirty-six (36)
15 months beginning from the date of the last verified continuing
16 education course completed for reinstatement of an expired license.
17 F. An apprentice registration certificate shall be issued for
18 one (1) year, at which time the apprentice may reregister upon
19 meeting the requirements of the Construction Industries Board and
20 paying the renewal fee.
21 D. G. A student apprentice registration certificate shall be
22 issued for the registrant’s school year. A student apprentice
23 registration expires on the last day of the registrant’s school
24 year, at which time the apprentice may reregister upon meeting the
ENGR. S. A. TO ENGR. H. B. NO. 3673 Page 4
1 requ irements of the Construction Industries Board and paying the
2 renewal fee.
3 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.”
4 Passed the Senate the 4th day of May, 2026.

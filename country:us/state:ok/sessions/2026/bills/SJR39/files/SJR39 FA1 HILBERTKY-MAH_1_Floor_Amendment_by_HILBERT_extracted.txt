4 SECTION 2. The Ballot Title for the proposed Constitutional
5 amendment as set forth in SECTION 1 of this resolution shall be in
6 the following form:
7 BALLOT TITLE
8 Legislative Referendum No. ____ State Question No. ____
9 THE GIST OF THE PROPOSITION IS AS FOLLOWS:
10 This measure amends Section 8B and Section 8C of Article 10 of
11 the Oklahoma Constitution. Section 8B currently limits annual
12 growth in fair cash value of most types of real property to five
13 percent (5%) or to three percent (3%) for homesteads. For real
14 property for which a homestead exemption is granted and real
15 property classified as agricultural land, it would limit growth
16 to one and seventy-five-hundredths percent (1.75%) for 2027 and
17 subsequent years. For other types of real property, it would
18 limit growth in fair cash value to four percent (4%) annually.
19 The measure would change Section 8C in Article 10 that prohibits
20 any increase in fair cash value of homestead property for
21 persons age sixty-five (65) or older whose income does not
22 exceed certain limits. This provision is sometimes referred to
23 as the “senior freeze”. Instead of prohibiting any increase in
24 fair cash value for these homesteads, there would be an annual
Req. No. 17391 Page 7
1 limit of one and seventy-five-hundredths percent (1.75%). The
2 income test for this new structure would be different. The
3 annual limit could be reduced based on how much gross household
4 income the homestead owner had in the preceding year. Depending
5 on the amount of gross household income the property owner had
6 in the preceding year, if the income level was one hundred
7 percent (100%) or less of the income amount determined using an
8 income measurement from federal law, the fair cash value of the
9 homestead could not increase. If the income amount was in
10 excess of certain thresholds, the limit on increases in fair
11 cash value for homesteads owned by persons age sixty-five (65)
12 or older could be thirty-five-hundredths percent (0.35%), seven-
13 tenths percent (0.7%), one and five-hundredths percent (1.05%),
14 one and four-tenths percent (1.4%) or one and seventy-five-
15 hundredths percent (1.75%).

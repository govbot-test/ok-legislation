8 PROPOSED OVERSIGHT COMMITTEE SUBSTITUTE
9 An Act relating to public finance; requiring cabinet
secretary approval for salary increases in excess of
10 specified percentage; imposing limitation on maximum
salary increases; requiring agencies to adopt rules
11 related to job performance; excluding certain
licensed professional persons from certain
12 restrictions; providing for applicability to certain
employee category; providing for inapplicability to
13 non-appropriated agencies; providing for
inapplicability to certain employee categories;
14 providing for codification; providing an effective
date; and declaring an emergency.
15
16
17 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
18 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
19 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 280.1 of Title 62, unless there
20 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
21 A. No executive branch agency shall increase the salary of a
22 state employee by more than ten percent (10%) with respect to any
23 fiscal year unless the cabinet secretary with jurisdiction over the
24 agency formally approves such increase. The Office of Management
Req. No. 16275 Page 1
1 and Enterprise Services shall prescribe a form to be used by a
2 cabinet secretary and at least one copy of the approval shall be
3 retained by the agency providing the salary increase and one copy
4 shall be retained by the Office of Management and Enterprise
5 Services.
6 B. The maximum bonus amount that any state agency may provide
7 any state employee shall be ten percent (10%) of the base salary
8 amount in effect for the employee as of the original hire date for
9 the employee or as of the anniversary of such original hire date for
10 an employee with service in excess of one (1) fiscal year. The
11 Office of Management and Enterprise Services shall prescribe a form
12 to be used by executive branch agencies for the approval of a bonus
13 amount authorized by this subsection.
14 C. Each state agency shall establish, pursuant to permanent
15 administrative rules, a maximum authorized bonus amount for each
16 employee position within the agency and shall also establish metrics
17 or other performance measures with respect to each such employee
18 position or job category which shall either already be in effect as
19 of the date the bonus becomes effective or which shall take effect
20 not later than thirty (30) days after the employee is hired.
21 D. Salary adjustments for all employees performing services for
22 an executive branch agency in excess of ten percent (10%) may be
23 authorized as a result of performance reviews, a change in job
24 duties or titles or based on substantial modification of existing
Req. No. 16275 Page 2
1 duti es and responsibilities which shall be documented by the agency
2 using a form prescribed for such purpose by the Office of Management
3 and Enterprise Services.
4 E. The provisions of subsection A and subsection B of this
5 section shall not be applicable to the executive director or other
6 chief executive officers of an agency. Such positions shall be
7 subject to rules established by the agency on pay, bonuses and
8 raises pursuant to subsection C of this section.
9 F. The provisions of subsection A and subsection B of this
10 section shall not be applicable to job positions or categories
11 requiring advanced degrees or licensing or accreditation including,
12 but not limited to, medical doctors, certified public accountants,
13 and professional engineers. Such positions shall not be subject to
14 the provisions of subsection A or subsection B of this section.
15 Such positions shall be subject to rules established by their
16 employing agency on pay, bonus and raises pursuant to subsection C
17 of this section.
18 G. The provisions of subsection A and subsection B of this
19 section shall not be applicable to wholly non-appropriated state
20 agencies or their employees. Wholly non-appropriated state agencies
21 shall not be subject to the rulemaking provisions of this section.
22 Such agencies shall remain subject to the pay movement mechanisms
23 listed in subsection B of Section 840-2.17 of Title 74 of the
24 Oklahoma Statutes. Salary increases or bonuses exceeding ten
Req. No. 16275 Page 3
1 perc ent (10%) received by any employee of an agency governed by this
2 subsection shall be documented by the agency on a form prescribed
3 for such purpose by the Office of Management and Enterprise
4 Services. At least one copy shall be retained by the agency
5 providing the salary increase or bonus and one copy shall be
6 retained by the Office of Management and Enterprise Services.
7 H. The provisions of subsection A and subsection B of this
8 section shall not be applicable to the employees of institutions
9 within The Oklahoma State System of Higher Education, employees of
10 any career technology district or to employees of any common school
11 district. The provisions of subsection A and subsection B of this
12 section shall be applicable to employees of the Oklahoma State
13 Regents for Higher Education.
14 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
15 SECTION 3. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
16 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
17 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
18 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.

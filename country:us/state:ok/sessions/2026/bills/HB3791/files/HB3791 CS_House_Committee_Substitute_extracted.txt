8 An Act relating to health care; allowing health care
providers to prescribe alternative treatment;
9 prohibiting repercussions; allowing parents or legal
guardians to provide alternative treatments to their
10 child; prohibiting legal repercussions; providing for
codification; and providing an effective date.
11
12
13
14 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
15 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
16 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 7500 of Title 63, unless there
17 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
18 A. A physician, including allopathic and osteopathic, advanced
19 practice nurse practitioner, and physicians assistant shall have the
20 right to prescribe, recommend, or suggest holistic or natural
21 medicine or alternative treatment to patients under his or her care
22 if the health care provider believes the treatment will be effective
23 or beneficial to his or her patient to relieve pain or help cure the
24 patient's ailment.
Req. No. 17111 Page 1
1 B. A parent or legal guardian shall have the right to treat his
2 or her child with holistic or natural medicine or an alternative
3 treatment to what the health care provider suggests or recommends.
4 It shall be the parent or legal guardian's right to deviate from a
5 suggestion or recommendation from a health care provider if the
6 parent or legal guardian believes that is in the best interest of
7 the child.
8 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

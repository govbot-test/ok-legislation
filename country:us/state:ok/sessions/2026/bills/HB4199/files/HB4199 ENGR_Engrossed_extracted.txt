12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
14 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 28.4 of Title 56, unless there
15 is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
16 A. The Legislature finds and declares that a Tri-Share
17 Workforce Pilot Program which allows participating employers,
18 employees, and the state to share equal responsibility for child
19 care of dependent children from birth to eight (8) years of age by
20 the state matching the employer's contribution each up to one-third
21 (1/3) of the total cost with the parent paying the remaining cost,
22 to assist parents and caretakers to participate in the workforce.
23 B. The Oklahoma Department of Commerce shall develop and
24 implement a three-year Tri-Share Workforce Pilot Program during the
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4199 Page 1
1 fisc al year beginning July 1, 2026, testing the feasibility of
2 participating employers and the access to employees to high-quality,
3 affordable child care. The Tri-Share Workforce Pilot Program shall
4 consist of two urban and two rural counties. The Department shall
5 select counties based on interest in employer participation and key
6 industry workforce needs.
7 C. Eligibility for the Tri-Share Workforce Pilot Program shall
8 consist of employees of participating employers whose household
9 income is between one hundred fifty percent (150%) and two hundred
10 fifty percent (250%) of the federal poverty level and who are not
11 eligible for child care subsidy.
12 D. The Oklahoma Department of Commerce shall establish
13 guidelines to implement the Tri-Share Workforce Pilot Program, and
14 technical assistance shall be included in the Tri-Share Workforce
15 Pilot Program. The Oklahoma Department of Commerce may contract
16 with existing training programs or other qualified providers with
17 experience relevant to Tri-Share Workforce Pilot Program
18 participants for such technical assistance.
19 E. Upon completion of the Tri-Share Workforce Pilot Program, if
20 it is determined that the project was effective in achieving the
21 objective of assisting employees with improving the stability,
22 affordability, access, and quality of child care, the Oklahoma
23 Department of Commerce shall implement a similar statewide program
24 for participating employers and qualified employees.
ENGR. H. B. NO. 4199 Page 2
1 F. The Oklahoma Department of Commerce shall report to the
2 Legislature each year on the participation of employers and
3 employees, the availability of child care facilities, the costs of
4 child care facilities, and, to the extent practicable, the impact
5 the Tri-Share Workforce Pilot Program has had on the community in
6 assisting parents and caretakers to participate in the workforce or
7 access training that can advance their degrees. Following
8 successful completion of the Tri-Share Workforce Pilot Program,
9 effectiveness of the Tri-Share Workforce Pilot Program shall be
10 evaluated by the Oklahoma Department of Commerce in consultation
11 with the Oklahoma Incentive Evaluation Commission.
12 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
13 Passed the House of Representatives the 26th day of March, 2026.

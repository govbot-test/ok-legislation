4 Section 10-9.17. A. It shall be unlawful and a misdemeanor
5 punishable by a fine of not more than Five Thousand Dollars
6 ($5,000.00) for any person to act, operate, or do business or
7 advertise as a commercial poultry waste applicator or to land-apply
8 poultry waste as a private poultry waste applicator unless such
9 person has obtained a valid applicator's certificate issued by the
10 Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry.
11 B. It shall be unlawful and a misdemeanor punishable by a fine
12 of not more than Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00) for any person to
13 sell, transfer, do business in sales of poultry waste, or to
14 advertise for sale more than ten (10) tons of poultry waste per
15 calendar year unless the person has obtained a valid poultry waste
16 applicator's certificate or has a poultry feeding operation
17 registration issued by the Department.
18 C. Applicator's certificates shall be issued by the Department
19 to applicants who qualify under the provisions of the Oklahoma
20 Poultry Waste Applicators Certification Act.
21 C. D. Certificates shall be issued only upon application
22 therefor to the Department on a form of application prescribed by
23 the Department. The application shall contain information regarding
24
Req. No. 16202 Page 11
1 the applicant's qualifications and proposed operations and such
2 other information as may be specified by the Department.
3 D. E. A certificate shall be issued only after satisfactory
4 completion of such certification standards as determined by the
5 Department.
6 E. F. 1. The Department shall issue a commercial applicator
7 certificate for a five-year period if a properly completed
8 application is filed with the Department not later than the first
9 day of January of the subsequent five-year period. A registration
10 fee of Seventy-Five Dollars ($75.00) shall accompany every initial
11 and renewal application for certification.
12 2. The Department shall issue a private applicator certificate
13 for a five-year period if a properly completed application is filed
14 with the Department not later than the first day of January of the
15 subsequent five-year period. A registration fee of Fifteen Dollars
16 ($15.00) shall accompany every initial and renewal application for
17 certification. The Fifteen Dollar ($15.00) registration and renewal
18 fee shall be waived if the private applicator is also registered
19 pursuant to the Oklahoma Registered Poultry Feeding Operations Act.
20 3. The Department shall issue all certificates for five-year
21 periods beginning January 1, 2025, and expiring on December 31,
22 2029, followed by successive five-year periods.
23 4. All such fees shall be deposited in the Agriculture
24 Regulation Revolving Fund to be used for the purpose of implementing
Req. No. 16202 Page 12
1 the provisions of the Oklahoma Registered Poultry Feeding Operations
2 Act and the Oklahoma Poultry Waste Applicators Certification Act.
3 F. G. Any person issued an applicator's certificate shall
4 comply with the education provisions of subsection F of Section 10-
5 9.5 of this title.

8 Section 10-9.18. A. Every certified poultry waste applicator

10 Oklahoma Department of Agriculture, Food, and Forestry regarding all
11 poultry waste land-applied by such applicator for the period from
12 July 1 of the previous year through June 30 of that year. The
13 report shall contain the following information, as applicable:
14 1. The legal description and or GPS coordinates, county, and
15 the poultry feeding operation registration number, if available, or
16 the GPS coordinates where the poultry waste was produced or
17 acquired;
18 2. The legal description, field identifier, and county where
19 the poultry waste was land-applied;
20 3. Date of each field application or date of each sale or
21 transfer;
22 4. Total and per-acre amount of poultry waste of each field
23 application, or the total amount of poultry waste for each sale or
24 transfer;
Req. No. 16202 Page 13
1 5. Name, mailing address, and telephone number of the person
2 for whom poultry waste was applied, sold, or transferred;
3 6. The most recent soil test analysis results obtained for each
4 field receiving poultry waste; and
5 7. The most recent poultry waste analysis results dated prior
6 to the land application; and
7 8. Such other information as may be required by the State Board
8 of Agriculture.
9 B. The Department shall make available forms to be used in
10 making such report.
11 C. Applicators who seek to obtain a renewal certificate shall
12 submit the report with their application for renewal. No renewal
13 certificate may be issued without submission of this report.
14 D. Any certified poultry waste applicator selling or
15 transferring more than ten (10) tons of poultry waste per calendar
16 year and not performing land application services shall ensure the
17 person acquiring the poultry waste is aware of the requirements of
18 the Oklahoma Poultry Waste Applicators Certification Act and is
19 provided the most recent poultry waste analysis results dated prior
20 to the transfer.

6 A Concurrent Resolution recognizing National Lineman
Appreciation Day; praising linemen from Oklahoma
7 electric providers; and celebrating their work to
power homes, businesses, and localities across the
8 state.
9
10 WHEREAS, Oklahoma linemen are the true heroes of the electric
11 power industry because they are responsible for maintaining and
12 repairing electrical power grids 24 hours a day, 365 days a year;
13 and
14 WHEREAS, these dedicated professionals are often the first to
15 respond during storms and other catastrophic weather events so other
16 first responders can get to the scene safely; and
17 WHEREAS, linemen operate under dangerous conditions, risking
18 their lives high atop power lines to keep electricity flowing; and
19 WHEREAS, linemen also play a vital role in Oklahoma’s economy by
20 maintaining the energy infrastructure that powers homes, businesses,
21 and localities across the state; and
22 WHEREAS, linemen deserve recognition for their hard work to
23 power local communities and keep people safe during emergencies.
24

1 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED BY THE SENATE OF THE 2ND SESSION
2 OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE, THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
3 CONCURRING THEREIN:
4 THAT the Oklahoma Legislature recognizes April 21, 2026, as
5 National Lineman Appreciation Day.
6 THAT the Oklahoma Legislature extends its sincere gratitude to
7 linemen from Public Service Company of Oklahoma, Oklahoma Gas and
8 Electric Company, Oklahoma Municipal Power Authority, the Grand
9 River Dam Authority, local electric cooperatives, Liberty Utilities,
10 and all other electric providers for their dedicated service and
11 swift response to restore power during emergency situations.
12 THAT the Oklahoma Legislature commends local linemen for their
13 vital role in powering homes, businesses, and localities across the
14 state.
15 THAT the Oklahoma Legislature honors linemen for their critical
16 service in all types of weather, recognizing them as essential first
17 responders who risk their lives to ensure the safety and well-being
18 of all communities.

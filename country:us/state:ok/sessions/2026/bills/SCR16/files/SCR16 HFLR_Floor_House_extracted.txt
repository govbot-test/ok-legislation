16 WHEREAS, Oklahoma women have made historic contributions to the
17 development, growth, and strength of our state and nation in
18 countless ways; and
19 WHEREAS, even though women did not yet have the right to vote,
20 in 1907, Kate Barnard became the first woman elected to a statewide
21 office in Oklahoma, and one of the first women elected to a
22 statewide office in the country; and
23 WHEREAS, in 1933, Angie Debo received a Ph.D. in history from
24 the University of Oklahoma, and though she would never hold a

20 THAT the Oklahoma State Legislature applauds and recognizes the

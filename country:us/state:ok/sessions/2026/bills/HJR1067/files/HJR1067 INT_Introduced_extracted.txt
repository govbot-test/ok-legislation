8 A Joint Resolution directing the Secretary of State
to refer to the people for their approval or
9 rejection a proposed amendment to Section 2 of
Article XXV-A of the Constitution of the State of
10 Oklahoma; allowing the State of Oklahoma not cover
Medicaid expansion if federal financial participation
11 drops below ninety-percent matching; providing ballot
title; and directing filing.

15 BE IT RESOLVED BY THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES AND THE SENATE OF THE
16 2ND SESSION OF THE 60TH OKLAHOMA LEGISLATURE:
17 SECTION 1. The Secretary of State shall refer to the people for
18 their approval or rejection, as and in the manner provided by law,
19 the following proposed amendment to Section 2 of Article XXV-A of
20 the Constitution of the State of Oklahoma to read as follows:
21 A. In addition to those otherwise eligible for medical
22 assistance under Oklahoma's Medicaid program, the State shall
23 provide medical assistance under Oklahoma's Medicaid program to Low
24 Income Adults.

1 B. No greater or additional burdens or restrictions on
2 eligibility or enrollment shall be imposed on persons eligible for
3 medical assistance pursuant to this Article than on any other
4 population eligible for medical assistance under Oklahoma's Medicaid
5 program.
6 C. If the federal financial participation is less than ninety
7 percent (90%) for Medicaid expansion, the Oklahoma Legislature may
8 decide to not cover the additional costs to the state.
9 SECTION 2. The Ballot Title for the proposed Constitutional
10 amendment as set forth in SECTION 1 of this resolution shall be in
11 the following form:
12 BALLOT TITLE
13 Legislative Referendum No. ____ State Question No. ____
14 THE GIST OF THE PROPOSITION IS AS FOLLOWS:
15 This measure amends the Oklahoma Constitution. The measure
16 amends Section 2 of Article 25A by allowing the State of
17 Oklahoma to not cover the Medicaid expansion if the federal
18 financial participation drops below ninety percent (90%)
19 matching.

6 An Act relating to alcoholic beverage licenses;
amending 37A O.S. 2021, Section 2-115, which relates
7 to special event licenses; requiring certain
applicant to hold certain liability insurance;
8 requiring certain individual to ensure licensure of
certain event; updating statutory language; and
9 providing an effective date.
10
11
12 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
13 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 37A O.S. 2021, Section 2-115, is
14 amended to read as follows:
15 Section 2-115. A. A special event license may be issued to an
16 organization, association, or nonprofit corporation organized for
17 political, fraternal, charitable, religious, or social purposes.
18 The holder of a special event license is authorized to sell and
19 distribute alcoholic beverage beverages on the premises for which
20 the license is issued.
21 B. The ABLE Alcoholic Beverage Laws Enforcement (ABLE)
22 Commission shall promulgate rules governing the application for and
23 the issuance of special event licenses. Such application shall
24 require the applicant to hold general liability insurance, including
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2178 SFLR Page 1
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 liqu or liability coverage, in an amount not less than One Million
2 Dollars ($1,000,000.00) per occurrence and Two Million Dollars
3 ($2,000,000.00) aggregate.
4 C. The restrictions and rules which apply to the sale of mixed
5 beverages on the premises of a mixed beverage licensee also apply to
6 the sale of such beverages under the authority of a special event
7 license. Any act which if done on the premises of a mixed beverage
8 licensee would be a ground for revocation or suspension of the mixed
9 beverage license is a ground for revocation or suspension of a
10 special event license.
11 D. No special event license may be issued for any premises
12 already licensed by the ABLE Commission.
13 E. No special event license shall be required for an
14 organization, association, or nonprofit corporation which is an
15 economic development chamber or similar entity, provided the event
16 is not conducted primarily for fundraising purposes, and provided
17 the services of a licensed caterer are used to provide and
18 distribute the alcoholic beverages at the event.
19 F. An individual holding an employee license who is serving any
20 alcoholic beverage at a special event shall ensure that such event
21 has been properly licensed.
22 SECTION 2. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.
23 COMMITTEE REPORT BY: COMMITTEE ON BUSINESS AND INSURANCE

14 amended to read as follows:
15 Section 39-102. As used in the Improvement District Act, the
16 singular includes the plural and:
17 1. “Acquired” means the acquisition of property or interests in
18 property by purchase, gift, condemnation or other lawful means;
19 2. “City” means any city or town incorporated pursuant to the
20 laws of Oklahoma;
21 3. “Engineer” means a city engineer, city official, employee or
22 other person competent to advise and assist the governing body in
23 planning and making an improvement;
24

5 c. preparation and publication of notices of hearings,
6 resolutions, ordinances and other proceedings,
7 d. fees and expenses for engineers, attorneys, laborers
8 and other personal services,
9 e. rights-of-way, materials and other lawful expenses
10 incurred in making any improvement, and
11 f. capitalized interest, funding of reserves, premiums
12 for reserve surety bonds, and obtaining bond
13 insurance, letters of credit or other credit
14 enhancements or liquidity instruments;
15 5. “District” means an area designated by the governing body to
16 be benefited by an improvement and subjected to payment of special
17 assessments for all or a portion of the cost of the improvement;
18 6. “Governing body” means the city council, city commission or,
19 board of trustees of an incorporated city or town, or the board of
20 supervisors of a master development district;
21 7. “Governing document” means the document governing the
22 creation of the improvement district or master development district;
23 8. “Improve” means to construct, reconstruct, maintain,
24 restore, replace, renew, repair, install, equip, extend, purchase,

6 reimprovement of any prior improvement made pursuant to any other
7 act;
8 9. 10. “Mail” means by first-class mail;
9 10. 11. “Master development district” means an area of a
10 municipality with defined limits and boundaries that operates

13 against the real property therein for the financing or repayment of

21 development district or improvement district; and
22 15. “Publish” or “publication” means printing in a newspaper
23 which maintains an office in the city or town and is of general
24 circulation within the city or town, or, if there is no newspaper

3 issues thereof, at least seven (7) days apart.
4 SECTION 2. AMENDATORY 11 O.S. 2021, Section 39-103, is
5 amended to read as follows:
6 Section 39-103. A. The governing body of any city may create
7 one or more districts improvement districts for the purpose of
8 making or causing to be made any improvement or combination of
9 improvements that confer special benefit upon property within the
10 district.
11 B. Upon receipt of an application for a proposed master
12 development district, the governing body of any city shall approve
13 the creation of the master development district if the applicant
14 demonstrates the following:
15 1. One hundred percent (100%) of the surface owners within the
16 boundaries of the proposed master development district have
17 consented in writing to its creation and approved the master
18 development district’s governing document; and
19 2. The project area comprises at least two hundred fifty (250)
20 acres or the projected private-sector investment within the district
21 boundaries exceeds Two Hundred Fifty Million Dollars
22 ($250,000,000.00).
23 C. A master development district’s governing document shall
24 create an independent board of supervisors, which shall be the
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2060 SFLR Page 4
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 gove rning body of the master development district. The board of
2 supervisors shall have the same rights and powers as the governing
3 body of a city has over improvement districts, but shall be
4 independent of the municipal governing body.
5 D. The governing bodies of improvement districts and master
6 development districts shall have authority to make such improvements
7 or combinations of improvements as it deems beneficial to the
8 district. Such improvement or combination of improvements may
9 include the following, without limitation because of enumeration:
10 1. Acquisition of property or interest in property when
11 necessary for any of the purposes authorized by the Improvement
12 District Act;
13 2. Opening, creating, widening and extending or altering of
14 streets to improve paving, and surfacing, constructing and
15 reconstructing gutters, curbs, sidewalks, crosswalks, driveway
16 entrances and structures, drainage facilities, and service
17 connections from sewers, water, gas, electricity and other utility
18 mains, conduits or pipes;
19 3. Constructing or improving main and lateral storm water
20 drains and sanitary sewer systems and facilities;
21 4. Installation or improvement of street lights and street
22 lighting systems;
23 5. Construction or improvement of water mains and waterworks
24 systems;
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2060 SFLR Page 5
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 6. Improvement of parks, playgrounds and recreational
2 facilities;
3 7. Improvement of any street, parking or other facility by
4 landscaping, or planting of trees, shrubs and other plants;
5 8. Constructing or improving dikes, levees and other flood
6 control works, gates, lift stations, bridges and streets appurtenant
7 thereto;
8 9. Constructing or improving vehicle and pedestrian bridges,
9 overpasses and tunnels;
10 10. Constructing or improving retaining walls and area walls on
11 public ways or land abutting thereon;
12 11. Constructing or improving property for off-street parking
13 facilities, including construction and equipment of buildings
14 thereon;
15 12. Constructing or improving pedestrian malls; or
16 13. Acquiring or installing pieces of art;
17 14. Acquiring, constructing, or improving libraries,
18 recreational facilities, or other buildings or facilities; or
19 15. Constructing or improving offsite facilities or
20 infrastructure serving all or a portion of land within a district;
21 notwithstanding that, such facilities or infrastructure may also
22 serve areas outside a district, but subject to cost apportionment
23 requirements of subsection A of Section 39-110 of this title.
24

(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 SECTION 3. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
2 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 39-103.2 of Title 11, unless
3 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
4 A. A district is authorized to purchase, construct, acquire,
5 own, operate, maintain, repair, improve, or extend inside and
6 outside its boundaries any and all works, improvements, facilities,
7 plants, equipment, and appliances necessary to accomplish the
8 purposes of the district authorized by law, including all works,
9 improvements, facilities, plants, equipment, and appliances
10 incident, helpful, or necessary to:
11 1. Supply water for municipal uses, domestic uses, power,
12 commercial purposes and all other beneficial uses or controls;
13 2. Collect, transport, process, dispose of, and control all
14 domestic, industrial, or communal wastes whether in fluid, solid, or
15 composite state;
16 3. Gather, conduct, divert, and control local storm water or
17 other local harmful excesses of water in a district;
18 4. Irrigate the land in a district;
19 5. Alter land elevation in a district where it is needed; and
20 6. Navigate coastal and inland waters of the district.
21 B. To ensure the financial stability of a master development
22 district and the security of its assessments, the municipality and
23 the master development district shall enter into a master
24 development agreement.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2060 SFLR Page 7
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 C. The master development agreement shall legally vest the
2 development rights including, but not limited to, permitted uses,
3 densities, and intensities of use, for the duration of the
4 district’s existence. The municipality shall not apply subsequent
5 changes to zoning or land-use regulations that materially impair the
6 district’s ability to generate the revenue necessary to service its
7 bonded indebtedness.
8 SECTION 4. AMENDATORY 11 O.S. 2021, Section 39-115, is
9 amended to read as follows:
10 Section 39-115. A. To pay all or any part of the cost of the
11 improvement, the governing body may issue, in the name of the city
12 or a public trust entity acting on behalf of the city, the district
13 may issue bonds in one or more series and in amounts not exceeding
14 the total cost of the improvement financed by each series, including
15 costs of issuance, capitalized interest, funding of reserves,
16 premiums for reserve surety bonds, and obtaining bond insurance,
17 letters of credit or other credit enhancement or liquidity
18 instruments in connection with each series. If the bonds recite
19 that:
20 1. The proceedings relating to making the improvement and
21 levying the assessments to pay for the improvement have been done in
22 compliance with law; and
23
24
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2060 SFLR Page 8
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. All prerequisites to the fixing of the assessment lien
2 against the tract or parcel of land benefited by the improvement
3 have been performed;
4 such recital shall be conclusive evidence of the facts recited.
5 B. The bonds shall:
6 1. Recite the terms and conditions for their issuance;
7 2. Be payable from the money collected from the assessment
8 authorized in Section 39-111 of this title;
9 3. Bear a rate of interest not less than two percent (2%) of
10 the rate of interest on the deferred installments of the assessment;
11 and
12 4. Mature not later than thirty (30) years after the date of
13 issuance of the bonds of a particular series.
14 C. Payment of the bonds issued for a storm sewer, lighting,
15 street, alley, curb, gutter or sidewalk improvement may be
16 supplemented from gasoline tax money remitted by the State of
17 Oklahoma on or before a date not more than twelve (12) months after
18 the last deferred installment of an assessment is due from the owner
19 of a tract or parcel of land so assessed. Payment of the bonds
20 issued for a water, sewer, gas, electric or other improvement may be
21 supplemented from the funds received by the water, sewer, gas,
22 electric or other facility on or before a date not more than twelve
23 (12) months after the last deferred installment of an assessment is
24 due from the owner of a tract or parcel of land so assessed.
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2060 SFLR Page 9
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 D. The bonds may be issued to the contractor in payment for the
2 construction of the improvement or may be issued and sold:
3 1. In payment of the city’s proportion of the cost of the
4 improvement;
5 2. In payment of the proportionate cost if the improvement is
6 done in cooperation with another governmental agency;
7 3. In payment of the construction of the improvement done under
8 contract; or
9 4. In reimbursement to the city district if the city district
10 constructed the improvement with city district owned or leased
11 equipment and city district employees.
12 E. Any city may contract for the issuance and sale of bonds or
13 assignable certificates.
14 F. Bonds or assignable certificates may be sold at a public or
15 private sale at a discount.
16 G. After the passage of thirty (30) days from the publication
17 of the ordinance or resolution authorizing the issuance of district
18 bonds, any action attacking the validity of any proceedings had or
19 taken by the governing body of the city preliminary to and in the
20 authorization and issuance of the bonds described in the notice is
21 perpetually barred.
22 H. Bonds issued pursuant to this section shall not:
23 1. Constitute public debt, liability, or obligation on behalf
24 of the municipality; or
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2060 SFLR Page 10
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 2. Be considered for any purpose of any constitutional,
2 statutory, or charter debt limitation applicable to the municipality
3 or impair the municipality’s bonding authority.
4 SECTION 5. This act shall become effective November 1, 2026.

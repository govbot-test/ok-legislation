O.S. 2021, Section 39-101, 39-102, and 39-103, which
8 relate to short title, definitions, and creation of

13 rights and powers to board of supervisors of master
development districts; authorizing lien on real

development districts; authorizing districts to make

requiring obligations be payable from certain
16 sources; authorizing districts to take certain
actions related to works or improvements; granting

certain parties enter into a master development
18 agreement; establishing requirements for master
development agreements; directing the State Treasurer

covenants; amending 11 O.S. 2021, Section 39-115,
20 which relates to the issuance of certain bonds;
authorizing the issuance of certain bonds by
21 districts; modifying and specifying applicability of
certain provisions; updating statutory language;
22 providing for codification; and providing an
effective date.

10 SECTION 3. AMENDATORY 11 O.S. 2021, Section 39-103, is
11 amended to read as follows:
12 Section 39-103. A. The governing body of any city may create
13 one or more improvement districts for the purpose of making or
14 causing to be made any improvement or combination of improvements
15 that confer special benefit upon property within the district.
16 B. A master development district shall be created upon the
17 approval of an application to the relevant governing body of a city
18 or county in which the proposed master development district is
19 situated, pursuant to the conditions in this subsection. Upon
20 receipt of an application for a proposed master development
21 district, the governing body of any city or board of county
22 commissioners of any county may approve the creation of the master
23 development district if the applicant demonstrates the following:
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2060 Page 6
1 1. One hundred percent (100%) of the surface owners within the
2 boundaries of the proposed master development district have
3 consented in writing to its creation and approved the master
4 development district’s governing document and improvement plan;
5 2. The governing document prepared by the applicant includes
6 all of the provisions in subsection F of this section; and
7 3. An improvement plan for the district that is conducive to
8 the long-term development of the city or county, as determined by
9 the governing board of the city or board of county commissioners of
10 the county.
11 C. If the proposed master development district is located
12 wholly within an unincorporated area of a county, the county may
13 approve the creation of the master development district by vote of
14 the board of county commissioners or other county entity designated
15 by the board of county commissioners, under the same conditions
16 provided in this section. If the proposed master development
17 district is located wholly within the municipal limits of a city,
18 the city may approve the creation of the master development district
19 by vote of the governing body of the city. If the proposed master
20 development district is partially within an unincorporated area of a
21 county and partially within the municipal limits of a city,
22 application for the proposed master development district shall be
23 made to both the board of county commissioners and the city, and
24

2 development district.
3 D. Any protest challenging the creation of a master development
4 district shall be filed within ten (10) business days following the
5 official approval of the district by the governing body, unless
6 otherwise preempted by law.
7 E. A master development district may adopt its own bylaws,
8 rules, and regulations for the conduct of its business and the use
9 of its facilities.
10 F. A master development district’s governing document shall
11 create an independent board of supervisors, which shall be the
12 governing body of the master development district. The governing
13 document shall include provisions that:
14 1. Restrict members of the independent board of supervisors and
15 their immediate family members from holding an interest in any bond
16 or other securities issued by the board; and
17 2. Delineate provisional officers of the board and a process
18 for transferring control of the board to surface property owners of
19 the district within a reasonable time frame, not to exceed ten (10)
20 years from the initial board appointments.
21 G. The board of supervisors of a master development district
22 shall have the same rights and powers over a master development
23 district that the governing body of a city has over improvement
24 districts, and shall have such other rights and powers as may be

10 state, county, and municipal taxes, including ad valorem, and senior
11 to all other liens or encumbrances, including mortgage liens.

11 SECTION 4. NEW LAW A new section of law to be codified
12 in the Oklahoma Statutes as Section 39-103.2 of Title 11, unless
13 there is created a duplication in numbering, reads as follows:
14 A. An improvement district or master development district is
15 authorized to purchase, construct, acquire, own, operate, maintain,
16 repair, improve, or extend inside and outside its boundaries any and
17 all works, improvements, facilities, plants, equipment, and
18 appliances necessary to accomplish the purposes of the district
19 authorized by law, including all works, improvements, facilities,
20 plants, equipment, and appliances incident, helpful, or necessary
21 to:
22 1. Supply water for municipal uses, domestic uses, power,
23 commercial purposes, and all other beneficial uses or controls;
24
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2060 Page 11
1 2. Collect, transport, process, dispose of, and control all
2 domestic, industrial, or commercial wastes whether in fluid, solid,
3 or composite state;
4 3. Gather, conduct, divert, and control local storm water or
5 other local harmful excesses of water in a district;
6 4. Irrigate the land in a district;
7 5. Alter land elevation in a district; and
8 6. Navigate waters of the district.
9 B. An improvement district or master development district may
10 enter into contracts with public or private entities for the
11 construction, operation, management, maintenance, or provision of
12 facilities and services related to district facilities and
13 improvements.
14 C. To ensure the financial stability and legal status of a
15 master development district and the security of its assessments, the
16 governing body of the approving city or board of county
17 commissioners of the approving county, or both, as provided in
18 subsection C of Section 39-103 of Title 11 of the Oklahoma Statutes,
19 and the board of supervisors of the master development district
20 shall enter into a master development agreement.
21 1. The master development agreement shall constitute a binding
22 contract and shall legally vest in the master development district
23 development rights including, but not limited to, permitted uses,
24 densities, phasing schedules, development standards, and intensities
ENGR. S. B. NO. 2060 Page 12
1 of u se, for the duration of the master development district’s
2 existence. The city or county shall not unilaterally apply
3 subsequent changes to zoning, land-use regulations, impact fees, or
4 building codes that substantially impair the master development
5 district’s ability to generate the revenue necessary to service its
6 bonded indebtedness.
7 2. The city or county shall finalize and execute the master
8 development agreement within sixty (60) days of the date the master
9 development district is created by vote of the applicable governing
10 body; provided, such timeline may be extended by mutual agreement of
11 the city or county and the master development district. To ensure
12 statewide consistency and reduce administrative delay, the State
13 Treasurer, in consultation with the Council of Bond Oversight, the
14 Oklahoma Development Finance Authority, and any other officials
15 needed, shall develop and make publicly available a Standard Master
16 Development Agreement template to serve as the required basis for
17 all such agreements. Any modifications to the standard template by
18 a city or county shall be limited to project-specific technical
19 infrastructure requirements and shall not impair the core vested
20 rights or the financial security of the master development district.
21 D. A master development district may acquire, lease, sell,
22 convey, or otherwise dispose of real or tangible property, including
23 for facilities, parks, streets, or other improvements as permitted
24 by law.

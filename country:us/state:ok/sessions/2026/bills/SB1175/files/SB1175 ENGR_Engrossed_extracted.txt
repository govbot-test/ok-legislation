7 An Act relating to the Oklahoma Water Resources
Board; making an appropriation; requiring that
8 portions of certain appropriated funds be used for
certain purposes; providing an effective date; and
9 declaring an emergency.
10
11 BE IT ENACTED BY THE PEOPLE OF THE STATE OF OKLAHOMA:
12 SECTION 1. There is hereby appropriated to the Oklahoma Water
13 Resources Board from any monies not otherwise appropriated from the
14 General Revenue Fund of the State Treasury for the fiscal year
15 ending June 30, 2027, the sum of Nine Hundred Twenty-five Thousand
16 Dollars ($925,000.00) or so much thereof as may be necessary to
17 carry out the provisions of Enrolled Senate Bill No. 259 of the 2nd
18 Session of the 60th Oklahoma Legislature.
19 SECTION 2. From the funds appropriated to the Oklahoma Water
20 Resources Board in Enrolled Senate Bill No. 1177 of the 2nd Session
21 of the 60th Oklahoma Legislature, the Board shall deposit the sum of
22 Ten Million Dollars ($10,000,000.00) to the Rural Economic Action
23 Plan Water Projects Fund (225), created pursuant to Section 2002.1
24 of Title 62 of the Oklahoma Statutes. The Board may enter into
ENGR. S. B. NO. 1175 Page 1
1 cont racts with financial institutions and execute such instruments
2 as may be necessary to hold and disseminate loan funds in accordance
3 with applicable regulations and Board policies.
4 SECTION 3. From the funds appropriated to the Oklahoma Water
5 Resources Board in Enrolled Senate Bill No. 1177 of the 2nd Session
6 of the 60th Oklahoma Legislature, the sum of Two Million Dollars
7 ($2,000,000.00) shall be used for a project to design a regional
8 water system in northeast Oklahoma.
9 SECTION 4. This act shall become effective July 1, 2026.
10 SECTION 5. It being immediately necessary for the preservation
11 of the public peace, health or safety, an emergency is hereby
12 declared to exist, by reason whereof this act shall take effect and
13 be in full force from and after its passage and approval.
14 Passed the Senate the 14th day of April, 2026.

7 SECTION 1. AMENDATORY 70 O.S. 2021, Section 2621, is
8 amended to read as follows:
9 Section 2621. There is hereby created the Tulsa Reconciliation
10 Education and Scholarship Program to preserve awareness of the
11 history and meaning of the civil unrest that occurred in Tulsa in
12 1921. The purpose of the program is to provide a scholarship award
13 to residents high school students of the Tulsa School District,
14 which was greatly impacted both socially and economically by the
15 civil unrest that occurred in the City of Tulsa during 1921.
16 Beginning with the 2002-2003 school year, or as soon thereafter as
17 practicable, and subject to the availability of funds, the program
18 shall each year make scholarships available to as many students as
19 feasible, up to three hundred scholarships, to qualified residents
20 students of the Tulsa School District, and to qualified students of
21 any other public school district in this state who are direct lineal
22 descendants as defined in subsection C of Section 2623 of this title
23 and who meet the criteria set forth in Sections 2623 and 2624 of
24 this title, and who are intending to pursue studies at an
SENATE FLOOR VERSION - SB2040 SFLR Page 2
(Bold face denotes Committee Amendments)
1 inst itution of higher education in The Oklahoma State System of
2 Higher Education or a private institution of higher education, in
3 this state or who are intending to pursue studies in a postsecondary
4 career and technology education program in this state. The
5 scholarship shall be an amount of not more than not exceeding the
6 equivalent of the average amount of resident tuition for which an
7 eligible Tulsa Reconciliation Education and Scholarship Program
8 participant enrolled at an institution of higher education in The
9 Oklahoma State System of Higher Education or a postsecondary career
10 and technology education program, whichever is applicable, is
11 obligated to pay. The scholarship shall be paid as provided for in
12 Section 2625 of this title. The further purpose of this program is
13 to establish and maintain a variety of educational support services
14 whereby residents students who qualify for the program will be
15 prepared for success in postsecondary endeavors.
